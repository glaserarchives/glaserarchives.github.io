SVA Today
Thursday, October 11, 2018

From the Archives: "Design of Dissent"

This article from the spring 2018 Visual Arts Journal explores the Design of Dissent Collection in the Milton Glaser Design Study Center and Archives, which includes graphic responses to pressing social concerns.
EVENTS

1:00pm | MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Information Session

This information session will provide prospective students with a opportunity to meet with the MFA Photography, Video and Related Media chair, faculty, staff, current students and alumni, and to ask questions. RSVP here. 1:00 – 3:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 101C.

3:30pm | Dog Therapy for SVA Students

Students are invited to de-stress with New York Therapy Animals, presented by SVA Student Health and Counseling Services. 3:30 – 4:30pm. 133/141 West 21 Street, Room 301.

5:00pm | "MySelf: Rikers Island Exhibition" Reception

With NYC Health + Hospitals/Correctional Health Services, MPS Art Therapy presents this annual exhibition of work made by people incarcerated at Rikers Island. 5:00 – 8:00pm. Through Monday, November 19. Viewing hours by appointment; please contact arttherapy@sva.edu or 212.592.2610 to schedule. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:00pm | "Art As Witness: Political Graphics 2016 –18" Reception

This exhibition features work by prominent artists who use satire to respond to today's agitated political climate. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, November 3. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

7:00pm | National Coming Out Day Poetry Reading

SVA Library presents a poetry reading with four prominent, talented, queer poets: Sarah Sala, Candace Williams, Bakar Wilson and MFA Art Writing student Irene Villaseñor. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.
PRESS PICK

New Books from Telgemeier

Vulture talks with graphic novelist Raina Telgemeier (BFA 2002 Illustration) about her two new books coming out in 2019: Guts and Share Your Smile.
NYC PICKS

"Pete Souza: Throw Shade, Then Vote"

A solo exhibition of work by Pete Souza, former chief official White House photographer. Opening reception and book signing tonight, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, December 8. Steven Kasher Gallery, 515 West 26th Street.

"AIOP 2018: Body" Public Festival

Art in Odd Places presents visual and performance art in unexpected public spaces, featuring work by MFA Art Practice alumni Rachel Chick, Andrew Prieto and Alfredo Travieso, and MFA Fine Arts alumni Amy Finkbeiner and Denise Treizman. Through Sunday, October 14. Along 14th Street from Avenue C to the Hudson River.
TAKE NOTE

SVA Galleries Call for Submissions

SVA Galleries welcomes submissions of work by all current matriculated students (undergraduate and graduate) in any medium. A select student jury will review entries to be included in a group exhibition in 2019. Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 is required. Application available here. Deadline: Friday, November 30, 11:59pm. Contact Adam Cable at acable@sva.edu with any questions.

Maaza Mengiste, Unheard-Of Things

As part of its Quijote Talk series, MFA Art Writing presents a talk by Maaza Mengiste, who will ask the audience to examine the connections between language, violence and the image. Thursday, October 18, 6:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

How to Make TV in NYC

Producers reveal the challenges and opportunities of working in New York City, the secrets of the development process from pitch to pilot, and where the jobs are. RSVP here. Monday, October 22, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
