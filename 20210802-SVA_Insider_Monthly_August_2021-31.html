🎙️ Get to Know: Laurel Christy, Title IX Coordinator

﻿Tell us about your role at SVA.

As the Title IX coordinator, I am responsible for enforcing policies related to harassment, discrimination and sexual misconduct. At SVA the Title IX Office enforces policies not only related to sexual misconduct and sex-based harassment/discrimination (Title IX), but all types of protected characteristic harassment and discrimination.


You recently facilitated an Anti-Asian and Anti-AAPI harassment intervention training in partnership with the group Hollaback! Are there future offerings like this in the works?

Hollaback! is an amazing organization that conducts workshops related to reducing all forms of harassment and empowering individuals to make a difference, particularly by learning to be effective bystanders. At the recent training, they did a great job of using the Zoom Webinar platform to present an engaging and interactive session to the many students, staff and faculty who were present. Hollaback! continues to offer training programs to the public in August and September, and we are hoping to partner with them again in the fall on additional kinds of trainings.


What are the most significant takeaways from the training about how students, faculty and staff can protect themselves and each other when we return to campus?

The most important takeaway is for people to know there are options and resources for support and reporting, and that every person has the skills to make a difference in helping to support a safer community. The training focused on the “5 Ds of Bystander Intervention,” which offer options for everyone to find a way to report or intervene that fits their comfort level and the situation. The interventions covered in the training range from a direct approach toward the harassing individual, to finding someone to help, to documenting the situation so that help can be offered later, and more.


Given the intensity of the past year and a half, are there any new or notable safety features or resources, whether virtual or in-person, that students can expect to see this fall?

One of the best places to refer to current information regarding the SVA campus and safety is the website’s reopening page, sva.edu/reopening. There, everyone can find information about SVA’s safety measures with respect to COVID-19 protocols, as well as information on how to report and respond to incidents of harassment and discrimination. That page will continue to be updated as the community returns to campus this fall and will remain a great starting point for resources.
