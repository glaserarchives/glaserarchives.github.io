SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, December 17, 2015
Advertising that Appeals to the Heart
Communication Arts released their Advertising Annual in the November/December issue, and BFA Advertising students received half of the awards in the newly created student category. Read more about the winning student projects from faculty member and BFA Advertising alumnus Frank Anselmo's class, including two that take on humanitarian causes.
Tell Us What You Think!
We want to hear what you think about SVA Today. Take our readership survey—it shouldn't take longer than a few minutes—and let us know what you like, don't like and want to see in SVA Today.
Last Chance: "Humanimalands"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker, the Dear Climate collective, Sophia Hewson, Terence Koh, Celeste Neuhaus, Dana Sherwood and Alicia Toldi that examines the state of humanity and nature in the age of the Anthropocene. Curated by department fellow Patrick Jaojoco. Closes today, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
5:00pm: MFA Fine Arts Open Studios
MFA Fine Arts presents students showing recent work in their studios, with performances from 9:00 - 10:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.
6:00pm: Student Leadership Series, Leadership in Service
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents an information session about local volunteering opportunities for SVA students, faculty and staff, including signups for the SVA Holiday Angel campaign. Attendees will be entered into a $20 gift card raffle. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Turning the Met into a Classroom
Faculty member Peter Hristoff guest blogs at the Metropolitan Museum of Art website about his residency at the museum and the marathon drawing sessions he invited SVA students to attend.
NYC pick of the day
I For Paris
An exhibition of photographs curated by SVA faculty member Dahn Hiuni in honor of the victims of the November terrorist attacks in Paris. All sales proceeds support the families of the victims. Participating artists include Deborah Garwood, Lydia Grey, Peter Hassler, Daniella Pincas, Barbara Rosenthal and Mitchell Weissner, among others. Through Sunday, February 28, 2016. SuperFrench, 521 Halsey Street, Brooklyn.
Take Note
"Intimacy in Discourse" Closes Next Week
SVA Chelsea Gallery, in collaboration with Rail Curatorial Projects, presents an exhibition of paintings whose sizes affect their relationship to viewers. Curated by SVA faculty member Phong Bui. Closes Tuesday, December 22, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
