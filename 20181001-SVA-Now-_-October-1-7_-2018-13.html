SVA Now
October 1 – 7, 2018
News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Life Is An Art

In a conversation at SVA Theatre last week, feminist icon Gloria Steinem and artist and illustrator Barbara Nessim reflected on the early days of their relationship and careers in New York City and offered perspectives on our turbulent political times.

My First Year: Rianne Pyle

Second-year BFA Film student Rianne Pyle reflects on her first year at SVA and shares how she got into filmmaking.

All Aboard the SVA Shuttle

Students and staff at the Visual Arts Press created the designs adorning the SVA Shuttle bus, which transports the SVA community all over campus for free.
IN THE PRESS

Dr. Christine Blasey Ford

Last week, The New York Times featured this illustration of Dr. Christine Blasey Ford by Delcan & Company (BFA Graphic Design alumnus Pablo Delcan's design company), with pool photos by Tom Williams and Melina Mara.

#WhyIDidntReport

The New York Times on BFA Advertising students Ha Jung Song and Bowook Yoon and their instructor Thomas Shim, who created #WhyDidntIReport templates where New Yorkers could share their stories of sexual assault.

The "Underdogs" of New York Fashion Week

Nylon speaks with the people behind the scenes at New York Fashion Week, including event director Dario Calmese (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography).

A Weekend With Steve Heller

AIGA Eye on Design features MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller in its series "Weekend With a Designer."
EVENTS

Charles Traub on Real Photo Show with Michael Chovan-Dalton

A dialogue and book signing with MFA Photography, Video and Related Media chair Charles Traub and Michael Chovan-Dalton, as part of the Real Photo Show, Chavon-Dalton’s podcast. RSVP here. Monday, October 1, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

Ei Arakawa

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by faculty member and artist Ei Arakawa. Tuesday, October 2, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Ellen Harvey

BFA Fine Arts presents visiting artist Ellen Harvey, who will discuss her work. Tuesday, October 2, 7:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

"We Lit Up the Sky: Recent Embroideries and Lace"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of recent works by artist and faculty member Amy Wilson (BFA 1995 Fine Arts). Friday, October 5 – Friday, October 26. Reception: Friday, October 5, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
