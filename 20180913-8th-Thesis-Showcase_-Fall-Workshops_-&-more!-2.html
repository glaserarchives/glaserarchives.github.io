8th Thesis Showcase - Sept 20-22

Join us to celebrate the graduates of the School of Visual Arts' MFA Social Documentary Film with the 8th Annual Thesis Showcase at the SVA Theatre in NYC. This screening series is a preview of documentary shorts and features in-progress, the work of SocDoc's Class of 2018. Filmmakers will be present, and Q&As will follow each screening.

Thesis Showcase features the films of Jessie Adler (pictured), Yiwei Chen, Andrea Cordoba, Lifei Diao, Wanlu Lucy Huang, Atarah Jeffers, Bailin Li, Yicheng Li, Ana Montgomery-Neutze, Hanna Nordenswan, Bella Randle Racklin, Ziyu Tang, Tiantian Wang, and Chuang Xu.   All screenings are free & open to the public. Click here for more info on the films, screening schedule, and to RSVP.

New Fall Weekend Workshops

We've got three weekend workshops coming up in the Fall to help you brush up on your camera & sound skills and perfect your budget. Maybe you're just starting out and need the basics, or you are more experienced and want a refresher - these small classes strive to provide targeted and hands-on approaches to the work. 

Documentary Camera and Sound Intensive Oct. 13-14
Documentary Sound Workshop Oct. 27-28
Creating a Budget for Documentary Film: Nov 3-4

 

Now Streaming, In Theaters, on TV & in Festivals

Streaming Now: SKY & GROUND accompanying a large, extended family by foot from Syria to asylum in Germany. Directed by T. Tibbon & J. Bennett, produced by Maro Chermayeff (SocDoc department chair), cinematography by Axel Baumann.
L. Greenfield’s postcard from the edge of the American Empire GENERATION WEALTH, filmed by Jerry Risius (faculty) is now on Amazon Prime.

In Theaters: MATANGI / MAYA / M.I.A. a startlingly personal profile of the critically acclaimed artist, chronicling her journey from refugee immigrant to pop star opens Sept. 28 in NYC & LA. Directed by S. Lovering, produced by Lori Cheatle (faculty)

On TV: CRADLE OF CHAMPIONS amateur boxers compete for in NYC’s legendary Golden Gloves tournament. Directed by B. Bull; Cinematography by Tom Hurwitz, ASC (faculty) with many SocDoc faculty & alumni on crew.  Airs Sept. 21on Showtime.
PERSONAL STATEMENT Three high schoolers are determined to get their entire class to college, even though they aren't even sure they are going to make it there themselves. Directed by J. Dressner, produced by Beth Levison (faculty). In festivals now, airs on PBS World October 23

In Festivals: Caroline Berler’s (class of 2017) lesbian film history thesis film, DYKES, CAMERA, ACTION! (pictured) is sweeping the festival circuit. Look for it at Raindance and at an LGBTQ festival near you!

 

SocDoc at Camden Film Fest, Getting Real, IFP Week

SocDoc faculty are traveling all around the US - come say hi! At Camden International Film Festival (Maine) this weekend, Erik Spink & Amitabh Joshi are mentoring the North Star Residency. THE PRICE OF EVERYTHING, lensed by cinematography faculty Bob Richman also screens at the festival.

IFP Week (NYC, Sept. 17-20)presents several SocDoc works-in-progress. Alum Raul Paz Pastrana looks at the migrant trail BORDER SOUTH; faculty Deborah Dickson & Tom Hurwitz's directing collaboration WHAT IT TAKES set in a Bronx school; faculty producer Beth Levison has two: MADE IN BOISE and WOMEN IN BLUE; and faculty Lori Cheatle produces love letter mystery BETTY & HENRI. In the Conference, Rose Vincelli Gustine moderates a conversation about Grants & Funding on Monday 9/17 in partnership with SVA's BFA Film department.

And faculty Beth Levison, Kat Patterson and Rose Vincelli Gustine attend the biennial documentary convening: IDA's Getting Real (LA, Sept. 25-27). Check out Beth's panel about the sometimes contentious producers credit.
