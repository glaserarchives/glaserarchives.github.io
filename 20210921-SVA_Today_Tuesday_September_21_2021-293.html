Today: Mid-Autumn Festival
Join this year's in-person Mid-Autumn Festival gathering! There will be a screening of Netflix's Over the Moon and a paper lantern-making activity. A mooncake recipe will be distributed and there will be an opportunity to win a hand-pressured mooncake mould! Today, 4:30 – 6:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level. Capacity is limited, so RSVP here to save your spot.

Organized by 24th Street Residence fall resident assistants, the International Student Office and Student Health and Counseling Services.
Event

10:00am | Morning Meditation
Join Arielle and Abby from Student Health and Counseling Services for morning meditation. 10:00am on Instagram, @svastudenthealth. This week will be focused on achieving your goals. Participants of all levels are welcome.

7:30pm | After School Special: Drunk Bus
This new feature—whose SXSW premiere was canceled due to the pandemic—follows a directionless campus bus driver during his unpredictable late-night shift. After the screening, join numerous alumni who worked on the film for a Q&A, including co-director John Carlucci (BFA 1997 Film and Video) and artists Aditi Khosla (BFA 2014 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects), Ben Kim (BFA 2002 Graphic Design) and Dan Krupin (BFA 2006 Illustration). 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. RSVP here.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Fashion Illustrator Bil Donovan
Surface talks with BFA Illustration faculty member Bil Donovan (BFA 2001 Fine Arts), whose fashion illustrations are on view in "Drawing on Style," an exhibition at Cromwell Place in London.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Class of 2022: SVA Yearbook Needs Your Photo!
The SVA Yearbook needs selfies from students graduating in 2022. Submit your photo here by Saturday, September 25, to be included. Send up to three favorite photos of yourself for us to choose from. The yearbook is free to all graduating students.

Next Week | It's Complicated: Taking Up Space as Asian American Women in Higher Education
Presented by NCORE, this session should particularly benefit Asian American women in higher education who are interested in gathering, reflecting on and sharing strategies for individual and institutional change. Wednesday, September 29, 4:00 – 5:30pm. Register here.
