Community Lecture Series 
Art of Autism- Beyond Etiology: Engaging Expression through Creativity and Listening
Friday, April 14, 6:30-8 pm
133/141 West 21st Street, 
Room 101C

This lecture will discuss the latest research on the etiology of autism and later explores treatment plans using Applied Behavioral Analysis and Art Therapy.  This talk will feature how Art Therapy can promote communication, emotional growth, social interaction, and channel autistic behavior into expressive and creative outlet. Parental perspective and how to support families affected with autism will conclude this lecture.

What I Carry
Exhibition April 18-29
5th Floor Project Space
Opening Celebration and Performance April 21, 6-8pm

Within their residential program, Boys Hope Girls Hope scholars have been focused on issues of social engagement, marginalization, and self identity.  They have been exploring how, amidst tumultuous political times, to become agents of change.  What I Carry is a visual exploration into how our self identification, and all the physical and emotional realities we carry with us affect the process of becoming and creating this change.

Viewings by appointment. Please   contact arttherapy@sva.edu. Learn         more about Boys Hope Girls Hope NY         program at www.bhghny.org.
