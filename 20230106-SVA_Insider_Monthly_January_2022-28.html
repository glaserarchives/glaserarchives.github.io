🎙️ Get To Know Jasmin Smith, Circulation Manager, SVA Library

Tell us a little about your current role and what led you to SVA.

I am a circulation manager in the SVA Library, which means I hire, train and oversee the work of student employees who primarily shelve items and check items in and out to patrons. I also handle issues with patron accounts, missing items, requests for items that SVA does not own and other concerns as they arise. I was a COVID contact tracer under the NYC Health and Hospitals Test & Trace program prior to working at SVA. As someone who mostly worked in libraries before the pandemic, and knowing the city's contact tracing program was ending, I began looking for jobs in New York City academic libraries and was hired at SVA for my current role.


What is your favorite part of working in a library? Is there something that makes SVA’s Library special?

My favorite part of working in a library is connecting patrons with the information they are looking for as well as being among the first people to see new items acquired by the library. The most special thing about the SVA library is the wealth of knowledge among the staff. I would encourage students to come by and speak with our librarians at the reference desk in person about their interests and to make appointments with librarians to gain a more in-depth understanding of the resources the library has to offer.


What else would you like people to know about the SVA Library?

Managers and librarians work regular shifts in Gather, which students can access through the SVA Gather Concourse. Students are able to speak with us using Gather during our office hours instead of making a trip to the library; I'd encourage them to check it out.
