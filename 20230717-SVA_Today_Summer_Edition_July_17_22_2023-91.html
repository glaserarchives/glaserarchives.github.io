EVENTS

Monday | MFA Visual Narrative Prospective Students Day

Visit MFA Visual Narrative’s summer intensive semester in action—jump into classes, talk with students and learn more about the program with Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay). Finish the day with a tour of the 2023 thesis exhibition. Registration is required; RSVP here. Monday, July 17, 2:00 – 6:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 1001.

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Portia Munson

MFA Art Practice presents a talk with Portia Munson, a feminist and environmental artist working in installation, sculpture, painting and digital prints. Tuesday, July 18, 5:00 – 7:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.

Wednesday | Storyteller Series: Jenny Dorsey, Chef and Author

MFA Visual Narrative presents a talk with Jenny Dorsey, chef, author and leader of the nonprofit research organization Studio ATAO. Dorsey discusses how tools like virtual and augmented reality can be used to make physical food and drink experiences more memorable, meaningful and inclusive. Free. RSVP required; register here. Wednesday, July 19, 11:00am – 12:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Wednesday | DJ Workshop and MC Competition, Featuring DJ Scratch

SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion presents a workshop on the art of DJing and an MC competition with award-winning musician and producer DJ Scratch. DJ Scratch will talk about the history of DJing, the art of turntablism and demonstrate the techniques of scratching that made him famous. This event is the first installment in a series celebrating the 50th anniversary of hip-hop. Wednesday, July 19, 4:00 – 6:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

Friday | “We Made THIS” Reception

MFA Visual Narrative presents the thesis projects of its 2023 graduating class. Reception: Friday, July 21, 6:00 – 8:00pm; RSVP required. Through Tuesday, July 25. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

NYC PICKS

Open Studios: Keigwin + Company’s Rhapsody

A guided community dance workshop by Brooklyn Bridge Park artists-in-residence Keigwin + Company focused on creativity and physicality. Open to dancers and non-dancers of all ages. Tuesday, July 18, 6:30 – 8:00pm. Pier 3 Plaza, 334 Furman Street, Brooklyn.

Comrade Sisters Panel and Book Signing

Women of the New York Chapter of the Black Panther Party, Yasmeen Majid and Claudia Chesson-Williams, and Comrade Sisters co-author and photographer Stephen Shames discuss their Black Panther Party experiences and life after the BPP. Free. Friday, July 21, 6:30 – 8:30pm. Poster House, 119 West 23rd Street.

“And The Moon Be Still As Bright”

A group exhibition in collaboration with Civil Art and The Here and There Co. featuring work by Vincent Cy Chen (BFA 2015 Fine Arts). Through Friday, August 18. Harper’s Chelsea 512 + 534, 512 and 534 West 22nd Street.

“It’s Pablo-matic: Picasso According to Hannah Gadsby”

An exhibition examining Pablo Picasso’s complicated legacy through a critical, contemporary and feminist lens, curated by Australian comedian Hannah Gadsby and featuring work by Renee Cox (MFA 1992 Photography and Related Media). Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, September 24. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway.

Submissions Wanted: All-Staff Meeting “Hot Rods & Clunkers” Pre-Show

There’s nothing finer than the experience of hitting the open road in your first car. Whether that car was brand new or a lemon, it still gave you newfound freedoms. For this year’s All-Staff Meeting pre-show, we would like to highlight your first automobile. Please send us the make, model, color of your first car and one memory about it. Was it a fast car or an old jalopy? Did it spend more time at the garage than on the road? We want to hear all about it.


Send your submissions to dhalm@sva.edu by Friday, August 4. Thank you in advance for participating.

Create a Video/Animation, Win $500

SVA Theatre seeks a 10- to 20-second pre-roll video (animated or live action) to play before film screenings shown during this September’s After School Special 2023: SVA’s Alumni Film and Animation Festival.


Set the video to music that you have created yourself, own the copyright for, have obtained a license for, or is in the public domain, and please note its source when submitting. Download logos for the festival, SVA and SVA Theatre here. View examples of past winning videos here and here.


Upload submissions to Vimeo or YouTube, and send an email with a link to the video, your name, graduation year and program major to Adam Natale, director of SVA Theatre, at anatale@sva.edu, by Friday, August 25, 5:00pm. Email Natale with any questions. Final video format should be a digital file (e.g., QuickTime ProRes). The winner will receive $500.
