Alum Ken Lavey Featured in Vice

As a spinoff of his previous work, Perfect Odd Thing, Ken Lavey's latest series, Cavities, was created when he accidentally stumbled upon the unusual erotic qualities of the inside of watering cans. "I like the idea of these images evoking an ambiguous kinship with the body, which people might relate to in different ways, and consider their own physicality and fascination with the human body."

Visit Vice to see more of Ken's work.
Alum Brian Finke Featured in WIRED

Brian Finke (1998) traveled the world visiting ten labs, cooking schools and restaurants last summer shooting The Science of Delicious for National Geographic. The project opened his eyes to the bizarre lengths people go in pursuit of flavor. From a lab in Florida to a restaurant in Melbourne Australia, Brian taste tested ants, engineered tomatoes and bee larva. 

Visit Wired to read more of Brian's experiences. 
Documentary Film-Makers Face Prison Time for Taping Oil Pipeline Protests

Two documentary film-makers are facing decades in prison for recording US oil pipeline protests, with serious felony charges that first amendment advocates say are part of a growing number of attacks on freedom of the press. Deia Schlosberg, a New York-based film-maker, is facing three felony conspiracy charges for filming protesters on October 11th at a TransCanada Keystone Pipeline site in Pembina County in North Dakota, with prosecutors alleging that she was “recruited to record the criminal activity”. The 36-year-old – who produced a documentary called How to Let Go of the World and Love All the Things Climate Can’t Change – could face 45 years in prison.

Anges Martin Retrospective Opens at the Guggenheim 

The Guggenheim Museum presents the first major retrospective exhibition of the work of American painter Agnes Martin since her death in 2004. This historic survey, the most comprehensive ever mounted and featuring more than 110 objects, traces Martin’s career from her lesser-known work of the 1950s to her final canvases of the early 2000s.

On view through Jan. 11, 2017
Guggenheim Museum
1071 5th Ave, New York, NY

Alum Justine Kurland Featured in The New Yorker For Latest Book Highway Kind

In this article in the New Yorker, Justine Kurland (1996) explains balancing her life of being a mother and a photographer. For the first six years of her son's life, Justine traveled in a car around the country photographing the American landscape. Justine's photographs and writings are being published by Aperture later this year in a book, Highway Kind. 

Visit The New Yorker to read more about Justine's journey figuring out how to become a photographer and a parent.
