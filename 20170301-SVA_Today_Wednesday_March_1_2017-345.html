Next Stop: Cape Town

The next stop for "Underground Images," SVA's traveling exhibition of subway series posters, is Design Indaba's Nightscape in Cape Town, South Africa, through Friday, March 3.

12:30pm | Applying for Arts Opportunities

SVA Career Development will cover best practices for researching, assessing and applying for arts opportunities.12:30 – 2:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

6:30pm | Bob’s Burgers Screening and Q&A with John Roberts

Center for Communication and SVA Theatre present a screening of the show Bob's Burgers followed by a Q&A with Emmy Award-nominated actor John Roberts, who provides the voice of Bob’s wife, Linda. He’ll talk about breaking into comedy, gaining attention on YouTube with his viral hits and how he co-wrote a script and took it from pitch to pilot. Free with SVA ID; RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

Fold Origami, Embrace Diversity, Unite As a Community

SVA Library and the International Student Office present two origami workshops for students, staff and faculty to celebrate diversity at SVA. Students can also become "origami ambassadors" to teach others. A collection of the origami will be installed at the SVA Library and the 24th Street Residence. Tomorrow, March 2, 3:00pm, and Friday, March 3, 6:30pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

Biodesign Challenge Information Session

The Biodesign Challenge is a chance to collaborate with a group of SVA students and alumni to create an innovative project inspired by biology. The challenge offers art and design students the opportunity to envision future applications of biotechnology in a competition that highlights student work. If interested, contact biolab@sva.edu or attend the information session tomorrow, March 2, 3:00pm. SVA Bio Art Lab, 335 West 16th Street, 3rd floor.

"Once Upon a Time . . ." Reception

BFA Fine Art presents an exhibition of student work that reflects our current state of affairs in a time where we are faced with false news, alternative facts, gentrification, xenophobia and racism. Tomorrow, March 2, 6:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
