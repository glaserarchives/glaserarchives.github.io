News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Monday, July 24 - Sunday, July 30, 2017
FEATURES

Marshall Arisman Retrospective

SVA presents "Marshall Arisman: An Artist’s Journey From Dark to Light, 1972 – 2017," an exhibition spanning the legendary artist's 45-year career to date, featuring paintings, sculptures, etchings, editorial illustrations and short films. On view Saturday, August 19 – Saturday, September 30. Reception: Tuesday, August 22, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Summer Show Highlights

Two current New York exhibitions feature paintings by Ryan Brown (BFA 2006 Fine Arts) and Rebecca Ward (MFA 2012 Fine Arts), both of whom play with the idea of painting itself.

A Post-Grad "Dream Come True"

Christina Faraj (BFA 2016 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) discusses the connections she made at SVA, the inspiration that New York City provides, working at Pixar and more.
THIS WEEK

Tuesday: Christopher Lew and Mia Locks

MFA Art Practice presents a talk by Christopher Lew and Mia Locks, the curators of the 2017 Whitney Biennial. Tuesday, July 25, 12:30pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Thursday: MFA Visual Narrative Open Studios and Print Slam

MFA Visual Narrative presents the fourth annual extravaganza of storytelling work from its first- and second- year students. There will also be an exhibition and sale of work made this past summer semester. RSVP here. Thursday, July 27, 5:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 10th and 11th floors.

Sunday: Valerian and Atomic Blonde Screenings

The Visual Effects Society presents screenings of Valerian and the City of a Thousand Planets, Sunday July 30, 3:00pm, and Atomic Blonde, 7:00pm. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICKS

Murals at 30 Rock

Untapped Cities on the new temporary mural in Rockefeller Center that JM Rizzi (BFA 1997 Graphic Design) and artist Jason Woodside created.

The Punny Composer

Vanity Fair talks with Oscar-winning composer Michael Giacchino (BFA 1990 Film and Video) about how he often works witty puns into his film score track titles.

Kickin' It with Quesada

Uproxx interviews CCO of Marvel Entertainment Joe Quesada (BFA 1984 Media Arts) about diversity at Marvel and the importance of the story.
NYC PICKS

The Puzzle of Personal Identity: How Comic Books Illuminate Real World Issues

Ben Abelson, assistant professor of philosophy at Mercy College, will survey Marvel and DC storylines that raise questions about whether or not a person’s identity can survive radical changes. Tickets $12; purchase here. Tuesday, July 25, 7:00pm. Nowadays, 56-06 Cooper Avenue, Queens.

Animation Block Party

A festival of independent, professional and student animation at BAM Cinematek and Rooftop Films. Schedule here. Ticket prices and locations vary by screening. Thursday, July 27 – Sunday, July 30.

Women, Design, and Empowerment: I Will What I Want

Michelle Millar Fisher, an educator in art, architecture, and design histories at several institutions, will discuss how designs that are meant for women have governed, shaped, and facilitated modern and contemporary experiences. Tickets $20; purchase here. Friday, July 28, 7:00pm. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.

New York City Poetry Festival

A weekend of poetry readings, open mics and more. Free; RSVP here. Saturday, July 29 – Sunday, July 30, 11:00am – 6:00pm. Governors Island, Nolan Park, 10 South Street, #7.
TAKE NOTE

Submissions Wanted: All-Staff Meeting "Get to Work" Pre-Show

To start this year’s All-Staff Meeting, we’d like to highlight your first job. Babysitting? Collecting tickets at the movie theater? Folding clothes at The Gap? Dog walking? Magician’s assistant? Whatever you did we want to hear all about it. Please send us your first job title, the name of the company you worked for and in what city you worked. Thank you in advance for participating. Please send your submissions to Dan Halm at dhalm@sva.edu by Friday, August 4.
