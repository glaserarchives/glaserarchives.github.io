Tuesday, October 20, 2015

Decoding Agnes Martin
MFA Art Writing faculty member Nancy Princenthal speaks about the difficulty of writing her biography of the reticent painter, which was published this summer by Thames & Hudson. Princenthal speaks about her book and the work of Martin on Thursday, October 22, at 6:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
3:30pm: Mindfulness Workshop, Staying Active
Student Health and Counseling Services presents a mindfulness workshop to help students explore ways they can use mindful activity as a method of combating stress and slowing down an overactive mind. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
6:00pm: Dave McKenzie
The artist and MFA Fine Arts faculty member talks about his work. Presented by MFA Fine Arts. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:30pm: Cas Holman, Designing for Play
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Cas Holman discussing her experience creating designs that encourage open-ended imagination and allow users to develop habits of agency, independence and self-determination. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
7:00pm: Mirrorless Camera
MPS Digital Photography and Sony present department chair Katrin Eismann, Sony Alpha product manager Kenta Honjo, photojournalist Ben Lowy, photographer and filmmaker Ben Moon and portrait and wedding photographer Miguel Quiles in a panel discussion on how technological developments such as the mirrorless camera have improved the quality of digital photography, reduced the size and weight of cameras and increased accessibility to photography. Moderated by Pulitzer Prize-winning celebrity and portrait photographer Brian Smith. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
Press pick of the day
Lynn Shelton Partners with This American Life on New Film
The MFA Photography, Video and Related Media alumnus will direct The Incredible Case of the P.I. Moms, the real-life story of a P.I. agency created and staffed by "soccer moms."
NYC picks of the day
Inside the Four Seasons and Other Landmarked Interiors
Learn the backstories of the known and not-so-known landmarks in New York, such as the Dime Savings Bank, City Hall and Tweed Courthouse. Use code "Save50" for $10 tickets. 6:30pm, Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
Rookie Yearbook 4 Launch
Edited by Tavi Gevinson, the fourth edition of Rookie features interviews and exclusive content from Marina and the Diamonds, Willow Smith, Lorde and Donna Tartt. Tickets are $10 and can be used toward purchasing the book. 7:00pm, powerHouse Arena, 37 Main Street, Brooklyn.

"Techtonic Shift" Opening
MFA Computer Art presents an exhibition of work by department alumni and faculty members, featuring prints, videos and interactive installations that incorporate a variety of analog and digital media and experimentat with technical and conceptual layers. Saturday, October 24, through Wednesday, November 25, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
