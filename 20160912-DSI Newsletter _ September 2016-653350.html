The Measured Symposium.

Understanding and Scaling the Impact of Design

January 23–24, 2017

The social design process – human centered design principles, methods and tools – is being used by a growing number of funders, designers and social sector practitioners as an approach to solving complex social problems. Significant resources are being invested, and an enormous buzz has emerged about design’s potential for increasing impact, yet no comprehensive effort has been undertaken to document, measure or analyze its benefits. Until now.

DSI Advisor Erik Hersman in the Big News
When Mark Zuckerberg made his first visit to sub-Saharan Africa recently, he ended up at “the most famous of Africa’s Innovation hubs.” According to Forbes, “He also popped into the office of BRCK, a remarkable internet connectivity device that has since morphed into a clever education system for the developing world. Both have been co-founded by Erik Hersman – who is also the co-founder of Ushahidi, the real-time reporting tool created during the violence following Kenya’s disputed 2008 elections and is considered – along with M-Pesa – as one of Kenya’s greatest tech success stories.”

Listen for it: The Understatement

We’ve been working on a new digital journal, which we describe as “Thoughts on the State and Practice of Social Design.” It will be our vehicle for going deeper into what social design is, how it works and where it’s working. Our ambition is to bring more rigor to our constantly evolving and growing field, and we’ll publish articles from DSI and others we admire. The first issue will be available towards the end of September, with contributions from Hayley Hughes at IBM, Jeffrey Hollender and Cheryl Heller.

In the News

DSI Alums Will Speak at A Better World By Design

’16 Alums David Rojas, Azmina Alimohd, Manolo Ampudia, Emily Herrick, Lauren Gardner, Caroline McAndrews and Rinat Sherzer will present at this year’s BWxD on September 23rd-25th. The theme is “Interplay,” defined as the personal scale and role that objects and designs play in changing the way we relate to our surroundings.

DSI Students Will Once Again Participate in the Social Good Summit

Karina Davila, Jade Broomfield, Marlyn Martinez Marrero and Wen Tang will assist with the Social Good Summit, a two-day conference examining the impact of technology and new media on social good initiatives around the world. The theme this year asks the question, “What type of world do I want to live in by the year 2030?” Great Question! This is the third year our students have participated in this Summit. You can see last year’s efforts here.

An Update on the V Store by Maia Kaufman, ‘16

After a weekend sprint led by DSI faculty Jane Englebardt, The V Store is exploring launching with a digital platform; specifically a subscription box business model. This will allow for the introduction of new products and ideas, as well as helpful, medically-accurate (and of course, well-designed) information and instructions. The V Store will also pursue live events such as festivals and pop-up stores, with a physical store still in the works for a later date.

Follow the V Store on social media, for regular posts with helpful information that breaks taboos on women’s health and sexuality.

One of Many Summer Internships
Karina Davila, ’17, was a Summer Design Fellow at the Office of Innovation at UNICEF this summer. She says her most challenging undertaking was to create a new on-boarding action that puts the employees experience first. As part of her process,
she researched the office’s current environment and interviewed new employees about implementations that would have been helpful during their first days. She also shadowed team members, during the on-boarding process to see if she could identify gaps.
