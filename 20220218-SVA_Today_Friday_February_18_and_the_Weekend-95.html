Events

The Book Show
An exhibition of work by the MFA Illustration as Visual Essay class of 2022, curated by department chair Marshall Arisman and faculty member and alumnus Anna Raff. Through Saturday, March 12. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

The Museum of Extraordinary Things
An exhibition of work by MFA Illustration as Visual Essay students, inspired by the book of the same title by Alice Hoffman and curated by faculty member, alumnus and BFA Illustration and BFA Cartooning Chair Viktor Koen. Through Saturday, March 12. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

AASU Postcard Drive
The SVA Asian American Student Union (AASU) invites the SVA community to write and decorate postcards this week in support of the Ain't I A Woman?! campaign protesting 24-hour workdays for local home-care workers. Postcards will be available at both the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, and SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, through today.

10:00am | Resident Assistant Information Session
Interested in being an RA this summer or next academic year? Attend this information session to learn more about the position and receive the application materials. Attendance at an information session is mandatory to receive the application materials. Today, 10:00am ET, on Zoom.

11:00am | Riots and Power: A Radical Examination Through Art and Exhibitions
Berlin-based curator Natasha Ginwala talks with artist Natascha Sadr Haghighian about racial violence and the deepening crises of capitalism in an “age of riots.” Presented by MA Curatorial Practice. Today, 11:00am ET, on Zoom.

12:00pm | Book Launch: Intersectionality in the Arts Psychotherapies
MPS Art Therapy hosts Jessica Collier and Corrina Eastwood, editors of Intersectionality in the Arts Psychotherapies (2022), who will be joined by various chapter authors—including SVA faculty member and alumnus S.J. Langer—for a talk about the book. Today, 12:00pm ET, on Zoom.

12:00pm | Room Selection Housing Tours
If you are a current SVA student who is interested in living in the 24th Street, Gramercy or Ludlow residences for the coming academic year, stop by for one of Residence Life's housing tours, taking place today from 12:00 – 4:00pm.

To connect with a tour of the 24th Street and Gramercy residences, visit the Residence Life office, 340 East 24th Street, between 12:00 – 4:00pm. To join a tour of the Ludlow Residence, stop by the security desk at 101 Ludlow Street during those hours. Be sure to complete and show your eligible Cleared4 badge on your phone to the security officer at either location. For more information, visit sva.edu/residencelife.

2:00pm | SVA Language Exchange Social
Join Cong (Holly) Fu from Student Health and Counseling Services and William Park from the International Student Office, connect with other SVA community members based on your language interest and practice your desired language (including English)! Today, 2:00pm ET, on Zoom. RSVP here.

5:00pm | Beyond Representation: Becoming a Leader in the Industry, With Hsiang Chin Moe
SVA's Asian American Student Union and Women In Animation host this talk with BFA Animation Chair Hsiang Chin Moe (MFA 2008 Computer Art). Today, 5:00pm ET, on Zoom. Register here.

6:30pm | Fab Friday with Joo Young Yoon
Joo Young Yoon, senior designer at Claudia Li, talks about the future of fashion and new opportunities for creative thinkers within the industry. She will also be teaching a new fashion-focused graphic design course in the fall. Fab Friday is a weekly design inspiration guest lecture series hosted by BFA Advertising and BFA Design. Tonight, 6:30pm ET, on Zoom.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

The Booster Bus Is Back
All eligible students, faculty and staff who need a COVID-19 vaccine booster can get one at the booster bus, which will be hosted on campus by Student Health and Counseling Services for two days next week. Eligible recipients can receive a $100 incentive.

The bus will be parked outside the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, Tuesday, February 22, and Thursday, February 24, 9:00am – 6:00pm. Appointments are not necessary.

Bring proof of prior vaccination and proof of ID (i.e. government-issued driver's license or passport). For questions about booster eligibility, click here. Remember to upload your booster verification into Cleared4.
