There is an Air Quality Alert set for the New York City area due to wildfires occurring in Canada. This means that the Air Quality Index is higher than the standard for healthy breathing due to pollutants or particles in the air. While this alert is in effect, exposure to the outside air could be harmful for those with certain health conditions such as asthma or cardiac conditions, as well for those who are very young or over age 65. 


It is best to limit your outdoor exposure during this time, especially if you fall in those categories. When you can, avoid opening your windows and use central air conditioning. It may be helpful to wear a well-fitting mask when going outside. If you need a mask, please visit a security desk on campus and you will be provided with one. Please monitor your health carefully and seek medical attention if symptoms arise.
