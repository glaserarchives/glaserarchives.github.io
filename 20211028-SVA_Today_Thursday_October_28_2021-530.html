SVA x The Hugh
The Hugh, a new space in midtown Manhattan, features designs by BFA Interior Design Chair Carol Bentel and her firm, Bentel & Bentel; an exhibition of student work curated by BFA 3D Design Chair Kevin O'Callaghan (BFA 1980 Graphic Design); and a mural by BFA Illustration faculty member Yuko Shimizu (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay).
Events

9:30am | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 9:30 – 11:00am, the International Student Office (ISO) hosts virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to "International Student Office Staff" headline).

Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu if you have any questions.

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour today, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

3:30pm | All-Staff Meeting
After many months of remote work, come catch up with your colleagues, hear what's happening around the College and get a little taste of Halloween at the 2021 All-Staff Meeting.

To attend in person, your SVA ID is required and you must have completed the Cleared4 process at least two hours prior to the start of the meeting. For those unable to attend in person, the meeting will be live-streamed. Pre-register now for a link.

Doors open at 3:30pm. Program begins at 4:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Cariño Wins NewFest Special Mention
Cariño, the thesis film of Roshel Amuruz, Carlos Taborda and Ashley Williams (all BFA 2021 Computer Arts, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) has won a Special Mention prize at the 2021 NewFest LGBTQ+ Film and Media Festival! Via Twitter.
in town and online

Gilded Figures: Wood and Clay Made Flesh
The Hispanic Society Museum and Library presents an exhibition of rarely shown polychrome sculptures from 1500 – 1800. Through Sunday, January 9. Thursday – Sunday, 12:00 – 6:00pm. Free. All visitors must show ID and proof of COVID-19 vaccination. Entrance on the west side of Broadway between 155th and 156th Streets.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

SVA Destinations: Film and Animation Behind the Scenes in Los Angeles
SVA Destinations has announced a winter 2022 program in Los Angeles. Join faculty member Sal Petrosino on behind-the-scenes visits to Hollywood-area animation and film studios and meet with some of today's leading visual artists in the entertainment industry. Sunday, February 27 – Saturday, March 5. Enrollment limited to 20 students. For more information and to apply, visit destinations.sva.edu.

Submit Your Work to Illustrators 64 Annual Competition
The Society of Illustrators is accepting entries for its Illustrators 64 Annual Competition. Deadline: Friday, October 29, 11:59pm. For more information and to submit work for consideration, click here.

Next Week | Accra Shepp and Sarra Idris
Photographer and SVA faculty member Accra Shepp joins filmmaker and editor Sarra Idris to discuss Radical Justice: Lifting Every Voice, Shepp's book of portraits taken during the 2011 Occupy Wall Street and 2020 Black Lives Matter protests. Presented by MFA Photography, Video and Related Media. Tuesday, November 2, 6:30pm. Register here.
