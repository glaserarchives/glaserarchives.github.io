🎙️ Get to Know: Sam Modenstein, Executive Director, External Relations

Congratulations on your forthcoming retirement after nearly three decades at SVA! Can you summarize your SVA journey up to this point?

How do I make a rather long story short? I arrived at SVA as a freelance professional organizer, working in the Finance Office. In fall 1994, with my background in advertising and broadcast production, and a desire to do work that was meaningful, I became the director of Career Planning and Placement [now Career Development]. In 1998, I founded the Office of Promotion and became its director, and finally, in 2000, I was named the first executive director of External Relations, which houses the offices of Alumni Affairs and Development, Career Development, Communication and SVA Galleries.


In what ways has SVA changed the most? The least?

The growth of the College is the most obvious change: the increased number of departments and offices, students, staff and the larger physical plant. I am thrilled to see growth in the diversity of leadership and management, particularly the increase in women. When I started at SVA, I believe I was one of only two or three women directors and for more than a decade I was the only woman executive director. These numbers have substantially—and thankfully—improved. What has remained constant is the leadership of the Rhodes family, the core values they established for the institution and instill in us every day, and the incredibly creative faculty and staff who share those values.


Of all the major projects you've helped usher in over the years, including SVA Shows, participating in art fairs and the All-Staff Meeting, is there one you're particularly proud of?

I’ve had the privilege to produce some amazing projects and events over the years, but I am particularly proud of creating the Internship for Credit program. Since its inception in 1996, the program has provided hundreds of SVA students the chance to gain hands-on experience working with creative professionals while receiving credits.


Can you share a favorite SVA memory that will stick with you forever?

I won’t forget meeting and speaking with longtime Congressman John Lewis at the 2014 pre-commencement reception, where he signed my copy of March. During the next day’s ceremony, he told us all to “make good trouble.” He was just one of the very many remarkable individuals I met through my association with SVA.


I have been given so many opportunities during my tenure here: to try out new ideas and grow in my career, to interact with so many accomplished individuals, to travel around the country and the world. My hope is that I have given something back to the College, its students and to those I have worked with over these many years.

﻿﻿ The Distinguished Staff Awards: Deadline Soon!

Each year at the All-Staff Meeting, SVA recognizes outstanding staff contributions to the College with the presentation of two Distinguished Staff Awards. Each award includes a cash prize of $5,000. It’s up to you to nominate one of your administrative-staff colleagues to receive this award. Award information, a list of eligible employees and the nomination form are available at staffawards.sva.edu. Nominations are due at 5:00pm ET on Friday, May 6.
