Art Education and Conservation in Madagascar
August 3 – 26
For Artists, Photographers, Teachers, and Scientists
Course number: AEG-5231-A
Tuition: $4,400

DEBORAH ROSS AND ROSE VIGGIANO
Join us this summer for an unforgettable experience in Madagascar. This program will take place at Centre ValBio, a research center located on the edge of Ranomafana National Park and dedicated to protecting Madagascar’s unique and biologically diverse ecosystems through conservation science and projects that directly benefit the local people. We will take expeditions into the rainforest to observe and learn about wildlife, and visit village schools where local educators will facilitate art/science workshops. Participants will collaborate with Malagasy educators and field experts to develop new art and science curriculum, supporting the ongoing efforts to bring environmental education to both indigenous and international communities. 
For more information about this program, please contact Rose Viggiano at rviggiano@sva.edu or 212.592.2449.
