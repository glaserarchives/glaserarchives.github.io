SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, December 3, 2015
Photo by Aria Isadora/BFA.com.
SVA Opens at Miami Art Week
It's Miami Art Week, and SVA Galleries is exhibiting work by alumni at PULSE Miami Beach, while BFA Photography and Video has a booth showing recent alumni work at Miami Project. Other SVA alumni are represented at galleries exhibiting at fairs throughout Miami.
"Humanimalands" Opens
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker, the Dear Climate collective, Sophia Hewson, Terence Koh, Celeste Neuhaus, Dana Sherwood and Alicia Toldi that examines the state of humanity and nature in the age of the Anthropocene. Curated by department fellow Patrick Jaojoco. Through Thursday, December 17. Reception Monday, December 7, 6:30 - 8:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
6:00pm: "Almost Home" Reception
An exhibition curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellows Valerie Amend, Becky Nahom, Ikechukwu Onyewuenyi and Vera Petukhova. The works on view reflect the different strands of "home," as defined by geography, built and virtual space, domestic life and memory. Through Tuesday, December 15, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
7:00pm: MPS Digital Photography Online Information Session
MPS Digital Photography presents an online information session for prospective students. Hear from students, alumni and department chair Katrin Eismann during this unique live session. Log-in information related to the session will be sent upon registration.
Press pick of the day
Art Basel Artist of the Day: Samuel T. Adams
Paper interviews the MFA Fine Arts alumnus about his geometric and abstract paintings.
NYC picks of the day
Matthew Jensen: "The Wonder Under"
Artist Matthew Jensen discusses his work currently on view at 99 Plymouth, which uses photos of found objects to capture the histories of public landscapes in New York City. 7:00pm, 99 Plymouth Street, Brooklyn.
The Museum of Feelings
The interactive installations comprising this exhibition invite emotional responses from visitors. Admission is free. Through Tuesday, December 15. Brookfield Place, 230 Vesey Street.
Star Wars and the Power of Costume
Featuring 70 hand-crafted costumes from the first six blockbuster Star Wars films, this exhibition reveals the artists’ creative process—and uncovers the connection between character and costume. General admission tickets are $27.50. Through March 31, 2016. Discovery Times Square, 226 West 44th Street.
Take Note
VASA Holiday Bazaar
The Visual Arts Student Association presents a holiday bazaar, featuring student artwork, zines, crafts, food and much more. Tomorrow, 6:00 - 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Dukhtar
The SVA Theatre and The Iqra Fund present a screening of Dukhtar, which tells the story of a mother and daughter on the run after her daughter is promised in marriage to a much older man. Members of the film's production team and Iqra Fund founder Genevieve Walsh will answer questions following the screening. Discounted tickets are $12.25 for SVA students, faculty and staff with the code "student." Saturday, December 5, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Michael Garofalo: The Cut and Paste Narrative
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents producer Michael Garofalo discussing his work with NPR's StoryCorps. Tuesday, December 8, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
MFA Social Documentary Film Information Session
MFA Social Documentary Film presents an information session and department tour for prospective students, featuring students, faculty and department chair Maro Chermayeff. Thursday, December 10, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
