Fall Show Highlights

Three current New York exhibitions feature work by Ilan Averbuch (BFA 1982 Fine Arts), Matthew Pillsbury (MFA 2004 Photography, Video and Related Media) and Pacifico Silano (MFA 2012 Photography, Video and Related Media) and bring politics to the fore through artistic intervention and imagination.

SVA at NY Comic Con

This past weekend, SVA was well-represented at New York Comic Con 2017, where SVA community members showcased their work and participated in panel discussions.

Art by the Incarcerated

A Q&A with Lesley Achitoff (MPS 2004 Art Therapy), who with Robert Belgrod (MFA 2016 Art Therapy) curated "Safe Spaces," an exhibition featuring artwork by people incarcerated on Rikers Island. On view through Thursday, October 26. MPS Art Therapy Project Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
IN THE PRESS

Preview: KAWS at Frieze

Hypebeast presents an early look at KAWS' (BFA 1996 Illustration) solo booth at Frieze London, which opens on Tuesday, October 17.

Gerard Way Tells Stories with Heart

The Guardian profiles musician and comic book creator Gerard Way (BFA 1999 Cartooning), who unveiled new titles at New York Comic Con.

Behind the Scenes of New York Indie Theater

PDN on Kent Meister's (MPS 2017 Digital Photography) photo series "We Are Stories: Face of New York Indie Theater," which documents what goes on backstage. These photos will be on view at MPS Digital Photography's show "Detours," opening Saturday, October 14.
EVENTS

Franklin Boyd

MFA Fine Arts presents department faculty member, lawyer, collector and entrepreneur Franklin Boyd, who will address the issue of determining the legal creator of an artwork in this age of casual collaborations and collectives. Tuesday, October 10, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

A Conversation with Mari Nakano

MFA Interaction Design presents a talk by deputy director of Product and Design for the New York Mayor’s Office of Economic Opportunity Mari Nakano, who helps shape one of the nation’s first civic service design studios. RSVP online. Wednesday, October 11, 6:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

Memory of Oblivion

MA Curatorial Practice fellow Maria Sáenz curated this exhibition, which creates a paradoxical dialogue by juxtaposing the concepts of memory and oblivion. Through Wednesday, October 25. Reception: Thursday, October 12, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
