News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Tuesday, April 25, 2017
Photo of Alex Hovet by Nir Arieli.

Where Physical and Digital Memory Meet

A Q&A with MFA Photography, Video and Related Media student Alex Hovet, who won a 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award for her project "External Memory Devices," a multimedia installation that examines the new scientific process of encoding a person’s digital files within DNA.
EVENTS

3:00pm | Dog Therapy

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and New York Therapy Animals host this end-of-year dog therapy event for SVA students. Stop by between classes and work to de-stress. 3:00 – 4:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th-floor flex space.

6:30pm | Elizabeth Glickfeld

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by design writer, editor, publisher and educator Elizabeth Glickfeld, who founded the independent design magazine Dirty Furniture. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Debi Cornwall

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer Debi Cornwall, whose long-term exploration of the legacy of "Gitmo," the U.S. Guantanamo Bay Detention Camp, has resulted in multiple exhibitions and now a monograph, Welcome to Camp America. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK

"Dreamy" Drawings

Vice's blog Creators on illustrator Tomer Hanuka (BFA 2000 Illustration), whose "intent in illustration is storytelling through visuals."
NYC PICKS

Paula Scher: Designer as Educator

A screening of the documentary about BFA Design faculty member Paula Scher that was commissioned by the department and directed by recent alumna Claudine Eriksson. The screening will be followed by a panel discussion with faculty members and alumni Peter Ahlberg and Gail Anderson, designer Joe Marianek and designer and alumnus Zipeng Zhu. 6:30 – 8:30pm. The Type Directors Club, 347 West 36th Street.

"Cindy Sherman: Once Upon a Time"

An exhibition of works by photographer Cindy Sherman spanning 30 years and focusing on three of her most acclaimed series. Through Saturday, June 10. Mnuchin Gallery, 45 East 78th Street.
TAKE NOTE

MFA Computer Art Thesis Presentations

MFA Computer Art presents this two-night event showcasing the thesis projects of its graduating class. The first night will highlight fine art and motion graphics work; the second night will feature animation. Tuesday, May 2, and Wednesday, May 3, 6:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
Katherine Bernhardt (MFA 2000 Fine Arts), Pink Panther + a Cigarette, 2016, acrylic and spray paint on canvas. Via Arts + Culture Texas. The news site covered Bernhardt's exhibition of large and vibrant paintings at The Modern Art Museum of Fort Worth.
