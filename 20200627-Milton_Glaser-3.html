To the SVA Community,

Milton Glaser, a central and essential member of the SVA community for 60 years, died last night in New York City where he was born. Arguably the most important and celebrated designer in America, he was the first graphic designer to be awarded the National Medal of Arts.

 

He began teaching what would become his renowned and remarkably fertile class in 1960, when SVA had existed for just twelve years and had only recently become School of Visual Arts. His class, Design and Personality, hosted by our burgeoning Continuing Education program, was open to all by interview and quickly became known as a launching pad for a broad constellation of successful and consequential creative professionals. Despite the complexities and demands of his own multifaceted career, Milton taught this course every Wednesday evening until 2002 when he began teaching in SVA’s MFA Design program.

 

Milton was also essential to the governance of a growing SVA. First elected to the Board of Trustees in 1982, he succeeded Silas Rhodes as Chair in 2007. His guidance and support proved crucial to me as our college developed and grew. Similarly, over those decades Milton became the social conscience of the College, offering wise counsel and proposing an unending stream of important ethically centered projects. 

 

Our beloved friend and compass Milton Glaser will be missed every day at SVA, around this city, and around the world.

 

A link to his New York Times obituary is below. As details of events in his honor become available, we will post them on the SVA website.

 

With gratitude for our friend and mentor and in sadness for our loss,

 

David Rhodes
