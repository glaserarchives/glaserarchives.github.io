Asian Journal on SVA Student and Filmmaker Ida Anita Del Mundo
Alumni Scholarship recipient and MPS Directing student Ida Anita Del Mundo discusses her newest project, Never Forget, a short film that tells a deeply emotional story about family, history, and how Filipino American culture shapes the identity of the global Filipino community. Via Asian Journal.

12:00pm | The Public Seminars: Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung–No Make Erreur
MA Curatorial Practice presents Bonaventure Soh Bejeng Ndikung, current director of Savvy Contemporary in Berlin and future director of Haus der Kulturen der Welt, to discuss “No Make Erreur,” a 1986 pop song that offered biting criticism of the capitalist urge to exploit and appropriate. Today, 12:00pm ET, online. Register here.

6:30pm | The Imagination Business: New Careers in Animation
Center for Communication presents a panel of animators who bring ideas to life in new and surprising ways as they continually reimagine the possibilities of their profession. Featuring Dominick Bedasse (MFA 2019 Computer Arts), from Titmouse; Frank Lee (MFA 2005 Computer Art), from Gartner; Amanda Sparso (BFA 2018 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects); from Psyop; and faculty member Cat Gulácsy, from Calling All Talent. Free and open to the public. Reserve tickets here. Tonight, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

From Past to Present: The Work of Audre Lorde
Lincoln Center's poet-in-residence Mahogany L. Browne (Black Girl Magic) and poet Cheryl Boyce-Taylor (Mama Phife Represents) join archivist and memory worker Zakiya Collier to discuss selections from Sister Outsider, collected essays from the iconic activist, author and poet Audre Lorde. Free. Tonight, 7:30pm. David Rubenstein Atrium at Lincoln Center, 61 West 62nd Street.

Contemplations
440 Gallery presents “Contemplations,” a group show by gallery artists Leigh Blanchard, Karen Gibbons (MPS 2005 Art Therapy) and David Stock. The show features a collection of mixed-media paintings, photography and digital work, centered around contemplation. Through Thursday, April 24. 440 Gallery, 440 Sixth Avenue, Brooklyn.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Visual Opinion Submission Deadline Tomorrow
Visual Opinion is accepting submissions for its annual “Graduation” issue, highlighting the work of graduating SVA students. Both undergraduate and graduate students are welcome to submit work by tomorrow, April 7, 5:00pm ET. Contributors must be matriculated students, must be located in the U.S. and must be graduating in the 2021 – 2022 academic year. Please send submissions to vo@sva.edu with the subject-line format “firstname_lastname_title.” Visit the Visual Opinion Instagram, @VisualOpinion, for more information.

Next Week | Children of the First Uprising: Strategies for Change and Land Reclamation in Ecuador
SVA Humanities and Sciences faculty member Quito Ziegler, and their class, Advanced Studies in Collective Action, in collaboration with the Indigenous Student Union, present indigenous land defender Emilio Paulo Chong. Chong will speak on the history of his people, the Wankavilka, an indigenous community that was never conquered by the Incas and is one of the oldest civilizations in South America, as he shares the ways they have regained ancestral territories from the Ecuadorian government, companies and descendents of colonial landowners. Monday, April 11, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.
