SVA Today
Thursday, April 27, 2017

"Sexual Fragments Absent"

A Q&A with MA Curatorial Practice student Ikechukwu Casmir Onyeruenyi about his thesis project, which features the work of three artists who untangle the aesthetic, affective and socio-political trappings of BDSM using performance, video and self-portraiture. The exhibition will take place tonight, 7:00 – midnight, at Paddles, 250 West 26th Street. RSVP to ionyewuenyi@sva.edu.
EVENTS

Take Our Daughters and Sons to Work Day

SVA Human Resources presents Take Our Daughters and Sons to Work Day, open to children ages 5 – 18. Activities throughout the day include a film and animation workshop, an art-for-kids workshop, lunch provided by Schnippers, "SVA BINGO" and a campus tour.

5:00pm | MFA Fine Arts Open Studios

MFA Fine Arts presents this one-night-only event where you can come and see what the students in the program are making, doing and thinking about. 5:00 – 9:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.
PRESS PICK

"Unsung NYC"

The New York Times on a new virtual reality project by MFA Interaction Design student David Al-Ibrahim that takes participants into an immersive soundscape of 1600s Mannhattan.
NYC PICKS

Quest Screening and Q&A

A screening of the documentary Quest, which chronicles an African-American family’s joys and struggles for eight years beginning at the start of the Obama presidency. A Q&A with the director, the producer and the family will follow. Tickets $7 – $14; purchase here. 7:00pm. Brooklyn Academy of Music, Peter Jay Sharp Building, 30 Lafayette Avenue, Brooklyn.

"Activist New York"

An ongoing exhibition focusing on the history of social activism in New York City from the 17th century through today. Admission prices vary. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Chelsea Pegram Talk and Reception

The Visible Futures Lab presents a talk and reception with current artist-in-residence Chelsea Pegram, an artist who uses sculpture, installation and printed media to explore the subject of transformation as it reflects shifting notions of agency, intention and embodiment. Tomorrow, April 28, 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

RisoLAB Print Slam Exhibit and Sale

MFA Visual Narrative presents a sale of student prints, books, zines and Riso experiments, along with a pop-up exhibition of Riso work from four continuing education and undergraduate classes. All proceeds go to the artists. Attendees can also see demos of the Risograph machines and learn about the RisoLAB summer classes. Monday, May 1, 6:00pm. SVA RisoLAB, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.

New Trial: Chronicle of Higher Education

SVA Library has begun a site license trial with the Chronicle of Higher Education, a top source of news and information for college and university faculty members and administrators. The Chronicle's website features the complete contents of the latest issue, daily news and advice columns, thousands of current job listings, previously published content, discussion forums, and career-building tools such as online CV management, salary databases and more. The trial can be accessed from the library's A-Z Databases page and will be active until Thursday, May 25.
