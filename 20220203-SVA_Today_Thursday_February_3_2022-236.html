Events

The Way We See Us
An exhibition of multidisciplinary works by SVA students in honor of Black History Month, co-curated by Angelica Dalzon (BFA Illustration) and Kyra Husbands (BFA Visual & Critical Studies). Through Monday, February 21. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour today for a one-hour session with Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross. She will be available on Zoom to field questions about job searching, interviewing and other career-related topics. Join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join. Today, 10:00 – 11:00am ET.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm ET, SVA's International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to "International Student Office Staff" headline). Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu if you have any questions.

6:00pm | Stand Up Against Street Harassment
Presented by SVA Student Affairs, Hollaback is offering a free one-hour interactive training session to educate people on how to safely intervene when witnessing street harassment, or experiencing it themselves. During this session, you’ll learn a clear, adaptable and expert-approved set of tools that have been proven to reduce the prevalence of street harassment: Hollaback’s “5 D’s of bystander intervention.” Tonight, 6:00 – 7:00pm ET, online. Register here.

7:00pm | The Day May Break
Join photographer Nick Brandt for a discussion about his long-term projects including his latest, “The Day May Break,” an introduction to a global series of projects portraying people and animals impacted by environmental destruction. The talk will be followed by a Q&A. Presented by SVA Continuing Education. Tonight, 7:00 – 8:30pm ET, online. RSVP here.

8:00pm | MFA Visual Narrative Application Q&A
The MFA Visual Narrative program empowers the next generation of storytellers with the skills and expertise to change the world through story. If you are interested in applying, drop in to chat with department chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay), who will be available to answer any questions about the program and the application process. Applications are due Friday, February 11, for second-round reviews, with acceptances continuing on a rolling basis for a June 2022 start as space remains available. Tonight, 8:00 – 9:00pm ET, online. Register here.
