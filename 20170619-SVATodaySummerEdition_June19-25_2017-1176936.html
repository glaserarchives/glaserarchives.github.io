THIS WEEK

Monday: OutCinema 2017 Pride Party

SVA Theatre, NewFest and NYC Pride present the New York City premiere of the short film Cherry Pop, followed by a Q&A with the film's stars and director Assaad Yacoub, and a post-screening party. Tickets $30 – $60; purchase here. Monday, June 19, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tuesday: Martha Rosler

MFA Art Practice presents a lecture by artist Martha Rosler. Tuesday, June 20, 12:30 – 2:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Wednesday: Jonathan F.P. Rose

MFA Art Practice presents a talk by Jonathan F.P. Rose, whose business, public policy and not-for-profit work all focus on creating more environmentally, socially and economically resilient cities. Wednesday, June 21, 6:00 – 7:30pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Wednesday: MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Thesis Exhibition Reception

An exhibition of work by members of the MFA Photography, Video and Related Media class of 2017, curated by faculty member Bonnie Yochelson. Through Saturday, July 8. Reception: Wednesday, June 21, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Thursday: All-Staff Art Show

The fifth annual All-Staff Art Show is an exhibition of works in various media by SVA staff members. Thursday, June 22 – Thursday, June 29. Reception: Thursday, June 22, 5:30 –8:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Thursday: MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Thesis Screening

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a screening of thesis projects by members of its class of 2017. Thursday, June 22, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Friday: Yoga and Meditation for Creativity and Wellness

Student Health and Counseling Services presents this workshop on basic yoga and meditation skills to promote well-being and stress reduction. Participants will receive free passes to Laughing Lotus Yoga Studio. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. RSVP to Navah Steiner at nsteiner@sva.edu. Friday, June 23, 2:00 – 3:30pm. Art Therapy Studio Space, 132 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

MAT Art Education Exhibition

MAT Art Education presents "Re-treat, Re-born, Re-venge, Re-position, Re-charge, Re-peat," an exhibition of thesis projects by its class of 2017, curated by department chair Rose Viggiano. Saturday, June 24 - Saturday, July 8. Reception: Friday, July 7, 4:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Portfolio Prize 2017

Aperture chooses Natalie Krick (BFA 2008 Photography) as the winner of this year’s Aperture Portfolio Prize, which "highlights artists whose work deserves greater recognition."

Toxic Air in Steam Valley

Vice premieres a documentary by Adam Banicki (MFA 2012 Social Documentary Film) that investigates the effects of the compromised air in an industrial Pennsylvania town.

MPS Branding Thesis: Rebranding the American Dream
