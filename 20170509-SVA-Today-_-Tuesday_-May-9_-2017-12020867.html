Commencement 2017

Writer, activist and feminist leader Gloria Steinem will deliver the keynote address at SVA’s 41st annual commencement exercises, and President David Rhodes will confer degrees on some 1,170 undergraduate and graduate degree candidates. The event is not open to the public, but will stream live at sva.edu/commencement. 1:00pm. Radio City Music Hall, 1260 Avenue of the Americas.
EVENT

6:15pm | The Drowning Screening and Director Q&A

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue invites you to join director Bette Gordon for a screening of her film The Drowning, a psychological thriller based on Pat Barker's novel Border Crossing, starring Julia Stiles and Josh Charles. A Q&A with Gordon will follow the film. Free with SVA ID (two guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). 6:15pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Pickle-Patterned Pillows, Plates

Poppytalk previews IKEA's forthcoming STUNSIG collection of home goods and accessories, which includes products featuring designs by alumnus Malcolm Stuart (BFA 2004 Fine Arts).
NYC PICKS

Talking Privilege

A conversation between comedians Hari Kondabolu and Jordan Carlos about race, gender, social class and privilege. Tickets $5 – $10; purchase here. 7:00pm, Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.

"Where We Are"

An ongoing exhibition that focuses on works from the Whitney’s collection made between 1900 – 1960 and proposes a framework of everyday relationships, institutions and activities that form an individual's sense of self. Free with SVA ID. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
TAKE NOTE

MFA Visual Narrative Summer Continuing Education Info Session

Spend an evening with MFA Visual Narrative faculty, see their and their students' work, and find out more about visual storytelling, self-publishing, Risograph prints, and how to match content with format. Thursday, May 11, 6:30 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.

King Arthur: Legend of the Sword Screening

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of the film, starring Charlie Hunnam and Jude Law and directed by Guy Ritchie. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Tuesday, May 16, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
