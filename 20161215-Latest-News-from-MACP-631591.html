Dear Friend of MACP,
We’re in the last days of our first term, and we’re excited to host tonight at 6:30 the next “Local Address” event. The Local Address series focuses on the cultural and political urgencies touching on institutions around the world. For each event, we bring to New York the leader of an institution so that we hear firsthand what is happening in specific international situations. We’re thrilled tonight to have José Roca, founder and director of FLORA ars + natura in Bogotá, Colombia, speaking with Gabriela Rangel, director of visual arts and chief curator of the Americas Society, and Ana Sokoloff, a leading art advisor from Bogotá, now based in New York.
In our Curatorial Roundtable, we had a wonderful presentation by Matthew Lyons, a longtime curator at The Kitchen, one of New York’s leading interdisciplinary cultural institutions, who spoke to us about the challenges and satisfactions of curating that spans visual art, performance, music, and dance.
Tim Griffin, the director and chief curator of The Kitchen, is on our faculty, and Tim’s news is that he’s a contributor to the just-announced online keywords anthology for contemporary cultural practice, In Terms of Performance, underwritten by The Pew Center for Arts & Heritage and the Arts Research Center at the University of California, Berkeley.
Last for this quick note, I’m delighted to say that Dr. Sarah Demeuse, writer and curator, and co-founder of the adventurous cultural practice called Rivet, has joined our faculty. Sarah will oversee the exhibition practicum, and we are excited and honored to have her with us!
We are now accepting applications for the September 2017 incoming class. See more about the program here. Apply here.

More soon,
Steven Henry Madoff
Chair, MA Curatorial Practice
