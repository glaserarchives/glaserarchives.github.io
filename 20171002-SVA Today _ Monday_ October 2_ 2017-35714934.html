SVA Today
Monday, October 2, 2017

My First Year: Shuyue Zhang

Second-year BFA Design student Shuyue Zhang talks about everything from the best feedback she's gotten in class to her biggest challenge in college so far.
EVENTS

7:00pm | An Evening with Christoph Niemann

In conjunction with his exhibition at the SVA Chelsea Gallery, “The Masters Series: Christoph Niemann,” Niemann will share his musings, insights and creative process during this talk. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Andrianna Campbell

BFA Fine Arts presents a talk by art historian and writer Andrianna Campbell, who will speak about modern and contemporary American art. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 335 West 16th Street, ground floor.

8:00pm | A Very Sordid Wedding Premiere

SVA Theatre and NewFest present the New York City premiere of the new film A Very Sordid Wedding, starring Caroline Rhea and Leslie Jordan and directed by Del Shores. Cast and crew will be present, and all guests are invited to a party in the lobby following the event. SVA ID holders (plus one guest) can request free tickets at the venue beginning at 7:00pm. Screening begins at 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Motivation and Storytelling

On Shopify Masters, Joey Cofone (BFA 2013 Design) and Adam Kornfield, founders of Baron Fig, discuss the importance of motivation and storytelling.
NYC PICK

"Exquisite Plants"

An interactive installation that explores shape-building and abstraction through play, by artists Adam Frezza and Terri Chiao. Through Thursday, November 2. Brooklyn Bridge Park, 99 Plymouth Street, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE

So You Want to Be an Activist?

Come meet local community leaders from Dominicanos USA (DUSA) and learn about the importance of being civically engaged and how to make change happen. Voter registration forms will be available. Open to SVA students. Light refreshments and food will be served. Thursday October 5, 6:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

MFA Products of Design Open House

Attendees will meet faculty, alumni and current students, see projects, learn about the program, tour the studio, classroom spaces and Visible Futures Lab, and ask questions about the program. Refreshments will be served. RSVP here. Friday, November 10, 2:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 7th floor.
