World Contraception Day

Today is World Contraception Day, a global campaign to raise awareness of birth control and advocate for better education around sexual and reproductive health. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services has partnered with Favor to expand access to reliable, accessible and affordable contraceptive care. SVA students can go here to sign up for birth control that is free with insurance, or as low as $7 if you are paying cash. When you sign up with Favor, you can transfer your current birth-control prescription to Favor and start receiving refills by mail, or have your doctor write your next prescription directly to Favor.


Today on Instagram @svastudenthealth, join Favor clinician Erin Flynn, NP, and SVA Nurse Abby Wohl, BSN, RN, who will answer all of your questions about birth control. And see today’s “Ask the Nurse” column for one of the most common birth-control questions Student Health and Counseling Services hears.


Have a question about reproductive health and sexual hygiene we didn’t answer? Email health@sva.edu.

SVA’s Nurse Abby continues to answer your questions regarding COVID-19 and other health-related concerns. Read below for the latest Q&A and use this link to see the question archive. You can also submit any questions you have here.


I’ve heard of continuous birth control (i.e., regularly taking a pill). Can I really skip my period completely? Is that safe? What about heavy bleeding /spotting/side effects?


Favor’s blog post addresses this. Skipping or delaying your period is not harmful. Some doctors even see skipping periods as beneficial, allowing people to avoid pain or the potential cost of a period. Other research has shown that skipping multiple periods using birth control can actually limit your likelihood to develop certain cancers, like ovarian cancer.
