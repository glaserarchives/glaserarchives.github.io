Subject: SVA Announcement: New Executive Director Appointed
From: SVA Info
Date: 5/6/22, 10:41 AM
To: allstaff@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu

Good morning,


It is my pleasure to announce that Angie Wojak has been promoted to executive director of External Relations, effective June 1.

 

Angie has over 25 years of leadership experience in higher education, having directed career services for SVA, Columbia University and Parsons School of Design. She has served as SVA Career Development director for the past 10 years, supervising the internship-for-credit program, career counseling services and recruiting events. Angie has also focused on creating collaborations and cultivating relationships with industry professionals and inspiring cultural figures in the arts.

 

As an alumnus of SVA (BFA 1990 Media Arts, with a focus on illustration), Angie has a deep connection to the institution and is a passionate advocate for students, alumni and the College. She holds an MFA in Fine Arts from the University of Missouri where she was honored with a Distinguished Alumnus Award.

 

She is the author of two books on art and design careers. The third edition of her book, Starting Your Career as an Artist, will be published in November 2022 and will feature a new chapter on how artists can build resilience during times of crisis and change.

 

She has lectured at universities and conferences around the world, including the Annecy International Animated Film Festival, Cannes Lions, Comic Con International, Harvard University, Kyoto University of Art and Design and the New York Film Festival. She serves on the international advisory board for the VIEW conference, a computer graphics and VFX event held in Turin, Italy.

 

Angie has created thought-provoking, public conversations between high-profile creatives, faculty and alumni. For example, she moderated a conversation between Gloria Steinem and artist Barbara Nessim about their careers and life-long friendship, and she recently led a panel on climate change and storytelling featuring Oscar-nominated animators, game designers and a NASA climate systems scientist.

 

Angie has also collaborated with her colleagues to co-produce the SVA Destinations class in San Francisco and SVA Premieres in LA, a weeklong event that showcases recent graduates’ work in animation and film while helping these emerging artists build their professional networks.


Please join me in congratulating Angie on this significant advancement at SVA.



Anthony P. Rhodes

Executive Vice President
