News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Monday, August 21 - Sunday, August 27, 2017

SVA at SIGGRAPH

SVA department chairs and staff spent a week in LA at this year’s SIGGRAPH, the world’s largest annual conference and exhibition of computer graphics and interactive techniques, where many alumni showcased their animation, computer art, VFX and VR work.

Student Wins Big at TDC Awards

At this year's Type Directors Club awards show, BFA Advertising student Jose Neves won the top student award as well as overall Best in Show from a pool of over 1,800 professional and student entries.

PRESS PICKS

Total Solar Eclipse

To mark today's solar eclipse, MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox created a 360-degree illustration for The Washington Post that examines the history of solar eclipse myths.

MPS Fashion Photography's Got "The Look"

Three recent MPS Fashion Photography alumni won different prizes in PDN's The Look contest: Nima Chaichi was the winner in the editorial category, Lena Davis won honors in the beauty category and Ricardo Rivera received the grand prize in the advertising category.

Unconventional Advertising

Free Range interviews BFA Advertising faculty member Frank Anselmo about the Unconventional Advertising class that he founded and teaches at SVA and the many awards his students have won.
NYC PICKS

Books Beneath the Bridge

An outdoor event featuring a reading by author Jill Eisenstadt from her new "darkly funny" novel Swell, followed by a Q&A and book signing. Monday, August 21, 7:00pm. Brooklyn Bridge Park, Pier 1, The Granite Prospect.

How to Get Personal in Print

Author and instructor Nancy Davidoff Kelton leads this workshop where she will give writing exercises and techniques to help you tap into your experiences. Admission and a copy of Kelton's book is $40; purchase here. Wednesday, August 23, 6:30pm. Strand Books, 828 Broadway.

Hot Jazz Moonlight Social

An outdoor performance by the Gotham Kings, a New York City-based jazz band, inspired by the current exhibition "Posters & Patriotism: Selling World War I in New York." Tickets $20 – $25 (includes museum admission); purchase here. Thursday, August 24, 7:00pm. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
