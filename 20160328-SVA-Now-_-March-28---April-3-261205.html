SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts. Week of March 28 - April 3, 2016
Features
Artist Carrie Mae Weems to Speak at Commencement
SVA is pleased to announce visionary photographer and MFA Art Practice faculty member Carrie Mae Weems as this year's commencement speaker.
Photographing Cuba's Transition
Following President Obama's historic trip to Cuba, SVA Close Up highlights the recent SVA Destinations trip to Havana, where MPS Digital Photography Chair Katrin Eismann and faculty member and alumnus Jaime Permuth led a weeklong exploration of the country's culture and heritage.
Spring Exhibitions Roundup
SVA Close Up showcases exhibitions by SVA faculty members and an alumnus, currently on view in New York City, that explore everything from the diversity of America to geometric abstraction to the fine line between love and hate.
SVA Students Win Big at Interaction 16
Earlier this month, MFA Interaction Design students Kohzy Koh and Song Lee won the international student design challenge for their project WristGuard at the 2016 Interaction Conference, held in Helsinki, Finland.
EVENTS
Richard Tuschman
MPS Digital Photography presents photographer Richard Tuschman, best known for his “Hopper Meditations" series, discussing his practice and his current project, Once Upon A Time in Kazimierz, an open-ended novella told in photographs. Tuesday, March 29, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
No Sellouts
An MFA Fine Arts panel discussion featuring recent alumni speaking about their art-related businesses. Moderated by department chair Mark Tribe, panelists include Leah Dixon (2014), co-owner of Beverly's, a New York City bar and art space; Mike Ursuta (2007), co-founder of Ramiken Crucible gallery; and Brian Whiteley (2013), creative director of Satellite Art Show. Tuesday, March 29, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Benjamin Prosky: Designing Culture
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Benjamin Prosky, Architizer co-founder, the challenges of presenting visually compelling architecture and design in contemporary design publications. Tuesday, March 29, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Dana Schutz
BFA Fine Arts presents artist Dana Schutz, best known for her wildly expressive figures, discussing her work. Thursday, March 31, 7:00pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
IN THE PRESS
The Next Pixar?
MoviePilot hails Taking the Plunge, the thesis project by 2015 BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni Thaddaeus Andreades, Elizabeth Ku-Herrero, Nicholas Manfredi and Marie Raoult, for its Pixar-like quality. Click through to watch the film.
13 Years in Chinatown
The New York Times' Lens Blog on a project by Thomas Holton (MFA 2005 Photography, Video and Related Media) documenting a family in New York's Chinatown over a 13-year period.
The SVA Project in New York
The Cut features BFA Photography and Video students' photographs of spring fashions and accessories for the latest installment of New York's "Out of the Box" series.
Mixing Jewelry with Fine Art
The New York Times' T magazine visits the Los Angeles home of jeweler and designer Kathryn Bentley (BFA 2000 Fine Arts) for a look at the furnishings and art that inspire her.
