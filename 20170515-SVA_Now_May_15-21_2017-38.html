SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
May 15 - 21, 2017

"We Are Woke!"

A recap of the 2017 Commencement exercises, where 1,170 students gathered at Radio City Music Hall to receive their diplomas and hear from keynote speaker Gloria Steinem.

Dusty Award Winners

The winners of the 28th Dusty Film & Animation Festival Awards have been announced!

SVA at Watch Week

SVA partnered with the Madison Avenue BID for a public art exhibition titled "A Moment in Time." Under the mentorship and supervision of 3D Design chair Kevin O'Callaghan, students created monumental six-foot tall, watch-themed sculptures commemorating a specific moment.

Staying Connected with SVA

In this article from the spring 2017 Visual Arts Journal, Director of Alumni Affairs and Development Jane Nuzzo shares the joys and benefits of staying connected with SVA after graduation.

Next Stop: AIGA Philadelphia

"Underground Images," SVA’s traveling exhibition of its iconic, faculty-designed subway posters, is being hosted by the Philadelphia chapter of AIGA. The exhibition will be on view through June 30, at Bluecadet, 1526 Frankford Avenue, Philadelphia.

3D Animated Documentary on "Perceptions of Time"

MFA Computer Art student and 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award recipient Ninaad Kulkarni talks about his thesis project, KCLOC.
IN THE PRESS

"The Best of All Revolutions"

Harper's Bazaar on the best quotes from Gloria Steinem's speech at SVA's 2017 Commencement Exercises.

The Heart of Steven Universe

Slate praises Rebecca Sugar's (BFA 2009 Animation) animated show Steven Universe and discusses why it is so appealing to such a variety of demographics.

"Audacious . . . and Rewarding"

Vice's Broadly reviews "Sexual Fragments Absent," a one-night-only show organized by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Ikechukwu Casmir Onyewuenyi and staged late last month in Manhattan.

Pickle-Patterned Pillows

Poppytalk previews IKEA's forthcoming STUNSIG collection of home goods and accessories, which includes products featuring designs by alumnus Malcolm Stuart (BFA 2004 Fine Arts).

Ashly Leonard Stohl and David Carol

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by Ashly Leonard Stohl and David Carol, creators of Peanut Press, a new imprint dedicated to publishing quality photography books, as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tuesday, May 16, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"Yuk Yuk, Boo Hoo" Screening

The Project Class of 2017, an undergraduate honors class of the BFA Advertising and BFA Design programs, takes you to the movies with nine short films in this end-of-semester screening. RSVP here. Thursday, May 18, 6:00 – 9:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
