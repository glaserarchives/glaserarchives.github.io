SVA Destinations in Barbuda

Learn about the culture of Barbuda and be inspired by its natural beauty in a two-week creative program led by photographer Mohammid Walbrook (BFA 2016 Photography and Video).

EVENTS

10:00am | Yoga for Artists: Anxiety and Muscle Tension Relief

Take a break with SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and yoga instructor Melissa Boo. Learn yoga poses that will help reduce anxiety and provide tension relief. Rest and restore in a free, beginner-friendly, virtual yoga class. Open to the entire SVA community—students, staff and faculty. RSVP required; register here. Today, March 24, 10:00 – 10:45am, online.

1:30pm | Gartic Phone Drawing Game

Show off your artistic skills and enjoy delicious donuts with VASA while playing Gartic Phone, the game of telephone—with drawing! Students are required to bring an iPad/tablet and smart pen. Today, March 24, 1:30 – 3:30pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:00pm | Community Lecture Series: Fields Within the Field

MPS Art Therapy presents a virtual presentation by Michael Franklin, Ph.D., ATR-BC, on the intersections between the studio model and social justice art therapy, specifically nonclinical methods and interventions for people in need of communal expression. Tonight, March 24, 6:00 – 7:30pm, online.

7:00pm | ISO Gallery Turkish Heritage Opening Reception and Turkey/Syria Earthquake Relief

Join the International Student Office’s opening reception for their exhibition of work celebrating Turkish heritage. Check out the work of SVA students, enjoy Turkish delights and tea and support the victims of the recent tragedy in Turkey and Syria. Organized by ISO and Turkish SVA students. Open only to the SVA community. RSVP is required; register here. Tonight, March 24, 7:00pm. 340 East 24th Street, ground floor.

Tomorrow | SWAN Day 2023

SVA Theatre and BFA Film present the 15th annual Support Women Artists Now (SWAN) Day with a series of short films followed by a panel featuring director Annetta Marion, producer Dana Offenbach and Academy Award-winning actress Estelle Parsons. Free and open to the public with RSVP; register here. Saturday, March 25, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

NYC PICKS

Octogenarian Women of Jazz

Saxophonist/flutist Carol Sudhalter celebrates her 80th year by leading a quintet of “Octogenarian Women of Jazz” in a concert of beloved jazz standards and originals by members. $12 for students. Tonight, March 24, 8:00pm. Flushing Town Hall Theater, 137-35 Northern Boulevard.

New Irish Fiction

A day-long symposium to discuss the way forward for Irish fiction in a time of migration, right-wing populism and increasing demands for gender, racial, economic and climate justice. Free. Saturday, March 25, 10:30am – 5:00pm. Joseph D. Jamail Lecture Hall, Pulitzer Hall Columbia University, 2950 Broadway.

That Slapstick Show: Whatever Happened to Wanda Wiley?

A screening of silent cinema rarities from the 1920s and ’30s featuring a live musical accompaniment by Queens-based ragtime pianist Charlie Judkins, hosted by Nelson Hughes and SVA faculty member Tommy José Stathes. Tickets are $10. Sunday, March 26, 6:00pm. Q.E.D., 27-16 23rd Avenue, Queens.

“Kara Walker: Harper’s Pictorial History of the Civil War (Annotated)”

An exhibition of work by artist Kara Walker focused on the omission of African Americans from Harper’s Pictorial History of the Civil War, a two-volume anthology published in 1866 and 1868. $13 for students. Through Sunday, June 11. New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West.
