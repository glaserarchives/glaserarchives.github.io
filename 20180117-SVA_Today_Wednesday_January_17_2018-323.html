SVA Today
Wednesday, January 17, 2018

Artist Erwin Redl on Whiteout

The MFA 1995 Computer Art alumnus talks about his site-specific installation, now on view at Madison Square Park.
EVENTS

6:00pm | VASA Club Fair

All students are welcome to stop by to learn about all of the clubs offered at SVA, meet new people and discover ways to get involved on campus. 6:00 - 8:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. For more information, email clubs@sva.edu.

6:00pm | Visible Futures Lab Artist-in-Residence Jonathan Sims

The lab's latest artist-in-residence talks about his practice and his plans for the seven-week residency. The talk will also be broadcast live on the Visible Futures Lab's Facebook page. 6:00 - 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

7:00pm | Comparing Notes on the RisoLAB Residency

MFA Visual Narrative hosts a presentation and discussion featuring its latest RisoLAB artists-in-residence, Alix Pentecost Farren and Rachel Shim. 7:00 - 9:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

7:30pm | "Mattia Casalegno: The Knowledge of the Body" Reception

An exhibition featuring the work of multimedia artist Mattia Casalegno, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow John Elammar. Reception tonight, 7:30 - 9:30pm. Exhibition on view through Wednesday, January 24. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Visible Futures Lab Equipment Demonstrations

SVA's Visible Futures Lab is hosting a series of hands-on demonstrations illustrating the power and use of many of its digital fabrication tools, including laser cutters, 3D printers, vinyl cutters, CNC machines, and more. Through Sunday, January 21. Day, evening and weekend times are available. Those interested must sign up here.
PRESS PICK

Cannaday Chapman on His MLK Day Google Sketch

Illustrator Cannaday Chapman (BFA 2008 Illustration) talks about his Google Sketch, which appeared on the tech giant's website yesterday in celebration of Martin Luther King Jr. Day.
NYC PICKS

Adam Gopnik on A.J. Liebling's Bon Vivant!

New Yorker writer Adam Gopnik discusses A.J. Liebling’s memoir, Bon Vivant! Between Meals: An Appetite for Paris, and recounts its influence on his own work. Free. 7:00pm. Albertine, 972 Fifth Avenue.

Ahmed Mater: Mecca Journeys

An exhibition of the artist's photographs documenting the rapid redevelopment of the city of Mecca, which welcomes millions of Muslim pilgrims every year. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, April 8. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
