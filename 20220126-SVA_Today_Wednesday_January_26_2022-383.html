Community Fridge Reopens
The SVA community fridge is now open for the spring semester! The fridge is located in the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, and will offer fruits, vegetables, eggs and bread; boxed salads, sandwiches and wraps donated by Trader Joe’s; and nonperishables such as rice, pasta and granola bars. The fridge policy is, “Take what you need; leave what you can.” Student Center hours are Monday – Friday, 10:00am – 10:00pm, and Saturday and Sunday, 12:00 – 6:00pm.

With a credit or debit card, you can donate any amount from $1 up to help fund the fridge. Click here to contribute and write “Community fridge” in the comment box to ensure that your donation is directed to the project.

Friday | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Members of the Asian and Asian American communities continue to face harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Faculty and staff who self-identify as AAPI are invited to join this virtual forum to connect on these or other topics for peer support. New members are welcome. If you are interested in participating, please fill out this Google Form to register. If you are unable to participate, you can still fill out the Google Form to indicate your interest in future opportunities and be included in future correspondence. Friday, January 28, 11:00am – 12:30pm ET, on Zoom. Zoom information will follow.

Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work from the SVA community that reflect on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.'s legacy and life's work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA's observance of Black History Month in February. For more information or to submit your work, click here.
