SVA Today
Friday, April 1, and the Weekend
SVA Bonanza in Print
Of the 56 "inspiring designers" featured in the spring issue of Print, nearly half of them are members of the extended SVA community. Read more about these superstar designers and their work.
Last Chance: "BFA Advertising and BFA Design Portfolio"
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition of student work, featuring ads, campaigns, designs, motion graphics and 3D designs. Closes today, Art Directors Club, 106 West 29th Street.
Last Chance: "Mentors"
BFA Photography and Video presents an exhibition of student work inspired by mentorships with leading arts figures. Closes today, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Last Chance: "What Are You Trying to Say?"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that focuses on image and language production and comments on mass consumer culture and channels of information. Closes today, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
1:00pm: Relaxation Techniques
Student Health and Counseling Services share relaxation techniques for relieving stress. Open to SVA students. SVA Student Center 217 East 23rd Street.
4:30pm: Seeds of Peace Fundraiser
The International Student Office presents a fundraiser for Seeds of Peace, an organization dedicated to cultivating youth leadership in communities divided by conflict. The fundraiser includes sales of baked goods and original artwork by SVA students and staff. There will also be a raffle and a photobooth. 4:30 - 7:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
7:30pm: April Fools' Ball
VASA presents the April Fools' Ball, a combination masquerade and burlesque featuring performances by SVA students and professional performers. Tickets are $15. Open to SVA students (limit one non-SVA guest per student). SVA ID required for entry. 7:30 - 11:30pm, The Box 189, Chrystie Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Fighting Cancer to Design Again
CBS News on the inspiring story of Adalis Martinez (BFA 2013 Design), whose battle and recovery from colon cancer has renewed her hope of a future as a graphic designer. Her design for Christian Kracht's Imperium was named among the best book covers of 2015 by The New York Times.
NYC WEEKEND PICKS
Groundhog Day
A screening of the 1993 film, starring Bill Murray and Andie MacDowell, with a discussion to follow. Suggested admission of $5, includes snacks and beverages. Tonight, 7:00pm, New York Society for Ethical Culture, 2 West 64th Street, 4th floor.
MoCCA Arts Festival
A two-day multimedia festival celebrating independent comics, cartoons and animation, featuring 400 exhibiting artists, talks, workshops, and more. SVA faculty members Steve Brodner, Bill Kartalopoulos, Peter Kuper and Keith Mayerson, as well as alumni Bill Plympton and Rebecca Sugar will appear on panels. MFA Visual Narrative students will be a tables A120-21. Admission is $5 per day. Saturday, April 2, and Sunday, April 3, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan West, 639 West 46th Street, and Ink48 Hotel, 653 11th Avenue.
Smorgasburg
The seasonal, open-air food market returns, with 100 vendors at the Williamsburg and Prospect Park locations. Saturday, April 2, 11:00am - 6:00pm, East River Park, Brooklyn. Sunday, April 3, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Prospect Park, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE
Aaron Shurin: The Skin of Meaning
MFA Art Writing presents Aaron Shurin, poet and professor emeritus of the University of San Francisco, discussing his new book, The Skin of Meaning, a collection of essays fueled by gender and queer studies combined with radical traditions in poetry. Thursday, April 7, 6:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
SVA Student Survey
The Student Affairs Office and VASA welcome student feedback with their annual student survey. To access the survey, visit my.sva.edu and click the link for the "2015-16 Student Survey." The survey is open until Sunday, April 10, 11:59pm.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
April is National Poetry Month, and MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman designed this year's poster commemorating the month-long celebration of verse. Click through to request a free copy of the poster from the American Academy of Poets.
