SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, January 12, 2015
Image by Mary Huang.
Opening Today: "Data Collection"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by Visible Futures Lab artist-in-residence and Continuum Fashion founder Mary Huang. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Marie Katherine Vigneau, the work on view in the exhibition reflects Huang's fluency in 3D design, digital manufacturing and code to redefine the craft and experience of fashion. Through Wednesday, January 20. Reception Thursday, January 14, 7:00 - 9:00pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
1:00pm: The Big Short
SAG/AFTRA presents a screening of The Big Short. Following the screening, supporting actors John Magaro and Adepero Oduye will answer questions. Free with SVA ID (two guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Seating is first-come, first-served. Doors open approximately 30-45 minutes prior to showtime. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:00pm: Kara Rooney
MFA Fine Arts presents faculty member Kara Rooney (MFA 2008 Art Writing and Criticism) discussing her work in performance, sculpture and new media installation. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
7:00pm: Yunghi Kim
MPS Digital Photography presents photojournalist Yunghi Kim discussing her work, including coverage of stories in Rwanda, Kosovo, Iraq and Afghanistan. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Press pick of the day
Coping with Disease and Making Art
The Kansas City Star spoke with MFA Fine Arts alumnus Dylan Mortimer about his new exhibition, "Cure," and living with cystic fibrosis.
NYC pick of the day
Stratocaster Sessions
A musician plays a 1957 Fender Stratocaster, part of "Making Music Modern: Design for Ear and Eye." Admission is free with SVA ID. 12:30pm, MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street, 3rd floor.
Take Note
NYC Fire and Disaster Drill
As part of New York City's Community Emergency Response Team, a training exercise consisting of a controlled fire and disaster drill will be conducted at the Stein Senior Center on East 23rd Street today at 6:30pm. The drill location is directly across the street from SVA's 209 East 23rd Street location. Expect to see FDNY and NYPD personnel in action. To learn more, visit nyc.gov/cert.
Thank Heaven for Boys and Girls
School of Visual Arts presents “Thank Heaven for Boys and Girls,” an exhibition of work by BFA Visual & Critical Studies students. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice Director of Curatorial Research Jovana Stokic. Saturday, January 16, through Saturday, February 6. Reception Tuesday, January 19, 6:00 - 8:00pm, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
SVA in New Mexico Information Session
Humanities and Sciences presents an information session for this summer's two-week creative writing trip to Taos, New Mexico. Thursday, January 21, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 605C.
Words Call for Submissions
Words, SVA's literary publication, is now open for submissions. Students, faculty and staff can submit poetry, short stories, personal essays, short film scripts and one-act plays. Submissions should be addressed to SVA faculty member and Words editor Louis Phillips via email: lphillips@sva.edu or louisprofphillips@gmail.com. Please include the phrase "Submission to Words 83" in the subject line, and include a name, address, email and phone number on all submissions. RTF or Word documents are preferred, no PDFs or Adobe documents. The deadline for all submissions has been extended until Monday, January 25.
