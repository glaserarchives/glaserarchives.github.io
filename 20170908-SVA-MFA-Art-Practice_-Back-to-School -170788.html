ART PRACTICE: THE BACK TO SCHOOL EDITION 
Angela Conant (AP 2013), Self-Portrait, risograph 

Angela Conant (AP 2013) will be participating in several events this month. How to Draw The Human Figure, an exhibition with work by Conant, GaHee Park and Sophy Naess recently opened at AGENCY in DUMBO, Brooklyn. The show runs through September 28th.

Conant is also the co-curator, along with Grayson Cox, of IPCNY's biennial pop-up exhibition and fundraiser, Published by the Artist. Conant and Cox have invited a wide range of artists, as well as the Sunview Luncheonette (SVL) to participate in the exhibition. The SVL, as the organization-in-residence, will be providing their risograph printer, and inhabiting IPCNY's workshop space, which will function as a place where artists can print and edition RISO prints throughout the donation drop-off period. Conant and Cox explain their concept for the show: "Publishers of art often work alone or in small printmaking collaboratives. IPCNY’s Published by the Artist will congregate some of these individuals and collaboratives within the context of an art publishers’ hub. Assembling the works of artists, printers and publishers will build connections and command resources to sustain the print community as well as IPCNY itself." The show opens on September 22nd and runs through October 5th.  

Finally, Conant and Max Sherwood are the co-organizers of a video and film screening in Newburgh, NY. The screening will take place of September 30th as a part of Glenlily Grounds, a two-day annual sculpture and installation exhibition.

Monika Lin (AP 2017), From the Bones of the Fish at Central Booking 
 
Art Practice graduate Monika Lin (AP 2017) was recently profiled by Jacqueline Bishop in HuffPost. Lin describes her recent work, "In the past, our folk and fairy tales were for the common folk. It was only when they were transformed from oral tales into literary tales that they became something for the elites. When the elites became involved the oral tales developed moralistic elements and gender disparities and gender issues became more pronounced. For me, I want the wildness back, and I want the sensuality and the pleasure.”
Ben Quesnel (AP 2017), Unburdened Bare installed at Governors Island

Ben Quesnel (AP 2017) is among the artists included in this year's Governors Island Art Fair organized by 4heads. An article in Gothamist described Quesnel's work, Unburdened Bare, as one of the fair's highlights, calling it "an almost-spooky, almost-cute stuffed creature that's easily overlooked sitting down there at the bottom of some back stairs, but may startle you when you see it."
