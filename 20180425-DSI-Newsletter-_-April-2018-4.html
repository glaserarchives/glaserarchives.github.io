THE ALUM ISSUE
It’s the end of another school year, and for this newsletter, we’re focusing not on what goes on inside our program, but on what our graduates are doing out in the world. We have always defined success as more than the number of students who come to get their graduate degree here, but also on the ultimate metric of what our alums are doing out in the world. Are their careers exciting and fulfilling? Was the degree worth the effort? Did we prepare them not just to work, but to be the creative leaders of change? Did we help them clarify and apply their values for justice and equity to real issues in the world? Did we help them learn to adapt and pivot without losing sight of their vision? Did we teach them how to apply what they learned to vastly diverse situations? You can see for yourself below.

By the numbers, we’ve graduated 91 students so far, not counting those who will leave us in less than two weeks. They have come from 28 countries with different challenges, cultures and beliefs, and have melded together in cohorts of friends for life. If you are interested in becoming one of our graduates, the first step is to apply for Fall 2018.

Check out where they are now on our website.

CURRENT STUDENT NEWS
We hope you’ll join us at our annual thesis show May 1 from 1 p.m. to 5 p.m. in the SVA Theater. RSVP for a firsthand look at our students’ innovative projects: https://dsi.sva.edu/thesis-2018/.
Lu Rodrigues will intern at hiCOlab in Burlington, Vermont, this summer. She’ll be on a team of four, working to design for a nationally recognized academic medical center and regional health system. 
Ishita Jain will join the Autodesk Foundation team as a designer in San Francisco next month. She will design communications materials; conduct customer research, capturing and analyzing qualitative and quantitative data; and facilitate workshops in support of the Foundation's overall event and convening experience.
Amanda Finuccio and Yuka Fukuoka earned the SVA Alumni Scholarship to fund their thesis projects.
Qianshang Chen, Sandy Guberti-Ng and Hrudaya Veena Yanamandala competed in the Beyond Hackathon, where they were tasked with designing innovative digital experiences for Henry Street Settlement’s annual gift drive.
Yuka Fukuoka has been selected as a finalist in the Microsoft US Imagine Cup, a competition where student developers solve world challenges. She is helping people who stutter overcome their social anxiety with her thesis project: VRbal. She also recently participated in Verizon Connected Futures III, where she earned second place.
Hrudaya Veena Yanamandala was selected as a 2018 CUP public access fellow.
Lirong Yang has accepted a position at SAIC Motor in Shanghai. She will work there after graduation.
With the new Fast Track program, DSI students are taking their bold ideas into the world: Zongliang Shang and Qianshang Chen are using their thesis project to reduce food waste, and create a profit doing so. The two are using damaged and ugly fruit from local retailers to create water kefir — a delicious drink that’s healthy and sustainable. Zongliang and Qianshang are looking to produce their first batch of the drink in April, and will work to sell the product in Bushwick.
Catalina Cipri is creating a bot that provides access for job seekers to get the career counseling they need 24/7 — in English, Spanish and Arabic. She wants native speakers to feel a connection to the bot, so that they are able to get the support they need quickly and is working to make the bot’s voice feel natural and the conversations organic and friendly.
Taylor Sokolowski is working to encourage interfaith dialogue between Christian and Muslim women. This is important, because some American Christians have fears and misconceptions about Islam, but the majority haven’t even met a Muslim person. In April, Taylor will be testing a website that ultimately inspires offline conversations between Muslim and Christian women.
