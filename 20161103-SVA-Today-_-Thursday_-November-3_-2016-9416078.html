Tonight: See the Music

MFA Social Documentary Film and MFA Visual Narrative present an advance screening of an episode of Soundbreaking, PBS's new music documentary miniseries, followed by a panel featuring former MTV/VH1 executive Rob Barnett, series producer/director and MFA Social Documentary Film Chair Maro Chermayeff, series editor and SVA faculty member E. PRESS PICK
Tony Rosenthal's Alamo (1967), the cube-shaped sculpture in Manhattan's Astor Place, covered with yarn in 2011. Photo from Flickr, via The Guardian (UK).

Astor Place "Cube" Returns

This week, Tony Rosenthal's Alamo (1967), the landmark "cube" sculpture in Manhattan's Astor Place, was reinstalled after two years of renovations to the area. In The Guardian, MFA Art Practice Chair David Ross argues that the space might be better used for temporary installations.

2016 SVA Staff Survey

To help SVA better serve its employees, the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group and Human Resources have worked together to create the 2016 SVA Staff Survey. All staff are encouraged to complete it by Friday, November 11. The survey is voluntary and anonymous, and results will be kept confidential. Click the headline to access the form.

Judith Stein, Eye of the Sixties

MFA Art Writing presents Judith Stein discussing her book Eye of the Sixties, about art dealer Richard Bellamy, who helped launch the careers of such artists as Donald Judd, Claes Oldenburg and James Rosenquist. Thursday, November 10, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

Alan Kaufman, The Outlaw Bible of American Art

The writer will discuss and present images featured in the latest book in his Outlaw Anthology Series. A book signing and reception, with refreshments, will follow. Tuesday, November 15, 7:00 - 9:00pm, SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

Submit to Words 85

SVA students, faculty and staff are encouraged to submit their poetry, stories, essays, short film scripts and one-act plays for the next edition of Words, the College's literary journal. Submit via email to lphillips@sva.edu by January 5; all entries should include the writer's name, address, email and phone number.
