All Faculty Brief  

September 2022


Dear  Colleagues:

On behalf of everyone at SVA, please allow me to welcome you back for the Fall 2022 semester.  If you are new to SVA, we are delighted to have you as a member of our community.

On Tuesday, I sent each of you a rather detailed memo providing updates to SVA’s COVID-19 policies and protocols.  If you haven’t yet done so, please take some time to review the memo; it includes valuable information for returning and new members of the faculty.

As I noted in a welcome back email to all students, on which the faculty was copied, SVA will open with the mask requirement that was in place at the end of the spring semester and throughout the summer.  I realize wearing a mask while teaching is fraught with challenges.  The COVID response team, which continues to meet weekly, is developing a set of metrics that will determine when it is safe to relax our mask policy, and I will update you accordingly. 

In this welcome back edition of the All Faculty Brief, I’m excited to share many engaging and important updates from the Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion; the Office of Learning Technologies, which is launching a new online teaching and meeting platform called SVA Concourse; the SVA Libraries; the Office of the Registrar; and an invitation from the Office of Student Health and Counseling to all faculty to participate in Narcan training.  Those who participate in this training will receive a Narcan kit.

Finally, I am pleased to report that we are building back our student enrollment, after significant declines resulting from the pandemic.  This fall we have a total of 4,207 students registered:  3,585 undergrad and 612 graduate students.

I wish you all the best for a successful and productive fall semester.

Warm regards,
Chris

Four DEI Education Session Videos to Inspire Your Start to the Fall 2022 Semester

The DEI Office and leaders of the DEI Task Force held a series of Brown Bag DEI Education Sessions.  The themes that were covered were related to topics that emerged from the BIPOC Student Document, as well as topics that emerged from a series of DEI roundtable diversity discussions.  Our Brown Bag sessions are informal settings to discuss important subjects.  Our goal with these sessions is to spread awareness about these topics that adversely affect the holistic development of our students.  Our hope is that these discussions lead toward helping the Chairs and Faculty develop and implement more inclusive and equitable approaches towards learning and engagement.  Here is a list of the recorded sessions which can be accessed through SVA Media Space:


How the Environment Shapes the Experience, Roni K. White, MS, NCC, LCPC

The Asian Student Experience: Incorporating an Anti-biased Approach in Education, Adam Krumm

Translanguaging: Fostering Linguistically Diverse Art and Design Classrooms, Keren Moscovitch, Ph.D.

Ableism, Nicole Lorenzetti, Ph.D.
 
Jarvis Watson, Ed.D, director, Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion would like to recognize Gail Anderson and Catherine Rosamond for their vision and leadership associated with the development of the DEI Brown Bag Education Sessions.  Their collaborative pedagogical approach toward developing these sessions resulted in spreading awareness, and developing meaningful dialogue among our Academic Chairs.

El Museo del Barrio Virtual Tour: Estamos Bien - La Trienal 20/21

September 21, 2022 at 5:30 PM, online

Please join the education staff of El Museo del Barrio for a guided virtual tour of ESTAMOS BIEN – LA TRIENAL 20/21, featuring more than 40 artists from across the United States and Puerto Rico, representing a diversity of generations, genders, ethnic, and racial backgrounds. The exhibition utilizes an intersectional approach to Latinx identity. The phrase Estamos Bien (We Are Good) resonates with the present-day moment in the United States. With many of the works created by artists in the past two years, the exhibition offers students an opportunity to examine several of the current concerns of Latinx communities including class and racial dynamics, displacement and gentrification, climate change, and the impact of the global pandemic.

The tour is presented by the Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion and open to all students, faculty, staff and alumni. Due to the intimate nature of inquiry and exchange with El Museo’s educators, participation is limited to thirty-five people. Please RSVP here. The link will be shared with you on the day of the event.

Simple Syllabus and the New Syllabus Library
The Syllabus Library is now available to the SVA community through the Simple Syllabus app in MyID. The Syllabus Library enables you to view other course syllabi to support a more cohesive learning experience for students across different sections of a course. Making course syllabi transparent across the College also encourages improved alignment between upper and lower-level courses.

To access the Syllabus Library, go to myID.SVA.edu and click on the Simple Syllabus icon to open the app on your Dashboard. For more information about Simple Syllabus and the new Syllabus Library, visit this page in the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas.

Fall 2022 Updates and Information from the Office of

the Registrar

Faculty Contracts for the 2022-2023 Academic Year
In an effort to minimize contract revisions, please note that class meeting details (days, times, location, etc.) no longer appear on your contract. You may view all meeting details and monitor live enrollment in your classes by viewing your rosters in MyServices. 

It should be noted that in most cases an individual course must have at least 10 students enrolled in order for the course to appear on your contract. Fluctuations in enrollment can be expected during the official course adjustment periods when students are allowed to add and drop classes (September 6 - 13), so contract revisions may be unavoidable. Make contact with your academic department if you have any questions regarding your teaching schedule.

ID Cards
All SVA community members must have an active ID card to enter any campus building. If you need a new card, complete the SVA ID Card Request Form, which is available as a tile on your MyID dashboard. The Registrar's Office team will notify you by email and will arrange to have your card available for pick-up. Your ID card links to the Cleared4 self-attestation questionnaire that you must complete daily prior to entry into any SVA building.

Attendance Policy Reminder
Students who are marked absent for one-third of a course's total sessions will be administratively withdrawn from the course with a grade of "W". Individual faculty members may adopt an attendance policy that is more restrictive than the institution's policy, but students will not be officially withdrawn until the absence threshold in the policy is met. All faculty members should note attendance expectations and how absences may affect a student's final grade in their syllabi. The full attendance policy can be viewed in the most recent SVA Handbook.

Important Note on Registration for Fall 2022
Please note that individual faculty members cannot grant permission to students to register for a course that has reached its maximum capacity. Students may only be registered for open courses with available seats, with the permission of their academic advisors. Students are not allowed to attend any class for which they are not officially registered. You may monitor live enrollment in your classes by viewing your rosters in MyServices.

Chosen Names and Pronouns on Rosters
Students are able to officially declare the preferred/chosen names and pronouns by which they wish to be referred, and you will see this information reflected on your class rosters in MyServices and on your attendance rosters in Canvas. If a student informs you of their chosen name or pronouns and the information does not appear on your rosters, please take note of their wishes in your personal records and encourage them to officially declare their personal identity options in their user profile in MyServices. A student's chosen name and pronouns should be respected at all times.
