SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, September 25, 2015
Capturing Stories From Around the World
MFA Social Documentary Film presents a three day showcase of thesis projects. Films tackle issues such as the Chinese sex industry, religious ritual and ecological disaster. Tonight through Sunday, September 27, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
"Hi Rez / Lo Rez" Opens Tomorrow
BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition that brings together traditional forms with new digital processes. Curated by department chair Suzanne Anker. Saturday, September 26, through Saturday, October 17, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Three Free Films at SVA Theatre
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a free screening of He Named Me Malala (12:30pm), and the Visual Effects Society presents free screenings of Hotel Transylvania 2 (5:00pm) and Everest (8:00pm). An SVA ID is required for attendance (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Doors open approximately 30 - 45 minutes prior to showtime. Saturday, September 26, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Building Bridges Between Cultures
MPS Art Therapy students traveled to Auroville, India to experience and learn local culture and art forms.
NYC weekend picks
New York Film Festival
The 53rd annual festival includes premieres, retrospectives, revivals, free screenings and special events. Today through Sunday, October 11, various locations.
NYC Coffee Fest
Enjoy free coffee tastings, interactive workshops, demonstrations from world-class baristas, street food, coffee cocktails, live music, art and much more. Ticket prices vary. Saturday, September 26 and Sunday, September 27, 69th Regiment Armory, 68 Lexington Avenue.
Free Museum Day
Museums all over New York, including The Morgan Library & Museum, Jewish Museum and Asia Society and Museum, offer free admission in a national initiative sponsored by Smithsonian. Check list for more participants. Saturday, September 26.
A Taste of Gramercy
Sample the array of food available in Gramercy. Ticket prices vary. Saturday, September 26, 11:00am - 3:00pm, Irving Place between 17th and 18th Streets.
Atlantic Antic
Celebrate Boerum Hill, Brooklyn Heights, Cobble Hill and Downtown Brooklyn at New York's oldest and largest street festival. Sunday, September 27, 12:00 - 6:00pm, Atlantic Avenue, Brooklyn.
Take Note
Assembly Required: A Wandering Sukkah
A mobile, interactive sukkah experience to commemorate the Jewish holiday of Sukkot. Production assistance by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Patrick Jaojoco. September 27, 1:00 - 5:00pm, Brooklyn Invisible Dog Art Center, 51 Bergen St, Brooklyn.
Free Screening of "Freeheld" (2015)
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of Freeheld, starring Julianne Moore, Ellen Page, Michael Shannon and Steve Carell. A question-and-answer session with producer Michael Shamberg will follow. An SVA ID is required for attendance (one guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Tuesday, September 29, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Mindfulness: An Experiential Workshop
In this workshop, students learn practical mindfulness-based techniques that will help reduce the impact of negative thought, anxiety and stress, as well as identify patterns that may block creative energy. Open to SVA students. RSVP: nsteiner@sva.edu. Wednesday, September 30, 3:15 - 5:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 413F.
Fall 2015 Student Club Fair
Visual Arts Student Association (VASA) presents the annual club fair. Stop by and browse all the clubs and organizations available to students. Wednesday, September 30th, 6:30 - 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
