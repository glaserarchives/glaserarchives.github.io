The Next Wave of Filmmakers Debut at SVA Premieres
Tomorrow, June 1, SVA will launch SVA Premieres, its new film and animation showcase, at the Linwood Dunn Theater in Hollywood. Read more about (and watch trailers for) a few of the films that will screen at the event.
The May Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios for May include an illustrated look at blind faith, a creative and colorful binding project, stunning fashion photography, and more.
THIS WEEK
Tuesday (5/31): "Pixel Perfect" Opens
An exhibition of thesis projects by graduating MFA Computer Art students. Reception Thursday, June 2, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Thursday (6/2): "Facsimiles" Reception
SVA Galleries presents a juried exhibition of SVA student work that highlights the process of transformation and challenges the concept of authenticity. Reception Thursday, June 2, 5:00 - 7:00pm. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Friday (6/3): "Digital Short Story: Small Bytes, Big Fiction" Reception
MFA Visual Narrative presents its third annual exhibition of digital short stories and their accompanying physical artwork. Reception Friday, June 3, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE WEEK
The Language of Light
Profoto's Rising Light series highlights the work and technique of MPS Digital Photography student Angelique Ambrosio.
NYC PICKS OF THE WEEK
Moholy-Nagy: Future Present
The first comprehensive retrospective of the work of László Moholy-Nagy (1895 – 1946) to appear in the United States in nearly fifty years. Student tickets are $18; $25 general admission. Through Wednesday, September 7, Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, 1071 Fifth Avenue.
Arcade Classics: Video Games From the Collection
An exhibition of more than 30 classic (and playable) video arcade games released between 1972 and 1993. Student tickets are $11; $15 general admission. Through Sunday, October 23, Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35th Avenue, Astoria, Queens.
TAKE NOTE
Facade Work Continues at 205, 209 East 23rd Street
Select facade demolition and removal has been completed for 209 and is 50% complete on 205. During this removal period there will be loud construction noise associated with the project Monday through Friday between the hours of 7:30am and 3:30pm with occasional days until 5:30pm and 9am to 5pm on Saturdays. In the front of 205 there is a container for debris removal which will be present through the duration of the project. Waterproofing on 209 will take place over the next two weeks, weather permitting. The material is odorless and not a volatile organic compound. Periodic updates will be provided in SVA Today in the coming weeks. For questions, please contact Richard Riccio, executive director of Facilities, 212.592.2156.
Fresh Grad 2016 #01
This week, MFA Products of Design student Roya Ramezani, MFA Interaction Design student Josh Sucher and MFA Design student Sarah Wilson will discuss their work at Fresh Grad 2016 #01, AIGA's annual showcase of design work by students from schools across the country. Student tickets are $15; $20 general admission. Thursday, June 2, 6:30 - 8:30pm, Parsons School of Design, 63 Fifth Avenue, Room U100.
Makers Market
SVA presents a fair showcasing alumni-made products such as apparel, jewelry and accessories; furniture, housewares and decorative objects; and stationery, self-published books, magazines and comics. For more information and a participating vendors list, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
