Spotlighting the Next Generation of BIPOC Climate Activists

Carlos Jaramillo (BFA 2015 Photography) photographed Native Hawaiian climate activist Yvonne “Von” Mahelona for Vogue as part of “Tokala,” a photography series led by Marcus Correa and Jaramillo that focuses on BIPOC climate activists.

NYC PICK

The Dr. Kothari Honorary Lecture: Malavika Sarukkai on Bharatanatyam Dance

The second annual Kothari Lecture on Indian Dance will be delivered by one of the world’s most accomplished Bharatanatyam dancers, Malavika Sarukkai. Tonight, September 25, 6:00 – 8:00pm. New York Public Library for the Performing Arts, Dorothy and Lewis B. Cullman Center, Bruno Walter Auditorium, 40 Lincoln Center Plaza.

Tomorrow | i3 Photo Lecture: Rob Hann

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk with self-taught British-born photographer Rob Hann. Tuesday, September 26, 7:00 – 8:30pm. RSVP for location.

Thursday | La Bodega Hispanic Heritage Welcome Meeting

Kick-off the celebration of Hispanic Heritage with La Bodega—the best place to get connected to the Latin American community on campus—during a night of games and a movie screening. Vengan, vamos a festejar! Thursday, September 28, 5:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. Stay tuned to the club’s Instagram, @labodegasva, for updates!

Sunday | Myco Film Festival

The New York Mycological Society, founded by John Cage, presents the inaugural Myco Film Festival—an evening of short fiction, VR, documentary, animation and experimental films illuminating the fascinating world of fungi and exploring mycology in bold new ways. SVA community members can enter the code “SVA” to receive a $5 discount off tickets (you must use your sva.edu email address to qualify for the discount). Sunday, October 1, 5:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
