Our Spring Lecture and Working Lunch series continues next week at the SVA Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism!

On Tuesday, March 1 at 6:30 p.m., please join us for an evening lecture by IDEO design researcher Alex Gallafent, who will introduce The Little Book of Design Research Ethics, a new publication from IDEO. 

At our Working Lunch at 12:30 p.m. on Thursday, March 3, Nick Rappolt and Matt Basford of the experience design agency Beyond will explore the challenge of juggling data and creativity.

Our Tuesdays evening lectures are followed by Q&As and a drinks reception, where you can take the opportunity to chat with our guest speakers as well as students and faculty of the program. At our monthly Working Lunch Series, guest presenters address practical issues facing designers and design researchers in their day-to-day work with an informal talk over a brown bag lunch in our library seminar room. All programming is free and open to the public but you must register to attend. We hope to see you!

Tuesday, March 1, 6:30 p.m.
Alex Gallafent, "Design Research through Three Lenses:
Journalism, Prototyping, and Improv"

Register
 
Alex Gallafent, a radio journalist turned design researcher at IDEO, will share stories from his work in public radio and the BBC, from his experiences in the field with IDEO clients, and from his background in theater and improv. He will also introduce The Little Book of Design Research Ethics, a new publication from IDEO that expresses how the design company thinks about its values when seeking insights from others.

Alex Gallafent is a design lead at IDEO New York, with a focus on design research, writing, and audio. He leads project teams in media, government, city innovation, and experience design. He is a former foreign correspondent for public radio’s "The World" and the BBC World Service, reporting from across the globe on everything from the rise of digital gurus in India to the intersection of religion and public health in southern Africa. At IDEO, he’s excited to bring the techniques of journalism, audio storytelling, and theater to bear on design challenges large and small. Before joining IDEO, Alex worked on large-scale experiences with ESI Design and on a museum concept design at Local Projects. He is a contributing writer at TED.com, where he shares ideas on urban change, global development, and other subjects. After graduating in philosophy from Oxford, Alex trained in classical acting at LAMDA. He has performed and taught Shakespeare, composed music for plays in London’s West End, and appeared in sketch comedy at the Edinburgh Festival. In New York, he performs improv, tells stories, and plays drums for the city’s only improvised rock'n’roll storytelling band.

Thursday, March 3, 12:30 p.m.
Working Lunch with Nick Rappolt and Matt Basford of Beyond:
"Data and Creativity—Friends or Foes?"

Register
 
These days, you can’t do anything without being told that data can help you. But however useful it can be, a battle is currently being fought between data and human instinct--the "hunch" or "gut feeling." We’re often taught that math equals fact, and that to measure something is to understand it. Yet human insight and creative interpretation remains crucial. Data tells you what—but it’s only a creative human being that can interpret the why. In their lunchtime talk, Nick Rappolt and Matt Basford will explore this challenge and explain the working practice at Beyond that brings data and creativity firmly together.

Nick Rappolt is CEO of Beyond, which he co-founded five years ago with funding from parent company Next 15 and created through the acquisition and merger of three creative firms located in the United States and UK. With a background in advertising and digital marketing, Nick has worked with companies including Apple, Facebook, Google, Virgin, Novartis, GE and Accel. He graduated from Leeds University with a degree in Economics and Philosophy.

Matt Basford is the general manager of Beyond's New York office, the fastest growing of the company's four locations. Matt operates at the center of Beyond’s design, technology, and strategy specialties, building teams to address multi-disciplinary challenges. Matt, a long-time New Yorker, graduated from NYU and currently resides in Brooklyn.

Apply to MA Design Research

For Fall 2016

The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is still accepting applications for Fall 2016 on a rolling basis, as space allows. Successful candidates will be granted significant scholarships. Please contact us for details.

This one-year, high-impact MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject.
