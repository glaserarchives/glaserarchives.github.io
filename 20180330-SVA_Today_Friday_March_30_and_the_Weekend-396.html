SVA Today
Friday, March 30, and the Weekend

Illustrating the "Mythology" of Game of Thrones

A Q&A with Craig Coss (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative), illustrator of the recently released Game of Thrones tarot cards, about how he got involved in the project, challenges he faced throughout the process, and more.
EVENTS

5:00pm | "Gifts from the Exiles: Creative Art Therapists of Color" Reception and Panel

MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition that explores the role of altruism in a diverse society. Reception: 5:00 – 6:30pm. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor. Following the reception, there will be a panel discussion featuring the artists whose work is on view in the exhibition. 6:45 – 9:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

7:00pm | On Black Performance

Writer Nana Adusei-Poku, artist NIC Kay, curator and artist Jaamil Olawale Kosoko and choreographer and writer Will Rawls discuss the relationship between the black body, the intimacy of the stage and public space. 7:00 – 9:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Saturday | SWAN Day Screening: Winnie

Join NY Women in Film & Television for their free annual Support Women Artists Now (SWAN) Day screening of Pascale Lamche's Winnie, the Sundance Film Festival award-winning documentary, followed by a Q&A with the filmmaker and a reception for all guests. RSVPs requested but not required; do so here. Tomorrow, March 31, 1:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

"Embodying a Fluid Reality"

The New York Times debuts Michael Bailey-Gates' (BFA 2015 Photography) new photo series "Horse in the Rough."
NYC PICKS

Smorgasburg

The open-air food festival featuring over 100 local vendors opens tomorrow, March 31, through the summer. Open Saturdays, 11:00am – 6:00pm, 90 Kent Avenue, Brooklyn, and Sundays, 11:00am – 6:00pm, Prospect Park Breeze Hill, Brooklyn.

Hand-lettering Workshop at Cooper Hewitt

Join graphic designer and illustrator Aditi Panchal for a fun workshop that explores the craft of hand-drawn lettering. No experience necessary. Tickets $20 – $30; purchase here. Tomorrow, March 31, 1:00pm. Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.

Waterfront

The Brooklyn Historical Society presents an exhibition and multimedia experience that brings to life the vibrant history of Brooklyn's coastline through stories of workers, artists, industries, activists, families, neighborhoods and ecosystems. BHS DUMBO, 55 Water Street.
TAKE NOTE

Become a better observer and interpreter by studying and writing about various visual media on this SVA Destinations trip to Cambridge, England, taking place from July 22 – August 5. Worth 3 Humanities and Sciences credits. Apply here by Monday, April 30. For more information, click here or email Laurie Johenning at ljohenning@sva.edu.
