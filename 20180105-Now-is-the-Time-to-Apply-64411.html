Dear Friend,

Application season is now active, and I wanted to send a note to encourage you to get your application in by February 1 if you are planning to apply! To reiterate about the program: 
Based on the doorstep of Manhattan’s Chelsea art district, with hundreds of galleries and museums around us, our two-year master’s degree in Curatorial Practice program is a professional training program. We are dedicated to thorough, hands-on training taught by prominent working curators engaged in contemporary curatorial work. History and theory are components of the program, and they are integrated with the crucial aspect of the education here, which is the doing of curatorial work. During the students’ time in MA Curatorial Practice, they will make several shows—each of which we help subsidize. Over the two years, students meet with a total of 42 curators in small group sessions, one a week every week for three of the four semesters, while another semester is spent thinking with distinguished artists about curatorial projects. Each year, we also take our first-year students every to a major biennial in Europe (the Venice Biennale, documenta, Manifesta, and the Berlin Biennale are examples) for viewing and to meetings with major curators at the exhibition. This trip’s round-trip flight is given free to the students, based on a special grant; it is not a charge to students as part of tuition or fees. Many of the students then stay in Europe and do internships there during the summer between the two years of the program. These internships are at major institutions either there in Europe or elsewhere, and we have arranged internships in cities ranging from New York to London, Montpellier to Minneapolis, London to Rome, Berlin, Singapore, Stockholm, and Bogotá, among others. Our mentors are the directors or chief curators at these institutions or elsewhere. 
Our Faculty at Large includes some of the world’s leading curators, directors, and theorists, including Maria Lind, Terry Smith, Hou Hanru, Mick Wilson, and Daniel Birnbaum, among others. And as many of you know, our public programs bring our students into direct contact with the most renowned figures, such as our recent Curatorial Activism summit in New York, with Defne Ayas, Ute Meta Bauer, Nicolas Bourriaud, Carolyn Christov-Bakargiev, Sofía Hernández Chong Cuy, Joshua Decter, Clémentine Deliss, Adrienne Edwards, Irmgard Emmelhainz, Elena Filipovic, Boris Groys, Maria Lind, Antonia Majaca, Hans Ulrich Obrist, Jack Persekian, Pi Li, María Belén Sáez de Ibarra, Terry Smith, Mick Wilson, Brian Kuan Wood, and Tirdad Zolghadr. 
Our recent graduates are now starting their careers with curatorial positions at such important institutions as the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art, the NTU Centre for Contemporary Art Singapore, the Hammer Museum in Los Angeles, ICI, e-flux, and Art in General in New York, and the collections of the Inter-American Development Bank in Washington, DC. 
We offer some support grants toward tuition, and our tuition is highly competitive with other programs in the United States. You can view our courses, faculty, and more at macp.sva.edu. 
Now is the time to do your application! We encourage you to send it by February 1.Don’t hesitate to reach out to us at macp@sva.edu or by phone at +1 212 592 2274. We welcome visits to our beautiful space at 132 West 21st Street, New York, NY 10011. 
Steven Henry Madoff, Chair
MA Curatorial Practice
