Mental Health, Medical and Legal Resources

As a campus, we are horrified and saddened to witness the death of Tyre Nichols, another Black American killed as a result of police brutality, and the 19 lives lost to date in the mass shootings in Monterey Park and Half Moon Bay, California. Please know that there are resources at SVA for anyone who needs support during this time. We are here for you and want you to stay safe.


SVA Student Health and Counseling Services: Free, confidential and short-term therapy and psychiatric services for all SVA students, Monday – Friday, 9:00am – 5:00pm. Email health@sva.edu or call 212.592.2246 to make an appointment.


24/7 Emotional Support Resources:

1) NYC WELL is a free mental-health support resource offering English, Spanish and Mandarin speakers and access for translators in about 200 languages.

Call 1.888.NYC.WELL (1.888.692.9355)

Text “WELL” to 65173

Chat online

2) The Samaritans organization operates a confidential hotline when you need someone to talk to: 212.673.3000.

3) Crisis Text Line offers free, around-the-clock support. Text “HOME” to 741741.

4) Cigna Total Behavioral Health To access Cigna's mental-health services, visit the Stress and Emotional Wellness page on mycigna.com.


If you protested this past weekend or are planning to protest in the coming days, know that there are resources available to you in the event that you need legal or medical support. However, if you are an international student, note that being arrested while in the U.S. in F-1 nonimmigrant status can have far-reaching consequences in unexpected ways. Please be aware of this when participating in protests and protect yourself.

Wednesday | VASA Election Info Sessions

Want to influence change at SVA and create a positive impact for the student body? Get the information you need to build a team and run in the 2023 – 2024 VASA election. Attendance at one info session is mandatory to run. An in-person session will be held at the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, on Wednesday, February 1, 6:30pm. A virtual session will be held over Zoom on Friday, February 10, 12:00pm.

Call for Submissions: 2023 HOPE Art Competition

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents the 2023 HOPE Art Competition. The creator of the winning piece will win a prize of $500 and all of the finalist’s work will be showcased in the SVA Flatiron Windows exhibition space at 133/141 West 21st Street. This year’s theme is a suicide prevention campaign because together, we can find hope. The artwork will serve as a reminder to SVA students that #YouAreNotAlone and #YourLifeMatters. You can find more information here, along with submission guidelines. The submission deadline is Monday, February 6.

Call for Submissions: Honoring Turkish Heritage

SVA’s International Student Office calls for submissions of artwork that relates to Turkish culture or artwork from students with Turkish backgrounds. Open to all students! The deadline for submissions is Thursday, February 2. Submissions will be displayed in the ISO reception area at 340 East 24th Street as part of an exhibition running Monday, February 6, through Friday, March 31. Access the submission requirements and form here. Contact Chisato Takeyama, ctakeyama@sva.edu, with any questions or to schedule an artwork drop-off at ISO.
