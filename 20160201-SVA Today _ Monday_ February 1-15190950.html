SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Monday, February 1, 2016
Remember
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of director Atom Egoyan's Remember (2015), starring Academy Award winners Christopher Plummer and Martin Landau. Producer Robert Lantos will answer questions after. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). RSVP required. Tonight, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:00pm: "Harlem First" Symposium
MFA Design for Social Innovation presents a symposium on the "Harlem First" initiative, featuring Rob Carmona; Carmen Quinones; Dr. Prabhjot Singh, director, Arnhold Global Health Institute; New York City District Attorney Cyrus R. Vance Jr.; and politician Clyde Williams. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:30pm: SVA Club Fair
VASA presents the spring 2016 club fair for students to browse and join student-run organizations. 6:30 - 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

NYC picks of the day
The Apartment
Cafe Grumpy presents a free screening of the classic 1960 film, starring Jack Lemmon and Shirley MacLaine. 7:30pm, 193 Meserole Avenue, Brooklyn.
The Real Thing
An exhibition of work by four female photographers who experiment with gender roles, sexuality and constructed identity. Through Saturday, February 27, Flowers Gallery, 529 West 20th Street.
Take Note
SVA Resident Assistant Information Sessions
SVA Residence Life is seeking RAs for the summer and 2016 - 2017 academic year. Interested candidates must attend an information session to apply. Sessions will be held today at noon, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street; today at 10:00pm, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine; Tuesday, February 2, 3:30pm, SVA Student Center; Wednesday, February 3, 6:00pm, SVA Student Center; Thursday, February 4, 10:00am, SVA Student Center; and Friday, February, 5, 12:30pm, SVA Student Center. Contact raselection@sva.edu for more information.
Titmouse Presents "5-Second Animation Night"
Titmouse, an animation studio founded by Chris (BFA 1994 Animation) and Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1994 Film and Video), presents a screening of animation odds-and-ends and cartoons from studio animators. $5 tickets for SVA students, faculty and staff go on sale today. (Two ticket limit and one non-SVA guest only.) Public sale begins Thursday, February, 4. Friday, March 4, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Movie Ticket Sales Suspended on Wednesday
Discounted AMC ticket sales will be suspended on Wednesday, February 3. Sales will resume on Friday, February 5, at 2:00pm in the Student Engagement and Leadership Office, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine. Contact activities@sva.edu for more information.
