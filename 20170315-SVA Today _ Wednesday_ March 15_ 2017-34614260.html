SVA Today
Wednesday, March 15, 2017

A "Pretty/Dirty" Private Tour

An in-depth look at the private tour artist and SVA faculty member Marilyn Minter gave to a group of SVA alumni of her retrospective "Pretty/Dirty." The exhibition is on view through Sunday, April 2, at the Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn. Admission is free with SVA ID.
EVENTS

1:00pm | ISO Discussion on Executive Order and Immigration Concerns

SVA's International Student Office will provide an overview of the recent political actions on immigration and answer questions. All students, faculty and staff are welcome. 1:00 – 2:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

2:30pm | Captured Screening and Q&A with Clayton Patterson

Since 1979, Clayton Patterson has dedicated his life to documenting the final era of raw creativity and lawlessness in New York City's Lower East Side, a neighborhood famed for art, music and revolutionary minds. The SVA Library will host Patterson, who will introduce this documentary about his life and hold a Q&A. RSVP here. 2:30 – 4:30pm. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, media room.

3:30pm | Orientation Leader Information Session

To learn more about becoming an orientation leader, attend an information session today, 3:30pm or Tuesday, March 21, 6:30pm. Applications are open online. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

4:30pm | Tribeca Talks: Gillian Anderson and Jennifer Nadel

As part of UN Women HeforShe Arts Week, SVA Theatre presents actor Gillian Anderson and journalist Jennifer Nadel, who will talk about their book We: A Manifesto for Women Everywhere. They will discuss changing how we relate to each other so that we cooperate and connect with compassion. A limited amount of free tickets for the SVA community and their guests (one guest per person) will be available at the door one hour prior to the event, with SVA ID. 4:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Designing Resistance: How to Create and Distribute Political Art and Design

Volunteers from the Interference Archive will talk about their popular "propaganda parties" and discuss how independent artists and designers can collaborate with community organizations to create and distribute material that conveys a political message. After the discussion, participants will plan their own party. RSVP here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Library, 380 2nd Avenue, 2nd floor

TAKE NOTE

Local Address: In Beirut—Art, Education, Institutions, Politics

MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on the political context of post-civil war Beirut and Lebanon, and what has been enabled and dis-enabled artistically and structurally. Panelists include Mirene Arsanios, Moukhtar Kocache, Ghalya Saadawi and Gregory Sholette. RSVP here. Tomorrow, March 16, 6:30pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Strong Voices in Action

An open mic night of music, dance, poetry and all kinds of expression. Join in an artistic discussion about leadership, social justice and activism. If you are interested in participating, email Oliver Colbert at ocolbert@sva.edu. Tomorrow, March 16, 6:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Visible Futures Lab: Artist-in-Residence Chelsea Pegram

SVA's Visible Futures Lab will welcome incoming artist-in-residence Chelsea Pegram, who uses sculpture, installation and printed media to explore the subject of transformation as it reflects shifting notions of agency, intention and embodiment. She will give an brief introduction to her work and her plans for her residency at the Visible Futures Lab. Tomorrow, March 16, 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Iconic Branding Insights with Kashiwa Sato

SVA's International Student Office Cultural Partner Program invites students to a talk by Japanese creative director Kashiwa Sato, who will discuss his transformative approach to branding strategy. Tickets $5. Sign up here soon, as there is a 10-student limit. Contact Yoko Anderson at yanderson@sva.edu with questions. Wednesday, March 22, 6:30pm. Japan Society, 333 East 47th Street.

"Canon on the Big Screen" with Russell Carpenter

Academy Award-winner and cinematographer Russell Carpenter and photographer and director Tyler Stableford will screen and discuss their short film, The Calling, which was shot with the new Canon EOS C700 camera. Technical specialists from both Canon and B&H Photo Video will be in attendance. Register here. Monday, March 27, 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
