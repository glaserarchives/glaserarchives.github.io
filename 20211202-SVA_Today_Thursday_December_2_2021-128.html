Events

9:30am | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 9:30 – 11:00am ET, SVA's International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to "International Student Office Staff" headline). Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu if you have any questions.

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour today for a one-hour session with Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross. She will be available on Zoom to field questions about job searching, interviewing and other career-related topics. Join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join. Today, 10:00 – 11:00am ET.

6:00pm | Wrong Man at the Right Time
Curator and writer Judith Stein speaks on Richard Bellamy, the countercultural art dealer who inadvertently midwifed today's market for contemporary art. The event will be in-person for SVA community members and online for the general public. Tonight, 6:00pm – 7:30pm ET. To attend online, click here.

7:00pm | Book Launch: Terry Smith, "Curating the Complex and the Open Strike"
MA Curatorial Practice Chair Steven Henry Madoff, editor of a new essay series from Sternberg Press, speaks with renowned art historian, art critic and artist Terry Smith, who contributed the series' inaugural work. Presented by MA Curatorial Practice. Tonight, 7:00 – 8:00pm ET, online. Register via Zoom.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Charles Traub in The Washington Post
The Washington Post features a slideshow of diptychs from MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Chair Charles Traub’s Tickety-Boo, a new book that collects more than 200 smartphone photos taken by Traub over the past four years.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

New York Botanical Garden Glow
The second annual New York Botanical Garden Glow starts today. Walk through over a mile of colorfully illuminated displays and installations, with the Haupt Conservatory and Mertz Library Building as glowing centerpieces. Through Saturday, January 22. New York Botanical Garden, 2900 Southern Boulevard, Bronx.

Saturday, December 4, 10:00am – 12:00pm ET, online. Register here.

Art Education faculty and alumni will present an overview of the program, explaining coursework and answering any questions about requirements, portfolios, student teaching, schedules, teacher certification and the job market for K – 12 art teachers. Thursday, December 9, 5:30 – 7:00pm ET, online.
