Events

Last Chance | "In and Out of Time"
An exhibition of work by 31 BFA Fine Arts students and alumni. Curated by BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker. Closes today, November 5. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

3:30pm | Fab Friday with Priest and Grace
Priest and Grace is a Brooklyn-based design studio founded by Robert Priest and Grace Lee. Their expansive network of artists produce top quality work that’s been recognized by The Society of Publication Designers, American Society of Magazine Editors and many others. They will be sharing their story and the conception of their global football magazine Eight by Eight. Today, 3:30pm ET, on Zoom. Presented by BFA Advertising and BFA Design.

Saturday | Undergraduate Open House
Do you know someone interested in pursuing a BFA in advertising, design, fine arts or interior design? Be sure to tell them about tomorrow's open house, featuring unique presentations by each department chair, panel discussions with current SVA students and an opportunity to speak directly with Admissions and Financial Aid. Attendees also receive a $50 application fee waiver if they apply for early action. Tomorrow, 10:00am – 1:00pm ET. Register here.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Gail Anderson on Creative Boom's Podcast
Creative Boom features BFA Advertising and BFA Design Chair Gail Anderson (BFA 1984 Media Arts) for this podcast episode, where Anderson shares a bit about herself as well as some hard lessons she’s learned from the pandemic.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Let's Talk: Stop Sponsored Police Violence and Anti-Black Racist Harassment
Hollaback hosts Bridgett Floyd, sister of the late George Floyd, and Michael S. Williams, founder of the Black of Black Project, for a talk with UniQue Webster, Hollaback’s director of External Affairs, about strategies to help put an end to the disparate treatment Black Americans experience in the legal system and the participation of Black Americans in American political and social life. Tonight, 7:00pm ET. Join on Facebook.

Next Week | Cards That Care
On Tuesday, November 9, join Student Engagement and Leadership to write messages and decorate cards for New York seniors isolated due to the COVID-19 pandemic while hanging out with your friends and watching The Muppet Movie (1979). Drop in anytime, 7:00 – 8:30pm, in the Student Center, 217 E 23rd St, and make a card. See you then!

November 10 | MFA Visual Narrative Info Session
Next Wednesday, 7:00 – 9:00pm ET, department chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) invites you to learn more about a program that empowers the next generation of storytellers with the visual narrative skills and expertise to change the world through story. Join if you’re ready to test the limits of your skills and what your stories are capable of doing.

November 11 | MA/MAT Art Education Info Session
Next Thursday, 5:30 – 7:30pm ET, department faculty and alumni will present an overview of the program, explaining coursework and answering any questions about requirements, portfolios, student teaching, schedules, teacher certification and the job market for K-12 art teachers.

November 12 | Blizzard Entertainment Virtual Presentation and Art Contest
Next Friday, 2:00pm, learn more about the gaming industry and how your talents might fit in with SVA Career Development at a virtual industry visit with Blizzard Entertainment. Hear what it's like working on the World of Warcraft team and learn more about their 11th Annual World of Warcraft Student Art Contest. Register first on Eventbrite and then on the Blizzard Entertainment website.

Apply for the Clio Art Fair in Los Angeles
By specifically targeting artists without any exclusive gallery representation, Clio Art Fair focuses attention on the kinds of contemporary art and interventions that are being created by independent artists the world over. Early bird deadline is Monday, November 15. The festival will take place in Los Angeles, February 17–20, 2022 and New York City, September 8 – 11, 2022.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Q&A with Toba Siebzener at Spark Animation Fest
Toba Siebzener (BFA 2014 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) will do a Q&A for the upcoming film Ron's Gone Wrong as part of the virtual Spark Animation Festival. Siebzener is the character technical director of the animated robots for this first film from Locksmith Animation, and currently works for London VFX house Double Negative.

Before Yesterday We Could Fly: An Afrofuturist Period Room
Featuring a collage by Lorna Simpson (BFA 1982 Photography), this project embraces the African and African diasporic belief that the past, present, and future are interconnected and that informed speculation may uncover many possibilities. The Metropolitan Museum of Art, 1000 Fifth Avenue.
