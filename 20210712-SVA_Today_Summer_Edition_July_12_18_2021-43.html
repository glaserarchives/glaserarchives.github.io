Monday, July 12, 9:00pm ET | Register here

Tuesday, July 13, 2:00pm ET | Register here

In the meantime, consult the Reopening SVA page for information on our plans to safely reopen for in-person classes in the fall.

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Wura-Natasha Ogunji
Wura-Natasha Ogunji is a visual artist and performer whose works include drawings hand-stitched into tracing paper, videos and public performances. Register here to join her lecture, presented by MFA Art Practice, on Zoom. Tuesday, July 13, 9:30 – 11:00am ET.

Opening Tuesday | Work From Home
View this exhibition of work by eight MFA Art Practice students, curated by Jacquelyn Strycker, faculty member and director of operations and online curriculum, which explores the fractured domesticity that we’ve all experienced over the past 15 months. Tuesday, July 13 – Monday, August 2, at galleries.sva.edu.

Wednesday | Overcoming Barriers to Mental Health Care
Huilin Lai, LCSW-R therapist and founder of Ally Counseling and Therapy, will join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services (SH&CS) in a conversation about how BIPOC domestic and international students can learn to rise above the stigma, navigate the U.S. healthcare system, develop useful coping skills and prioritize mental health. Wednesday, July 14, 11:30am – 12:30pm ET. Join on Instagram Live.

July is BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month and SH&CS is recognizing it with weekly programming. Follow @svastudenthealth on Instagram to participate live or watch later on IGTV.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, July 15, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Thursday | Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American and Xenophobic Harassment
This interactive training co-sponsored by SVA's Student Health and Counseling Services, International Student Office and Title IX Office will teach you Hollaback!’s 5Ds of bystander intervention methodology. We’ll start by talking about the types of disrespect that Asian and Asian American people are facing right now—from microaggressions to violence—using a tool we call the "spectrum of disrespect." You’ll learn what to look for and the positive impact that bystander intervention has on individuals and communities. We’ll have time at the end for practice and you’ll leave feeling more confident intervening the next time you see Anti-Asian/American harassment online or in person. Thursday, July 13, 3:00pm ET. Register here to join on Zoom.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Wednesday | Music on the Green
In collaboration with Carnegie Hall, Madison Square Park presents a free concert series inspired by Maya Lin’s installation, Ghost Forest, that brings musicians to reflect on the themes of the exhibition. Concerts are held every Wednesday through August 11 on the Oval Lawn.

Wednesday | Bastille Day Celebration in Central Park
This outdoor event will feature live jazz, dancing and a new French film screening in celebration of Bastille Day. Presented by the Consulate General of France in New York, the French Institute Alliance Française and the Committee of French Speaking Societies. Wednesday, July 14, 6:30pm. SummerStage, Rumsey Playfield, Central Park.

Friday | Can You Bring It: Bill T. Jones and D-Man in the Waters Premiere
This film celebrates dancer and choreographer Demian Acquavella and records the struggle of today’s young dancers to interpret the emotional anguish of the early AIDS epidemic era. MFA Social Documentary Film faculty members Tom Hurwitz and Ann Collins were the co-director/cinematographer and editor, respectively. Premiering on Friday, July 16, at Film Forum, 209 West Houston Street. Get tickets here.
