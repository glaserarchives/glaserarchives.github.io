SVA Today
Monday, March 14, 2016
When In Rome...
Interested in typography, book and lettering design, or epigraphy? Find out more about SVA's design workshop in Rome, as well as other SVA Destination programs, such as printmaking in Havana, painting in Barcelona and digital illustration in Berlin.
6:30: The Great Age of the Seljuqs: A Conversation with Deniz Beyazit
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents Deniz Beyazit, curator, Metropolitan Museum of Art, discussing the museum's upcoming exhibition "The Great Age of the Seljuqs," which highlights the history of medieval art in Anatolia and Iran from the 11th through the 13th centuries. Moderated by faculty member Peter Hristoff, with a Q&A; to follow. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Medical Facilities Near SVA
Student Health and Counseling Services reminds the SVA community that most medical conditions can be treated at urgent care facilities, and to be aware of their hours and insurance policies. Orientation Leader Information Sessions
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents information sessions for students interested in becoming orientation leaders. The sessions will be held today, 10:00am and 3:00pm, and tomorrow, 6:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
The Never Ending Project 1.0
BFA Interior Design presents an exhibition documenting the department's participation in last year's Never Ending Project, a week-long design challenge with a focus on interior design and architecture that brought together three teams from three different continents. BFA Interior Design students were teamed up with students from ESAG Peninghen in Paris and the Swinburne University of Technology in Melbourne to develop a buildable design for a control room and docking area for a drone. Thursday, March 17, through Monday, April 4. Reception Thursday, March 24, 6:00 - 8:00pm. VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
New York Fashion Film Festival
MPS Fashion Photography presents this annual screening of fashion films that recognizes outstanding creative work and encourages discourse on the nature of the genre and its growing relevance. Thursday, March 17, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Home and the Poetics of Space
BFA Interior Design and Student Health and Counseling Services present Esther Sperber, founder of Studio ST Architects, and Navah Steiner, a licensed creative arts therapist in the Student Health and Counseling Services Office, discussing the concept of home-based architectural principles and psychoanalytic theories about memory, internal space and interpersonal relationships. Wednesday, March 23, 12:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
