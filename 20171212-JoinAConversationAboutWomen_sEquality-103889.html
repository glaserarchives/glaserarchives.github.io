Join A Conversation About Women's Equality
In 1975, the women of Iceland went on strike for equality, and won. Join us to find out how they did it.
MFA Design for Social Innovation at SVA
View this email in your browser



 
One day, ninety percent of the women in Iceland walked off the job and out of their homes, refusing to work, cook or take care of the children from noon to midnight. The strike transformed the role of women in their society. 

For the ninth year, Iceland ranks first in gender equality – while the U.S. has dropped to 49th. Because this is such a timely issue, we are hosting an evening with Pamela Hogan, the Emmy award-winning director of The Long Friday, a documentary in progress about this untold story. Joining us will be Elisabet Gunnarsdóttir, and Guōrún Hallgrimsdóttir, two of the feisty, hilarious organizers of the strike.

Please join us for a sneak preview and a conversation with women who created and lived this moment of history –– the day the women of Iceland brought their country to its knees.
