2017 Design Writing Summer Intensive: FINALE!

Performance and Reception
Friday, June 16, 4–7pm
136 W 21 Street, 2nd Floor
 

Dear Friends of Design Research,

Please join us this Friday afternoon for the FINALE of our 2017 Summer Intensive!

In this two-week course devoted to research and writing about design, participants were introduced to a range of techniques for constructing compelling narratives about digital artifacts, objects, and spaces, and they experimented with different research methods and writing formats—essays, profiles, reviews, manifestos, and more.

Meet our stellar SI Class of 2017 and come see the fruits of their intensive two weeks!

4-5pm in the library: Guest actors directed by SI instructor Craig Taylor will perform original monologues and dialogues written by the students for Craig's "Speaking of the Streets" project, where students drew inspiration from contemporary voices overheard in NYC neighborhoods.

5-7pm in the studio: Join us for a drinks reception and pick up the series of 12 Design Provocations, a publication of manifestos inspired by SI instructor Karrie Jacobs' "Complexity and Contradiction in the City" project and the premise that "any object, whether or not it has a designer pedigree, can be a worthy subject of criticism."  

2017 Summer Intensive instructors included: Virginia Heffernan, Molly Heintz, Steve Heller, Karrie Jacobs, Jennifer Kabat, Adam Harrison Levy, Robin Pogrebin, Craig Taylor, and Rob Walker.

Spend a Full Year at SVA Design Research, Writing & Criticism

The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is accepting applications for Fall 2017 on a rolling basis, as space allows. Successful candidates will be granted significant scholarships. This one-year, intensive MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject.
