News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Learning from Women in the Industry

Hosted by SVA Alumni Affairs, this panel will tackle the topic of gender equality and the experiences of women in the workplace. RSVP here. Wednesday, February 28, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Design Still Matters

MPS Branding chair Debbie Millman discusses her approach to the engaging conversations she's been hosting for 13 years on her podcast, Design Matters, as its latest season kicks off.

A "One-of-a-Kind" Comeback

The dresses BFA Design students created for the Madison Avenue BID's One-of-a-Kind Luxury event last fall are currently on view at the SVA Gramercy Gallery, through Friday, March 16. Reception: Wednesday, February 28, 6:00pm.

Only in NYC: Shopping Spotlight

From the latest issue of SVA Style, here's a list of offbeat New York City stores that only stock a single item or category of merchandise.
IN THE PRESS

Photographing Elegance

Refinery29 on the style of Harlem resident and socialite Lana Turner, who Dario Calmese (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography) photographed for his series "Amongst Friends."

"How We Got Here"

BFA Illustration faculty member Steve Brodner illustrates the origins of "fake news" for Columbia Journalism Review.

"Elusive, Effusive" Art

ArtNews on artist Amy Sillman's (BFA 1979 Fine Arts) latest series of drawings, on view through Saturday, March 3, at Gladstone 64, 130 East 64th Street.

Global Warming Graphics

The New York Times featured illustrations by Louisa Bertman (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative) in this article about Americans changed their minds about global warming.
EVENTS

Jonathan Sims Gallery Opening

The Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence Jonathan Sims, who will discuss his work and process while his new works are revealed in the lab's gallery. Thursday, March 1, 7:00pm SVA Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor

ARTNOIR: Critical Sound Boarding

MFA Fine Arts and ARTNOIR present Critical Sound Boarding, where artists Elia Alba, Firelei Báez and Saya Woolfalk will discuss their individual practice, social commentary, as well as how their education and relationships with other artists and each other have shaped their work. Tuesday, February 27, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

MFA Fine Arts Open Studios

MFA Fine Arts presents a one-night-only opportunity to see what students in the program are making, doing and thinking about. Thursday, March 1, 5:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.
