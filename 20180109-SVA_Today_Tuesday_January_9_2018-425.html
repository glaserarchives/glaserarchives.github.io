SVA Today
Tuesday, January 9, 2018

Students Create Community

The Mentoring and Relationship Services (MARS) club is a student-founded and -run program where BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects students donate their time to help peers, share tips and create community.
EVENTS

6:30pm | CE Design Information Session

Spend an evening with some of the SVA Continuing Education graphic design faculty. See their work and their students' work and ask questions about working in the world of graphic design. A short portfolio review will follow. 6:30 – 8:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 311.

6:30pm | Robyn O'Neil: Lecture and Screening

MFA Fine Arts presents a lecture by artist Robyn O'Neil and a screening of We, The Masses, a film that she wrote and art directed. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

7:00pm | Jamel Shabazz

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by Jamel Shabazz, an icon of social documentary and street photography in New York City. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"A Year of Style, In Pictures"

The New York Times rounds up its favorite photos that it published throughout the year, including work by Peter Ash Lee (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography), Ryan Shorosky (BFA 2013 Photography) and Dina Litovsky (MFA 2010 Photography, Video and Related Media).
NYC PICKS

Trigger: Gender as a Tool and a Weapon

An exhibition of work by intergenerational artists who use a variety of mediums to investigate gender’s place in contemporary art and culture and to instigate a movement toward more fluid expressions of identity. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, January 21. New Museum of Contemporary Art, 235 Bowery.

Roadside Picnic

An exhibition featuring Chinese artists living in New York whose works defy the "exoticization" of their Chinese-ness. Free. Through Saturday, January 27. Chambers Fine Art, 522 West 19th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Spring Club Applications

Clubs are a great way to be involved on campus and engage with people who share your interests. Check the application for requirements and start your student club today. Deadline: Thursday, January 11.

SVA Galleries Call for Submissions

SVA Galleries welcomes submissions of work by all current matriculated students (undergraduate and graduate) in any medium. A select student jury will review entries to be included in a group exhibition this year. Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 is required. Application available here. Deadline: Friday, January 12, 5:00pm. Contact Marysia Komer at mkomer@sva.edu with any questions.

MFA Social Documentary Film Camera and Sound Classes

Build your filmmaker’s toolkit with MFA Social Documentary Film's new continuing education classes. Attend the intensive Camera Workshop from January 27 – 28, with filmmakers Amitabh Joshi and Erik Sprink (Tashi's Turbine) or the Documentary Sound Workshop from February 24 – 25, with master sound recordist JT Takagi (Strong Island). Students will gain hands-on experience and professional techniques on industry standard equipment.

Words 87: Call for Submissions

Words is a collection of writings by SVA students, faculty and staff and guest writers, and is published twice a year by Humanities and Sciences. To be considered for publication, submit poetry, short stories, short film, theater scripts and/or personal essays as a document attachment (no PDFs) to Louis Phillips, SVA faculty member and editor of Words, at lphillips@sva.edu, with the subject "Submission to Words." Include your name, email address and phone number. Deadline: Tuesday, January 30.

Flu Season Is Here

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services asks for your help in keeping the SVA community well by preventing the spread of flu and other illnesses. If you are sick, please stay home from school/work for at least 24 hours after your fever is gone without the use of a fever-reducing medicine. Support and encourage SVA students, faculty and staff who have a fever to stay home. Click here for more information and prevention tips.
