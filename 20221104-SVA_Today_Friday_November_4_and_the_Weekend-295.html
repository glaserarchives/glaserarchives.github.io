Friday, November 4, and the Weekend

Two Views of New York, From Edward Hopper and a Historic Black Gallery

The New Yorker’s Hilton Als explores the New York City art world of the ’70s and ’80s by looking at two current exhibitions, “Edward Hopper’s New York” and “Just Above Midtown: Changing Spaces,” which features work by Dawoud Bey (1977 Photography), Albert Chong (BFA 1981 Photography), Janet O. Henry (1965 Illustration), Rosemary Mayer (1969 Fine Arts), Jorge L. Rodriguez (BFA 1976 Fine Arts) and Lorna Simpson (BFA 1982 Photography).

10:00am | Yoga for Artists: TLC for Hands, Wrists and Shoulders

Give your hands, wrists and shoulders a break with a rejuvenating stretch. Join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and instructor Melissa Boo for a free, virtual and gentle yoga class. Beginner friendly. All you need is a flat surface and one to three “soft squares” (pillows, blankets, towels, etc.) RSVP required; register here. Open to all registered students, staff and faculty. Today, November 4, 10:00am, online.

Career Development and SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion present a virtual conversation with Yuko Shimizu, award-winning illustrator and SVA faculty member. Shimizu will present images from her illustrious career and discuss her work and creative process. Register here. Today, November 4, 1:00pm, online.

6:00pm | Community Lecture Series: Art Therapy and Palliative Care
