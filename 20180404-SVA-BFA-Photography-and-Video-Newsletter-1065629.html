Alum Sinjun Strom (2017) interviewed by Refigural about her photographic practice.
An Rong Xu for PDN's 30

Alum An Rong Xu (2012) selected for PDN's 30, a yearly award is given to 30 photographers who demonstrate a distinctive vision, creativity, and versatility.
Slate Features Photo Essay by Alex M. Smith 

Alum Alex M. Smith (2007) featured on Slate with a photo essay about motherhood, time, and care.
Brian Finke Exhibits at Clamp Art

Alum Brian Finke's (1998) sixth solo show is on view at Clamp Art through May 12, 2018. The exhibit coincides with the release of his newest monograph Hip Hop Honeys.
Aperture Interviews Lucy Gallun on New Photography

Aperture looks behind the scenes of MoMA’s latest new photography showcase in an interview with Mentor Lucy Gallun.
Areté Venue Student Open Call

Areté Venue is holding an open call for students to submit work to their flat file program, from which they may curate group and solo exhibitions throughout the year. Currently, enrolled students are welcome to apply. There is no fee to apply or to participate in the program.
SVA Destinations: Action Surf Photography in Salina Cruz, Mexico

SVA Destinations offers an intensive 5-day workshop taught by Art Brewer focused on action surf photography, taking place from August 12 - 17, 2018.
Feminine Archetypes in VOGUE Italia's New Photo Series

Photographer Lois Cohen speaks with VOGUE on her series Metamorphosis, which explores themes from religion to pop culture.
Photographer Pushes the Power of Female Gaze and Integrates Her Body with the Camera

Dazed interviews photographer Dani Lessnau, who uses her vagina to create a pin-hole camera to capture fleeting portraits of her lovers.
Documenting New York's Outsiders in the 60s

Huck magazine shares photographer Contance Hansen's newly uncovered images of Brooklyn during an era of purposeful federal neglect during the tumultuous 60s.
 
New York Times: Women in the Spotlight at Museums 

The New York Times shares a list of museum shows that feature work by women from early 20th-century suffrage to contemporary sculpture.
Detroit's Slow But Sure Change Documented in Ongoing Photo Series

Photographer Camilo Jose Vergara has been photographing the slow change of Detroit for several years and recently revisited the city as it clears land for parks and transforms its downtown imagery.
Labs New Artists II Open Call

Red Hook Labs hosts their international open call for Labs New Artists II, a group show at Labs Gallery taking place in June 2018. The deadline to apply is May 1, 2018.

Wired Features Cover by Jake Rowland

Alum Jake Rowland (2006) created the latest cover of Wired magazine, digitally collaging a portrait of Facebook's Mark Zuckerberg and a similarly-featured model with makeup.
Upcoming Ina Jang Solo Exhibit at Foley Gallery

Alum Ina Jang (2010) exhibits at Foley Gallery, opening on April 4, 2018, 6-8PM.
Soho Photo Gallery Hosts Lecture with Joyce Culver 

Faculty member Joyce Culver will be giving a lecture at Soho Photo Gallery encompassing her practice and speaking about how family, community, culture, and politics define her work. The event is taking place on Thursday, April 12, 2018, from 6 - 7pm.
Babes Who Hustle Interviews Megan Breukelman

Alum Megan Breukelman (2016) talks with Babes Who Hustle about her personal icons, faves, and deeper cuts about work and life.
Jordan Cruz in Upcoming Re: Exhibition

Staff member, alum Jordan Cruz (2015), and current MFA student, is featured in this recurring exhibition curated and organized by staff member Max C. Lee, opening on May 26, 2018.
Jill Greenberg Launches Platform for Female Photographers

Mentor Jill Greenberg's new platform Alreadymade houses a growing list of established female photographers to help combat the unbalanced numbers of women with photography degrees and how few are tapped for ad campaigns.
SVA Destinations: Fashion & Portrait Photography in Barcelona 

SVA Destinations offers a 2-credit studio class in Barcelona taught by faculty member Janusz Kawa from July 7 - 17, 2018.
Young Photographers Resurrect 35mm Film 

Many young photographers are shifting away from digital and embracing the return of film. The Reflex, a successfully crowdfunded 35mm SLR camera, will begin production this spring.
The Rise of the Pay-to-be-Featured Economy on Instagram

Feature accounts are hitting up creatives to post their work for a cost, instead of paying artists to have their art on their account. Is this the rise of a new normal for Instagram and will it help combat reposting without permission?
Lensculture Interviews Nicholas Nixon

Photographer Nicholas Nixon speaks with Lensculture about the past 40 years of making portraits.
Zoe Leonard Survey at The Whitney Museum

Photographer Zoe Leonard has a survey of her photographic and sculptural work on view at the Whitney through June 10, 2018.
