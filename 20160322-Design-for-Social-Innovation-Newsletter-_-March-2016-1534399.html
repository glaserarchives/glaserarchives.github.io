Inside a Thesis Prototype: The V Store

By Maia Kaufman, class of ’16
Rarely in life do we get to have something we’ve been picturing in our heads for a long time come to life in the real world, so this has been an amazing and mind-bending experience. I came up with the concept for The V Store just over a year ago: a comprehensive, approachable concept store for sexual and reproductive healthcare products, services, and information, staffed with sex educators and medical professionals. I can honestly say that the final product was exactly what I had been picturing all this time.

Social Impact Design and the New Age of Uncertainty

Originally posted on Impact Design Hub
The current refugee crisis and the mass migrations that climate change is likely to trigger will disrupt not only the lives but the identities of hundreds of millions of people — torn from place, uncertain (at best) of their context in the world. Where we come from is, to an enormous extent, who we are. Culture determines the food we eat, what we learn, who we marry and how we live. The place where we live determines our culture, and so our identity.

News
DSI students Nazl Cangönül, Lauren Gardner, Kara Isabella and Caroline McAndrews were invited to participate in the recent Design 4 Impact Conference in New York, bringing together educators, innovators, and advocates with the common goal of implementing more design-driven curriculum in K-12 education.
 
At social impact firm Reboot, Emily Herrick (class of ’16), is helping clients in the international development sector, such as the World Bank, make better use of well-designed infographics to help readers understand and process complex data and ideas. Read about her latest work here.
 
Margarita Korol (class of ’16), has joined Another Limited Rebellion as Intercity Director of Community Engagement for their #CreativeSprint 30-day challenge, a global art game that encourages a daily creative practice.  The next challenge begins in April, and Margarita will be coordinating a NYC kickoff event. Get more details and sign up at creativesprint.co.
 
Rodrigo Muñoz (class of ’16), and thesis partner Rinat Sherzer, are partnering with Community Access’s East Village Access project, which supports people who are living with mental health disorders, as part of their thesis project using sensorial experiences to generate a new language to deal with anxiety.  
 
This semester, artist, programmer, musician, and new Faculty member Luisa Pereira, is co-teaching Technologies for Designing Change with faculty member Claire Lin. Luisa’s work has been presented in festivals and galleries in New York, London, São Paulo, Buenos Aires, Santiago de Chile and Montevideo, at venues including the Museum of Contemporary Art in Santiago, SXSW, and the FILE festival. Her projects have appeared in Wired magazine, and on art and technology websites such as Gizmodo and Creative Applications. Luisa holds a BS in Systems Engineering from Universidad Ort, where she also taught Interaction Design, and an MPS from NYU’s Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP), and served as a post-graduate research fellow there from 2013 to 2014. She is currently a member of NEW INC, an art and technology incubator at the New Museum.
 
Cheryl Heller will be speaking at AIGA Connecticut on April 4, as part of their Creative Impact Series, in a discussion about designing a life (and career) with purpose.
 
Faculty member Hannah du Plessis will be tackling necessary but difficult conversations about race, privilege and white guilt, at a March 31 workshop in Pittsburgh.
