SVA Today
Tuesday, March 15, 2016
Rosson Crow on Interiors, Fashion and the Big Sky of LA
SVA Close Up recently caught up with Rosson Crow (BFA 2004 Fine Arts) to learn more about her inspirations. Crow's work was most recently exhibited at the Armory Show in New York.
6:00pm: Smoke School of Art
MFA Fine Arts presents the Smoke School of Art (SSA), an Atlanta-based nonprofit arts group that addresses contemporary issues in art, culture and philosophy, discussing the group's mission. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:15pm: One More Time
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue invites you and a guest to attend a screening of One More Time, starring Christopher Walken and Amber Heard, followed by a Q-and-A with the director, Robert Edwards. SVA ID required. SVA Theatre.
6:30pm: Anne Guiney: The City as Research Project
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Anne Guiney, research director at the Van Alen Institute, discussing the city as a research project, the relationship humans have with their environment and methods of turning research into action. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
7:00pm: Margit Erb
Margit Erb is the founder and director of the Saul Leiter Foundation, which is dedicated to preserving the art and legacy of the influential New York City photographer and painter, and the photographer's former assistant. Presented by the MPS Digital Photography. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Loss, Pain and Family Secrets
L.A. Weekly highlights "Tear Drops and Three Dots," a new exhibition of photographs by Star Montana (BFA 2013 Photography), now on view at the Vincent Price Museum in Los Angeles.
NYC PICK OF THE DAY
In Conversation: Dana Spiotta and Joshua Ferris
Join novelist Dana Spiotta, author of Innocents and Others, in conversation with Joshua Ferris, author most recently of To Rise Again at a Decent Hour. Innocents and Others "follows two women filmmakers as their lives unfold, darkly exploring forms of connection, success and desire." McNally Jackson Books, 52 Prince Street, 7:00pm.
TAKE NOTE
The Back of the Painted Beyond
MFA Art Writing presents art historian Michael Ann Holly discussing the invisible realm beyond the vanishing point of backgrounds in early modern Northern paintings. With introductory remarks by faculty member Michael Brenson. Thursday, March 17, 6:30pm. MFA Art Writing, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
The Will Eisner Influence
The SVA Library and the National Cartoonists Society present a panel discussion on the influence of pioneering comics artist and longtime SVA faculty member Will Eisner. The event is part of Will Eisner Week, a worldwide series of events celebrating comics, graphic novels and the legacy of the comics visionary.Thursday, March 17, 7:30pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd floor, amphitheater.
Women and Cars: New Perspectives
In this lecture in honor of late SVA faculty member Phil Patton, curator and art historian Russell Flinchum will trace the history of the stylistic development of the automobile and the role of female designers in converting the horseless carriage into the "object de luxe" of the 1920s. Tuesday, March 22, 6:30pm. At MA Design Research, Writing, and Criticism, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
Jan Pinkava, co-director of Ratatouille, met with students from SVA in SF, an SVA Destinations trip, and introduced them to Google's Spotlight Stories. Photo by Hsiang Chin Moe, director of operations, MFA Computer Art. Via Instagram.
