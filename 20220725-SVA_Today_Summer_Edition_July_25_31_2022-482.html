Titmouse, Inc.

Titmouse Animation Studio Flies High
In 2000, SVA alumni Chris and Shannon Prynoski founded Titmouse, a now thriving animation studio that has produced such series as Big Mouth and Harriet the Spy. Read the Visual Arts Journal story about the studio and its founders above.
EVENTS THIS WEEK

Last Chance | “And Then . . . 2022”
MFA Visual Narrative presents its thesis exhibition of visual stories by five graduates of the program’s class of 2022. Closes today, July 25. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Tomorrow | Practice Lecture Series: Ben Davis
MFA Art Practice presents a talk by art writer Ben Davis. Tuesday, July 26, 12:30 – 2:00pm. Registration required; RSVP here. MFA Art Practice, 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.

Friday | MFA Visual Narrative Open Studios and Riso Print Slam
MFA Visual Narrative presents its annual open studios show featuring work from first- and second- year students in a range of media, as well as Riso-printed work for sale at Print Slam in the RisoLAB. Friday, July 29, 5:30 – 8:00pm. Registration required for members of the general public; RSVP here. MFA Visual Narrative, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.

Last Chance | “This Too Shall Pass”
MFA Art Practice presents an exhibition of thesis work from its class of 2022. Through Saturday, July 30. Van Der Plas Gallery, 156 Orchard Street.

Last Chance | “Public Private”
An exhibition of work by five MFA Art Practice students, curated by the program’s director of operations and faculty member Jacquelyn Strycker. Through Saturday, July 30. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Ongoing | “Outro: World-Building Within and Outside the Studio”
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of thesis projects by its class of 2022, curated by Herb Tam, curator and director of exhibitions at the Museum of Chinese in America. Through Monday, August 15. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
SVA IN THE PRESS
An Rong Xu

An Rong Xu for The New York Times
An Rong Xu (BFA 2012 Photography) took a series of photographs for the recent New York Times piece, “He Had a Dark Secret. It Changed His Best Friend’s Life.”

Heirlooms, Redefined
Eve Lyons (BFA 2010 Photography) and The New York Times’ Narrative Projects team asked 10 Black photographers to document objects that represent their family histories.

“The Absurdity of Consumer Culture”
MFA Fine Arts faculty member Mika Rottenberg (BFA 2001 Fine Arts) explores humanity’s rampant production in her latest exhibition at Hauser & Wirth. Via Whitewall.

2022 NYSCA/NYFA Artist Fellows
Yael Eban (MFA 2014 Photography, Video and Related Media), Rachel Papo (MFA 2005 Photography, Video and Related Media) and Kathy Shorr (BFA 1988 Photography) have all been named as 2022 NYSCA/NYFA Artist Fellowship recipients.

Examining Romantic Traditions Around the World
Reema Mehta (MPS 2022 Branding) is featured in Print for her series exploring how various cultures around the world define love.
NYC PICKS

“Mirror Image: A Transformation of Chinese Identity” at Asia Society
An exhibition of 19 artworks by seven artists born in mainland China in the 1980s, curated by SVA faculty member Barbara Pollack. $7 for students; purchase tickets here. Asia Society and Museum, 725 Park Avenue.

Summer for the City: Godzilla Screening
Lincoln Center’s Summer for the City presents an outdoor screening of Godzilla (1954). Free; first-come, first-served or limited advance reservation available. Thursday, July 28, 9:00pm. Hearst Plaza, 30 Lincoln Center Plaza.

Sputnik Yoga
The nomadic yoga studio, Sputnik Yoga, presents a free class at Oculus Plaza. Register here. Wednesday, July 27, 5:30 – 6:30pm. Oculus Plaza at Church Street and Fulton Street.

“Marley Trigg Stewart: The Hills Keep Burning in California”
A solo exhibition of work by photographer Marley Trigg Stewart, curated by Pacifico Silano (MFA 2012 Photography, Video and Related Media). Through Sunday, July 31. Tiger Strikes Asteroid New York, 1329 Willoughby Avenue #2A, Brooklyn.
