SVA Storms Comic Con
Led by the student group Cartoon Allies, SVA's booth at the annual convention, which opens today, gives students the chance to meet publishers and artists and get an early look at the latest in comics and popular entertainment.
1:00pm: CINE PitchFest
CINE presents a chance for five indie producers to pitch their projects to executives in only five minutes. Panelists will critique each pitch and hold a post-session Q-and-A with the producers and audience. Free to the first 25 SVA students who register with the code "svacinepf15" at cine.org/pitchfest/tickets. $10 student tickets, $20 general admission. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
1:00pm: Class of 2016 Yearbook Photos
Photo sessions for the 2016 SVA yearbook take place today from 1:00 - 5:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, 3rd floor.
7:30pm: "Remember Me Offline" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that focuses on the unique experience that women have in the digital age. "Remember Me Offline" investigates the different ways cultural producers are using chat rooms, pornographic images, online dating, social networking and online gaming to advocate for their identities as women and as artists. The reception features live performances by Casey Jane Ellison and Kaja "Cxzy" Andersen. Through October 22, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
8:00pm: "The Martian" Screening
SVA and the Visual Effects Society present a 3D screening of The Martian (2015), directed by Ridley Scott and starring Matt Damon, Jessica Chastain and Chiwetel Ejiofor. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
MoMA PS1 in Queens.
SVA Alumni, Faculty in "Greater New York" at MoMA PS1
ARTnews has announced the lineup for PS1's fourth exhibition spotlighting New York-based artists. Participating artists include MFA Fine Arts faculty member Dara Birnbaum, Collier Schorr (BFA 1985 Communication Arts), Lorna Simpson (BFA 1982 Photography) and BFA Fine Arts faculty member Sue Williams, among others.
NYC picks of the day
Pixar: The Design of Story
A new exhibition featuring original and rare artwork from the renowned animation studio. Free admission with SVA ID. Through August 2016, Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.
Narcissus Now
The Onassis Festival presents a series of programs celebrating and analyzing the Greek myth of Narcissus and its lasting impact on culture. Featuring seminars, screenings, installations, guided tours and more. Free with registration. Through Sunday, October 11, The Onassis Cultural Center, 645 Fifth Avenue.
