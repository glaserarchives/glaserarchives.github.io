"Be a Freedom Machine": More Tips from Jerry Saltz

Following his talk at SVA last winter, the senior art critic at New York returned this summer to speak with students and offer even more advice for artists.

Alumni Film and Animation Festival Begins Tonight!

This year's festival kicks off tonight at 7:30pm with the New York City premiere of Spring Break Zombie Massacre, directed by Robert John Carnevale (BFA 2007 Film and Video). The film is co-written by stars and best friends Sam Suchmann and Mattie Zufelt, who met at the Special Olympics. A discussion with the film's cast and crew will follow the screening. Click through for a full schedule of screenings taking place through Sunday, September 18, at the SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

Mid-Autumn Festival

The International Student Office, Student Health and Counseling Services and the SVA Chinese Students Organization present a festival with Korean traditional dance, music performances, calligraphy and fan painting. There will also be Chinese mooncake, tea tasting and Japanese food. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Friday, September 16, 12:15 - 2:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

SVA at Photoville

BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography are presenting exhibitions at this year's Photoville, the annual photography exhibition housed in shipping containers located along the waterfront of Brooklyn Bridge Park. Wednesday, September 21, through Sunday, September 25, Brooklyn Bridge Plaza, Water Street at New Dock Street, Brooklyn.

Critical Engagement: Art and Community

MPS Art Therapy presents its 32nd annual conference, with a keynote speech on socially engaged art practice presented by multidisciplinary artist Pablo Helguera, workshops and a panel discussion. Participating speakers include storyteller and creative director Melissa Malzkuhn; artist and Elastic City founder and director Todd Shalom; and sensory experience artist Miriam Simun. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff. Friday, September 23, 10:00am - 4:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
