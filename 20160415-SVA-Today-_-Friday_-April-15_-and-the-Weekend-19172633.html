SVA Today
Friday, April 15, and the Weekend
Fearless Behind the Camera [Video]
SVA Features talks to Dan Minahan (BFA 1987 Film and Video) about how his experience at SVA led him to direct episodes of Game of Thrones, Homeland and Marco Polo, among other series. The sixth season of Game of Thrones premieres Sunday, April 24, on HBO.
Opening: "Low-Grade Euphoria"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of performance, video, sound and installation works. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellows. Performance tonight, 6:00 - 10:00pm, featuring Terry Boyd, Puppies Puppies and Aya Rodriguez. Through Friday, April 29, Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 3rd floor, Brooklyn.
5:30pm: Meet and Eat with Cultural Partner Program
The International Student Office presents students in the Cultural Partner Program discussing the community service projects they completed this semester. Students can learn more about the program and sign up for next year. Food from different cultures will be served. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 302.
Saturday: "SVA Computer Art: Senior Thesis Projects"
BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects presents work by graduating students. Reception Wednesday, May 4, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Through Saturday, May 14, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Saturday: Around the World: Multicultural Event
The Student Affairs Office, Black Student Union, Chinese Student Organization, Filipino Student Association and Il Circolo Italiano (Italian Club) present a celebration of cultural diversity at SVA, with performances and karaoke, as well as cuisine from the Caribbean, China, Italy, the Philippines, and elsewhere. Open to SVA students. Saturday, April 16, 2:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Comics for Dangerous Humans
Rolling Stone interviews former My Chemical Romance frontman Gerard Way (BFA 1999 Cartooning) about launching his new DC Comics imprint, Young Animal.
NYC WEEKEND PICKS
Drawing the Guggenheim
A workshop using drawing to understand the museum's architecture. Admission is $20, materials included. Saturday, April 16, 10:00am - 1:00pm, Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, 1071 Fifth Avenue.
The American Side
A free screening of the contemporary film noir directed by BFA Film faculty member Jenna Ricker, with production design by BFA Film faculty member Debbie De Villa. Ricker and co-writer Greg Stuhr will answer questions following the screening. Saturday, April 16, 10:30am, DGA Theater, 110 West 57th Street.
Earth Day 2016
Celebrate Earth Day with dozens of exhibitors, interactive displays, live performances and activities. Sunday, April 17, noon - 7:00pm, Union Square, North Plaza, East 17th Street and Park Avenue.
TAKE NOTE
Spring Book Sale
The SVA Library presents its spring sale, featuring books and periodicals on art, design, criticism and more. All titles are priced $5 or less; credit card purchases only. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Monday, April 18, through Wednesday, April 20, 10:00am - 6:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
Day Night Day Night
BFA Animation and BFA Film present Day Night Day Night (2006), a thriller about young woman who plans to detonate a bomb in Times Square. Director Julia Loktev and editor Michael Taylor will answer questions following the screening. Wednesday, April 20, 6:30pm, 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
