🎙️ Get To Know Hanaan Louis, Media Development Coordinator, Admissions

As a 2020 graduate of SVA, could you tell us a little about what led you back to the College and into the position of media development coordinator?

SVA was always a second home to me because they’ve welcomed me with open arms as both a student and student worker. Within the last year or so, I was seriously working towards combining my interest in digital marketing and my prior background of film and photography into a profession. It just so happened that a new position opened up for the media development coordinator role in Admissions and things aligned perfectly for my current and future goals.


Congratulations on the launch of SVA’s new TikTok account! What makes social media an important avenue of communication for the College?

Thank you! Social media, specifically TikTok, is an alternative means for education, inspiration, insight, news—you name it. Young people in particular are diversifying the ways in which they obtain information. In fact, social channels are seen and used more like search engines now, despite being also entertaining. Social media is a way for SVA to tap into new audiences and to broaden our horizons. We’re spreading information about SVA in exciting contexts and in new formats that are less traditional and less expected.


How is TikTok different from other social media platforms like Instagram and Twitter, particularly for students?

People are coming to TikTok to make content that everyone can relate to, including useful videos that are educational or to broaden a message or idea. On TikTok, most people are not aiming for perfect alignment with angles or word placement, clothing and hairstyles. In many ways, it is more authentic. People feel seen because of all the different content on that app. TikTok also gives you the space to discover new interests and hobbies that you didn’t know you wanted to pursue in the first place. I like to think of Instagram as the more responsible older sister that TikTok got inspiration from and did whatever and still succeeded. Twitter is direct and less creative, like a parent, and Facebook is a well-meaning grandparent. While all of these social media channels have their benefits, ultimately, TikTok is the best tool for our current recruitment efforts. This is the driving force behind my work.


How does being an alumnus of SVA help inform the kind of content and strategy you put forth in your work?

I graduated from the Film department with a concentration in directing. I made sure I got hands-on experience in every aspect of filmmaking while attending SVA, while running my own blogs and businesses outside of school. I am also a photographer, creative producer, and content creator/influencer and so with my prior experience in editing and directing, it was easy to combine that into the social media marketing realm. Being a former campus tour guide at the school has also helped me establish good relationships with faculty and allowed me to gain insight into each department. All of this helps me develop a killer strategy and content ideas that would resonate with our audience.
