SVA Today
Friday, September 24, and the Weekend

"My Art Portfolio" with Talya Alsberg [Video]
In our new video series, "My Art Portfolio Then and Now," SVA students and alumni share the progression of their work from the start of their time at SVA to their senior year. First up is multidisciplinary storyteller and tattoo artist Talya Alsberg (BFA 2021 Photography and Video). Watch the video here.
Events

12:30pm | The Education(s) of an Artist
In this panel discussion, artists Lisa Di Donato, Yam Chew Oh (MFA 2019 Fine Arts) and Arantxa Ximena Rodriguez (MFA 2019 Fine Arts) and curator Keren Moscovitch (MFA 2005 Photography, Video and Related Media) will discuss the myriad educational pathways available to artists, both inside and outside of the institution. Presented by SVA Continuing Education. 12:30 – 1:30pm. RSVP here to join on Zoom.

These artists are all included in the exhibition "Material Index," curated by Moscovitch, at NYC's Affordable Art Fair. Learn more at Continuing Education's blog.

All Weekend | After School Special
See below for all of the After School Special events happening for free this weekend! The alumni film and animation festival concludes on Sunday, September 26. All events take place at SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tonight, 8:00pm | SVA Premieres: Best of 2020 and 2021
SVA spotlights exceptional live-action, documentary and animated shorts by its most recent graduates, including films nominated for Student Academy Awards. Join many of the filmmakers and artists, who will be in attendance. Tonight, 8:00pm. RSVP here.

Saturday, 2:00pm | Vivo
See this Netflix animated feature on the big screen and stay for a Q&A with head of cinematography/layout, Yong Duk Jhun (MFA 2000 Computer Art). Saturday, September 25, 2:00pm. RSVP here.

Saturday, 7:30pm | In the Heights
Watch the cinematic version of Lin Manuel Miranda's Tony Award-winning Broadway musical about life in Washington Heights, then join several BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni as they discuss how their behind-the-scenes artistry brought the film to life. Saturday, September 25, 7:30pm. RSVP here.

Saturday, 11:59pm | The Midnight Gospel
This midnight screening of three episodes from the acclaimed adult animated series is followed by a discussion with several BFA Animation alumni who worked with Titmouse Animation to create this show for Netflix. Saturday, September 25, 11:59pm. RSVP here.

Sunday, 2:30pm | Raya and the Last Dragon
Watch one of Disney's newest animated features and stay to hear BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni discuss their work on the film's opulent animation. Sunday, September 26, 2:30pm. RSVP here.

New York on Alumnus Kenny Rivero

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Register Now | Examining Institutional Characteristics Behind 2015 Black Student Protest Events
The National Conference on Race and Ethnicity (NCORE) presents this webinar, featuring Michael Carhart, PhD and student advisor at Seton Hall University, and Stephanie Mayer, PhD, MBA and financial disbursement analyst at Columbia University. The two will discuss their study examining the institutional characteristics underlying Black student protests in 2015. Wednesday, October 6, 4:00 – 5:30 ET. Register here.
