SVA BFA Photography and Video Newsletter
Rufus Barkley Wins Barneys Perfect Shot Competition

Teen Vogue interviews SVA BFA Photography & Video student Rufus Barkley, who won the Barneys New York Foundation Perfect Shot Competition and got to shoot Barneys' current look book.
Hailun Ma Photographs Winter Gowns for New York Magazine's Wedding Issue

Alum Hailun Ma (2015) and current MPS student photographs seasonal gowns for the latest New York Magazine's Wedding Issue.
Collier Schorr Slams a Culture of Sex Abuse in Creative Industries

SVA alum Collier Schorr (1985) calls on art world and media professionals to stand up against harassment and abuse within the industries.
Alums Included in SVA Exhibition Street Smart: The Intersection of Art and Design in the City

Alums Kevin Amato (Above, 2003), Justin Aversano (2014), and Travis Rix (2014) are included in a group exhibition of alumni at SVA's Chelsea Gallery, on view from November 18 to December 20, 2017.
Lenscratch Features Lisa Elmaleh

Lenscratch features faculty member Lisa Elmaleh and her series American Folk, documenting musicians from West Virginia in tintype with her portable darkroom.

AI-AP Selects Malcolm Lightner's Photo for AMERICAN PHOTOGRAPHY 33

Faculty member Malcolm Lightner's photograph, Native, 2011, from Mile O' Mud: The Culture of Swamp Buggy Racing has been selected as one of the winning photographs in the AMERICAN PHOTOGRAPHY 33 competition and will appear in the accompanying book, available now.
Max C. Lee Opens Latest Iteration of Re: Curatorial Series

Faculty member Max C. Lee's ongoing curatorial series with collaborator Erin Davis, Re:, opens Saturday, November 18, 6-10pm and will be on view through December 10, 2017.
DEAR DAVE, Launches New Features Content

For DEAR DAVE,'s newest Features interview, Editor-in-Chief and BFA Photography & Video Department Chair Stephen Frailey spoke with Courtney Asztalos about her series Purple Domino, a collection of atmospheric portraits of women in casinos.
President Obama's Photographer Reflects on The Past Eight Years

Peter Souza, chief official White House photographer for President Obama, shares some thoughts and memories of his tenure with the Obama Administration, as well as a small fraction of the 1.9 million photos he took.
ChromaLuxe and Lucie Foundation Team Up to Offer New Scholarship

The FINE ART SCHOLARSHIP helps support emerging fine art photographer by awarding $3,000, as well as three prints on ChromaLuxe aluminum and inclusion in the tenth anniversary MOPLA group show. The deadline is January 20, 2018.
 
New Photo Algorithm Turns Low-Res Photos into Detailed Images

Computer scientists at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems in Germany have developed a new tool called "EnhanceNet-PAT" which uses AI to create high-def versions of low-resolution images.
Joseph Rodriguez's Photos of Spanish Harlem in The 80s Opens at Bronx Documentary Center

Joseph Rodriguez's exhibition Spanish Harlem: El Barrio in The 80s opens on Saturday, November 11, and runs through December 23, 2017.
Lensculture Interviews Debra Klomp Ching of Klompching Gallery 

Mentor Debra Klomp Ching shares some gallery insights as the co-founder and director of a photography gallery in New York City.
The New York Times Features Street Photographer Richard Sandler

Richard Sandler's photos from the 70s and 80s showcase a city experiencing turmoil and rapid change amid national tensions.

Tagtagtag Features Brendan Jo

Senior Brendan Jo's photographs featured in Tagtagtag magazine's Journal section.
Ilona Szwarc Photographs Janet Jackson, Peaches, and More for The New York Times

Alum Ilona Szwarc (2013) created portraits of musical heavy-hitters for The New York Times' article on women creating and producing their own music and their role in the industry.
Musée Magazine Interviews Nicholas Contrera

Alum Nicholas Contrera (2016) is featured in a video interview on Musée magazine about his photography, politics, and music.
METAL Magazine Interviews David Brandon Geeting

Alum David Brandon Geeting (2011) talks with METAL about his photography and shares several photos from his ongoing series, Stolen Ideas.
Booooooom Features Sebastiano Arpaia

Alum Sebastiano Arpaia's (2016) new photographs featured on Booooooom.
Max C. Lee and Signe Pierce Exhibit in Group Exhibition Safe

Staff member Max C. Lee and collaborator Erin Davis, as well as alum Signe Pierce (2011), have work in a multimedia exhibition about safe spaces in network culture.
Sean Hemmerle Publishes New Book of Middle Eastern Portraits

Faculty member Sean Hemmerle's newest book, THEM, is a collection of portraits taken across the Middle East with accompanying text of his journal entries while there. A talk and book signing will take place on Tuesday, December 12, at 7PM in MFA Photography, Video, and Related Media's Big Room on the first floor.
Cooties Webzine Interviews Katelyn Kopenhaver

Alum Katelyn Kopenhaver (2016) talks about her photography, process, and feminism with Cooties.
SVA's Fall 2017 Visual Arts Journal Features Frank Ockenfels 3

Alum Frank Ockenfels 3 (1983) is featured on Visual Arts Journal with several images of his collaged journals.
Steven Mayer in Reflection Pools Exhibit in Portland

Staff member Steven Mayer exhibits in a two-person show, Refection Pools, with Peggy Sisouvong at Grapefruits in Portland, OR.
Carrie Mae Weems in Conversation with Mary J. Blige for W Magazine

Carrie Mae Weems photographs Mary J. Blige for W Magazine and talks with her about women, race, and the future.
Condé Nast Bans Terry Richardson

Condé Nast International has banned Terry Richardson from their publications, including Vogue. This comes after past accusations of his sexual misconduct have resurfaced.
Google Launches 3D Repository for Virtual Reality

Google's new site, Poly, is a warehouse for users to download and share 3D objects and scenes for AR and VR projects.
Joel Meyerowitz’s Photos of the Objects in Cézanne’s Studio

Hyperallergic reviews a new book of Joel Meyerowitz's photographs taken in Cézanne’s studio in Aix-en-Provence, France.
Collodion Process Ambrotypes on View at Yossi Milo Gallery

Artist Ben Cauchi exhibits his wet-plate ambrotypes in his first solo exhibition in the U.S. As above, so below is on view through December 9, 2017.
