SVA Today
Tuesday, February 22, 2022

The Booster Bus Is Back
All eligible students, faculty and staff who need a COVID-19 vaccine booster can get one at the booster bus, which will be hosted on campus by SVA Student Health and Counseling Services for two days this week. The bus will be parked outside the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, today and Thursday, February 24, 9:00am – 6:00pm. Appointments are not necessary, and eligible recipients can receive a $100 incentive.

Bring proof of prior vaccination and proof of ID (i.e., government-issued driver's license or passport). For questions about booster eligibility, click here. Remember to upload your booster verification into Cleared4.
Events

Bill Komoski: Recent Paintings
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of new work by artist and SVA faculty member Bill Komoski. Through Friday, March 25. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

11:00am | Yoga for Artists: Poses to Help You Sleep Better
Join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and instructor Melissa Boo for a free, beginner-friendly virtual yoga class focusing on improving sleep habits and night routines. Learn yoga poses that may help improve the quality of your sleep, reduce stress and encourage relaxation. Today, 11:00am ET, online. RSVP required.

12:00pm | Interested in Becoming an RA?
Interested in being a resident assistant this summer or next academic year? Attend one of the mandatory information sessions taking place this week to learn more about the position and receive the application materials. Today, 12:00pm ET, and tomorrow, February 23, 10:30am ET, on Zoom.

3:00pm | Responsive Design on Editor X with Wix Playground Academy
Join SVA Career Development for a live site creation demo and workshop to help you drill down on responsive web design and adopt a responsive approach to future projects. Wix Playground Academy will cover building fluid sites that suit any screen size using Editor X, a drag-and-drop creation platform for web designers. This is an advanced-level workshop for design students as well as those who are already using or building a Wix website. A 60-minute interactive presentation will be followed by 30 minutes of Q&A and open discussion. SVA design majors who attend this workshop will be invited to apply for a website review with Wix designers. Today, 3:00pm ET, online. Register here.

4:00pm | Practice Lecture Series: Beatrice Glow
MFA Art Practice presents a talk by artist-researcher Beatrice Glow, whose diverse practice includes sculptural installations, olfactory art, emerging media and multi-lingual publishing.

5:00pm | Campus Dialogue: The Aftermath of the Black Lives Matter Cases
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA hosts a talk with Gloria Browne-Marshall, playwright, constitutional law professor and civil rights lawyer, about the aftermath and impact of recent Black Lives Matter-related trials. This will be a dinnertime discussion about topics relating to the cases and their impact on the nation, with an opportunity for Q&A. Today, 5:00pm ET, on Zoom. Register here.

6:30pm | Vera Lutter
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a talk by artist and program alumnus Vera Lutter, who has won international renown for her camera obscura images and her video work. Tonight, 6:30pm ET, online. Register here.

7:00pm | David Burnett
MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photojournalist David Burnett, as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tonight, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.

Last Chance | HOPE Art Competition
Today is the last day to see the posters submitted to the 2022 HOPE Art Competition, on view at the SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street. Congratulations to Grand Prize winner You Jeong Hwang (BFA Design), Fan Favorite Winner Oliver Perry Rauch (BFA Illustration) and all participants in this year's competition!

Tomorrow | Cyrano Special Screening
United Artists is presenting a special, red-carpet screening of the new film Cyrano, starring Peter Dinklage, at the SVA Theatre tomorrow. A limited number of seats are available to the College community—each SVA ID holder can bring one guest.

To attend, a reservation is required by today, February 22, 5:00pm; email svatheatre@sva.edu with the subject line""Cyrano Screening." All guests must show proof of vaccination and booster (if applicable), and must also get a PCR test at a specific location in Chelsea between 9:00am – 6:30pm tomorrow. (More information will be provided with ticket confirmation.) Wednesday, February 23, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Returning Student Room Selection
SVA returning student room selection continues this week. Current residential students can see details regarding the room selection process in their email. Any eligible student who is interested in reserving housing for the 2022 – 2023 academic year can email reslife@sva.edu or click here.

Friday | Ed Benguiat Interment at Arlington National
Longtime faculty member and type designer Ed Benguiat (1927 – 2020) will be interred at Arlington National Cemetery on Friday. In addition to teaching at SVA for almost 50 years and creating more than 600 typefaces, Benguiat served in the Army's Air Corps during World War II. Friday, February 25, 3:00pm ET, on Zoom. Please mute your device when joining the call.
