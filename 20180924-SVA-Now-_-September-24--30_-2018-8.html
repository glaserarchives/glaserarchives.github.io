News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
September 24 – 30, 2018
News and events from around SVA

The Creative Minds Behind Krammer & Stoudt

A Q&A with Courtenay Nearburg (MPS 2013 Fashion Photography) and Mike Rubin, the creators of menswear label Krammer & Stoudt.

The Plot to Kill Hitler

A Q&A with John Hendrix (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay) about his new graphic novel The Faithful Spy, the true story of a man with a plot to kill Hitler during World War II.

My First Year: Mauricio Velazco

Second-year BFA Computer Arts, Computer Animation and Visual Effects student Mauricio Velazco reflects on his first year at SVA.
IN THE PRESS

"Movie Stars Have Heroines, Too"

In this New York Times article, actresses, directors and writers talk about women in film who inspire them. One of the inspirations mentioned is filmmaker Lynn Shelton (MFA 1995 Photography and Related Media).

The Infamous Putin Portrait

Hyperallergic on how artist Brian Whiteley (MFA 2013 Fine Arts) claims he hung a painting of Russian President Vladimir Putin in a Trump International Hotel suite in Washington D.C., and no one noticed.

"Reimagining the Male Gaze"

T: The New York Times Style Magazine on how a group of illustrators, including John MacConnell (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay), is redefining how queer bodies are represented in art.
EVENTS

Gloria Steinem and Barbara Nessim in Conversation

SVA Career Development presents Gloria Steinem and artist Barbara Nessim, who will talk about the role their long-lasting friendship has played in sustaining their work and how the context of New York City itself influenced their careers. The event is first come, first served, and will also stream live here. 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Scheimpflug Lecture: Pacifico Silano

MFA Photography, Video, and Related Media presents a visiting artist lecture and discussion with distinguished alumnus Pacifico Silano, as part of its Scheimpflug Lecture Series 30th anniversary programming. RSVP here. Tuesday, September 25, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

"Outside These Walls" Reception

BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of artworks made outside of SVA's facilities during the last year. Organized by the program's director of operations, Tyler Rowland. Wednesday, September 26, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, October 13. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21 Street.

"Forgetting, Losing, Looping" Reception

MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition, curated by program fellow Kate Benedict, that constructs a dialogue surrounding the role of popular culture in contemporary life. Wednesday, September 26, 7:00pm. Through Tuesday, October 9. CP Project Space. 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
