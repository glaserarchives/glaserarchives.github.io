SVA Today
Friday, March 25, and the Weekend

Glaser’s Art Makes Life
Today’s excerpt from The Glaser Gazette, “Glaser’s Art Makes Life,” is an essay penned by SVA founder, the late Silas Rhodes, on the occasion of Milton Glaser’s Masters Series show in 1989.
Events

Last Chance | Bill Komoski: Recent Paintings
Today is the last day to see this exhibition of recent work by artist and SVA faculty member Bill Komoski, presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:30pm | Fab Friday: Homecoming with Mo and Shantanu
This week’s guests for BFA Advertising and BFA Design’s Fab Friday are two accomplished graduates from 2021, Mo Hinojosa and Shantanu Sharma. After graduating from SVA, Mo joined January Third, a creative agency based in Washington, D.C., and Shantanu joined Wieden + Kennedy. In this special homecoming installment, they will bring us up to speed on their post-SVA lives and cover “things we wish we knew before graduating.” Tonight, 6:30pm ET, on Zoom.

8:00pm | Titmouse 5 Second Night
SVA Theatre presents this annual event, for which Titmouse animators create and screen whatever goodies have been floating around their minds all year. Titmouse, founded by SVA alumni Chris and Shannon Prynoski, employs many SVA graduates, and several animators/artists will be in attendance. Tonight, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. Tickets $5 – $15; purchase here.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Tom Huhn for Krisis
Art History and BFA Visual & Critical Studies Chair Tom Huhn has contributed an essay to Krisis, a contemporary philosophy journal, for a special issue commemorating the 70th anniversary of Minima Moralia: Reflections From Damaged Life, by Theodor Adorno.
NYC PICKS

Art for Change: The Artist & Homeless Collaborative
The New-York Historical Society presents “Art for Change: The Artist & Homeless Collaborative,” an exhibition examining the history of modern homelessness in New York City. Through Sunday, April 3. Admission is pay-as-you-wish from 6:00 – 8:00pm on Fridays. New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West, Joyce B. Cowin Women’s History Gallery.

After the Burn
Michael Eade’s solo exhibition, “After the Burn,” co-curated by Xiao Zong (MA 2021 Curatorial Practice), explores the idea of coexisting with the nonhuman world. Opening tomorrow, March 26, 2:00 – 8:00pm, with a curatorial walk-through at 3:00pm. On view through Sunday, June 5. Fou Gallery, 410 Jefferson Avenue #1, Brooklyn.
ANNOUNCEMENT

Sunday | MobiusLab Club
The MobiusLab student club is hosting a virtual meeting this weekend with special guest Alwyn Hunt, co-founder of The Rookies, a community of next-gen creatives that have a passion for making games, films and animations. Sunday, March 27, 6:00pm ET, via Zoom.
#SVAWESOME
Jinuk Choi (MFA 2020 Computer Arts) is among the nominees for the prestigious Television Academy Foundation’s 41st College Television Awards. Choi is nominated in the Animation Series category for producing, writing and directing Beyond the Line. The awards ceremony takes place tomorrow, March 26. Image via Choi’s website
