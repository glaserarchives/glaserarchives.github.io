SVA MA Curatorial Practice Announces Spring 2018 Major Year-End Exhibitions


Dear Friend,

We’re thrilled to announce the opening of our major year-end exhibitions. The openings are this Thursday, April 19, from 6 to 9 pm at the Pfizer Building, located at 630 Flushing Avenue, Brooklyn, NY 11206. All are invited and, of course, the exhibitions are free and open to all. The exhibitions continue, by appointment, until May 4. Here are listings of the shows, with links to the press releases within each listing:

Cocoon, curated by Lux Yuting Bai, concerns how one “becomes what one is” by examining the concepts of authenticity and absurdity in an existential framework. Full press release here.

Bric-á-Brac, curated by Jacqueline Kok, explores diverse implications of human behavior within shifting geopolitical structures. Inspired by Guy Debord’s concept of the dérive. Full press release here.

The Future of Labor, curated by Noelia Lecue Francia. What is the science of transience and how will the dynamics of plenary digitization shape the ethics of labor going forward? Full press release here.

Inspired by Trevor Paglen’s inquiries into the production of space, RE:ACTION, curated by Amanda Lee, examines contemporary techniques of distortion, while offering compensatory prospects of belief in belief. Full press release here.

Femmexplicit Digitalia, curated by Jasa McKenzie, surveys and celebrates ways in which explicit female sexuality and corporeality are becoming a symbol of power in society today. Full press release here.

Same Light, New Exposure, curated by Birdie Piccininni, considers contemporary photographic portraiture and the complex intersubjectivities of photographers, their sitters, and the optics of negotiated inquiry and the doubt of fact. Full press release here.

Isla imaginaria, curated by Natalia Viera Salgado, interrogates notions of territory, citizenship, displacement, erasure, and the question of progress on the island of Puerto Rico today. Full press release here.

Sanctuary, curated by Piper Ross Ferriter, presents a 21st-century reading of Leo Tolstoy’s What Is Art? toward a shared conviction of empathy. Full press release here.

Are We in Sync?, curated by John Elammar, Jesse Firestone, Xinyi Ren, María Alejandra Sáenz, Michele Thursz, and Andrea Valencia, brings together more than a dozen international artists to explore alternative constructs and perceptions of time. Full press release here.

Please come! The SVA MA Curatorial Practice is now on rolling admissions for Fall 2018. See all about the program, including applying, at our website here.

Best wishes,
Steven Henry Madoff
Chair, MA Curatorial Practice
