All Faculty Brief |

December 2021


Dear Colleagues:

I hope you had a peaceful and restful Thanksgiving.  It is hard to believe we are nearing the end of the fall 2021 semester--our first semester of in-person classes since spring 2020.  The students I have seen around campus seem very happy to be back at SVA and in NYC, as are those of you whom I have had the pleasure of seeing out and about.

The last two years have been quite the rollercoaster ride, yet we managed to make our way forward to this moment with all our parts relatively intact.  As I have said on many occasions, we overcame what at the time seemed like insurmountable obstacles.  And none of this would have been possible without the hard work and dedication of the SVA faculty.  Thank you!

The uncertainty that has come to characterize living with a pandemic continues to this day.  The latest COVID variant, Omicron, is raising concerns among the scientific and public health communities.  Much remains unknown about the effects of Omicron.  We are keeping a close eye on this variant, and to the extent it may affect our operations, we will update you on any developments.

As you will see, this edition of the All Faculty Brief is chock full with important and useful updates from the SVA Library; Office of Learning Technology; Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion; and the Registrar.

Good luck with the remaining three weeks of the semester.  Stay healthy and safe during this holiday season.


All best wishes,

Chris

SVA Faculty Book Club Evolves as a Resource and It's Never Too Late to Join

The Faculty Book Club is open to SVA faculty members and staff who believe that education is a form of activism. The group meets on Zoom once a month for an hour to discuss and take a deeper dive into either a book, article, or film that touches on social justice issues. During the meetings, participants learn together as teachers and mentors through sharing their new understanding and insights. The contents of the titles that are covered often reaffirm what we know and believe in, but at times, hearing new voices and ideas can fundamentally upend our realities. 
 
The Faculty Book Club is supported by the SVA Library and OLT. The titles are available to read and view remotely via SVA Library databases, and discussions are ongoing on Canvas. We are all busy as instructors and professionals in our respective fields--it doesn’t matter if you can’t finish a book or if you post your discussion comment a month later--- what counts is that you participate and that we cultivate a culture of professional growth. 
 
Faculty members are welcome to pitch a relevant title and facilitate the discussion. A few have used the book club as a space to workshop and refine their ideas for assignments or lectures, before presenting them to students.

Voicethread App in Canvas | Faculty Feedback Requested
The Voicethread app was integrated into Canvas in 2013 to provide options for asynchronous group critiques. At the time, Voicethread was the best option we had available, but it remains unclear how helpful the app has been for SVA faculty. Therefore, to help us determine if Voicethread should be phased out in 2022, please respond to our brief (1-4 questions) poll. 

Click here and let us know if you have or have not ever used Voicethread in Canvas. If you have used Voicethread, you will be asked a few follow-up questions about your experience. Thank you for sharing your feedback! 

Miro Workshop to Introduce a Terrific Online Collaboration Tool 
Miro is an easily accessible online whiteboard, which many SVA instructors already use for concept mapping, mood-boarding, and collaboration. It can also be used to conduct online group critiques, synchronously or asynchronously, and a Miro board can be embedded directly into a Canvas page for easy access and interaction. Miro is a potential replacement for Voicethread.

Important Reminders from the Registrar's Office


Attendance
Please continue to record student attendance in Canvas on a regular basis. It is extremely important for the College to receive accurate attendance in a timely manner, as we use the information to identify students who may be struggling. We have a network of administrators (advisors, student affairs staff) who provide additional support to students when warranted, which is imperative to student success efforts. Attendance data also helps us to identify students who may have stopped attending, which is incredibly important for financial aid compliance and enrollment forecasting. If attendance is recorded incorrectly, administrative intervention efforts are significantly hindered.

A Closing Note From the Office of Diversity, Equity

and Inclusion

There has been an encouraging increase in awareness in the past years of the challenges we all face regarding racial equity, structural racism and exclusion. Late last year, SVA established its Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) office, for which I am the director, and a DEI Task Force, comprising members of the College’s administration, academic leadership and staff. Together, we are charged with fostering a campus climate in which differences and diversity are celebrated and respected, and all are welcomed and heard. In the months since, working together with SVA students, faculty and staff, we have made significant progress, and there is much planned for the 2021 – 2022 academic year.

Currently, we are developing a strategic plan for DEI at SVA, one that aligns with SVA's mission, core values and current strategic plan. This process consists of four phases, with each phase serving as a key component toward building the foundation for SVA’s DEI identity.

SVA's Land Acknowledgement

The School of Visual Arts acknowledges that the campus resides on unceded Indigenous lands, specifically the Lenapehoking or the Land of the Lenape (“lun-NAH-pay”). The main Lenape meeting place, the Kintecoying or "Crossroads of Three Nations" was located near Astor Place, blocks away from SVA's present campus, before their forced migrations which began in 1626. The School of Visual Arts acknowledges that colonial narratives have overpowered and often shielded mainstream recognition of the historic and continual displacement of Native people by the United States.

SVA recognizes its ethical obligation as an educational institution to offer resources that shed light on accurate depictions of history. This acknowledgement indicates a commitment to confronting genocide, displacement, and the erasure of Indigenous peoples while recognizing that land acknowledgement is only a starting point for dialogue, relationships, and informed action. In an effort to gather resources about the perseverance of Indigenous communities as they relate to our mission as an art library, the SVA Library has created a subject guide about contemporary artists native to the Americas. We will continue to build on this resource and others.
