SVA Today
Wednesday, November 8, 2017

Club 57 at MoMA

East Village venue Club 57, an essential part of New York's urban rejuvenation in the 1980s with a cohort of SVA students at its core, is the subject of a major retrospective at The Museum of Modern Art: "Club 57: Film, Performance, and Art in the East Village, 1978 – 1983."
EVENTS

6:00pm | An Evening with Gabo Arora

MFA Interaction Design presents an evening with Gabo Arora, creative director and senior advisor at the United Nations, founder and president of Lightshed, a VR and social impact start-up, and an award-winning filmmaker. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

6:30pm | Student Leadership Series: From College to Professional and Beyond

SVA Student Engagement and Leadership and Residence Life present a panel discussion where SVA alumni will discuss their experiences at SVA and in their field, transitioning from college to career and how students can prepare now for success in the future. Refreshments will be served. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. 6:30 – 7:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

7:00pm | 19th Annual Animation Show of Shows

SVA Theatre, MFA Computer Arts and BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects present the Animation Shows of Shows, featuring internationally acclaimed animated short films. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff; RSVP here. 7:00 – 10:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Sentimental Snapshots

Lenscratch on Michelle Groskopf's (BFA 2003 Film and Video) upcoming photo book Sentimental, a collection of portraits and details that combine to reflect the human experience.
NYC PICKS

Women's Suffrage Turns 100! Part 3: The "Nasty Woman" Stigma

Panelists Joan Malin, former president of Planned Parenthood of New York City, Inc.; Allison K. Lange, historian of women and gender; and Jamia Wilson, executive director and publisher at Feminist Press, discuss the history of the mischaracterization of women. Tickets $5; purchase here. 6:30pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street.

Greenhouse

An exhibition of Brian Batt's new series of paintings and drawings inspired by urban horticulture, accompanied by a pop-up show of “Women & Flowers,” a new photographic series by Christina Arza (MFA 2015 Photography, Video and Related Media). Opening preview tonight, 6:00pm. Through Sunday, November 19. Foley Gallery, 59 Orchard Street.
TAKE NOTE

Art Therapy Counseling Sessions

MPS Art Therapy Counseling offers 50-minute sessions with second-year graduate students, who will help you to use art to express yourself and to deal with stress and anxiety. Open to SVA students. Email Val Sereno at vsereno@sva.edu for more information.

SVA Library Fall Book Sale

SVA Library presents its fall book sale, featuring a wide range of books from across collections, including fine art and design. All books will be free – $5; debit/credit only. Open to SVA students, faculty, staff and alumni. Tuesday, November 14 – Thursday, November 16, 10:00am – 6:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
