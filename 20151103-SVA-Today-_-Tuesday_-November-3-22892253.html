SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, November 3, 2015
The Legacy of Comics Legend Will Eisner
MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller speaks to SVA Close Up about the legacy of comics legend Will Eisner. On Wednesday, November 11, the SVA Alumni Society, along with the Will and Ann Eisner Family Foundation, presents Jules Feiffer and Paul Levitz discussing his work and honoring the 75th anniversary of The Spirit.
Photo by Zhe Zhu (BFA 2015 Photography).
SVA Dominates "Avant-Guardian" Selections
Four of this year's Surface "Avant-Guardian" winners are SVA alumni. The annual competition singles out ten emerging photographers chosen by a star jury and the magazine's editors.
6:00pm: The 33
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of The 33, the true story of the Chilean miners that were trapped in a gold and copper mine in 2010. Lou Diamond Phillips will be on hand to answer questions following the screening. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:00pm: Brody Condon
Artist Brody Condon discusses his work. Presented by MFA Fine Arts. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:30pm: Julia Kaganskiy, Networks as Generators of Creativity
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Julia Kaganskiy discussing the creative potential of collaboration and cross-disciplinary networking in both culture and commerce, including NEW INC., the shared workspace and professional development program she directs at the New Museum. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Press pick of the day
Christine Sun Kim, Face Opera II, 2013.
Games of Skill
Christine Sun Kim (MFA 2006 Fine Arts) speaks with Rhizome about her sound-oriented work, including her interactive installation "Game of Skill 2.0," which is currently on view at MoMA PS1's Greater New York exhibition.
NYC pick of the day
Swampland
An exhibition of photographs by Ruth Wetzel highlighting the diverse ecosystems and plant life of New York. Admission is free. Through Friday, November 13. Arsenal at Central Park, 830 Fifth Avenue, 3rd floor gallery.
Take Note
Gym Membership Survey for Students and Faculty
The Visual Arts Student Assocation (VASA) in conducting a survey of SVA students and faculty members about the possibility of partnering with the New York Sports Club for discounted memberships. The survey can be found here, and the deadline is Thursday, November 5, 5:00pm.
I'm Making a Boy Band
MA Curatorial Practice, Asia Contemporary Art Week (ACAW), the Asian Contemporary Art Consortium and SCREEN present a performance of original songs by EXP, the reverse-engineered K-Pop band that is part of the conceptual group I'm Making a Boy Band (IMMABB) led by artist Bora Kim. A conversation with Kim, Karin Kuroda, Samantha Y. Shao and EXP will follow the performance. Friday, November 6, 7:00pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
International Student Office Moving
This week, the International Student Office is moving to a temporary location at 132 West 21st Street, 12th floor. Advisement sessions during this time are by appointment only. Contact iso@sva.edu for more information and to schedule an appointment. Normal ISO operations will resume on Monday, November 9.
