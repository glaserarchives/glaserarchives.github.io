SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
July 10 - 16, 2017

Fulbright Semi-Finalist

MFA Visual Narrative student Rosa Chang was selected as a semi-finalist for the Fulbright-National Geographic Digital Storytelling Fellowship, a highly competitive, fully-funded fellowship program.

From SVA to Iceland

A Q&A with BFA Visual and Critical Studies faculty member Emily Weiner (MFA 2011 Fine Arts) about her current exhibition in Iceland with Ragnheiður Gestsdóttir and Theresa Himmer (MFA 2011 Fine Arts), who all met while in the MFA Fine Arts program at SVA.
IN THE PRESS

Weaving Spider-Man's Web

The New York Times on Joe Sinnott (1953 Cartooning), who has been drawing Spider-Man comics for over 50 years.

"Classic Americana in Las Vegas"

The New York Times on Ryan Shorosky (BFA 2013 Photography), whose photos capture the variety of people who work in Las Vegas, especially those with unconventional jobs.

The Power of Children's Art

Hyperallergic interviews Brian Belott (BFA 1996 Fine Arts) about the power and complexity of children's art in light of his recent exhibition "Dr. Kid President, Jr." in New York City.
EVENTS

MPS Branding Thesis: Repositioning the American Dream

The MPS Branding class of 2017 will present their thesis projects, which reposition the American Dream through the lenses of capitalism, immigration, technology, commerce and the pursuit of happiness. Tuesday, July 11, 11:00am – 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

The Artist as Activist

MFA Art Practice presents a panel discussion on the role that art can play in effecting social and political change. Panelists include Ben Davis, Shaun Leonardo, William Powhida, Daniel Tucker and Caroline Woolard. Wednesday, July 12, 6:00 – 8:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.
