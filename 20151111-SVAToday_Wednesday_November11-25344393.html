SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, November 11, 2015
SVA Named "Military Friendly School"
Just in time for Veterans Day, SVA has earned its fifth straight military-friendly distinction from Victory Media. Click through to read more about the honor, and about SVA's history of supporting America's veterans.
Your Designs Wanted
The SVA Theatre is seeking new animations and computer art for its marquee. All designs must be original and under one minute in length, and can promote the College or the SVA Theatre, or simply be unique artistic expressions. Winners will receive a $100 gift card to the SVA Campus Store. Further instructions regarding format, and the SVA Theatre logo, can be found here. SVA logos can be found here. Submit video links with your name and department to svatheatre@sva.edu.
6:00pm: Designing for Designers
MFA Interaction Design presents Adobe's Andrew Couldwell (Portfolio) and Talin Wadsworth (Project Comet) discussing their work at the company, and how they approach designing new products. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
7:00pm: Book Launch with John Thackara
MFA Products of Design presents a launch party for faculty member John Thackara's new book, How To Thrive in the Next Economy: Designing Tomorrow's World Today (Thames & Hudson). Thackara will read and share stories from his book before a conversation with department chair Allan Chochinov. A book signing will follow. 136 West 21st Street, 7th floor.
7:00pm: Will Eisner, Celebrating 75 Years of The Spirit
The SVA Alumni Society, in partnership with the Will and Ann Eisner Family Foundation, presents Pulitzer Prize-winning cartoonist and author Jules Feiffer, along with former publisher and president of DC Comics Paul Levitz, discussing the legacy of pioneering comics artist and longtime SVA faculty member Will Eisner. Tickets are $5 for SVA students and $20 for general admission. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Supporting Syrian Refugees with #OrangeVest
UK-based Art-Sheep profiles MFA Fine Arts student Georgia Lale's participatory performances in support of Syrian refugees. Her next performance will take place on Sunday, November 15, at the Brooklyn Bridge.
NYC picks of the day
Veterans Day Parade
The 2015 America's Parade features over 250 groups and 20,000+ participants, including veterans of all eras, military units, civic and youth groups, businesses, and top high school marching bands from across America. The parade starts at approximately 11:25am and goes up Fifth Avenue, from 26th to 52nd Street.
New York Comedy Festival
Comedy Central presents its annual festival celebrating stand-up and improv, featuring Kathy Griffin, Norm MacDonald and Sarah Silverman, among others. Ticket prices and locations vary. Through Sunday, November 15.
Take Note
International Students Office Resumes Operations
The International Students Office has resumed normal operations at its temporary location at 132 West 21st Street, 12th floor. For questions, contact iso@sva.edu.
"Fuse" Exhibition Tour
MPS Digital Photography presents a gallery tour of "Fuse," an exhibition of thesis projects from the class of 2015, with department chair Katrin Eismann and the exhibiting artists. Friday, November 13, 5:00pm, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Free Admission to Quartz Conference
The Next Billion is a conference sponsored by Quartz that brings together leaders and industry experts to discuss the challenges and opportunities of connectivity in industries around the world, including education, health, technology, commerce and finance. Admission is free with code "QZSVA." Monday, November 16, 8:30am - 6:00pm, Conrad New York, 102 North End Avenue.
Youth
The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences presents a screening of director Paolo Sorrentino's Youth, starring Michael Caine, Harvey Keitel, Rachel Weisz and Jane Fonda. Special guests from the film will answer questions following the screening. Attendees must remain in the theater through the conclusion of the question-and-answer session. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Friday, November 20, 6:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
