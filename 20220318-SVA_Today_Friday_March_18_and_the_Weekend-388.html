Events

2:00pm | Tips and Strategies for the Job Search Process
SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the International Student Office present their monthly virtual support space for international students. This month’s meeting will feature Tricia Ross, assistant director of SVA Career Development, who will offer advice on internship and job-search strategies; International Student Advisor William Park, who will answer immigration- and work authorization-related questions; and Cong (Holly) Fu, international outreach therapist, who will provide tips about managing the job search and post-graduation planning stress. Today, March 18, 2:00pm ET, on Zoom. Registration is required; register here.

6:00pm | Extra-Strength Pain Relief
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents “Extra-Strength Pain Relief,” a show independently produced by 13 fourth-year program students who share a studio space at SVA. Reception tonight, March 18, 6:00pm. Through Thursday, March 24. Rogue Space Penthouse, 508 West 26th Street, 12th floor.

6:30pm | Fab Friday: Expectation vs. Reality

This week’s guests for BFA Design and BFA Advertising’s Fab Friday will be interaction and product designers Steven Jung and Jaquelyn Wang. Jung and Wang have amassed a tremendous amount of industry know-how in the past eight years, working at such companies as Google, AKQA and R/GA. They will share their self-proclaimed “cringe-worthy” trials and errors to help soon-to-be graduates avoid the mistakes that they made. Tonight, March 18, 6:30pm ET, on Zoom.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Call for Entries: “Perevorot (Revolution) Illustrated”
SVA Galleries invites the SVA community to participate in “Perevorot (Revolution) Illustrated,” an international exhibition showing solidarity with the people of Ukraine, scheduled to be held in early April at multiple locations around the world, including the SVA Flatiron Windows. The exhibition protests the repression of Russian artists, journalists and citizens for voicing their opposition to the war in Ukraine. The SVA effort will be curated by Russian artist and curator Natasha Konyukova. Submitted works (illustration, comics, photographs) may involve any interpretation of the idea of “revolution”, and should be completely wordless and black and white. Photographs may not contain documentary images of the war in Ukraine. The call for entries can be found here, or email art@white-lanes.ru. The deadline is Thursday, March 24.

Apply for the Clio Art Fair
The Clio Art Fair focuses on independent artists from all over the world who do not have any exclusive gallery representation. The “early bird” deadline is Monday, May 9. This year’s fair will take place September 8 – 11 at 550 West 29th Street.
