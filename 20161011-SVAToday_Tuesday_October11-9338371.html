SVA Today
Tuesday, October 11
FEATURE
Roan with Kilda and April, by Rachel Papo

Life as a Homeschooler

From the fall issue of the Visual Arts Journal, Rachel Papo (MFA 2005 Photography, Video and Related Media) looks into the world of homeschooling.
EVENTS

6:00pm: Jesse Darling

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by the artist. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:30pm: Aileen Kwun and Bryn Smith: Twenty Over Eighty

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents program alumni Aileen Kwun (2011) and Bryn Smith (2013) discussing their recently published book Twenty Over Eighty: Conversations on a Lifetime in Architecture and Design (Princeton Architectural Press), a collection of profiles and interviews with 20 creative professionals over the age of 80. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm: Anastasia Samoylova

MPS Digital Photography presents photographer Anastasia Samoylova discussing her work, which uses images sourced from the Internet to create layered, collage-like compositions that reinterpret the concept of landscape photography. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

The Politics of Sound

Art in America interviews sound artist Christine Sun Kim (MFA 2006 Fine Arts).
NYC PICK OF THE DAY

James Turrell: Meeting

A site-specific installation that opens onto the sky. Admission is free with SVA ID. MoMA PS1, 22-25 Jackson Avenue, Queens.
TAKE NOTE

Cartoonists: Foot Soldiers of Democracy

A screening of the 2014 documentary about 12 cartoonists from around the world who risk their lives to defend democracy. Organized by Valeria Cordero (BFA 2005 Photography), executive director, Venezuelan American Endowment for the Arts. Free for SVA students with discount code "SVA," $10 general admission. Friday, October 14, 10:00am, WeWork SoHo West, 69 Charlton Street, 2nd floor.

In a Valley of Violence

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening In a Valley of Violence, starring Ethan Hawke and John Travolta. Director Ti West (BFA 2003 Film and Video) and producer Peter Phok (BFA 2003 Film and Video) will answer questions following the screening. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Tuesday, October 18, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
