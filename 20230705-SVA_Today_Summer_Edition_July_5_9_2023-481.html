SVA is collaborating with the Korean-American beauty brand, JOAH, and faculty member Hyesu Lee (MFA 2011 Illustration as Visual Essay) on a design competition celebrating inclusivity. Read more about the initiative and see the alumni-designed submissions at the link above.

New SVA Subway Poster by Carol Fabricatore

The latest SVA subway poster, for summer 2023, was created by artist and SVA faculty member Carol Fabricatore (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay). Click the link above to see a video about Fabricatore and her work.

SVA IN THE PRESS

“Murray Hill Is Ready to Share His Personal Life”

Murray Hill (MFA 1997 Photography, Video and Related Media) shares photos of his Pride season activities for The New York Times’s “Seven Photos” series.

The Making of Twin Sparks

The Rookies takes a behind-the-scenes look at how BFA 2023 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects graduates Ollie Yao and Heather Yun created their 3D animated short film, Twin Sparks.

Society of the Snow

Composer Michael Giacchino (BFA 1990 Film and Video) has teamed up with director J.A. Bayona for a new Netflix survival thriller based on true events. Via Bloody Disgusting.

At Home with Genevieve

Designer and TV host Genevieve Gorder’s (BFA 1998 Graphic Design) new lifestyle show, At Home With Genevieve, premiered last month on Crackle.

Transforming the Mundane in Comical Illustrations

Jill Arteche, a current resident in SVA’s Artist Residency Illustration and Visual Storytelling program, is featured in a Coconuts Manila article spotlighting standout Filipino artists at the Manila Illustration Fair.

NYC PICKS

New Louis Armstrong Center

The Louis Armstrong House Museum opens its new Louis Armstrong Center this week featuring the exhibition “Here to Stay,” which details the life of Louis Armstrong and his five-decade career. Opening Thursday, July 6. $8 – $14 for students. The Louis Armstrong House Museum, 34-56 107th Street, Queens.

The Wedding: New York’s Biggest Day

Lincoln Center invites hundreds of couples—from those getting married to those renewing vows or anyone simply celebrating their love for the city—to participate in a multicultural ceremony with various faith leaders, live musical performances, desserts and karaoke. Free. Saturday, July 8, 5:30pm. Damrosch Park, Amsterdam Avenue and West 62nd Street.

Trans Art Bazaar

Shop clothes, jewelry, food, art and more at New York City’s biggest trans and nonbinary artist market featuring more than 80 vendors from around the country. Free. Sunday, July 9, 1:00 – 7:00pm. House of YES, 2 Wyckoff Avenue, Brooklyn.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Next Week | Storyteller Series: Skooby Laposky, Sound Designer and Artist

MFA Visual Narrative presents a talk with sound designer and artist Skooby Laposky, who has shaped the personalities of consumer robots, produced original music for couture runway shows and added depth to documentary films with his field recordings and original scores. Free. RSVP required; register here. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

MFA Visual Narrative Prospective Students Day

Visit MFA Visual Narrative’s summer intensive semester in action—jump into classes, talk with students and learn more about the program with Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay). Finish the day with a tour of the 2023 thesis exhibition, “And Then.” Registration is required; RSVP here. Monday, July 17, 2:00 – 6:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 1001.

DJ Workshop and MC Competition, Featuring DJ Scratch

SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion presents a workshop on the art of DJing with award winning DJ and producer DJ Scratch. DJ Scratch will talk about the history of DJing, the art of turntablism and demonstrate the techniques of scratching that made him famous. This event is the first in a SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion series of events celebrating the 50th anniversary of hip-hop. Wednesday, July 19, 4:00pm. SVA Amphitheater, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd Floor.

Create a Video/Animation, Win $500

SVA Theatre seeks a 10 to 20 second pre-roll video (animated or live action) to play before film screenings shown during this September’s After School Special 2023: SVA’s Alumni Film and Animation Festival.


Set the video to music that you have created yourself, own the copyright for, have obtained a license for, or is in the public domain, and please note its source when submitting. Download logos for the festival, SVA and SVA Theatre here. View examples of past winning videos here and here.


Upload submissions to Vimeo or YouTube, and send an email with a link to the video, your name, graduation year and program major to Adam Natale, director of SVA Theatre, at anatale@sva.edu, by Friday, August 25, 5:00pm. Email Natale with any questions. Final video format should be a digital file (e.g., QuickTime ProRes). The winner will receive $500.
