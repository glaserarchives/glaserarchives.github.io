Friday, January 21, and the Weekend

The Colorful World of Rich Tu
Big Wide World profiles Rich Tu (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay), vice president of digital design for the MTV Entertainment Group at ViacomCBS, who shares his experience growing up in the U.S. as an aspiring artist and living through Donald Trump’s presidential campaign as a first-generation immigrant.
Events

All Day | Booster Bus
Need a vaccine booster? All eligible SVA students, faculty and staff in need of a COVID-19 vaccine booster can get one today at the “booster bus,” provided by Student Health and Counseling Services and located in front of the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. 9:00am – 6:00pm. No appointments required.

Bring proof of prior vaccination and a photo ID (i.e., government-issued driver's license or passport). For questions about booster eligibility, click here.

2:00pm | Virtual Support Space for International Students: New Year’s Resolutions
Do you have a New Year's resolution that you want to share? Join Holly from Student Health and Counseling Services and Will from the International Student Office for a Spring 2022 Meet and Greet Virtual Support Space for international students. There will be resources offered as well as opportunities to connect with others, share your resolutions and an open forum for international students to ask any questions. Today, 2:00pm ET, on Zoom.

7:30pm | Free Screening: Spider-Man: No Way Home
SVA Theatre presents Spider-Man: No Way Home, starring Tom Holland. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Click here for SVA Theatre's current COVID-19 guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Tonight, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Knox Martin: Garden of Time
Presented by Hollis Taggart, “Knox Martin: Garden of Time” is an exhibition featuring Martin’s works inspired by nature from across over 50 years of his artistic career. Through Saturday, February 5. Hollis Taggart, 521 West 26th Street, ground floor.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
In celebration of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s birthday, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work reflecting on Dr. King’s legacy and life’s work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA’s observance of Black History Month in February. For more information or to submit your work, click here.

COVID-19 Reporting and Vaccine Booster Mandate
If you've tested positive for COVID-19, you must report your status to SVA as soon as possible. Students should email student-covid-alert@sva.edu; staff should email employee-covid-alert@sva.edu.

SVA also requires the vaccine booster for those who are eligible; proof of the booster must be uploaded to Cleared4 by Monday, January 24. Click here for more information about SVA's COVID-19 policies and protocols.
