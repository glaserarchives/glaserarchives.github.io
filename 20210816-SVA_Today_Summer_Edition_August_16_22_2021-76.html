EVENTS THIS WEEK

Wednesday | Internship-for-Credit Information Session for Fall 2021
Learn how you can qualify for the SVA Internship-for-Credit Program this fall. SVA Career Development and the International Student Office will cover the online application system, student enrollment criteria, the responsibilities of both students and employers, how to find and apply for an internship, special requirements for international students and deadlines and procedures for the fall semester. If you are an international student, attendance at this session is a requirement before submitting your application. Wednesday, August 18, 10:00 – 11:00am ET. Register here.

Opening Wednesday | MFA Visual Narrative Open Studios
The Open Studios show represents the culmination of the intensive summer semester at SVA's MFA Visual Narrative program. See this daring body of work by first- and second-year students, Wednesday, August 18 – Friday, September 17, at galleries.sva.edu.

Opening Wednesday | SVA: Making Art More Than Ever
This in-person exhibition of projections shows multidisciplinary works by 59 students and recent graduates. To give a historic contrast to these all-new works, the exhibition will also include a selection of popular SVA subway posters from the past six decades. Wednesday, August 18 – Thursday, September 9. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, August 19, 10:00am ET, after the Advocating for Your Career in the U.S. workshop. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Next Week | Summer Drawing Triathlon
This event combines three approaches to drawing in an intense three-hour session. Using wet and dry mediums with references from life, art history and imagination, participants will create over 100 drawings in one evening, led by visual artist and SVA faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). Presented by SVA Continuing Education and BFA Visual & Critical Studies. RSVP here to join on Zoom. Wednesday, August 25, 6:00 – 9:00pm ET.

New Course for International Students: English for Professional Communication
In this six-week online course, students will learn to write formal emails, participate in job and internship interviews, make small talk to network like a pro, and much more. Fluency and pronunciation will be an important part of this course as students develop communicative confidence.

Students can contact their academic advisor to sign up. For more information about this course, please contact Helene Rubinstein, associate chair of English as a Second Language, at hrubinstein@sva.edu.

There are two sessions of this course, taking place via Zoom, Monday, September 27 – Monday, November 1: Mondays, 9:00 – 11:00am ET (EVD0341A), and Mondays, 12:00 – 2:00pm (EVD0341B).

Hollaback! Trainings this Week
Thursday, August 19, 4:00pm ET
Eight Tools To Mitigate Implicit Bias
Everyone holds implicit biases. That doesn’t make us "bad people," it just means that we have work to do. This one-hour, interactive training will teach you how to understand and begin to mitigate your own implicit biases using eight tools. Register here.


Friday, August 20, 1:00pm ET
Resilience: This Moment And Beyond
The one-hour, interactive and experiential learning experience will teach you how to author your own resilience, using Hollaback!’s resilience methodology: sit with what is, create your story and be in choice. By taking this training you are making a choice to pursue resilience, and that’s a powerful first step. Register here.
