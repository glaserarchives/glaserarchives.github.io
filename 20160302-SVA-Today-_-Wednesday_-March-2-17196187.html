SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, March 2, 2016
Taking Humor Seriously
Last week, author Sloane Crosley spoke with MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism faculty member Adam Harrison Levy about using humor in her writing to find new perspectives and connect with people. Watch the conversation on SVA Close Up.
6:30pm: Billions
The Center for Communication and the SVA Theatre present a screening of "The Deal," an episode from the new Showtime series Billions, starring Paul Giamatti and Damian Lewis. A panel discussion featuring the show's producers will follow. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff; $10 general admission. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Damien by Namsa Leuba. Courtesy the artist and Art Twenty One, Lagos.
African Perspective
In Aperture, Emmanuel Iduma (MFA 2015 Art Writing and Criticism) interviewed photographer Namsa Leuba, a graduate of SVA's Photo Global Program, about her work in advance of Leuba's upcoming presentation at the Armory Show's "Focus: African Perspectives."
NYC picks of the day
VOLTA NY
A showcase of contemporary art from emerging international artists. One-day passes for students and seniors are $20, $25 general admission. Through Sunday, March 6, Pier 90, 50th Street at 12th Avenue.
SPRING/BREAK Art Show
A curator-driven art fair featuring "Material Pressure," curated by Andrea McGinty (MFA 2014 Fine Arts) with work by SVA alumni, and "FAQ," an exhibition of work by David Mramor (MFA 2008 Fine Arts). Advance tickets are $10, $15 at the door. Through Monday, March 7, Skylight at Moynihan Station, 421 Eighth Avenue.
Take Note
Adam Peacock: "Machines for Automating Taste, a New Meritocracy"
The Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence Adam Peacock discussing and presenting a preview of his new exhibition, "Machines for Automating Taste: A New Meritocracy." Thursday, March 3, 7:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
Kanopy Database Trial
The SVA Library now offers trial access to the video streaming database Kanopy, a Netflix-like user experience with a selection of over 26,000 documentaries, feature films and training videos. The trial ends on Friday, March 25. To access the database, visit library.sva.edu.
