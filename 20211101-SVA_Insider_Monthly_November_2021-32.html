🎙️ Get to Know: Robert O'Loughlin, Director of Facilities

Congratulations on your new role! Tell us about your department and what this new position entails.

SVA’s Facilities department is responsible for the appearance, repair and upkeep of all of the buildings, mechanicals and cleanliness of all SVA properties. Our staff takes great pride in ensuring everybody's safety and comfort while in any SVA building. We work 24/7 and have been on-site throughout the pandemic. My new position requires me to direct the maintenance staff in ensuring that our campus is as safe and clean as possible for our students, residents, faculty and staff.


What was the preparation process like to create a safe and welcoming environment in SVA buildings for this very different school year?

Preparing the campus for this year was different due to the pandemic. Aside from the usual repairs and cleaning we normally do, we had the added responsibility of ensuring that all areas were properly disinfected according to the CDC guidelines. Working together with other departments, we needed to develop new procedures, policies and signage for anyone who was coming on campus to follow that was clear, concise and easy to follow. We also needed to stock and supply all buildings with materials and supplies that were never used on campus before.


Is there any new technology or platform you’ve adopted to help manage safety protocols for residences or other SVA facilities this year?

In order to manage the new safety protocols across campus we need to constantly monitor the CDC and other governmental guidelines to make sure we’re compliant. We also need to make sure we always have the latest sanitizing equipment and train our maintenance technicians on its proper usage.


In what ways were you challenged to rethink the ways the SVA community uses on-campus facilities for 2021 – 2022?

We needed to come up with a cap on the amount of students and staff that could occupy an area at the same time while being socially distant. We also had to keep in mind that this is a college where some students like to gather together in common areas to discuss classes and critique each other’s work. A lot of work went into and continues to go into keeping our campus operational, especially in areas that nobody sees. Without the support and hard work of our maintenance staff, this job would be a lot more difficult.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Introduction to Gratitude Meditation

In this audio-only course, instructor Jack Kornfield leads you through a gratitude meditation aimed at awakening a joyful freedom, one of the goals of a spiritual life.


Painting Foundations: Creating Palettes for the Landscape

The course teaches painting techniques for quickly capturing the mood of a landscape throughout the four seasons, from a vibrant summer's day to a muted winter snow scene.


Improve Cognitive Flexibility at Work

In this course, clinical psychologist and best-selling author Andrea Bonior teaches how to build daily mental habits to increase adaptability and clear-thinking, as well as practice incorporating innovation into your daily life.
