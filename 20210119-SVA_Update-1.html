I hope this email finds you and your family safe and healthy. 

 

On May 11, 2020, I informed the SVA Community that due to the COVID-19 pandemic requiring the suspension of in-person instruction, several cost-cutting measures had to be taken to mitigate the effects of the decline in revenues.  They were:

 

1.     A hiring freeze to 6/30/21.

2.     Deferred salary increases for the fiscal year 2020-21.

3.     A suspension of SVA’s matching contributions to the 401(k) plan.

4.     A hold on capital projects that were not related to health and safety.

 

We had hoped that those measures would be the only ones necessary to deal with the downturn in revenue.  That was not the case.  On November 1, 2020, the College instituted tiered progressive salary reductions for all employees who work a full calendar year.  

 

We have proceeded very cautiously but, unfortunately, the downward trend in revenue has continued into the spring 2021 semester and we now project a deficit of $36.2 million for the fiscal year ending 6/30/2021.  These losses required us to furlough some employees as of January 15, 2021. This is a cost-cutting measure we hoped we would never have to take, but it became necessary to help SVA get through this extremely challenging economic time.  

 

We hope that as we get closer to the summer and fall of 2021, the current situation will take a turn for the better so no further cost-cut measures will be necessary, but that possibility cannot be ruled out.

 

Thank you for your continued hard work in helping SVA get through this crisis.  Your efforts, dedication, and sacrifice are greatly appreciated.

 

Stay safe and well.


Sincerely,


David Rhodes
