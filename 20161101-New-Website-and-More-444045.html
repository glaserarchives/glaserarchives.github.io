Dear Friend,
A quick word to tell you that we’ve just launched our entirely new website! And it’s extraordinary to see how many remarkable curators, artists, institution directors, writers, art dealers, and other professionals have joined us (see Guests under the People link at the top of the new site’s screen). The site makes it easy to see what we have done, with archives of past exhibitions and events, as well as what’s coming up, along with every detail of the program, its courses, faculty, and students. The same address as before—macp.sva.edu—but a far richer and deeper resource for anyone interested in the program. 
 
And as part of the program and our commitment to connect with the global arts community, we’re excited to have launched “Local Address,” an ongoing series of panel discussions and conversations that bring together remarkable speakers from abroad to discuss the political, social, and economic urgencies that impact cultural production in different parts of the world. Our first discussion focused on China, featuring Philip Tinari, director of the Ullens Center for Contemporary Art, Beijing, with Pi Li, senior curator at M+, Hong Kong, and Xiaoyu Weng, an associate curator of Chinese art at the Guggenheim Museum. In the coming months, we’ll have extraordinary speakers from Colombia, South Africa, the Caribbean region, and Lebanon. These first-person narratives bring to our students and our broad New York audience invaluable insights into cultural and world politics that we’re tremendously proud to present. 
 
And a last note: We’re thrilled to announce two new faculty members, Christiane Paul and Laurel Ptak. Christiane is the longstanding adjunct curator of new media arts at the Whitney Museum and a world-renowned expert in the field. Her most recent book is A Companion to Digital Art in the series Blackwell Companions to Art History. Laurel is the director of the artist-run space in lower Manhattan, Triangle Arts Association. She was named one of the 100 Leading Global Thinkers in 2014 by Foreign Policy magazine and the prior year published as co-editor with Marysia Lewandowska the book Undoing Property from Sternberg Press. You can find out more about them on our new site!
  
Please visit our site and, if you’re in New York, come by to see one of our exhibitions and learn more about the program. Applications season is soon beginning and we’re happy to answer any questions you may have. See you soon,
Steven Henry Madoff
