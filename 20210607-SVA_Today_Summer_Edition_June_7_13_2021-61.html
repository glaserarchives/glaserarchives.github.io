EVENTS THIS WEEK

Closing Today | Finding Space
A juried exhibition of multidisciplinary works by SVA students that re-examines the role of space and proximity during aberrant times. Organized by SVA Galleries, the exhibition is on view online through today, June 7, at galleries.sva.edu.

Tuesday | i3 Photo Lecture: Alyssa Coppelman
Alyssa Coppelman is an Austin-based photo editor and photo book consultant who also works as an art researcher with Harper's Magazine and Oxford American. Presented by MPS Digital Photography. Tuesday, June 8, 7:00 – 8:30pm ET. Join on Zoom.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, June 10, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Opening Thursday | Here Together
This exhibition of thesis projects by the MFA Fine Arts class of 2021 explores with criticality and empathy the profound interconnectedness of the world we live in. Curated by independent curator María del Carmen Carrión, the exhibition will open with an all-day reception on Thursday, June 10, 11:00am – 8:00pm ET, and be on view through Sunday, June 20. 79 Warren Street.

Opening Thursday | a throw of the dice will never abolish chance
An exhibition of multidisciplinary works by 10 artists from the Residency Alumni Network at SVA. Curated by visual artist and Summer Residency faculty member Andrea Champlin, the exhibition will be on view from Thursday, June 10, through Wednesday, July 7, at the SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Friday | Shifting the Narrative: Megan Izen
Megan Izen, managing editor of Word Force at Narrative Initiative, will explore what narrative is, the role artists play in shaping narratives and how your art can make change. Presented by MFA Visual Narrative. Friday, June 11, 1:30 – 3:00pm ET. RSVP here.

Closing Soon | Before We Begin, Again
In this exhibition of their thesis work, selected MFA Computer Arts 2021 graduates reconsider histories and memories, while creating space for possible worlds that have yet to exist. On view through Saturday, June 12, online at galleries.sva.edu.

Early Voting in in the New York City Primary Elections
Early voting in New York City starts Saturday, June 12. Click here to look up your early voting location. If you’re planning on voting by mail in the New York Primary Elections you must request an absentee ballot. This year, New York City will be introducing the new ranked-choice voting systems; click here for more information. For a complete list of candidates on this year’s ballot, visit whosontheballot.org.

Election Dates and Deadlines for the NYC Primary Election:
  • Early Voting Dates: June 12 – 20
  • Absentee Voting Application Deadline: Tuesday, June 15
  • Primary Elections: Tuesday, June 22
For additional voting resources visit sva.edu/studentaffairs. Contact SVA Student Affairs if you have any questions; call 212.592.2214 from Monday – Friday, 9:00am – 5:00pm, or email studentaffairs@sva.edu.

Show Your #SVAPride
#SVAPride buttons are now available at security desks across SVA campus! Pick yours up in-person today or email Adam Krumm, akrumm@sva.edu, to get them delivered to your department's office on-campus. Off-campus delivery is not available.

New GoSVA Mobile App
SVA IT is pleased to announce that the entirely redesigned GoSVA mobile app is now available on the Apple App Store for iPhone and Google Play Store for Android. This new mobile app provides on-the-go access to all SVA essential systems and information for students, faculty, staff and visiting guests. If you have the prior version of the GoSVA app installed on your device please uninstall it before installing this new version. More information and links to the app stores can be found at go.sva.edu. An installation guide is available at technology.sva.edu.

The National Conference of Race and Ethnicity 2021
The National Conference of Race and Ethnicity (NCORE) is the premier national DEI resource for higher-education institutions, providing an annual diverse and inclusive forum that welcomes higher-education professionals as well as undergraduate and graduate students. The regular conference will be held in a virtual format Tuesday, June 8 – Friday, June 11 (with a Pre-Conference Institute taking place today, June 7). Click here to register for $100 – $350.
