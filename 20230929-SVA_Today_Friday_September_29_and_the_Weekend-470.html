NYC PICK

City Kids Sketch Night

A figure drawing night featuring New York City-based artists who will be modeling their performance looks during a DJ set highlighting the models’ original music. $10 for undergrad students. Tonight, September 29, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.

AAAAH!

A two-day event celebrating the diversity and creativity of the Asian community through art exhibitions, a design fair and live performances led by MFA 2023 Design for Social Innovation alumni Effy Tan and Mouhui Sun. Free. Saturday, September 30, 11:00am – 8:00pm. 68 Franklin Street.

Making Music With Code

The hacker collective NYC Resistor hosts this three-hour workshop exploring how to create music with code, using a web-based tool called Strudel, with instructors Dan Gorelick and MFA Interaction Design faculty member Viola He. Tickets start at $30. Saturday, September 30, 12:00 – 3:00pm. NYC Resistor, 87 Third Avenue, 4th floor, Brooklyn.

“Intercodex”

A solo exhibition of work by former faculty member Emily Cheng, who combines semiotics and visual imagery to portray spiritual connections between humanity, nature and the cosmos. Through Saturday, October 28. Village One Art, 150 West 25th Street.

Fall 2023 Voter Registration Drive Begins Today

SVA’s fall 2023 Voter registration drive begins today, September 29, and runs through Friday, October 20. All who are eligible to vote in New York but are not yet registered are encouraged to sign up in time for this November’s elections and have their voices heard.

Registrar’s Office, 205 East 23rd Street

Monday – Friday, 9:00am – 5:00pm


SVA Student Affairs, 340 East 24th Street

Monday – Friday, 9:00am – 5:00pm


Visit sva.edu/studentaffairs for information about times and other details, as well as links to voter-registration sites for Connecticut and New Jersey residents.

This Weekend | Myco Film Festival

The New York Mycological Society, founded by John Cage, presents the inaugural Myco Film Festival—an evening of short fiction, VR, documentary, animation and experimental films illuminating the fascinating world of fungi and exploring mycology in bold new ways. SVA community members can enter the code “SVA” to receive a $5 discount off tickets (you must use your sva.edu email address to qualify for the discount). Sunday, October 1, 5:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Next Week | Sound Recording for Feature Films With Mark Ulano and Patrushkha Mierzwa

BFA Film and MPS Film Directing present Academy Award-winning sound recordist Mark Ulano (1975 Film and Video), known for his work on Titanic, and boom operator Patrushka Mierzwa to discuss their aesthetic approach to sound recording for feature films and their collaborations with the production crew. Monday, October 2, 1:00 – 2:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.

Next Week | Talk With Joseph Kai, Author of the Queer Lebanese Graphic Memoir Restless

BFA Illustration and the Comics Visual Arts Library present a talk with Joseph Kai, author of Restless, the new graphic novel that meditates on the anxieties of queer sex, political revolution and chosen family. Tuesday, October 3, 6:30pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

2023 Annual Safety and Security Reports

SVA is committed to the safety and security of all members of the College community. In accordance with the federal Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act of 1990, the Annual Safety and Security Report (ASSR), the Annual Fire Safety Report (AFSR) and the Campus Crime Statistics for this year are now available. These reports contain statistics and institutional policies concerning fire safety, as well as information on campus security issues such as sexual assault and other matters. Copies of these reports can be accessed in the Health and Safety section of the SVA Consumer Information webpage here. Contact SVA Institutional Research, 212.592.2829, with any questions regarding this information.
