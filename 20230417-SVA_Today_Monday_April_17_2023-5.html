6:30pm | Carmen Screening and Director Q&A

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of Carmen, the feature directorial debut of acclaimed choreographer Benjamin Millepied starring Melissa Barrera, Oscar nominee Paul Mescal and Rossy de Palma. Millepied’s reimagining of Carmen tells its story through an experimental dreamscape featuring an original score by Nicholas Britell and original songs by Britell, Tracy ‘the D.O.C.’ Curry, Taura Stinson and Julieta Venegas. There will be a Q&A with Millepied following the screening. Registration is required and attendees must present their SVA ID at the door (non-SVA guests are permitted if accompanied by an attendee with an SVA ID); RSVP here. Watch the trailer here. Tonight, April 17, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Wednesday | Plan 75 Screening and Director Q&A

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of Plan 75, starring Japanese actress Chieko Baisho and directed by Chie Hayakawa (BFA 2001 Photography). Plan 75 tells the story of three ordinary citizens whose lives intersect in a near dystopian future where Japan’s government launches Plan 75, a program encouraging the elderly to terminate their own lives to relieve its rapidly aging population’s social and economic burdens. Watch the trailer here. There will be a Q&A with writer and director Hayakawa following the screening. Registration is required and attendees must present their SVA ID at the door (non-SVA guests are permitted if accompanied by an attendee with an SVA ID); RSVP here. Seating is limited. Wednesday, April 19, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

#SVAwesome

Two MFA Social Documentary Film cinematographers have their films nominated for Peabody Awards in the documentary category: Lucy & Desi and Aftershock. Faculty member Axel Baumann is cinematographer for Lucy & Desi, a film about TV’s most powerful couple in the 1950s, comedians Lucille Ball and Desi Arnaz; the film’s crew also includes additional editor Inaya Graciana Yusuf (2013). Aftershock was filmed by Jenni Morello (2011) and follows the story of two young black women who died from childbirth complications and how their families galvanize activists, birth workers and physicians to face America’s grave maternal health crisis. Aftershock’s crew also includes assistant producer Yuby Hernandez (2020). Via @svasocdoc.
