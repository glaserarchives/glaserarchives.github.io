News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
March 13 – 19, 2017

SVA at SXSW

A rundown of the many SVA community members participating in the 2017 South by Southwest Conference and Festival, happening through Sunday, March 19, in Austin, Texas.

"Underground Images" in South Africa

A recap of the most recent stop for "Underground Images," SVA’s globe-trotting exhibition of signature subway posters, at Design Indaba in Cape Town, South Africa.

A "Pretty/Dirty" Private Tour

An in-depth look at the private tour given by artist and SVA faculty member Marilyn Minter to a group of SVA alumni of her retrospective "Pretty/Dirty." The exhibition is on view through Sunday, April 2, at the Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
IN THE PRESS

"Video Games Matter"

The Jerusalem Post on MFA Design for Social Innovation faculty member Asi Burak's new book, Power Play: How Video Games Can Save the World.

"Dark Trouble Is Brewing"

Hyperallergic on Delano Dunn's (MFA 2016 Fine Arts) "challenging, surprising" work at Long Gallery in New York City.

A Star in Our Midst

Surface spotlights Star Montana (BFA 2013 Photography) and her portraits, which are shot in the homes and streets of Los Angeles’s Eastside.
EVENTS

Lizzie Widdicombe and Adam Harrison Levy

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a conversation between the Talk of the Town editor at The New Yorker, Lizzie Widdicombe, and department faculty member Adam Harrison Levy. RSVP here. Tonight, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

100 Years of Genius: The Life and Legacy of Will Eisner

SVA Library and SVA Cartoon Allies present comics artist and filmmaker Frank Miller and SVA faculty member Klaus Janson, who will discuss pioneering comics legend and former SVA faculty member Will Eisner. Tuesday, March 14, 7:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

My Entire High School Sinking into the Sea Screening

BFA Cartooning, BFA Illustration and SVA's Comix Weirdos present a screening of graphic novelist Dash Shaw's (BFA 2005 Illustration) feature film, followed by a Q&A with Shaw himself. Thursday, March 16, 7:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
