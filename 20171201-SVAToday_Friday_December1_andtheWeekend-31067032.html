SVA Today
Friday, December 1, 2017

Podcast Pros' Fave Shows

Some of SVA's podcast experts share their top picks for art, design and cultural audio programming.
EVENTS

Noon | MFA Computer Arts Fall Open Studio

MFA Computer Arts presents video, animation, audio and interactive installations and physical computing by its students. Noon – 5:00pm.133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor.

7:00pm | Views of Japan with Willard Huyck and Gloria Katz

A discussion and slideshow presentation with filmmakers and screenwriters Gloria Katz and Willard Huyck (American Graffiti, Indiana Jones and the Temple of Doom, French Postcards) about their new book, Views of Japan, a memoir about their love of Japanese photography and their experiences collecting it. Book signing and refreshments to follow. Free. RSVPs not accepted or required. 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | MFA Social Documentary Film Information Session

If you are interested in combining social activism and creativity and passionate about storytelling, MFA Social Documentary Film encourages you to attend its information session, where you will connect with the chair, faculty, staff and alumni from the program. RSVP here. Tomorrow, December 2, 11:00am – 1:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
PRESS PICK
Rachel King, A World of Devices, 2017. From "2017 America."

Capturing America in 2017

The Week on BFA Photography and Video's exhibition "2017 America," which features photos by high school students that "take an artfully direct approach to social issues." On view through tomorrow, December 2, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery.
NYC PICKS

Day With(out) Art 2017

To commemorate the 28th annual Day With(out) Art on World AIDS Day, Visual AIDS presents screenings of the video program Alternate Endings, Radical Beginnings. Click here for a schedule of all the screenings in New York City.

New Museum 40th Anniversary Weekend

Free admission and extended hours (10:00am – 8:00pm) at the New Museum in celebration of its 40th anniversary. Throughout the weekend there will also be a selection of public conversations with artists. Schedule here. Tickets $5. Saturday, December 2 – Sunday, December 3. New Museum, 235 Bowery.

Grand Bazaar NYC

A market featuring wares by local artists, designers and vintage dealers. Sundays, 10:00am – 5:30pm. 100 West 77th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Student Leadership Series: Time and Stress Management Workshop

The end of the semester can be challenging. Join Kayla Vogel and Christine Gilchrist from SVA Student Affairs to talk about some helpful strategies and tips for time and stress management. Contact activities@sva.edu for more information. Tuesday, December 5, 6:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Lissa Rivera

MPS Digital Photography presents photographer and curator Lissa Rivera (MFA 2009 Photography, Video and Related Media), best known for her series "Beautiful Boy." Part of the i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tuesday, December 5, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Concepts of Art and Play Therapy with Children Using Diverse Communication

As part of its Community Lecture Series, MPS Art Therapy presents a talk by Sarah Valeri, MA, ATR-BC, LCAT, about exploring the range of kinesthetic, relational and aesthetic communication in art therapy sessions for children with developmental delays. Friday, December 8, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

The Shape of Water Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Guillermo del Toro's The Shape of Water, starring Sally Hawkins and Michael Shannon. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Saturday, December 9, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
