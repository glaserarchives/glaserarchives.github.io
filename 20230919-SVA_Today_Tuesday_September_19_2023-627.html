“Dear Ayandeh: Open Letters to a Free Iran” at SVA

The SVA Flatiron Project Space held an exhibition this weekend featuring notes of support for Iranians protesting for freedom, in partnership with demonstrations commemorating the life of Mahsa (Zhina) Amini. Via Hyperallergic.

EVENTS

5:00pm | The Artists Roundtable: Josh Kline (New York)

MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with Josh Kline, an artist best known for creating immersive installations—using video, sculpture, photography and design—that question how emergent technologies are being used to change human life in the 21st century. Tonight, September 19, 5:00 – 6:00pm, online.

6:30pm | Kay Gabriel: A Queen in Bucks County

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the SVA Honors Program present poet and professor Kay Gabriel to speak about and read from her new book, A Queen in Bucks County, an epistolary sequence about sex, exchange and social space set along the Northeast Corridor. Tonight, September 19, 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street.

7:30pm | The Nun II Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of the new horror film The Nun II. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Tuesday, September 19, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
