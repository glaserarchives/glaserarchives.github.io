SVA Today
Thursday, April 21, 2016
Rendering by Ailin Li
BFA Interior Design Thesis Reception
An exhibition of thesis projects by graduating students, including an adaptive redesign of a Typhoon nuclear submarine, an assisted living facility in Brooklyn and a no-kill dog shelter community center. Reception tonight, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Friday, April 22, Art Directors Club, 106 West 29th Street.
6:30pm: Owen Gleiberman
The SVA Library presents the former Entertainment Weekly film critic discussing and signing copies of his new book, Movie Freak: My Life Watching Movies. The first 30 guests will receive a free copy of the book. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
7:00pm: NY Photo Salon
MPS Digital Photography presents photographers Elias Weiss Friedman, Kah Poon, Lucy Schaeffer and Ellen Wallenstein discussing their work. Each will answer questions following their 20-minute presentations. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
A Steven Universe Children's Book
The Los Angeles Times reports that Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation) has written a children's book based on her Cartoon Network series.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY
Lee Krasner
An exhibition spanning the artist's five-decade career. Reception tonight, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 4, Robert Miller Gallery, 524 West 26th Street.
Malcolm Lightner and Mile O' Mud
The BFA Photography and Video faculty member and director of operations celebrates the release of his new monograph, Mile O' Mud. 7:00pm, powerHouse Arena, 37 Main Street, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE
Ecuador, Japan Earthquake Relief Efforts
MFA Design for Social Innovation students are raising funds for victims of Ecuador's April 16 earthquake. All contributions will be donated to the Red Cross in Ecuador. Donations here. For more information, contact Carmen Lopez at clopez6@sva.edu, Rodrigo Muñoz at rmunoz2@sva.edu or Andrea Cisneros Ruiz at acisnerosruiz@sva.edu. The International Student Office is organizing a separate relief effort for victims of the recent earthquake in Kumamoto, Japan. For more information, contact Yoko Anderson at yanderson@sva.edu.
Massage Chair and Dog Therapy
VASA presents two days of relaxation for students, with massage chair therapy on Tuesday, April 26, 4:00 - 7:00pm, and dog therapy on Wednesday, April 27, 1:00 - 2:00pm, at the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Deborah Willis
MPS Digital Photography presents curator, writer, artist and educator Deborah Willis, one of the nation’s leading historians of African American photography and culture, discussing her work. Tuesday, April 26, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F. Willis's lecture will be also simulcast on Youtube. Please log-in at 6:50pm EST on Tuesday, April 26th.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
BFA Illustration student Toma Vagner's interpretation of Hans Christian Andersen's fairy tale "The Naked Emperor," which is on view in "Poisoned Apples and Smoking Lamps," at the SVA Chelsea Gallery through Saturday, April 23. Image via Tumblr.
