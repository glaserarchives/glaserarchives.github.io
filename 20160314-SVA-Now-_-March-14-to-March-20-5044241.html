SVA Now
March 14 - 20, 2016
Features
When in Rome...
Interested in typography, book and lettering design, or epigraphy? Find out more about this SVA's design workship in Rome, as well as other SVA Destination programs, such as printmaking in Havana, painting in Barcelona and digital illustration in Berlin.
Inside Disney's Zootopia
Disney animator Darrin Butters recently visited BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects to discuss working on the new film Zootopia. Student Mitchell Jao wrote about Butters' talk for SVA Close Up.
SVA at PULSE New York
The SVA Galleries booth at the PULSE Contemporary Art Fair in New York, held March 3-6, featured work by SVA graduate and undergraduate students. Here are some highlights from a few of the artists whose work was on view.
EVENTS
Photograph by Saul Leiter.
Margit Erb
MPS Digital Photography presents Margit Erb, founder and director of the Saul Leiter Foundation, which is dedicated to preserving the art and legacy of the influential New York City photographer and painter, discussing the 18 years she assisted Leiter. Tuesday, March 15, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Help Is on the Way!
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present a satirical “museum” of vehicles designed in an attempt to help others, including a 1956 hot dog vendor’s cart, a Harley Davidson motorcycle, bicycles and a 1940s wooden canoe. Curated by Kevin O’Callaghan, chair of 3D Design. Reception Wednesday, March 23, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Wednesday, March 16, through Saturday, April 9, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
New York Fashion Film Festival
MPS Fashion Photography presents the sixth annual screening of fashion films that recognizes outstanding creative work and encourages discourse on the nature of the genre, its growing relevance to the world of business and fashion and the potential for fashion film to be a platform for invention, creativity and cultural inquiry. Thursday, March 17, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
IN THE PRESS
Still from Orisha’s Journey. Courtesy of Abdul Ndadi.
Breaking Down Stereotypes
Okay Africa spotlights Orisha's Journey, an animated short film by Abdul Ndadi (BFA 2013 Animation) inspired by African folktales.
Loss, Pain and Family Secrets in East L.A.
L.A. Weekly highlights "Tear Drops and Three Dots," a new exhibition of photographs by Star Montana (BFA 2013 Photography), now on view at the Vincent Price Museum.
Turning Snacks into Art
Interview profiles Carly Mark (BFA 2011 Fine Arts), whose exhibition of food label-inspired work was featured at this month's Armory Show.
