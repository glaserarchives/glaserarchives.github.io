SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, November 18, 2015
Essential Tips for Emerging Artists
SVA Close Up spoke to SVA Career Development Director Angie Wojak about her new book, co-authored with Parsons School of Design faculty member Stacy Miller, Starting Your Career as an Artist: A Guide to Launching a Creative Life (Allworth Press/SVA). Wojak and Miller will lead a panel discussion about social media best practices for artists tonight, 6:30pm, CUE Art Foundation, 137 West 25th Street.
10:00am: Fall Book Sale
The SVA Library presents a fall book sale, featuring books on art, design, criticism and more. All books are priced $20 and under. Credit and debit card purchases only. Sale hours are 10:00am - 6:00pm, through Friday, November 20, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
6:00pm: In-House Perspectives
InSource presents three discussions on the unique challenges faced by in-house creative teams and professionals. Speakers include Ilana Greenberg, creative director, Graphic Design USA; David Lesué, creative director, Workfront; and Martin Schott, director, creative services, Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. Free for SVA students with code "InSourceSVAFree." General admission tickets are $55. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:00pm: Anomalisa
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of writer-director Charlie Kaufman's Anomalisa, co-directed by Duke Johnson and starring Jennifer Jason Leigh, Tom Noonan and David Thewlis. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). RSVP required. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Illustrating Arthur Miller's Dissonance
The New York Times highlights an illustration of playwright Arthur Miller by BFA Illustration faculty member Riccardo Vecchio (MFA 1996 Illustration as Visual Essay). The work adorns the new collected edition of Miller's plays, published by Penguin to commemorate the writer's centenary.
NYC picks of the day
National Lampoon with Steven Heller
MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller moderates a discussion between three National Lampoon artists and writers on the satiric magazine's cultural legacy. Admission is free. 6:00pm, Glenn Horowitz Bookseller Penthouse, 20 West 55th Street.
¡Presente!: The Young Lords in New York
An exhibition highlighting the work of activist group The Young Lords, featuring commissioned artwork by MFA Fine Arts faculty member Miguel Luciano. $5 for students and seniors, $9 general admission. Through December 12, El Museo del Barrio, 1230 Fifth Avenue.
Take Note
Axure Meetup
MFA Interaction Design student David Mahmarian is starting a local chapter of the Axure Meetup, a group dedicated to the interactive prototyping tool that allows users to create prototypes without coding, with varying levels of fidelity. The output can be something basic that works in a browser, or a fully functional, more complex prototype that passes variables and uses conditional logic. No prior knowledge of coding is required to join the group. The first group meeting takes place tonight, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
Robin Winters
MFA Art Practice presents artist and SVA faculty member Robin Winters discussing his ongoing exhibition, "Free Standing Sentence." Saturday, November 21, 6:00pm, Present Company, 254 Johnson Avenue, Brooklyn.
