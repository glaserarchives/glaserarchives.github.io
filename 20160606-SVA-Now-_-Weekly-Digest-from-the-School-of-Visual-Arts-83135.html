SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts June 6 - 12, 2016
FEATURES
The Keyboard Waffle Iron, by Chris Dimino (BFA 2002 Graphic Design)
7 Things You'll See at Makers Market
Get a sneak peek at some of the goods available at this weekend's Makers Market, a showcase of SVA alumni-made products such as a keyboard-shaped waffle iron, handmade animal pillows, an interactive cloud lamp, and more. For more information and a participating vendor list, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
A Relationship in Transition
Watch Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography) discuss her recently published book, Relationship, a collaboration with artist Rhys Ernst that documents their six-year partnership. Drucker and Ernst will be at Strand Books in New York on Thursday, June 9, to discuss the book.
The May Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios in May include an illustrated look at blind faith, a creative and colorful binding project and stunning fashion photography.
Cover Stories: Steven Millhauser's Voices in the Night
Janet Hansen (BFA 2007 Graphic Design) talks about her cover design for the Pulitzer Prize-winning author's latest collection of short stories, which was named by The New York Times as a "Best Cover of 2015."
IN THE PRESS
Photo by Alex Welsh for The New York Times
"A Labor of Love"
The New York Times profiles MFA Art Practice faculty member Lia Gangitano, founder of alternative art space Participant, Inc.
An Exclusive Look at Revengeance
Cartoon Brew premieres the trailer for Revengeance, the latest film by animator and SVA alumnus Bill Plympton.
8-Bit Watercolors
Creative Boom highlights the Atari-inspired paintings of Adam Lister (BFA 2003 Fine Arts), which capture iconic scenes from famous movies.
EVENTS
Bon Duke
MPS Digital Photography presents photographer and director Bon Duke (BFA 2005 Photography, MPS 2012 Fashion Photography) discussing his work. Tuesday, June 7, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Storytellers Lecture Series: Broken Frontier Anthology
MFA Visual Narrative presents a panel discussion moderated by Broken Frontier Anthology co-editor and contributor Tyler Chin-Tanner on how to curate an anthology, fund a project through Kickstarter, manage contributors, and more. Panelists include letterer Taylor Esposito, MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox, writer Greg Pak, artist Tom Raney and writer Fred Van Lente. Thursday, June 9, 6:30pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Makers Market
A fair showcasing alumni-made products such as apparel, jewelry and accessories; furniture, housewares and decorative objects; and stationery, self-published books, magazines and comics. For more information, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
404: IRL Not Found
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents an exhibition of work by students Adam Cable, Sullivan Gardner and Tim Hodge that considers how images within the digital living and work space can combine into a single viewpoint based on a viewer's perspective and proximity. Through Wednesday, June 29, SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
