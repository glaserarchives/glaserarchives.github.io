FEATURE

SVA Guide to Flatiron

Following our guide to Chelsea, here are a few suggested places to visit, things to do and foods to eat in the Flatiron District. Stay tuned for more New York City neighborhood guides.
Events

The Book Show

MFA Illustration as Visual Essay presents its annual "Book Show" exhibition, featuring publications illustrated and written by members of the class of 2017. Reception Monday, September 19, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition opens today, and runs through Thursday, September 29, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

12:15pm: Mid-Autumn Festival

The International Student Office, Student Health and Counseling Services and the SVA Chinese Students Organization present a festival with Korean traditional dance, music performances, calligraphy and fan painting. There will also be Chinese mooncake, tea tasting and Japanese food. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Saturday: "The Biophilia Hypothesis"

BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work inspired by biologist E. O. Wilson's belief that human beings share an instinctive bond with other living systems. Curated by department chair Suzanne Anker. Reception Wednesday, September 21, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Saturday, October 15, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Saturday: DJ Set by April Hunt

As part of "The Beat Goes On," April Hunt performs a DJ set that weaves through various genres and periods. 4:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Saturday: Stephan Frailey in Conversation with Ellen Carey

As part of "n e w f l e s h," BFA Photography and Video Chair and DEAR DAVE, Editor-in-Chief Stephen Frailey talks with photographer Ellen Carey. 4:00pm, Rubber Factory, 29 Ludlow Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

Designing for Death

In Core 77, MFA Products of Design Chair Allan Chochinov talks with Natsuki Hayashi (MFA 2016 Products of Design) about her thesis project, which investigated assisted suicide techniques.
NYC WEEKEND PICKS

New York Coffee Festival

A celebration of all things coffee, with tastings, demonstrations and competitions. Tickets are $20. Through Sunday, September 18, 69th Regiment Armory, 68 Lexington Avenue.

NY Art Book Fair

The 11th annual fair of artists’ books, catalogs, monographs, periodicals and zines, featuring over 370 booksellers, antiquarians, artists, institutions and independent publishers. Don't miss the SVA table! Through Sunday, September 18, MoMA PS1, 22-25 Jackson Avenue, Queens.

Brooklyn Book Festival

A daylong event featuring panels, discussions and author talks. Sunday, September 18, 10:00am - 6:00pm, multiple locations.
TAKE NOTE

Constitution Day

Saturday, September 17, is Constitution Day. To commemorate the occasion, click here to learn more about the history of the Constitution and its meaning today with an interactive guide and insight from scholars.

A Theater and a Battleground: Professions of Childhood in the Arts and Public Policy of 1940s New York

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents Sean McCann, author and professor of English at Wesleyan University, discussing urban childhood and adolescence in 1940s New York City, the political and historical issues behind urban policy of the time and why artists became fascinated with stories and images of children in the city. Monday, September 19, 6:30pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Mobilizing to Defeat Climate Change

The New Republic presents three of the nation’s leading environmental activists—writer and organizer Bill McKibben; Reverend Lennox Yearwood Jr., environmental campaigner and president of Hip Hop Caucus; and Elizabeth Yeampierre, environmental justice attorney and executive director of UPROSE—discussing the social, political and biological impacts of climate change. Admission is free. Tuesday, September 20, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

The Chemistry of Anxiety

Student Health and Counseling Services presents a discussion about the causes of stress and anxiety, and how it affects our bodies and minds and can interfere with the creative process. Open to SVA students. Wednesday, September 21, 3:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
