SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, October 30, 2015
SVA Halloween 2015
Last week, VASA and WSVA co-hosted their annual Halloween party, and SVA Close Up got a firsthand look at the student costumes on display.
8:00pm: Crimson Peak
The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of director Guillermo Del Toro's Crimson Peak, starring Mia Wasikowska, Jessica Chastain, Tom Hiddleston and Charlie Hunnam. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
A Russian Illustrator at Home (and at Work)
T magazine profiles children's book writer and illustrator Dasha Tolstikova (MFA 2012 Illustration as Visual Essay) at her home studio in Greenpoint, Brooklyn.
NYC Weekend Picks
Frank Stella: A Retrospective
A career-spanning exhibition of the artist's work, including paintings, reliefs, maquettes, sculptures and drawings. Stella will speak about his work and American art on Tuesday, December 1. More information here. Admission is free with SVA ID. Through February 2016. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
Elise Engler: A Year on Broadway
An exhibition of gouache, watercolor and colored pencil works created by the SVA faculty member during a year-long project to document every block of Broadway in Manhattan. Reception tonight from 6:00 - 9:00pm. Through December 20, Robert Henry Contemporary, 56 Bogart Street, Brooklyn.
Luminous Mischief
Choreographer Stephen Petronio and his company present a free-form dance performance beneath artist Teresita Fernández's installation Fata Morgana. 6:00 - 7:00pm, Madison Square Park, Broadway at 23rd Street.
Rocky Horror Picture Show
A free screening of the cult classic musical, starring Susan Sarandon, Tim Curry and Meat Loaf. 6:00pm, McCarren Park Pool Deck, Brooklyn.
Village Halloween Parade
Dress up and march in the 42nd annual costume parade. Saturday, October 31, 7:00 - 10:30pm, Sixth Avenue north from Spring Street to 16th Street. Participants enter the parade at Canal Street.
Take Note
Discounted Movie Ticket Sales Suspended Until Wednesday
Discounted AMC movie tickets sales are suspended until Wednesday, November 4. Sales will resume beginning at 2:00pm at the Student Engagement and Leadership Office, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine.
Discounted Jets Tickets Available
Discounted tickets to the New York Jets-Buffalo Bills football game go on sale at 2:00pm on Monday, November 2. The game is scheduled for Thursday, November 12, at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey. Tickets are $20 with SVA ID (limit one per person). Credit and debit card purchases only. Tickets can be purchased at the Student Engagement and Leadership Office from 9:00am - 5:00pm at the George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine.
MA Critical Theory and the Arts Open House
MA Critical Theory and the Arts presents an open house for prospective students, with department chair Robert Hullot-Kentor and other faculty members discussing the program. Wednesday, November 4, 5:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
Spectator Under Siege
MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on the role of exhibition makers and the politics of curating in the image dominant era of the Internet. Participants include Sofia Hernandez Chong Cuy, Jens Hoffmann, Maria Lind and Raimundas Malasauskas. Moderated by department chair Steven Henry Madoff. Thursday, November 5, 7:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
MFA Computer Art Information Session
MFA Computer Art presents an information session for prospective students featuring students, alumni and faculty discussing their work and experience in the department. Saturday, November 7, 2:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
