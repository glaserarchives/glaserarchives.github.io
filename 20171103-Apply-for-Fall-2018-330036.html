David Osit ('11) Wins an Emmy for
Thank You For Playing

We are thrilled to announce that Soc Doc alumnus David Osit (Class of 2011, pictured here with his co-director, Malika Zouhali-Worrall and POV's Chris White) has received an Emmy for Outstanding Arts & Culture Documentary for his film Thank You For Playing.  The film was also nominated in two other categories, and was broadcast on PBS “P.O.V.” in 2016. David is our first SocDoc alum to win an Emmy! Kudos!

Thank You For Playing is now streaming & downloadable on your favorite digital platforms. For more with David, watch this interview, part of our ongoing series of Alumni Profiles.

 

DOC NYC: SocDoc Films & Panels

Thrilled that DOC NYC (November 9-16, 2017) has chosen 13 films from our students & faculty! Our students and faculty will be attending the Doc NYC Pro Conference, and taking in many films.

Of note: SocDoc Showcase of student films; 32 Pills: My Sister's Suicide (producer Beth Levison, Editor Toby Shimin, both faculty; alumni crew); Cradle of Champions (DP Tom Hurwitz, ASC, faculty; alumni crew); Maynard and Sammy Davis Jr.,: I've Gotta Be Me (both directed by Samuel D. Pollard, faculty); One Of Us (DP Jenni Morello, alumna); Sky and Ground (DP Axel Baumann, faculty; Producer Maro Chermayeff, Dept. Chair) and others!

AND - Faculty Ross Kauffman, Samuel D. Pollard, Rose Vincelli Gustine, and alumna Jenni Morello are on panels during the Doc NYC Pro Conference. Huge congrats to Producing faculty Thom Powers on the line up - he is Artistic Director and co-founder of DOC NYC.

More info on these films and others on our blog. Hope to see you at some of the incredible films our alumni and faculty are working on!

Prospective Students: 
Attend Fall Process & Style Classes

 

If you're interested in joining SVA's MFA Social Documentary Film program, we encourage you to come by for a tour of the department, and to sit in on a class, our weekly screening series, Process & Style. Screenings are followed by in-depth Q&A with the filmmakers. Screenings are only for SocDoc students and prospective students. Check out the full line up and find info on how to attend here.

Not in NYC? Watch videos of our recent discussions about Hooligan Sparrow, PBS' music series Soundbreaking, and Masterclasses from Michael Apted (the 7 Up series) and Marcel Ophuls (The Sorrow and the Pity).
