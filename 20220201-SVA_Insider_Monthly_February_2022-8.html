🎙️ Get to Know: Arielle Kempler, Associate Director of Student Health and Counseling Services

Congratulations on your new role! Can you summarize it for us?

I oversee the amazing Student Health and Counseling Services (SH&CS) staff who help create and foster a safe, healthy and welcoming environment for the SVA community. I also work with other departments and senior staff to ensure the safety and well-being of students on SVA's campus. I am part of two teams that address students in need and any other emergent issues for SVA students, and that assist with COVID-19 response and contact tracing.


With the recent surge in COVID-19 cases, what preparation was needed from your team specifically to ensure a safe return to campus for students?

The SH&CS team has been working closely with other departments at SVA to ensure the safe return of students to campus and to help mitigate the spread of COVID-19. Besides the new policies put into place by SVA (including the booster mandate and the resident testing requirement), SH&CS has helped secure on-site testing for the SVA community twice a week for all those required to or who just want to test. We also brought the "Booster Van" to campus during the week of January 18th to help those who have not yet received a booster shot, and SVA nurse Abby Wohl has also been answering COVID-19-related questions in the "Ask the Nurse" feature in SVA Today. The team also continues to monitor and trace all student cases of COVID-19 and answer all pandemic-related inquiries via the student-covid-alert@sva.edu inbox.


What resources are you providing for students who may be feeling overwhelmed?

We offer free, confidential and short-term counseling services to all SVA students. Students can make an appointment to speak to one of the SH&CS therapists if they feel overwhelmed and need someone to talk to. Teletherapy sessions are available to all students via a secure platform. We also facilitate programming around mental wellness and self-care, like dog therapy. Information about our offerings can be found on our Instagram @svastudenthealth and our linktr.ee.


Do you have any tips for students on getting through the rest of the school year?

COVID-19 has made all of us pivot, change expectations, experience loss and channel our inner strength. What I have learned about myself and the SVA community during this challenging time is that we are resilient! In the face of all these challenges, we have still accomplished goals, thrived and learned new things. COVID-19 is not over yet, and there are more challenges ahead, but you don’t have to face them alone. Use your community and the resources around you for support, take time to care for both your physical and mental wellness, prioritize what is most important to you and practice setting boundaries. Remember that nothing lasts forever, and though this all feels overwhelming right now, it will pass. And lastly, remember that Student Health and Counseling Services is here for you! Reach out to health@sva.edu if you need us.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning's online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Making DIY Business Videos Start to Finish

Learn how to make professional-looking business videos that inspire customers to buy your products and services. Michael Tringe teaches how to script your content, shoot quality visuals with a DSLR camera and edit the video into a story with free software.


Writing: The Craft of Story

In this course, author Lisa Cron digs into the craft of writing a compelling story based on what the brain is wired to respond to in every story we hear.


Thinking Creatively

While we often think of creativity as an inherent trait, anyone can learn how to be more creative, whether they're an artist or an actuary. Instructor Drew Boyd shares the importance and power of creativity, goes over the barriers to creativity and shows you some behaviors and techniques that can help you generate novel ideas or solutions to problems.
