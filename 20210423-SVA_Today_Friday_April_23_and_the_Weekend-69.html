Events

9:00am | Virtual Support Space for International Students: Self-Care Tips
Do you feel overwhelmed by schoolwork, finals, graduation and current events? Come take a break and join Will from the International Student Office and Holly from Student Health and Counseling Services to de-stress and connect with fellow international students! They will share stress management and self-care tips for finals week. 9:00 – 10:00am ET. Join on Zoom. Note: This will be their last session for this academic year.

9:30am | MPS Art Therapy Thesis Presentations
Join MPS Art Therapy in celebrating the work of the program's class of 2021, as the students present their graduate thesis research. 9:30am – 1:00pm ET. Register here for a Zoom link.

1:00pm | Power Lunch Creative Careers with Carey Pietsch
SVA students and alumni are invited to join SVA Career Development for this Q&A with comic artist and writer Carey Pietsch, who is best known as a cartoonist and co-adapter on The Adventure Zone graphic novel series. 1:00 – 2:30pm ET. Register here.

6:00pm | Long Gone Gulch Panel
Want to gain insight on storytelling and developing a pitch for television? Join Women in Animation SVA for a panel discussion and Q&A with Tara Billinger and Zach Bellissimo, creators of the animated web series Long Gone Gulch. 6:00pm ET. Open to students, faculty and staff. RSVP here.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Louise Fili to Receive RIT Goudy Award
Congratulations to designer, longtime SVA faculty member and 2016 SVA Masters Series honoree Louise Fili, who is the 2021 recipient of the Rochester Institute of Technology's Frederick W. Goudy Award for Excellence in Typography!
SOCIALLY DISTANCED PICKS

Keeping Hope Alive Children's Book Event
Books of Wonder presents an event with authors and MFA Illustration as Visual Essay alumni Stephen Savage and Brian Floca, who each will share their new books and answer questions from attendees. Tomorrow, April 24, at 1:00pm ET via CrowdCast. Register here.

Dawoud Bey: An American Project
Spanning from his earliest street portraits in Harlem to his most recent series imagining an escape from slavery on the Underground Railroad, this exhibition attests to Dawoud Bey's (1977 Photography) profound engagement with the Black subject. On view through October 3, 2021, at the Whitney Musuem, 99 Gansevoort Street.
