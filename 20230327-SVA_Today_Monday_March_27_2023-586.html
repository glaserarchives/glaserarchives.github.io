Dana Davenport

Envisioning The Collective Futurity of Black and Asian Communities

Dana Davenport (BFA 2015 Photography) is featured in Bust for her elaborate box braid chandeliers that explore the complexities surrounding interminority racism as a mixed-race Black American and Korean woman.

BFA Fine Arts presents an artist lecture with Erin M. Riley, a tapestry weaver living and working in Brooklyn. Tonight, March 27, 7:00 – 8:00pm, online.

Macy’s Flower Show

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tomorrow | IRL: Portraits of Something You Can Feel

SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion and SVA Library West present a collage workshop and mini-lecture—make your own textural, glitter collages using vintage 1990s and 2000s magazines, rhinestones, glitter and more! Tuesday, March 28, 4:00 – 6:00pm. RSVP here. SVA Library West 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

Thursday | LEGO-of-Stress

Take a break with SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and build your own LEGO creations to relieve stress and practice mindfulness. Enjoy a cup of cocoa, iced tea and some snacks! Thursday, March 30, 2:30 – 4:30pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

Thursday and Friday | VASA’s Spring Market

The spring market is just around the corner—purchase art from fellow SVA students who diligently prepared posters, apparel, prints, zines, crochet, crafts, photography and more! There will be 40 different vendors each day for a total of 80 students. Thursday, March 30, and Friday, March 31, 5:00 – 8:00pm. 216 East 23rd Street.

Sign Up for WSVA: Unplugged!

WSVA Radio’s live talent show returns! Do you have songs to sing? Jokes to share? Heed the call and sign up to perform here by Friday, March 31, 11:59pm.

Invitation: “Georgia O’Keeffe: To See Takes Time” Reception at MoMA

SVA staff and faculty are invited to an opening reception at the Museum of Modern Art for “Georgia O'Keeffe: To See Takes Time,” Tuesday, April 4, 7:00 – 9:00pm. Available spots are limited. RSVP here by Friday, March 31. RSVPs will be confirmed via email.

Next Week | A Thousand and One Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of the Sundance Film Festival award-winning film A Thousand and One. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, April 2, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Next Week | The Algorithmic State: Digital Repair With Kader Attia

MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with artist Kader Attia on notions of repair, both in terms of physical objects and societies, that help us to reconsider damage, dysfunction and trauma in relation to the ways in which AIs are being deployed now and in the future. Monday, April 3, 12:00 – 1:00pm, online.
