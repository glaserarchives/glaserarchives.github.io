A Celebration of "Diverse and Brilliant Visions"
For the 10th anniversary of MPS Digital Photography's i3: Images, Ideas and Inspiration lecture series, Chelsea News recently spoke with Jaime Permuth, program alumnus and the series' longtime curator and host, about past i3 lectures as well as the program's current Photoville exhibition.
Events

11:00am | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Members of the Asian and Asian American communities continue to face harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Today, 11:00am – 12:30pm ET, faculty and staff who self-identify as AAPI are invited to join this virtual forum to connect on these or other topics for peer support. Some of you may have participated in April; new members are welcome. If you are interested in participating, please fill out this Google Form to register. Zoom information will follow.

If you are unable to participate, you can still fill out the Google Form to indicate your interest in future opportunities and to be included in future correspondence.

6:00pm | Community Lecture Series: Response Toward the Asian Community During the Pandemic
In this virtual lecture, Tokyo-born art therapist Makiko Harada will share her own experiences and review the history of hatred toward Asians. 6:00pm. Register here to get a Zoom link.

6:30pm | FAB FRIDAY With Omnivore
Omnivore is a transcontinental graphic design studio located in Brooklyn, Portland and Los Angeles. Founded by three second-generation Asian American women, who have been collaborating since 2002. Alice Chung, Julie Cho and Karen Hsu are joining BFA Design to discuss their journey, practice and origin of the three-headed design beast. Join this Fab Friday at 6:30pm on Zoom.

Saturday | Undergraduate Open House | Session 1
Do you know someone interested in pursuing a BFA in advertising, design, fine arts or interior design? Be sure to tell them about tomorrow's open house, featuring unique presentations by each department chair, panel discussions with current SVA students, and an opportunity to speak directly with Admissions and Financial Aid. Attendees also receive a $50 application fee waiver if they apply for early action. Tomorrow, 10:00am – 1:00pm ET. Register here.

Virtual open houses will be held for SVA's other undergraduate departments on Saturdays, October 23 and November 6.
ANNOUNCEMENT

Reminder | SVA All-Staff Meeting on Thursday, October 28
The SVA All-Staff Meeting returns to the SVA Theatre on Thursday, October 28. After months of remote work, come catch up with your colleagues, hear what’s happening around the College and get a little taste of Halloween. To attend in person, your SVA ID is required and you must have completed the Cleared4 process at least two hours prior to the start of the meeting. Doors open at 3:30pm. Program begins at 4:00pm.

For those unable to attend in person, the meeting will be live-streamed. Pre-register now to receive your link to tune in live.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Tune in to VIEW Conference Online
VIEW Conference is the premiere international event on computer graphics, interactive techniques, digital cinema, 2D/3D animation, VR and AR, gaming and visual effects. Running Sunday, October 17 – Friday, October 22, it features robust online events that you can access for free by registering here.

SVA community highlights:
-MFA Computer Arts Chair Terrence Masson will moderate "Artist Stories of the Pandemic," on Sunday, October 17, 2:15pm ET.
-Director of Career Development and BFA Illustration alumnus Angie Wojak will moderate "Landing Your Dream Job in Animation," on Wednesday, October 20, 1:45pm ET; as well as "Climate Change and Storytelling," on Thursday October 21, 2:45pm ET.

NewFest's LGBTQ+ Film Festival
NewFest's 33rd annual New York LGBTQ+ Film Festival will take place beginning today, online and in person, including screenings at SVA Theatre, through Tuesday, October 26. Students can use discount code STUDENT21 for 20% off virtual all-access streaming passes and $4 off individual tickets (to both in-person and virtual screenings). Purchase passes here.

Gowanus Open Studios
Brooklyn’s biggest celebration of local art and artists features more than 400 artists and venues in the greater Gowanus neighborhood who will give the public a glimpse inside the former factories, warehouses and studio buildings of this vibrant neighborhood. Saturday, October 16 – Sunday, October 17. Learn more here.
