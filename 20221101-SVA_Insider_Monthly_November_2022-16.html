🎙️ Get to Know: Michelle Mackin, manager, Alumni Affairs and Development

You’ve been in your role with Alumni Affairs for about a year now. What was your SVA journey that led to this current position?

I joined SVA as a temp in the communication coordinator position just over six years ago, and officially became a staff member in January 2017. I spent five years writing SVA Today, managing the events calendar and working with the Communication team on content strategy. The manager role in Alumni Affairs is communication-heavy, so it was a very natural next step in my career here.


Congratulations on being named a 2022 Distinguished Staff Award winner! What does it mean to you to win this award, and what do you hope to contribute as a member of the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group?

Thank you! It is an honor to be recognized by my peers when I was nominated with so many other deserving staff members. As a member of DSAG, I’m looking forward to getting to know staff members from across the College and having actionable discussions about how we can continue to support the SVA community.


What were the challenges and successes you had leading to this award? Is there anything you've accomplished since you began with Alumni Affairs that you're particularly proud of?

During the pandemic, our department has had to pull back on certain alumni benefits, such as access to the SVA Library and hosting in-person events. But we shifted our focus to supporting alums in other ways, including the monthly alumni newsletter for promoting and celebrating alumni accomplishments and sharing SVA news.


Alumni Affairs has some big projects in the works, including Miami Art Week 2022, where alumni work will be shown at Untitled Art Fair. What can you share about any of these or other exciting things coming up?

We’re so excited to be able to travel and spend time with alumni after two long years of hiatus! This month, we’ll be co-hosting an alumni reception with the Korean Alumni Association in Seoul; in December, we’ll host a mixer for alumni during Miami Art Week and attend Untitled and other fairs to see their work; and we're planning spring events in San Francisco and Los Angeles. We’re also in the midst of the SVA Alumni Society’s Support the Talent campaign, which provides funding for scholarships and awards for current students. Last year, we gave nearly $90,000 to 99 students. If any staff members would like to contribute, they can do so here.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Learning Design Research

Authors Peni Acayo and Andy Schwanbeck walk you through the various types of research (primary vs. secondary, quantitative vs. qualitative, etc.) so that you get a sense of which are appropriate for the job at hand.


iPhone Photography: Capturing and Editing Raw Photos

Do you want to get more from the camera in your pocket? Instructor Richard Harrington walks you through which settings matter most and which apps you need to download, ensuring you understand your iPhone and the file formats you can use.


User Experience for Web Design

In this course, Chris Nodder teaches you how to apply some simple interaction design principles to your site to make it behave in a way that users expect and enjoy. Chris covers the key things people want from websites, how they search for information, how they read online and how to structure your content to take advantage of this.
