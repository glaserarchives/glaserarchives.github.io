Subject: Update on the Masking Policy at SVA
From: SVA Info
Date: 11/21/22, 11:13 AM
To: allstudents@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu, allstaff@sva.edu

Good Morning,

 

I’m writing to provide an update on discussions about adjusting our mask-wearing policy on campus. I understand that wearing a mask indoors for long periods can be uncomfortable. I appreciate the patience and cooperation that all of you have demonstrated in complying with this policy and the other health and safety measures that have proven to be effective in keeping infection rates low at the College.

 

SVA will be able to change to a “masks encouraged but not required” option as soon as it is safe to do so. We have determined that this level is one where transmission rates of the virus are about where they were in the spring of 2021 before the Delta variant created a surge of infections, hospitalizations and deaths. (The transmission rate is known to be the most effective form of measurement because it defines the probability that an infection will occur among susceptible people within a specific group.) In spring 2021, the transmission rate in the general population of New York City over a seven-day period was about 100 per 100,000 people, or 1,200 new infections per day. That rate, when translated to SVA’s own community population of 5,500, is equal to about seven new infections per week, or one new infection per day.

 

Currently, the seven-day NYC transmission rate is 212 per 100,000 people, or 2,527 per day. Here at SVA, during the week of November 14-20, the seven-day campus transmission rate is 22 per 5,500 people, or more than 3 per day. Unfortunately, while numbers have held somewhat steady for the past two weeks, they are still high and are expected to surge following the Thanksgiving holiday, after people travel to gather with family and friends. As mask-wearing is proven to be the most effective way to minimize the transmission of the virus, we still have a way to go before we can loosen this preventive measure.

 

Please do not be discouraged. I am hopeful that as more people stay up-to-date and receive the newer bivalent booster—which is proven to be highly effective against the BA.4 and BA.5 Omicron subvariants—those rates should certainly be on the decline. In order to keep apprised of these rates, updates will be provided each week on the home page alert banner of sva.edu.

 

This is as good a time as any to remind you to make an appointment to get the bivalent booster, as well as a quadrivalent flu shot, if you haven’t already. It is safe to get both at the same time.

 

I wish you all an enjoyable and safe Thanksgiving break and the end of the fall semester.

 

David Rhodes

President, SVA
