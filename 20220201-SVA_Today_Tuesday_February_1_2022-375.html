ISO's 2022 Lunar New Year Poster Competition
Happy Lunar New Year and congratulations to Zhiyu You (BFA Illustration), winner of this year's Lunar New Year poster competition hosted by the International Student Office. Follow ISO's Instagram, @sva_iso, as they post the entries this month—and see below for more on ISO's Lunar New Year fortune bag giveaway and an ISO/SVA Community Fridge Lunar New Year Food Share!

Black History Month 2022
On the Realize DEI blog, Dr. Jarvis Watson, director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA, writes about focusing on Black health and wellness during this year's Black History Month, and discusses upcoming BHM-related events at SVA and in and around New York City.
Events

Fortune Bag Baskets
In celebration of the Lunar New Year holiday, the International Student Office is offering baskets of small fortune bags for students to take today in the lobby of the 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, and at SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street.

12:00pm | Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American Harassment and Xenophobia
Presented by SVA Student Affairs, Hollaback will host a one-hour interactive bystander intervention training session to discuss the types of disrespect that Asians and Asian Americans are currently facing, the five strategies for intervention and how to prioritize your own safety while intervening. There will be time at the end of the session for practice, so you'll leave feeling more confident about intervening the next time you see anti-Asian/American harassment online or in person. Today, 12:00 – 1:00pm ET, online. Register here.

Ongoing | SVA ♥ Milton
This week is your last chance to see SVA's tribute to the late, legendary designer and longtime faculty member Milton Glaser. Due to COVID-19 precautions, in-person viewing is restricted to SVA students, faculty and staff. Closing Saturday, February 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Creative Boom's Top 50 Graphic Design Books
Creative Boom's list of "50 essential books every graphic designer should read" includes titles by the late faculty member Ivan Chermayeff, faculty member Tom Geismar, MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller, MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman, former faculty member Judith Wilde (BFA 1979 Fine Arts; MFA 1994 Illustration as Visual Essay) and former BFA Design Chair Richard Wilde.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Comic Strip Book Club: Matt Baker's Phantom Lady
The New York Public Library kicks off Black History Month with an open discussion of the late Black artist Matt Baker's work on the Golden Age comic Phantom Lady. Today, 4:30 – 5:30pm ET, online. Register here.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

SVA Community Fridge's Lunar New Year Food Share
Starting today, the SVA Community Fridge, located in the SVA Student Center, will have packaged food and fruits donated by the International Student Office, in celebration of Lunar New Year. If you can contribute Lunar New Year-related food donations (no frozen food, please), please do so through Friday, February 11. You can also donate any amount to help fund the fridge. Click here to contribute and write "community fridge" in the comment box to ensure that your donation is directed to the project. Student Center hours are Monday – Friday, 10:00am – 10:00pm; Saturday and Sunday, 12:00 – 6:00pm.

Together We Have the Power to End Harassment
Presented by SVA Student Affairs, Hollaback will host various training sessions to support the safety of the Asian and AAPI (Asian American and Pacific Islander) community. See the list below to sign up for the trainings:

Stand Up Against Street Harassment: Thursday, February 3, 6:00 – 7:00pm ET
Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American and Xenophobic Harassment (Available in Korean): Thursday, February 17, 8:00 – 9:15pm ET
How to Respond to Harassment for People Experiencing Anti-Asian/American Harassment: Thursday, February 24, 5:00 – 6:00pm ET

Submit to Visual Opinion
Visual Opinion is accepting submissions for its upcoming issue, "Utopia and Dystopia." Email your submission to vo@sva.edu with your name, title of the piece, your year and major by Thursday, February 17, 5:00pm ET. Please note that contributors must be matriculated students and located in the U.S.
