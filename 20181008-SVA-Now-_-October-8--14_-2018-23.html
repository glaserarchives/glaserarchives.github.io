News and events from the School of Visual Arts

"Art As Witness" at SVA

"Art As Witness: Political Graphics 2016-18," is open now at the SVA Chelsea Gallery. The exhibition features work by prominent artists who use satire to respond to today's agitated political climate. Don't miss the reception this Thursday, October 11, at 6:00pm.

"Obsessorize" on Madison Avenue

3D Design students from BFA Design are back on Madison Avenue with "Obsessorize: Common Objects Uncommon Accessories," a public art exhibition of stylish sculptures on view through Friday, October 26.

From Tonya to Thelma

The Hollywood Reporter on how I, Tonya director Craig Gillespie (BFA 1989 Media Arts) will direct an English remake of 2017 Norwegian supernatural coming-of-age film Thelma.

Interior Design on Nourishing the Senses, a new book showcasing the restaurant designs of award-winning architectural firm Bentel & Bentel, led by BFA Interior Design Chair Carol Rusche Bentel, Paul Bentel and Peter Bentel.
EVENTS

Undergraduate Open Houses

SVA Admissions is hosting open houses for prospective students and their families on three upcoming Saturdays: October 13, October 20 and November 3. Different majors will be represented at each session. Learn more and register at sva.edu/openhouse.

Mario Sorrenti and Dennis Freedman

BFA Photography and Video presents photographer Mario Sorrenti in conversation with creative director Dennis Freedman, discussing Sorrenti’s career and his recent book, Kate (Phaidon). Tuesday, October 9, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Activated

In celebration of its 30th anniversary, MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a panel discussion featuring distinguished program alumni who have achieved great notoriety through creative advocacy. RSVP here. Tuesday, October 9, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

Vera Sacchetti

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by Vera Sacchetti (MFA 2011 Design Criticism), associate curator of the 4th Istanbul Design Biennial, who will discuss the curatorial team's key questions and concepts, and the process of making the biennial. RSVP here. Wednesday, October 10, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

Curators of the Sharjah Biennial 14: Leaving the Echo Chamber

MA Curatorial Practice hosts a panel featuring presentations and discussions with the Sharjah Biennial 14 curators Zoe Butt, Omar Kholeif and Claire Tancons, and the Sharjah Art Foundation President and Director Hoor Al Qasimi, followed by a Q&A moderated by faculty member Brian Kuan Wood. Wednesday, October 10, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
