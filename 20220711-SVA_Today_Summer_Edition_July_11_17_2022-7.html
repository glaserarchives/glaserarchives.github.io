ANNOUNCEMENT

SVA’s Nurse Abby continues to answer your questions regarding COVID-19 and other health-related concerns. Read below for the latest Q&A and use this link to see the question archive. You can also submit any questions you have here.

I have read that the COVID-19 omicron BA.5 subvariant is circulating now. What do I need to know? Are there any new precautions I should take?

COVID-19 continues to mutate. The latest subvariant is BA.5, which is the most transmissible variant yet and currently accounts for almost half of new COVID-19 infections in the U.S. People can be reinfected even if they’ve had COVID as recently as earlier this year.

Student Health and Counseling Services is vigilantly monitoring this subvariant and any potential impacts to our community. Preliminary data suggest that omicron may cause more mild disease, although some people may still have severe disease and need hospitalization. It’s important to continue to take steps to protect yourself.

Remember to do your part to mitigate the spread of COVID-19 and keep our campus as healthy as possible:
  • Make sure you are up-to-date with your COVID-19 vaccinations, including a booster when eligible.
  • Wear a well-fitting mask while indoors around others at all times.
  • Monitor yourself for symptoms and answer the daily Cleared4 health attestation honestly every time you come to campus.
  • If you suspect it—get tested!
