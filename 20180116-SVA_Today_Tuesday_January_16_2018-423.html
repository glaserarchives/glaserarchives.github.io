El Museo @ SVA: May Contain Moving Parts

Curated by El Museo del Barrio’s Rocio Aranda-Alvarado, this exhibition features work by artists of the Caribbean diaspora, many of whom are SVA alumni. Reception: Thursday, January 18, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, February 10. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
EVENTS

6:30pm | Alison Kuo

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by artist and faculty member Alison Kuo (MFA 2014 Fine Arts). 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Visible Futures Lab Equipment Demonstrations

SVA's Visible Futures Lab will be hosting a series of hands-on demonstrations illustrating the power and use of many of the lab's digital fabrication tools, including laser cutters, 3D printers, vinyl cutters, CNC machines, and more. Through Sunday, January 21. Day, evening and weekend times are available. Those interested must sign up here.

Edel Rodriguez Interprets Fire and Fury

TIME on the story behind the viral cover that BFA Illustration faculty member Edel Rodriguez created for last week's issue.
NYC PICK

Esperanza Spalding Selects

Musician Esperanza Spalding uses the Cooper Hewitt's collection to explore how design, like music, both evolves and devolves in the process of transforming. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, January 21. Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.
TAKE NOTE

"Mattia Casalegno: The Knowledge of the Body" Reception

CP Projects Space presents an exhibition, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow John Elammar. in which artist Mattia Casalegno reflects upon the similarities between machines and humans. Tomorrow, January 17, 7:30pm. Through Wednesday, January 24. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

MAT Art Education Open House

Come learn about the program, meet faculty and hear from alumni on their experiences in art education. RSVP here. Friday, January 19, 5:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 4th floor.
