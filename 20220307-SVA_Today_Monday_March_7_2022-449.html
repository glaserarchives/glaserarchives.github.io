March Exhibitions and Virtual Events
As spring approaches, SVA is welcoming the public back to its gallery spaces. This month brings a wealth of in-person exhibitions and virtual events presented by a variety of undergraduate and graduate departments.

SVA Shows 2022
SVA has begun its rollout of year-end and thesis events and exhibitions. Keep tabs on the ever-expanding schedule at sva.edu/svashows.

Ray Billingsley on NPR
NPR interviews Curtis cartoonist and comics artist Ray Billingsley (BFA 1979 Cartooning). Last year, Billingsley became the first Black recipient of the Reuben Award for Outstanding Cartoonist of the Year. To read a Q+A with Billingsley from the fall/winter 2020 Visual Arts Journal, click here.
NYC PICKs

Soft-Firm's Drive-Thru
Inspired by the classic drive-in movie experience, Soft-Firm's Drive-Thru is a reconfigurable outdoor theater featuring a series of videos and performances by Brooklyn cultural organizations and artists, including Simon Benjamin (BFA 2005 Graphic Design) and Nicholas Fraser (MFA 2008 Fine Arts). A schedule of free screenings and performances may be found here. Video and live performances are ongoing until April 14. The Plaza at 300 Ashland, 85 Flatbush Avenue, Brooklyn.

Chris Schanck: Off-World
Found materials covered in layers of shimmering foil and glistening resin converge on the corner of art and design in Chris Schanck's (BFA 1998 Fine Arts) first retrospective exhibition, "Chris Schanck: Off-World," recently featured in Curbed. Through January 8, 2023. Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.
