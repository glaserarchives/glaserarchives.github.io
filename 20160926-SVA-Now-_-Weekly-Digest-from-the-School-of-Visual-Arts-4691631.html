SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts September 26 - October 2
FEATURES

SVA Guide to Gramercy

Continuing with the SVA guide to NYC neighborhoods series, here are a few suggested things to do, see and taste in Gramercy. Catch up on the other guides to Chelsea and Flatiron.

Fall Exhibition Roundup

Highlights from the fall exhibition season in New York City include shows by BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker and faculty members Lynda Benglis and Joel Perlman.

The Best of "The Beat Goes On"

Relive the SVA Chelsea Gallery's "The Beat Goes On" exhibition, which closed Saturday, September 17, with press highlights and a slideshow.
IN THE PRESS

SVA Alumnus Wins Genius Grant

Lauren Redniss (MFA 2000 Illustration as Visual Essay) has been named a 2016 MacArthur Foundation Fellow.

The Art of Instagram

Time's Lightbox on the pop art-influenced photography of BFA Photography and Video faculty member Joseph Maida.

Brilliant and Raw

Yahoo! News highlights portraits of youth culture in the Bronx by Kevin Amato (BFA 2003 Photography), from his recently published book, The Importants.
EVENTS

Untitled (Gender and Representation) 2016

SVA and BFA Photography and Video present a daylong symposium that seeks to address the complex issues of gender that have dominated cultural conversations in the last several years. Panelists include Jennifer Blessing, Kate Bornstein, Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography), Ron Gregg, M. Lamar, Zanele Muholi, and others. Free for SVA students, $40 general admission. Saturday, October 1, 9:30am - 7:00pm.

The Secret: Shape of Destiny

MFA Fine Arts presents a collaborative exhibition of sculpture, painting and video created by students Scott Ferguson and Andrew Jilka, and curated by Zoe Frederick. Reception Wednesday, September 28, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Friday, October 14, SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Permanent Press: A Talk by Aki Sasamoto

MFA Art Writing presents Aki Sasamoto discussing her current exhibition "Delicate Cycle," which is on view at the Sculpture Center in Queens. Thursday, September 29, 6:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

Under Pressure: Institutional Practice and China

MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on the question of artistic national representation in the current global order, in which China is more connected and influential than ever. Panelists include Pi Li, Philip Tinari and Xiaoyu Weng. Thursday, September 29, 7:30pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
