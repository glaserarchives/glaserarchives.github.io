NYC Picks

AAAAH! Weekend Art and Design Fair

The Asian Artist and Activities Hub (AAAAH!) presents a two-day art fair event celebrating the diversity and creativity of the Asian community. Admission is free but priority entry is given to those with reserved tickets; RSVP here. Today, April 21, and Saturday, April 22, 11:00am – 8:00pm. 365 Broadway.

“Third World Mixtapes: The Infrastructure of Feeling”

A solo exhibition of work by SVA Art History faculty member Shellyne Rodriguez (BFA 2011 Visual & Critical Studies), documenting the intellectuals who have shaped her sociopolitical thinking and the diverse social fabric of South Bronx. Through Saturday, April 22. P·P·O·W, 390 Broadway, 2nd floor.

The Acoustic Chaos of the Electric Mayhem—The First Fifteen Years: 1975 – 1990

In anticipation of the new Disney+ series Muppets Mayhem, the Museum of the Moving Image presents a retrospective of the in-show, all-Muppets band Electric Mayhem’s best musical (and nonmusical) moments. Student tickets: $11. Saturday, April 22, 1:00pm, and Sunday, April 23, 12:30pm. Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35th Avenue, Queens.

Sunday Screenings: Wangechi Mutu

A screening of films by artist Wangechi Mutu, as part of the exhibition, “Wangechi Mutu: Intertwined.” Free admission with SVA ID and government-issued ID. Sunday, April 23. The New Museum, 235 Bowery.

Announcements

Admissions Is Hiring Summer Student Ambassador Tour Guides

Are you interested in helping prospective students and families get to know SVA? Admissions is hiring tour guides to give campus tours of SVA’s 11 undergraduate majors and common spaces. Students must have a 3.0 GPA or above and live on or commute to campus throughout the summer. Check out the job description here for more information.

Tuesday | Polite Society Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of Polite Society, the feature directorial debut of director Nida Manzoor, starring Priya Kansara and Ritu Arya. Polite Society follows martial artist-in-training Ria Khan, who believes she must save her older sister from her impending marriage, pulling off the most ambitious of all wedding heists in the name of independence and sisterhood. Watch the trailer here. Registration is required and attendees must present their SVA ID at the door (non-SVA guests are permitted if accompanied by an attendee with an SVA ID); RSVP here; seating is limited. Tuesday, April 25, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Thursday | Another Last Dance

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents its open studios, featuring student work in the 4th-floor studios and hallways along with a fundraising book fair. Books will be available for sale on the ground floor and 4th floor of the building. Thursday, April 27, 5:00 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, ground and 4th floors.

Thursday | MFA Fine Arts Open Studios

Meet artists from around the world who are pursuing an MFA in Fine Arts at the department’s spring 2023 open studios. Thursday, April 27, 5:00 – 9:00pm. MFA Fine Arts, 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.

Saturday | BFA Film Thesis Screenings

BFA Film presents two days of diverse films by students from all over the world. Reception: Saturday, April 29, 8:00 – 10:00pm. Saturday, April 29, 2:30 – 8:00pm, and Sunday, April 30, 2:00 – 10:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Submit Your Work to the Global Design Graduate Show

All artists and designers who graduated or are graduating from undergraduate or graduate programs from September 2022 through August 2023 are eligible to submit their end-of-year projects to the Global Design Graduate Show, an international competition judged by representatives from top brands and agencies. This year’s initiative is sponsored by Gucci. Submission deadline is Thursday, August 31. For more information, click here.
