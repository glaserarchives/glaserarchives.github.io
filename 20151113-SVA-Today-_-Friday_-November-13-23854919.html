SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, November 13, and the Weekend
"Underground Images" Debuts in Rio de Janeiro
SVA's globetrotting exhibition of the College's subway posters recently opened at the Antonio Carlos Jobim Institute in Rio de Janeiro in connection with Semana Design Rio.
Curious About Teaching Art?
SVA Close Up spoke with MAT Art Education Chair Rose Viggiano about the department's program and the qualities of a successful art teacher. MAT Art Education hosts an information session today from 5:30 - 7:30pm at 132 West 21st Street, 4th floor.
Last Chance: "(Still) In Process"
MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition of an ongoing collaborative project initiated by women from the Tamil Nadu region of India in partnership with Sankalpa: Art Journeys in Auroville, India. By appointment only. Contact arttherapy@sva.edu to schedule a visit. Closes today, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
4:30pm: MFA Social Documentary Film DOC NYC Showcase
MFA Social Documentary Film presents a showcase of short films by department students as part of the DOC NYC film festival. MFA Social Documentary Film is DOC NYC's official educational partner. Tickets are $10. IFC Center, 323 Sixth Avenue.
5:00pm: "Fuse" Exhibition Tour
MPS Digital Photography presents a tour of "Fuse," an exhibition of thesis projects from the class of 2015, with department chair Katrin Eismann and the exhibiting artists. "Fuse" closes tomorrow. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
6:00pm: "Me U and the Robot" Reception
MFA Fine Arts presents a collaborative performance by department students Eli Barak and Ragnheiður Káradóttir, who will transform the white space of the gallery into an alternate world filled with sculptures and found-art objects that explore the potential of chain reactions and unhindered play. Curated by department students Jonathan Schouela and Ali Shrago-Spechler. VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
Saturday: MFA Fine Arts Information Session
MFA Fine Arts presents an information session for prospective students. Tour the department and its facilities, and meet with department chair Mark Tribe, faculty, students and alumni. 2:00 - 4:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.
Press pick of the day
Inside The Finest Hours
Director and alumnus Craig Gillespie (BFA 1989 Media Arts) spoke with MovieWeb about his latest film, The Finest Hours, which tells the true story of the U.S. Coast Guard's most dangerous sea rescue.
NYC Weekend Picks
Chances with Wolves DJ Set at MoMA
The DJ duo Chances with Wolves will play their signature combination of obscure, strange, lost or forgotten sounds and music at The Newsstand installation, as part of the exhibition "Ocean of Images: New Photography 2015." Admission is free with SVA ID. 3:00 - 5:00pm, MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street, 3rd floor gallery.
Bushwick Flea
An outdoor flea market featuring vintage items, handmade crafts, artwork and specialty foods. Saturday, November 14, and Sunday, November 15, 10:00am - 6:00pm, corner of Wyckoff Avenue and Willoughby Avenue.
The Salon Art + Design
An exhibition of historical, modern and contemporary art and design from 55 galleries from around the world. Tickets are $22. Through Monday, November 16. The Park Avenue Armory, 643 Park Avenue.
Take Note
Social Media for Artists Panel Discussion and Book Launch
Authors of the new book Starting Your Career as an Artist: A Guide to Launching a Creative Life (Allworth Press/SVA), Angie Wojak, director of SVA Career Development, and Stacy Miller, Ed. D., faculty at Parsons School of Design, will lead a panel discussion on social media tactics for artists. Participants include artist Faith Holland, artist and assistant director of SVA Career Development Anna Ogier-Bloomer and Larry Ossei-Mensah, a cultural critic and co-founder of The Medium. A book signing will follow. Wednesday, November 18, 6:30pm, CUE Art Foundation, 137 West 25th Street, ground floor.
Faculty and Staff Appreciation Day at the SVA Campus Store
On Thursday, November 19, the SVA Campus Store is offering faculty and staff members free gift cards and 500GB hard drives with every new (in-stock) computer purchase, as well 20% discounts on SVA merchandise. All purchases made on November 19 includes entry in a raffle for prizes. The Campus Store is located at 207 East 23rd Street and is open Monday - Friday, 10:00am - 6:00pm.
