🎙️ Get to Know: Dan Halm, Project Manager, External Relations; Curator, SVA Galleries Booth at Untitled, Art Miami Beach

Tell us a little about your history with SVA and your current role.

From the moment I walked into the College as an 18-year-old freshman, SVA felt like home to me. As a student, I worked in the then public-relations office for my four years of undergraduate, and upon graduation was hired to join the staff with a commitment to work full-time for two years. Now, 26 years and two office changes later, I am the project manager for External Relations.


How and when did SVA start exhibiting in Miami, and how has the experience has evolved over the years?

SVA showing at art fairs began with an idea from [Executive Vice President] Tony Rhodes in 2003, when we applied and were accepted to the Affordable Art Fair in New York City. Following the rise of Art Basel Miami Beach and the satellite fairs, we decided we should continue being the first art college showing in fairs and make a move to present work there.


Since 2007, SVA Galleries has shown at a number of fairs in Miami, starting with the Aqua Art Fair and including Miami Project, PULSE and now Untitled, Art Miami Beach. Over the years it occurred to me that the fairs offer participants an opportunity to go through what I call "art fair boot camp,'' learning about curation, installation, sales, editioning work, travel preparation and promotion.


In 2020, SVA's participation in UNTITLED, ART was virtual. How was the process for selecting artists different and what were some of the challenges you faced?

Because of the pandemic, in 2020 all the art fairs either went virtual or were canceled outright. SVA continued to support its recent graduates by hosting a virtual booth at Untitled, Art, OVR, which replaced the Miami edition of the fair.


The major difference in selecting works for this digital iteration was the fact that most of them were sourced from digital exhibitions, open studios and recommendations from the departments. I also never met with any of the recent graduates in person or made studio visits. All conversations, meetings and selections were done over Zoom, a tricky way to view art for sale.


This year, the fairs are back in person. How will it be different and what are some things you're excited about with this year's showing?

I'm excited to showcase the nine recent graduates who will be a part of the SVA Galleries booth to an energized art market and fair season. It will be great presenting the work to collectors from all over the world, gallery owners, curators and the general public. And I'm excited to be going back to Miami, as nothing compares to seeing art in person and diving into all the details and seeing the artist's hand at work.


The pandemic made me realize how much I missed being in the same room as art that makes me happy. It's an honor for me to help facilitate and educate this group, to see their full potential and present them to an industry eager to discover new talent.
