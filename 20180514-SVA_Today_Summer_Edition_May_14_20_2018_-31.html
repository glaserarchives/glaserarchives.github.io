Monday | Liz Deschenes and Steel Stillman

BFA Photography and Video and Dear Dave, magazine present artist Liz Deschenes and artist and writer Steel Stillman, who will discuss the development of Deschenes’ camera-less photograms and installations and her upcoming show in New York. Tonight, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Monday | Stage and Screen: SpongeBob SquarePants The Musical

A screening of episodes of SpongeBob SquarePants that inspired the musical and director Tina Landau’s TEDTalk about her vision for the show, followed by a Q&A with the musical's bookwriter Kyle Jarrow and executive producer Susan Vargo. Tickets $5; purchase here by clicking "Book Now." Tonight, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tuesday | Lynn Saville

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by Lynn Saville, a photographer of cities at twilight and dawn, as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tuesday, May 15, 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Thursday | "Conflict" Reception

A juried exhibition of work by SVA students that address conflicts arising from symptomatic behaviors in society. Thursday, May 17, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, June 2. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Friday | Champions of Change for Gender Equality Awards

The Metro NY Chapter of UN Women presents this awards ceremony, which recognizes those who make significant contributions to women's empowerment and gender equality in their professional and personal lives. Reception, 6:00pm; ceremony, 7:00pm. Tickets $25 for SVA staff/faculty with code SVAMBR18, and $15 for SVA students with code SVASTUDENT18; purchase here. Friday, May 18, 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sunday | Stage and Screen: The Band's Visit

Enjoy the movie that inspired The Band's Visit, one of Broadway's biggest plays this year. Tickets $5; purchase here by clicking "Book Now." Sunday, May 20, 3:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sunday | Deadpool 2

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Deadpool 2, starring Ryan Reynolds. Admission is free with SVA ID (two guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Sunday, May 20, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

Pop-Up Magazine

Pop-Up is a magazine performed live, with a host of multimedia storytellers and a live band. Topics include an art world mystery, a crime story that starts at a sex therapy institute and a prison-to-Hollywood tale. The SVA community can enjoy discounted tickets by entering the code STUDENT during check out; purchase here. Monday, May 21, 7:30pm. David Geffen Hall, 10 Lincoln Center Plaza.

Trembling Mountain with Kesang Tseten

MFA Social Documentary Film presents a screening of the documentary Trembling Mountain with the film's director Kesang Tseten. Free; RSVP to Amitabh Joshi at ajoshi@mfasocdoc.com. Tuesday, May 22, 6:30pm. SocDoc Auditorium, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.

LDV Vision Summit

The LDV Vision Summit will explore how visual technologies that leverage computer vision, machine learning and artificial intelligence are revolutionizing business and humanity. Leading technologists, tech execs, investors and startups will come together to see 80 expert speakers showcase the cutting edge in visual tech. SVA students can get 2-for-1 tickets for $100 ($50 each) by emailing contact@ldv.co from your SVA email to get the code and register. Visit ldv.co/visionsummit for more info. Wednesday, May 23 – Thursday, May 24. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Freelance Isn't Free

Celebrate the first anniversary of the Freelance Isn’t Free Act (Local Law 140) with a special Q&A with local creative, technical, government and business leaders to review the enhanced protections for freelance workers, freelancer rights and remedies for wage theft violations. Free and open to the public. RSVPs not accepted or required. Wednesday, May 24, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Call for Submissions: All-Staff Art Show

The Distinguished Staff Advisory Group presents its annual All-Staff Art Show, featuring work by SVA's administrative staff. Submit artwork here by Friday, May 25. Reception: Thursday, June 14, 5:00pm. On view Wednesday, June 13 – Saturday, June 23. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
