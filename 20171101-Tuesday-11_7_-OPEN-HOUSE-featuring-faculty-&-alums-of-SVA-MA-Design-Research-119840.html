OPEN HOUSE!

SVA Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism
136 West 21st Street, Second Floor 
Tuesday, November 7, 6:00–9:00 p.m.

RSVP Here

 

"Designers that rise to the top can write, articulate, weave a narrative, and communicate ideas in a compelling way." Khoi Vinh, Principal Designer, Adobe


Considering graduate study in design research? Passionate about storytelling in multiple media? Interested in the contexts and consequences of design? Please join us in the SVA Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism, for an evening of insights and behind-the-scenes access into the world of design.

Hear firsthand from program faculty, students, and alumni about the ways in which they engage with design research and writing, and how they put it into practice in the workplace. 

Featured speakers will include:

Journalist Adam Harrison Levy in conversation with writer/critic Karrie Jacobs.

Author Kimberlie Birks on her new book for Phaidon about design for children. 

Editors Julia van den Hout & Kyle May on Guns, the latest edition of the journal Clog.

Designer Ida Benedetto on "Writing the System," the department's lunchtime series on design + tech.

Researcher Karisa Senavitis on "Data Feels," digital objects of collective healthcare.

With alumni hosts:

Angela Riechers
Anna Marie Smith
Anne Quito
Avinash Rajagopal
Chappell Ellison
Christina Milan
Derek Love
Derrick Mead
Mariam Aldahi
Paul Olmer
Trilby Schreiber
...and more!

Following the evening’s presentations, prospective students will have the opportunity to discuss their own goals for graduate study, tour the department, and continue conversations over drinks and refreshments. Doors open at 6:00pm, presentations begin at 6:30pm.

Study at SVA Design Research!


The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is a one-year, intensive MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject. 

We will begin reviewing applications in January for Fall 2018 enrollment. Successful candidates will be granted significant scholarships. Apply here.
