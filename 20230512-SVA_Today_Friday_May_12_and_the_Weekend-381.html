12:00pm | Industry Day 2023

SVA IN THE PRESS

“To Be at War”

Arin Yoon (MFA 2009 Photography, Video and Related Media) talks about her ongoing photography project, a documentation of her life as a mother and member of the military community, in an interview for Lenscratch.

NYC PICKS

“A Spell for Refusal” Reception

A solo exhibition of Opal Mae Ong (BFA 2016 Illustration) featuring paintings produced during a yearlong residency with Arsenal art contemporain Montreal. Reception: Tonight, May 12, 5:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 17. Arsenal Contemporary Art New York, 21 Cortlandt Alley, 2nd floor.

MOCA Talks: Writing on Queer Experiences With Kevin Chen and His Novel Ghost Town

A reading and discussion with Germany-based Taiwanese novelist Kevin Chen (陳思宏) on his book Ghost Town, the story of Keith Chen, who escapes his traditional family and the shadow of murder, to Berlin in search of acceptance as a gay man. Free. Tonight, May 12, 6:00 – 7:00pm. Museum of Chinese in America, 215 Centre Street.

Ecological City: Procession for Climate Solutions

A theatrical pageant of performances, giant puppets, costumes and visual art exploring climate solution initiatives throughout the community gardens, neighborhood and waterfront on the Lower East Side of New York City. A team of students from the BFA Fine Arts Bio Lab have created a collection of ceramic sculptures that provide a much-needed water source for urban pollinators for the event. Free. Saturday, May 13, 11:00am – 5:00pm. See the procession route here.

Rashida Bumbray at Dia

Performance artist Rashida Bumbray presents her solo interpretation of a score realized collaboratively by Hewitt and Jamal Cyrus that builds upon traditions of indeterminate musical notation and the fractal logic of the jazz standard. Free. Sunday, May 14, 12:00pm. Dia Chelsea, 537 West 22nd Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk with Alicia Bruce, an award-winning working-class photographer, community collaborator, educator and activist based in Scotland. Monday, May 15, 7:00 – 8:30pm. RSVP for location.

Next Week | “Milton Glaser: POP”

Steven Heller, design historian and co-chair of MFA Design; artist, designer and longtime SVA faculty member Mirko Ilić; and Beth Kleber, head of the Milton Glaser Design Study Center and Archives and the SVA Archives, present an exhibition based on Milton Glaser: POP, their book collecting work created by the late designer, illustrator and SVA faculty member Milton Glaser during the 1960s and ’70s. Wednesday, May 17, through Monday, June 5, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery.

Next Week | SVA Premieres
