Tuesday, May 9, 2023

Capturing the “Fire and Thunder” of Contemporary Life

During her six-month-long Image Equity Fellowship, Nasrah Omar (BFA 2012 Photography) created “Proteus Effect,” a photography series exploring the effect of virtual spaces on the development of identities, agendas and ideologies. An exhibition of work by 2022 fellowship recipients, “Through Fire and Thunder,” opens at Pioneer Works in Brooklyn on Friday, May 12.

EVENTS

11:00am | Justin Zhuang, Proposal to Publication: The Making of Everyday Modernism

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents author Justin Zhuang (MFA 2015 Design Criticism) to discuss the development of his recent book, Everyday Modernism: Architecture and Society in Singapore, which uncovers the many untold stories of the Southeast Asian city-state’s modernization. Zhuang will be joined by design critic Vera Sacchetti to explore his process of researching, writing and collating this collection of modern architecture in Singapore. This talk is part of MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism’s spring program series, “How To:_____.” Today, May 9, 11:00am – 12:30pm, online.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

March With SVA at NYC Pride 2023

Calling all SVA LGBTQIA+ community members and allies: the offices of Academic Affairs and Diversity, Equity and Inclusion are excited to announce that the Provost has again secured space for SVA students, faculty, alumni and staff to walk in the New York City Pride March! Join together in celebration to further the affirmational and inclusive campus that is SVA while simultaneously protesting and demonstrating against the recent spread of anti-LGBTQIA+ and anti-trans legislation through U.S. state houses. This year’s NYC Pride March theme is “strength in solidarity.” Sign up here. The march will take place on Sunday, June 25.

#SVAwesome

Frog and Toad, a new animated show produced by Apple TV Studios and Titmouse, premiered in late April. This retelling of a beloved book series was brought to life with the help of multiple SVA alumni, including executive producers and Titmouse founders Chris Prynoski (BFA 1994 Animation) and Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1997 Film and Video), storyboard artist Alex Swanson (BFA 2017 Animation) and compositor Moksha Rao (BFA 2020 Animation).
