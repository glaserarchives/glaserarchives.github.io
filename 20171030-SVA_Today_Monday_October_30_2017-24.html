SVA Today
Monday, October 30, 2017

October Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios this month include a website redesign, fanciful music festival illustrations and photographs for Harper's Bazaar Ukraine.
EVENTS

6:30pm | Halloween Pumpkin Carving Contest

The International Student Office presents a Halloween pumpkin carving contest for students. Winners will receive a movie ticket prize. Hot cider and donuts will be available in exchange for a small donation to the Boys and Girls Club of Puerto Rico. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

#HumanityVisibilityEqualsPower

For Aperture, Matthew Higgs interviews fashion photographer Collier Schorr (BFA 1985 Communication Arts) about the evolving language of the fashion image and how her work challenges convention.
NYC PICKS

Boos and Books: A Literary Halloween

A night of games, prizes, writing activities, food and drink. Ages 21 and up. Tickets $20; purchase here. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.

Pop-Up Magazine

In partnership with MFA Visual Narrative, Pop-Up Magazine presents a "live magazine" performance, featuring photography, film, radio and original music all performed live onstage. Tickets start at $36; purchase here. 7:30pm. David Geffen Hall, 10 Lincoln Center Plaza.
TAKE NOTE

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Fall 2017 Salon

A salon showcasing the in-progress works of MFA Photography, Video and Related Media students, ranging from performance-based pieces to mixed-media installations to print-based works. At this salon, there will also be a print and book sale, from which all proceeds will be donated to Puerto Rico relief funds. Friday, November 3, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street.

Pass / Age / Tress / Pass

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the Flatiron Project Space present an exhibition of recent work by faculty member Suzanne Joelson, curated by faculty member Peter Hristoff. Wednesday, November 8 – Friday, December 1. Reception: Thursday, November 9, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
