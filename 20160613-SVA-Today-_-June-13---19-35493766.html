SVA Today
June 13 - 19
FEATURES
Still from Bloody Dairy, by Min Liu
SVA at Annecy
Shift, by MFA Computer Art 2015 graduates Yijun Liu and Maria Cecilia Pugliesi, and Bloody Dairy, by 2012 graduate Min Liu, are competing in the Graduate Film and Short Film categories, respectively, at this year's Annecy International Animation Film Festival.
SVA Premieres in LA
On June 1 and 2, 40 new SVA graduates from nine graduate and undergraduate departments showed their films to industry professionals at the inaugural SVA Premieres, which took place at Hollywood's Linwood Dunn Theater.
Cover Stories: Christian Kracht's Imperium
Designer Adalis Martinez (BFA 2013 Design), author Christian Kracht and translator Daniel Bowles talk about the cover design for the novel's U.S. edition, which was named by The New York Times as a "Best Book Cover of 2015."
Before and After SVA: Jonas Christiansen
The 2014 BFA Advertising alumnus talks about some favorite SVA memories, what it's like transitioning to the work world, his current gig at Facebook, and more.

PRESS PICKS OF THE WEEK
Still from Taking the Plunge
Taking the Plunge Wins Top Prize at ADC Awards
Ad Age reports that the film, made by 2015 BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni Thaddaeus Andreades, Elizabeth Ku-Herrero, Nicholas Manfredi and Marie Raoult, won the Black Cube for best-in-show at the 95th annual Art Directors Club Awards, making it the first student entry to receive the honor.
The Politics of Pop Culture
Vice's Creators Project reviews "Pop Culture Is Where the Pedagogy Is," the recent MFA Fine Arts thesis exhibition.
Documenting A Pre-Teen's Struggle with Scoliosis
Hannah Smith Allen (MFA 2007 Photography, Video and Related Media) writes about the photo series "Breaking the Girl," by Julia Cybularz (MFA 2007 Photography, Video and Related Media), for American Photo.

TAKE NOTE
Strike a Pose
The SVA Theatre, NewFest and NYC Pride present an advance screening of Strike a Pose, a documentary that follows seven male dancers on Madonna's 1990 Blonde Ambition tour. Dancers from the film will be on hand for a talk and post-screening party. $5 for SVA students, faculty and staff; $25 general admission. Monday, June 20, 7:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Cat Del Buono
MPS Digital Photography presents the 2008 MFA Photography, Video and Related Media alumnus discussing her work, which incorporates performance, interactive video and humor. Tuesday, June 21, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Ken Burns In:Pictures
SVA and The Modern School of Film present the documentary filmmaker discussing and screening clips from Buster Keaton's The General (1926), Carol Reed's The Third Man (1949) and Tom McCarthy's The Station Agent (2003), all of which have influenced his understanding of the American experience. $5 for SVA students, $10 for faculty and staff (two ticket limit and one non-SVA guest permitted); $20 general admission; $60 VIP reserved seating. Tuesday, June 21, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MoMA Corporate Member Open House
The Museum of Modern Art invites employees of Corporate Members to a free open house. RSVP required. Wednesday, June 22, 6:00 - 8:30pm, MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street.
Constellations of Memory
An exhibition of thesis projects by the MAT Art Education class of 2016. Reception Friday, July 8, 4:00 - 6:00pm. Saturday, June 25, through Thursday, July 14, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
