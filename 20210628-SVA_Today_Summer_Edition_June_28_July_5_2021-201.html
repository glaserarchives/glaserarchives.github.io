SVA Today
Monday, June 28 – Monday, July 5, 2021

SVA Creators: Zipeng Zhu
In our latest SVA Creators video, Chinese-born designer, art director, illustrator and animator Zipeng Zhu (BFA 2013 Design) discusses making dazzling, eye-popping visuals for Instagram, as well as for his studio and online shop.

Rise Up in Power and Thrive
Dr. Jarvis Watson, SVA's director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion on the significance of Pride Month and the continuing work toward a truly inclusive and equitable society.

Alexis Rockman in the Hamptons
Today is the last day of artist Alexis Rockman's (BFA 1985 Fine Arts) exhibition, "Shipwrecks," at Guild Hall in East Hampton. Read about the artwork in our Q&A with Rockman here.
EVENTS THIS WEEK

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Catherine Opie
Catherine Opie is a Los Angeles-based artist known for her powerfully dynamic photography, which examines the ideals and norms surrounding the culturally constructed American dream and American identity. Join Opie's lecture, presented by MFA Art Practice, on Zoom. Tuesday, June 29, 9:30am ET.

Wednesday | Faculty Book Club
For the next book club meeting on Wednesday, June 30, the group will discuss the documentary 5 Broken Cameras by Emad Burnat and Guy Davidi (2011, 94 minutes) which is available on Tubi. Join on Zoom. 4:30 – 5:30pm ET.

As a reminder, we invite all faculty to join us on Canvas for more readings and discussion, all of which can be done at your own pace.

Friday | Independence Day Weekend Hours
SVA's administrative offices will be closed starting Friday, July 2, 1:00pm ET, and will reopen on Tuesday, July 6, 9:00am ET, in observance of Independence Day. Enjoy the long weekend!

Last Chance | Making & Mentoring: Artist-Teachers Speak

This exhibition features paintings, photography and sculptures by 16 MAT Art Education graduates who have dedicated their lives to mentoring and teaching art to children and students of all ages. On view through Thursday, July 1, online at galleries.sva.edu.

Last Chance | All-Staff Art Show
This exhibition features works in various media by administrative staff members at SVA. Organized by the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group and SVA Galleries. On view through Friday, July 2, online at galleries.sva.edu.
SVA IN THE PRESS
Art by Boyang Yu; photo by Billy Anania.

SVA MFA Fine Arts Students "Look Beyond the Pandemic"
Hyperallergic reviews "Here Together," MFA Fine Arts' thesis exhibition, which was on view in Tribeca earlier this month.

Kenny Scharf: When Worlds Collide
The New York Times reviews the new documentary about artist and "poster boy for downtown New York art in the '80s" Kenny Scharf (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). The film, which was co-directed by Scharf's daughter, is out now in theaters and online.

Debbie Millman: The Long Road to My Pandemic Wedding
For the cover story of the latest The Washington Post Magazine, MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman wrote a candid essay about her experience coming out.

Bringing Abolition to the Museum
Artist and activist Shellyne Rodriguez (BFA 2011 Visual & Critical Studies) speaks with Boston Review about museum labor practices and Strike MoMA.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Virtual SUNY SPECTRUM Conference
SUNY SPECTRUM, hosted by the State University of New York, is the nation's largest education conference devoted to preventing and responding to sexual and interpersonal violence against members of the LGBTQI+ community. Monday, June 28 – Friday, July 2. Get tickets for $25 here.

"Dodi King: Mirror Mirror" at Superposition Gallery
Superposition Gallery, founded by artist and curator Storm Ascher (BFA 2018 Visual & Critical Studies), presents an exhibition of work by artist Dodi King. Through Wednesday, June 30. 526 West 26th Street, 10th floor.

Lydia Lunch: The War Is Never Over Screening
The premiere of Lydia Lunch: The War Is Never Over, a film by SVA faculty member Beth B (BFA 1976 Fine Arts) about confrontational multi-media artist Lydia Lunch, is taking place on Wednesday, June 30, at IFC Center in New York. Beth B and Lunch will both be in-person at the 7:40pm ET show; buy tickets here.

Macy's 4th of July Fireworks Spectacular
See the 45th annual Macy's 4th of July Fireworks Spectacular along the East River, accompanied by a score and music performances. Sunday, July 4, beginning around 8:00pm ET. Watch in-person or live on NBC.
