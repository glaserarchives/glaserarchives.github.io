Friday, May 5, and the Weekend

Worm: A Cuban American Odyssey

BFA Illustration faculty member Edel Rodriguez’s forthcoming illustrated memoir, Worm, recounts his childhood in Cuba, his family’s imprisonment in a detention center and their eventual arrival in Florida. Read about Rodriguez’s writing of Worm in an interview with MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller for Print.

EVENTS

1:00pm | 2023 MFA Products of Design Thesis Presentations

Celebrate the 10-year anniversary of MFA Products of Design with thesis presentations from the 2023 graduating class—14 graduating students will each present their year-long thesis work, consisting of research, artifacts, services, experiences and platforms. Today, May 5, 1:00 – 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Last Chance | “The Lion King x SVA”

BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition reimagining the iconic logo for The Lion King on Broadway on the occasion of the show’s 25th anniversary. Students and their mentors attended the show and met with some of the Disney Theatrical Group team for exclusive insights into the production. With their mentors’ guidance, students spent a month bringing their new interpretations to life. Final pieces range from 3D constructions to motion graphics and Risograph prints. Closes today, May 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Sunday | Guardians of the Galaxy, Vol. 3 Screening

SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Guardians of the Galaxy, Vol. 3. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, May 7, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

NYC PICK

Poster House: First Friday

The Poster House museum is partnering with Welcome to Chinatown for programming that celebrates the culture, community and resilience of Chinatown during a special “first friday” event. Free. Today, May 5, 10:00am – 9:00pm. Poster House, 119 West 23rd Street.

K-pop Dance Night With DJ Chen

A dance night hosted by DJ Chen, whose set features songs performed by Korean pop groups with members from various AAPI backgrounds, including NCT, Seventeen and Stray Kids. The night will kick off with a dance workshop and K-pop cover group performances. Free. Tonight, May 5, 7:30pm. David Rubenstein Atrium, 61 West 62nd Street.

The Cinco de Mayo Parade

Celebrate Mexican history, culture and victory over French forces in the 1862 Battle of Puebla with festivities, food and mariachi music. The parade marches down Central Park West from 106th Street in the Upper West Side. Sunday, May 7, 12:00pm.

Therapy Dogs on the Farm

Relax with therapy dogs on Randall’s Island in honor of Mental Health Awareness Month. Free. Saturday, May 6, 2:00 – 3:00pm. Urban Farm, 20 Randall’s Island Park, Wards Meadow Loop.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Chance | Distinguished Staff Award Nominations

Each year at the All-Staff Meeting, SVA recognizes outstanding staff contributions to the College with the presentation of two Distinguished Staff Awards. Each award includes a cash prize of $5,000. It’s up to you to nominate one of your administrative-staff colleagues to receive this award. Award information, a list of eligible employees and the nomination form are available at staffawards.sva.edu. Nominations are due today, May 5, 5:00pm.

Next Week | Animation Celebration Screening

BFA Animation presents a screening of animated projects by graduating students. Saturday, May 13; doors open at 11:00am, screening starts at 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. The celebration continues outside of the SVA Theatre at the “Animation Celebration Exhibition.”

American Society of Interior Designers 2023 Equity and Design Summit

The American Society of Interior Designers New York Metro presents the inaugural Equity in Design Summit. Discover solutions to promote diversity, equity and inclusion in all facets of interior design from over 15 industry leaders including Patti Carpenter, Corey Damen Jenkins, SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Director Dr. Jarvis M. Watson and Kia Witherspoon. BFA Interior Design: Built Environments student Brianna Toussaint will also be speaking on a panel at the event. Learn how these visionaries are developing the foundation to tackle challenges and better our collective community. Non-member students tickets: $20; ticket price includes light breakfast, lunch and reception. Register here. Thursday, May 18, 9:00am – 6:30pm. New York School of Interior Design, 170 East 70th Street.
