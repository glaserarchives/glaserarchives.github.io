Friday, November 20, and the Weekend
Ellen Phelan, Audubon (Cactus, Wrends, and Prickly Pear), 2006, oil on linen. Courtesy of the artist and Joel Shapiro.
Saturday: "Intimacy in Discourse" Reception
SVA Chelsea Gallery, in collaboration with Rail Curatorial Projects, presents “Intimacy in Discourse: Unreasonable Sized Paintings,” curated by SVA faculty member Phong Bui. Opening reception tomorrow at 5:00pm. Through Tuesday, December 22. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Noon: Critical Information, Text and Image
MFA Art Writing presents an interdisciplinary conference examining the contemporary dialogue between art, media and society. Organized by second-year MFA Art Writing students, the conference provides a forum for discussions at the juncture of media, theory, criticism and the visual arts. Until 6:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
4:00pm: Director's Dialogue with Brett Hailey
MPS Directing presents writer and director Brett Haley discussing his most recent feature film, I'll See You In My Dreams, which premiered at the 2015 Sundance Film Festival. 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.
6:00pm: Youth
The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences presents a screening of director Paolo Sorrentino's Youth, starring Michael Caine, Harvey Keitel, Rachel Weisz and Jane Fonda. Special guests will answer questions following the screening. Attendees must remain in the theater through the conclusion of the discussion. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:30pm: A Conversation with Shuddhabrata Sengupta
MFA Art Practice, MA Curatorial Practice and MFA Photography, Video and Related Media present Shuddhabrata Sengupta of Raqs Media Collective, based in New Delhi, discussing the role of artists, curators and educators in the collective’s work. Organized by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Valerie Amend. 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Saturday: "Almost Home" Opens
An exhibition of work curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellows Valerie Amend, Becky Nahom, Ikechukwu Onyewuenyi and Vera Petukhova. The works on view in "Almost Home" reflect the different strands of "home," as defined by geography, built and virtual space, domestic life and memory. Reception Thursday, December 3, 6:00pm. Through Tuesday, December 15, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Saturday: The Peanuts Movie
The Visual Effects Society presents a 3D screening of The Peanuts Movie, based on Charles Schulz's beloved comic strip. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). 2:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Saturday: Robin Winters
MFA Art Practice presents artist and SVA faculty member Robin Winters discussing his ongoing exhibition, "Free Standing Sentence." 6:00pm, Present Company, 254 Johnson Avenue, Brooklyn.
Sunday: Last Chance for "Maximum Entropy"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition curated by Mohammad Golabi that explores the effects, conditions and appearance of decay within the realm of new contemporary architecture. The exhibition features video, installation, sculpture and photography works by Elaine Byrne, Kiyarash Eghtesadi, Keiichi Matsuda and Farideh Sakhayifar. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Sunday: West Side Story
West Side Story star George Chakiris will be present for a screening of the Academy Award-winning 1961 film, for which he won Best Actor. Chakiris will answer questions following the screening. A portion of the evening’s proceeds will benefit the CHARGE Syndrome Foundation. Tickets are $5 for SVA students, faculty and staff with code "SVA2015" (limit four tickets per order. Must present SVA ID at the door). General admission tickets are $35. 5:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Sunday: Macbeth
The Shakespeare Society presents a preview screening of the new film adaption of Macbeth, starring Michael Fassbender and Marion Cotillard. Preceding the screening will be a brief introduction by Yale University Shakespeare scholar Brian Walsh. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). There are a limited number of free tickets available on a first-come, first-served basis. General admission tickets are $20. 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Rebecca Sugar Writes the Best Music on TV
Pitchfork breaks down the BFA 2009 Animation graduate's songwriting skills for Adventure Time and her own show, Steven Universe.
NYC Weekend Picks
Coney Island: Visions of an American Dreamland, 1861 - 2008
The first major exhibition to explore the kaleidoscopic visual record of 150 years of Coney Island history. Admission is free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, March 13, 2016. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
Photo-Poetics: An Anthology
A group exhibition of 10 artists that examines new developments in contemporary photography. Through Wednesday, March 23, 2016. Student and senior tickets are $18, general admission is $25. Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, 1071 Fifth Avenue.
Washington Square Park Music Festival
A celebration of strings featuring compositions by Jerome Kern, Ernest Bloch and Richard Strauss. Admission is free. 8:00pm, St. Joseph's Church, 371 Sixth Avenue.
Ron Howard at Lincoln Center
Hollywood icon Ron Howard discusses his career as an actor, producer and director, including his new film, In the Heart of the Sea. Tickets are free and will be available at the box office on a first-come, first-served basis starting one hour prior to the talk. Limit one ticket per person. 5:00pm, Lincoln Center, Elinor Bunin Munroe Film Center, 144 West 65th Street.

Space is limited, so reservations are recommended by the end of today. Click the headline to RSVP. For questions, contact proffice@sva.edu.
