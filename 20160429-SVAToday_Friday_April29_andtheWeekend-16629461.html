The April Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios for April include a creative branding project, an illustrated reflection on technology, a stunning look at dancers at rest, and more.

5:00pm: MFA Fine Arts Open Studios
Until 9:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.
6:00pm: Fresh Meat
Browse, barter or buy SVA students’ self-published titles at this annual comics fair hosted by Cartoon Allies, the College’s organization for students interested in comics and cartooning. Until 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
7:00pm: "Future Memories" Reception
An exhibition on how architecture is affected by war, socio-political injustice, mass migrations and xenophobia. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Mohammad Golabi. Through Saturday, May 21, Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 3rd floor, Brooklyn.
7:00pm: "Ornamentation of the Joint" Reception
An exhibition of work by photographers Jude Broughan (BFA 1999 Fine Arts), Mark Dorf, Penelope Umbrico (MFA 1989 Fine Arts) and Letha Wilson. Curated MA Curatorial Practice fellow Allison Peller. Through Saturday, May 21, Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 3rd floor, Brooklyn.
7:00pm: MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Spring Salon
Until 10:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.
Saturday: "MFA Illustration as Visual Essay Thesis Exhibition" Opening
10:00am - 6:00pm. Reception Monday, May 16, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Through Thursday, May 19, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Saturday: "Brokedown Palace" Opening
SVA Galleries presents an exhibition of work by SVA students selected by a jury of their peers, featuring work that pushes the boundaries of their mediums through formal and conceptual experimentation. 10:00am - 6:00pm. Reception Monday, May 16, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Thursday, May 19, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Saturday: no//thing but noise
A live performance that explores noise through poetry, sound art, performance and music. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Maya Castro Gutierrez. 7:00 - 9:00pm, location to be disclosed on the day of the event. Contact mayacastrogutierrez@gmail.com for details.

TAKE NOTE
Food for Fines
Through Wednesday, May 18, the SVA Library is waiving $2 fines for every can of food donated to support City Harvest. No ramen noodles, frozen food, or perishables. For questions, contact library@sva.edu. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
Popular Culture Is Where The Pedagogy Is: Explorations of Provocation and Praxis
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work by graduating students that explores three categories of art making processes: observational aesthetics, active engagement and aesthetic neo-formalism. Curated by Jasmine Wahi. Reception Thursday, May 26, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Closing party Saturday, June 4, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Thursday, May 26, through Sunday, June 5, The Hole, 312 Bowery.
