Thursday, February 16, 2017

Truth Over Fiction

A Q&A with SVA faculty member Kathy Brew, who is curating the Museum of Modern Art's annual nonfiction film festival, Doc Fortnight. Through Sunday, February 26. Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.
EVENTS

2:30pm | Smart, Safe, Sex Positive

Students are invited to meet with representatives from SVA Student Affairs as they distribute free condoms and safer sex supplies. Ask a nurse your questions and find out about online and local resources. 2:30 – 4:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

3:30pm | Blue Sky Company Presentation

SVA Career Development presents representatives and artists from Blue Sky Studios, who will discuss their internship application process and their own career paths. RSVP here. 3:30 – 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

5:30pm | MAT Art Education Information Session

Department faculty and recent alumni will discuss the program and the field of art education. 5:30 – 7:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 4th floor.

6:00pm | Studio Intensive in Oaxaca Information Session

Faculty members Steve DeFrank and Mary Jo Vath will discuss the SVA Destinations program Independent Projects: Studio Intensive in Oaxaca, Mexico. 6:00 – 7:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 402C.

6:00pm | Reception: "Systems"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a solo exhibition by 2015 alumnus Taylor Baker, featuring a series of stylized systems that question the suggested nature of things. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Wednesday, March 15. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

6:30pm | Brand New: Art and Commodity in New York, 1979 – 1989

MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion led by SVA faculty member Gianni Jetzer, with renowned artist Julie Wachtel and Columbia University PhD candidate Leah Pires. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

7:00pm | Mark Neville in Conversation with Adam Bell

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents renowned photographer Mark Neville, who will discuss his first commercially available book, Fancy Pictures (Steidl 2016), with department faculty member and advisor Adam Bell . RSVP here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
