EVENTS

9:00am | Curatorial Roundtable: Bernardo José de Souza

MA Curatorial Practice presents a virtual talk by Bernardo José de Souza, an independent curator based in Madrid and Rio de Janeiro. Today, March 15, 9:00 – 10:00am, online.

6:30pm | How to Launch a Career in Video Games

The game industry is the fastest-growing area of media and entertainment. Whether you want to be a developer, producer, marketer or editor, find out how you can build your skills to pursue your passion in this thriving field. Companies represented on the panel include Avalanche Studios, Mokuni Games and T2. Free and open to the public. Tonight, March 15, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | MFA Visual Narrative Application Q&A

MFA Visual Narrative presents a virtual Q&A with Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) about the program and application process. Attendees will receive an $80 application fee waiver if they apply for summer 2023 admission. Register here. Tonight, March 15, 7:00 – 8:00pm, online.

Opens Today | Women’s History Month: A Half-Century of Underground Images

In celebration of Women’s History Month, SVA Galleries presents a selection of woman-designed posters from “Underground Images,” the ongoing traveling exhibition of SVA’s subway posters. Through Tuesday, March 28. SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

NYC PICKS

Picturing Black Femme and Queer Communities in New York Now

Artists Laylah Amatullah Barrayn, Naima Green and Nona Faustine (BFA 1998 Photography) discuss photography as a creative apparatus for shifting dominant narratives, historical representations and contemporary discourses about Black women, femme and queer folks, families and communities in New York and beyond. Student tickets: $10. Tonight, March 15, 6:30pm.

Working on It at First Look Festival

SVA is partnering with the Museum of the Moving Image to present a special sidebar feature for works-in-progress by current SVA students Jai Jackson, Vince Li, Robert McMahon and Patricia Marrero Perez. The Working on It program will be screening on Thursday, March 16, 12:30 – 2:30pm. BFA Film students receive free admission with their SVA ID. Day tickets for students: $11. All SVA students receive 20% off ticket prices with the code “FLPartner20.” Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35 Avenue, Astoria.

Call for Submissions: Visual Opinion Graduation Issue
