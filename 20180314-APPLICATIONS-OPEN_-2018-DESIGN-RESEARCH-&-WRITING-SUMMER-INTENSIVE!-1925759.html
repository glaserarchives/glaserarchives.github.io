Announcing the Design Writing & Research
Summer Intensive

June 4–15, 2018
School of Visual Arts, New York City

Spaces fill quickly, apply today!

If you care about design and language, and are interested in learning how to write meaningfully about products, graphics, urban infrastructure, and buildings, then join us this summer in the School of Visual Arts MA Design Research department for a two-week intensive course devoted to researching and writing about design. 

Participants will be introduced to a range of techniques for constructing compelling narratives about images, objects, and spaces. You will experiment with different research methods and writing formats, visit multiple design studios, and complete several projects across media, including a collaboratively produced publication. 

Each participant will have a dedicated workstation in the light-filled Design Research studio in New York’s Chelsea district, 24-hour access to department resources, and will be instructed by renowned writers and editors, such as: design author Steven Heller; architecture writer and critic Karrie Jacobs; author, critic, and The Weeklings co-founder Jennifer Kabat; BBC interviewer and Design Observer contributor Adam Harrison Levy; The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin; author and playwright Craig Taylor; design and business columnist Rob Walker; and architecture critic Mimi Zeiger, among others.
 
A robust daily schedule of seminars, lectures, workshops, and one-on-one consultations will be supplemented with visits to the city’s design collections, archives, and libraries, as well as behind-the-scenes access to new exhibitions, buildings, and urban planning developments. Participants will also have the opportunity to visit, interview, and profile some of New York’s most prominent design studios in the fields of architecture, interaction design, graphic design, fashion, and product design. 

Tuition: $1,950 USD
Priority application deadline: April 1, 2018 (if you are interested in SVA housing, early application recommended).

Apply here or find out more on the Summer Intensive website!

You'll be in great company if you join us this summer! Intensive participants travel to SVA from all over the world, and arrive with experience in diverse careers ranging from design, architecture, and journalism, to curation, education, and event organization. Some successful Summer Intensive participants apply to and are offered scholarships to our full-year MA Design, Writing & Criticism program. 

Past participants of the Intensive have included: director of the Art Foundation Department at VCU Qatar; Design History teacher at the ESPM Undergraduate Design Program in São Paulo; instructor at the University of Applied Arts, Vienna; in-house designer at the Milwaukee Art Museum; partner at architecture practice Venkataramanan Associates in Bangalore; director of Archivo Diseño y Arquitectura, a nonprofit Design and Architecture space in Mexico City; founder of design-led startup Make Works in Glasgow; owner of Spark Design Space in Reykjavík; photographer for Cookfox Architects in New York City; and contributing editor of the Norwegian fashion journal Personae, among many others!

How to Apply to the Summer Intensive 


SVA Design Research and Writing Summer Intensive requires the following materials:
  • Completed application form
  • Writing sample: Up to 2,000 words of published or unpublished writing (such as essays, blog posts, or articles) about design, architecture, or related subjects.
  • Statement of purpose (250–500 words)
  • CV

Priority application deadline: April 1, 2018.
Thereafter, applications are accepted on a rolling basis, if space is available.

Spend a Full Year at SVA Design Research, Writing & Criticism

The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is accepting applications for Fall 2018 on a rolling basis, as space allows. Successful candidates will be granted significant scholarships. This one-year, intensive MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject.
