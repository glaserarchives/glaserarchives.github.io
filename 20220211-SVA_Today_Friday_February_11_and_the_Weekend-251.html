Tolerance Project Posters on Display in East Village
amNY reports on a collaboration between MFA Illustration as Visual Essay faculty member Mirko Ilić and MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller for the groundbreaking Tolerance Project, an art exhibition that promotes social acceptance through the power of design. Through Thursday, February 24, handcrafted posters designed by contributing artists from around the world will be on display in the windows of Cooper Union's Foundation Building in the East Village, with all the pieces sharing the message of inclusion, diversity and, of course, tolerance.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Brooklyn Talks: White Shoes with Nona Faustine
Artist Nona Faustine (BFA 1994 Photography) discusses her new book, White Shoes, with contributors Jessica Lanay, Pamela Sneed and Seph Rodney. White Shoes collects self-portraits of Faustine taken in locations around New York that were the sites of slave auctions, burial grounds, slave-owning farms and slave ship docking locations. The conversation will be followed by a book signing. Tickets are $16 and include after-hours access to the ongoing exhibition “The Slipstream: Reflection, Resistance, and Resilience in the Art of Our Time.” Tonight, 7:00 – 9:00 pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
