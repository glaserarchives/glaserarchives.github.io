Rob Giampietro
Design Lead, Google Research & Machine Intelligence
Tuesday, December 12, 6:30 p.m (Doors at 6:00 p.m.)

Rob Giampietro is Design Lead for Google’s Research & Machine Intelligence team in New York. From 2015–17 he was Creative Lead for Google’s Material Design studio, managing projects that included four SPAN Design & Technology Conferences, the launch and relaunch of design.google.com, an overhaul of the Google Fonts directory including significant new typeface commissions, and the ongoing expansion and development of the Material Design system itself. Rob has been a Senior Critic in RISD’s MFA Graphic design since 2006, and his writing about design has been published widely, from Eye Magazine to BusinessWeek to Google Design’s own site. From 2010–15, he was Principal at Project Projects in New York. He is the recipient of a 2013 MacDowell Colony Fellowship and the 2014–15 Katherine Edwards Gordon Rome Prize at the American Academy in Rome.

Study at SVA Design Research!


The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is a one-year, intensive MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject.
