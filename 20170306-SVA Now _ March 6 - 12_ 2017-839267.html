SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
March 6 - 12, 2017

SVA's Mythbuster

CNET on how Jon Lung (MFA 2016 Products of Design) won the Mythbusters: The Search televised competition and will now be a host of the popular Science Channel show Mythbusters.

All Are Welcome

MFA Design Co-chair Steven Heller, Provost Jeff Nesin and faculty member Ross MacDonald collaborated to design a positive message in the face of the executive order on immigration.

The New Creators

Presenting the lineup for SVA's Talent Shows, the annual series of exhibitions, presentations, open studios, portfolio reviews and screenings, showcasing the next generation of artists and creative professionals graduating from the School of Visual Arts.
IN THE PRESS

"My Path to Hollywood"

Warren Drummond (BFA 1984 Media Arts) writes about working with Denzel Washington, storyboarding Fences, diversity in the film industry and more in The New York Times.

"Waste Not, Want Not"

Artsy on "up and coming" artist Josh Citarella (BFA 2010 Photography) and his dystopian work for the 2017 Armory Show.

Betty Boop Reimagined

Marie Claire on how designer Zac Posen designed two Betty Boop-inspired dresses with the help of MPS Branding students and King Features Vice President of Creative Frank Caruso (BFA 1985 Media Arts).
EVENTS
Self portrait by Danielle McDuffie.

Portrait Drawing Exhibition 2017

BFA Fine Arts faculty member Brooke Larsen presents an exhibition of self-portraits by his first-year students, focused on his three golden rules: motivation, curiosity and discipline. Through Tuesday, March 14. SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street.

"Systems"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a solo exhibition by 2015 alumnus Taylor Baker, featuring a series of stylized systems that question the suggested nature of things. Through Wednesday, March 15. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
