Please join us Friday, June 17, 4:00–8:00 p.m.
Grand Finale Celebration! 
SVA Design Writing & Research Summer Intensive 2016
 

SVA Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism
136 West 21st St., between 6th and 7th Aves, 2nd Floor


Please join us on Friday, June 17 to celebrate the work of this year's Summer Intensive participants, who hail from all corners of the globe. For the last two weeks, they have immersed themselves in assignments, studio visits, and the city itself, and we invite you to meet them and witness the fruitful results.

We'll begin at 4:00pm with Speaking of the Streets, a series of short plays inspired by the voices of New York's streets, instigated by playwright and author Craig Taylor, and brought to life with a troupe of guest actors.

At 5:30pm, we'll present this year's Platform Project, created with architecture critic Mimi Zeiger and graphic designer Neil Donnelly.  The project explored how, as a design writer and thinker, to take a critical position on issues in the built environment, and used recent development on 57th Street in Manhattan as spring board. The assignment culminated in a live debate on the street in Midtown—come watch the video highlights and pick up a pamphlet!

We'll continue the evening with drinks and conversation in the studio space, with participants, instructors, alums, and friends.

Friday, June 17
4:00 p.m. Speaking of the Streets
5:30 p.m. Official launch of Platform Project 2016
4:00–8:00 p.m. Reception

We look forward to seeing you for a fun evening of words and wine! 

Molly, Emily, Lorena and the 2016 Summer Intensive crew
