SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts. Week of April 4 - 10, 2016
Features
Talking Animation with Nerdland Filmmaker
SVA Features talks to Chris Prynoski (BFA 1994 Animation), who founded Titmouse Animation Studio in 2000 with his wife and fellow SVA alumnus Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1994 Film and Video). Nerdland, the first feature film produced by Titmouse which is also directed by Prynoski, will premiere at the SVA Theatre on Thursday, April 14, 8:30pm, as part of the Tribeca Film Festival.
SVA to Honor Louise Fili with Masters Series Award and Exhibition
The longtime BFA and MFA Design faculty member has designed for Pantheon Books, Paperless Post, Tiffany & Co., Williams-Sonoma and the U.S. Postal Service, among others. The exhibition opens on Friday, October 14, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
SVA Bonanza in Print
Of the 56 "inspiring designers" featured in the spring issue of Print, nearly half of them are members of the extended SVA community. Read more about these superstar designers and their work.
The Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios for March include a colorful candy-hungry monster, a creative nod to Nintendo’s Mario and a hilarious example of how to “cultivate an attitude of raditude.”
In the Press
Turning the Tide
Hi-Fructose on a new exhibition of vivid portraits by Jenny Morgan (MFA 2008 Fine Arts), which recently opened at Beers London.
NY1 Visits "Help Is on the Way!"
NY1's Roger Clark stopped by the SVA Gramercy Gallery to speak with "Help Is on the Way!" curator Kevin O'Callaghan, chair of 3D Design, and several students about the work in the exhibition.
Sci-fi in Bangkok
The Bangkok Post interviewed Stephan Zlotescu (MFA 2007 Computer Art) about his short film True Skin, which was filmed throughout the city and recently optioned by Amazon Studios for a potential sci-fi series.
Turning Personal Tragedy into Art
Indiewire spoke with filmmaker Ana Maria Hermida (BFA 2009 Film and Video) about her new film La Luciérnaga (The Firefly), which is inspired by the aftermath of her brother's death and is contending for Best Foreign Film, Best Lead Actress and Best Supporting Actress at the 2016 Madrid International Film Festival.
Events
Salman Rushdie In:Pictures
School of Visual Arts and The Modern School of Film present Booker Prize-winning author Salman Rushdie discussing and screening clips from the films Pather Panchali (1955), Star Wars (1977) and The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014), all of which have influenced and inspired him. Doors open at 7:30pm. Tickets are $20. VIP reserved seating tickets are $60. Saturday, April 9, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
From Darkroom to Daylight
MPS Digital Photography and BFA Photography and Video present a screening of photographer Harvey Wang’s new film From Darkroom to Daylight, which explores how the dramatic shift from film to digital has affected photographers and their work. Friday, April 8, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MFA Computer Art Open Studios
MFA Computer Art presents a showcase of work by graduating students, including video, animation, audio, interactive installations and physical computing. Reception Thursday, April 7, 5:00 - 7:00pm. Studios are open from noon - 7:00pm. Thursday April, 7, and Friday, April 8, 133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor.
