Friday, February 26, 2016, and the Weekend
Elizabeth Ku-Herrero (center-left) and Nick Manfredi (center) at the Student Academy Awards in 2015.
SVA Student Academy Winners Talk Oscars
SVA Close Up spoke to BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni Elizabeth Ku-Herrero and Nick Manfredi, whose film Taking the Plunge won a 2015 Student Academy Award, about their experience at the ceremony and what to look for during Sunday's Oscars broadcast.
6:30pm: The Business of Art Therapy
MPS Art Therapy presents Brooke Campbell, LCAT, RDT-BCT, and Andrew Rizkalla of Creative Kinections on how to transition from student to professional, or professional to business-minded entrepreneur, in the field of art therapy. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Saturday: "LinkedIn," "Surrogates," "Artist/Printer" and "Books" Open
BFA Fine Arts presents four exhibitions of student work, curated by department chair Suzanne Anker, Gary Sherman, assistant to the chair, and faculty members Gunar Prande (MFA 1993 Fine Arts) and David Sandlin. Reception Thursday, March 3, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, March 12, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

NYC Weekend Picks
Central Park Tour
Tour some the park's most iconic features. Tickets at $15. Today, 11:00am, meet outside the Dairy Visitor Center and Gift Shop.
Lower East Side Gallery Tour
Explore one of the city's most dynamic gallery areas. Includes a discussion with artist Casey Ruble. Tickets are $25. Tomorrow, 1:00pm, 195 Chrystie Street.
Artist Brunch Chat
Residency Unlimited and the Ace Hotel present artist Elizabeth Zvonar discussing how her collages draw on historical references, contemporary advertising and popular culture. Admission is free. Sunday, February 28, noon, Ace Hotel, 20 West 29th Street.
The Big Oscars Quiz Thing
Test your knowledge of movie and Oscars history before watching the 88th annual awards ceremony. Tickets are $10 ($15 at the door). Sunday, February 28, doors open at 5:00pm, quiz begins at 5:30pm, Le Poisson Rouge, 158 Bleecker Street.
Take Note
An Affair of Books: Open House and Book Fair
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a fair featuring over 50 titles by SVA faculty members and department alumni for sale. Book signings and tours of the department's new space will also be available. Thursday, March 3, 6:00 - 9:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.
VIP Passes to PULSE
The External Relations Office has a limited number of VIP passes to PULSE New York, a contemporary art fair, available to students, faculty and staff. Email extrelations@sva.edu to claim a pass (limit one pass per person). Students and faculty must pick up their passes in person, staff can receive their pass via interoffice mail. Each pass admits two. Passes are available on a first-come, first-served basis, and are valid Thursday, March 4, through Sunday, March 6, at the Metropolitan Pavilion, 125 West 18th Street.
