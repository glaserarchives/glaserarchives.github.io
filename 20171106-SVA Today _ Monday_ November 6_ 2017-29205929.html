Floyd Norman at SVA

Award-winning animator, writer and comic book artist Floyd Norman joined students and guests last week to screen and discuss Floyd Norman: An Animated Life (2016), a documentary that explores his groundbreaking career.

11:30am | Last Chance: Yearbook Photo Shoot

SVA Yearbook is hosting one final make-up photo shoot for the 2018 edition. All graduating students who have not already done so are encouraged to dress how they like, stop by and have their photo taken. The yearbook is given to students at commencement for free. 11:30am – 1:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, ground floor.

Inside KAWS' Brooklyn Home

Architectural Digest takes readers on a tour of KAWS' (BFA 1996 Illustration) Brooklyn home, which features his collection of artwork and furniture by renowned artists and designers.
NYC PICKS

The Imaging Alliance Salutes Photographers Who Give Back

The Imaging Alliance salutes photographers who have used their talents to heighten awareness of important social and cultural issues through the power of imaging. Sophie Brill (BFA 2017 Photography and Video) will be honored with the Anthony M. Ruotolo Photojournalism Award. Honorees will give presentations, and a Q&A and reception will follow. Tickets $10 for SVA students, faculty and staff with code SVASTUDENT; purchase here. 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Women's Suffrage Turns 100! Part 2: Reshaping Politics

Brooklyn Historical Society director of public history Julie Golia leads this discussion with Christine Quinn, the first female speaker of the New York City Council and former mayoral candidate, and A'Shanti F. Gholar, political director for Emerge America, the only organization dedicated to recruiting, training and empowering Democratic women to run for public office. Tickets $5; purchase here. 6:30pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street.

Performa 17

A series of live performances that showcase the essential role of art in society. Event list here. Through Sunday, November 19. Ticket prices vary; use code SVAPERFORMA5OFF to get $5 off general admission tickets. Locations vary by event.
TAKE NOTE

NYC General Election Tomorrow

New York City will host its general election tomorrow, November 7. If you are registered to vote in the city, you are encouraged to take advantage of this opportunity to make your voice heard. Don't know where to start? Check out the NYC Voter Guide to find out who is running, what their platforms are and what proposal initiatives to expect on your ballot. Additional resource links can be found in the Student Affairs section of sva.edu, Voter Registration Information dropdown.

MPS Directing Information Session

MPS Directing presents its fall 2017 information session, an opportunity to tour the facility, meet with the chair, faculty, department staff, current students and alumni, and learn about the program. RSVP here. Thursday, November 9. 6:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.

DOC NYC U: School of Visual Arts Showcase

MFA Social Documentary Film is partnering with DOC NYC, the preeminent festival for documentaries in the U.S., to present a showcase of six films by the program's current students. Tickets $12; purchase here. Friday, November 10, 5:00pm. IFC Center, 323 Sixth Avenue.

Good Art, Bad City: A Pop-Up Shop by Artistic Noise

Members of the nonprofit Artistic Noise present a night of participatory art-making, where the New York City community can help them transform the MPS Art Therapy Project Space into their vision of a pop-up shop. Friday, November 10, 5:00pm. MPS Art Therapy Project Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Faculty and Staff Appreciation Week

SVA Campus Store presents Faculty and Staff Appreciation Week from Monday, November 13 – Friday, November 17. Stop in for discounts on select Apple laptops and iPad Pro models and spin the prize wheel for up to 25% off SVA branded merchandise. All shoppers will receive a free gift and be entered into a weekly raffle for great prizes. Stop by the store before the event to set up your SVA Campus Store Technology Loan and recycle your old tech with Encore! for a gift card towards a future purchase. Email campusstore@sva.edu for more information. SVA Campus Store, 207 East 23rd Street.

Copyright Fundamentals for Art Information Professionals

SVA Library presents Greg Cram, the associate director of copyright and information policy at the New York Public Library, who will present tools and strategies for addressing the intellectual property, privacy and contract rights associated with works in museum and library collections. Tuesday, November 14, 7:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
