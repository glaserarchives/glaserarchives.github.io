Black History All the Time

Students Exploring Futuristic Building Technologies
The work of BFA Interior Design student Xinze Li has been featured in a Metropolis article on how students are envisioning the next generation of building technology as a vehicle for social change.
NYC PICKS

Picture the Dream: The Story of the Civil Rights Movement Through Children’s Books
An exhibition that delves into the events, people and themes of the civil rights movement through children’s picture books. Through Sunday, July 24. Admission is pay-as-you-wish from 6:00 – 8:00pm on Fridays. New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West, Joyce B. Cowin Women’s History Gallery.

Whitney Biennial 2022: Quiet as It’s Kept
Featuring an intergenerational and interdisciplinary group of 63 artists and collectives whose works reflect the challenges, complexities and possibilities of the American experience today, the 2022 Whitney Biennial takes the pulse of the contemporary artistic moment. Student tickets are $18. Through Monday, September 5. The Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
