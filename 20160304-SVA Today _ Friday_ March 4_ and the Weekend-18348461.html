Friday, March 4, 2016, and the Weekend
Photo by BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects Chair John McIntosh.
Inside Disney's Zootopia
Disney animator Darrin Butters recently visited BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects to discuss working on the new film Zootopia, which opens today. Student Mitchell Jao wrote about the experience for SVA Close Up. Catch a free screening of the film on Saturday, March 12, 4:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
A Break for Spring Break
SVA Today will be off next week for spring break, and will resume on Monday, March 14.
8:00pm: Titmouse 5-Second Animation Night
Titmouse, an animation studio founded by Chris (BFA 1994 Animation) and Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1994 Film and Video), presents a screening of animation odds-and-ends and cartoons from studio animators. $5 tickets for SVA students, faculty and staff (two ticket limit and one non-SVA guest only). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Tough Guys (Have Feelings Too)
The Montreal Gazette reviews the emotional children's book by Keith Negley (MFA 2013 Illustration as Visual Essay).
NYC Weekend Picks
Vertigo
A screening of Alfred Hitchcock's classic thriller, with remarks by documentarian Ric Burns. Free with pay-as-you-wish museum admission. Tonight, 7:00pm, The New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West.
New York Comic Book Marketplace Big Apple Con
A comics convention and expo, featuring artists Neal Adams, David Lloyd and more. Tickets are $20. Saturday, March 5, 10:00am - 6:00pm, Penn Plaza Pavilion, 401 Seventh Avenue.
New York Music New York
A screening of concert footage of punk and new wave bands in New York clubs from 1979 - 1983, from the show Inner-Tube. Admission is free. Sunday, March 6, 7:00pm, Howl! Arts, 6 East 1st Street.
Take Note
Discounted Regal Cinemas Movie Tickets
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office now offers discounted movie tickets to Regal Cinemas. Tickets to AMC Theatres are no longer available. Tickets can be purchased Monday, Wednesday and Friday, 2:00 - 5:00pm, at the George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine. Contact activities@sva.edu for more information.
Midnight Special
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of writer-director Jeff Nichols's sci-fi film Midnight Special. Nichols will answer questions following the screening. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Monday, March 7, 5:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Registration for 2016 - 2017
Online registration for undergraduate students will begin the week of March 14 and ends on Friday, March 25. Current BFA students should check their SVA email for details, including specific registration dates and times. For more information, including helpful guides and tutorial videos, visit sva.edu/regbook.
Margit Erb
MPS Digital Photography presents Margit Erb, founder and director of the Saul Leiter Foundation, which is dedicated to preserving the art and legacy of the influential New York City photographer and painter, discussing the 18 years she closely assisted Leiter. Tuesday, March 15, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Learning Through Performance: An Evening with Ernesto Pujol
MFA Art Practice presents site-specific performance artist, social choreographer and faculty member Ernesto Pujol discussing his work. Pujol is known for his silent, durational, walking performances as portraits of people and places within familiar landscapes and architecture that have nevertheless become invisible. Thursday, March 17, 6:30pm, 335 West 16th Street, ground floor media room.
