Updates From March 2023

Greetings SVA Community,


This is our second edition of our DEI Newsletter.  We will be sending out updates from our office on a monthly basis.  As we move into a new season of diversity, equity, and inclusion we will have more opportunities for the campus to participate on committees, forums, and events.  Please stay tuned for updates and for ways that you can be involved. 

We also want to recognize and send our condolences to families in Nashville, Tennessee that lost young people to gun violence. Loss of life due to gun violence and the loss of our youth should never be aligned, however, this is a narrative that we are experiencing way too often. The DEI Office and campus leaders are working on developing interventions to address this and various recent tragedies. Our office strongly suggests that you seek out the resources available to you via our Student Health and Counseling Services and through our Human Resources Office. 

Updates From the DEI Task Force


Anti-Racist / Anti- Bias Training:  Dr. Crystal Fleming
In preparation for implementing the strategic plan for diversity, equity, and inclusion, the DEI Task Force will be participating in a four- part Anti-Racist/Anti-Bias Pedagogy and Leadership Training.  These trainings will be led by seasoned professor and author, Dr. Crystal Fleming.  Dr. Fleming is a professor of Sociology and Africana Studies at Stony Brook University.  She is recognized for her anti-racist work and, more notably, for her book, How to be Less Stupid About Race.

     •  Introduction to Anti Racist Pedagogy, Principles and Praxis

     •  Developing Racial Literacy: The Impact of Visual Culture

     •  Conflict, Controversies, and Racial Trauma: 
        Strategies for Community Healing and Harm Reduction

     •  Institutionalizing Change: Roadmaps toward Justice
Discussions with MSU Students and Academic Chairs
Two of the DEI Task Force members, Gail Anderson (Chair, BFA Design & BFA Advertising, and the Creative Director Visual Arts Press) and Catherine Rosamond (Chair, MFA & MA Art Education) have developed a series of Brown Bag DEI Educational Sessions with the Chairs. These sessions are designed to provide a safer space for chairs to gain awareness about topics related to inclusion and equity within the classroom environment.


One of the goals in the DEI Strategic Plan is to develop stronger relationships between students and professionals at SVA. With the hopes of strengthening the relationships between the academic departments and students, Gail and Cathy invited members of the Multicultural Student Union (MSU) to share their collective lived and academic experiences.  On March 22 and March 23, two private conversations with members of MSU were moderated by the Associate Director of Student Affairs, Maria Rovira-McCune. In a Zoom format, Maria asked students a series of pre-screened questions from the planning committee, while the chairs actively listened.  These conversations are one set of many discussions that will serve as a strategy to impact pedagogy, curriculum, and sense of belonging within the classroom.

Some of the themes that emerged from these discussions will have an impact on the following: 

     •  Avoiding tokenism and student isolation

     •  Increasing diversity within the curriculum and among the faculty

     •  Embracing nuances of current events

     •  Respecting the intersectionality of identities

     •  Graduate student experience

DEI x Library West

On March 28th, the DEI Office and Library West collaborated to celebrate Women’s History Month through a collage event, Something You Can Feel. The workshop included a mini lecture about women collage artists, such as SVA alum Mickalene Thomas. They had refreshments, snacks and supplies for students to make their own collages. This was the first event organized in collaboration with SVA Library West for a monthly series called, IRL (In Real Life): making art and friends. 

Schomburg Comics in Color Conference: April 14-15

The Schomburg Research Center will be hosting the 11th Annual Black Comics Festival on April 14-15.  This festival will bring together,  “...animators, Blerds, bloggers, cosplay lovers, fans, families, illustrators, independent publishers, and writers to celebrate Black comic books and graphic novels and provides a platform to get the works directly to readers.” Registration is free.
