STAFF PROFILE: ALLISON HEWITT WARD 
In addition to working for Art Practice, Allison Hewitt Ward is a writer and critic. She is the co-editor of Caesura, an online art publication, as well as a contributor to Even Magazine, for which she recently reviewed Georgia O'Keeffe, Carol Rama and Sturtevant exhibitions, in an article titled "The Feminine Mistake." Her writing can also be seen in the October edition of The Brooklyn Rail, in which she reviewed the Kara Walker exhibition at Sikkema Jenkins & Co. 

Hewitt Ward will be representing SVA Art Practice this Saturday, October 22nd in San Francisco, and on Saturday, November 4th in Chicago at the National Portfolio Day Graduate Events. Stop by, show her your work and learn more about the MFA Art Practice program! 
Mathias Kessler, Ilulissat S010

OTHER NEWS
In this month's Brooklyn Rail Ernesto Pujol (AP faculty) critiques "The Cult of Creative Failure," Anthony Hawley (AP 2014) reviews Ad Minolti, Hovey Brock (AP 2015) writes about Amanda Ross-Ho and Peter Saul's "Fake News," and Allison Hewitt Ward (AP staff) discusses Kara Walker's hit show at Sikkema Jenkins. 

Work from Jason Mena (AP 2019) is included in the group show, "Relational Currents: Contemporary Art of the Caribbean Archipelago" at the Museum of Latin American Art.  

"Visions of Nature," an exhibition at Kunst Haus Wien that examines our relationship with nature includes work by Mathias Kessler (AP 2013). 
Dara Birnbaum (AP Faculty), Still from Kiss the Girls: Make Them Cry
 
Work by Art Practice Faculty member Dara Birnbaum is currently on view at the Met Breuer in "Delirious: Art at the Limits of Reason, 1950-1980," an exhibition of postwar art organized by Kelly Baum. Birnbaum will be participating in a panel discussion about the exhibition on Friday, November 3rd at the museum.

Birnbaum's work will also be on view at the Dallas Museum of Art as part of  the museum's first-ever exhibition dedicated to time-based media, "Truth: 24 frames per second." The show opens on October 22nd and runs through January 28th. 
Brenda Perry-Herrera (AP 2014), The Felling
 
A solo exhibition of work from Brenda Perry-Herrera (AP 2014) opens this week on Thursday, October 19th at One Art Space in New York City. Born in Juarez, Mexico and raised at the US-Mexican border region of El Paso, Texas, Perry-Herrera's work explores themes of sociopolitical and ecological relevance. In 2010, she began investigating the lumber sold in her local hardware store, tracking it to a timber-felling site outside of Houston, Texas. It was from this research that she originated the idea of perceiving contemporary events in retrospect. Here, in her most recent work, the artist imagines a world where trees are only a memory in a time when natural and man-made disasters deplete bucolic environments.
