10:00am and 4:00pm | Career Development Virtual Office Hours
Join SVA Career Development's weekly virtual office hours today, 10:00 – 11:00am and 4:00 – 5:00pm ET. During both one-hour sessions, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

11:30am | MFA Design for Social Innovation Thesis Show
MFA Design for Social Innovation presents a live stream of the graduating class of 2021's thesis projects. The show will highlight a year of collaborations with community partners to address various social issues. 11:30am – 4:30pm ET. Register here.

6:00pm | Derrick Alston: From SVA to ESPN
Join Career Development and BFA Design for a talk with Derrick Alston (BFA 2003 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects), coordinating designer of motion graphics at ESPN Creative Studio. Alston will talk about his journey from SVA to ESPN, how he learned to adapt to a constantly evolving creative environment and why diversity needs to become a core value in the design world. 6:00pm ET. Register here to join on Zoom.

6:30pm | The 100: Night of 100 Drawings
Visual artist and longtime SVA faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts) will host this free workshop—a nonstop marathon of 100 fast-paced figure drawings from life, complemented with exercises where participants draw from imagination and observation. Presented by SVA Continuing Education, BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the Honors Program. 6:30 – 9:00pm ET. Register here to get a Zoom link.

SOCIALLY DISTANCED PICK

Art and Empathy at Brooklyn Museum
Join art therapist Sarah Pousty and Brooklyn Museum educator Dalila Scruggs as they make space for self-care, conversation and connection. Today, they'll explore a work of art by Lorraine O'Grady through close looking and discussion, then participants will create artworks of their own. Hosted in partnership with the Brooklyn Public Library’s Healthy Communities Initiative. 3:00 – 5:00pm ET. Free; register here.
