DOMCAKE on Designing for the New Threads App

Sang Hyoun Han (BFA 2018 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects), also known as DOMCAKE, talks about being a key design team member for Instagram’s new service and Twitter rival, Threads.

July’s Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios last month include a book for English-learning beginners in China, SVA Risograph works and a colorful animation edited around an existing TED Talk.

SVA Career Development will hold their last virtual office hour of the summer on Thursday, August 17, 10:00 – 11:00am. A career counselor will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. SVA IN THE PRESS

Ja’Tovia Gary for The Cut

Ja’Tovia Gary (MFA 2014 Social Documentary) talks about her favorite media, connection to Toni Morrison and her film Quiet As It’s Kept in an interview with The Cut.

A Tour of Olympia Gayot’s “Effortlessly Cool” Office

Olympia Gayot (BFA 2003 Fine Arts), director of womenswear at J.Crew, takes Elle Decor on a tour of her Manhattan office and talks about the objects, art and furniture that fill her world.

Artsy’s List of 10 Emerging Painters Born in the 2000s

Darin Cooper (BFA 2023 Fine Arts) is featured in Artsy’s list of 10 emerging painters born in the 2000s.

Amy’s Big Brother

The prequel to BonHyung Jeong’s (BFA 2018 Cartooning) graphic novel Kyle’s Little Sister is set to be released in December. Via Bleeding Cool.

NYC PICKS

“In Being Double: The Pace Staff Show”

An exhibition of artworks by Pace Gallery’s international staff members, featuring work by Amanda Kopp (BFA 2011 Fine Arts). Through Friday, August 18. Pace Gallery New York, 540 West 25th Street.

India Day Parade

Celebrate India Independence day at New York City’s 41st annual India Day Parade, featuring performances, food vendors, appearances by Bollywood celebrities and more. Free. Sunday, August 20, 12:00 – 6:00pm. The parade begins at East 38th Street and proceeds down Madison Avenue.

“A Happy Beginning”

A group exhibition spotlighting Asian artists emerging in the New York art scene, including 2023 MFA Fine Arts alumni Lanyi Gao, Jingyao Huang, Katinka Huang, Nianxin Li and Tianshu Zhang, as well as Suyi Xu (MFA 2022 Fine Arts). Through Sunday, August 27. Latitude Gallery, 64A Bayard Street.

Real Rap: Hip-Hop Star Power on Screen

In celebration of the 50th anniversary of hip hop the Museum of the Moving Image presents a film series focused on hip-hop artists who have appeared on the big screen from the 1990s to the 2000s. Student tickets $11. Through Saturday, October 21. Screening days and times vary; learn more here. Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35th Avenue, Queens.

“Interest in Humanity: Portraits of Yesterday and Today”

A group exhibition centered around portraiture featuring selected works by both mid-20th-century masters and contemporary emerging artists including MFA Fine Arts alumni Helia Chitsazan (2023) and Suyi Xu (2022). Through Sunday, October 22. Fou Gallery, 410 Jefferson Avenue, #1, Brooklyn.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Create a Video/Animation, Win $500

SVA Theatre seeks a 10- to 20-second pre-roll video (animated or live action) to play before film screenings shown during this September’s After School Special 2023: SVA’s Alumni Film and Animation Festival.


Set the video to music that you have created yourself, own the copyright for, have obtained a license for, or is in the public domain, and please note its source when submitting. Download logos for the festival, SVA and SVA Theatre here. View examples of past winning videos here and here.


Upload submissions to Vimeo or YouTube, and send an email with a link to the video, your name, graduation year and program major to Adam Natale, director of SVA Theatre, at anatale@sva.edu, by Friday, August 25, 5:00pm. Email Natale with any questions. Final video format should be a digital file (e.g., QuickTime ProRes). The winner will receive $500.

“Underground Images: A History”

SVA Galleries presents an exhibition of the more than 200 posters from its iconic “subway series,” which has been a mainstay of New York City transit station walls over the past 75 years. Tuesday, August 29 – Saturday, October 14. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
