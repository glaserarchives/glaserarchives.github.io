Tuesday, December 15, 2015
10 Last-Minute Book Ideas for the Holidays
The holiday season is in full swing, and SVA Close Up has 10 books by alumni and faculty members that are perfect for anyone on your gift-buying list.
Last Chance: "Almost Home"
An exhibition curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellows Valerie Amend, Becky Nahom, Ikechukwu Onyewuenyi and Vera Petukhova. The works on view reflect the different strands of "home," as defined by geography, built and virtual space, domestic life and memory. Closes today at 7:00pm, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
2:00pm: Smart Classroom Training
SVA Audio Visual Services presents an information session on how to use the College's "smart" classroom technology. Topics covered include classroom equipment, trouble-shooting techniques and technical support information. RSVP to rbarton@sva.edu. 136 West 21st Street, Room 830F.
6:00pm: Christiane Paul in Conversation with Jon Ippolito and Joanne McNeil
Christiane Paul, adjunct curator of new media arts at the Whitney Museum of American Art and associate professor in the School of Media Studies at The New School, leads a conversation with writers Jon Ippolito and Joanne McNeil, both of whom are recipients of the Thoma Foundationâ€™s inaugural Digital Arts Writing Awards. Presented by The Thoma Foundation and MFA Fine Arts. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:15pm: Screenwriter Charles Randolph and Concussion
SVA Filmmaker's Dialogue presents screenwriter Charles Randolph discussing his work on The Big Short, starring Ryan Gosling and Brad Pitt. Following the discussion there will be a preview screening of Concussion, starring Will Smith as a doctor up against the NFL. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:00pm: United Photo Industries
In 2011, Sam Barzilay, Laura Roumanos and Dave Shelley founded United Photo Industries, a Brooklyn-based, photographic art-presenting cooperative. Their annual festival, Photoville, takes place in repurposed shipping containers and has become the largest photographic event in New York City. Presented by MPS Digital Photography. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

The Ongoing Battle for Studio Space in New York
Crain's New York profiles BFA Fine Arts and BFA Visual & Critical Studies faculty member Steve DeFrank (MFA 1990 Fine Arts), among other New York artists, in a story about the struggle for artists to find affordable studio space, from Chelsea to Bushwick.
NYC picks of the day
Don DeLillo in Conversation with Antonio Monda
The acclaimed writer discusses post-war Italy, neorealist film and the influence of Michelangelo Antonioni's Red Desert (1964) on his work. $10 for students, $20 general admission. 6:30pm, Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, 1071 Fifth Avenue.
Jean Tinguely
An exhibition of sculptures by the artist, whose works incorporated salvaged and found objects, often from junkyards. Through Saturday, December 19, Gladstone Gallery, 530 West 21st Street.
Take Note
Correction: Chosen Name Declaration Form
Yesterday's SVA Today mistakenly reported that students, faculty and staff could change how their name appears on all College records. Preferred names will be used wherever possible; however, legal names will continue to be required for certain documents. To download a Chosen/Preferred Name Declaration Form, click on the link in the headline.
Off the Wall: Exploring the Intersection of Design and Graffiti
MFA Products of Design presents students discussing their semester-long investigation of street art and graffiti in New York City. Friday, December 18, 7:00pm, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
