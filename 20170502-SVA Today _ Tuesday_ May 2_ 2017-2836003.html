SVA Today
Tuesday, May 2, 2017

KAWS Exclusive [Video]

Pop artist and designer Brian Donnelly (BFA 1996 Illustration), also known as KAWS, talks about his beginnings as a street artist, the influence of New York City and fellow alumnus Keith Haring on his work and how he "lucked out" by going to SVA.
EVENTS

6:00pm | "Beyond Optics" Reception

An exhibition of work by students selected by a jury of their peers, featuring a combination of conflicting emotions that reflect the overwhelming condition of daily life and our different ways of making sense of it. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, May 20. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor

6:00pm | BFA Cartooning and BFA Illustration Open Studios

BFA Cartooning and BFA Illustration present projects by their graduating classes. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 310 East 22nd Street, 7th floor.

6:00pm | MFA Computer Art Thesis Presentations

Members of MFA Computer Art's class of 2017 present their thesis works. The presentations showcase the graduates’ unique approaches to the themes and modes of creative self-expression. Tonight and tomorrow, 6:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

6:00pm | MFA Illustration as Visual Essay Thesis Exhibition Reception

MFA Illustration as Visual Essay presents an exhibition of work by its graduating class. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, May 20. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

6:15pm | The Circle Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of The Circle, starring Tom Hanks and Emma Watson. Followed by a Q&A with the producer, Anthony Bregman. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

"Defying Categorization"

Artnet News lists Joshua Citarella (BFA 2010 Photography) as one of 10 contemporary artists taking photography in new directions.
NYC PICK

"Two for Tuesday" at Quad Cinema

Screenings of Jeff Lieberman's horror films Just Before Dawn and Blue Sunshine for the price of one. Tickets $12 – $15; purchase here. 7:00pm. Quad Cinema, 34 West 13th Street.

"She Inspires"

An exhibition featuring 60 contemporary artists, including Nichole Washington (MPS 2016 Digital Photography), with works honoring inspirational women. Opening reception tonight, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Through Saturday, May 20. The Untitled Space, 45 Lispenard Street, Unit 1W.
TAKE NOTE

MFA Art Writing Thesis Reading

Graduating students in the MFA Art Writing class of 2017 present their writing and research to the public in this annual thesis reading. Monday, May 8, 6:00 – 8:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor
IMAGE OF THE DAY
Nichole Washington (MPS 2016 Digital Photography), Power of We, 2017, via New York. The magazine recently published an article about the exhibition "She Inspires," opening today at the Untitled Space, and featuring this piece.
