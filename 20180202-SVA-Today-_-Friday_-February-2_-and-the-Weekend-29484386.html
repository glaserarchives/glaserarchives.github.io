EVENTS

11:00am | SVA Library West Opening

SVA Library staff welcomes the College community to the soft opening of its newly renovated SVA Library West branch. Eat cookies, tour the space and learn about library services, including the new video game room. 11:00am – 4:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

2:00pm | Self Care for Creatives

MPS Art Therapy presents this workshop, where you will learn to prevent creative burnout and discover new ways of incorporating creativity. RSVP to Val Sereno at vsereno@sva.edu. 2:00 – 3:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

3:00pm | 2018 VASA Election Information Session

Students interested in running for the Visual Arts Student Association student government positions must attend one of two information sessions in order to run. Candidates run as a team of four, including president, vice president, treasurer and student senate chair. Today, 3:00pm, and Thursday, February 15, 6:30pm. SVA Student Engagement and Leadership, 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. Election packets will be available at sessions and are due Wednesday, February 28, 3:00pm.

Rewriting a Family's History

Artsy on how MFA Fine Arts faculty member Laurel Nakadate used photoshop to introduce her recently deceased mother to her newborn son in her new series "The Kingdom."
NYC PICKS

First Saturdays at the Brooklyn Museum

Free art and entertainment, focused on the theme of Black History Month. Tonight, 5:00 – 11:00pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway.

FIAF Animation First Festival

A French animation festival with award-winning feature films and shorts, virtual reality, interactive workshops and panel discussions. Through Sunday, February 4. Ticket prices and locations vary.

Edvard Munch: Between the Clock and the Bed

An exhibition featuring 43 of artist Edvard Munch's landmark compositions created over 60 years. Through Sunday, February 4. The Met Breuer, 945 Madison Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Information Session: SVA in Cambridge, England

Join SVA Destinations for an information session to learn about the course Writing Visual Culture in Cambridge, England, taking place July 22 – August 5. Worth 3 Humanities and Sciences credits. Thursday, February 8, 6:30 – 7:30pm. Room 605C.

Titmouse Presents "5 Second Animation Night"

An annual event where Titmouse animators create whatever goodies have been floating around in their minds all year. Titmouse, founded by SVA alumni Chris and Shannon Prynoski, employs many SVA graduates, and several animators/artists will be in attendance. Their work includes China, IL; Metalocalypse; Motorcity; Randy Cunningham; Superjail!; The Venture Brothers; commercials and short films. Tickets are limited; purchase here for $5. Friday, March 16, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
