The Way We See Us
SVA celebrates Black History Month with “The Way We See Us,” a new exhibition presented by the Black Student Union featuring multidisciplinary works by Black artists at SVA that celebrate Black individuality as a form of activism. Co-curated by current undergraduate students Angelica Dalzon (BFA Illustration) and Kyra Husbands (BFA Visual & Critical Studies). Through Monday, February 21. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Events

6:30pm | Fab Friday with Mike Dupree
VFX artist Mike Dupree has created stunning visual effects for a wide range of media from television to commercials, feature films, game cinematics and music videos. You might recognise his work from the opening title sequence of Netflix's Patriot Act with Hasan Minaj, Epic Games's Gears of War 5 and HBO's Game of Thrones. With over 10 years of experience in this field, Dupree has amassed a diverse range of skills that informs his ability to solve problems creatively and efficiently. He will be joining us to share how he started, show us some dazzling work and impart some creative problem solving wisdom. Presented by BFA Advertising and BFA Design. Tonight, 6:30pm ET, on Zoom.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Acid Garden
Charles Moffett presents “Acid Garden,” a group exhibition featuring works from alumni Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts), Judy Mannarino (BFA 1981 Fine Arts), Kenny Rivero (BFA 2010 Fine Arts), Steve DeFrank (MFA 1990 Fine Arts), Joey Gonnella (BFA 2020 Visual & Critical Studies), Michelle Weinberg (BFA 1983 Fine Arts), Gabrielle Benak (BFA 2019 Fine Arts) and BFA Illustration and Cartooning chair emeritus Thomas Woodruff. Through Saturday, February 19. Charles Moffett, 511 Canal Street, second floor.

Announcing the Fall 2021 Term Honors Recipients
A total of 797 students from SVA's 11 undergraduate majors have been awarded Term Honors for fall 2021, and the list of recipients is now available on MySVA (accessible from your MyID dashboard). To be included, students must successfully complete 12 credits of coursework, without any grades of incomplete and earn a term GPA within the top 20% of all students in their major department. Students whose term GPA is within the top 5% of all students in their major department receive High Term Honors. Congratulations to the honorees!
