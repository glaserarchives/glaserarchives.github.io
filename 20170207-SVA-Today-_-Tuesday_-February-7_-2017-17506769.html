SVA Today
Tuesday, February 7, 2017

From SVA to Seattle

The Seattle Times on how a new gallery in Seattle is revamping "The Design of Dissent," an exhibition of political posters originally organized by SVA faculty members Milton Glaser and Mirko Ilic in 2005.
EVENTS

2:00pm | The Studio Visit: Making Professional Connections

SVA Career Development presents this workshop, which will help you understand how to ask for studio visits, how to make the most of them, tips and best practices and how to follow up. RVSP required. 2:00 – 4:00pm. 133 West 21st Street, MFA Fine Arts Flex Space, 9th floor.

6:30pm | Sam Lubell: The Making of Never Built

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents author Sam Lubell who will talk about the process of writing his books Never Built Los Angeles and Never Built New York, and the challenge of working with designers to bring the books to life through exhibitions. 6:30 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Arthur Jafa in Conversation with Amy Taubin

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a screening of two films by Jafa: Dreams Are Colder Than Death (2013) and Love Is the Message, the Message Is Death (2016). Following the screening, he will talk with critic and faculty member Amy Taubin. RSVP here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

7:00pm | Gillian Laub

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by the filmmaker and photographer whose most recent project, Southern Rites, is the result of a decade exploring issues of racism in the American South. Part of the i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

8:00pm | MFA Visual Narrative Online Q&A Session

Department chair Nathan Fox will answer questions about the program and the application process. 8:00 – 10:00pm. For more information and to RSVP, click here.
PRESS PICK

"Caught Between Two Cultures"

The Los Angeles Times on Ooldouz Alaei Novin’s (MFA 2016 Photography, Video and Related Media) three-screen video installation Our Persian Garden, which examines the feelings of alienation that Novin and other Iranian immigrants have experienced.
NYC PICKS

A Seat at the Table: Women Writers of Color on Access, Ambition and Activism

Featuring readings, panel discussions and performances. Free; RSVP here. 7:00pm. Ace Hotel, 20 West 29th Street.

"AnOther Expedition: Monet’s Garden"

A solo exhibition of cyanotype prints made by artist Pipo Nguyen-duy in Claude Monet’s garden in Giverny, France. Free. Through Saturday, April 1. ClampArt, 247 West 29th Street, ground floor.
TAKE NOTE

Business Basics: Copyright and Contracts

SVA Career Development presents this information session, which will help artists understand why maintaining one’s copyright is important to long-term sustainability. RSVP required. Tomorrow, February 8, 6:15pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"Voyeur Village" at the RisoLAB

MFA Visual Narrative’s RisoLAB artist-in-residence Harvey Redding exhibits "Voyeur Village," an 800-piece three-dimensional cityscape created in the lab. It features multiple structures with open windows displaying scenes of erotic allure and intrigue. Opening event Friday, February 10, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Open Saturday, February 11 – Sunday, February 12, noon – 6:00pm. Prince Street Project Space, 127-B Prince Street.
