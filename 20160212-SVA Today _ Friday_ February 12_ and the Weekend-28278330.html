SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, February 12, and the Weekend
Pushing the Boundaries of Publishing
BFA Fine Arts students, staff and faculty will represent SVA at Printed Matter's L.A. Art Book Fair, which opens today. Thinking outside traditional publishing modes, students will exhibit their zines, digital embroidery, 3D-printed objects and more.
5:30pm: "Take Care" Reception
SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents an exhibition of student posters on the theme of self-care, created for this year's HOPE Competition. The winner will be announced at tonight's reception. Through Saturday, February 20, SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
7:00pm: Galentine's Day
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office and VASA present a dinner buffet of breakfast foods, female-empowering crafts and a raffle for a prize donated by Babeland. Open to SVA students who donate $3 or more to support the National Network of Abortion Funds. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
7:30pm: Self-Defense Tutorial
SVA Residence Life and Student Health and Counseling Services present Sensei Michelle Gay and George Washington Residence RAs Cat and Ken discussing self-defense tips and techniques. George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine.
Saturday: "tie in" Opens
MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition of work by second-year department students and the people they work with through their internships. Curated by Liz DelliCarpini, department internship coordinator. Reception Thursday, February 25, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, March 12, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Sunday: Silence of the Lambs
The SVA Theatre presents a 25th anniversary screening of the Academy Award-winning film The Silence of the Lambs (1991), starring Jodie Foster, Anthony Hopkins and Scott Glenn. Free and open to the public. Seating is first-come, first-served. Doors open approximately 30 - 45 minutes prior to showtime. 7:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Marilyn Minter's "Pretty/Dirty" World
The MFA Fine Arts faculty member spoke with ARTnews about her life and work. Minter's traveling retrospective "Pretty/Dirty" opens at the Brooklyn Museum this fall.
NYC Weekend Picks
"Beauty": Cooper Hewitt Design Triennial
The fifth installment of the museum’s signature contemporary design exhibition series, "Beauty" focuses on aesthetic innovation and celebrates design as a creative endeavor that engages the mind, body and senses. Admission is free with SVA ID. Open today through Sunday, August 21, Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.
Superstar: The Life and Times of Andy Warhol
A screening of a documentary about the pop icon and artist. General admission is $18, $12 for students and seniors. Tonight, 7:00pm, The Morgan Library and Museum, 225 Madison Avenue.
Bloody Valentine's Weekend
A Valentine's Day-themed haunted house tour. Tours run Friday through Sunday. Click through for schedule. Tickets start at $30. Blood Manor, 163 Varick Street.
Ice Festival
The Central Park Conservancy presents a winter festival featuring ice sculptures, a "silent disco," a creative photo challenge and food trucks. 3:00 - 7:00pm, Saturday, February 13, Central Park Naumburg Bandshell.
Take Note
Duplicates, Dummies and Dolls
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by Sara Mejia Kriendler (MFA 2013 Fine Arts), which explores modes of production through her use of discarded Styrofoam and plastic packaging as a representation of waste and excess. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Becky Nahom. Thursday, February 18, through Thursday, March 3. Reception Thursday, February 18, 6:30pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
NY Photo Salon: Patricia Lopez, Greg Miller and J. Sybylla Smith
MPS Digital Photography presents photographers Patricia Lopez, Greg Miller (BFA 1990 Photography) and J. Sybylla Smith discussing their work. Each photographer will answer questions following their 20-minute presentation. Thursday, February 18, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
