SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, October 29, 2015
Higher Ground by Kate Gilmore. Image courtesy of ArtPrize.
Kate Gilmore on Winning ArtPrize
Following the announcement that she won the 2015 ArtPrize Juried Grand Prize for her installation piece Higher Ground, SVA Close Up spoke with the MFA Fine Arts faculty member and alumnus about her work and the life-changing award.
6:00pm: "Techtonic Shift" Reception
MFA Computer Art presents an exhibition of work by department alumni and faculty members, featuring prints, videos and interactive installations that incorporate a variety of analog and digital media. Through Wednesday, November 25, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
7:30pm: "Hard as a Rock" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that reinterprets Kay Rosen’s 1991 video Sisyphus, which addresses the subject of futility through 72 misspellings of the name “Sisyphus.” Curated by curatorial fellow Lal Bahcecioglu. Through Thursday, November 12, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Press pick of the day
No Such Thing as Bad Weather
Lauren Redniss' new book, Thunder & Lightning: Weather Past, Present, Future, which was published on Tuesday by Random House and illustrates the diversity of weather phenomena on earth, is profiled in The New York Times.
NYC picks of the day
Rick Meyerowitz's National Lampoon
An exhibition of archival and original artwork created by illustrator and author Rick Meyerowitz for the satirical magazine National Lampoon. Opening reception at 6:00pm. Through December 5, RARE at Glen Horowitz Bookseller, 17 West 54th Street.
Design Talks: Walter Hood
National Design Award-winning landscape architect Walter Hood discusses his renovation of Cooper Hewitt's Arthur Ross Terrace and Garden. Tickets are $8 for students, $10 for members and $15 general admission. 6:30pm, Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.
Take Note
Julia Kaganskiy: Networks as Generators of Creativity
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents NEW INC. director Julia Kaganskiy discussing the creative potential of collaboration and cross-disciplinary networking in both culture and commerce. Tuesday, November 3, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Society for Experiential Graphic Design XLab Conference
SEGD presents XLab, an intersection of technology, place and design, featuring leading experiential and environmental graphic designers, thinkers and makers discussing their projects and insights. Free for SVA students. Thursday, November 5, 9:00am, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MFA Visual Narrative Information Session
MFA Visual Narrative presents an information session for prospective students, featuring an opportunity to speak with department chair Nathan Fox, alumni, faculty and students, and to tour the new RisoLab. Saturday, November 7, 4:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
