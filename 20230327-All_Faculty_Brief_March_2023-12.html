All Faculty Brief

March 2023

Dear Colleagues,

Happy Women’s History Month.  I hope this installment of the All Faculty Brief finds you well and ready for spring. 

As SVA continues to recover from the devastating effects of the pandemic, we have, over the last year, been able to restore annual increases to faculty salaries of 4% for the fall of 2022, after a retroactive increase of 7% in the spring of 2022. 

After reviewing what we believe will be a slightly higher than expected student retention rate, and early indications of a strong incoming class for the 2023-24 academic year, I am pleased to announce that the sabbatical program, which was suspended during the pandemic, has been restored.  A description of the program can be found in the SVA Handbook.  

Beginning Wednesday, March 28, the Office of the Provost will begin accepting sabbatical applications and nominations, from the chair of your department of record, for the 2023-24 academic year.  Review of applications will begin immediately, with a priority submission deadline of April 17.  Notifications will be issued on a rolling basis.

The sabbatical application process and forms are available on the Academic Affairs page under the Offices tab in the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas.  Applications for the 2024-25 academic year will be available in October 2023.

I realize this news comes late in the academic year, but we didn’t want to delay the restoration of this important benefit for another year.

If you have any questions regarding the sabbatical application process, please contact Michael Severance, Academic Affairs Manager, at provost@sva.edu.

In this installment of the Brief, you will find useful and interesting updates from the SVA Library, the Registrar, the Director of DEI, and the Office of Learning Technology.

As always, we appreciate your dedication and commitment to our students and to SVA.


Wishing you all the best,
Chris

SVA Library Adds New Digital Resources

The Library is always adding resources, both physical and digital, to expand its collections. Check out these new digital resources, now available for you and your students:

Art & Architecture ePortal
The A&AePortal platform features authoritative scholarship in the history of art, architecture, decorative arts, photography, and design, and also provides invaluable content in related disciplines, such as African American studies, religious studies, women’s studies, environmental studies, and visual culture. It contains 300 titles across 25 subject areas and 60,000 associated images.

Interaction of Color 
A&AePortal also contains the complete digital, interactive edition of Josef Albers' seminal work, Interaction of Color. With an additional 60 plates from the 1963 Complete Edition, this new digital edition is also packed with elegant and innovative features that help users explore the book's essential concepts and experiment with color the way Albers intended. Users need to create a personal account to access the content.

Fashion and Race Database
Containing original essays, profiles, and object analyses primarily authored by racialized and minoritized scholars and writers, the Fashion and Race and Database provides a roadmap for lasting change in the fashion system, as it offers lessons and resources that diversify how we understand fashion. Users will need to create a personal account.

Find all the Library's subscription databases here, and our guide to finding and accessing digital resources here. Happy researching!

Spring 2023 Reminders From the Office of the Registrar


Registration Information for the 2023-2024 Academic Year
Registration for the 2023-2024 academic year began on March 6. You can monitor enrollment in MyServices for any classes that you are scheduled to teach. Please note that students are not allowed to register for closed classes, even with instructor approval. Students will have opportunities to add and drop courses during the official course adjustment periods.

Faculty Absence Form
As a reminder, all faculty must inform SVA when they are absent from a course they are teaching, even if a make-up session will be scheduled at a later time. To streamline this process, faculty members should complete the Faculty Absence Form when they are absent. You will need to be logged in to your MyID account to complete the form.

Attendance
Please continue to record student attendance in Canvas on a regular basis. It is extremely important for the College to receive accurate attendance in a timely manner, as we use the information to identify students who may be struggling. We have a network of administrators (advisors, student affairs staff) who provide additional support to students when warranted, which is imperative to student success efforts. Attendance data also helps us to identify students who may have stopped attending, which is incredibly important for financial aid compliance and enrollment forecasting.

Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form
Please note that the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form is available on your MyID dashboard. The form may be completed at any point in the semester if you feel that a student is underperforming academically. Once completed, the form is sent to the student, the student's advisor, and the Chair of the student's major department.

Please do not consider this form a penalty to your students. Rather, this is a tool to make clear to students that you have serious concerns about their academic performance and to give them actionable suggestions for what they will need to do in order to pass your class. Ideally, this notice provides students the impetus to get back on track before it is too late.

OLT's Faculty Ignite Video Series in Media Space: Navigating the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation

In case you missed the recent SVA Faculty Ignite video about Navigating the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation, check it out here.  Instructors can use this evaluation to officially share their concerns about a student with that student's advisor and department. The ultimate goal is to make sure that students have the support that they need to succeed, whether that is extra help in a particular course or assistance related to larger issues outside of the classroom.
