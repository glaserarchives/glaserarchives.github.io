SVA Today
Tuesday, March 29, 2016
From Freelance to Freaknik to Film [Video]
SVA Features talks to Chris Prynoski (BFA 1994 Animation), who founded Titmouse Animation Studio in 2000 with his wife and fellow SVA alumnus Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1994 Film and Video). Nerdland, the first feature film produced by Titmouse which is also directed by Prynoski, will premiere at the SVA Theatre on Thursday, April 14, 8:30pm, as part of the Tribeca Film Festival. Tickets go on sale today.
6:00pm: No Sellouts
An MFA Fine Arts panel discussion featuring recent alumni speaking about their art-related businesses. Moderated by department chair Mark Tribe, panelists include Leah Dixon (2014), co-owner of Beverly's, a New York City bar and art space; Mike Ursuta (2007), co-founder of Ramiken Crucible gallery; and Brian Whiteley (2013), creative director of Satellite Art Show. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:00pm: "The Tide" Reception
A juried exhibition of student work, inspired by the inner workings of the mind. Through Saturday, April 9, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
6:30pm: Benjamin Prosky: Designing Culture
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Benjamin Prosky, Architizer co-founder and executive director of the American Institute of Architects New York Chapter (AIANY) and the Center for Architecture, discussing the role of contemporary design publications in addressing the challenges of presenting visually compelling architecture and design. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
7:00pm: Richard Tuschman
MPS Digital Photography presents photographer Richard Tuschman, best known for his “Hopper Meditations" series, discussing his practice and his current project, Once Upon A Time in Kazimierz, an open-ended novella told in photographs. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Turning Personal Tragedy into Art
Indiewire spoke with filmmaker Ana Maria Hermida (BFA 2009 Film and Video) about her new film La Luciérnaga (The Firefly), which is inspired by the aftermath of her brother's death and is contending for Best Foreign Film, Best Lead Actress and Best Supporting Actress at the 2016 Madrid International Film Festival.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY
The Two Warhols
New York University professor Thomas Crow discusses Andy Warhol's transition from successful commercial illustrator to fine artist. Admission is free for students with ID; $15 general admission. 6:30pm, The Morgan Library and Museum, 225 Madison Avenue.
Studio Job MAD HOUSE
An exhibition of the highly expressive and opulent work produced by Job Smeets and Nynke Tynagel. Admission is $12 for students, $16 general public. Through Sunday, August 21, Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.
TAKE NOTE
VASA Elections
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents a question-and-answer session with VASA candidates on Wednesday, March 30, 6:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. There will also be a candidate meet-and-greet on Monday, April, 4, noon - 1:00pm, SVA Student Center. Voting will begin on Tuesday, April 5. Stay tuned for more details. For questions, contact activities@sva.edu.
