Events

Today | Soft Boundary
“Soft Boundary” is a group exhibition that examines the everyday distortions that occur around us, curated by nine MA Curatorial Practice students under the supervision of curator and faculty member Noam Segal. Through Wednesday, April 27. Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 3rd floor, Brooklyn.

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm ET, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).

2:00pm | Love Letters to Survivors Art Event
April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services, the College’s Title IX Office, and New York City’s DOVE Program invite survivors and allies to show support for sexual-assault survivors and contribute to an art display of empowering art, affirmations and uplifting messages. To learn more about the #SurvivorLoveLetter movement, click here. Today, 2:00 – 4:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

5:00pm | SVA Student Community Forum
In response to the challenges we continue to face in these socially and politically fraught times, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion; Student Health and Counseling Services; and Humanities and Sciences are hosting an in-person forum today. Please join to share your voices and concerns, collectively explore solutions and come together as a community. Today, 5:00pm. 380 Second Avenue, 8th floor, Room 803B.

6:00pm | Identity, Art and Migration: Artist Refugees >From Nazi Germany and Today
BFA Visual & Critical Studies, the SVA Honors Program and The Fritz Ascher Society for Persecuted, Ostracized, and Banned Art presents a panel discussion probing historical and contemporary fault lines of persecution, migration, intolerance, cultural complexity and art. Today, 6:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:00pm | MA Curatorial Practice Year-End Exhibitions
MA Curatorial Practice presents the final projects by its class of 2022. Reception: Today, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Through Wednesday, April 27. Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, Brooklyn.

7:00pm | WSVA Middle School Dance
Join WSVA for a middle-school throwback dance party complete with stickers, soft drinks and classic hits from the early 2000s. RSVP for guaranteed entry and a goody box. Today, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

The Land of Scars Screening Screening
Contemporary artist Angelo Musco, whose work has been seen everywhere from Art Basel to the Venice Biennale, devoted four years of his life to The Land of Scars, telling his own story through a visual diary. A limited number of free tickets to the screening are available to SVA students. Email tsbcommunications@gmail.com by Wednesday, April 20, with the subject line “SVA Student Request,” and include your name and how many tickets you would like to request. The screening will take place Wednesday, April 27, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
