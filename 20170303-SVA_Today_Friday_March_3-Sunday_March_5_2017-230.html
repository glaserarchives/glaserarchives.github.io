SVA Today
Friday, March 3 - Sunday, March 5, 2017

The New Creators

Presenting the lineup for SVA's Talent Shows, the annual series of exhibitions, screenings and other events featuring work by SVA’s graduating students.

Spring Break Starts on Monday!

We wish everyone a restful break. Classes, and SVA Today, will resume on Monday, March 13.
EVENTS

10:00am | Study Abroad Information Session

Learn about spending a full semester abroad or participating in a short intensive in locations around the world. 10:00 – 11:00am. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 202.

6:30pm | Fold Origami, Embrace Diversity, Unite as a Community

SVA Library and the International Student Office present their last origami workshop for students, staff and faculty. Students can also become "origami ambassadors" to teach others origami. A collection of the origami will be installed at the SVA Library and the 24th Street Residence. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

8:00pm | Titmouse Five-Second Animation Night

SVA Theatre presents Five-Second Animation Night, where Titmouse artists and animators present the animated goodies that have been floating around in their minds all year. Tickets $5 – $10; purchase here. 8:00 – 10:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | Logan Screening

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of Logan, starring Hugh Jackman. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Tomorrow, March 4, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

"Design as a Practice"

Print presents fun facts about BFA Design faculty member Timothy Goodman (BFA 2007 Graphic Design), who will be a judge for the magazine's Regional Design Awards.
NYC PICKS

"Georgia O'Keeffe: Living Modern"

An exhibition that looks at how the renowned artist proclaimed her progressive lifestyle through a self-crafted persona. Through Sunday, July 23. Tickets $12 with SVA ID and include museum admission; purchase here with code BKMCORP17, or in person at the Membership Host Desk. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.

"Words for Water"

Indigenous women poets, writers and musicians use language, story and song to fight for environmental and cultural justice. Tickets $8 – $10; purchase here. Sunday, March 5, 4:00 – 6:00pm. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
TAKE NOTE

Orientation Leader Information Sessions

To learn more about becoming an orientation leader, attend an information session on Tuesday, March 14, 6:30pm, or Wednesday, March 15, 3:30pm. Applications are open online. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Strong Voices in Action

An open mic night of music, dance, poetry and all kinds of expression. Join in an artistic discussion about leadership, social justice and activism. If you are interested in participating, email Oliver Colbert at ocolbert@sva.edu. Thursday, March 16, 6:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
