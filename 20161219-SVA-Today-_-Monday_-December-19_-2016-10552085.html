PRESS PICK

Soundbreaking Success

The Hollywood Reporter has named Soundbreaking: Stories From the Cutting Edge of Recorded Music one of the best TV features of the year. It was produced and directed by Mayo Chermayeff, the chair of MFA Social Documentary Film.
NYC PICK

"Martin Scorsese"
An exhibition about the award-winning director’s career and life, featuring production materials, behind-the-scenes images from his films, objects from his childhood and more. Through April 23, 2017. $11 for students with SVA ID; $15 for adults. Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35th Avenue, Astoria.
TAKE NOTE

Call for Submissions: Black History Month Exhibition

SVA’s Student Engagement and Leadership Office and the Residence Life present the Black History Month Student Exhibition, opening in February 2017 and featuring works by black and African American student-artists of SVA. Any interested students who identify as black, African American and/or of the African diaspora may submit as many pieces as they would like, in any medium of their choosing. Deadline has been extended to January 3, 11:59pm. Selections will be announced January 9. To submit, click here. For more information, email Oliver Colbert, Coordinator of Student Engagement and Leadership, at ocolbert@sva.edu.

The Measured Summit

MFA Design for Social Innovation presents The Measured Summit, which will bring together leaders and practitioners from business, the social sector, foundations, technology and design to address how the design process contributes to social impact programs. For more information, visit measured.design. Tuesday, January 24, 8:00am – 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 23rd Street.

Give a Gift Subscription to NYTimes.com

The SVA Library has a Site License to NYTimes.com, which enables anyone with an SVA email address to register for their own subscription to NYTimes.com by using this link. Additionally, if you are a faculty member using the Times in your class and have a statement on your syllabus recommending NYTimes.com to your students, you are eligible to receive a gift subscription to NYTimes.com for a friend or family member for the semester. To give this gift, please send your spring syllabus, the number of students in your class and the contact information for the person you’d like to receive the free gift subscription to Michael Mooney at michael.mooney@nytimes.com. If you are not currently using the Times in your class but would like to learn more, contact Michael Mooney at the email address above.
