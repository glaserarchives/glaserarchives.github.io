Events

11:00am | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Members of the Asian and Asian American communities continue to face harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Faculty and staff who self-identify as AAPI are invited to join this virtual forum to connect on these or other topics for peer support. New members are welcome. If you are interested in participating, please fill out this Google Form to register. If you are unable to participate, you can still fill out the Google Form to indicate your interest in future opportunities and be included in future correspondence. Today, 11:00am – 12:30pm ET, on Zoom.

3:00pm | Spring Club Fair
Today is the last day of the Spring Club Fair. Clubs are a great way to be involved on campus and engage with people who have the same interests as you. There are many new clubs, so be sure to come to check them out. Email clubs@sva.edu if you have any questions. Today, 3:00 – 5:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Monday | Indigenous Futures: The Future Is a Territory to Defend
In collaboration with the SVA Indigenous Student Union, Humanities and Sciences faculty member Quito Ziegler and their class Advanced Studies in Collective Action present “Indigenous Futures: The Future Is a Territory to Defend.” Young climate activists from Futuros Indígenas and Legado Gaia will speak about Indigenous values, their experiences at the recent U.N. climate talks and the future of environmental movements. Co-sponsored by Humanities and Sciences and the Indigenous Student Union. Open to all SVA students and faculty. Monday, January 31, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom. RSVP here.

Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work from the SVA community that reflect on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.’s legacy and life’s work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA’s observance of Black History Month in February. Submissions are due Monday, January 31. For more information or to submit your work, click here.

Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American Harassment and Xenophobia
Hosted by Student Affairs, Hollaback will host a one-hour interactive bystander intervention training session to discuss the types of disrespect that Asian and Asian American folks are currently facing, the five strategies for intervention and how to prioritize your own safety while intervening. There will be time at the end of the session for practice, so you’ll leave feeling more confident about intervening the next time you see Anti-Asian/American harassment online or in person. Tuesday, February 1, 12:00 – 1:00pm ET, online. Register here.

VASA Elections Info Session
Do you want to build a team to become the VASA leadership next year? To qualify to run in the upcoming college-wide election, interested candidates must attend one information session. For more information, email activities@sva.edu. Wednesday, February 2, 1:00pm, SVA Library West, 133 West 21st Street, and Friday, February 11, 3:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Taxes 101: The Basics of Income and Sales Tax for Creative Individuals
Led by Minerva Financial Arts founder and Arts & Numbers author Elaine Grogan Luttrull, this workshop will review federal and state income tax basics for creative individuals and discuss common deductions claimed by creative entrepreneurs. We’ll also review common sales tax requirements as well as the obligation to investigate the laws in other states where artists make sales. Presented by SVA Career Development. Wednesday, February 2, 3:00 – 5:00pm ET, on Zoom. Register here.
