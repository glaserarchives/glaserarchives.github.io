October Exhibitions and Events

Fall is here and SVA’s October calendar is filled with collaborative exhibitions, topical lectures, the Masters Series, the MFA Social Documentary Film thesis showcase and more.

EVENTS

Today | “The Magician’s Breakfast: Recent Paintings by William Patterson”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a solo exhibition of recent paintings by William Patterson (MFA 2019 Fine Arts; BFA 2013 Visual & Critical Studies), on view through Friday, October 28. Reception: Thursday, October 6, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:30pm | MFA Social Documentary Film Information Session

MFA Social Documentary Film presents an information session about the program and its facilities, led by faculty and alumni who are ready to provide answers to questions about the program and application process. Tonight, October 4, 6:30 – 8:00pm, online.

Ongoing Exhibitions

  • •12th Annual SocDoc Thesis Showcase, through Sunday, October 9. Online; click here to register for online access.
  • •“Darwin’s Paradox: A Decade of Bio Art,” through Monday, October 17. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
  • •“Dot Dot Dot,” through Monday, October 17. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
  • •“The Masters Series: Lynsey Addario,” through Saturday, October 29. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
  • •“Enduring Heritage,” through Friday, December 30. Online and at Saint-Rémy, Saint-Paul, 13210 Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Chrissie Iles to Co-Curate the 2024 Whitney Biennial﻿

MA Curatorial Practice faculty member Chrissie Iles will be curating the 2024 Whitney Biennial, alongside Meg Onli. Via The Art Newspaper.

NYC PICK

“Allen Frame: Whereupon”

A solo exhibition of photography by BFA Photography and Video faculty member Allen Frame. Through Saturday, November 5. Gitterman Gallery, 3 East 66th Street, 1B.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Graduating Students: It’s Not Too Late! Submit Your Photos

Calling all 2023 graduating students (both graduate and undergrad)! The SVA 2023 Yearbook needs your photos to be complete. Students from the 2023 graduating class can submit up to three images of themselves to be included in the book. Help make it memorable and send in a photo! The yearbook is free to all 2023 graduates.

Nathan Fox at New York Comic Con

MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) will have a booth in Artist’s Alley at New York Comic Con. Stop by Table D42 to chat about the program and meet students and alumni. Thursday, October 6 – Sunday, October 9. Javits Center, 655 West 34th Street.

Friday | Yoga for Artists: Increasing Creativity and Improving Concentration

Join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services for a free virtual yoga class. Explore ways to get creativity flowing by focusing on the mind-body connection. All current students are welcome! No prior experience is needed. All you need is a flat surface and one to three soft squares (pillows, blankets or towels). RSVP required. Open to the entire SVA community. Friday, October 7, 10:00 – 10:45am, online.
