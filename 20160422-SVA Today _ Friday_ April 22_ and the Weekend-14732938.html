From the spring 2016 issue of Visual Arts Journal, a look at SVA alumnus Jason Bard Yarmosky's portraits of his grandparents.
9:00am: MPS Art Therapy Thesis Presentations
Graduating students present their work. Friday, April 22, 9:00am - 3:00pm, National Arts Club, 15 Gramercy Park South.
Saturday: "Venture 16: Entrepreneurship Unfolded" Opens
An exhibition of innovative, market-ready ventures by graduating MFA Design students, including products, services, apps, and more. Reception Monday, May 16, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Monday, May 16, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Saturday: BFA Fine Arts Open Studios
BFA Fine Arts students open their studios to the public. Reception Monday, April 25, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Saturday, April 23, and Sunday, April 24, noon - 5:00pm, 335 West 16 Street, lower level - 5th floor.
Saturday: "Low-Grade Euphoria" Performance
An exhibition of performance, video, sound and installation works, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellows. Performance Saturday, April 23, 4:30 - 9:00pm, featuring Antenes, Great City, Laraaji and Max. C. Lee. Exhibition runs through Friday, April 29, Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 3rd floor, Brooklyn.
Sunday: "Sneak A Peek" Closes
An exhibition of video work displayed from windows along West 22nd Street between Eighth and 10th Avenues, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Lal Bahcecioglu. Through Sunday, April 24.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Live Drawing on Instagram
Adweek highlights a collaboration between Jonathan Bartlett (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay) and Persol Sunglasses, in which the artist is illustrating a user-generated dystopian graphic novel live on Instagram.
NYC WEEKEND PICKS
Dustin Yellin
The artist and founder of Pioneer Works, an art and community space in Red Hook, Brooklyn, discusses his work. Admission is free, but seating is first-come, first-served. Tonight, 6:15pm, The Rubin Museum of Art, 150 West 17th Street.
Bust Craftacular
An "indie craft fair shopaganza," with free activities and pampering. The first 200 attendees receive a free swag bag. Saturday, April 23, 11:00am - 7:00pm, Factory Floor at Industry City, 233 37th Street, Brooklyn.
Culture Shock
The High Line's spring kick-off includes music, food, poetry and a photo contest. Saturday, April 23, noon - 4:00pm, High Line, from Gansevoort Street to West 26th Street.
Arbor Fest
Celebrate Earth Week with a petting zoo, nature and garden tours, crafts and food. Admission is $10. Sunday, April 24, 11:00am, - 6:00pm, Queens Botanical Garden, 43-50 Main Street, Flushing.
