News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
July 30 – August 5, 2018

SVA at the 2018 Emmys

Several SVA community members have been honored with Emmy nominations this year, including BFA Film faculty member Jennifer Fox for her film The Tale and Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation) for an episode of her Cartoon Network show Steven Universe.

Before and After SVA: Gerald Sheffield

U.S. Army veteran Gerald Sheffield (BFA 2015 Fine Arts) shares his experiences in and out of school and what advice he has for incoming students.
THIS WEEK

Thursday | "Me" Reception

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the SVA Flatiron Project Space present "Me," an exhibition featuring work by program alumnus William Patterson and two current students, Joey Gonnella and Nani Inocentes, and curated by faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). Thursday, August 2, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Friday, September 7. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
PRESS PICK

Re-Stitching North and South Korea

The New York Times on how for the past 10 years, South Korean Kyungah Ham (MFA 1995 Fine Arts) has been creating collaborative embroideries with anonymous artisans in North Korea through a network of smuggling and bribery.
NYC PICKS

NYC Summer Streets

Nearly seven miles of NYC's streets, from Brooklyn Bridge to Central Park, are opened for people to play, run, walk and bike. Route and activities here. First three Saturdays in August, 7:00am – 1:00pm.

A Summer Movie Under the Stars

Free outdoor movie screenings of family-friendly films and live musical performances. RSVPs are encouraged but not required. Wednesdays through August 22. Prospect Park's Long Meadow North, located nearest to the Grand Army Plaza entrance at Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.

Harlem Week

Four weeks of events celebrating Harlem's assets and unique flavor by promoting the neighborhood's rich African, African-American, Caribbean, Hispanic and European history, culture, arts, religion, entertainment and sports. Schedule and locations here. Through Saturday, August 25.

Funny Ladies at The New Yorker: Cartoonists Then and Now

A commemorative exhibition celebrating the creativity and fortitude of some of the women who drew cartoons for The New Yorker past and present. Through Saturday, October 13. Admission $10 – $15. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

Mission Impossible - Fallout

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Mission: Impossible - Fallout, starring Tom Cruise. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Friday, August 10, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
