Alumni Receive Prestigious Grants

Chelsi Bullard (class of 2017) was selected as one of Chicken and Egg Accelerator Lab grantees for her thesis film Kids Can Spit. 

Dana Kalmey (alumnus) and Erik Spink (faculty) were both awarded the 2017 Impact Partners Producers' Fellowship.  

And lastly, the 2017 SVA Alumni Scholarship Awards went to four of our thesis students-- Caroline Berler for Dykes, Camera, Action!; Chelsi Bullard for Kids Can Spit; Nottapon (Kai) Boonprakob for Come and See; and James Stuart for Rail Ties.

Congratulations to all!

PINKS: Korean LGBTQ & Activist Documentary Screenings

SAT, APR 1 – TUE, APR 4

This April we're hosting a series of Korean films curated by PINKS. These documentaries vividly capture minorities — trans folks, lesbians, gays, women, workers, and victims of state violence — in their moments of livelihood, struggle, protest, and triumph in the Korean society. Through unpacking of different identities and systems of work, we hope to highlight the intertwining nature of struggle and resistance across politics of identity and border.

See the full schedule for showtimes. Free! RSVP is required.

Tribeca is Coming!

The SocDoc community has a strong showing at this year's Tribeca Film Festival, including our department chair Maro Chermayeff's new CNN series, Soundtracks: Songs that Defined History, and No Man's Land, edited and produced by SocDoc alumnus David Osit, who also scored the film.

Check out our lineup and see you there!
