SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, February 5, 2016, and the Weekend
A still from Waterfront.
Documenting the Global Water Crisis
Last month at Sundance, Crystal Moselle (BFA 2002 Film and Video) announced Waterfront, a new documentary project that shines a light on how water crises in developing countries disproportionately affect women.
6:00pm: "Sharing/Space" Reception
MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition of work by students at Cooke Center Grammar School and Cooke Center Academy. Curated by program alumni Kate DeRaffele and Ayde Rayas. Viewing by appointment only. Contact arttherapy@sva.edu to schedule a visit. Through Wednesday, February 10, SVA Studio Gallery, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
Saturday: "Gone" Opening
MFA Illustration as Visual Essay presents an exhibition of work by members of the class of 2017. Curated by faculty member Viktor Koen and featuring illustrations by students enrolled in his Book Seminar course. Reception Wednesday, February 10, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Thursday, March 3, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Saturday: "Women in Illustration" Opening
MFA Illustration as Visual Essay presents an exhibition of work by female department alumni who have recently authored or illustrated books. Curated by Marshall Arisman, department chair, and Kim Ablondi, the department’s director of operations. Reception Wednesday, February 10, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Thursday, March 3, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Saturday: MFA Visual Narrative Information Session
MFA Visual Narrative presents an information session and interactive presentation for prospective students, featuring department chair Nathan Fox, faculty members and students, as well as a tour of the RisoLab. 3:00 - 5:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
Saturday: The 5th Wave
The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of science fiction thriller 5th Wave. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Seating is first-come, first-served. Doors open approximately 30 - 45 minutes prior to showtime. 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
2016 PEN Literary Awards Shortlist SVA Faculty and Alum
The shortlists for the 2016 PEN Literary Awards include Agnes Martin by MFA Art Writing faculty member Nancy Princenthal and Thunder & Lightning: Weather Past, Present, Future by Lauren Redniss (MFA 2000 Illustration as Visual Essay). Congratulations!
NYC Weekend Picks
Winter Carnival
The second annual festival features ice skating, curling lessons, silent disco and live music. Noon - 10:00pm, today and tomorrow, Bryant Park, 42nd Street at Sixth Avenue.
Food Nostalgia
An exhibition of paintings that focuses on food iconography and production, featuring Kira Nam Greene (MFA 2004 Fine Arts). Through Sunday, March 13, Radiator Gallery, 10-61 Jackson Avenue, Long Island City.
Tim Miller Book Launch
Illustrator and MAT Art Education alumnus Miller launches his new book Snappsy the Alligator (Did Not Ask to Be in this Book). Saturday, February 6, 1:00pm, Books of Wonder, 18 West 18th Street.
Take Note
Jill Magid
MFA Fine Arts presents New York-based artist and writer Jill Magid discussing her performance-based practice that explores the emotional, philosophical and legal tensions between the individual and "protective" institutions. Tuesday, February 9, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Seasons of Loss: Racism and Resistance in the Cultural Field
MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on racism, as a subject and influence, and the general trend of broader social critique in the arts. Tuesday, February 9, 7:00pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
"elmuseo@SVA" Panel Discussion
MFA Fine Arts presents a panel discussion featuring "elmuseo@SVA" curator Rocío Aranda-Avarado, curator, El Museo del Barrio, and select artists from the exhibition. Participating panelists include Manuel Acevedo and MFA Fine Arts alumni Alejandro Guzman (2009), K.C. Tidemand (2015) and Denise Treizman (2013). Tuesday, February 16. 6:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Image of the day
Photos from "Down in the South Bronx" by BFA Photography and Video student Kasey-Lynn Rodriguez, now on view at 214 East 21st Street through Friday, February 12. Image via the SVA BFA Photography and Video Instagram.
