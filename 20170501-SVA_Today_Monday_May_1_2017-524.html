News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Monday, May 1, 2017

Cultural Differences and Similarities

A Q&A with BFA Photography and Video student Rujie Wang, who won a 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award for her project "Made in China," which explores Western stereotypes of Asians through a series of photos of products purchased at 99-cent stores in America.
EVENTS

Noon | MFA Design for Social Innovation Thesis Presentations

MFA Design for Social Innovation’s graduating class presents their thesis projects on the critical topics of today, including health, climate change, education, language, elder care and more. RSVP here. Noon – 4:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. Reception to follow, 4:30 – 6:30pm, at 136 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:00pm | RisoLAB Print Slam Exhibit and Sale

MFA Visual Narrative presents a sale of student prints, books, zines and Riso experiments, along with a pop-up exhibition of Riso work from four continuing education and undergraduate classes. All proceeds go to the artists. Attendees can also see demos of the Risograph machines and learn about the RisoLAB summer classes. 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA RisoLAB, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.
PRESS PICK

"Keep the Spark and Stimulation"

Forbes interviews Sprayground chief creative officer and CEO David Ben-David (BFA 2005 Graphic Design) about the inspiration for company's new Smart Pack backpack.
NYC PICKS

A Conversation with Alvin Lucier

Pioneering experimental music icon Alvin Lucier will discuss the tenets of his exploratory work as part of the Red Bull Music Academy Festival. Tickets $15; purchase here. 7:30pm. Red Bull Arts New York, 220 West 18th Street.

NYC Indie Film Festival

A week of independent cinema screenings, including short films, feature films, music videos and animated works, as well as panels and parties with indie filmmakers. Through Sunday, May 7. Schedule here. Price and location varies by event.
TAKE NOTE

The Circle Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of The Circle, starring Tom Hanks and Emma Watson. Followed by a Q&A with the producer, Anthony Bregman. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Tomorrow, May 2, 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

MFA Computer Art Thesis Exhibition

MFA Computer Art presents this exhibition that showcases the graduating class’s thesis projects and how they vision the future of art making. On view Tuesday, May 30 – Saturday, June 10. Reception: Tuesday, May 30, 6:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
