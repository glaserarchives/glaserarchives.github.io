Black History Month Events at SVA

               
                                                                                                  February 8, 2022
Dear SVA Community,


As we continue to celebrate Black History Month (BHM), we invite you to attend two virtual events that address topics and important issues associated with the Black/African American experience. As I wrote earlier, the Association for the Study of African American Life and History (ASALH) determined that this year’s BHM theme of Black Health and Wellness focuses on the “the legacy of not only Black scholars and medical practitioners in Western medicine, but also other ways of knowing (e.g., birth workers, doulas, midwives, naturopaths, herbalists, etc.) throughout the African Diaspora. The 2022 theme considers activities, rituals and initiatives that Black communities have done to be well.”  

In alignment with this year’s theme, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA seeks to sustain positive cultural and mental health through dialogue and discussion. These are two of many approaches toward addressing challenging topics associated with justice, equity, diversity and inclusion. 

DEI at SVA will be hosting two online events in the coming weeks, regarding the lived experiences of Black/African American artists and the Black Lives Matter movement. These events are opportunities for all SVA students, faculty, staff and alumni to attend, learn and grow.  

Both events are free, but require your registration. We hope to engage the SVA community in these and additional DEI events that span various intersectional identities and lived experiences. Please take the time to go over the descriptions below and register at your earliest convenience. If there are any questions, or if you are in need of special accommodations to attend, please email me at diversity@sva.edu.

BHM Panel Discussion: Thursday, February 17, 5:30pm EST


In Their Own Words—The Legacy of Black/African American Artists 
A conversation hosted by SVA DEI, featuring Michael Barclay lll (student, BFA Design), vice president of the SVA Black Student Union; Rob Fields, director of the Sugar Hill Children’s Museum of Art and Storytelling; Dr. Georgette Grier Key, director of the Eastville Historical Society; TJ Sterling, comics creator and entrepreneur; Storm Ascher (2018BFA Visual & Critical Studies), artist, curator and entrepreneur; and Michele Washington (2011 MFA Design Criticism), designer, consultant, writer and educator. Register here.

BHM Campus Dialogue: Tuesday, February 22, 5:00pm EST


The Aftermath of the Black Lives Matter Cases
SVA DEI hosts a talk with Gloria Browne-Marshall, playwright, constitutional-law professor and civil rights lawyer, about the aftermath and impact of recent Black Lives Matter-related trials. This will be a dinnertime discussion, where we invite the SVA community to discuss topics relating to the cases, highlight their impact on the nation and provide an opportunity for questions and answers, while eating your favorite dish. There will also be an opportunity for Q&A. Register here.
