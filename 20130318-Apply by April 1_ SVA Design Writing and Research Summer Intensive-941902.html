Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive
June 3–14, 2013
School of Visual Arts
New York City
dcrit@sva.edu
 
A reminder that the priority deadline for this summer's SVA Design Writing and Research Intensive is April 1. Apply today!

The 2013 Design Writing and Research Intensive is aimed at those who would like to refine their skills as thinkers, researchers, and storytellers. For practicing designers and writers alike, the Intensive offers methods and insights for understanding and writing compellingly about images, objects, spaces, and infrastructure. 

Each participant in this richly programmed two-week Intensive will have a workstation in the light-filled D-Crit studio in New York’s Chelsea district, 24-hour access to department resources, and the opportunity to study closely with renowned writers and editors including Karrie Jacobs, Jennifer Kabat, Adam Harrison Levy, Robin Pogrebin, Alice Twemlow, Rob Walker, and Mimi Zeiger.
Participants will learn essential research and writing techniques, and then develop several projects in media ranging from tweets and video essays to manifestos, personal essays, and articles, culminating in a collaboratively produced publication. By the end of the program, participants will have completed several pieces of writing, contributed to a publication, formulated ideas for stories, and garnered a robust set of tools and approaches for writing authoritatively and imaginatively about design.
 
A tightly programmed daily schedule of seminars, lectures, writing workshops, and one-on-one tutorials will be supplemented with site visits, design studio tours, and exhibition openings, allowing participants to directly interact with prominent designers, architects, urban planners, and curators. Among the design studios we will visit are: Antenna Design, frog, Kiss Me I’m Polish, Emily Oberman at Pentagram, Mary Ping, Sagmeister & Walsh, and Streng.

Rob Walker, author of the Significant Objects project, will lecture on how to develop narratives around objects, while Adam Harrison Levy will lecture on research methods. Participants will engage in close observation, archive research, and other means of data gathering, and then experiment with strategies to illuminate an object’s significance through storytelling.

Working in consultation with design journalist Jennifer Kabat, and BBC interviewer Adam Harrison Levy, participants will develop their interviewing techniques, prepare questions, and do background research, before visiting some of New York’s most prominent design studios in the fields of architecture, interaction design, graphic design, fashion design, and product design. Each participant will interview a principal designer and write a studio profile for critique in a mid-Intensive review session.
 
Guided by The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin, participants will be introduced to the principles of reviewing across genres and across media, with a focus on the exhibition review as a type. After some initial exercises to hone writing skills, the development of a point of view and argument, and some reading exercises to examine exemplars of the form, participants will visit a selection of design exhibitions, and meet and interview their curators, before writing their own reviews, and presenting them for critique.
 
For more details on projects, see the Intensive website.
The SVA Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive welcomes applications from students and working professionals alike. If you care about design and language, and are interested in learning how to write meaningfully about products, graphics, urban infrastructure, and buildings with instructors such as urban critic Karrie Jacobs, and author Rob Walker, then consider applying to this two-week intensive today! Applications are due April 1, tuition is $2,000 for two weeks of instruction, and all application details can be found on the Intensive website.
