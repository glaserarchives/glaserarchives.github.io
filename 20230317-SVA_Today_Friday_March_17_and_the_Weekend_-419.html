SVA Destinations in Ireland

Fuel your passion for making art on the coast of Western Ireland this summer in the beautiful village of Letterfrack.

EVENTS

Last Chance | “The Neighborhood of the Self”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a collection of recent work by Ryan J. Brady (BFA 2013 Visual and Critical Studies) that probes the radical multiplicity of identity through a language of improbably shaped canvases that unify into painting-sculpture hybrids. Closes today, March 17. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Tomorrow | AI and the Lens and Screen Arts Spring Symposium

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a comprehensive, one-day symposium focused on the pervasive emergence of AI and its effects upon the lens and screen arts. Saturday, March 18, 10:00am – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tomorrow | La Bodega: Trip to “Guillermo del Toro Crafting Pinocchio” at the MoMA

Join La Bodega on a trip to the “Guillermo del Toro Crafting Pinocchio” exhibition at the Museum of Modern Art. Saturday, March 18, 1:00pm; RSVP here. The group will meet at the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, and depart together at 1:00pm.

Ongoing Exhibitions

  • •“Mentors 2023,” through Monday, March 27. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
  • •“Women’s History Month: A Half-Century of Underground Images,” through Tuesday, March 28. SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

NYC PICKS

St. Patrick’s Day Parade

Watch the world’s oldest and largest St. Patrick’s Day Parade, marching up Fifth Avenue from East 44th Street to East 79th Street. Today, March 17, 11:00am – 4:30pm.

Holi at the Seaport

The Culture Tree and the South Street Seaport Museum present a celebration of the traditional Indian festival of colors. Saturday, March 18, 11:00am – 2:00pm. The Seaport, 19 Fulton Street.

The Future Is Femme: A Women’s History Month Pop-Up Shop

Shop home decor, clothing, jewelry and more from women, femme, gender-expansive and trans businesses. Saturday, March 18, 2023, 1:00 – 7:00pm. New Women Space, 188 Woodpoint Road, Brooklyn.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Call for Submissions: Visual Opinion Graduation Issue

All seniors graduating this 2022 – 2023 academic year are invited to submit their work for Visual Opinion’s annual graduation issue—paintings, drawings, photography, sculptures, designs, film stills, poems, etc. The deadline is Monday, March 20. Submit your best artwork to vo@sva.edu in a high resolution format of 300+ dpi. More information is available on Instagram @visualopinion.

Next Week | Bill Plympton Presents SLIDE

MFA Computer Arts presents a special test screening of Academy Award-nominated animator Bill Plympton’s (1969 Cartooning) upcoming feature film, SLIDE, an animated Western about a slide guitar-playing cowboy. Monday, March 20, 12:00 – 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Next Week | Community Lecture Series: Fields Within the Field

MPS Art Therapy presents a virtual presentation by Michael Franklin, Ph.D., ATR-BC, on the intersections between the studio model and social justice art therapy, specifically nonclinical methods and interventions for people in need of communal expression. Friday, March 24, 6:00 – 7:30pm, online.

Next Week | ISO Gallery Turkish Heritage Opening Reception and Turkey/Syria Earthquake Relief

Join the International Student Office’s opening reception for their exhibition of work celebrating Turkish heritage. Check out the work of SVA students, enjoy Turkish delights and tea and support the victims of the recent tragedy in Turkey and Syria. Organized by ISO and Turkish SVA students. Open only to the SVA community. RSVP is required; register here. Friday, March 24, 7:00pm. 340 East 24th Street, ground floor.

Next Week | SWAN Day 2023

SVA Theatre and BFA Film present the 15th annual Support Women Artists Now (SWAN) Day with a series of short films followed by a panel featuring director Annetta Marion, producer Dana Offenbach and Academy Award-winning actress Estelle Parsons. Free and open to the public with RSVP; register here. Saturday, March 25, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
