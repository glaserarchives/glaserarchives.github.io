On January 7, Tyre Nichols, a 29-year-old Black man, was beaten by five Memphis, Tennessee police officers following a traffic stop. He later died of his injuries. Here is a timeline of events in his death. This evening, a video of the incident is expected to be released to the public. As a campus, we are saddened to witness the loss of another Black/African American as a result of police brutality. We anticipate that there will be a range of emotions associated with the sharing of this video. Please know that there are resources at SVA for anyone who needs support during this time. We are here for you and want you to stay safe.

 

24/7 Emotional Support Resources:

1)    NYC WELL is a free mental-health support resource. NYC Well offers English, Spanish or Mandarin speakers and has access for translators in about 200 languages.

Call 1.888.NYC.WELL (1.888.692.9355)

Text “WELL” to 65173

Chat online

2)    The Samaritans organization operates a confidential hotline when you need someone to talk to. Call 212.673.3000.

3)    Crisis Text Line offers free, around-the-clock support. Text “HOME” to 741741.

4)    Cigna Total Behavioral Health To access services to help manage life events visit the Stress and Emotional Wellness page on MyCigna.com.

 

Student Health and Counseling Services: Free, confidential and short-term therapy and psychiatric services for all students at SVA. Office Hours are Mon-Fri 9:00am-5:00pm. Email: health@sva.edu or call: 212.592.2246 to make an appointment.
