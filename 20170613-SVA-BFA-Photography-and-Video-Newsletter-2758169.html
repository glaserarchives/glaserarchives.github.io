Joshua Citarella Featured in Artnet News

Artnet News lists alum Joshua Citarella (2010) as one of 10 contemporary artists taking photography in new directions.
The Swinging Pendulum on Display at SVA's Gramercy Gallery

Alum Harrison Weinstein (2017, above) exhibits in The Swinging Pendulum, a group show with works by SVA alumni and students. The exhibition is on view Friday, June 2, through Thursday, June 15, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 E 23 St, New York City.
Efrem Zelony-Mindell Curates Exhibition at The Camera Club of New York 

Alum Efrem Zelony-Midell (2011) curates Familiar Strange, an exhibition of new works by the MFA 2017 graduates from the International Center of Photography-Bard College. The exhibition will run June 29-July 5, 2017 with an opening reception on Wednesday, June 28, 6-8PM.
Darin Mickey Featured in VICE 

VICE interviewed alum Darin Mickey (1998) about how his photographs capture the weirdness of ordinary life. 
Grace Spencer Raising Money to End Child Sex Slavery

Rising senior Grace Spencer is raising money for Remember Nhu, a nonprofit organization committed to ending child sex slavery through prevention. Learn more at www.remembernhu.org.

Sophie Calle's Public Art Project in Green-Wood Cemetery 

Artist and photographer Sophie Calle presents a new conceptual work with Creative Time that will be on view at Brooklyn's Green-Wood Cemetery for the next 25 years. Here Lie the Secrets of the Visitors of Green-Wood Cemetery entails a hollow obelisk for visitors to leave their secrets on a slip of paper, to which Calle will return periodically to cremate them.
Bushwick Community Darkroom Offers Opportunities for Photographers

Located at 110 Troutman Street in Brooklyn, the Bushwick Community Darkroom, founded by alum Lucia Rollow (2009), offers fully stocked facilities for rentals, classes and workshops, and drop-off and mail-in film development.
Red Hook Labs Hosts Photography Portfolio Review

Photographers seeking career advice, feedback and connections are invited to apply to Red Hook Labs Photo Portfolio Review taking place on Saturday, June 17 at Red Hook Labs, 133 Imlay St, Brooklyn.
Alumni Exhibit at MARYMARY projects

Alumni, Allyson Anne Lamb (2011), Ken Lavey (2015), and Desiree Kong (2017, above) exhibit at MARYMARY projects. Opening reception Thursday, June 15, 6-8PM, 529 W 20 St, 6th Floor. 
BFA Photography & Video Faculty, Alumni, and Mentors Among Winners of 2017 PDN Annual Awards

Alum Justine Kurland (1996) and Mentor Richard Renaldi (above) along with alum Brian Finke (1998) and Faculty Elinor Carucci have been awarded the PDN Book and Magazine Awards, respectively. 
Ilona Szwarc's Photographs Accompany Article in New York Times Magazine

Alum Ilona Szwarc's (2013) photographs are paired with Charles Seibert's article about the healing power of bringing inner city children and horses together. 
Natalie Krick Wins the 2017 Aperture Portfolio Prize

Alum Natalie Krick (2008) has been selected as the winner of this year's Aperture Portfolio Prize and will exhibit at Aperture Gallery in New York.
Lacy Wood Interviewed by i-D

Alum Lacy Wood (2017) spent months photographing the bedrooms of teenage girls across New York City. In this Q&A, Wood answers questions about girlhood, women's rights, and more. 
Claire Christerson, Jessie Askinazi, and Pussy Riot's Nadya Tolokonnikova Create PSA for The East Los Angeles Women's Center

This conceptual video, co-created by staff member and alum Claire Christerson (2015) and fellow artist Jessie Askinazi, with Pussy Riot's Nadya Tolokonnikova, highlights the Trump Administration's cuts to policies and programs that help women.
Staff Member Max Lee's Exhibition Listed in Artnet News

In its eleventh iteration, RE: Art Show was chosen as one of the "Editor's Picks" for 9 things to see in New York in the last week of May. This exhibit will host work from alum Claire Christerson (2015) among others.

Baxter St Camera Club of New York 2017 Annual Juried Exhibition Open Call

Baxter St is pleased to announce an open call for entries for its Annual National Photography Competition, this year juried by curator and art historian Andrianna Campbell. Photographers and photo-based artists working in any genre are eligible to apply. The deadline to apply is June 21, 2017.
Megan Breukelman, Editor-in-Chief of Atlas Magazine, Shares Tips on How to Get Featured in Photo Magazines

Alum Megan Breukelman (2016), the Editor-in-Chief of Atlas Magazine, speaks with Photojojo to share helpful hints for photographers looking to have their work featured in photography publications.
