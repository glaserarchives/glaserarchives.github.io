News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Today
Wednesday, April 5, 2017

Career Fair 2017

A rundown of Career Development's 2017 career fair, where students and recent alumni got to meet with representatives from companies like BuzzFeed, Facebook and Nickelodeon.
EVENTS

11:00am | Sign Up for a New York Public Library Card

Two NYPL librarians will be on hand to sign up SVA students, faculty and staff and answer any questions. 11:00am – 3:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

3:30pm | Procrastination and Technology: Staying Focused in a World Full of Distractions

Student Health and Counseling Services and Residence Life present this workshop, which will focus on identifying specific technology-related distractions and learning about how to challenge these problematic behaviors. Students only. RSVP to Jennifer Wilson at jwilson4@sva.edu. 3:30 – 5:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 202.
PRESS PICK

The Allegories of an Alumnus

Dangerous Minds on artist Martin Wittfooth (MFA 2008 Illustration as Visual Essay), whose work "tells us something deeply profound about our existence."
NYC PICK

Propaganda by Design

A panel of leading graphic designers, including faculty members Mirko Ilic and Paula Scher, will discuss a selection of rarely exhibited propaganda posters and consider parallels in the world of graphic design and illustration today. Tickets $10 – $15; purchase here. 6:30pm. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Image Machines: A Screening Program with Discussion by Grahame Weinbren

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a series of screenings of short international films. Program here. Tomorrow, April 6, 1:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

The Digital Revolution and the Future of Work with Ryan Avent

MFA Interaction Design presents senior editor and economics columnist at The Economist Ryan Avent, who will look at how technology is affecting the economy, what workers can expect in future and what challenges society can expect to face as we adjust to a digital world. RSVP here. Tomorrow, April 6, 6:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

Freelancing Basics

This workshop will provide an overview of the crucial elements of successful freelancing with resources and general information relevant to all disciplines. RSVP here. Friday, April 7, 1:00pm. 136 West 21st St, Room 418F.
