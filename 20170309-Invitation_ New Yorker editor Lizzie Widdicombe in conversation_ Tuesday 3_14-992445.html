Lizzie Widdicombe
In conversation with Adam Harrison Levy
Tuesday, March 14, 6:30 p.m.


Lizzie Widdicombe is a writer and editor for The New Yorker’s Talk of the Town section, where she has worked since 2006. She has written over a hundred Talk of the Town stories, as well as features on subjects ranging from the food replacement Soylent to the fashion designer Jeremy Scott to the pop star Taylor Swift. Lizzie will be in conversation with SVA Design Research faculty member Adam Harrison Levy, a writer and freelance documentary film producer and director specializing in the art of the interview. They’ll talk about writing and editing short-form versus long-form, the role of humor in non-fiction, and how to find compelling stories in unexpected places. 

For the BBC Adam has conducted interviews with a wide range of actors, writers, musicians and film-makers including Meryl Streep, Philip Glass, and Paul Auster. He has produced and directed interviews for films such as Lou Reed Remembered, The Kennedys, and D-Day to Berlin. He is a contributing writer for The Design Observer.

Study at Design Research!


The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is accepting applications on a rolling basis for Fall 2017. Significant scholarships are available for successful candidates. This one-year, intensive MA program is well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. In providing the tools and techniques for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design, the program amply prepares its graduates for future-facing careers in research, publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, and entrepreneurship, or for continued studies in a design-related subject.
