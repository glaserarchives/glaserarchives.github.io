SVA Today
Monday, February 13, 2017

SVA Women Strong

BFA Film and BFA Animation present an evening celebrating SVA women filmmakers and animators. There will be screenings of five short films by women alumni, followed by a panel of accomplished women in the industry discussing the issues they face. Reception to follow. Tonight, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
EVENT

6:30pm | Hoarding, or Adventures in Form

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an evening with Gregg Horowitz, professor of philosophy at Pratt Institute, who will discuss hoarding: the compulsive inability to discard possessions. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
PRESS PICK

LGBTQ TV

Vibe on award-winning filmmaker Abdool Corlette’s (BFA 2013 Film and Video) and producer Adam Vazquez’s (BFA 2013 Film and Video) new documentary series, Other Boys NYC, about queer and transgender men of color, which premieres on Slay TV on Monday, February 20.
NYC PICKS

Geek Girls Do Galentine’s Day

A meet-up with self-identifying "geek girls" like filmmaker and comic book creator Che Grayson and blogger and designer Yissel Ayala. Tickets $10; purchase here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.

The Pen and the Brush

A lecture and book signing with Anka Muhlstein, author of The Pen and the Brush: How Passion for Art Shaped Nineteenth-Century Novels. Free; copies of the book will be available for purchase. 7:00pm. La Maison Française, New York University, 16 Washington Mews.
TAKE NOTE

Blue Sky Company Presentation

SVA Career Development presents representatives and artists from Blue Sky Studios, who will discuss their internship application process and their own career paths. RSVP here. Thursday, February 16, 3:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

MAT Art Education Information Session

Department faculty and recent alumni will discuss the program and topics pertinent to the field of art education. Thursday, February 16, 5:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 4th floor.

Taylor Baker, "Systems"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a solo exhibition by Taylor Baker (BFA 2015 Visual & Critical Studies), featuring a series of stylized systems that question the suggested nature of things. Thursday, February 16 – Wednesday, March 15. Reception: Thursday, February 16, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

2017 HOPE Competition Reception

Student Health and Counseling Services presents the opening reception for this year's HOPE Competition exhibition, "Expressing Your Unique Identity." This year's theme aimed to honor individuality and unique identities, and creating a community of acceptance at SVA. The first-, second- and third-place winners, chosen by BFA Visual & Critical Studies Chair Tom Huhn, will be announced. Friday, February 17, 5:30pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery,133/141 West 21st Street.

NYC International Children’s Film Festival

Screenings of the best new films from around the world for ages 3 – 18, with filmmaker Q&As, workshops and parties. Visit nyicff.org/tickets and use code 17SVAT when ordering online to receive $2 off of each ticket. Friday, February 24 – Sunday, March 19. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street, and other venues in Manhattan.
