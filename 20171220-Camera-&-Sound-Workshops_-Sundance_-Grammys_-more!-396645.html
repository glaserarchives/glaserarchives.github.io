Winter Camera & Sound
Weekend Workshops

New this year, SocDoc is offering two weekend-long camera and sound workshops workshops that are open to the public.

Documentary Camera and Sound Intensive (Jan 27-28) and Documentary Sound (Feb. 24-25) can be taken individually or together, and are meant to build your skill set as a director, cinematographer, sound recordist and an overall filmmaker. The classes, taught by our experienced filmmaker faculty, also give vital tips on how to apply these skills to set up industry-quality shoots on a limited budget and in the field.

Registration is open now - limited space available. Click here for more info.

SocDoc at Sundance, Grammy Awards & More!

We are thrilled that we have several faculty and alumni films at Sundance 2018 this year! These include Alumni films CRIME + PUNISHMENT (Dir.: S. Maing; Consulting Editor David Osit, ‘11); DARK MONEY (Dir.: K. Reed; Associate Producer/Assistant Editor Sandra Itainen ‘17, pictured above). Faculty films include: GENERATION WEALTH (Dir.: L. Greenfield; DP Jerry Risius); MATANGI / MAYA / M.I.A (Dir.: S. Loveridge; Producer Lori Cheatle); THE PRICE OF EVERYTHING (Dir.: N. Kahn, DP Bob Richman); SEEING ALLRED (Dirs.: S. Sartain, R. Grossman; DP J. Risius). THIS IS HOME (Dir.: A. Shiva; Editor Toby Shimin). Congratulations everyone - we look forward to seeing the films!


🏆🏆TWO of the Grammy nominations for Best Music Film are our faculty films:
Two Trains Runnin' directed by Sam Pollard
Soundbreaking: Stories From The Cutting Edge of Recorded Music - Directed & executive produced by SocDoc Chair Maro Chermayeff , edited by Donna Shepherd and Sam Pollard, produced by Amy Schewel, camera by Axel Baumann (all faculty); add'l editing by Lauren Kraus (class of 2013).
You can watch Soundbreaking on your favorite streaming platforms. 

Congratulations Maro & Sam on your nominations!! We look forward to seeing your red carpet looks!
