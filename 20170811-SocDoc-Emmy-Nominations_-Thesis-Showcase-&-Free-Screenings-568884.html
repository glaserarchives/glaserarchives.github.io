David Osit ('11) Earns Three Emmy Nominations for THANK YOU FOR PLAYING

A big shout-out to our own David Osit (class of 2011) for his News and Documentary Emmy Awards nominations for his film Thank You For Playing: Best Documentary, Outstanding Editing: Documentary, and Outstanding Arts & Culture Documentary. The film is directed and produced by David Osit and Malika Zouhali-Worrall, and David also served as the film's editor, cinematographer, and composer.
 
David is the first SVA MFA Social Documentary alumnus to receive an Emmy nomination. Congratulations! Read more about the film, and our faculty films' nominations here.

 

Alumni & Faculty at Toronto Film Fest, IFP Week, and on PBS POV

Our steadfast SocDoc alumni and faculty are covering themselves in glory! 

Two SocDoc titles premiere at Toronto Film Festival, full of glittery high profile documentaries we can't wait to devour. The prolific Sam Pollard (Thesis Directing faculty) premieres his latest: Sammy Davis, Jr.: I've Just Gotta Be Me, and Oscar nominees Heidi Ewing & Rachel Grady's latest, One Of Us, features the work of Director of Photography Jenni Morello (class of 2011).

At IFP Week, only international co-production market in the U.S. featuring stories for all platforms, SocDocers are presenting their works in progress to industry seeking new work. Featured projects include Busy Inside directed by Olga Lvoff (class of 2013), produced by Victor Ilyuhkin (class of 2014); The Evidence of Things Not Seen directed by Ja’Tovia Gary (class of 2014); The Drum Also Waltzes: In Search of Max Roach directed and produced by Sam Pollard (Faculty) and Ben Shapiro;  and Personal Statement produced by Beth Levison (Faculty).

On your television set (or streaming device), check out three faculty films on PBS POV: Swim Team, produced and edited by Ann Collins; Cameraperson, directed by Kirsten Johnson, and the Oscar-nomianted short Joe's Violin, filmed by Bob Richman.

More info on all these projects and programs - and much more! - on our blog!

SVA Alumni Film Festival: Tashi's Turbine; Sneak Peek of Dykes, Camera, Action!

 

SVA's After School Special alumni film festival runs September 11-17, 2017 at the SVA Theatre and features some of School of Visual Arts’ most accomplished film, television, animation, and visual effects alumni. Screenings are free & open to the public, and filmmakers are present for Q&A. Screenings include:

>> a sneak peek of Caroline Berler's Dykes, Camera, Action!- a history of lesbian cinema from the women who made it happen (pictured above). The excerpt screens as a part of the SVA Premieres program featuring exceptional live action, documentary, and animated shorts by SVA's Class of 2017 graduates. Following the screening, join the filmmakers in the lobby for drinks and dessert. Fri, September 15, 2017, 8:00 PM

>> Tashi's Turbine - directed, filmed and edited by Amitabh Joshi, and co-produced by Erik Spink (both SocDoc faculty & class of 2014). Tashi Bista dreams of installing a makeshift wind turbine in Namdok, a remote village nestled high in the Himalayas of Nepal. Namdok, battered by wind and cold, has been in darkness for centuries. Tashi grew up in this region; he is determined to bring lights to Namdok, and to prove himself to the skeptical village.  Sun, September 17, 5:00 PM
