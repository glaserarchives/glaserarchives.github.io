EVENTS THIS WEEK

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Pablo Helguera
Pablo Helguera is a New York-based visual artist whose work involves performance, drawing, installation, theater and other literary strategies. Register here to join Helguera's lecture, presented by MFA Art Practice, on Zoom. Tuesday, July 6, 9:30 – 11:00am ET.

BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month
July is BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month and SVA Student Health and Counseling Services (SH&CS) is recognizing it with weekly programming. Follow SH&CS on Instagram, @svastudenthealth, to participate live or watch later on IGTV.

Opening Program: Cultivating a Wellness Mindset on Campus
Wednesday, July 7, 12:00 – 1:00pm ET
Join SH&CS and Dr. Jarvis Watson, SVA's Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, to learn about campus resources, SVA initiatives, community building and ways to support your mental health. Open to all SVA students, staff and faculty on Instagram Live. See the Announcements section below for more BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month events.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, July 8, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Thursday | What Is a Museum? Art, Artists and Audiences
This panel gathers critics, scholars and museum professionals to ask what a museum is and can be in the third decade of the 21st century. Moderated by MFA Art Practice Chair David A. Ross with panelists Tyler Green, Kelli Morgan and Allison Hewitt Ward. Register here to join on Zoom. Thursday, July 8, 6:00 – 8:00pm ET.

Last Chance | a throw of the dice will never abolish chance
This exhibition of multidisciplinary works by 10 artists from the Residency Alumni Network at SVA, curated by visual artist and Summer Residency faculty member Andrea Champlin, is on view in-person through Wednesday, July 7, at the SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor. The exhibition can also be seen safely from the sidewalk.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month: Upcoming Events
July is BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month and SVA Student Health and Counseling Services (SH&CS) is recognizing it with weekly programming. Follow @svastudenthealth on Instagram to participate live or watch later on IGTV.

In addition to the opening program this Wednesday (see the Events section above), the following events will take place this month:

Overcoming Barriers to Mental Healthcare
Wednesday, July 14, 11:30am – 12:30pm ET
Huilin Lai, LCSW-R therapist and founder of Ally Counseling and Therapy, joins SH&CS in a conversation about how BIPOC domestic and international students can learn to rise above the stigma, navigate the U.S. healthcare system, develop useful coping skills and prioritize mental health.

Vision Board Party: Meditate and Create for BIPOC Mental Health Month
Monday, July 19, 12:00pm ET
Join Purple, an educator, activist, community organizer, healer and founder of WOC Sister Collective, for an Instagram takeover! Purple will facilitate a meditation focused on mental wellness, healing and empowerment and teach us how to make a vision board to manifest our dreams. Learn about radical visioning, intention setting and self-care! Open to all SVA students, staff, and faculty.

COVID-19 Community Conversations: Mental Health, Equity and Resilience
Wednesday, July 28, 9:30am – 12:30pm ET
This training is open to all SVA staff and faculty, and will cover the mental-health impact of COVID-19, the effect of trauma and grief, health disparities in communities of color as well as coping skills and self-care. It is a three-hour training that will take place on Zoom. Sign up here.

New GoSVA Mobile App
The entirely redesigned GoSVA mobile app is available on the Apple App Store for iPhone and Google Play Store for Android. This new mobile app provides on-the-go access to all SVA essential systems and information for students, faculty, staff and visiting guests. If you have the prior version of the GoSVA app installed on your device please uninstall it before installing this new version. More information and links to the app stores can be found at go.sva.edu. An installation guide is available at technology.sva.edu.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Allen Frame: NYC 1981
An exhibition of color photographs by BFA Photography and Video faculty member Allen Frame from New York in 1981, presented in conjunction with the release of his new book, Fever. Through Friday, July 16, at Gitterman Gallery online, with a simultaneous in-person show at Matte Editions, 1899 Fulton Street, Brooklyn.

The Art of Coney Island
A juried exhibition exploring the spirit of Coney Island and featuring artwork by SVA faculty member Carol Fabricatore (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay), Christopher Spinelli (BFA 1989 Media Arts) and many others. Starting Saturday, July 10, it is open every Saturday and Sunday, 1:00 – 6:00pm ET, through August 15. Brooklyn Waterfront Artists Coalition, 481 Van Brunt Street, Door 7, Brooklyn.
