SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
July 17 - 23, 2017

Trolleys to Mexico

A Q&A with El Paso city council member Peter Svarzbein (MFA 2011 Photography, Video and Related Media) on his efforts to reestablish a Texas-Mexico trolley line, a project that stemmed from his thesis at SVA. This article was featured in the spring 2017 Visual Arts Journal.

"Endless Job Opportunities"

Liron Ashkenazi (BFA 2016 Design) discusses the valuable lessons her teachers taught her in college, having "endless job opportunities" after graduating from SVA, working with clients like Beyoncé and more.
IN THE PRESS

"Ephemeral" and "Ghost-Like" Messages

Los Angeles Times on Gary Simmons (MFA 1988 Fine Arts) new installation at the California African Art Museum "Fade to Black," which "addresses pop culture as well as issues of race, class and the fragmented, fleeting nature of memory."

Exploring Human Fascination with the Wild

Paste interviews Jen Lee (BFA 2008 Illustration) about her drawing process and her new graphic novel Garbage Night.

Star Montana, Wild Child

Los Angeles Times interviews photographer Star Montana (BFA 2013 Photography) on her current exhibition "I Dream of Los Angeles," on view at the Main Museum in LA.
EVENTS

"And Then. . ." Presentations and Exhibition

The MFA Visual Narrative students of the class of 2017 proudly present their eight multimedia storytelling thesis installations. Thesis presentations: Tuesday, July 18, 6:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater. Exhibition on view through Monday, July 24. Reception: Friday, July 21, 6:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"Whistling Whims"

An exhibition of works by SVA students selected by a jury of their peers, featuring illustrations, sculptures, photographs and digital collages. Through Saturday, August 5. Reception: Tuesday, July 18, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
