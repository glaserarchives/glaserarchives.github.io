May 22 - 29, 2017
A still from Under Your Spell by Mikey Asanin, screening during the main program of SVA Premieres.

SVA in Hollywood

SVA will return to Hollywood to present SVA Premieres, the College’s annual film and animation showcase, on Thursday, June 1, at The Academy's Linwood Dunn Theater at the Pickford Center for Motion Picture Study in Los Angeles.

"The Rise and Fall of Masculinity"

A Q&A with 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award winner Lily Maslanka (BFA 2017 Visual & Critical Studies), whose final project appropriates the suit of armor, a trope of masculinity, to celebrate the "butch" woman.

Dusty Awards Show Highlights

A recap of SVA's 28th Dusty Film & Animation Festival Awards, where 20 awards were presented to the next wave of up-and-coming animators and filmmakers earlier this month.
IN THE PRESS

"Virtual Plenitude"

In this New Yorker article, visual artist Sarah Sze (MFA 1997 Fine Arts) discusses her piece Timekeeper and what inspired it.

Best of 2017 Commencement Speeches

The New York Times lists the "best and most noteworthy" commencement speeches this graduation season and includes Gloria Steinem's speech at SVA's ceremony earlier this month.

Stories That Need to Be Told

I-D interviews Lacy Wood (BFA 2017 Photography and Video) on her photos of teen girls in their New York City bedrooms.
EVENTS
Bill Wehmann, from "Death by Comics"

Digital Short Story Show

MFA Visual Narrative presents this exhibition of projects by students in the Digital Short Story class, whose task was to create, write and present an original, self-published digital short story in 15 weeks. Through Wednesday, June 7. Reception: Friday, June 2, 6:00 - 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

"Transfiguration"

An exhibition of work by SVA students selected by a jury of their peers, featuring paintings, collages, photography and sculptures that explore the relationship between memory and experience. Through Thursday, June 15. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
