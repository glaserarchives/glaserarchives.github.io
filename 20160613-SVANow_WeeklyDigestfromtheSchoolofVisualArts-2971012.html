SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts June 13 - 19, 2016
FEATURES
Recent graduates and SVA staff pose after the reception following the live action screening at SVA Premieres on June 2. Photo by Star Montana (BFA 2013 Photography).
SVA Premieres in LA
On June 1 and 2, 40 new SVA graduates from nine graduate and undergraduate departments showed their films to industry professionals at the inaugural SVA Premieres, which took place at Hollywood's Linwood Dunn Theater.
Cover Stories: Christian Kracht's Imperium
Designer Adalis Martinez (BFA 2013 Design), author Christian Kracht and translator Daniel Bowles talk about the cover design for the novel's U.S. edition, which was named as a "Best Book Cover of 2015" by The New York Times.
SVA at Annecy
Shift, by 2015 graduates Yijun Liu and Maria Cecilia Pugliesi, and Bloody Dairy, by 2012 graduate Min Liu, are competing in the Graduate Film and Short Film categories, respectively, at this year's Annecy International Animation Film Festival.
Before and After SVA: Jonas Christiansen
The 2014 BFA Advertising alumnus talks about some favorite SVA memories, what it's like transitioning to the work world, his current gig at Facebook, and more.
IN THE PRESS
Still from Taking the Plunge
Taking the Plunge Wins Top Prize at ADC Awards
Ad Age reports that the film, made by 2015 BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumni Thaddaeus Andreades, Elizabeth Ku-Herrero, Nicholas Manfredi and Marie Raoult, won the Black Cube for best-in-show at the 95th annual Art Directors Club Awards, making it the first student entry to receive the honor.
Documenting a Pre-Teen's Struggle with Scoliosis
Hannah Smith Allen (MFA 2007 Photography, Video and Related Media) writes about the photo series "Breaking the Girl," by Julia Cybularz (MFA 2007 Photography, Video and Related Media), for American Photo.
Five Decades with Billy Sullivan
Crave on Still, Looking. Works 1969 – 2016, a book surveying the work by the SVA alumnus and BFA Photography and Video faculty member.
The Politics of Pop Culture
Vice's Creators Project reviews "Pop Culture Is Where the Pedagogy Is," the recent MFA Fine Arts thesis exhibition.
EVENTS
Photo by Christophe Tedjasukmana
NY Photo Salon with Erin Mahoney, Jay Maisel, Christophe Tedjasukmana and Holly Tomlin
MPS Digital Photography presents the four photographers discussing their work. Thursday, June 16, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
BioArt Open Studios
See work by participants in the Continuing Education Summer Residency BioArt program. Thursday, June 16, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 335 West 16th Street, 3rd and 5th floors.
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Thesis Exhibition
Reception Wednesday, June 22, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Saturday, June 18, through Friday, July 1, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
All-Staff Art Show
SVA Galleries and the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group present the annual exhibition of work by administrative staff at the College. Reception Thursday, June 23, 5:30 - 8:00pm. Saturday, June 18, through Friday, July 1, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
