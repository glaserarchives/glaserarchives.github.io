All Faculty Brief

April 2023

Greetings:

Welcome to the end-of-semester installment of the All Faculty Brief.  I hope it finds you well.

Last week, the Office of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion hosted a “Day of Healing” at the SVA Theatre.  DEI director Jarvis Watson invited a clinical psychologist to discuss the many facets of trauma, and offered different ways to begin the process of healing from traumatic events that are both personal and shared.  The event will be available online soon; I’ll get back to you when it’s been posted.  These opportunities to come together, whether in-person or virtually, promote a greater sense of community and allow us the chance to enjoy a purposeful shared experience.  We plan to offer more events like this in the coming academic year.

On March 29, I hosted a gathering of all department chairs to discuss the implications of generative artificial intelligence (AI).  We discussed, among other things, how we as an institution can best navigate these largely uncharted waters.  Some chairs are vehemently opposed to the use of AI by students, while others took a more moderate and nuanced approach.  What struck me was the degree to which students are leary of AI because many regard the use of AI as cheating, and many fear that AI will one day replace them in the workplace. 

What came out of this 90 minute discussion was the need to provide guidance to students about when it’s appropriate to use AI, and the importance of making it clear by way of attribution that the work students are submitting was produced in whole or in part through an AI platform.  Ultimately, it will be up to individual faculty members to decide whether, and to what degree, students may incorporate AI into their work.  

SVA’s academic integrity policy will be updated to include the use of AI without disclosure and/or attribution as plagiarism.  We are working on language for “best practices” in the use of AI in one’s work, and will share this language for you to incorporate into your syllabi or otherwise distribute to your students.  To aid us in developing these best practices, I would be grateful if you would take this very quick survey developed by our colleagues in the Office of Learning Technologies: Faculty Feedback Form: AI in Education.  Thank you.

With the end of the year comes the course evaluation process.  The evaluation system opened about ten days ago and will remain open until the last day of classes.  Please encourage your students to complete the evaluations for your class(es).  Perhaps allocate 15 minutes at the end of a class to allow students to complete the evaluation instrument.

Please read on for important updates from the various offices and departments that make up the Office of Academic Affairs.

Good luck with the remaining days of the spring 2023 semester.  As always, thank you for your commitment to your students and to SVA.


All best wishes,

Chris

End of Semester Support from the Office of Learning Technologies (OLT)

As we near the final push of the spring semester, this Faculty Ignite video, Weighting Assignments in Canvas, may be helpful for you. Before calculating your students' final grades, follow a few simple steps and let Canvas automatically weight all assignments in your course based on the grading system you shared in your syllabus. 

Additionally, did you know that you and your students have access to over 5,000 courses via SVA's LinkedIn Learning subscription? LinkedIn Learning courses are a terrific reference library, and instructors can share specific course chapters with their students to easily deliver high-quality video tutorials. This recent Faculty Ignite video, Creating a LinkedIn Learning Playlist for Your Course, walks you through the process.

Interested in seeing more? You can view all Faculty Ignite videos and many other interesting SVA-related videos! by accessing MediaSpace via myid.sva.edu. You can click here to navigate directly to the Faculty Ignite channel and have the option to subscribe the channel.

Contact your friends at OLT if you have suggestions for other Faculty Ignite! videos or to receive direct assistance with your course content.

Spring 2023 Grading and Attendance Reminders and Summer 2023 Information from the Office of the Registrar 

Final Grading for Spring 2023 Courses
Final grades and attendance for the Spring 2023 term are due by Monday, May 1 at 5:00 pm. We realize this deadline coincides with the last day of the term, so all efforts to submit your grades by the deadline are greatly appreciated. This is especially so if you have any graduating students in your classes. If you have already submitted your grades and attendance, please accept our sincerest thanks! 

All grades must be submitted online via the MyServices portal (which is accessible through your MyID account). For detailed instructions on how to submit grades on MyServices, view our Submitting Grades Online via MyServices guide. Additionally, please post attendance for all class sessions via SVAttendance in Canvas by May 1. For instructions or assistance, view our SVAttendance Faculty Guide.

Students may view their grades in MyServices starting May 2.

Summer 2023 Semester
The Summer 2023 semester officially begins on May 2 and ends August 21. If you are teaching any courses in the summer months, please take attendance via SVAttendance in Canvas as you normally would. You may also wish to note the following College holidays:

     •  Monday, May 29: Memorial Day, College closed
     •  Monday, June 19: Juneteenth holiday, College closed
     •  Saturday through Tuesday, July 1 - 4: Independence Day holiday, College                  closed

Spring 2023 Course Evaluations

This semester's online course evaluations are now LIVE for all undergraduate and graduate students to complete. Continuing Education students will receive theirs on Monday, April 10. Links to the evaluations were sent to each student’s SVA email account. They will simply follow that link to complete their confidential evaluations.

To help us with this important project, please take a moment during your class to encourage students to complete their evaluations by computer or mobile device. If students encounter any technical issues, they should contact the Office of Learning Technologies by phone at (212) 592-2313, or by email at olt@sva.edu.
