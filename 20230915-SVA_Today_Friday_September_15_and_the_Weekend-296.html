Friday, September 15, and the Weekend

Mark Lang

SVA’s “Living Heritage” Exhibition Opens in Provence

Today, “Living Heritage” opens in the historic Saint-Paul de Mausole monastery located in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France, for the 12th year of a transatlantic partnership between SVA and the Valetudo Association.

EVENTS

3:00pm | SVA Club Fair

Clubs are a fantastic way to make friends, discover new interests and get involved on campus. Explore over 30 of SVA’s clubs at the two-day club fair this week. Today, September 15, 3:00 – 5:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Opens Today | “Living Heritage”

For more than a decade, the School of Visual Arts and the Valetudo Association have held a series of public exhibitions of inspiring artwork to celebrate the restorative power of art and the joy that comes from experiencing it with others. See the work from this year’s exhibition in SVA’s online gallery here. Through Sunday, December 31. Online and at Saint-Rémy, Saint-Paul, 13210 Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France.

Last Chance | “Leigh Behnke: Time Travelers and Ghost Ships”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents recent paintings by Leigh Behnke as part of its annual celebration of a faculty member who has made a substantial contribution to SVA and its students. Closes today, September 15. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

“A Gleeful Romp Through Strange and Treacherous Territory”

Richard Brody writes about Craig Gillespie’s (BFA 1989 Media Arts) latest film, Dumb Money, for The New Yorker.

NYC PICKS

Hester Street Fair

A downtown open-air market of New York-based vendors, food, DJs and more. Saturday, September 16, 11:00am – 7:00pm. Pier 17, 89 South Street.

“Geometric Shapes: A Continual Inner Dialogue” Reception

A solo exhibition of work by Ji Eun Lim (MAT 2019 Art Education) created during her residency at Brooklyn Art Cluster, curated by Tzu-Ying (Naomi) Chan (MA 2023 Curatorial Practice). Opening reception: Saturday, September 16, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Friday, September 29. Brooklyn Art Cluster, 540 President Street, Brooklyn.

African American Day Parade

The 54th annual African American Day parade returns with performances by musicians, dancers and more; this year’s theme is “celebrating African American culture.” Sunday, September 17, parade begins at 1:00pm. The parade proceeds from 111th Street to 137th Street on Adam Clayton Powell Jr. Boulevard (Seventh Avenue), Harlem.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Next Week | The Artists Roundtable: Josh Kline (New York)

MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with Josh Kline, an artist best known for creating immersive installations—using video, sculpture, photography and design—that question how emergent technologies are being used to change human life in the 21st century. Tuesday, September 19, 5:00 – 6:00pm, online.

Next Week | SVA Graduate Admissions International Applicant Q&A

SVA invites all prospective international graduate students to learn about the graduate application process and meet staff from both the International Student Office and Graduate Admissions. Get advice about international application requirements, English proficiency tests and how to apply. Friday, September 22, 10:00 – 11:00am.
