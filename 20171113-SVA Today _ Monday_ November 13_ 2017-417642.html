SVA Today
Monday, November 13, 2017

What Does America Stand for Today?

MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman and two program alumni, Anna Buchbauer and Andrew Miller, talk about "Brandstand: States of America," an event that will bring together writers, activists and designers to tackle the question of what America signifies today. Wednesday, November 15, 7:00pm, at the SVA Theatre.
EVENTS

Faculty and Staff Appreciation Week

The SVA Campus Store presents Faculty and Staff Appreciation Week through Friday, November 17. Stop in for discounts on select Apple laptops and iPad Pro models and spin the prize wheel for up to 25% off SVA branded merchandise. All shoppers will receive a free gift and be entered into a weekly raffle for great prizes. Set up an SVA Campus Store Technology Loan and recycle your old tech with Encore! for a gift card toward a future purchase. Email campusstore@sva.edu for more information. SVA Campus Store, 207 East 23rd Street.

2:00pm | SVA in San Francisco Information Session

SVA Career Development and SVA Destinations present two information sessions about the spring break course in San Francisco, which will take students behind the scenes at Pixar, Google, Industrial Light & Magic, and more. 2:00 – 3:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street Room 301C.
PRESS PICK

Alumnus Anna Fine Wins Pixels of Fury

Shuttershock presents Anna Fine (BFA 2012 Advertising) as the winner of this year's Pixels of Fury, a live design competition where contestants designed art for their own sci-fi/horror TV show.
NYC PICKS

Tell Me Something Good: A Brooklyn Rail Roundtable

A panel discussion with Brooklyn Rail publisher Phong Bui along with Jonas Mekas, Shirin Neshat, Shahzia Sikander and Jack Whitten, in celebration of Tell Me Something Good, a book of interviews conducted by the Rail. Tickets $15 – $29.95; purchase here. 7:00pm. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.

The Contenders 2017

A screening series, curated by The Museum of Modern Art's department of film, of the most innovative and influential films of the past year. Tickets free with SVA ID, but must be picked up in-person. Through Friday, January 12. The Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

Dog Therapy

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents dog therapy with New York Therapy Animals. Open to all SVA students. Tomorrow, November 14, 2:30pm. MFA Fine Arts flex space, 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.

Sign Up for a Free New York Public Library Card

An NYPL librarian will be on hand to sign up SVA students, faculty and staff and answer questions. Wednesday, November 15, 11:00am – 2:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

Exchange: Beyond the Boundaries of the Written Word

SVA Library presents a night of readings by Celia Bland, Christine Shan Shan Hou, Emily Hunt and Simone Kearney, four poet-artists who mingle their written works with visual art. Refreshments will be served. Wednesday, November 15, 7:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
