SVA Today
Tuesday, November 8, 2016
Lorna Simpson, Left, 2016, india ink, acrylic and screenprint on Claybord. Via Forbes.

"The Process Detaches Me From the Product"

On Saturday, November 19, "FOCUS: Lorna Simpson," featuring new work by the BFA 1982 Photography graduate, opens at The Modern Art Museum at Fort Worth, Texas, where it will join "Where the End Starts," a major survey of work by fellow SVA alumnus KAWS, a.k.a. Brian Donnelly (BFA 1996 Illustration). In a recent Forbes Q&A to promote the show, Simpson talked about her art, the Black Lives Matter movement and her love of Frank Ocean and Kanye West.

Election Day 2016: Make Your Voice Heard

The 2016 presidential election is today, and New York State polls are open from 6:00am - 9:00pm. If you are registered to vote, click here to confirm your polling location and to see what will be on your ballot. All are encouraged to vote, and to register to vote if they have not already done so, for future participation at the polls.

Picturing Science

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media faculty members Marvin Heiferman and Oliver Wasow discuss Wasow's contribution to Heiferman's project "Seeing Science," which examines the influence and impact of visual culture on the sciences. RSVP here. Wednesday, November 9, 1:00 - 3:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

2016 SVA Staff Survey

To help SVA better serve its employees, the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group and Human Resources have worked together to create the 2016 SVA Staff Survey. All staff are encouraged to complete it by Friday, November 11. The survey is voluntary and anonymous, and results will be kept confidential. Click the headline to access the form.

"Canstruction"
