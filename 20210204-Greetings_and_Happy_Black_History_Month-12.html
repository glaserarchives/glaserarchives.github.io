Dear SVA Community,


Greetings, and Happy Black History Month.  I joined SVA as director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) in early December, and the past few months have been an enlightening and enriching experience. The campus community has embraced me with open arms and with open minds.  Each interaction, Zoom call, and welcoming correspondence has inspired me to want to know more about our College and begin this new journey in creating a more inclusive and equitable campus climate.
Carter Woodson biographical cartoon by Charles Alston, 1943
As I write this letter at the start of Black History Month, and just weeks after the inauguration of Kamala Harris as the first African American and Indian woman Vice President, I cannot help but to reflect on the many heroes and trailblazers like her who have sacrificed so much of their time, spirit and lives for me and many other cultures, nationalities and identities. Another hero is the father and founder of Black History Month, Dr. Carter G. Woodson, who once stated, “The mere imparting of information is not education.” Dr. Woodson argued that our mission as educators should be to intertwine purpose, expectancy and hope into how we teach, communicate, and inspire others.  How we engage with each other should be grounded in improving the human condition and creating a climate of respect and understanding for all.  As your director of DEI, my plan and vision will be aligned with Dr. Woodson’s vision, and SVA’s vision, mission, and core values set out in the College’s strategic plan.


During the past two months, I have begun meeting with campus stakeholders with the hope of developing an understanding about DEI priorities through authentic informal and formal conversations. Beginning this month, I will be convening with the DEI Task Force to focus on how we reimagine DEI at SVA. This task force is composed of a select group of faculty, administrators, staff and students that have contributed to building the foundation for systemic change at SVA. Together, we will collaborate and develop a Strategic Plan for Diversity, Equity and Inclusion. 
The plan will supplement SVA’s 2018-23 Strategic Plan and include the following:

     • A plan for driving the institution’s overall diversity strategy

     • Approaches to galvanizing community stakeholders

     • Methods for delving deeper into the vision, definition, rationale, and                   strategy for implanting diversity, equity, and inclusion priorities


The development of this plan will be created in four phases:

Phase 1: Reconvene the DEI Task Force

     • Developing the shared definition and vision for DEI

     • Developing key working groups

     • Creating a calendar of action items


Phase 2: Data Collection and Diversity Round Tables

     • Conducting Diversity Round Table Discussions with key College                        stakeholders

     • Implementing a Diversity Audit of current and past DEI initiatives

     • Launching a Diversity Video Podcast: Diversity in 3D


Phase 3: Communicating Progress to the Campus

     • Planning a Town Hall Meeting for DEI

     • Sharing progress with the College through various communication                     methods

Phase 4: DEI Action Plan Analysis Phase

     • Analysis of collected data

     • Development of action items

     • Creation of assessment and accountability instruments

     • Launching of the DEI Action Plan
Within the month, we will provide more detailed information on the DEI section of SVA.edu, as well as at an upcoming town hall meeting.  This is a large mission, but this is a mission that will be achieved with the contributions of all College stakeholders.


Collectively, we will be the change that we desire to see in the world.  As your director of DEI, I want to remind you that I am purpose driven, and I will work tirelessly and strategically to fulfill the mission of positive systemic change.  Just as many that have come this way before me, I am led by a spirit of the expectancy for a new era of equality, justice and unity.  To paraphrase a portion of The Black National Anthem, “Lift Every Voice and Sing.” 


I am filled with the hope that what the present has brought us will not stop our quest for victory over intolerance, hate, and discrimination.  Let’s build this future together.


Sincerely,


Jarvis M. Watson, Ed.D.
Director of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion
