🎙️ Get to Know: Adam Natale, Director of SVA Theatre

Tell us a little about your history with SVA and your current role.

My first full week working at SVA was the week of Hurricane Sandy in 2012. It was a very memorable start, with the Theatre shut down due to the weather! But everything has been on the upswing in the decade since. I've seen the SVA Theatre grow significantly, hosting around 30 film festivals and countless movie premieres, lectures, conferences and SVA events annually. What I'm most proud of are the events that I help produce, like After School Special and SVA Premieres. It's really heartening to be so closely tied to so many significant alumni through these programs. I'm excited to see the art (including films) that's created by our students and alumni during what will potentially be a post-pandemic renaissance!


The SVA Theatre recently returned to in-person screenings and events. That must have been an incredible feeling for you after such a long pause. What have been the biggest challenges and greatest rewards of the transition back to in-person programming?

It's definitely been exciting to welcome people back to the Theatre. You can see it on everyone's (masked) faces—they're thrilled to return. It's not just about getting out of the house. A lot of the events we host are annual and people block the days or weeks on their calendars years in advance. So, the biggest reward is to be able to feel everyone's excitement and happiness. The biggest challenge? Getting people to keep their masks on!


The annual fall festival After School Special is returning to SVA in September! Are there any big changes in the works this year? Any particular screening you’re most looking forward to?

We were extremely fortunate with 2021's festival to host several premieres. Since many festivals were nixed due to COVID, several alumni filmmakers lost out on big premiere screenings. We were thrilled to play substitute host to those. This year’s festival will include at least one animation/VFX panel that will be wholly separate from the screenings. It's something we haven't tried yet and I'm excited to see what people think! While I can't yet announce the films for the festival, we have one Sundance gem, a Peabody Award-winner and a Disney film that we don't talk about—"no, no, no!".

﻿﻿ Save The Date: The 2022 SVA All-Staff Meeting

Come celebrate 75 years of SVA! The 2022 SVA All-Staff Meeting takes place on Thursday, August 25, at the SVA Theatre. Breakfast starts at 8:00am, and the program begins at 9:00am. Join us for news and College updates, the Distinguished Staff Award winners, a benefits fair, and more. To ensure everyone's safety, your SVA ID is required and you must have completed the Cleared4 process at least two hours prior to the start of the meeting.


In the meantime, remember to send your submissions to the “Rock On” Pre-Show, where we will be highlighting the first concert you ever went to. Was it Pearl Jam? Duran Duran? Earth, Wind and Fire? Metallica? Please send an email with your first concert experience, the concert venue and what you recall from seeing them live to dhalm@sva.edu by Friday, August 5.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Graphic Design Foundations: Typography

This course explains good typographic practices so that you can develop an eye for type and understand how to effectively use it.


Learning Personal Branding

Learn the secrets of personal branding in this course with marketing expert Chelsea Krost, who explains how to develop your story, craft your messaging and define your audience.


What is Graphic Design

Learn how design is used to convey ideas and concepts, and to enhance interactive experiences. Discover the impact that strong visual communication has on individuals, businesses and the world.


Learning Design Research

Authors Peni Acayo and Andy Schwanbeck walk you through the various types of research (primary vs. secondary, quantitative vs. qualitative, etc.) so that you get a sense of which are appropriate for the job at hand.
