SVA Today
Thursday, May 19, 2022

The SVA Alumni Behind Signs of Life
Signs of Life, a long-in-the-works planetarium show for the Griffith Observatory in Los Angeles, premieres at the landmark institution tomorrow. Read the fall/winter 2019 Visual Arts Journal article about the SVA alumni involved in its production at the link above.
Events

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).

Today will be the last ISO virtual drop-in hour until the fall semester. After today, international students can schedule an appointment with an ISO advisor here. ISO will announce updated fall 2022 advising hours in August.

7:00pm | Aline Smithson: Collecting Stardust With One Hand
SVA Continuing Education presents a talk with visual artist, editor and educator Aline Smithson, who is best known for her conceptual portraiture and a practice that uses humor and pathos to explore the performative potential of photography. Tonight, 7:00pm, online; register here.

7:00pm | SVA Motion Portfolio Screening
BFA Design presents its annual screening of motion-graphics portfolios from members of the program’s latest graduating class. Tonight, 7:00pm; register here. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

NYC PICKS

The Riverside Bench Screening
NYC Parks’ Movies Under the Stars program presents a free screening of The Riverside Bench, directed by Austin Chang (BFA 2013 Film and Video). Tonight, 7:30pm. Riverside Park at the runner’s track on West 74th Street.

“Illustrator as Witness”
An exhibition presenting a selection of author and illustrator Nora Krug’s (MFA 2004 Illustration as Visual Essay) politically motivated visual nonfiction work. Through September 4. Student admission: $10. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.

Brooklyn Public Library’s Night of Ideas
A free nighttime event featuring philosophical debates, musical performances, screenings and lively forums. Saturday, May 21, 7:00pm. Central Library, 10 Grand Army Plaza, Brooklyn.
