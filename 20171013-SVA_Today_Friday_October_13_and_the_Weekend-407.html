SVA Today
Friday, October 13, and the Weekend

NYC Exhibition Roundup

From "sinful" designs to protest art to gender fluid expressions, here are several current and upcoming exhibitions at museums that SVA students, faculty and staff can visit for free.
EVENTS

Saturday | Undergraduate Open House

SVA Admissions presents an open house for prospective undergraduate students of Animation, Interior Design, Photography and Video and Visual & Critical Studies. 9:00am – 3:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | Yearbook Photo Shoot

SVA Yearbook is shooting portraits for the 2018 edition. All fourth-year students are encouraged to dress how they like, stop by and have their photo taken. The yearbook is given to students at commencement for free. This will be the only weekend shoot. Tomorrow, October 14, noon – 5:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, ground floor.

Saturday | Blade Runner 2049

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Blade Runner 2049, starring Ryan Gosling and Harrison Ford. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted nor required. Tomorrow, October 14, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Men and Women Co-Working

BFA Interior Design Chair Jane Smith discusses gender-imposed barriers in the workplace and the necessity of challenging them, for Real Estate Weekly.
NYC PICKS

NYsferatu: Symphony of a Century

A screening of NYsferatu: Symphony of a Century, hand-animated by artist Andrea Mastrovito, followed by a panel "Our Vampires, Our Selves: Immigrants, Desire, Fear" with Mastrovito and two religious studies scholars at NYU. Free; RSVP to info@moreart.org. Tonight, 6:00pm. Cantor Film Center, 36 East 8th Street.

Study Sessions: Yulan Grant

Yulan Grant, a New York-based multidisciplinary artist from Kingston, Jamaica, will select a work of art on view in the Whitney’s permanent collection galleries and use it to think through an urgent question in our current political landscape. Free with SVA ID; register here. Tonight, 7:00pm. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.

Design Fest

As part of Cooper Hewitt Design Week, the museum presents a day of design workshops for all ages. Schedule here. Free; registration recommended. Tomorrow, October 14, 11:00am – 4:00pm. Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.

Celebrate Mexico Now Festival

A week of literature, theater, film, culinary and music events that celebrates Mexico's contemporary art and culture. Schedule here. Tomorrow, October 14 – Sunday, October 22. Locations vary by event.
TAKE NOTE

VASA Student Senator Information Sessions

Want to represent your program and get involved in the student government? Attend an information session and learn how to become a Student Senator with VASA. Email vasa@sva.edu with any questions. Monday, October 16, 1:00pm, and Thursday, October 19, 5:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Creatively Reusing Materials to Engage Participation

As part of its Community Lecture Series, MPS Art Therapy presents a talk by John Cloud Kaiser, MA, whose work focuses on giving people a creative participatory experience with an art piece while at the same time creating a dynamic work of art. Friday, October 20, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
