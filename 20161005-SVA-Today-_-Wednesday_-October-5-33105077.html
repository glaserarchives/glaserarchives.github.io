SVA Today
Wednesday, October 5
FEATURE

"Underground" in Romania

"Underground Images," the travelling retrospective of SVA's long-running subway poster series, recently opened in Timisoara, Romania, with a special three-tiered exhibition that is on view at various locations in the city through October and November.
EVENTS

1:00pm: Pixar Information Session and Screening

BFA Animation, BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects, MFA Computer Art and SVA Career Development present representatives from Pixar discussing the studio's internship program, as well as writer-directors Andrew Coats and Lou Hamou-Lhadj screening and discussing their short film Borrowed Time. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Click here to RSVP. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

6:00pm: "Inside and Out" Reception

MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition of work by Rikers Island inmates. Exhibition runs through Monday, October 17, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:30pm: Point and Shoot

BFA Photography and Video presents a screening of the documentary Point and Shoot (2014), which is about a soldier who documented his travels to Libya to help rebels overthrow Muammar Gaddafi. Filmmaker Marshall Curry will answer questions following the screening. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

Surrealist Photography

Communication Arts highlights "The Nostalgia of the Infinite," a series of work by Ping Wang Xin (MPS 2016 Digital Photography) that is currently on view in "Sight Seen."
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY

NYC's Biggest Pizza Party

Slice Out Hunger presents over 50 pizzerias offering $1 slices. All proceeds support area hunger-relief programs. 6:00pm, 155 Sullivan Street.

Real People Fight the Swipe

Susie Lee of Siren, a dating app, talks about the "shopping-for-humans" online dating model and alternatives. 6:30pm, International Center of Photography, 250 Bowery.
TAKE NOTE

Sergei Miroshnichenko

MFA Social Documentary Film presents the Russian documentary filmmaker discussing and screening clips from his films. Saturday, October 8, 10:30am, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Anastasia Samoylova

MPS Digital Photography presents photographer Anastasia Samoylova discussing her work, which appropriates naturalistic images of mountains, trees, flowers and the ocean from the Internet to create layered, collage-like compositions that reinterpret the concept of landscape photography. Tuesday, October 11, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
