SVA is Nominated for a Webby!

It’s Webbys season and SVA.edu has been nominated as an outstanding website and mobile site in the school/university category for the Webby People’s Voice Awards. Vote here by Thursday, April 20, to help capture this prestigious award!

EVENTS

3:00pm | “Not too hot, Not too cold” Reception

MA Curatorial Practice presents a curatorial project organized and facilitated by student Sophia Park exploring the role of friendship in art collectives and collaborative art practices. Through Thursday, April 27. Reception is tonight, April 14, 3:00 – 8:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Sunday | Renfield Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Renfield, starring Nicolas Cage. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, April 16, 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sunday | The Pope’s Exorcist Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of The Pope’s Exorcist, starring Russell Crowe. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, April 16, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Women’s Fund for Media, Music and Theatre Awardees

Chelsi Bullard (MFA 2017 Social Documentary Film), Maya Cozier (BFA 2016 Film) and Catya Plate (1988 Fine Arts) have been selected to receive the New York City Women’s Fund grant from the Mayor’s Office of Media and Entertainment.

NYC AND ONLINE PICKS

Latinx Art and American Pasts and Futures

A virtual conversation on Latinx art and how three museums are navigating the framing of national culture and our understandings of U.S. art and history. Free. Today, April 14, 1:00 – 2:00pm, online.

The Schomburg Center’s Annual Black Comic Book Festival

A festival celebrating Black comic books and graphic novels featuring panel discussions, workshops and cosplay showcases. Free. Today, April 14, 10:00am – 8:00pm, and tomorrow, April 15, 10:00am – 8:00pm. Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture, 515 Malcolm X Boulevard.

The Manhattan Vintage Show

The largest vintage show in New York City featuring over 90 dealers and their collections of vintage clothing, jewelry, accessories and textiles. Student tickets: $15. Today, April 14, 1:00 – 7:00pm, and tomorrow, April 15, 11:00am – 6:00pm. Metropolitan Pavilion, 125 West 18th Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Chance to Apply: The Culture, Design and Fine Arts in New York Summer Program Is Hiring

SVA’s Culture, Design and Fine Arts in New York Summer Program, a three-week workshop for international college students, is seeking program assistants for July 2023. SVA students and recent alumni are invited to apply. Contact the Office of Programs for International Students at opis@sva.edu to request position details. To apply, email your resume and statement of interest to opis@sva.edu by tomorrow, April 15.

Next Week | VASA April Town Hall: End of Year Recap

Next Week | Venture 23: Open for Business

MFA Design presents a showcase of final venture projects pitched live by graduating students. Friday, April 21, 5:30 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. This event will also be streamed online.

Animation Celebration Screening

BFA Animation presents a screening of animated projects by graduating students. Saturday, May 13; doors open at 11:00am, screening starts at 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. The celebration continues outside of the SVA Theatre at the “Animation Celebration Exhibition.”
