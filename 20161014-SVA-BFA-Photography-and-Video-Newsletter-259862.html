Alum Joni Sternbach Surf Photographer Shortlisted for National Portrait Gallery Prize

The National Portrait Gallery of London has announced Alum Joni Sternbach (1977) as a contender for the Taylor Wessing Portrait Prize. Joni, a New York native, uses the wet plate collodion method to create tintype portraits of surfers. “It’s a process that draws you in with its beauty and luscious qualities. It’s hard to resist!”
Alum Morten Andenæs Exhibits at Galleri Riis

Morten Andenæs' (2002) second exhibition at Galleri Riis Stockholm speaks to his interest in the role representation plays, not only in describing the world as something given, but in constituting it. Narratives of social identity, of inclusion and exclusion, of family ties and social bonds all emanate from the basic impulse to re-present the world through language and images.

Black Women Artists Matter: In conversation with Tiffany Smith
via New Museum

Tiffany Smith's (above) practice analyzes themes of race and identity in relation to communities of people of color. From this perspective, Smith explores how individual narratives can be influential in uncovering a multitude of multi-cultural experiences. The New Museum spoke with Smith regarding her perception of Simone Leigh’s Black Women Artists for Black Lives Matter and how she feels work like Leigh’s can bring a narrative to an under-represented sector within the world of contemporary art.

Visit the New Museum to read the conversation. 
Senior Harrison Weinstein's Photographs of Actress Rebecca Hall Featured in Movie Maker Magazine 

Featured in the fall issue of Movie Maker Magazine is an article about the new film, Christine. Directed by Antonio Campos, Christine is a film about Christine Chubbuck, the television reporter who took her life on air in 1974. Christine is played by actress Rebecca Hall. Senior Harrison Weinstein was asked by the associate editor of Movie Maker Magazine to photograph Rebecca Hall for the magazine's two-page article about the movie. Harrison brought Rebecca and her crew here to SVA's studios this past summer to photograph her.

Interview with Mentor Ryan McGinley in Musee Magazine

Traveling and photographing the American landscape on yearly road trips since 2014, Ryan McGinley has created a body of work representing the nude body in American landscapes. "Every summer, I would just chip away a part of America. We would hit the road and shoot for three months straight." The interview includes discussions about Ryan's works, studio spaces, and process.

Faculty Steel Stillman and Staff Member Max C. Lee Exhibit In Art F City's Must See Exhibit

Curators Erin Davis and Max C. Lee (above) are launching the fourth iteration of their “ever-evolving group exhibition” housed within the former compounding facility of the now-defunct Pfizer pharmaceutical factory. The exhibition introduces a new structure to the exhibition format, with no set duration, multiple openings, and a dynamic environment. For its fourth exhibition, artists explore two levels of vast industrial spaces with looming mixing tanks, chemical ducts, and emergency hazmat showers. Each room boasts remnants of their past uses for the artist to examine. 

Saturday, October 16, 6-10PM

Staff Introduction - Steven Mayer

Steven is the Administrative Assistant for BFA Photography & Video. He grew up in New Jersey and received his BFA in Sculpture from the Tyler School of Art in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He completed his MFA at Rutgers Mason Gross in 2014 and currently lives and maintains his studio in Brooklyn. Previously, he was the Visitor Services and Membership Manager at SculptureCenter, taught BFA Video and Media at Rutgers University, and worked at the Sally D.
