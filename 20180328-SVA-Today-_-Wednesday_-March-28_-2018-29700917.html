SVA Today
Wednesday, March 28, 2018

Dining by Design

A few BFA Interior Design students presented their work this past weekend as part of Dining by Design, an exhibition event that invites participants to transform a raw space into an immersive dining exhibit and raises awareness and funds for HIV/AIDS research, education, treatment and advocacy.
EVENTS

3:00pm | Dog Therapy

De-stress with New York Therapy Animals, presented by SVA Student Health and Counseling Services. 3:00 – 4:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 1022.

6:30pm | Phil Patton Lecture: Natasha Jen with Khoi Vinh

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a lecture by award-winning designer and SVA alumnus Natasha Jen and designer and writer Khoi Vinh, celebrating the memory of department faculty member Phil Patton. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Jackie Greene and Bill Plympton Music Video Extravaganza

SVA Theatre presents the world premiere of musician Jackie Greene and animator Bill Plympton's (1969 Cartooning) new animated short film, The Modern Lives, and a Q&A with both artists. RSVP here. 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Haring's Work with Students

Artsy on the mural Keith Haring (1979 Fine Arts) worked on with high schoolers in 1989, which has been reinstalled in Chicago's Cultural Center as a highlight of their "Year of Creative Youth."
NYC PICKS

RU Talk: New Family

Curator and critic Wu Jianru shares her curatorial research regarding the evolving formation of Asian families, followed by a conversation with Zoe Jiang Meng, PhD candidate of cinema studies at NYU, while showing work by New York-based artist Dachal Choi. Free. 6:30pm. Residency Unlimited, 360 Court Street, Brooklyn.

"Adrian Piper" Member Previews

As corporate members at The Museum of Modern Art, SVA faculty and staff can visit the museum during its Member Preview Days to see "Adrian Piper: A Synthesis of Intuitions, 1965–2016" before it opens to the public. Show your SVA ID at the admission desk to get your ticket. Through Friday, March 30, during museum hours. The Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

Netflix's Amateur

SVA Theatre presents a special advance screening of Netflix's original film Amateur, written and directed by Ryan Koo and produced by BFA Film faculty member Chip Hourihan. A Q&A with cast and crew will follow. Free; RSVPs not accepted or required. Thursday, April 5, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Seeking Photos of Tim Rollins

MFA Art Practice is gathering photos of the late SVA alumnus and faculty member Tim Rollins for a visual archive of his life and work, and to draw from for his memorial on Monday, April 30, at the SVA Theatre. Upload photos as .jpeg files to this Dropbox and include a text file with your photo file name, names of people pictured, location and date, as well as your name and email. Images sized at 1920x1080 pixels (or close) are preferred. Email artpractice@sva.edu with questions.
