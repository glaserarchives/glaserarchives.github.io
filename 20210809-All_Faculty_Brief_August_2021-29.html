All Faculty Brief | August 2021

August 9, 2021

Greetings:

I hope this issue of the All Faculty Brief finds you well and enjoying these last few weeks of our summer break.

The arrival of the fall 2021 semester comes, for all of us, with a mixture of excitement and trepidation.  We have throughout the summer been emerging from our isolation into some version of normalcy, fleeting as it may seem at times.

New student orientation begins on Monday, August 30, and classes commence on Tuesday, September 7.  A remarkable amount of planning has gone into preparing for the reopening of SVA.  Chief among our various priorities is the safety of each member of our community.  Vaccinations and masks are the two most powerful weapons against the continuing spread of COVID-19 and its multiple variants.  These are what allow us to open the fall semester to in-person classes.

To that end, every member of the SVA community must provide proof of vaccination and wear an appropriate face covering at all times while inside an SVA building.  Even if you are teaching all your classes online, we ask that you submit proof of vaccination; there may well be times that you need to visit the campus, and without your vaccination records on file, you won’t be granted access.  Below you will find all the necessary information to submit your vaccination records.

This issue of the All Faculty Brief is chock full of important information about the reopening of our campus.

From the Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI), director Jarvis Watson presents a new installment of the video podcast Diversity in 3D, titled,  “A Pride Month Reflection.”  Our Registrar provides important information about teaching contracts, the restoration of the College’s attendance policy, student registration, and the publication in MyServices and on faculty rosters of students’ pronouns and preferred names.  The Office of Learning Technologies (OLT) introduces a new syllabus tool called Simple Syllabus, along with course building resources and support from OLT staff.  The SVA Libraries announce their hours of operation for the eastside and westside locations, and the addition of new library resources

The College has identified rooms across campus that are available to faculty to conduct their online courses, particularly for those who teach an online course shortly before or after an in-person class.

The SVA Theatre opens this fall.  Read on to learn more about the theatre’s safety protocols.

As always, I am grateful for the hard work and dedication you invest in your students, and the professionalism and aplomb with which you have weathered the COVID wars. Thank you!  I look forward to seeing as many of you as possible throughout the fall semester.

Wishing you all the best for an excellent start to the 2021-22 academic year.

 
Warm regards,
Chris

Important New ID Requirement for the Fall 2021 Semester


As part of enhanced screening protocols, all SVA community members must have an active ID card to enter any campus building. If your ID was issued prior to fall 2015 or if it does not look like the template above, you will need a new card. complete the SVA ID Card Request Form, which is available as a tile on your MyID dashboard. The Registrar's Office team will notify you by email and will arrange to have your card mailed to you or available for pick-up.
Your ID card will link to the new COVID-19 safety system that you will need to fill out daily prior to entry into any SVA building. Please be on the lookout for the announcement of the new online system, which will be coming in early August and require some initial input of information.

A Pride Month Reflection


Pride Month symbolizes and represents the continued fight for freedom of identity and expression for the LGBTQIA+ (Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, Transgender, Queer, Questioning, Intersexual, Asexual, Two Spirit and Ally) community. As an ally, Dr. Jarvis Watson, Director, Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, wanted to spread awareness about the month and its purpose, but also provide depth regarding the letters within the acronym.

It is important to create an opportunity and a space to hear directly from individuals that identify as being part of the LGBTQIA+ Community and provide authentic perspectives on their lived experiences and their views on particular topics.

This episode of Diversity in 3D is a reflection of Pride Month and its cultural significance for the LGBTQIA+ Community.  Creating a brave space to hear such authentic perspectives about Pride month are two student leaders that identify as being part of the LGBTQIA+ Community, Daniel Doubalay and Elena Kim, Co-Presidents of LBGTQ SVA Student Organization, joined by Dr. Watson. The conversation can be viewed here or by clicking on the screenshot above.

Fall 2021 Updates from the Registrar's Office


Faculty Contracts for the 2021-2022 Academic Year
In an effort to minimize the number of revisions that typically occur prior to the fall semester due to fluctuating student enrollments, please note that initial contracts will be distributed via SVA email during the first week of August. It should be noted that in most cases an individual course must have at least 10 students enrolled in order for the course to appear on your contract. Fluctuations in enrollment can be expected during the official course adjustment periods when students are allowed to add and drop classes (August 5-6 and September 7-13), so in certain cases contract revisions may be unavoidable. You may monitor live enrollment in your classes by viewing your rosters in MyServices. Please make contact with your academic department if you have any questions regarding your teaching schedule.

Attendance Policy Reinstated for Fall 2021
Note that SVA's official attendance policy will be reinstated beginning in the Fall 2021 semester. Students who are marked absent for one-third of a course's total sessions will be administratively withdrawn from the course with a grade of "W". Individual faculty members may adopt an attendance policy that is more restrictive than the institution's policy, but students will not be officially withdrawn until the absence threshold in the policy is met. All faculty members should note attendance expectations and how absences may affect a student's final grade in their syllabi. Faculty members may also wish to consider extending a certain amount of clemency with absences due to illness, particularly while COVID-19 remains a threat to public health. The full attendance policy can be viewed in the most recent SVA Handbook.

Important Note on Registration for Fall 2021
Please note that individual faculty members cannot grant permission to students to register for a course that has reached its maximum capacity. Students may only be registered for open courses with available seats, with the permission of their academic advisors. Students are not allowed to attend any class for which they are not officially registered. You may monitor live enrollment in your classes by viewing your rosters in MyServices.

Chosen Names and Pronouns on Rosters
Students are able to officially declare the preferred/chosen names and pronouns by which they wish to be referred, and you will see this information reflected on your class rosters in MyServices and on your attendance rosters in Canvas. If a student informs you of their chosen name or pronouns and the information does not appear on your rosters, please take note of their wishes in your personal records and encourage them to officially declare their personal identity options in their user profile in MyServices. A student's chosen name and pronouns should be respected at all times.

Required Syllabus Update in Canvas
Prior to the start of the fall semester, SVA faculty are required to enter their course syllabus into the College's new syllabus tool called Simple Syllabus. There are lots of advantages to using Simple Syllabus including that once your Fall 2021 syllabus is entered, you will be able to automatically copy it to future semesters.
To make this transition as easy as possible, please join your friends at OLT for a workshop in August. Several dates are available, but please sign up today to ensure your preferred date/time: Intro to Simple Syllabus & Using Active Learning to Drive Engagement is listed on OLT's Eventbrite page.

Preparing for the Fall Semester: In-person and Online
Faculty are encouraged to revisit the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas which includes new and updated teacher resources and training options. You can also schedule one-to-one appointments with instructional designers and media specialists from OLT.
August is the perfect time to attend a few SVA faculty professional development workshops. We encourage all faculty teaching this fall semester to attend Intro to Simple Syllabus & Using Active Learning to Drive Engagement. In addition to covering the basics of using Simple Syllabus, we will dive into some of the active learning principles that enhance in-person and online courses. Here too you will find the highly recommended Transitioning to Online 2.0: Designing Excellence in Online Art & Design Ed for all faculty who will be teaching online this semester. So much of what was covered in the 2020 version of Transitioning to Online, by necessity, was emergency remote teacher training and could not address some important aspects involved in promoting quality online learning. We hope you will attend and use this resource as an opportunity to take a more calm and intentional approach to plan activities for any Fall 2021 courses that will run online. All of the above and other engaging faculty resources on topics such as using Voicethread for online group critiques and mastering your video edits can be found and registered for here.

Current COVID-19 Safety Protocols In Practice at the

SVA Theatre


For the remainder of the summer, events held at SVA Theatre will require that you present your vaccination card and a photo ID for entrance.  If you are not vaccinated, you will be required to show proof of a negative COVID-19 test taken within 72 hours prior to the event.  Unvaccinated guests will be seated in a different section, socially distant from other guests.  All guests are required to wear masks while inside, and food and drink (except water) are not permitted.  Our COVID-19 protocols are reviewed and revised often, so stay tuned for updates.

SVA's Land Acknowledgement


The School of Visual Arts acknowledges that the campus resides on unceded Indigenous lands, specifically the Lenapehoking or the Land of the Lenape (“lun-NAH-pay”). The main Lenape meeting place, the Kintecoying or "Crossroads of Three Nations" was located near Astor Place, just blocks away from SVA's present campus, before their forced migrations which began in 1626. The School of Visual Arts acknowledges that colonial narratives have overpowered and often shielded mainstream recognition of the historic and continual displacement of Native people by the United States.


SVA recognizes its ethical obligation as an educational institution to offer resources that shed light on accurate depictions of history. This acknowledgement indicates a commitment to confronting genocide, displacement, and the erasure of Indigenous peoples while recognizing that land acknowledgement is only a starting point for dialogue, relationships, and informed action. In an effort to gather resources about the perseverance of Indigenous communities as they relate to our mission as an art library, the SVA Library has created a subject guide about contemporary artists native to the Americas. We will continue to build on this resource and others.
