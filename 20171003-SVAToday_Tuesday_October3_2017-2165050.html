Christoph Niemann, Downtown, silkscreen, 2016.

"The Masters Series: Christoph Niemann" Reception Tonight

SVA honors acclaimed illustrator, artist and author Christoph Niemann with the 29th annual Masters Series Award and Exhibition, featuring the illustrations, magazine covers, animations and digital works that have built his reputation as one of the most renowned creative talents in the field today. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Saturday, November 4. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
EVENTS

5:00pm | Mid-Autumn Festival

SVA's International Student Office presents the Mid-Autumn Festival, featuring a reception with food, moon cakes and tea, followed by a screening of The Tale of Princess Kaguya by Studio Ghibli, Japan. RSVP here. Reception: 5:00 – 6:30pm, 24th Street Residence terrace; screening: 7:00 – 9:00pm, 24th Street Residence media room, 340 East 24th Street.

6:30pm | Andrea Lipps

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Andrea Lipps, a writer, educator and assistant curator of contemporary design at Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum. She will discuss Dutch designer Joris Laarman, in light of his current retrospective at the museum. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

6:30pm | Mika Rottenberg

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by Mika Rottenberg (BFA 2001 Fine Arts), whose elaborate visual narratives draw on cinematic and sculptural traditions to forge a new language. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

7:00pm | The 2017 AICP Show

In collaboration with SVA, The Association of Independent Commercial Producers (AICP) presents a free screening of the 2017 AICP Show, which offers insight into commercial filmmaking and the power of advertising. Work in the show is included in the archives of the film department at The Museum of Modern Art and is assembled by a curatorial panel of prominent industry leaders. A Q&A with representatives from Final Cut, Union Editorial and COPILOT Music + Sound will follow. Free. 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Dodging and Burning: A Night of Memoir and Fiction

SVA Library presents a night of readings by Alan Kaufman, whose 2000 memoir Jew Boy has just been republished; Rebbie Ratner, director of the 2016 documentary Borderline; and New York Post reporter Khristina Narizhnaya. Hosted by SVA librarian David Pemberton. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Juno Seo

BFA Fine Arts presents a talk by Juno Seo, an independent curator based in Seoul, South Korea, who will discuss his contemporary art activities in Asia. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

The Interior Lives of Women

Artsy's list of 15 exhibitions to see in New York this month includes "The Future Is Elsewhere (If It Breaks Your Heart)," a show of figurative paintings by Heidi Hahn (BFA 1989 Fine Arts), which opens on Thursday, October 12, at Jack Hanley Gallery, 327 Broome Street.
NYC PICK

"Mummies"

An up-close look at rarely exhibited mummies from around the world. Tickets start at $16.50 (includes museum admission); purchase here. Through January 7. American Museum of Natural History, Central Park West at 79th Street.
TAKE NOTE

SVA Supply Drive for Natural Disaster Relief

Please join SVA Student Affairs and VASA in gathering supplies for the victims of natural disasters in Mexico, Puerto Rico and the Caribbean. Supplies needed include bottled water, diapers and baby wipes, first-aid supplies, toiletries, non-perishable/canned food, batteries, heavy duty trash bags, portable lamps, disinfectant wipes and water purifying tablets. Drop off supplies Wednesday – Friday, 10:00am – 10:00pm, at the SVA Student Center, 215 East 23rd Street. Those who are interested and able can make monetary donations to the Red Cross, Unicef or United Way.

Leigh Ledare and Steel Stillman

BFA Photography and Video and Dear Dave, magazine present artist Leigh Ledare and artist and writer Steel Stillman, who will discuss Ledare’s current solo exhibition at The Art Institute of Chicago and the complex interpersonal dynamics that are at the heart of his work. Thursday, October 5, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
