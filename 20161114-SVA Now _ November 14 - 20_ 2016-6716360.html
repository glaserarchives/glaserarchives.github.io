IN THE PRESS
Wheel by Pink Sparrow, concept by Pablo Delcan (BFA 2012 Graphic Design). Photo by Henry Leutwyler, via The New York Times.

The 'Redesign' Craze

In yesterday's New York Times Magazine, MFA Products of Design faculty member Rob Walker wrote about "makeover mania" and MFA 2015 Interaction Design graduates Luke Stern and Amy Wu's successful redesign of New York City's CitiBike kiosks.

'Like a Stroll through the Louvre'

The Los Angeles Times interviews MFA Social Documentary Chair Maro Chermayeff and her co-director and co-producer Jeff Dupre about Soundbreaking, their PBS miniseries on the history of recorded music, which premieres Monday, November 14. Read our piece on the series here.

'The Process Detaches Me From the Product'

On Saturday, November 19, "FOCUS: Lorna Simpson," featuring new work by the BFA 1982 Photography graduate, opens at The Modern Art Museum at Fort Worth, Texas, where it will join "Where the End Starts," a major survey of work by fellow SVA alumnus KAWS, a.k.a. Brian Donnelly (BFA 1996 Illustration). In a recent Forbes Q&A to promote the show, Simpson talked about her art, the Black Lives Matter movement and her love of Frank Ocean and Kanye West.

Artist: Gordon Parks

John Edwin Mason

A talk by Mason, associate professor, University of Virginia, and the author of the forthcoming book, Gordon Parks: American Photographer, the first in-depth study of the photo-essays on race and social justice that Parks produced forLife from 1948 through 1970. Presented by MPS Digital Photography. Tuesday, November 15, 7:00 - 8:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Kalup Linzy

MFA Fine Arts presents faculty member and video and performance artist Kalup Linzy discussing his work. Tuesday, November 15, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
