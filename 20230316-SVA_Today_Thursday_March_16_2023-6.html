NYC AND ONLINE PICKS

Growing Up Underground Talk

Letterform Archive presents an interview with MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller on what it was like to design counterculture publications in the 1960s and ’70s on the occasion of his new memoir, Growing Up Underground. Free. Today, March 16, 12:00 – 1:30pm, online.

Library After Hours: Icons of Women’s History

A night of dancing, performances and exclusive access to New York Public Library collections in celebration of Women’s History Month—activities include librarian-led book matchmaking, screenings of rare 16mm films and more. Tickets are pay-what-you-wish, with a suggested cost of $15. Friday, March 31, 7:00pm. Stephen A. Schwarzman Building, Fifth Avenue at 42nd Street.

#SVAwesome

BFA Illustration faculty member Edel Rodriguez has released his new illustrated memoir, Worm, recounting his childhood in Cuba, his family's imprisonment in a detention center and their eventual arrival in Florida. Worm tells the story of Rodriguez’s coming-of-age as an artist and activist grappling with questions of patriotism and belonging in the United States. Via @bfacomicsandbfaillustration.
