SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, December 22, 2015
The Inspiring Story of Adaptive Athletes
SVA Close Up spoke with cinematographer and co-producer Michael Sassano (BFA 2011 Photography) about his new documentary Tin Soldiers, which tells the story of those who have overcome amputation, spina bifida and paralysis as adaptive athletes.

We want to hear what you think about SVA Today. Take our readership survey—it shouldn't take longer than a few minutes—and let us know what you like, don't like and want to see in SVA Today.
Last Chance: "Intimacy in Discourse"
SVA Chelsea Gallery, in collaboration with Rail Curatorial Projects, presents an exhibition of paintings whose sizes affect their relationship to viewers. Curated by SVA faculty member Phong Bui. Closes today. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Press pick of the day
Best Exhibitions of 2015 From Around the World
Hyperallergic's list of the best exhibitions of the year from around the world includes "Patriots, Citizens, Lovers..." by BFA Photography and Video faculty member Carlos Motta (BFA 2001 Photography), which is currently on view at the Pinchuk Art Center, Kiev.
NYC picks of the day
Monika Baer: "On Hold"
The artist's first solo exhibition in New York features her signature paintings. Through Saturday, January 16, Greene Naftali Gallery, 508 West 26th Street, 8th floor.
Deborah Kass: "No Kidding"
An exhibition of new mixed media paintings by the artist whose OY/YO sculpture is currently on view in Brooklyn Bridge Park. Through Saturday, January 23, Paul Kasmin Gallery, 515 West 27th Street.

NYC Fire and Disaster Drill
As part of New York City's Community Emergency Response Team, a training exercise consisting of a controlled fire and disaster drill will be conducted at the Stein Senior Center on East 23rd Street today at 6:30PM. The drill location is directly across the street from SVA's 209 East 23rd Street location. Expect to see FDNY and NYPD personnel in action. To learn more, visit nyc.gov/cert.
Star Wars: The Force Awakens in 3D
The Visual Effects Society presents a 3D screening of Star Wars: The Force Awakens. Following the screening, there will be a live-streamed panel discussion with a team of visual effects artists who worked on the film. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Seating is first-come, first-served. Doors open approximately 30 - 45 minutes prior to showtime. Sunday, January 10, 3:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
