SVA BFA Photography and Video Newsletter

#MAKETHISWALL: A Collaborative Department Project
 
#MAKETHISWALL is a collaborative project addressing our current political climate. We invite you to post your photos, draw, paint, put up found objects, and hang posters on the back wall of the 5th floor at 214 E 21 St. On Instagram, use the hashtag #makethiswall and tag @svabfaphotovideo. 
Evocation Collection Interviews Sinjun Strom

Senior Sinjun Strom discusses the differences between her personal projects and her fashion photography, what drives her as a photographer, and her use of film and zines. 
Evie McKenna's Tufts of Flowers at Wave Hill 

Faculty Evie McKenna's exhibition, Tufts of Flowers, is on display through July 9, 2017 at Wave Hill House.
Ryan Shorosky Photographs the Professional Bull Riders Circuit

Once a year, the Professional Bull Riders circuit takes over Madison Square Garden. Alum Ryan Shorosky (2013) got into the arena and behind the scenes to photograph the riders for The New York Times.

Harvard College Film Festival
 
Harvard's first ever Film Festival run by Harvard students is open to undergraduate students. Film's must be under 15 minutes in length. Submission deadline is March 15, 2017. 
Deana Lawson, Judy Linn, and Paul Magi Sepuya at Sikkema Jenkins Gallery

Hyperallergic reviews the work of photographers Deana Lawson, Judy Linn, and Paul Magi Sepuya currently exhibiting in a group show at the Sikkema Jenkins Gallery.
Protecting Our Nature and Our Sacred Land at Queens Museum

Protecting Our Nature and Our Sacred Land is an exhibition of photographs of Tonto National Forest taken by Standing Fox, Floor Grootenhuis and Erin Turner. The work seeks to convey the importance of the sacred lands of the Apache in Oak Flat, Arizona.
AnOther Expedition: Monet’s Garden at Clamp Art

Pipo Nguyen-duy creates cyanotypes using plant materials from Claude Monet's garden. This exhibition is on display until April 1, 2017. 
Penumbra Hosts Spring Lecture Series 

The Penumbra Artist Series brings to life the work of featured photographers and other notable guest artists and scholars as they discuss their work and process. It is held on Tuesday nights at 7:00PM.
Raymond Pettibon: A Pen of All Work at The New Museum

Using his signature interplay between images and text, this retrospective of Raymond Pettibon's drawings chronicle the history, mythology, and culture of America.
Open Forum with Stephen Frailey 

Department Chair Stephen Frailey and Student Senators Ken Castaneda and Brooke Trisolini host a Town Hall Meeting which is open to BFA Photography and Video students on Wednesday, February 22, at 9:00PM in the 2nd floor hall of 214 E 21 St. 
Time Lightbox Features Fernando Gomez

A month before the election, alum Fernando Gomes (2015) traveled for three days on Amtrak from New York to Seattle documenting the rural towns and broken dreams of those traveling west.
Katie Murray's That Shadow, My Likeness Opens in Memphis

Faculty member Katie Murray's twenty-fifth show with Tops Gallery opens this Friday, February 24, and runs through April 2, 2017. That Shadow, My Likeness, a title borrowed from Walt Whitman's
Leaves of Grass, engages sight, sound, and the passing of time. Murray's intimate color portraits, still lifes, and audibly dense video, Slaying, consider sequential, cyclical, and repetitive nature.
Masood Kamandy's The Effect of Lightning on a Rainbow

Alum Masood Kamandy (2004) featured on ARTFIXdaily for the experimental iPhone camera software he developed, as well as his upcoming exhibition at Luis De Jesus Los Angeles. The Effect of Lightning on a Rainbow opens on February 18 and runs through March 25, 2017.
Draft 14 Call For Submissions

Open to all current New York State college and university students, Draft 14 is an RIT student run publication and exhibition. The deadline to submit is February 17, 2017.
Submissions Now Open For International Fine Art Photography Awards  

Hosted by the Paris College of Art, the International Fine Art Photography Awards has added two new awards this year and will be juried by Anne Cartier-Bresson and Steve Bisson. Final deadline is March 31, 2017. 
BFA Photography and Video Introduces New Website

The new BFA Photography and Video website features information about department events and exhibitions, our facilities, and the curriculum.
 
Europe's Migrant Trail Documented Through Instagrams of Refugees in the New Yorker
 
Photographer Tomas van Houtryveof combines video footage of sites along the migrant trail in Europe with overlaid with screenshots of images posted by refugees on Instagram from those same sites.
CNN Style: The Power of Photography

Derek Blasberg, contributor for DEAR DAVE, Magazine's upcoming 25th issue, talks to experts on both sides of the lens, from leading fashion and portrait photographers to an avid celebrity collector. 
Thomas Albdorf's General View at Deli Gallery

In his second solo exhibit at Deli Gallery, Thomas Albdorf focuses on the intersection area between photography and sculpture and their shifting perceptions.
Empathy and Photography

Magnum Photos explores the central role of empathy in documentary photography, pulling excerpts from their recent event at the Barbican Centre with photographer Olivia Arthur, Save the Children’s director of creative content Jess Crombie, and writer & photographer Colin Pantall.
