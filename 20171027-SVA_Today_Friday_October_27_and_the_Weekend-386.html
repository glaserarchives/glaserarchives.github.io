News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Friday, October 27, and the Weekend

Tomorrow: An Evening with Floyd Norman

SVA presents a screening of Floyd Norman: An Animated Life, a documentary about award-winning animator, writer and comic book artist Floyd Norman. Afterwards there will be a Q&A with Norman, hosted by BFA Animation faculty members Howard Beckerman and Mario Menjivar. Tomorrow, October 28, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
EVENTS

9:00am | Last Chance: Yearbook Photos

Today is the last day that SVA Yearbook is shooting portraits for the 2018 edition. All graduating students are encouraged to dress how they like, stop by and have their photo taken. The yearbook is given to students at commencement for free. 9:00am – 3:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, ground floor.

3:15pm | Yoga and Meditation for Wellness and Creativity

Student Health and Counseling Services presents this workshop on basic yoga and meditation skills to promote well-being and stress reduction. Yoga mats will be provided. Participants will receive free passes to Laughing Lotus YogaStudio. Open to SVA students only. RSVP to Navah Steiner at nsteiner@sva.edu. 3:15 – 4:45pm; please arrive at 3:00pm. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:00pm | John Hartmann Opening Reception

SVA's Visible Futures Lab presents the opening reception for architect and artist John Hartmann, where he will discuss his work at the lab and exhibit new pieces. Refreshments will be served. 6:00 – 9:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.
PRESS PICK

An Accomplished Art Director

Rogue interviews the art director of Bloomberg Markets Josef Reyes (BFA 2005 Graphic Design) about the state of design today, the battle between print and digital and how SVA and his early work shaped him.
NYC PICKS

Magnum Photos Inside Out

A day-long forum on how Magnum photographers have shaped visual narratives within a fractured and uncertain world, featuring MFA Art Writing Chair David Levi Strauss. Free; RSVP here. Today, 10:15am – 5:35pm. The Roosevelt House Public Policy Institute at Hunter College, 47-49 East 65th Street.

Boo at the Zoo

A special Halloween event at the Bronx Zoo, featuring a hayride, haunted forest, nature trek, and more. Zoo admission $26.95 – $36.95; purchase here. Through Sunday, October 29. Bronx Zoo, 2300 Southern Boulevard, Bronx.

Halloween at the Museum

Trick-or-treating, arts and crafts, fun with roaming cartoon characters and live performances at the Museum of Natural History. Tickets $12 – $15; purchase here. Tomorrow, October 28, 2:00 – 6:00pm. American Museum of Natural History, Central Park West at 79th Street.
TAKE NOTE

SVAlert Test

Today at approximately 1:00pm, there will be a test of SVAlert, the College's electronic notification system. All students, faculty and staff will receive messages via selected contact options. When you receive the test message, you do not need to take any action.
