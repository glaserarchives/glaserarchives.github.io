In this month: another school year concluded, another cohort of social design leaders out in the world to put their learning into practice. The class of 2017 is doing research for their thesis projects, the new class of 2018 is waiting in the wings. Summer is the time to reflect, and prepare for the future.

The Story of a Class with Big Ambition and Broad Reach
In their Communication Design class, students choose among a group of clients, then work with them for fifteen weeks, learning and using their communication skills to create new programs and products. In this photo, a group of Puerto Rican students helped test an idea for Basura Cero. From left are James, Carlos, Steven, Nicolas, and Jose.
Many wise people have said that all change begins with language. In other words, new ideas can’t be manifest until we put them into words. It’s how we share, how we expand and extend understanding and creativity.

We agree, and believe that language is one of the essential tools of social change. We also believe that the vast majority of problems facing business and humans and the planet can be solved with communication. Even wars, if people would just sit down and listen to each other at the appropriate moment. Communication is the basis of all relationships, and good communication is the way strong relationships are maintained. But it takes skill and deep human understanding and craft.

You Can Come Home Again: Alums Return for a Special Lecture Night
DSI Alumni (l-r): Sebastian Barerra, Josh Treuhaft, Rhea Rakshit, Pragya Mishra, Akshata Malhotra.
Alumni Pragya Mishra, Josh Treuhaft, Rhea Rakshit, Sebastian Barrera and Akshata Malhotra returned to DSI last month for an exchange of knowledge and insights at the DSI Global Guest Lecture. They spoke to both current cohorts about their jobs, their journey, their passions, the wicked problems they’re working to solve, and how what they learned at DSI is helping them succeed.

Get Funded to Design a Better World
We have a special love for the Sappi Ideas That Matter program because it was originally created by DSI Chair Cheryl Heller. For the past 17 years, Ideas that Matter has given great designers the funds they need to fuel great change, helping establish over 500 charitable programs — that’s more than $12.5 million toward improving lives and communities worldwide. Well, it’s a new year and we’re looking for fresh ideas!

Student and Faculty News
Rodrigo Muñoz (Class of ’16) has been appointed professor at Universidad San Francisco de Quito (USFQ) in Quito-Ecuador at the School of Communications and Contemporary Arts (COCOA). He will be teaching a variety of courses, including Fundamentals of Design and Software, Thesis and Research. His study of Communication Design at DSI will be the basis of his approach to solving social problems.

Bruno Silva (Class of ’16) will speak at the health conference Transform 16, hosted by the Mayo Clinic Center for Innovation, in Minnesota this September. Transform 16 aims to engage with the world’s top thinkers to boldly envision a sustainable future for health.

Amer Jandali (Class of ’16) is working with trends forecasting consultancy Now Plus One to inspire companies in the way they think about their futures and stir conversations about sustainability, and with sustainability strategist Leyla Acaroglu on design research to create systems and products that ease strain on the planet.

Chair Cheryl Heller spoke at AIGA Connecticut in May, as part of their Creative Impact Series, in a discussion about designing a life (and career) with purpose.

Faculty member Lee Sean Huang’s work with the Awesome Foundation was recently profiled on CCTV America’s “Full Frame” show. The full interview can be viewed here.

Faculty member Despina Papadopoulos, founder of Principled Design / Studio 5050, a systems design and strategy studio specializing in wearable technologies, and who has been named one of New York City’s “Six Most Interesting Women in Tech,” gave a recent presentation at TEDx Vilnius – watch it here.

Faculty member Archie Lee Coates IV, Executive Director of + POOL, joined a panel at the Waterfront Alliance Conference discussing the current state of water quality.

Join faculty members Marc Rettig and Hannah du Plessis, who are organizing their second Frontier Retreat in Montana this coming July.

Faculty member Schuyler Brown’s essay, “The World in a State of Extreme Transition: Moving from Sustainability to Regenerative Design,” was published on Uplift in May. Read it here.

Faculty member Lina Srivastava spoke as part of the breakout panel called ‘At the End of Every Data Point is a Human Being: Technology for the Global Refugee Crisis’ at Civic Hall’s Personal Democracy Forum 2016, held June 9-10 in NYC. This week she will be part of the discussion panel ‘The Emerging World of Virtual Reality and Human Rights’ at the Human Rights Watch Film Festival on June 15.
