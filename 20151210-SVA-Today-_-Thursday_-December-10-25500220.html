SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, December 10, 2015
"Make it Here": The New SVA Subway Posters
BFA Design faculty members Gail Anderson and Joe Newton collaborated on the latest installment of posters for SVA's subway campaign. The posters begin appearing in stations across the city this week. SVA Close Up spoke with Anderson, who is also director of design and digital media at the Visual Arts Press, about the series and her designs.
Alumnus Wins Jury Award from Directors Guild
Alcee Walker (MFA 2014 Social Documentary Film and MPS 2015 Directing) recently won the Directors Guild of America Student Film Awards' Jury Award for his film Inferno, which he developed as his MPS Directing thesis project. SVA Close Up spoke with Walker about making the film and his experience at SVA. The awards ceremony takes place tonight at DGA New York.
Noon: MFA Computer Art Open Studios
MFA Computer Art presents students showing recent work in their studios. Participating artists include Yuxi Cao, Wengu Hu, Nakyung Lee and Yunling Song. Studios are open from noon - 7:00pm today and tomorrow. Reception tonight, 5:00 - 7:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor.
6:00pm: MFA Social Documentary Film Information Session
MFA Social Documentary Film presents an information session and department tour for prospective students, featuring students, faculty and department chair Maro Chermayeff. 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
6:30pm: The MFA Question
Emerging Leaders of New York Arts (ELNYA) presents a panel discussion and information session about the pros and cons of MFA degrees and how to find the right program. Speakers include SVA Provost Jeff Nesin and ELNYA fellow Elizabeth Lee. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
Throwback Thursday with Animorphs
Vice interviews SVA faculty member David B. Mattingly about his cover illustrations for the '90s children's book series Animorphs.
NYC pick of the day
Cocoa in the City: NYC Chocolate Makers
Historical gastronomist Sarah Lohman discusses the history and production of chocolate treats, from bean to bar. Tickets are $12, tasting included. 7:00pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.
Take Note
Off the Wall: Exploring the Intersection of Design and Graffiti
MFA Products of Design presents students of faculty member Lawrence Abrahamson's Design Research course discussing their semester-long investigation of street art and graffiti in New York City. Friday, December 18, 7:00pm, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
SVA in Los Angeles Information Session
BFA Animation and BFA Film present an information session about the SVA in Los Angeles trip from March 7 - 11, 2016. The session will go over the trip's itinerary, which includes studio visits to Bad Robot and Disney Animation and a chance to meet leading filmmakers and production artists. Thursday, December 17, 1:00 - 1:30pm, 209 East 23rd Street, Room 310.
