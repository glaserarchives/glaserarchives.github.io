TASHI'S TURBINE on PBS

Premiering on PBS World this Sunday, November 13, 2016, Tashi’s Turbine is an uplifting tale of a small village’s attempt to harness renewable, sustainable energy -- the power of the wind. Directed by Amitabh Joshi, and produced by Erik Spink, both current faculty and SocDoc alumni (2013). The film airs at 10pm ET via Facebook Live Stream and simultaneously on the network (in NYC on WLIW/21) - a first for PBS World. There will also be a Q&A with Amitabh and the film's subject, Tashi via Facebook Live. Read more...

Soundbreaking: PBS November 14-23

Soundbreaking: Stories from the Cutting Edge of Music by award-winning director and SocDoc chair Maro Chermeyeff and co-director Jeff Dupre airs on PBS weekdays November 14-23 10/9c. Soundbreaking explores the nexus of cutting-edge technology and human artistry that has created the soundtrack of our lives. The series also features the work of SocDoc faculty Amy Schewel (producer), E. Donna Shepherd (editor), Samuel D. Pollard (producer & editor), Tom Hurwitz (cinematographer) and Axel Baumann (cinematographer). See the complete schedule.

Michael Apted: Craft and Career

Earlier this Fall, living legend Michael Apted joined us in the theater to discuss lessons in life and the craft of documentary. Apted is a British filmmaker best known in the documentary world for his internationally acclaimed, multi-award winning sequels based on the original Seven Up! documentary: 7 Plus Seven, 21 Up, 28 Up, 35 Up, 42 Up, 49 Up and most recently 56 Up, which aired on ITV in the United Kingdom & PBS in the US, and was released theatrically in the United States to much acclaim.  Watch the full discussion!

Vacant Light Wins Paley DocPitch

Erik Spink (SocDoc faculty and 2013 alumnus) wins the Paley DocPitch Competition: Be in the Room! with his Hart Island: How The Other Half Dies. This competition offers a $5,000 grant from A&E IndieFilms for an unfinished or work-in-progress documentary from an emerging filmmaker. Congrats, Erik and the Vacant Light team!
