November Events and Exhibitions

Lectures, timely political discussions and exhibitions—including a new BFA Photography and Video exhibition featuring work by students and alumni—fill out the month’s calendar.

EVENTS

12:00pm | Coffee Chat With Career Development

Join SVA Career Development for a midday coffee break and learn what they have to offer during your studies at SVA. Ask questions, connect with staff and learn how to best utilize one-on-one career counseling appointments* and virtual office hours. Coffee will be provided. Topics include: Career Development’s services and resources, SVA’s Internship-for-Credit Program and career-related events and workshops. Today, November 2, 12:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.


*Freshmen and first-year undergraduate and graduate students must attend this workshop before booking a career counseling appointment.

12:00pm | Día de Muertos

SVA Continuing Education presents a celebration of Día de Muertos (Day of the Dead), a Mexican tradition of pre-Hispanic origin that celebrates the memory and presence of the deceased. Today, November 2, 12:00 – 4:00pm. Flatiron North Plaza, East 23rd Street and Broadway.

7:00pm | MA Curatorial Practice Distinguished Global Curator Lecture: Kate Fowle

MA Curatorial Practice presents curator Kate Fowle, who will discuss what it means to be “artist-centered.” Tonight, November 2, 7:00 – 8:30pm, online.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Next Week | How Do I Find an Internship? Tips for Planning a Successful Internship Search

Designed specifically for students planning to intern while at SVA, this interactive workshop will provide an overview of how to search for internships and what materials can assist you. Topics will go beyond writing a resume and cover letter: learn how to determine what kind of internship you want, create a search plan and a strong application, apply and follow-up appropriately, use virtual tools, build your network and more. Come with your questions, resumes and favorite note-taking method. A helpful resources packet will be provided to attendees. Wednesday, November 9, 12:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Next Week | MFA Visual Narrative Virtual Info Session

MFA Visual Narrative presents a virtual information session with Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay), chair of MFA Visual Narrative. Wednesday, November 9, 6:30 – 8:00pm, online.

Artivism: A Few of My Long-Term Feminist Art Projects (1978 – 2022)

A virtual discussion with artist Mónica Mayer about her feminist art projects as part of Artivism: The Power of Art for Social Transformation, an Adelphi University event series co-produced by Humanities and Sciences faculty member Argie Moutafis-Agelarakis. Registration required; RSVP here. Monday, November 14, 4:30pm, online.

#ThxBirthControl Day

Wednesday, November 16, is #ThxBirthControl Day! Does having accessible and affordable birth control impact your life? Do you want everyone to access the birth control that’s right for them? Join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services in celebrating ongoing access to birth control for all bodies who choose it and share why birth control is important to you here. Nurse Abby will share your responses via social media on Wednesday, November 16.
