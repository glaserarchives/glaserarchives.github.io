Let's Make 2018 Great!

This year may have already kicked off with a bomb cyclone and blizzard-level conditions, but SVA department chairs still have high hopes and strong intentions for 2018 from here on out.

Top Portfolios of 2017

This roundup of the most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios for each month of 2017 includes intriguing illustrations, portrait photographs and short animations.

NYC Winter Exhibition Roundup

A list of exhibitions across New York City that will allow you to explore the five boroughs and help spark your creativity in this new year.

Tim Rollins with Kids of Survival (KOS) in 1988.

Remembering Tim Rollins

Art News on the legacy of MFA Art Practice faculty member and activist Tim Rollins (BFA 1977 Fine Arts), who passed away last month.

"A Year of Style, in Pictures"

The New York Times rounds up its favorite photos that it published throughout the year, including work by Peter Ash Lee (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography), Ryan Shorosky (BFA 2013 Photography) and Dina Litovsky (MFA 2010 Photography, Video and Related Media).

Don't-Miss Subway Posters

Untapped City's list of 13 NYC art exhibits not to miss includes SVA Acting Chairman of the Board Milton Glaser's latest series of posters, which he created for the College, adorning subway stations around the city.

Best Animations of 2017

The Playlist lists Dash Shaw's (BFA 2005 Illustration) feature film My Entire High School Sinking into the Sea as one of the best animated films of 2017.

Continuing Education Information Sessions

This week, attend an information session for SVA's Continuing Education programs, where you will learn about course offerings from faculty members and be able to ask questions. Programs include Advertising, Computer Art, Design, Film, Video and Animation, Fine Arts, Illustration and Cartooning, Interior Design, Photography, Visual Narrative and Visible Futures Lab. Click here for dates, times and locations.

Robyn O'Neil: Lecture and Screening

MFA Fine Arts presents a lecture by artist Robyn O’Neil and a screening of We, The Masses, a film that she wrote and art directed. Tuesday, January 9, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Jamel Shabazz

MPS Digital Photography presents Jamel Shabazz, an icon of social documentary and street photography in New York City, as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tuesday, January 9, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

The Long Friday: Screening and Discussion

Join MFA Design for Social Innovation for a screening of The Long Friday, a documentary-in-progress about the day 90% of women in Iceland walked off the job and out of their homes. This strike laid the foundation for Iceland's status today as the world's most gender-equal country. A conversation with the strike's leaders will follow thescreening. Tickets $12; purchase here. Wednesday, January 10, 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
