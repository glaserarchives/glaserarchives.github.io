SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts April 25 - May 1, 2016
Jason Bard Yarmosky, Wintered Fields (triptych), 2015
Jason Bard Yarmosky on His Loving, Offbeat Portraits of His Grandparents
From the spring 2016 issue of Visual Arts Journal: a look at SVA alumnus Yarmosky's portraits of his grandparents.
From Medical Marijuana to Sex: Five Innovative Design Projects
MFA Design class of 2016 students recently joined forces for Venture 16, a presentation of their thesis projects. A total of 19 innovative, market-ready projects were presented in all, ranging from a nifty new device for medical marijuana patients, to a platform that aims to redefine sex education.
Steven Heller Receives 2016 Carle Honor
MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller was named one of four 2016 Carle Honorees, who are recognized for their "instrumental role in making children’s books a vibrant and influential art and literary form in America."
IN THE PRESS
A Steven Universe Book
The Los Angeles Times reports that Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation) has written a children's book based on her Cartoon Network series.
Collaborating for a Cause
Artsy interviews MFA Fine Arts faculty member Marilyn Minter about her collaboration with Miley Cyrus for Planned Parenthood.
Live Drawing on Instagram
Adweek highlights a collaboration between Jonathan Bartlett (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay) and Persol Sunglasses, for which the artist is illustrating a user-generated dystopian graphic novel live on Instagram.
EVENTS

MPS Digital Photography presents curator, writer, artist and educator Deborah Willis, one of the nation’s leading historians of African American photography and culture, discussing her work. Tuesday, April 26, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F. This event will be streamed live beginning at 6:50pm.
MFA Design for Social Innovation Thesis Presentations
Graduating students discuss their thesis projects. Thursday, April 28, noon - 5:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MFA Fine Arts Open Studios
MFA Fine Arts presents students showing recent work in their studios. Friday, April 29, 5:00 - 9:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.
