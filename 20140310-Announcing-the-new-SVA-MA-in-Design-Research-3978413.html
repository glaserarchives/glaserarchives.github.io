Dear Beth,

I’m delighted to announce that we are now accepting applications for the new one-year SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism for Fall 2014. I’m also happy to let you know that significant scholarship support is available to all successful candidates.

Combining the rigor of its predecessor, D-Crit, and the velocity of our annual Design Writing & Research Summer Intensive, the SVA MA in Design Research offers a high-impact, targeted program of study well suited to the circumstances of established professionals, in addition to graduates wishing to continue their studies at an advanced level. With a two-semester, eight-month timeframe (September to May), the SVA MA in Design Research provides an intensive immersion in techniques for researching and interpreting design, its contexts and consequences. 

The program’s curriculum charts the cutting edge of design practice and is responsive to exciting developments in the media landscape. Through studying images, objects, and environments, and learning ways to construct multi-format narratives that bring them to life, students will be amply prepared to launch or develop research-related careers in publishing, education, museums, institutes, design practice, entrepreneurship, and more.

Although many of the features that have defined the D-Crit program will still be a part of the MA Design Research curriculum, I’m excited about the opportunity to introduce new faculty members, courses, research tools, and media workshops. I invite you to join us for an Open House/ Information Session on Saturday, March 22, so that I can share with you these plans, and discuss your individual goals for graduate study in design research. I’ve also invited some D-Crit alumni to tell us about the ways in which they implement critical research methods in their working practices at institutions and companies such as MoMA, Cooper Hewitt Design Museum, Project Projects, Facebook, Domus, ArchDaily, Metropolis, Rizzoli, Pratt, and RISD.

I look forward to discussing the evolved program in more detail with you. You can register for the Open House, or simply email me to arrange a phone or in-person appointment.

Warm wishes from,
Alice Twemlow
Founding Chair, SVA MA Design Research
