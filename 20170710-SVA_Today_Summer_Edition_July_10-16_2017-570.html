SVA Today
Monday, July 10 - Sunday, July 16, 2017
FEATURES

SVA's Fulbright Semi-Finalist

MFA Visual Narrative student Rosa Chang was selected as a semi-finalist for the Fulbright-National Geographic Digital Storytelling Fellowship, a highly competitive, fully-funded fellowship program.

Exhibiting in Iceland

A Q&A with BFA Visual and Critical Studies faculty member Emily Weiner (MFA 2011 Fine Arts) about her current exhibition in Iceland with Ragnheiður Gestsdóttir and Theresa Himmer (MFA 2011 Fine Arts), who all met while in the MFA Fine Arts program at SVA.
THIS WEEK

Tuesday, July 11

MPS Branding Thesis: Repositioning the American Dream
The MPS Branding class of 2017 will present the repositioning of the American Dream through the lenses of capitalism, immigration, technology, commerce and the pursuit of happiness. 11:00am – 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Andrea Fraser
MFA Art Practice presents a lecture by performance and video artist Andrea Fraser. 12:30 – 2:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Wednesday, July 12

The Artist as Activist
MFA Art Practice presents a panel discussion on the role that art can play in effecting social and political change. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Speed Critique
SVA Continuing Education presents a forum where you can pitch your work to various critics in three-minute intervals. RSVP to ce@sva.edu. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 602C.

The Origins of Stories
MFA Visual Narrative presents a talk by game designer Matthew Weise. 6:30 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Thursday, July 13

"Astral America" Reception
SVA Continuing Education and SVA Summer Residency Programs present an exhibition that addresses how the concepts of hyperreality and ethereality manifest in the contemporary notion of "America." 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Monday, July 31. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

MFA Visual Narrative Studio Tour and Information Session
Meet current MFA Visual Narrative students and program chair Nathan Fox for a tour of the department's studio space and an informative and interactive presentation. RSVP here. 6:00 – 7:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

"Murmurs from the Planet Earth" Reception
An exhibition of works by SVA students that offer both abstract and literal representations of the natural world, exploring earthly habitats through drawing, painting, photography, video and installation. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Tuesday, August 8. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

"Quality Time" Reception
MFA Art Practice presents an exhibition of student work that explores the concept of "quality time." 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Tuesday, August 8. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Saturday, July 15

War for the Planet of the Apes and Spider-Man: Homecoming
The Visual Effects Society presents screenings of War for the Planet of the Apes at 3:00pm, followed by a simulcast Q&A with the visual effects artists, and a screening of Spider-Man: Homecoming at 8:00pm. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
