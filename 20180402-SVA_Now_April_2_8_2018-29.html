News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
April 2 – 8, 2018
News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Illustrating the "Mythology" of Game of Thrones

A Q&A with Craig Coss (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative), illustrator of the recently released Game of Thrones tarot cards, about how he became involved in the project, the challenges he faced and more.

"Candid Yet Thoughtful, Frank Yet Deliberate"

Multidisciplinary, feminist-focused artist AnnaLiisa Ariosa-Benston (MFA 2016 Fine Arts) discusses her methods and art practice intentions.

March Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects from SVA Portfolios this month include an animated landscape, intriguing illustrations and motion graphic videos.

Dining by Design

BFA Interior Design students took part in the exhibition event Dining by Design, where they transformed a raw space into an immersive dining exhibit to raise awareness and funds for HIV/AIDS research, education, treatment and advocacy.
IN THE PRESS

"Embodying a Fluid Reality"

The New York Times Style Magazine debuts Michael Bailey-Gates' (BFA 2015 Photography) new photo series "Horse in the Rough."

"You and Your Private Journey"

Vice talks with painter Inka Essenhigh (MFA 1994 Fine Arts), whose work is currently on view at the Virginia Museum of Contemporary Art and will also be in two different New York galleries this month.

Looking at Outside In

The New York Times reviews Lynn Shelton's (MFA 1995 Photography and Related Media) film Outside In, which hit theaters last week.

A Call for Digital Protection

MFA Interaction Design faculty member Paul Ford on how it is time for a digital protection agency, for Bloomberg Businessweek.
EVENTS

Mindful Joint 2018: Contemporary Korean Art in Conversations

BFA Fine Arts presents this two-day symposium that brings together artists, writers and curators from South Korea and the U.S. to break the generalized perception of Korea and understand the complexities of artists’ practices and cultural backgrounds. Thursday, April 5, 6:30pm, and Friday, April 6, 1:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, various floors.

Lynne Tillman: Men and Apparitions

MFA Art Writing presents cultural critic and visiting professor Lynne Tillman, who will read from her new novel Men and Apparitions and discuss it with artist Adam Pendleton. Tonight, April 2, 6:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

First Offerings: A Selection of First Year Paintings

A selection of works by painting students from BFA Cartooning, BFA Design, BFA Fine Art and BFA Illustration who are enrolled in the SVA Honors Program, curated by BFA Fine Arts faculty member Leigh Behnke and student Stella Song. Through Friday, April 13. Reception: Thursday, April 5, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Netflix's Amateur

SVA Theatre presents an advance screening of Netflix’s original film Amateur, written and directed by Ryan Koo and produced by BFA Film faculty member Chip Hourihan. A Q&A with select cast and crew will follow. Thursday, April 5, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
