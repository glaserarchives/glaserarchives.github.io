D-Crit Spring News
D-Crit is springing!

It's a busy time of year at SVA MFA Design Criticism. As the second-years prepare to spread their wings after their graduating event, members of the Class of 2015 are finishing up end-of-semester projects and embarking on their own thesis research journeys. This week guest critic Rob Giampietro, partner of Project Projects, reviewed the students' proposed thesis topics: the changing status and implications of the design fake; the politics of preservation of mid-century American housing programs; the mutating contemporary arts magazine; and the twenty-first century museum's attempts to engage its publics.

Michael Bierut, Pentagram partner, was a guest critic for the second years' Applied Thesis projects, which ranged from a reality TV show to highlight the idiosyncrasies of micro-living, and a design fiction to expose the pseudo intellectualism of the urban surf aesthetic, to an activist intervention to draw attention to the dispersed nature of fashion exhibiting beyond the museum.

"Studio 360" host Kurt Andersen and "This American Life" writer Starlee Kine came in to listen to the first years' radio podcasts, which included a piece on how the blind experience art in museums and a takedown of a Ninja-themed Japanese restaurant.

We also reflected on the first years' dynamite lecture series, which featured: WNYC's "Radiolab" co-host Robert Krulwich; author, designer, and educator Ellen Lupton; ARTBOOK / D.A.P. publishing group president Sharon Helgason Gallagher; and Works That Work founding publisher Peter Bil'ak. If you missed these lectures and students' stellar introductions, you can watch videos of all of our past lectures online. 
 
Alumni of the department continue to help shape the future of design research, education, publishing, curation, and practice. Last year's class of 2013 have scored jobs as: senior editor at Michael Sorkin Studios; managing editor at PIN–UP magazine; curatorial assistant at Cooper Hewitt National Design Museum; and staff writer for Architizer, among other positions, and are busy writing for publications including Surface Magazine, Disegno, The Los Angeles Review of Books, Core77, Designers & Books, and L’ArcoBaleno.

Among other exciting graduate career news: William Myers is the jury chairman for a new Bio Art + Design Award, which awarded €75,000 to artists and designers this past February. Superscript, an editorial and research consultancy, directed by Molly Heintz, Avinash Rajagopal, Aileen Kwun, and Vera Sacchetti, will be presenting a project at the 2014 Venice Architecture Biennale. And Julia Van den Hout's journal CLOG is launching its tenth issue on Prisons, and the publication will also be represented at the 2014 Venice Biennale. Go grads!

The 2014 D-Crit Conference drew our most international crowd yet, with speakers and viewers attending and watching from Hong Kong, Estonia, New Zealand, and Kuwait.

Last Friday we staged the fifth annual SVA MFA Design Criticism graduating event, this year titled Lingua Franca. Moderated by British architecture critic and curator Justin McGuirk and launched by a keynote from novelist and critic Nicholson Baker, the conference featured the D-Crit Class of 2014, who presented their thesis research alongside guest speakers: curator of Design and Architecture at Hong Kong’s new museum M+ Aric Chen; material anthropologist Emily Stokes-Rees; and creator of the MIT Press Mediawork project Peter Lunenfeld.

With nearly 500 registered attendees, and another 100 watching online, "Lingua Franca" drew our most international crowd to date. Thanks to all of you for your support, and especially the D-Crit alumni who returned to the coop for the event, and to the families of our graduating students. Thanks also to our partners: MailChimp, Las Delicias Patisserie, La Colombe Coffee, and Vitra; conference identity designer Matthew Rezac; website designer Eric Price; photographer Michael George; and everyone at the SVA Theatre.
