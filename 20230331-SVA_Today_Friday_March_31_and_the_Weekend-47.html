Friday, March 31, and the Weekend

Carving Out Space

SVA faculty member Bon Duke (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography; BFA 2009 Photography) photographs Shuko Nakamura—a woman artisan taking on the male-dominated theatrical craft of traditional Japanese Noh masks—for T: The New York Times Style Magazine.

EVENTS

12:00pm | The Bound Book: A Three-Day Bookbinding Workshop

Learn several bookbinding techniques with artist Althea James at SVA Library West. Bring your own projects or use the provided array of fun, eccentric supplies. Today’s workshop is a free, experimental day. Today, March 31, 12:00 – 2:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

5:00pm | VASA’s Spring Market

The spring market is here—purchase art from fellow SVA students who diligently prepared posters, apparel, prints, zines, crochet, crafts, photography and more! Tonight, March 31, 5:00 – 8:00pm. 216 East 23rd Street.

8:00pm | Titmouse Animation 5-Second Night

SVA Theatre presents this annual event, for which Titmouse animators create and screen whatever goodies have been floating around their minds all year. $5 tickets available for SVA students and staff; purchase here. Tonight, March 31, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tomorrow | Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Dungeons & Dragons: Honor Among Thieves. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Saturday, April 1, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sunday | Embodied Design Collaboratory

MFA Design for Social Innovation brings together embodied researcher-practitioners and design creatives to discuss body-informed frameworks and methods within the field of social innovation. Sunday, April 2, 10:00am – 3:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Sunday | A Thousand and One Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of the Sundance Film Festival award-winning film A Thousand and One. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, April 2, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

A New Space for Emerging Artists

Incubator, a dynamic platform for emerging artists, is set to launch a permanent gallery and project space in London with a series of solo exhibitions, including one from Kesewa Aboah (BFA 2017 Fine Arts). Via Fad.

NYC PICKS

MoCCA Arts Festival

Manhattan’s largest independent comics and cartoon festival returns, featuring over 40 SVA alumni as well as demos by MFA Visual Narrative and RisoLAB at tables 112 through 117. Discounted tickets of $12 are available with an undergraduate student ID; reserve your tickets here. Saturday, April 1, 11:00am – 7:00pm, and Sunday, April 2, 11:00am – 6:00pm. Metropolitan Pavilion, 125 West 18th Street, with programming at SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133 West 21st Street.

Shine On: A Concert for Trans Day of Visibility

A series of performances by The New York City Gay Men’s Chorus and trailblazing actress and singer Peppermint in celebration of International Transgender Day of Visibility, hosted by comedian Murray Hill (MFA 1997 Photography and Related Media). Tonight, March 31, 7:00 – 8:30pm. Cooper Union, The Foundation Building, 7 East 7th Street.

Trans Art Bazaar

Shop clothes, jewelry, food, art and more at New York City’s biggest trans and nonbinary artist market featuring 80+ vendors from around the country. Free. Sunday, April 2, 1:00 – 7:00pm. House of YES, 2 Wyckoff Avenue, Brooklyn.

Nonprofit advocacy organization Right to Be hosts this free interactive training session that will give you a clear, adaptable and expert-approved set of tools to help you intervene safely and effectively to support trans equality. Today, March 31, 2:00 – 3:00pm, online.

Visual Opinion Is Hiring

Visual Opinion is seeking a managing editor and interns for the fall 2023 and spring 2024 semesters. To apply, email your resume, statement of interest and portfolio or portfolio sample to vo@sva.edu. Applications close Monday, April 3, 11:59pm. Questions? Email vo@sva.edu.
