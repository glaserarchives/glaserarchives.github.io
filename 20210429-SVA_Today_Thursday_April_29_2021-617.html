Events

10:00am | 31 Days of Oscar with TCM and Student Affairs
Join Student Affairs for special movie viewings of Turner Classic Movies: 31 Days of Oscar. Enjoy classic films and meet Student Affairs staff who can help answer questions about resources for SVA students. Join the last screening on Zoom and wind down for the end of spring 2021!

Today, 10:00am – 12:00pm ET: The Wizard of Oz (1939)

10:00am and 4:00pm | Career Development Virtual Office Hours
Join SVA Career Development's weekly virtual office hours today, 10:00 – 11:00am and 4:00 – 5:00pm ET. During both one-hour sessions, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Alternatively, book a private career-counseling session here.

6:00pm | MFA Fine Arts Spring 2021 Open Studios
Join MFA Fine Arts for its spring open studios, where each graduating student will show their end-of-year artwork online! Tonight, 6:00 – 9:00pm ET, find out what the current MFA Fine Arts candidates have been creating in their studios this year and connect with the artists one-on-one through a unique web experience. Preview the work and find all the details for tonight's event at svamfafineartsopenstudios.com.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Milton Glaser's Phone Book
In her latest "The Glaser Nobody Knows" column for Print, SVA's Head Archivist Beth Kleber discusses the Manhattan yellow-pages phone book that the late designer and SVA faculty member Milton Glaser designed in 1971.
SOCIALLY DISTANCED PICKS

Community Connection: Recent Incidents of Racially Motivated Bias, Violence and Hate
Join ACUHO-I and NADOHE for a moderated discussion to process the recent events of racially motivated violence and gather insights and approaches to help us move forward and better support the Asian American community. Attendees will have the opportunity to make comments and ask questions in real time. Today, 3:00pm ET. Register here to join on Zoom.

Behind the Curtain: Making Career Pathways Transparent
The roadmap to a career in media can be particularly challenging—especially for those from underrepresented communities. Hear from people who figured it out as they went along—including BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects faculty member and alumnus Ellen Su—and learn about training programs that expand access. Free. Today, 5:00pm ET. Register here.

This event is part of the New York International Children's Film Festival Industry Forum 2021, which will host local, national and international creative and cultural practitioners for frank and insightful conversations and help shape the future of kids' storytelling. Join for free online every Thursday through May 27. Click here for more info.

Next Week | Faculty Book Club
For the next book club meeting, the group will discuss The Fire Next Time by James Baldwin, which is available for free through the SVA Library. Join the Zoom meeting led by MA/MAT Art Education chair Catherine Rosamond, Wednesday, May 5, 4:30 – 5:30pm ET. All faculty are invited to join us on Canvas for more readings and discussion, all of which can be done at your own pace.

Next Week | The 100: Night of 100 Drawings
Visual artist and longtime SVA faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts) will host this free workshop—a nonstop marathon of 100 fast-paced figure drawings from life, complemented with exercises where participants draw from imagination and observation. Presented by SVA Continuing Education, BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the Honors Program. Thursday, May 6, 6:30pm ET. Register here to get a Zoom link.
