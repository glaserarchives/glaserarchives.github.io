Dear Friend

Season’s Greetings!

With this last message in 2016, I want to reach out to you about what I can only call the new state of curating. By this I mean that the political reality now before us must change what we do and how we engage our various publics, wherever we are. The social landscape is now under drastic revision, and to curate, from the Latin curare on which our English verb is based, meaning to care, is refreshed with the urgency of the situation. To care as a curator now signifies that we address the ethics of what it means for each and all of us to be together in a civil society. To care as a curator now means to think of our programming, our publications, our institutions as opportunities and places to confront this dire possibility that our citizenship and rights are under threat, and we must contest those threats. To be a curator now is to turn our creativity in our work with artists and thinkers to the social, political, economic, and environmental challenges to be faced and faced down, to be lifted up and enlightened and deepened. The citizen's voice of the curator has never been more crucial.
 
Those are our goals here at SVA’s MA Curatorial Practice program. I’m tremendously proud of the work we’ve done this year. We’ve had remarkable faculty and guests. Our population of students represents cultures from Africa to Asia to Europe, Latin America, and North America. In the next semester, we will bring together speakers from Johannesburg, Beirut, Barbados, from Washington, California, and New York. I am wishing you well, and I call out to you to join us in what we do intellectually and in our daily practices as professionals and citizens. Best wishes for 2017.
Steven Henry Madoff
Chair
