THIS WEEK
Tuesday: Dreaming Public Art
Madison Square Park Conservancy and SVA present a symposium featuring a keynote conversation with artist Martin Puryear. 7:30 - 10:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 W 23rd Street, reservation required.
Tuesday: Lydia Panas
MPS Digital Photography presents Lydia Panas (BFA 1984 Photography) discussing her work. Tuesday, May 24, 7:00 - 8:30pm, Room 418F, 136 West 21st Street
Thursday: Popular Culture Is Where the Pedagogy Is: Explorations of Provocation and Praxis
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work by graduating students. Curated by Jasmine Wahi. Open daily noon - 7:00pm, Thursday, May 26 - Sunday, June 5. Reception May 26, 6:00 - 9:00pm, The Hole, 312 Bowery.
Thursday: Facsimiles
An exhibition of work by SVA students selected by a jury of their peers. Organized by SVA Galleries. Thursday, May 26, through Saturday, June 11. Reception Thursday, June 2, 5:00pm - 7:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Sunday: X-Men: Apocalypse
The Visual Effects Society presents a free 3D screening of X-Men: Apocalypse, directed by Bryan Singer. Seating is limited and SVA IDs (or proof of enrollment) are required at the door. Continuing Education students are permitted. Each SVA community member may bring up to three guests. Reservations are not required, though all are encouraged to arrive early to secure their seats. Doors open approximately 30 – 45 minutes prior to the screening. Noon, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. For questions, email svatheatre@sva.edu.

NYCxDesign 2016: What We Saw, Loved, and Coveted
Curbed included "Parallel Times," a collection created by 20 second-year MFA Products of Design students, in their round-up of the best at NYCxDesign 2016.

All-Staff Art Show Submission Deadline June 1
SVA staff are invited to participate in the 4th annual All-Staff Art Show. Applications can be found at sva.edu/asas2016 and requires an image of the work, description and dimensions. All work should be no larger than 24" x 24".
New York Times Webinars, May 2016
The SVA Library subscribes to nytimes.com for all SVA students, faculty, and staff. Join The New York Times in Education on Thursday, May 26, to learn how to activate a nytimes.com account (11:00am), briefly tour nytimes.com (1:00pm) and tour the online teaching resources (2:00pm).
Walter Bernstein and The Front
The SVA Theatre and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences present a 40th anniversary screening of The Front (1976), starring Woody Allen and Zero Mostel. Screenwriter Walter Bernstein will speak with Michael Barker, co-president and co-founder of Sony Pictures Classics, about the years he spent on Hollywood's blacklist, which inspired the film. Tickets are $5. Tuesday, June 7, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
