SVA Today
Monday, April 30, 2018

April Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios this month include amusing advertisements, subtle drawings and the main title for Netflix's Altered Carbon.
EVENTS

6:30pm | Spotlight on Broadway

Center for Communication presents a panel about working on Broadway with the Tony-winning producer of Spring Awakening Tom Hulce, executive producer of SpongeBob SquarePants: The Broadway Musical Susan Vargo and Rick Miramontez, head of Broadway's O&M PR firm. Free with SVA ID and RSVP. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | A Celebration of the Life and Work of Tim Rollins

MFA Art Practice presents an evening celebrating the life of SVA alumnus and faculty member Tim Rollins (1955 – 2017), an inspiring and groundbreaking artist, educator and activist. The program will include contributions from artists, collaborators, art historians and friends of Tim. A reception will follow. RSVP here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Making the World More Beautiful

Juxtapoz on Inka Essenhigh's (MFA 1994 Fine Arts) solo show, open through Friday, May 25, at Miles McEnery Gallery in New York City.
NYC PICK

Between the Waters

This exhibition brings together artists from across the United States whose work responds to the precarious state of the environment through a personal lens. Free with SVA ID. Through July 2018. The Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
TAKE NOTE

Beast Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of the film Beast, starring Jessie Buckley and Johnny Flynn, with an introduction by the film's director Michael Pearce. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Tomorrow, May 1, 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Anything Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Anything, a new film starring John Carroll Lynch and Matt Bomer about a recently-widowed man who befriends a transgender woman. Lynch and director Timothy McNeil will introduce the film. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Wednesday, May 2, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

MFA Computer Arts Thesis Presentations

The MFA Computer Arts class of 2018 presents their thesis projects, featuring fine art, motion graphics and 3D animation work. The event will Livestream here. Tomorrow, May 1, and Wednesday, May 2, 7:00 – 10:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

MFA Products of Design Thesis Presentations

Join MFA Products of Design as its 17 graduating students present their yearlong thesis work, comprised of research, artifacts, services, experiences and platforms. RSVP here. Thursday, May 3, 11:00am – 5:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects Portfolio Night

BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects presents portfolio night, where guests can visit the program's facilities and see fourth-year students' work in 3D animation and visual effects. Thursday, May 3, 6:30 – 8:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, ground and 2nd floors.
