Dear Colleagues,

 

Last week, members within Black, Indigenous, and People of Color (BIPOC) communities across the country were tuned to hear the verdict in the trial for Derek Chauvin, the former Minnesota police officer who was indicted for his connection with George Floyd’s death.  When receiving the verdict of guilty on all three counts, there was a mixture of relief and sadness.  People were relieved that Chauvin was found guilty, but they knew that justice for the imbalanced number of deaths attributed to police activity and/or abuse is still being sought. During the trial and shortly after, three young people—Daunte Wright, 20, Adam Toledo, 13, and Ma’Khia Bryant, 16—lost their lives to encounters with law enforcement.  These cases, and similar narratives, have caused racial battle fatigue for many, including for SVA’s own faculty and staff that identify as BIPOC. In light of the pandemic and not having met in-person, an open conversation to discuss with our BIPOC peers across campus about how they have been coping is needed. 

 

On Friday, April 30, the DEI Office will be hosting a virtual forum from 12-1:30pm.  Interest for this forum has been demonstrated, and we would like to provide an opportunity for all of our BIPOC colleagues to participate. Please note the following:


  • For this discussion, we would like to provide a brave space for discussion and free expression for our faculty and staff that self-identify as Black, Indigenous, LatinX, and a Person of Color.  


  • As we begin to develop more inclusive campus-wide conversations and initiatives, there will be opportunities for allies to be involved and provide support.  Please respect the confidentiality and privacy of this initial forum.  

 

If you are interested in participating in the discussion, please register through this Zoom Link. Please note that we will not share your name with the institution; all names will be withheld within the DEI Director’s Office. You will have to voluntarily share your contact information with us if you would like to participate in the forum.


 

Thank you!

 

Jarvis M. Watson, Ed.D.
