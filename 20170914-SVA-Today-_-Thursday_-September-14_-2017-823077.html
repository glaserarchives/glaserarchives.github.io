SVA Today
Thursday, September 14, 2017

SVA's Guide to Chelsea

Following our guide to Gramercy, here are a few suggested places to visit, things to do and foods to eat in Chelsea. Stay tuned for more New York City neighborhood guides.
EVENTS

5:30pm | SVA Club Fair

Visual Arts Student Association (VASA) presents the club fair, where you can learn about all of the clubs offered at SVA, meet new people and discover ways you can get involved on campus. 5:30 – 7:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

6:00pm | "Paisley" Reception

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of objects, ephemera, photographs and textiles that have incorporated the paisley motif, as well as works by more than 40 contemporary artists interpreting it. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, October 7. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor

7:00pm | "Hey! You! Get Off of My Cloud, Part 1" Reception

MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that explores the impact of cloud computing on culture, economy and artistic practice, curated by program fellow Michele Thursz. 7:00 – 9:00pm. Through Wednesday, September 27. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

7:30pm | Doctor Strange

Continuing with After School Special, SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Doctor Strange (2016), followed by a Q&A with two of the film's visual effects artists, Jesse Flores (BFA 2010 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) and Paul Liaw (BFA 2002 Computer Art). Free. 7:30 – 10:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

8:00pm | Pitches, Previews and Performance

T.D. White and Loose Moon Productions presents a night of selected film pitches, short film screenings, including Last Licks and Will at Work, and performances. Free and open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Doors open at 7:30pm, event begins at 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Coach Honors Keith Haring

The Hollywood Reporter on the Keith Haring-inspired spring 2018 collection that luxury fashion brand Coach debuted at New York Fashion Week.
NYC PICKS

"I Fought the Law"

An exhibition of photos by Olivia Locher (BFA 2013 Photography) that feature enactments of eccentric laws in each of the 50 states. Through October 21. Reception tonight, 6:00pm. Steven Kasher Gallery, 515 West 26th Street.

Artist Studios Talk and Closing Reception

Museum of Arts and Design's artists from the summer session will present the work they created in the studios and illuminate perspectives that examine critical issues in contemporary craft, art and design. Free with museum admission. 6:30pm. Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.
TAKE NOTE

International Student Office Cultural Partner Program Information Session

Learn about the Cultural Partner Program and enjoy Vietnamese spring rolls. Open to all undergraduate students. Email cpp@sva.edu with any questions. Tomorrow, September 15, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Supergirl Documentary Screening

MFA Social Documentary Film presents a screening of Supergirl, a documentary about a preteen girl who broke a powerlifting world record, followed by a Q&A with the filmmakers, the subject and her family. Free. Thursday, September 28, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
