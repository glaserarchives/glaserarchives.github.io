Apply by April 1: SVA Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive

Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive
June 2-13, 2014
School of Visual Arts, New York City

Join us this summer in the School of Visual Arts MFA Design Criticism studio for a two-week intensive devoted to research and writing about design. Participants will be introduced to a range of techniques for constructing compelling narratives about images, objects, spaces, and infrastructure. You will experiment with different research methods, writing formats, and complete several projects across media, including a collaboratively produced publication. 

The priority deadline for this summer's SVA Design Writing and Research Intensive is April 1. Tuition is $2,250 for two packed weeks of instruction. 
Apply today, as spaces fill up fast! If you have any questions about the Intensive or your application, contact us at dcrit@sva.edu. 
 
Each participant in this richly programmed two-week Intensive will have a workstation in the light-filled D-Crit studio in New York’s Chelsea district, 24-hour access to department resources, and the opportunity to study closely with renowned writers and editors including Neil Donnelly, Steven Heller, Karrie Jacobs, Jennifer Kabat, Adam Harrison Levy, Robin Pogrebin, Alice Twemlow, Rob Walker, and Mimi Zeiger.
 
A robust daily schedule of seminars, lectures, workshops, and one-on-one consultations will be supplemented with visits to the city’s design collections, archives, libraries, design and architecture studios, as well as behind-the-scenes access to new exhibitions, buildings, and urban planning developments.

Among the design studios we will visit are: EAR Studio, Carla Diana Design,Randy Hunt/Etsy, Project No.8, Eight and a Half, NYC Department of Design & Construction, and SHoP Architects.
 
Rob Walker, technology and culture columnist for Yahoo News and Significant Objects author, will lead a project on developing narratives around overlooked and undervalued objects. Participants will engage in close observation, archive research, and other means of data gathering, and then experiment with strategies to illuminate an object’s significance through storytelling. 

Working in consultation with critic and The Weeklings co-founder Jennifer Kabat and BBC interviewer Adam Harrison Levy, participants will develop their interviewing techniques, prepare questions, and do background research, before visiting, interviewing, and profiling some of New York’s most prominent design studios in the fields of architecture, interaction design, graphic design, fashion, and product design. 
 
Guided by The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin, participants will be introduced to the principles of reviewing across genres and media, with a focus on the exhibition review. After initial exercises to hone writing skills and the development of a point of view and argument, and some reading exercises to examine exemplars of the form, participants will visit a selection of design exhibitions, and write their own reviews for critique.
 
For more details on projects and instructors see the Intensive website.
 
The SVA Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive welcomes applications from students and working professionals, designers and writers alike. If you care about design and language, and are interested in learning how to write meaningfully about products, graphics, urban infrastructure, and buildings with instructors and lecturers such as Karrie Jacobs, Michael Kimmelman, Mimi Zeiger, and Rob Walker, then apply to this two-week intensive today! 

"From the intensive project work to getting to see behind the scenes of New York's design firms and institutions, it was the enthusiasm and intelligence of my instructors and classmates that made the experience incredibly rewarding. I returned home inspired, with a notebook of ideas of new things that I want to write and projects that I want to try."
Leanne Prain, 2013 Intensive participant 

"The best part of the intensive was the clarity. It’s one thing to muddle your way through a dozen reviews or profiles on your own, but quite another to have a New York Times culture writer or a BBC interviewer outline exactly what you need to be doing, and how to improve."

"The intensive reinforced that I have a place in the architecture/design writing community, which has given me increased confidence and has been hugely helpful in figuring out my next steps."
