How to Fix American Democracy

The Washington Post asked writers and artists, including Visual Arts Press creative director Gail Anderson (BFA 1984 Media Arts), SVA's Acting Chairman of the Board Milton Glaser, SVA faculty member Jessica Hische and Nate Powell (BFA 2000 Cartooning), to propose one idea that could help fix the problems affecting American democracy.
NYC PICKS

BAMboo!

A free, family-friendly outdoor Halloween block party, featuring music, carnival games and a costume contest. 4:00pm. Brooklyn Academy of Music, 30 Lafayette Avenue, Brooklyn.

Village Halloween Parade

Dress up and join in this annual Halloween parade, featuring music, dancing, and more, or watch as it marches up Sixth Avenue. 7:00pm. Meet at Sixth Avenue and Canal Street; continues until 16th Street.

"Here Lie the Secrets of the Visitors of Green-Wood Cemetery"

A public art project by Sophie Calle that invites visitors to write down their secrets and place them in a grave designed by Calle, who will periodically collect the confessions and cremate them in a ceremonial bonfire. Free. Ongoing. Green-Wood Cemetery, 25th Street and Fifth Avenue, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE

Last Chance: Yearbook Make-Up Photo Shoot

SVA Yearbook is hosting one make-up photo shoot for the 2018 edition. All graduating students who have not already done so are encouraged to dress how they like, stop by and have their photo taken. The yearbook is given to students at commencement for free. Monday, November 6, 11:30am – 1:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, ground floor.

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism Open House

Hear from program faculty, students and alumni about the ways in which they engage with design research and writing, and how they put it into practice in the workplace. Tuesday, November 7. Doors open at 6:00pm; presentations begin at 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
