SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts July 25 - 31
FEATURES

Behind the Scenes of Vice Principals

Cinematographer Michael Simmonds (BFA 2000 Film and Video) discusses working on the new HBO series, starring Danny McBride and Walter Goggins.

Before and After SVA: Dana Davenport

The BFA Photography alumnus talks about creating socially charged work, balancing her work life and art life, what attracted her to New York City, and more.

John Irving on the Cover Design for In One Person

The author and Jackie Seow (BFA 1984 Media Arts), executive art director at Simon & Schuster, discuss the cover design for his 2012 novel.

The Beat Goes On

SVA recently announced "The Beat Goes On," an exhibition celebrating the intersection between art and music, with four distinct listening rooms by Elia Alba, Kevin Beasley, Paul D. Miller (DJ Spooky) and Tameka Norris (Meka Jean). Curated by visual artist Derrick Adams, "The Beat Goes On" opens at the SVA Chelsea Gallery on Saturday, August 20.

SVA Around the Globe

SVA students and recent alumni talk about their experiences studying abroad in Switzerland and Norway, as well as the SVA in Rome exchange program.
IN THE PRESS

Girls Night Out

Time highlights a series of photographs by Dana Litovsky (MFA 2010 Photography, Video and Related Media), which documents the women of the Meatpacking District's nightlife scene.

Matters of Life and Death

ARTnews on the reunion of Brian Whiteley (MFA 2013 Fine Arts) and his Legacy Stone, better known as the Donald Trump headstone, after it was confiscated by police in March.

The Horrors of Internment

The Los Angeles Times on One-Two-One-Seven: A Story of Japanese Internment, a documentary by Brett Kodama (BFA 2015 Film and Video).

Five Questions with Maggie Dunlap

In Amuse, the BFA Visual & Critical Studies student talks about her work, ageism and her favorite artists.
EVENTS
#cloud #ocean, by Astrid Bai

MFA Visual Narrative Open Studios and RisoLab Print Slam

MFA Visual Narrative students open their studios to the public, and Continuing Education Visual Narrative students exhibit work produced at the RisoLab. Thursday, July 28, 5:00 - 8:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Lunchtime Lecture Series: Jeanne Silverthorne

MFA Art Practice presents the sculptor discussing her work, including her cast rubber assemblages that reference the artist's studio. Tuesday, July 26, 12:30pm, 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.
