SVA Today
Wednesday, October 20, 2021

Beethoven Reimagined
Second-year BFA Design student Doah Kwon reimagines Beethoven as a contemporary artist of our day and age in “Beethoven Album,” a modern album cover for “Symphony No. 3” in honor of the musician’s 250th birthday. Via Graphis.
Event

Adoration
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents “Adoration,” an exhibition of paintings and drawings by John-Michael Byrd and Wayde McIntosh. Curated by the artists, the exhibition will be on view Wednesday, October 20 – Friday, November 12, at the SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

2:00pm | The Year of Uncertainty at the Queens Museum
Join Queens Museum President and Executive Director Sally Tallant, Public Programs Manager Catherine Grau, Director of Education Kimaada Le Gendre and artist-in-residence Julian Louis Phillips to explore how a new kind of institution can embrace the unknown. Presented by MA Curatorial Practice. Register here.

5:00pm | MPS Art Therapy Info Session
Prospective applicants are invited to join MPS Art Therapy for a virtual information session, at which a panel of current students, alumni, faculty and staff will provide insight into the interdisciplinary graduate program and the application process. Register here.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Crossing the Line Festival
The French Institute Alliance Française’s 14th annual fall festival brings an intriguing offering of theater, dance, talks and art to the institution's home base and to sites across New York. The festival will celebrate Africa’s cultural renaissance with works by interdisciplinary artists coming out of the continent and diaspora. Runs through Saturday, November 6, at the French Institute Alliance Française, 22 East 60th Street.
