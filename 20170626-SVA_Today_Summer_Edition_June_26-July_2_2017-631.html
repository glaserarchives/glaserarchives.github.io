SVA Today
Monday, June 26 - Sunday, July 2, 2017

SVA at Annecy

A recap of SVA's trip to the Annecy Animated Film Festival earlier this month, where the group engaged with industry veterans, talent recruiters and SVA alumni.

Now on View

Highlights from current exhibitions of painting collages by Bradley Castellanos (MFA 2006 Fine Arts), photography by Lissa Rivera (MFA 2009 Photography, Video and Related Media) and sculptures by Sam Tufnell (BFA 2002 Fine Arts).

"Sticking to Strategy"

Sal Romano (BFA 2016 Advertising) talks about a key piece of advice he received at SVA, his transition from student to creative professional and more.
THIS WEEK

Tuesday: Roberta Smith

MFA Art Practice presents a talk by The New York Times art critic Roberta Smith. Tuesday, June 27, 12:30 – 2:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Tuesday: Jennifer McClure

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by fine art and documentary photographer Jennifer McClure. Tuesday, June 27, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICKS
From an "Emergence" public intervention by Georgia Lale.

Art that Addresses Islamophobia

3:AM interviews artist Georgia Lale (MFA 2016 Fine Arts), whose work explores global humanitarian and economic issues, including Europe’s refugee crisis.

Panter's Songy of Paradise

The New Yorker talks with BFA Cartooning faculty member Gary Panter about his latest book Songy of Paradise, an illustrated story based on John Milton’s poem "Paradise Regained."

Creating an Alternate Reality

Thought Catalog interviews Brandon Woelfel (BFA 2016 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) about his first upcoming photography book Luminescence.
NYC PICKS

Who Is Muslim?

A panel discussion between Muslim men and women who challenge stereotypes, highlight alternative narrative and share their hopes for the future of America. Tickets $5; purchase here. Wednesday, June 28, 6:30pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.

"East Harlem Diptychs"

An exhibition featuring the work of BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography faculty member Ellen Wallenstein. Reception: Thursday, June 29, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Carter Burden Gallery, West Gallery, 548 West 28th Street, #534.

Brooklyn Museum First Saturdays

An evening of free art and entertainment, in conjunction with "We Wanted a Revolution: Black Radical Women, 1965 – 1985," currently on view. Saturday, July 1, 5:00 – 11:00pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
