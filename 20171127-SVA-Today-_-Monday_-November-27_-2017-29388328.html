Louise Bourgeois Unfolded

SVA faculty member and master printmaker Judith Solodkin talks about her friendship and collaborations with artist Louise Bourgeois, some of whose work is now on view in "Louise Bourgeois: An Unfolding Portrait" at The Museum of Modern Art.
PRESS PICK

Artists for Gender Equality

Through a series of videos, Artsy examines gender equality in the art world through the eyes of some of its most prominent figures, including MFA Fine Arts faculty member Marilyn Minter.
NYC PICKS

Gerhard Steidl: Why Physical Things Matter in a Digital World

Publisher Gerhard Steidl will discuss the continued importance of physical media in a world transformed by information technology with photographer Peter Badge and Marc Pachter, former director of the National Portrait Gallery and the National Museum of American History. Tickets start at $25; purchase here. 7:00pm. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.

Holiday Train Show

An exhibition of model trains trekking through a display of 150 New York City landmarks re-created with bark, leaves and other natural materials. Ticket prices vary; purchase here. Through Monday, January 15. New York Botanical Garden, 2900 Southern Boulevard, Bronx.
TAKE NOTE

MFA Computer Arts Open Studio

MFA Computer Arts presents its fall open studio, featuring video, animation, audio and interactive installations and physical computing by its students. Friday, December 1, noon – 5:00pm. Reception: Thursday, November 30, 5:00 – 7:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor.

MFA Social Documentary Film Information Session

If you are interested in combining social activism and creativity and passionate about storytelling, MFA Social Documentary Film encourages you to attend its information session, where you will connect with the chair, faculty, staff and alumni from the program. RSVP here. Saturday, December 2, 11:00am – 1:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Pieter Schoolwerth: Depicting the World "Once Removed"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a talk by painter and filmmaker Pieter Schoolwerth, followed by a conversation with SVA Art History faculty member and MFA Photography, Video and Related Media alumnus Lorne Blythe. Monday, December 4, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
