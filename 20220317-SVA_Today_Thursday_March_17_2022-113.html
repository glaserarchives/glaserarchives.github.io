SVA Today
Thursday, March 17, 2022

Roxane Gay Is SVA’s 2022 Commencement Speaker
Renowned and best-selling writer Roxane Gay will be the keynote speaker at SVA’s 47th annual commencement exercises taking place in person on Sunday, May 22.
Events

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:00pm | Revising Eastern Europe: Situated Practices and Authoritarian Regimes
MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with Zdenka Badovinac, one of Eastern Europe’s most distinguished curators and museum directors. Badovinac will be speaking with curator iLiana Fokianaki, artist Ištvan Išt Huzjan and St. Petersburg-based artist collective Chto Delat. Today, 12:00pm ET, online. Register here.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm ET, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).

DC Pride 2022
DC’s annual Pride anthology will include stories and cover art by Jen Bartel (BFA 2009 Illustration), Phil Jimenez (1991 Cartooning), Joshua “Sway” Swaby (BFA 2020 Cartooning) and Lynne Yoshii (BFA 2000 Cartooning).
NYC PICKS

St. Patrick’s Day Parade
Watch the world’s oldest and largest St. Patrick’s Day Parade, marching up Fifth Avenue from East 44th Street to East 79th Street. The parade begins at 11:00am.

A Deeper Listen: Concert and Meditation
As part of the Beginner’s Ear concert-meditation series at Lincoln Center, “A Deeper Listen” teaches audiences how to find greater depth and meaning in music by starting from a place of stillness. 7:30pm. Free. David Rubenstein Atrium at Lincoln Center, 61 West 62nd Street.

Laura Murray: “Watch Your Step” at the HERE Art Center
Laura Murray (BFA 2012 Fine Arts) is a mixed-media artist who creates highly textured, three-dimensional paintings that explore intersections between science, art and urbanism. The opening reception will take place tonight, 5:00pm – 7:00pm. On view through Saturday, May 7. HERE Art Center, 145 Sixth Avenue.
ANNOUNCEMENT

Next Week | Visiting Artist Lecture: Susan Hapgood
BFA Fine Arts presents a talk with Susan Hapgood, executive director of the International Studio & Curatorial Program in New York City. Hapgood has curated over 50 exhibitions, written approximately 70 journal articles and written and edited several books on varying topics in art. Monday, March 21, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.
