Friday, December 4 and the Weekend
#SAVEARTSPACE
BFA Photography alumni Justin Aversano and Travis Rix founded #SAVEARTSPACE and launched their second public art exhibition during Miami Art Week showcasing local art throughout the city's Wynwood neighborhood. SVA Close Up spoke with the two about the project.
6:00pm: VASA Holiday Bazaar
The Visual Arts Student Association presents a holiday bazaar, featuring student artwork, zines, crafts, food and much more. 6:00 - 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Saturday: "We Are Not Decorators" Opens
An exhibition of work by BFA Interior Design students that sets out to distinguish the terms "interior decorator" and "interior designer," clarifying distinctions between decorative arts, architecture and interior design that are often misunderstood. Reception Tuesday, December 8, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, January 9. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Saturday: Dukhtar
The SVA Theatre and The Iqra Fund present a screening of Dukhtar, which tells the story of a mother and daughter on the run after her daughter is promised in marriage to a much older man. Members of the film's production team and Iqra Fund founder Genevieve Walsh will answer questions following the screening. Discounted tickets are $12.25 for SVA students, faculty and staff with the code "student." 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

The Art of Math, the Math of Art
In The Guardian, BFA Visual & Critical Studies faculty member Lynn Gamwell writes about how years artists have incorporated mathematical concepts into their work for thousands of years. Her new book, Mathematics and Art (Princeton University Press), is an in-depth study of the topic.

New Database Trial: Harper's Bazaar Archive
The SVA Library now has trial access to the Harper's Bazaar Archive from Proquest's Fashion Studies package. The Harper’s Bazaar Archive is a comprehensive, searchable archive of every page, advertisement and cover of every issue of Harper’s Bazaar from its first appearance in 1867 to the current month. Reproduced in high-resolution color and supported by fully searchable text and indexing, this resource provides access to a chronicle of 20th century American and international fashion, culture and society. Available through Thursday, December 31.
2016 SVA Destinations: MPS Digital Photography in Havana
Spend a week in Cuba this March with MPS Digital Photography Chair Katrin Eismann and faculty member and alumnus Jaime Permuth, experiencing and documenting the area's vibrant visual culture during the country's transition into a new era. The deadline for applications is Saturday, January 30, 2016. For more information, contact mpsphoto@sva.edu.
