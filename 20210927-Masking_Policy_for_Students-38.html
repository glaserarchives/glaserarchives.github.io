Masking Policy for Students

September 27, 2021

Dear SVA Students:

SVA has established a set of protocols aimed at preventing the spread of the COVID-19 virus.  Chief among these protocols is the requirement that every member of the SVA community, and those visiting our campus, must wear a mask at all times while inside any SVA building. 

Besides full vaccination, wearing a mask that covers both your mouth and nose while indoors is our single best defense against the spread of the virus.  Wearing a mask is a shared responsibility that we take very seriously--as should you.

In the last couple of weeks, there have been several incidents of students not wearing masks inside of classrooms, studios, common areas, and lobbies, as well as eating in shared spaces.  In the interest of protecting every member of the SVA community, both practices are strictly prohibited. These prohibitions may be relaxed as we move forward with the semester.

Until such time, as of the date of this email, any student not wearing a mask is subject to disciplinary action under the Student Code of Conduct.  Possible sanctions for failing to wear a mask, include, but are not limited to:

• First offense:  Verbal warning
• Second offense:  Written warning 
• Third offense:  Placement on disciplinary probation for the balance of the semester
• Fourth Offense:  Suspension from SVA for the balance of the semester

The SVA Student Code of Conduct is outlined in the 2021-22 SVA Handbook, pages 77-79, and is administered by the Office of Student Affairs.

Don’t put yourself and the SVA community at risk for the spread of COVID.  Please, just wear a mask!

Sincerely,

Christopher J. Cyphers, Ph.D.
