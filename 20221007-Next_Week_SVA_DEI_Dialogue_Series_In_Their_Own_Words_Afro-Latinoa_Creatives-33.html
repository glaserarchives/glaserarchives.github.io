In honor of Hispanic Heritage Month, SVA’s Office of Diversity Equity and Inclusion invites the SVA Community to join us for this panel discussion with four dynamic Afro-Latino Creatives from various disciplines who will share their experiences of being Afro-Latino/a within the visual arts field.

Guests:

Sulma Arzu-Brown

Producer, Writer: D’Que Latino (First Afro-Latino TV Series)
For over fifteen years, Sulma Arzu-Brown has been working as a champion for diversity and inclusion, well before the term became a trending topic. Sulma’s work with e Garifuna Coalition USA and NYC Hispanic Chamber of Commerce has merited her many award recognitions.

She is the author of the award-winning book Bad Hair Does Not Exist!/Pelo Malo No Existe! and My Hair Comes With Me: Shifting the Paradigm of What Success Looks Like! She is the creator of the nation’s first Afro-Latino sitcom TV series entitled D’QUE LATINO. She utilizes her platform to bring honor to the Afro-Latino Diaspora.

Victor Cruz

Actor; Animator, Writer
Born in Portsmouth, VA, and raised in the heart of the South Bronx. At the age of sixteen, Victor performed stand-up comedy for the very first time in New York City. He studied art and musical theater at Talent Unlimited High School and attended the acting conservatory at SUNY Purchase College, where he received a B.F.A. The day before graduation from the program, he booked his first TV role on HBO’s The Sopranos. Victor has appeared in over fifty TV Shows, Films, and commercials. While pursuing his acting career, he produced his own content from sketches, short films, comedy shows, and features. Some of those projects include: The Victor Cruz Show (Comedy Sketch DVD), Victor Cruz's La Operación (Short Film), Congratulations Mr. González! (Short Film), The Stockroom (Feature Film), and TITA (The Animated Series). Victor runs his own acting studio The Victor Cruz Acting Studio and is on the faculty at HB Studio. Some of his TV and Film Credits include: The Good Cop, Blue Bloods, The Other Woman (2014), and West Side Story (2021).

He is currently voicing the character of Frankie Four Feet on the new PBS Kids show ALMA'S WAY, executive produced by Sonia Manzano and will appear alongside Morgan Freeman in the new film A Good Person coming in 2023.

David Heredia

Animator, Heroes of Color LLC, NYC Department of Education Vendor, SVA Alumnus BFA Animation '02 
David Heredia has worked for Walt Disney Animation, Warner Bros. Animation, and DC Collectibles. His six-time award-winning education video series "Heroes of Color" has been featured in the New York Times, on NPR, and on PBS Online. He lives in Santa Clarita, California, with his wife and three children.

Ehcrama Marche

Born and raised in Montebello, CA, she quickly discovered her passion for creating and has worked on a variety of projects from Music Videos, Short Films, Commercials, and Feature Films. Since graduating with a Bachelor's Degree in Film, Ehcrama has worked as a director of photography, art director and animator with her latest creative venture being the creator of an Animated, Cartoon, Telenovela Series called, "Hella Black Mexican" on YouTube.
