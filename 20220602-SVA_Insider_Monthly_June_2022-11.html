🎙️ Get to Know: Angie Wojak, Executive Director, External Relations

Congratulations on your new position as executive director of External Relations and a decade at SVA! Can you summarize your SVA journey up to this point?

I first came to SVA [as an undergraduate] in the mid-80s from rural Missouri with a full scholarship to study illustration with Marshall Arisman. I knew early on that I wanted to combine teaching with my studies, so I went on to earn my MFA at the University of Missouri. I began my career at Parsons School of Design, where I led the career services office for 13 years and then launched Columbia University’s School of the Arts career office. Throughout those years, I was closely involved with SVA as an alumnus, visiting as a guest speaker when I had a book launch, serving on the alumni scholarship jury and collaborating with the SVA career services staff on events and conferences.


Then I came to SVA as Career Development director and focused on industry outreach in the U.S., Asia and Europe. I was thrilled to work with animation and film students for the first time in my career. So through my time as a student to now being in a leadership role at SVA, It feels like coming full circle because I can celebrate SVA’s legacy of rich, visual storytelling while helping current students create their own stories and careers.


Looking back on your legacy with Career Development, what were some of your most memorable, rewarding experiences?

I think the most creatively fulfilling work I’ve done at SVA is focused on putting together amazing creative talents and creating these public dialogues. I’ve dedicated a lot of time to cultivating industry connections in LA and San Francisco with programs like SVA in San Francisco, SVA Premieres and Comic-Con. Seeing our alumni get jobs and then watching them give back to the community by offering career advice and portfolio reviews is incredibly satisfying. I’ve also had amazing experiences traveling to South Korea, Japan, Taiwan and China with my SVA colleagues as we build and strengthen connections with the SVA alumni community and industry. It’s really awe-inspiring to travel to the other side of the world and be embraced by generations of SVA graduates.


I have to thank my Career Development team, both current and former staff: Patty Romeu, Tricia Ross, Spencer Robelen and Anna Ogier-Bloomer.


What are you most looking forward to in your new role? Any ideas you can share this early?

I’m looking forward to building strategic partnerships and community initiatives. I’ve got lots of ideas on how to collaborate in new ways within the SVA community and with outside organizations. I want to create collaborations with a focus on New York City cultural institutions such as museums, non-profits, arts organizations, media and technology sectors. I also want to create collaborations built around timely themes like climate change, and create opportunities for an exchange of ideas through exhibitions and presentations involving students, alumni and outside experts.

Joseph Sinnott
