Message from the Provost
Welcome back from what I hope was a restful winter break.  If this is your first semester teaching at SVA, allow me to be among the first to officially welcome you aboard. I hope everyone has had a moment to peruse the newly redesigned SVA.edu website. The team of designers at Visual Arts Press, with input from across the College, produced a visually stunning and easy to use site.  What is more, the new SVA.edu site is compliant with new regulations of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA).
The College works continuously to improve the learning and overall experience of our diverse student body.  In this month’s All Faculty Brief, I am pleased to preview a new initiative called “SVA Teachers on Teaching,” led by the Office of Learning Technology (OLT). The short video below will be part of a growing library of content we hope will serve to inspire, and provide the kind of practical knowledge about teaching everyone can find useful.  Just as important as the content of the video library is the fact that it derives entirely from you. The marriage of your collective wisdom as art and design educators with the resources and expertise of OLT makes possible a valuable store of easily accessible information well-suited to a faculty of active working professionals.
Best wishes for a fantastic new year and a productive and rewarding spring semester!
Irene Rosner David, Ph.D., ATR-BC, LCAT, is the Director of Therapeutic Arts at Bellevue Hospital Center, New York. She has been on the faculty of SVA MPS Art Therapy since 2003.
SVA Teachers on Teaching
Teaching is its own art form that many of you have spent years refining. We are launching a new feature as we begin a new year: Teachers on Teaching. This new segment will feature a different interview with an SVA faculty member sharing their thoughts about art and design pedagogy. The goal is to facilitate the exchange of good teaching practices between the College's remarkable faculty.
Kicking off the new segment is Irene Rosner David of MPS Art Therapy. Click here to view a short video of her recent interview. Over time, we will organize this collection of videos into an archive available on the Faculty Knowledge Base. But for now, please share your feedback with the Office of Learning Technologies and let us know if you would be willing to spend half an hour talking about teaching in our video studio. Contact oltvideo@sva.edu.
Faculty Reminders for Spring 2020
Attendance Tracking — Your attendance rosters can be accessed through Canvas by logging into your MyID.SVA.edu account and selecting the "CANVAS LMS" app. Please record and post attendance for every student in each class session. Visit this link if you require more information. Note that students who are marked absent for one-third of the sessions of any course will be administratively dropped from the course with a "W" grade. SVA does not consider any student absence as excused for the purposes of this policy.
Online Textbook Maintenance — All colleges are required by federal law to publish required textbook information in order for students to receive federal financial aid. This instruction document demonstrates how to add or update required texts for your courses. Please make any revisions to your required textbooks within the first week of the term.
FERPA Compliance — The start of a new semester is always a good time to re-familiarize yourself with the federal law protecting the privacy of student records. This document outlines the basic principles of FERPA and ways that you can stay in compliance. Using the Canvas learning management system and your SVA email account to correspond with students and administrators is especially important in this regard. If you don’t remember your MyID.SVA.edu credentials or need help with your SVA email account, contact the IT Help Desk at 212.592.2400 or helpdesk@sva.edu.
Syllabus in Canvas — Please note that all faculty should submit course syllabi in Canvas. In the past, these documents were collected by department staff. Instead, now you'll enter your course syllabus into Canvas and it will be distributed to your department chair and Academic Affairs. This also gives students a consistent place to access all of their most up-to-date syllabi. Additionally, any new College-wide policies will be automatically updated for you within your syllabus. Click here to view a video describing the updated syllabus page in Canvas and access related course-planning resources.
Supervising an Independent Study — All faculty who are assigned to supervise an independent study will receive a collection of resources in their respective course in Canvas. Additionally, there is now information in the Faculty Knowledge Base that will help faculty manage the all-important kick-off meeting and support the student in tracking and communicating their goals and progress throughout the semester.
SVA Library New and Improved Catalog System
The library catalog has been fully migrated to a new and improved system, and you may be noticing some changes while searching for resources on our website. Logging in to view your account and using digital resources should be easier than ever, but there are still some issues we're working on. Please do let us know if you have any trouble finding resources, or if you'd just like a tour of the new system email reference@sva.libanswers.com and we’ll get right back to you.
We’re happy to help in any way we can!

An Evening with David Heredia, Freelance Entrepreneur

Thursday, January 30, 2020 6:30 pm
Presented by SVA Alumni Affairs and Development and BFA Animation
