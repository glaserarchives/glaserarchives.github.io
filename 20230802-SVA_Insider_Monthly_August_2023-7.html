🎙️ Get To Know: Angelica Passioura, coordinator, Disability Resources

You began your role as coordinator for the Disability Resources department this past spring. What led you to SVA?

I moved to New York City three years ago. In July of 2022, I was walking towards the East River to watch the fireworks with friends and passed by a window with student artwork. I remember thinking how fun it would be to work with people who create and are interested in the arts. Earlier this year, I made it my goal to explore my employment options in higher education. I started going to interviews, but the positions seemed like a good fit for me once I had my interview at SVA.


In a general sense, how have you seen resources for people with disabilities expand and improve?

In my opinion, resources for people with disabilities have expanded and improved as a result of technology. Technology has made it easier for people to connect and, as a result, advocate for themselves or seek help. Every day, newer and more evolved assistive technology helps some people with disabilities regain more control of their life or assist in doing certain tasks. However, a lot of work still needs to be done to improve resources for people with disabilities to make jobs, education, transportation and life in general more accessible.


As the new school year approaches, how do you hope to support students with disabilities in the SVA community?

I want to improve Disability Resources’ social media presence to increase our engagement and visibility at the College. I also hope to make our campus more accessible and inclusive through advocacy, student engagement and communication with the SVA community.
