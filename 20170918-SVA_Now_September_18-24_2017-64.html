SVA's Fall Online Courses

From the Art of Politics to Writing for Comics, SVA's online courses give students the opportunity to learn from outstanding professionals about a wide range of material. Enrollment deadline for many of them is today.

My First Year: Carlos Flores

Next in sva.edu's "My First Year" series is Carlos Flores, a second-year BFA Fine Arts student, who talks about everything from his favorite thing about going to college in New York City to his biggest laundry mistake.
IN THE PRESS

Milton Glaser's Logo Endures

Adweek on how SVA Acting Chairman of the Board Milton Glaser's "I ❤ NY" logo originated and how it has remained an iconic symbol for New York City.

Artists to Watch this Month

Artspace's list of eight artists to watch in September includes Andrea McGinty (MFA 2014 Fine Arts) and Claire Christerson (BFA 2015 Photography).

Art History Pixelated

Yatzer on Adam Lister's (BFA 2003 Fine Arts) "Art History 101," a new series of watercolor paintings that reconstructs famous works from art history, such as Michelangelo's David and Leonardo da Vinci's Mona Lisa, in pixelated forms.

"No Simple Highways" Reception

SVA Galleries presents "No Simple Highways," an exhibition of student work that explores how spaces, whether naturally occurring or architectural, shape the human experience. Reception tonight, 6:00 – 8:00pm. On view through Saturday, October 7. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"Mind Sets" Reception

BFA Fine Arts presents "Mind Sets," an exhibition of student work that explores the ways artists can employ their craft to express their concerns during uncertain times. Reception Wednesday, September 20, 6:00 – 8:00pm. On view through Saturday, October 14. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
