Anne Waldman on Poetry and Painting
SVA Close Up spoke with poet Anne Waldman on her writing career and association with the New York School of artists and writers. Waldman will speak at SVA as part of MFA Art Writing's Quijote Talks series on Thursday, November 19, 6:30pm, at 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
Emergency Response Training
On Monday, November 23, 10:30am, SVA's Emergency Management Committee will conduct a training session on how to respond, stay safe and keep informed during specific incidents that may occur on campus, including medical emergencies and acts of violence. Space is limited, so reservations are recommended. RSVP by Friday, November 20. For questions, contact proffice@sva.edu.
6:00pm: Sandino Scheidegger
MFA Fine Arts presents independent curator Sandino Scheidegger. He is the founder of the experimental contemporary art institutions Random Institute, located in Zurich, and Kunsthalle Tropical, located in a remote section of Iceland. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:15pm: Mediterranea
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of Mediterranea, starring Koudous Seihon and Alassane Sy. Writer and director Jonas Carpignano will answer questions following the screening. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:30pm: Brooke Hodge, The Curator's Eye
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents curator and writer Brooke Hodge discussing the behind-the-scenes explorations, conversations and negotiations that go into developing exhibitions about architecture, design and fashion. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Press pick of the day
Peace for Paris
MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller remarks in Fast Company on designer Jean Jullien's instantly iconic "peace for Paris" illustration, which he published following Friday's terrorist attacks.
NYC picks of the day
The Hirschfeld Century
David Leopold, author of The Hirschfeld Century, discusses the artist's legendary caricatures and the illustrations that defined his style. 6:30pm, Mid-Manhattan Library, 455 Fifth Avenue.
The Dark Side
A screening of the new "docufiction" film that was filmed during and after Hurricane Sandy, with cinematography by SVA faculty member Valentina Caniglia. Tickets are $10. 8:30pm, AMC Loews Kips Bay, 570 Second Avenue.
Take Note
SVAlert System Test
On Friday, November 20, at approximately 1:00pm, there will be a test of SVAlert, the College's electronic notification system. At that time, all staff and faculty, along with students who have registered for SVAlert, will receive messages via all contact options with which they are currently registered. When you receive the test message from SVA, you do not need to take any action. However, if you do NOT receive the test message, contact helpdesk@sva.edu. Students who are not already registered for SVAlert need to sign up by logging into MySVA. Given the possibility of future school closings, class cancellations or emergency situations, you are urged to register today. Please do not register parents or roommates without informing them first.
Call for Submissions for The Match Factory
The Match Factory, the Humanities and Sciences Department's biannual online literary magazine, is seeking submissions for its spring 2016 issue. Students, faculty and staff may submit poetry, short stories, film scripts, one-act plays, music or film reviews, essays, personal essays, memoirs or any other kind of writing. The deadline for submissions is Thursday, December 10.
MFA Fine Arts Open Studios
MFA Fine Arts presents students showing recent work in their studios. Thursday, December 17, 5:00 - 9:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.
