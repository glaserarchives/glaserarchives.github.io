ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Chance! Students: Submit Your Work to Visual Opinion
Submit your artwork to Visual Opinion today and get paid! VO is SVA's student-run magazine. The next edition's theme is "exposed." Deadline: Tomorrow, October 5, 9:00am. Email your submission to vo@sva.edu with your name, the title of the piece, and your year and major. Please note that only students residing in the United States can submit.

Listen to WSVA Live!
Starting today, SVA's radio station, WSVA, is live with shows and music brought to you by SVA students. Tune in and see the schedule on the website: wsvaradio.sva.edu.

Register Now | Examining Institutional Characteristics Behind 2015 Black Student Protest Events
The National Conference on Race and Ethnicity (NCORE) presents this webinar, featuring Michael Carhart, PhD and student advisor at Seton Hall University, and Stephanie Mayer, PhD, MBA and financial disbursement analyst at Columbia University. The two will discuss their study examining the institutional characteristics underlying Black student protests in 2015. Wednesday, October 6, 4:00 – 5:30 ET. Register here.

MFA Visual Narrative at New York Comic Con
Come see MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) at New York Comic Con! Fox is currently the co-creator and artist on the sci-fi comic book series The Weatherman, published through Image Comics. Thursday, October 7 – Sunday, October 10. Artist's Alley, Table C14. Proof of vaccination and masking will be required at NYCC, but many events are also available digitally.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

The 2021 Socrates Annual: Sanctuary
The 11 artists selected for Socrates Sculpture Park's annual exhibition respond to the question: "How can art function as a sanctuary, a place of refuge, rest and meditation—without resorting to escapism?" See the work through March 2022 at Socrates Sculpture Park, 32-01 Vernon Boulevard, Queens.
