SVA Today
Friday, March 25, and the Weekend
Song Lee and Kohzy Koh presenting WristGuard at Interaction 16.
SVA Students Win Big at Interaction 16
Earlier this month, MFA Interaction Design students Kohzy Koh and Song Lee won the international student design challenge for their project WristGuard at this year's Interaction Conference, held in Helsinki, Finland. Next year, SVA will be the official education partner of Interaction 17, which will take place at locations in New York City.
2:30pm: Dog Therapy
Student Health and Counseling Services and New York Therapy Animals present a special visit from therapy dogs to help SVA students de-stress in between work and classes. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 301C.
6:30pm: Seen and Heard: Reflections on Collaborative Art Process
MPS Art Therapy presents Gemma Burgio, ATR-BC, LCAT, and Valeria Koutmina, ATR-BC, LCAT, discussing their collaborative residency at SVA, which incorporated textile arts, mixed media and written narratives to explore attachment, spontaneity and empathic resonance. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:30pm: Strong Voices in Action
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents a discussion on leadership, advocacy and social justice, featuring music, dance, poetry, and more. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
7:00pm: naked romance and then some
Humanities and Sciences presents a multimedia performance by faculty member and poet Davida Singer, who will be accompanied by musicians and perform poetry from her collection naked romance and then some. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Turning the Tide
Hi-Fructose on a new exhibition of vivid portraits by Jenny Morgan (MFA 2008 Fine Arts), which recently opened at Beers London.
NYC Weekend Pick
Edgar Degas: "A Strange New Beauty"
An exhibition of work by the 19th-century French painter known for his chronicles of the ballet. Admission is free with SVA ID. Opens tomorrow. Through Sunday, July 24. MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street.
TAKE NOTE
Dana Schutz
BFA Fine Arts presents artist Dana Schutz, best known for her wildly expressive figures, discussing her work. Thursday, March 31, 7:00pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
April Fools' Ball
VASA presents the April Fools' Ball, a combination masquerade and burlesque featuring performances by SVA students and professional performers. Tickets are $10 until Monday, March 28; $15 after. Tickets can be purchased here. Open to SVA students (limit one non-SVA guest per student). SVA ID required for entry. Friday, April 1, 7:30 - 11:30pm, The Box, 189 Chrystie Street.
