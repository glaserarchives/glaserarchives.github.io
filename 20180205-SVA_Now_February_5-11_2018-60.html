News and events from the School of Visual Arts

"Underground Images" Travels to the Netherlands

SVA's subway posters continue their worldwide tour with two shows in the Netherlands, at two of the most publicly trafficked sites the exhibition has had on its five-year journey thus far.

Get to Know Harlem

Places to see, things to do and foods to eat in Harlem. Stay tuned for more New York City neighborhood guides.

January Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects from SVA Portfolios this month include eye-catching comics, spectacular illustrations and an intriguing zine.
IN THE PRESS

Rewriting a Family's History

Artsy on how MFA Fine Arts faculty member Laurel Nakadate used photoshop to introduce her recently deceased mother to her newborn son in her new series "The Kingdom."

"A Compilation of Magnificent Seeing"

Lenscratch on Almost True, Steven Bollman's (BFA 1983 Photography) latest book, featuring over 30 years of photographs from around the world.

Keith Haring Mural in Chicago

Time Out recommends alumnus Keith Haring's 1989 mural for Chicago's Grant Park, on display at the Chicago Cultural Center from March 3 to September 23.

From Art Student to Sushi Chef

Crains New York on how Oona Tempest (BFA 2014 Visual & Critical Studies) became a sushi chef.
EVENTS

Ana de Orbegoso

MPS Digital Photography presents Peruvian-born artist Ana De Orbegoso, whose interdisciplinary work explores different aspects of the individual or social psyche through the use of popular iconography. Tuesday, February 6, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

1987: Drawings by John Ruggeri

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents this exhibition of drawings by John Ruggeri (1973 Illustration, BFA 1984 Media Arts, MFA 1986 Illustration as Visual Essay) that document the devastating early years of the AIDS crisis in New York City. Thursday, February 8 – Wednesday, February 28. Reception: Thursday, February 8, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor

Cooke Center School SKILLS Group Zine Show

MPS Art Therapy presents an exhibition of zine work completed by the Cooke Center School SKILLS Group. Free zines available to attendees. Friday, February 9 – Friday, February 23. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Eco-Femmes: Next Nature

CP Projects Space presents this exhibition, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Jesse Bandler Firestone, which brings together queer eco-feminist artists, "eco-femmes," to posit a scenario in which nature and humans are entwined in exchanges that are emotional, physical and, at times, perverse. Thursday, February 8 – Wednesday, February 21. Reception: Friday, February 9, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
