New MFA Visual Narrative Scholarship Recipients Announced
Games and art tools studio Braintreehouse has partnered with SVA and the Visual Arts Foundation to create the Sketchboard Pro Scholarship for MFA Visual Narrative students. This year’s recipients are Laura Brown and Oret Peña.
Event

Today | Visiting Artist Lecture: Susan Hapgood
BFA Fine Arts presents a talk with Susan Hapgood, executive director of the International Studio & Curatorial Program in New York City. Hapgood has curated over 50 exhibitions, written approximately 70 journal articles and written and edited several books on varying topics in art. Tonight, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Peter Hristoff’s Subway Poster in Print
MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller writes about the latest addition to the SVA “subway series” posters, by faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts).
NYC PICK

Deeps of Peace
Color and memory combine and inspire the abstract paintings in “Deeps of Peace,” an exhibition of 15 paintings by Brian Rutenberg (MFA 1989 Fine Arts). Through Saturday, April 30. Forum Gallery, 475 Park Avenue at 57th Street.

Meet Your 2022 – 2023 VASA Officers
The votes have been counted and the 2022 – 2023 VASA officers have been chosen! Congratulations to the members of Team Nouveau: Sabrina Valderrama (BFA Design), president; Abigail Haven (BFA Design), vice president; Daria DelMonico (BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects), treasurer; and Jiayan (Carol) Wang (BFA Design), student senate chair.

Thursday | Insurgent Practices Against Neoliberalism
The SVA Honors Program and BFA Visual & Critical Studies present artist Jane Dickson and writer Sarah Schulman in conversation with artist and faculty member Shellyne Rodriguez (BFA 2011 Visual & Critical Studies) on their experiences as working artists in a changing and unequal New York City, from the 1970s to the present. Thursday, March 24, 6:00pm ET, online.

Deadline Extension: Submit to the Humanities and Sciences Writing Contest
The SVA Humanities and Sciences Writing Contest is open to undergraduates for submissions in poetry, short story, personal essay/memoir, critical essay and film script or play. Prizes in each category are $600, $300 and $100 for first, second and third places, respectively. Submissions should be sent to Edwin Rivera, erivera3@sva.edu, by no later than the extended deadline of Wednesday, April 6.
