The new session of the 

MA Curatorial Practice program has begun. Here's the latest news about what we’re up to in New York.

Dear Friend,
First, I’m thrilled to announce two new faculty: Kate Fowle and Wendy Vogel. Kate’s extraordinary career includes her work as chief curator of Moscow’s Garage Museum of Contemporary Art, executive director of Independent Curators International in New York, and the inaugural international curator at the Ullens Center for Contemporary Art in Beijing. She’ll teach a course on international programming. Wendy is a leading American art critic, who is a former editor at Art in America and a frequent contributor to Artforum, art-agenda, The Guardian, Mousse, and many other publications. Wendy is leading a new writing workshop in the program. As well, the distinguished museum director and scholar, Clémentine Deliss, based in Berlin, joins our faculty at large, offering seminars to our curatorial fellows. Chus Martinez, who has also served on our faculty at large, will come as well for a seminar session this term.

I’m equally thrilled to tell you that we just launched a special issue titled “We Would Prefer Not To” of the leading online and print journal, OnCurating. The issue takes political resistance and sanctuary as its subject, ranging across cultural history, exhibitions, artist projects, theory, fiction, interviews, and portfolios of images. Nearly all of the texts, I’m proud to say, are contributed by our curatorial fellows in the program. 

UPDATE:

Curatorial Activism and the Politics of Shock

At the same time, we’re now finalizing a new book about curatorial activism and questions around activism to be published by Sternberg Press in Berlin. The book, based on the summit we did here in New York last November, includes texts by a remarkable gathering of curators, including Defne Ayas, Ute Meta Bauer, Nicolas Bourriaud, Carolyn Christov-Bakargiev, Sofía Hernandez Chong Cuy, Joshua Decter, Clémentine Deliss, Irmgard Emmelhainz, Boris Groys, Hou Hanru, Maria Lind, Antonia Majaca, Gabi Ngcobo, Hans Ulrich Obrist, Jack Persekian, Pi Li, María Belén Saéz de Ibarra, Terry Smith, Nato Thompson, Mick Wilson, Brian Kuan Wood, Tirdad Zolghadir, and myself. 
The book will come out in 2019.

The semester is going to bring to our curatorial fellows a range of special guest curators to speak about their work, coming from Amsterdam, Winnipeg, Paris, Istanbul, Moscow, New York, Washington, DC, San Francisco, Los Angeles, and Berlin. And that is just this semester. Every one of these speaking engagements allows our students to have small class sessions with the visitors to learn about their practices, have ample time to ask questions and exchange ideas, and make important connections for the future. 

Special programming organized by our director of research, Brian Kuan Wood, who is also a founding editor of e-flux journal, will include a gathering with the curators of the upcoming Sharjah Biennial, a public discussion about new formations for museum structures with Clémentine Deliss, and an evening around the major exhibition at the Museum of Modern Art focused on the legendary Judson Dance Theater with the exhibition’s curators, Anna Janevski and Thomas J. Lax. As well, we’re looking forward to welcoming Hans Ulrich Obrist for a special talk. 

These are just a sampling of highlights for this semester, with our curatorial fellows in the midst of making their own exhibitions and projects not only here in our space but also at venues around the city and beyond. In fact, one of our current fellows, Xinyi Ren, will co-curate an exhibition at Shanghai’s Power Station, one of two proposals selected from a pool of over 200, with a stellar jury that included Homi Bhabha, Chris Dercon, Donna De Salvo, Ding Yi, Fei Dawei, Feng Yuan, Gao Shiming, Gong Yan, Hou Hanru, Mark Wigley, and René Lorenceau. More news to come about our curatorial fellows’ upcoming projects. 

Enjoy the fall, and of course a reminder that applications are now open for next year’s class of MA Curatorial Practice here at SVA. Find out more here.


Best wishes,
Steven Henry Madoff
Chair, MA Curatorial Practice
