Dear Friend,
 
Now, as a school year comes to a close that brought more than 50 curators and experts from around the world to the program, we’re launching seven year-end exhibitions and performances in a special opening-night celebration on April 21 in Brooklyn. You can see the details of every exhibition and performance here, featuring the work of more than 50 international artists. We’re super excited and proud of these curatorial events, which are open to the public and you’re very welcome to attend.
These openings follow on the heels of the great panel discussions that have been part of our “Local Address” series. The crisis politics of the Trump administration tend to drive Americans inward to deal with the ongoing assault on liberties we’ve taken for granted. But we have to remain attuned to culture and politics everywhere, and “Local Address” gives our students and audiences firsthand access to contemporary conditions around the world to inform and enlarge us. We complete the series this week on Thursday (details here), with a panel about the Fresh Milk Art Platform in St. George, Barbados, and the colonialist history of the Caribbean region, which we sent you an invitation to last week. This final talk follows two extraordinary evenings that considered post-civil war Beirut and post-apartheid Johannesburg.
We’ll continue the series next fall, and we’re also going to mount a remarkable public summit on curatorial activism, including more than 20 of the world’s leading museum curators and directors—and with whom our students, back from spending time at documenta as part of an annual grant we give them for travel and international internships, will have time to listen to and to meet.
Our internships this summer will also be with some of the world’s leading curatorial figures, such as Nicolas Bourriaud, Hou Hanru, Gaby Horn and Gabi Ngcobo, José Roca, and Maria Lind, only affirming the global nature of our program—which is why we emphasize how international our students and faculty are, coming from Australia, Belgium, Canada, China, Colombia, Germany, Iran, Iraq, Mexico, Puerto Rico, Portugal, Spain, Sweden, Turkey, the UK, and the United States to date.
And next year’s group of students promises to add even more diversity, although we’re still in the process of applications and acceptances. You can find everything you need to know about applying here. We’re looking forward to hearing from you and seeing you soon!

Best wishes,
Steven Henry Madoff
 
P.s. We’ve just had news today that Jasa McKenzie, one of our first-year students, has received an apexart Franchise Exhibition grant (an open call competition with hundreds of applicants) for a show she will curate in Japan in 2018. Congratulations, Jasa!
