SVA Today
Wednesday, April 18, 2018

Appreciating the Light [Video]

Next up in the SVA Features video series is Frank Ockenfels 3 (BFA 1983 Photography), a celebrated photographer who has immortalized the looks of David Bowie, Angelina Jolie, Kurt Cobain and pop culture touchstones like Mad Men, Breaking Bad, and many more.
PRESS PICK

"Building the Brooklyn Bridge"

The New York Times on Peter J. Tomasi's graphic novel The Bridge, which was illustrated by Sara DuVall (BFA 2015 Cartooning), about the history behind the Brooklyn Bridge.
NYC PICKS

The Job Seeker's Competitive Edge

Ajay Rajani, the founder of Core Labs, leads this workshop on how to make your portfolio stand out in a large pool of applicants. Tickets $10 – $15; purchase here. 8:00pm. Ustwo, 26 Broadway, 16th floor.

Adrian Piper: A Synthesis of Intuitions, 1965 – 2016

An exhibition featuring drawings, paintings, photographs, multimedia installations, videos, and performances, covering artist Adrian Piper's five-decade career. Free with SVA ID. Through July 22. The Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.

Inka Essenhigh: Manhattanhenge

A site-specific wall drawing by painter and alumnus Inka Essenhigh that replicates the effect of the sun when aligned precisely with the New York City grid. Through August 4. The Drawing Center, 35 Wooster Street.
TAKE NOTE

Surfacing: A New Series

Join New York Times visual journalists Malin Fezehai, Walter Thompson-Hernández and Stephen Hiltner, who will discuss their time with some of the world’s niche communities, as part of this series that explores the bonds and aspirations of these subcultures. Use discount code PHOTOVIP for $5 off; purchase here. Friday, April 20, 6:00pm. TheTimesCenter, 242 West 41st Street.

BFA Fine Arts Open Studios

BFA Fine Arts presents its spring open studios, where visitors can view student work. Monday, April 23, 6:00 – 9:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, ground floor.
