SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts May 2 - 8, 2016
FEATURES
Photo by Jonathas Nazareth
Art and Inspiration at The Met
SVA faculty member and alumnus Peter Hristoff talks about his experience as the Met's artist-in-residence, which is tied to the new exhibition, "Court and Cosmos: The Great Age of the Seljuqs," on view through Sunday, July 24.
The April Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios for April include a creative branding project, an illustrated reflection on technology, a stunning look at dancers at rest, and more.
Using the Power of Cooking to Fight Bullying
MFA Design for Social Innovation student Ivan Boscariol's Alumni Scholarship Award-winning project Boiling Down aims to reduce bullying and challenge gender norms through the power of cooking.
Why Four Creatives Are Making Miami Home
From the Visual Arts Journal's new Spotlight series, which focuses on cities and countries where alumni live and work.
IN THE PRESS
Behind the Label
Co.Design on the story of how longtime SVA faculty member Milton Glaser created the visual identity for Brooklyn Brewery thirty years ago, and continues to leave his mark on the brand.
Jessica Walsh Has No Filter
The Art Directors Club interviews the BFA Design faculty member.
What It Means to Be a Curator
The Absorbe interviews Dan Halm (MFA 2001 Illustration as Visual Essay), project manager, SVA External Relations.
"Focus On What You Know and Love Best"
AI-AP profiles Fernanda Cohen (BFA 2003 Illustration).
