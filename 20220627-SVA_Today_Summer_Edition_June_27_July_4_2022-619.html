SVA Today
June 27 – July 4, 2022

Elevating Black Comic-Book Visions and Voices
Read our interview with Eisner Award-nominated artist, writer and SVA alumnus Shawn Martinbrough (BFA 1993 Illustration) about “The Artist’s Experience: From Brotherman to Batman,” a new exhibition at the Society of Illustrators that celebrates Black comic-book artists, curated by Martinbrough and comics journalist and writer Karama Horne.
EVENTS THIS WEEK

1:00pm | Make-up Commencement
The make-up commencement ceremony for the classes of 2020 and 2021 will be held today, 1:00pm, at Radio City Music Hall, with filmmaker, artist and author John Waters as keynote speaker. The event will be live-streamed on sva.edu/commencement.

7:30pm | Elvis Screening
SVA Theatre presents a screening of Baz Luhrmann’s Elvis. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Visit svatheatre.com for SVA Theatre’s current COVID-19 guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Tonight, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tomorrow | Practice Lecture Series: Miatta Kawinzi
MFA Art Practice presents Miatta Kawinzi, a Brooklyn-based multidisciplinary artist, writer and educator of Kenyan-Liberian-American heritage. Tuesday, June 28, 12:30pm. RSVP here. MFA Art Practice, 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.

Tomorrow | i3 Photo Lecture: Azu Nwagbogu
MPS Digital Photography presents Azu Nwagbogu, founder and director of the African Artists’ Foundation, a nonprofit organization based in Lagos, Nigeria. Tuesday, June 28, 7:00pm, online via Zoom.

Tomorrow | Minions: The Rise of Gru in 3D
SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Minions: The Rise of Gru. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Visit svatheatre.com for SVA Theatre’s current COVID-19 guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Tuesday, June 28, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tomorrow and Wednesday | Narcan Training
Join Nurse Abby Wohl from Student Health and Counseling Services for a virtual SVA staff and faculty Narcan training session to learn more about how to reduce risk and prevent an opioid overdose. As a Narcan trainee, you will receive your own Narcan kit. Register here for either date, Tuesday, June 28, 2:00pm, and Wednesday, June 29, 2:00pm. If you are unable to attend, additional trainings will be offered throughout the year. Email Wohl at awohl@sva.edu for more information.

Wednesday | Artist Residency Programs Open Studios
SVA Continuing Education and Artist Residency Programs present an open-studios event featuring work by residency participants. Wednesday, June 29, 5:00 – 8:00pm. Members of the general public must register to attend and observe SVA COVID-19 protocols; register here. 133/141 West 21st Street, 4th, 8th and 9th floors.

Thursday | “Layers of Our Visible Past” Reception
An exhibition of artwork by MA/MAT Art Education students, curated by department chair Catherine Rosamond. Reception: Thursday, June 30, 5:00pm. Members of the general public must register at least 48 hours in advance to attend the reception and observe SVA COVID-19 protocols; register here. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Last Chance | BFA Interior Design: Built Environments Thesis 2022
An exhibition of thesis work from the BFA Interior Design class of 2022. Through Thursday, June 30, online.

Last Chance | “Body Transfer”
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents photographs and paintings by Ariane Lopez-Huici and BFA Fine Arts faculty member Mary Jones. Through Friday, July 1. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Ongoing | Thesis Exhibition 2022
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents its 2022 thesis exhibition of lens- and screen-based work by 12 students. Through Monday, July 11. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26 Street, 15th floor.
SVA IN THE PRESS
Via Curbed

Dario Calmese in Curbed
Artist, urbanist, director and brand consultant Dario Calmese (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography) was interviewed for Curbed’s “21 Questions” series.

Repair and Healing in the In-Between
SVA faculty member Tobi Kahn’s current solo exhibitions, “Tobi Kahn” and “Formation: Images of the Body,” offer tranquility and optimism in times of isolation and unrest. Via Hyperallergic.

Kembra Pfahler in Artforum
Performer, musician and artist Kembra Pfahler (1982 Fine Arts) talks about the driving forces behind her work in an interview for Artforum.

What Contemporary Queer Photography Looks Like in 2022
Joebert Tupas (MPS 2020 Fashion Photography) is featured in Digital Camera World’s round-up of contemporary LGBTQ+ photographers.

Bill Murphey for Linea
Bill Murphey (BFA 1975 Illustration) was interviewed for The Art Students League of New York’s online journal, Linea.
NYC AND ONLINE PICKS

Brooklyn Poetry Slam
A live and in-person evening of poetry and community with special guests and poets from all over the world. Tuesday, June 28, 6:30pm. Free; RSVP here. The Plaza at 300 Ashland on the corner of Lafayette Avenue and Flatbush Avenue, Brooklyn.

The Leech at Virtual Chattanooga Film Festival
The Chattanooga Film Festival presents virtual premieres of films from around the world, including Eric Pennycoff’s (MPS 2013 Live Action Short Film) feature The Leech. Individual screening tickets: $13. Films are available to watch online until the end of the festival on Wednesday, June 29, 5:00am.

Dos Estaciones Rooftop Film Screening
The Rooftop Films Summer Series presents a screening of Dos Estaciones, a film about an iron-willed businesswoman who fights the impending collapse of her tequila factory. Free. Seating is first-come, first-served. Wednesday, June 29, doors at 7:30pm. Fort Greene Park, Myrtle Avenue and North Portland Avenue entrance, Brooklyn.

It’s a Luxury to Look Back
An exhibition of mixed-media collages, paintings and drawings depicting the human experience by Angela Miskis (BFA 2013 Visual & Critical Studies). Opening reception on Thursday, June 30; RSVP here. Through Friday, July 29. Jamaica Center for Arts and Learning, 161-4 Jamaica Avenue, Queens.

Macy’s 4th of July Fireworks
The 46th annual fireworks show over the East River. This year’s celebration features celebrity performances, live music and more. Monday, July 4, 8:00pm. Free. Learn more about where you can watch the fireworks here.
