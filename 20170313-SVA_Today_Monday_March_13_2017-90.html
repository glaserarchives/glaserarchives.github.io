SVA at SXSW

A rundown of the many SVA community members participating in the 2017 South by Southwest Conference and Festival, happening through Sunday, March 19, in Austin, Texas.
EVENT

7:00pm | Laura Larson and Mark Alice Durant Reading and Book Signing

BFA Photography and Video and Saint Lucy Books present a reading and book signing with Laura Larson, author of the photography book Hidden Mother, and Mark Alice Durant, founder of Saint Lucy Books and author of 27 Contexts: An Anecdotal History in Photography. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Women in Design

Artsy names MFA Products of Design faculty member Paola Antonelli, MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism faculty member Alexandra Lange and BFA Design faculty member Paula Scher as some of the "16 Women Pushing Design Forward in the U.S."
NYC PICKS

Socially Relevant Film Festival

A film festival featuring stories dealing with social issues. Ticket prices vary; purchase here. For a 50% student discount, use code SRFF2017-STU. Through Sunday, March 19. MFA Social Documentary, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor, and other locations.

"Nan Goldin: The Ballad of Sexual Dependency"

An exhibition of 700 snapshot-like portraits sequenced against an evocative music soundtrack, formed out of the artist’s own experiences. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, April 16. The Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

Tribeca Talks: Gillian Anderson and Jennifer Nadel

As part of UN Women HeforShe Arts Week, SVA Theatre presents actor Gillian Anderson and journalist Jennifer Nadel, who will talk about their book We: A Manifesto for Women Everywhere. They will discuss changing how we relate to each other so that we cooperate and connect with compassion. A limited amount of free tickets for the SVA community and their guests (one guest per person) will be available at the door one hour prior to the event, with SVA ID. Tomorrow, March 14, 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

God Knows Where I Am Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue invites you and a guest to their screening of the Hot Docs Jury Prize winner, God Knows Where I Am, followed by a Q&A with directors Todd Wider and Jedd Wider and actress Lori Singer. Free with SVA ID. Tomorrow, March 14, 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Mónica de la Torre Talk and Reading

MFA Art Writing presents Mónica de la Torre, who will give a talk and read from her poetry book The Happy End/All Welcome. Tomorrow, March 14, 6:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

ISO Discussion on Executive Order and Immigration Concerns

SVA's International Student Office will host an information and discussion meeting to provide an overview of the recent political actions about immigration and answer questions. All students, faculty and staff are welcome. Wednesday, March 15, 1:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"Mentors"

An exhibition of works by selected BFA Photography and Video students, inspired by their working relationships with leading members of the New York City arts community. Monday, March 20 – Saturday, April 1. Reception: Thursday, March 23, 6:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Kong: Skull Island in 3D

SVA's Visual Effects Society presents a 3D screening of Kong: Skull Island, featuring Brie Larson, Tom Hiddleston and Samuel L. Jackson. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Saturday, March 25, 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

More Movies Coming Soon

Don't book your tickets just yet! SVA Theatre is going to have several free screenings for the SVA community in the next few weeks. Likely screenings include Power Rangers, Beauty and The Beast, Smurfs: The Lost Village and T2 Trainspotting. Once dates/times are confirmed, information will be included in SVA Today.
