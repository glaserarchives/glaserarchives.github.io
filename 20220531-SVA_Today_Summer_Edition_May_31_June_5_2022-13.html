News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
May 31 – June 5, 2022
Sandra Erbacher

Events in June
Things may be winding down with summer beckoning, but there are still exhibitions and events to enjoy all month long at SVA. This month, see interactive and video installations, sustainable sculpture, mixed-media work, photography and more.
Events

7:00pm | i3 Photo Lecture: Sim Chi Yin
MPS Digital Photography presents Sim Chi Yin, an artist from Singapore whose research-based practice includes photography, moving image, archival interventions and text-based performance, and focuses on history, conflict, memory and extraction. Today, 7:00pm, online via Zoom.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour Thursday, June 2, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Thursday | “Body Transfer”
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of photographs and paintings by Ariane Lopez-Huici and BFA Fine Arts faculty member Mary Jones. Reception on Thursday, June 2, 5:00 – 7:00pm; register here. Thursday, June 2 – Friday, July 1. SVA Flatiron Project Space,133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Thursday | “Re-sourced”
A group exhibition of sculpture, photography and mixed media work by four artists and alumni of SVA’s Artist Residency programs. Reception on Thursday, June 2, 5:00 – 7:00pm; register here. Thursday, June 2 – Thursday, June 9. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Saturday | “Throned”
SVA Continuing Education presents an interactive photo booth by Tiffany Smith (MFA 2015 Photography, Video and Related Media). Saturday, June 4, 2:00 – 6:00pm; register here. Brooklyn Bridge Park, Pier 1, Old Fulton and Furman Street.

Friday | Unearthing Public Art
Madison Square Park Conservancy’s annual symposium, which convenes eminent artists, scholars and curators, will investigate the central role of public art in exploring buried and manufactured histories in civic space. Featured guests include artists Cristina Iglesias, Alice Aycock (faculty, BFA Fine Arts), Maren Hassinger, Alan Michelson and Kennedy Yanko. Free and open to the public. Friday, June 3, 9:30am – 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Collier Schorr for Apple Pride Campaign
Collier Schorr (BFA 1985 Communication Arts) photographed the new “Shot on iPhone” Pride campaign, featuring Apple’s Pride-edition watch accessories.

Print Awards 2022
MFA Design student Jasmine Chan and BFA Design staff and faculty member Joe Newton were winners in this year’s Print Awards.

The Roles of Art and Beauty in Times of Crisis
Artist, MFA Fine Arts faculty member and 2021 commencement speaker Marilyn Minter was interviewed for Harper’s Bazaar on female representation, sex, aging and beauty.

Paula Scher for It’s Nice That’s Ukraine Print Sale

BFA Design faculty member Paula Scher is a participating artist in It’s Nice That’s print sale for Ukraine.
NYC PICKS

“Lacuna”
A group exhibition featuring BFA 2021 Photography and Video alumni Abbey Gilbert and Joni (Yonatan Glam). Through Saturday, July 2. HEREart, 145 Sixth Avenue.

Correspondences
A sculptural installation-performance offering multiple entry points to engage with questions of being, interdependence and coexistence, with sound work by Jeremy D. Slater (MFA 1996 Computer Art). Saturday, June 4, and Sunday, June 5, 11:00am – 9:00pm. Snug Harbor Cultural Center and Botanical Garden, 1000 Richmond Terrace, Staten Island.

Queens Night Market
An open-air night market in Queens featuring up to 100 independent vendors. Every Saturday through August 20. 5:00pm – 12:00am. Flushing Meadows–Corona Park, between Grand Central Parkway and Van Wyck Expressway.

Brooklyn Flea
An open-air market of furniture, vintage clothing, collectibles and antiques vendors, as well as a tightly curated selection of jewelry, art and crafts by local artisans and designers. Saturdays and Sundays through the Summer, 10:00am – 5:00pm. 80 Pearl Street, Brooklyn.
