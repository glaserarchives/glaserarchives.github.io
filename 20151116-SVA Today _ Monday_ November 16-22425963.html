SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Monday, November 16, 2015
MFA Fine Arts alumnus Mike Weiss at his eponymous gallery in Chelsea.
Six Perspectives on Running an Art Gallery
From the current issue of the Visual Arts Journal, six gallerists talk about their work, today's art market and more.
1:00pm: American Textures
The International Student Office presents American Textures, a documentary that follows six creative individuals of black, white and Latino heritage on a journey through diverse communities in the southern United States. A panel discussion with Arnd Wächter of Crossing Borders Education and SVA students will follow. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
5:00pm: Student Senator Open House
VASA presents an open forum for students to ask questions or voice concerns about their departments with student senators. Insomnia Cookies will be available for attendees. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
6:30pm: "Maximum Entropy" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition curated by Mohammad Golabi that explores the effects, conditions and the appearance of decay within the realm of new contemporary architecture. The exhibition features video, installation, sculpture and photography works by Elaine Byrne, Kiyarash Eghtesadi, Keiichi Matsuda and Farideh Sakhayifar. Through Sunday, November 22. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Press pick of the day
Exercising the Muscle of Brand Strategy
MPS Branding alumnus Gena Cuba discusses her experience in the program and her work with brands with PSFK.
NYC pick of the day
Grand Central Holiday Fair
Beginning today at 10:00am, Grand Central's annual holiday fair is open, featuring local artisans and craftmakers offering home goods, art, collectibles and much more. Through Thursday, December 24. Grand Central Station, Vanderbilt Hall, 89 East 42nd Street.
Take Note
Fall Book Sale
The SVA Library presents a fall book sale, featuring books on art, design, criticism and much more. All books are priced $20 and under. Sale hours are 10:00am - 6:00pm, Wednesday, November 18, through Friday, November 20. Credit and debit card purchases only. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
Social Media for Artists Panel Discussion and Book Launch
Angie Wojak, director of SVA Career Development, and Stacy Miller, Ed. D., faculty at Parsons School of Design, authors of the new book Starting Your Career as an Artist: A Guide to Launching a Creative Life (Allworth Press/SVA), will lead a panel discussion on social media tactics for artists. Participants include artist Faith Holland, artist and assistant director of SVA Career Development Anna Ogier-Bloomer and Larry Ossei-Mensah, a cultural critic and co-founder of The Medium. A book signing will follow. Wednesday, November 18, 6:30pm, CUE Art Foundation, 137 West 25th Street, ground floor.
Director's Dialogue with Brett Haley
MPS Directing presents writer and director Brett Haley discussing his most recent feature film, I'll See You In My Dreams, which premiered at the 2015 Sundance Film Festival. Friday, November 20, 4:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.
A Conversation with Shuddhabrata Sengupta
MFA Art Practice, MA Curatorial Practice and MFA Photography, Video and Related Media present Shuddhabrata Sengupta of Raqs Media Collective, based in New Delhi, discussing the role of artists, curators and educators in the collective’s work. Friday, November 20, 7:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Vice: Covering the Undercovered
The SVA Theatre and the Center for Communication present a panel discussion on how Vice Media, through its myriad channels and initiatives, is spotlighting emerging voices from all over the world. Participants include Alex Miller, global head of content, Vice, and Amel Monsur, creative director, Vice. Moderated by Michael Calderone, senior media reporter, The Huffington Post. Admission is free for SVA students and faculty members. General admission tickets are $10. Monday, November 23, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
SVA at PULSE Miami Beach
SVA Galleries will be exhibiting the work of 12 recent graduates from seven departments at this year's PULSE Miami Beach art fair. A limited number of PULSE VIP passes are available for those traveling to Miami. To receive a pass, which admits two to the preview and public exhibition hours, please contact extrelations@sva.edu no later than Tuesday, November 24. If you have work appearing in any of the fairs and would like it promoted by SVA's social media accounts, send the name of the fair and gallery to extrelations@sva.edu.
