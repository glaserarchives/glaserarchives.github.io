SVA BFA Photography and Video Newsletter
Alum Danielle Amy Staif's Photograph on the Cover of Time

Danielle Staif's (2016) "pussy hat" photograph, featured on the February 8th issue of Time,  boldly embodies the powerful resistance of protesters who marched in Washington, DC and in satellite marches across the globe, uniting to form the largest protest in U.S. history. 
 
Tonight at Aperture: 'On Feminism' Panel: Our Bodies, Online 

Tonight's conversation focuses on how women wield images of their bodies online as a tool of power and/ or as sexual objectification, exploring the question of who is allowed to use their body in this way.  Free to the public at 6:30pm. 
Sophomore Ryker Mcinnis Interviewed By Out Magazine

Out Magazine interviewed Ryker about his spoken word films, queer art, and what perspectives he's bringing to art. 
Chair Stephen Frailey's Review of Photo LA for ArtSpace

BFA Photography and Video Department Chair Stephen Frailey reviewed five standouts from 2017 Photo LA fair. 
Alum Devin Yalkin Photographs Trump Protests for Rolling Stone

Yalkin's (2010) black and white photographs depict several different anti-Trump groups protesting on Inauguration Day in Washington, D.C.
Faculty W.M. Hunt Review's Faculty Marcia Lippman's Exhibit
 
Appearing in L'oeil De La Photographie, W.M. Hunt reviews Marcia Lippman's painterly photographs from her current exhibition at the Nailya Alexander Gallery.
Photography Exhibit at Benrubi Gallery

Brooklyn-based photographer, Jude Broughan, exhibits mixed media works, photo collages and more through February 25 at Benrubi Gallery.
Perpetual Revolution: The Image of Social Change Exhibition at ICP

This exhibition, curated by Carol Squires (MPS Fashion Photography Faculty), runs through May 7 and presents critical issues transformed by visual culture including #BlackLivesMatter, gender fluidity, climate change, terrorist propaganda, the right-wing fringe, the 2016 election, and the refugee crisis.
LensCulture Portrait Awards 2017 Call For Entries
 
The fourth annual LensCulture Portrait Awards strive to discover the best and most interesting portrait photography from all over the world. The competition is open until March 7, 2017.
Staff Member and Alum Claire Christerson Participates in Group Show to Benefit Planned Parenthood

Christerson (2015) is participating in an art event hosted by Image Gallery benefiting Planned Parenthood Friday, February 10 at 7pm. 
Faculty Elizabeth Bick in The New Yorker

Elizabeth Bick latest series, "Coda", featuring a muse that she met on the subway, is on display through February 24 at the University of Texas Visual Arts Center, in Austin, TX.
Mentor Paul Moakley Reviews Daniel Cooney Fine Art's (Faculty) The Gay Essay In Time Lightbox

Anthony Friedkin's The Gay Essay, a documentation of gay culture in Los Angles and San Francisco between 1969-1972, is on display at Daniel Cooney Fine Art through March 4. 
Alum Molly Rapp at ACE Hotel

Curated by the Penumbra Foundation, alum Molly Rapp (2014) exhibits work at ACE Hotel Sunday February 5 at 3pm. 
Announcing: BFA Photo and Video's 3D Printer

Students, faculty, and staff can reserve the use of the department's new 3D printer. Located in the 504 digital lab, the 3D printer can be reserved online through the same system that is used for the printers. Want to learn more about 3D printers? Lynda.com has great tutorials. 

Staff Member Max C. Lee Hosts Performances at Re: Art Show

Artist Christopher Clary will perform his Rhizome commissioned work sorry to dump on you like this.zip at staff member Max C Lee's Re: Art Show this Saturday.
