SVA Today
Wednesday, October 4, 2017

Art by the Incarcerated

A Q&A with Lesley Achitoff (MPS 2004 Art Therapy), who with Robert Belgrod (MFA 2016 Art Therapy) curated "Safe Spaces," an exhibition featuring artwork by people incarcerated on Rikers Island. The opening reception takes place tonight, 5:00 – 8:00pm. MPS Art Therapy Project Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
EVENTS

10:00am | SVA Supply Drive for Natural Disaster Relief

Please join SVA Student Affairs and VASA in gathering supplies for the victims of natural disasters in Mexico, Puerto Rico and the Caribbean. Supplies needed include bottled water, diapers and baby wipes, first-aid supplies, toiletries, non-perishable/canned food, batteries, heavy duty trash bags, portable lamps, disinfectant wipes and water purifying tablets. Drop off supplies today – Friday, 10:00am –10:00pm, at the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

6:15pm | A Conversation with Kenny Fries

MFA Design for Social Innovation presents author Kenny Fries, who will discuss In the Province of the Gods, his latest book, which explores topics including cultural and physical difference, sexuality, love, loss, mortality and the ephemeral nature of beauty and art. RSVP here. 6:15 –7:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
PRESS PICK

OppArt: The Art of Protest

The Nation presents OppArt, a new series of artistic dispatches from the front lines of resistance, led by BFA Illustration faculty member Steve Brodner, BFA Cartooning faculty member Peter Kuper and artist Andrea Arroyo.
NYC PICK

Going into Town with Roz Chast

An evening with acclaimed author and New Yorker cartoonist Roz Chast to celebrate the launch of her new graphic memoir, Going into Town: A Love Letter to New York. Tickets $35 – $40; purchase here. 6:30pm. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.

Serendipity

On the occasion of the publication of Prune Nourry's artbook Serendipity, she, Clifford Ross and The New Yorker staff writer Raffi Khatchadourian will reflect on Nourry’s ten years of artistic creation and her many influences. Free. 7:00pm. Albertine Books, 972 Fifth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Dog Therapy

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services hosts a visit from New York Therapy Animals. Students can stop by to de-stress on Friday, October 6, 2:30 – 3:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Guest Lecture: Laura Forlano

MFA Products of Design presents a talk by Laura Forlano, a Fulbright award-winning and National Science Foundation funded scholar, is a social scientist and design researcher. Open to graduate students only. Wednesday, October 11, noon – 2:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Open House

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents their fall 2017 open house, where prospective students can meet the department chair, faculty, staff, current students and alumni, and have their work reviewed. RSVP here. Thursday, October 12, 1:00 – 3:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

Postponed: Andrea Lipps Talk

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism's talk by Andrea Lipps, which was scheduled for yesterday, October 3, has been postponed until Tuesday, November 28, 6:30pm. Lipps is a writer, educator and assistant curator of contemporary design at Cooper Hewitt Smithsonian Design Museum. She will discuss Dutch designer Joris Laarman, in light of his current retrospective at the museum. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
