SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, February 24, 2016
End-of-Year Showcases
SVA Talent Shows are an annual series of exhibitions, presentations, open studios, portfolio reviews and screenings showcasing the next generation of artists and creative professionals. Click through for more information and a full schedule of events and exhibitions.
Opening: "The Student Looking in the Mirror"
An exhibition of self-portraits by first-year drawing students, curated by BFA Fine Arts faculty member Brooke Larsen. Through Saturday, March 19, SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
1:30pm: Smart Classroom Training
SVA Audio Visual Services presents an information session on how to use the College's "smart" classroom technology. Topics covered include classroom equipment, trouble-shooting techniques and technical support information. RSVP to rbarton@sva.edu. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 311.
3:00pm: Challenging All-or-Nothing Thinking
Student Health and Counseling Services presents a discussion on techniques for finding a middle ground and attainable goals for those whose expectations of themselves are either too high or too low. Open to SVA students. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
6:30pm: Romance & Cigarettes
BFA Animation and BFA Film present a screening of Romance & Cigarettes (2005), starring James Gandolfini, Susan Sarandon and Kate Winslet. Director John Turturro will answer questions following the screening. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
Press pick of the day
The Moody Work of Bill Murphy
The BFA Media Arts alumnus speaks with Industry about his signature works that capture decay and transformation.
NYC picks of the day
Open Museums: a Conversation with Thelma Golden, Paulo Herkenhoff and Vasif Kortun
The Institute for Public Knowledge presents three curators discussing the changing role of museums in today's global art capitals. 6:30pm, Parsons School of Design, Starr Foundation Hall, 63 Fifth Avenue.
Young Frankensteins
An exhibition of work that showcases off-kilter and unconventional aesthetic techniques. Reception tonight, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Sunday, April 3, Leslie Heller Workspace, 54 Orchard Street.
Take Note
Billions
The Center for Communication and the SVA Theatre present a screening of "The Deal," an episode from the new Showtime series Billions, starring Paul Giamatti and Damian Lewis. A panel discussion featuring the show's producers will follow. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff; $10 general admission. Wednesday, March 2, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Special iPad Air Deal at SVA Campus Store
For a limited time, the SVA Campus Store is offering a special deal on purchases of in-stock iPad Air and iPad Air 2 models that includes a $25 instant rebate, $20 SVA Campus Store gift card and a free iPad sleeve. Trade in your old device with ENCORE and receive credit toward a new technology purchase. Offer valid through Friday, March 18, SVA Campus Store, 207 East 23rd Street.
Call for Submissions to The Match Factory
The Match Factory, the Humanities and Sciences Department's biannual online literary magazine, is seeking submissions for its spring 2016 issue. Students, faculty and staff may submit poetry, short stories, film scripts, one-act plays, music or film reviews, essays, personal essays, memoirs or any other kind of writing through the website's form or by email to SVA faculty member and Match Factory editor Edwin Rivera at riveramatchfactory@gmail.com. The deadline for submissions is Friday, April 8. The fall 2015 issue is available online.
Image of the day
A bottle from New York Pelagic, a project by faculty member George Boorujy (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) that sets to sea sealed bottles with illustrations of birds inside, was discovered last month on the French coast near Bordeaux, after three years at sea. Follow him on Instagram for more information about New York Pelagic, @georgeboorujy.
