EVENTS

Tuesday | i3 Photo Lecture: Gretchen Grace

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk with photographer, artist and designer Gretchen Grace. Tuesday, May 30, 7:00 – 8:30pm. RSVP for location.

Wednesday | The Made in New York Animation Project Showcase

The Made in New York Animation Project presents a showcase of participants’ animated work, alongside speakers from the Mayor’s Office of Media and Entertainment, The New York City Department of Probation and program graduates. Free and open to the public. RSVPs appreciated, but not required. Wednesday, May 31, 11:30am. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Opening Thursday | “change of he(art)”

MFA Computer Arts presents an exhibition of select thesis projects by more than 20 students from the class of 2023, curated by department advisor and faculty member Lotte Marie Allen and MoMA assistant exhibition designer LJ McNerney. Reception: Thursday, June 8, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Thursday, June 1, through Saturday, June 17. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street.

Thursday | “Amalgam Thread” Reception

SVA Artist Residency Programs presents an exhibition of artwork by four program alumni exploring materials and techniques traditionally associated with crafts. Reception: Thursday, June 1, 5:00 – 7:00pm. Thursday, June 1, through Thursday, June 8. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21 Street.

Friday | Transforming Public Art

Join Madison Square Park Conservancy for their annual symposium, Transforming Public Art, which considers the ways contemporary artists and art world leaders are challenging conventions in public art practice. Speakers include artist Shahzia Sikander, artist and SVA faculty member Sheila Pepe and curators from the Whitney Museum, the Pérez Art Museum and the Smithsonian American Art Museum. Free and open to the public with RSVP. To RSVP, email tmurraycole@madisonsquarepark.org. Friday, June 2, 9:00am – 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Ongoing Exhibitions:

  • •“Milton Glaser: POP,” through Monday, June 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
  • •Animation Celebration Exhibition, through Tuesday, June 6. SVA Flatiron Windows and SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
  • •“Ephemeral Vortex,” through Thursday, June 15, online.

NYC PICKS

Flamin’ Hot Screening

Women in Entertainment presents an advance screening of Flamin’ Hot directed by Eva Longoria. The film follows the true story of how a Frito Lay janitor disrupted the food industry by turning Flamin' Hot Cheetos from a snack into a phenomenon. Tickets are $5; register here. The first five people to register using the promo code “SVA” will get in for free! Wednesday, May 31, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Regal Union Square, 850 Broadway.

“Memento Vivere—What One Wants”

A solo exhibition of paintings and assemblages by South Korean artist Kyung Tae Kim (MFA 2017 Fine Arts), whose main focus is to explore the relationship between objects and spaces by using found objects. Reception: Friday, June 2, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Space 776 Gallery, 37-39 Clinton Street.

Global Mashup: India Meets Mali

Artists representing elements of Indian and Malian cultures will each perform an individual set, then come together to present a cross-cultural musical performance. Student tickets $15. Saturday, June 3, 7:00pm. Flushing Town Hall, 137-35 Northern Boulevard, Queens.

Self-guided LGBTQIA+ History Tours

The NYC LGBT Historic Sites Project documents sites in New York City connected to LGBTQIA+ history and provides educational materials that tell the often untold story of their influence on American history and culture. Explore the map of sites and view their curated themes here.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

SVA Today Begins Weekly Editions

Beginning today, SVA Today will be published on its summer schedule: once a week at the beginning of the week. Submissions to the summer edition of SVA Today must be received by 12:00pm on the last workday of the week to be included in the following week’s newsletter. To submit your event or announcement to SVA Today, click the SVA Today tile in myid.sva.edu or go to allconcern.sva.edu.

Special Campus Store Promos for the Class of 2023

Congratulations to the class of 2023!⁠ Trade in your used or outdated Apple products to earn a SVA Campus Store gift card valid towards the purchase of a new computer. Trade in your devices through tomorrow, May 31, to get $25 (for iPad trade-ins) or $50 (for Mac trade-ins) added to your gift card. A reminder that as alumni you can continue to shop at the SVA Campus Store and take advantage of education pricing for all your future tech orders, and continue to look out for our special promotions and discounts on tech several times a year.

All Staff Art Show: Deadline Extended

SVA staff are invited to participate in the 11th annual All Staff Art Show. Last year, over 45 works by talented staff were featured in the exhibition. Grab a piece from your collection or create something entirely new; all submissions are welcome. Deadline: Monday, June 5.

Career Development Summer Virtual Office Hours

SVA Career Development will hold their virtual office hours monthly during the summer. A career counselor will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Watch for an email the morning you would like to attend to receive access to a Zoom link for that day. Virtual office hour days this summer are: Thursday, June 15, 10:00 – 11:00am; Thursday, July 13, 10:00 – 11:00am; and Thursday, August 17, 10:00 – 11:00am.

Reclaiming Juneteenth, With Jelani Cobb

SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion presents a virtual Juneteenth program with Jelani Cobb, staff writer at The New Yorker as well as Columbia University’s Henry Luce Professor of Journalism and dean of Columbia Journalism School. Tuesday, June 20, 3:00 – 4:30pm, online. Register here.
