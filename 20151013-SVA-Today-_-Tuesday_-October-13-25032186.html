SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, October 13, 2015
Visiting faculty from Cuba's ISA University pose with a group from SVA. Photo by Chris Jessick.
SVA Strengthens Partnerships in Cuba
As part of an effort to take advantage of improving relations between the U.S. and Cuba, SVA recently welcomed to its campus four faculty members from ISA University of the Arts, the Caribbean nation's preeminent arts college.
1:30pm: Dog Therapy
Student Health and Counseling Services and New York Therapy Animals present a special visit from therapy dogs to help students de-stress in between work and classes. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
6:00pm: Dara Birnbaum
Artist and SVA faculty member Dara Birnbaum talks about her work. Presented by MFA Fine Arts. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:15pm: "Crimson Peak" Screening
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of director Guillermo Del Toro's Crimson Peak (2015), starring Mia Wasikowska, Jessica Chastain, Tom Hiddleston and Charlie Hunnam. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:30pm: Michael Kubo: Working Across Platforms
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Boston-based architect, author and curator Michael Kubo discussing the permeable boundaries between writing, curatorial and publishing platforms in design. 132 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Press pick of the day
Kate Gilmore's "Higher Ground," via Site:Lab.
SVA Alumni Win 2015 ArtPrizes
Kate Gilmore (MFA 2002 Fine Arts) won Grand Prize for Higher Ground, and Paul Amenta's (MFA 2000 Fine Arts) Site:Lab won Outstanding Venue at the seventh annual ArtPrize competition.
NYC picks of the day
MONUMENTAL: The Reimagined World of Kevin O'Callaghan
The SVA alum and faculty member discusses his work, currently on display in "MONUMENTAL," before the exhibition's reception at 5:00pm. 3:05pm, Farmingdale State College, Gleeson Hall, Room 104, 2350 Broadhollow Road, Farmingdale, NY.
Global Fashion, Women and the Creative Industries
Designer Reem Acra in conversation with Valerie Steele, director and chief curator of the Museum at FIT. Moderated by Maria Ann Conelli, dean of the School of Visual, Media and Performing Arts at Brooklyn College. Admission is free with RSVP. 7:00pm, The Graduate Center, Proshansky Auditorium, 365 Fifth Avenue.
CMJ Music Marathon
CMJ's 35th annual showcase of up-and-coming musical talent. Venues and ticket prices vary. Click through for detailed schedule. Through October 17.
Take Note
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Information Session
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents an information session featuring department chair Charles Traub discussing the curriculum and application process. Attendees are advised to bring samples of their work. Thursday, October 15, 1:00 - 3:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.
T.J. Demos Live From Caracas
MA Curatorial Practice presents a special live-stream of After Landscape: Perspectives and Traces of the Traveler, a conference from Caracas, Venezuela, featuring art historian and cultural critic T.J. Demos' lecture "Decolonized Nature: Art and Politics of Earth Jurisprudence." Demos' talk begins at 11:30am and will be broadcast in English; the rest of the conference will be in Spanish. Friday, October 16, 11:30am - 3:15pm, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
MFA Design for Social Innovation Open House
MFA Design for Social Innovation presents an up-close-and-personal introduction to the department. Department tours are available from 12:00 - 4:00pm. To schedule, contact Aubrey Hays, ahays3@sva.edu. Friday, October 16, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
