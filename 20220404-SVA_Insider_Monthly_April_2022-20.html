🎙️ Get to Know: Michelle Rossman, associate director of Visitor Services, Admissions

﻿Tell us about your history with SVA and your goals for your new position.

I started in Admissions in 2016 as an undergraduate Admissions coordinator and then as the assistant manager of Undergraduate Admissions, which is when I began interfacing more with my colleagues across SVA. This past summer, I moved from our "back-of-house" to my current position, where I plan our visitor interactions, like tours and open houses. My goal for Visitor Services is to provide a calm, friendly and informative experience for our prospective students. As I always say, we're here to help!


Accepted Students Day is April 9. Can you share anything new you're particularly excited about that’s in the works for this year?

We can't wait to welcome students and their families back to our first in-person ASD since 2019! This year, all guests will start their day at Chelsea Piers where there will be an info fair about student services, a club fair and the chance to meet with our various offices, including Financial Aid and Academic Advisement, before going on to see their individual departments across the College. We hope that [this schedule] helps maximize their time in our state-of-the-art facilities.


What are the biggest challenges when organizing a big event with so many moving parts?

We know and love our urban campus, but conveying that to guests (especially those from out of town) is tricky! We've streamlined the programming with specific tour times and published guests' itineraries on sva.edu/asd to keep everyone on the move and organized.


What is the main takeaway about SVA you'd like students and their families to leave ASD with this year?

My hope is that students and their families feel like they're already a part of our vibrant, creative community. We want them to experience the support we provide to our students and understand that, as a college, we are here to help them realize their artistic dreams. Talking with our current students throughout the day is invaluable and we couldn't do this without our many student representatives.


Anything else you'd like to add?

I want to extend my sincere and deep gratitude to my Admissions team and all of the offices and staff across campus who come together to support not only this event, but each other as an institution. I am so proud to work with you all at SVA. Thank you for all you do.

﻿﻿ Save The Date: Commencement Edition

The 47th annual commencement exercises of the School of Visual Arts will be held Sunday, May 22, 1:00pm ET, at Radio City Music Hall in Manhattan. This event will be the College’s first in-person graduation ceremony since 2019 and renowned writer Roxane Gay will be the keynote speaker. Additionally, a special make-up ceremony celebrating the classes of 2020 and 2021—who observed their events virtually—will be held on Monday, June 27, 1:00pm ET, also at Radio City.
