SVA Today
Wednesday, April 13, 2016
Illustration by Kevin Hsieh.
Poisoned Apples and Smoking Lamps
Don't miss the new BFA Cartooning and BFA Illustration exhibition, featuring visually reinterpreted fairy tales and adventure stories by third-year students. Reception tonight, 6:00 - 8:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor. Through Saturday, April 23.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Bodies at Rest
The Huffington Post highlights the photo series "Flocks" by Nir Arieli (BFA 2012 Photography), which captures dancers piled together and opens at Daniel Cooney Fine Art on Thursday, April 21.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY
Nice Weather
Art journalist Hunter Braithwaite moderates a panel with artists David Salle, Gary Stephan, Patricia Treib and Nicole Wittenberg. 6:00pm, Skarstedt Chelsea, 550 West 21st Street.
Richard Niessen Meets Euclid
Designers Richard Niessen and Craig Welsh discuss ornament, type and history in contemporary graphic design. Tickets are $8 for students, $15 general public. 6:30pm, Cooper Hewitt, Smithsonian Design Museum, 2 East 91st Street.
John Knight and Bettina Funcke
Artist John Knight discusses his work with MA Critical Theory and the Arts faculty member Bettina Funcke. Admission is $5. 7:00pm, Artists Space, 55 Walker Street.

TAKE NOTE
Meet and Eat with Cultural Partner Program
The International Student Office presents students in the Cultural Partner Program discussing the community service projects they completed this semester. Students can learn more about the program and sign up for next year. Food from different cultures will be served. Friday, April 15, 5:30pm, 209 East 23rd Street, Room 302.
Mark Wasiuta: The Making of "Environmental Communications: Contact High"
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Mark Wasiuta discussing the "Environmental Communications: Contact High" exhibition, the first major show of the West coast media collective Environmental Communications. Tuesday, April 19, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Owen Gleiberman
The SVA Library presents former Entertainment Weekly film critic Owen Gleiberman discussing his new book, Movie Freak: My Life Watching Movies. A book signing will follow. Thursday, April 21, 6:30pm, SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
