News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
March 26 – April 1, 2018

Maya Lin to Speak at Commencement 2018

SVA is pleased to announce that artist, designer and environmentalist Maya Lin will be the keynote speaker at this year's commencement exercises.

Mentors 2018

Behind every great artist often lies a wise mentor. BFA Photography and Video presents its annual exhibition, featuring work by fourth-year students that was inspired by their working relationships with leading members of the New York arts community. On view through Tuesday, March 27, at the SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

VASA Stops by Joes@Moe's

In the latest Joes@Moe's, host Zipeng Zhu (BFA 2013 Design) interviews the 2017-2018 Visual Arts Student Association (VASA), the governing body designed to represent the interests and needs of students at SVA.

Off to Vienna

The latest stop for "Underground Images," SVA’s traveling exhibition of its iconic, faculty-designed subway posters, is in Austria, at Vienna's Designforum gallery.
IN THE PRESS

An Alumnus' Imaginary World

Paper interviews Eli Wirija (MFA 2016 Design) about her new photography and short film project Alien Children.

"A Study of the American Psyche"

Forbes on Barnaby Furnas' (BFA 1996 Fine Arts) painting series "Frontier Ballads," on view through Saturday, April 14, at Marianne Boesky, 509 West 24th Street.

Wonder Woman Empowers Women Online

Collier Schorr (BFA 1985 Communication Arts) photographs Wonder Woman star Gal Gadot for Reebok.

Reading Nancy Comics

Dash Shaw (BFA 2005 Illustration) reviews Paul Karasik and Mark Newgarden's (BFA 1982 Media Arts) How to Read Nancy, a recent book that explains how comics work, using a single 1959 comic by Ernie Bushmiller.
EVENTS

On Black Performance

Writer Nana Adusei-Poku, artist NIC Kay, curator and artist Jaamil Olawale Kosoko and choreographer and writer Will Rawls discuss the relationship between the black body, the intimacy of the stage and public space. Friday, March 30, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Third Annual Phil Patton Lecture: Natasha Jen with Khoi Vinh

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a lecture by award-winning designer and SVA alumnus Natasha Jen and designer and writer Khoi Vinh, celebrating the memory of department faculty member Phil Patton. RSVP here. Wednesday, March 28, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

Jackie Greene and Bill Plympton Music Video Extravaganza

SVA Theatre presents the world premiere of musician Jackie Greene and animator Bill Plympton's (1969 Cartooning) new animated short film, The Modern Lives, and a Q&A with both artists. RSVP here. Wednesday, March 28, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

BFA Advertising and BFA Design Spring Exhibition

BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition of ads, campaigns, designs, motion graphics and 3D work by the programs' class of 2018. Through Thursday, April 26. Reception: Tuesday, March 27, 5:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
