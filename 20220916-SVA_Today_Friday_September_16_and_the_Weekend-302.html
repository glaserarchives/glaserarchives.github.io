12:00pm | SVA Club Fair

Clubs are a fantastic way to make friends, discover new interests and get involved on campus. Explore SVA’s clubs at the club fair today, September 16, 12:00 – 2:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Today | “Enduring Heritage”

For more than a decade, the School of Visual Arts and the Valetudo Association have held a series of public exhibitions of inspiring artwork, ranging from painting and photography to animation and illustration. This year, as every year, SVA and Valetudo celebrate the restorative power of art, and the joy that comes from experiencing it with others. See the works in SVA’s online gallery here. Through Friday, December 30. Online and at Saint-Rémy, Saint-Paul, 13210 Saint-Rémy-de-Provence, France.

NYC PICKS

“Dial Tone”

A solo exhibition of work by Dana Robinson (MFA 2019 Fine Arts). Through Sunday, October 9. A.I.R. Gallery, 155 Plymouth Street, Brooklyn.

Saturday Afternoon Cartoons: Back to School

SVA faculty member Tommy José Stathes hosts a showcase of early school-themed cartoons spanning the 1910s through the 1950s. Tickets: $17. Saturday, September 17, 1:30pm. Metrograph, 7 Ludlow Street.

One Earth Young Filmmakers Contest Screening

The One Earth Film Festival presents a screening of the 10 best short films from the past 10 years of the festival, including Verge (2018) by Chingtien Chu (MFA 2017 Computer Art). Free. Sunday, September 18, 11:30am – 1:00pm, online.

Carnaval De La Cultura Latina

For over 15 years, the Carnaval De La Cultura Latina has been held during the first week of Hispanic Heritage Month in Corona, Queens, offering a wide variety of vendors, arts and crafts, music, food and more. Free. Sunday, September 18, 3:00 – 10:00pm. 3700-06 Junction Boulevard, Queens.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Constitution Day 2022

Saturday, September 17, is Constitution Day, a day of federal observance which commemorates the day in 1787 when the U.S. Constitution was signed at the Constitutional Convention in Philadelphia. To learn more about the U.S. Constitution visit the U.S. Senate website, or pick up a free pocket-sized edition at SVA Student Affairs, 340 East 24th Street.
