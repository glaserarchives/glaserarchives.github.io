SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, November 10, 2015
Shred Til You're Dead
SVA Close Up spoke with Kathleen Hayes (BFA 1997 Photography) about her MPS Digital Photography thesis project, which focuses on the middle-aged skateboarding community. Hayes' series of photos is currently on view in "Fuse," at the SVA Gramercy Gallery through Saturday, November 14.
11:30am: Health and Wellness Fair
The Office of Student Affairs, the Yoga Club and the Soccer Club present tips and tricks to leading a healthy lifestyle. 11:30 - 3:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
6:00pm: John C. Welchman on Mike Kelley
MFA Fine Arts presents University of California, San Diego, Professor of Art History, Theory and Criticism John C. Welchman discussing artist Mike Kelley's contributions to contemporary art. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:00pm: PMDA Salutes Photographers Who Give Back
The School of Visual Arts, PDN magazine and the Photoimaging Manufacturers and Distributors Association (PMDA) honor three professional photographers and one student photographer who have used their talents to give back. The special event features presentations by honorees Tamara Lackey, Colby Brown, Ted Kawalerski and the student recipient of the Anthony M. Ruotolo Photojournalism Award. RSVP at rsvptopmda.com. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:30pm: MFA Art Practice Information Session
MFA Art Practice presents an information session and facilities tour for prospective students, with department chair David A. Ross, faculty, students and alumni. 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.
6:30pm: MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism Information Session
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents an information session for prospective students. Hear from students, alumni and faculty about how they use critical research techniques in their work, and about the value of research and writing in design practice, curation, education and publishing. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
7:00pm: Haley Morris Cafiero
Haley Morris Cafiero, artist and associate professor and assistant dean at Memphis College of Art, discusses her “Wait Watchers” photographs, which are featured in a new monograph, The Watchers, published by The Magenta Foundation. Presented by MPS Digital Photography. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
7:00pm: Sanya Kantarovsky
BFA Fine Arts presents New York-based artist Sanya Kantarovsky discussing the historical influences, graphic design corollaries and narratives that are referenced in his paintings. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
Press pick of the day
Masks and Memory
Blouin Artinfo profiles Rosson Crow (BFA 2004 Fine Arts) and her new exhibition of paintings, "Madame Psychosis Holds a Seance," inspired by Beverly Oliver and the Kennedy assassination, which opened earlier this month at Honor Fraser Gallery, Los Angeles.
NYC picks of the day
MIX NYC
The 28th annual Queer Experimental Film Festival featuring performances, installations and screenings. Ticket prices are $13 for individual events. Through Sunday, November 15. MIX Factory, 155 26th Street, Brooklyn.
The Witch of Lime Street
This illustrated lecture features the enthralling story of Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, Harry Houdini and the so-called Witch of Lime Street, whose lives collided in an era when science verged on embracing the paranormal. Admission is free. 6:30pm, Mid-Manhattan Library, 455 Fifth Avenue.
Take Note
Will Eisner: Celebrating 75 years of The Spirit
The School of Visual Arts Alumni Society, in partnership with the Will and Ann Eisner Family Foundation, presents Pulitzer Prize-winning cartoonist and author Jules Feiffer, along with former publisher and president of DC Comics, Paul Levitz, discussing the legacy of pioneering comic artist and longtime SVA faculty member, Will Eisner. Tomorrow, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MPS Directing Information Session
MPS Directing presents an information session for prospective students. Tour the department and learn more about the one-year intensive program from department chair Bob Giraldi and students. Thursday, November 12, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.
MFA Fine Arts Information Session
MFA Fine Arts presents an information session for prospective students. Tour the department and its facilities, and meet with department chair Mark Tribe, faculty, students and alumni. Saturday, November 14, 2:00 - 4:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.
Image of the day
An illustration by faculty member Yuko Shimizu (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay) from Michael Cunningham's A Wild Swan, which was released today by Farrar, Straus and Giroux.
