SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts. Week of April 11 - 17, 2016
FEATURES
The Dusty Film & Animation Festival Alumni Honorees Are...
Animator and historian Tom Sito (BFA 1977 Animation) and Academy Award-winning production sound mixer Mark Ulano (1975 Film and Video) will be honored at the 27th Dusty Film & Animation Festival with Outstanding Alumni Awards. The festival will take place Saturday, May 7, through Tuesday, May 10, at the SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Meet the Newest Curators
MA Curatorial Practice is hosting 10 exhibitions curated by its first graduating class, ranging from a cabaret performance in the West Village to a tribute to the ocean at the National Aquarium in Baltimore. Click through for the full schedule.
Study Digital Illustration in Berlin!
SVA Destinations offers a two-week trip (June 6 - 17) to study digital illustration in Berlin, a haven for local and international emerging artists and one of Europe's most dynamic cities. Participants will learn digital illustration techniques from daily lectures and visits to industry leading studios, agencies and gaming companies.
IN THE PRESS
Photo by Joy Asico.
Fighting to Make Comics Diverse
The Washington Post profiles comics artist Shawn Martinbrough (BFA 1993 Illustration), who left Batman behind to take ownership of his work.
Documenting Personal History
Juxtapoz highlights two recent series by Reuben Negron (MFA 2004 Illustration as Visual Essay), "Dirty Dirty Love" and "This House of Glass," which capture intimate moments with his models.
On the Rise, From North Dakota to New York
The Source interviews photographer Tory Rust (MPS 2015 Fashion Photography) about her burgeoning career.
EVENTS
Watch Live: MFA Design Thesis Forum
MFA Design presents design entrepreneurs premiering their unique products in an interactive public forum. This event will stream live at design.sva.edu/venture16. Tuesday, April 12, 1:00 - 7:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
SHEROES: She, Hero, Eros
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents artists Henrietta Mantooth, Joan Snyder and Betty Tompkins discussing their work and how the status of women has—and has not—changed in the art world over their 50-year careers. Monday, April 11, 6:30pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Vincent Cianni
MPS Digital Photography presents documentary photographer Vincent Cianni discussing his work, which explores community, memory and social justice. His most recent project is the "Gays in the Military" series. Tuesday, April 12, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
