Events

AASU Postcard Drive
The SVA Asian American Student Union (AASU) invites the SVA community to write and decorate postcards this week in support of the Ain't I A Woman?! campaign protesting 24-hour workdays for local home-care workers. Postcards will be available at both the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, and SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, through tomorrow, February 18.

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour today, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:00pm | Room Selection Housing Tours
If you are a current SVA student who is interested in living in the 24th Street, Gramercy or Ludlow residences for the coming academic year, stop by for one of Residence Life's housing tours, taking place every day this week on a rolling basis from 12:00 – 4:00pm.

To connect with a tour of the 24th Street and Gramercy residences, visit the Residence Life office, 340 East 24th Street, between 12:00 – 4:00pm. To join a tour of the Ludlow Residence, stop by the security desk at 101 Ludlow Street during those hours. Be sure to complete and show your eligible Cleared4 badge on your phone to the security officer at either location. For more information, visit sva.edu/residencelife.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm ET, SVA's International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to "International Student Office Staff" headline).

Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu if you have any questions.

12:30 and 7:30pm | Resident Assistant Information Sessions
Interested in being an RA this summer or next academic year? Attend one of the mandatory information sessions this week to learn more about the position and receive the application materials. Attendance at one information session is mandatory to receive the application materials. All sessions will be hosted on Zoom. Today, 12:30 and 7:30pm ET, and tomorrow, February 18, 10:00am ET.

5:30pm | In Their Own Words: The Legacy of Black and African American Artists
In honor of Black History Month, this intergenerational panel discussion will feature Storm Ascher (BFA 2018 Visual & Critical Studies), artist, curator and entrepreneur; Michael Barclay lll (student, BFA Design), vice president of the SVA Black Student Union; Rob Fields, director of the Sugar Hill Children’s Museum of Art and Storytelling; Dr. Georgette Grier Key, director of the Eastville Historical Society; TJ Sterling, comics creator and entrepreneur; and designer, writer and educator Michele Washington (MFA 2011 Design Criticism). Presented by Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA. Tonight, 5:30pm ET, online. Register here.

8:00pm | Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American and Xenophobic Harassment (Available in Korean)
SVA Student Health and Counseling Services; Diversity, Equity and Inclusion; and the International Student Office present this free, 75-minute interactive training session, hosted by Hollaback, on how to safely intervene when witnessing or experiencing street harassment. Learn Hollaback’s "Five Ds of Bystander intervention," a clear, adaptable and expert-approved set of tools that have been proven to reduce the prevalence of street harassment. Korean translation will be available. Tonight, 8:00 – 9:15pm ET, online. Register here.

in town and online

The NYC Parks Renaming Project: Celebrating Black Leaders
This exhibition, presented by the New York City Department of Parks and Recreation, presents historical and contemporary photography and texts to highlight some of the local parks and park features that have been renamed to honor the Black experience in the city. Through Monday, February 28. Arsenal Gallery, 64th Street and Fifth Avenue in Central Park, 3rd floor.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Call | Submit to Visual Opinion
The deadline for submissions for Visual Opinion's next issue, organized on the theme of "utopia and dystopia," is today, 5:00pm ET. Send your work to vo@sva.edu with the subject line formatted as "firstname_lastname_artworkname." Include in your email your name, the title of your piece and your major and anticipated graduation year. Contributors must be matriculated students and located in the U.S.

This Week | Returning Student Room Selection
Are you a current SVA student interested in living in College housing for the 2022 – 2023 year? Returning student room selection is taking place this week. Current residential students can find details about the process in their email. Eligible students who are interested in reserving housing for next year can email reslife@sva.edu or click here.

Tomorrow | Beyond Representation: Becoming a Leader in the Industry, With Hsiang Chin Moe
SVA's Asian American Student Union and Women In Animation will host a talk with BFA Animation Chair Hsiang Chin Moe (MFA 2008 Computer Art) about her experiences and the challenges of being a leader in the animation industry, a field in which women of color are underrepresented, particularly in leadership roles. Tomorrow, February 18, 5:00pm ET, online. Register here.

Next Week | Yoga for Artists: Poses to Help You Sleep Better
Join SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and instructor Melissa Boo for a free, beginner-friendly, 45-minute virtual yoga class focusing on improving sleep habits and night routines. Learn yoga poses that may help improve the quality of your sleep, reduce stress and encourage relaxation. Tuesday, February 22, 11:00am ET. Registration is required; RSVP here.

Next Week | BHM Campus Dialogue: The Aftermath of the Black Lives Matter Cases
In honor of Black History Month, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA will host a talk next week with Gloria Browne-Marshall, playwright, constitutional-law professor and civil rights lawyer, about the aftermath and impact of recent Black Lives Matter-related trials. This will be a dinnertime discussion, offering SVA community members the opportunity to talk about topics relating to the cases and their impact on the nation, as well as time for questions and answers, while eating their favorite dish. Tuesday, February 22, 5:00pm ET, online. Register here.
