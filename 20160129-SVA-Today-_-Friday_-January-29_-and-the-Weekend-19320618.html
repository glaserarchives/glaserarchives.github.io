SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, January 29, 2016, and the Weekend
Image by Jae Eun Seok (MPS 2015 Fashion Photography).
The Future of Fashion Photography
In connection with the MPS Fashion Photography 2015 Graduate Exhibition, the department presents a panel discussion featuring Petra Collins, photographer and filmmaker, Stephen Frailey, department co-chair, Karen Kaiser, stylist, and Ivan Shaw, faculty member and photography director, Vogue. Saturday, January 30, 2:00pm, Milk Gallery, 450 West 15th Street.
2:00pm: Chronicle Books Design Fellowship Information Session
SVA Career Development presents representatives from Chronicle Books in San Francisco discussing their Design Fellowship Program for graphic and industrial designers. Open to current SVA fourth-year students, graduate students and alumni who graduated in 2014 and 2015. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
6:00pm: "Crushing Debt" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work from different art practices on the theme of student loan debt. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Jovanna Venegas. Through Saturday, February 6, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
6:30pm: Race in My Face as a Clinician: How Issues of Cultural Identity Are Magnified in Private Practice
MPS Art Therapy presents Diana Gil-Velez and Sandra Ramos-Watt of CATs of Color discussing how issues of race manifest in their clinical work in private practice. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Press pick of the day
The Romance of Cuba
MPS Digital Photography faculty member and department alum Jaime Permuth spoke with American Photography about the growing popularity of Cuba as a photography destination.
NYC Weekend Picks
Artists on Artworks with Peter Hristoff
The faculty member, alum and current artist-in-residence at the Metropolitan Museum of Art discusses works in the museum's collection that have personal and artistic relevance. Tonight, 6:30pm, Metropolitan Museum of Art, Gallery 534, 1000 Fifth Avenue.
Dr. Strangelove
A screening of director Stanley Kubrick's classic 1964 black comedy set during the Cold War. Admission is pay-as-you-wish. Seating is first-come, first-served, Tonight, doors at 6:00pm and screening at 7:00pm, New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West.
Postcards from the Edge
Visual AIDs presents an exhibition and benefit sale of postcard-sized works by emerging and established artists. Saturday, January 30, 10:00am - 6:00pm, and Sunday, January 31, noon - 4:00pm, 530 West 22nd Street.
Take Note
"Harlem First" Sympsium
MFA Design for Social Innovation presents a symposium on the "Harlem First" initiative, featuring Rob Carmona, Carmen Quinones, Dr. Prabhjot Singh, director, Arnhold Global Health Institute, New York City District Attorney Cyrus R. Vance Jr. and politician Clyde Williams. Monday, February 1, 6:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Jamie Isenstein
MFA Fine Arts presents Brooklyn-based multimedia artist Jamie Isenstein discussing her work. Isenstein is the 2013 Creative Time Sandcastle Competition Champion. Tuesday, February 2, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Student Leadership Series: Leadership Positions on Campus
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents an information session for students interested in becoming a resident assistant, orientation leader or VASA member. All attendees will be entered into a $20 gift card raffle. Light refreshments will be served. Tuesday, February 2, 6:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
