Eve Sonneman

SVA’s Women-Designed Subway Posters

In celebration of Women’s History Month, SVA Galleries presents a special highlights exhibition from “Underground Images” on view at SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street, through Tuesday, March 28.

EVENTS

6:30pm | Bridging Time and Space: The Collaborative Photographic Practice

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a talk by artists Isaac Diggs (faculty, BFA Photography and Video) and Edward Hillel on their collaborative work offering a nuanced and penetrating look at the contemporary urban environment in America. Tonight, March 21, 6:30 – 8:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.

6:30pm | What the Hell Happened to Blood, Sweat & Tears? Screening

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of What the Hell Happened to Blood, Sweat & Tears?, a political thriller with a classic rock band at the heart of the action. The film involves the U.S. State Department, the Nixon White House, the governments of Yugoslavia, Romania and Poland and documentary footage that has been suppressed for over 50 years by one or all of the above. Watch the trailer here. There will be a Q&A with director John Scheinfeld after the screening. Open to SVA community members and their guests only. RSVP here. Tonight, March 21, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | i3 Photo Lecture: Joni Sternbach

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk with American artist, photographer and filmmaker Joni Sternbach (BFA 1977 Photography). Tonight, March 21, 7:00 – 8:30pm. RSVP here for location.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Chris Jordan/Simon & Schuster

“Black Panther, Fisher-Price Vets Team Up For Judge Kim Book Series”

Forbes takes a look at how comic, video game and toy industry veterans—including Joseph Illidge (BFA 1990 Media Arts), Christopher Jordan (BFA 1993 Illustration), Shawn Martinbrough (BFA 1993 Illustration) and Milo Stone (BFA 1996 Illustration)—came together to create the new graphic novel series, Judge Kim and the Kids’ Court.

NYC PICK

“Wangechi Mutu: Intertwined”

A major solo exhibition of work by Wangechi Mutu exploring the legacies of colonialism, globalization and African and diasporic cultural traditions. Free admission with SVA ID. Through Sunday, June 4. The New Museum, 235 Bowery.
