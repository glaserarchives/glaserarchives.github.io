SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, October 16, 2015
>From MPS Digital Photography student Jane Sheng's series "Awakening."
Opening: "Fuse"
MPS Digital Photography presents an exhibition of work by members of its class of 2015. Through Saturday, November 14, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Taking a Closer Look at "Hi Rez/Lo Rez"
Closing tomorrow, SVA Close Up takes a look at the work on display in the BFA Fine Arts show, curated by department chair Suzanne Anker, on view at the SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Noon: Shelly Ni on Designing Public Services
MFA Interaction Design presents Shelly Ni (MFA 2014 Interaction Design) discussing her experience designing public services for government as a Code for America fellow. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
Noon: T.J. Demos Live from Caracas
MA Curatorial Practice presents a special live-stream of After Landscape: Perspectives and Traces of the Traveler, a conference from Caracas, Venezuela, featuring art historian and cultural critic T.J. Demos' lecture "Decolonized Nature: Art and Politics of Earth Jurisprudence." Demos' talk begins at noon and will be broadcast in English; the rest of the conference will be in Spanish. noon - 3:15pm, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
6:00pm: MFA Design for Social Innovation Open House
MFA Design for Social Innovation presents an up-close-and-personal introduction to the department. Department tours are available from noon - 4:00pm. To schedule, contact Aubrey Hays, ahays3@sva.edu. 136 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
Saturday: SVA Open House
BFA Advertising, BFA Animation, BFA Design and BFA Fine Arts welcome prospective students to an open house and information session. All presentations will take place at the SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street, except BFA Fine Arts, which will present at 335 West 16th Street. Click through for a full itinerary of events. Saturday, October 17, 9:00am - 3:00pm.
Saturday: MPS Art Therapy Information Session
MPS Art Therapy presents an information session featuring faculty, students and alumni discussing their experiences in the program and answering questions. Saturday, October 17, noon - 2:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
Press pick of the day
The Power of a Logo
MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman in Wired on how Playboy's iconic logo and brand will long outlast their transition away from nudity.
NYC weekend picks
PrintFest
International Print Center New York presents a two-day event featuring graduate and undergraduate work from schools across the New York area, including SVA. Admission is free. Today, 3:00 - 8:00pm, and Saturday, October 17, noon - 6:00pm, Rogue Space, 508 West 26th Street, 9th floor.
"Young Frankenstein" Screening
Free screening of Mel Brooks' classic 1974 spoof of Frankenstein and horror movies, starring Gene Wilder, Madeline Kahn, Cloris Leachman, Peter Boyle and Terri Garr. Tonight, 6:30pm, Brooklyn War Memorial in Cadman Park Plaza, Brooklyn.
"Poorly Drawn Lines" Book Launch
Reza Farazmand's popular webcomic is now a book featuring fan favorites, new comics and original essays. Admission is free. Tonight, 7:00pm, powerHouse Arena, 37 Main Street, Brooklyn.
Open House New York
Many of New York's historical landmarks and cultural institutions open their doors for free tours, including the Cathedral Church of St. John the Divine, the Morgan Library and Museum and the Ukrainian Institute of America. Locations vary. Saturday, October 17, and Sunday, October 18.
Intimacy in Discourse: Reasonable Sized Paintings
Part one of a two-part exhibition curated by Brooklyn Rail editor and SVA faculty member Phong Bui that explores how various painting sizes affect artists and viewers. The second part, "Unreasonable Sized Paintings," will open at the SVA Chelsea Gallery in November. Sunday, October 18, through December 22, Mana Contemporary, 888 Newark Avenue, Jersey City.
Take Note
Mindfulness Workshop: Tolerating Difficult Emotions
Student Health and Counseling Services presents a workshop on practical mindfulness based techniques that will help reduce the impact of negative thought patterns and address one's ability to face and tolerate difficult emotions. This is the second of three mindfulness workshops. Attendance at the first is not required to participate in this workshop. Open to SVA students. RSVP to Navah Steiner, nsteiner@sva.edu. Wednesday, October 21, 3:15pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 413F.
VASA Halloween Party
VASA and WSVA Radio present their annual Halloween party with carnival games, prizes, a costume contest and an all-night dance party. Tickets are $10. Open to SVA students (one non-SVA guest allowed per student). Thursday, October 22, 7:30pm - midnight, Metropolitan West, 639 West 46th Street.
Ghouls Night In
VASA presents a night of Halloween-themed activities. Snack on mud and worms (pudding, Oreos and candy), play apple-cider pong and giant Jenga or get your face painted. There will also be a special movie screening. Friday, October 23, 7:00 - 9:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
VASA Fright Fest at Six Flags
Join VASA on a trip to Six Flags Great Adventure in Jackson, New Jersey, for Fright Fest. Use password "sixflags2015" to purchase $25 general admission tickets, including transportation. The bus will leave promptly at 10:45am from the SVA Student Center at 217 East 23rd Street, and depart from Six Flags at 8:30pm. Sunday, October 25.
