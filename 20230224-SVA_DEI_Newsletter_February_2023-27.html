Dear SVA Community,

Happy New Year! My name is Dr. Jarvis M. Watson, and I am the inaugural Director of the Office of Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion.  For the past two years, the DEI Office has been serving the needs of SVA’s students, faculty, employees, and alumni. Within these two years, the DEI Office has accomplished many achievements regarding programming, strategic planning, and community engagement.  In an effort to be more transparent, and highlight past and current accomplishments, our office will publish the monthly DEI Newsletter.  The DEI Newsletter will provide the following:
• Updates with DEI Strategic Planning
• Topics and Issues related to DEI
• Departmental initiatives and accomplishments
• SVA community member highlight 

DEI Strategic Planning Updates

Despite the challenges associated with Covid-19, The DEI Task Force continued to meet and develop ideas and strategies for systemic and immediate change at SVA.  I would also like to recognize the contributions of our students for developing a list of demands that hold us accountable for creating a just, equitable, diverse, and inclusive campus climate.  Listed below are updates regarding our strategic planning, as well as some initiatives that have and are currently taking place. 

DEI Task Force
Mission Statement: The SVA DEI Task Force seeks to create a just, brave and supportive environment for all campus community members (Students, Staff, Faculty, Alumni/ae, Professional network, vendors, surrounding campus, and greater SVA community), where they are able to thrive and succeed.  The DEI Task Force will align its mission and initiatives with the SVA’s mission, strategic goals and core values. 

Vision: The DEI Task Force will accomplish this mission by seeking out to achieve the following long-term and short-term goals.

Long Term: Within the next 2 to 4 years, SVA will become a more JEDI campus when we implement an action plan that:

• Implements recruitment, hiring, and promotion strategy(ies)
• Establishes education, training, and professional development
• Develops culturally relevant curriculum and inclusive Pedagogy 
• Incorporates assessment, accountability, and transparency Strategies 
• Develops communication and outreach
• Resource allocation for DEI initiatives
• Recruitment/Admissions/Access

Short Term: Within the next 3 to 4 semesters, SVA will become a more JEDI campus when we implement an action plan that:

• Strengthens the relationship between students and professionals (faculty
        and employees)
• Becomes intentional with communication being diverse, equitable,
and inclusive
• Establishes a series of campus collaborations and dialogues
• Creates a functional virtual space for DEI online resources
• Improves sustainable external relationships with cultural centers and   underserved communities

SVA Portraits of Identity

SVA Portraits of Identity, Yiran Sun: Portraits of Identity is a video series lifting up voices from SVA's diverse, global and multicultural community. Hear from a range of students, staff and faculty, each invited to share aspects of their lived experience at SVA and to talk about what it's like to be a part of our inclusive learning community in New York City.
