Wednesday, January 27, 2016
Installation view of "Semi-Quasi-Bower Recreational," an exhibition by Robert Melee (BFA 1990 Fine Arts).
2016 Winter Exhibition Highlights
SVA Close Up showcases several alumni exhibitions currently on view in New York City that explore everything from prehistoric cultures to the psychology of the everyday.
6:00pm: Student Leadership Series, Collaboration in Art
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office and Color Flow present an exhibition of work created by members of SVA student clubs. All attendees will be entered into a $20 gift card raffle. Refreshments will be served. Open to SVA students. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
7:00pm: Adam Peacock
The Visible Futures Lab welcomes its newest artist-in-residence Adam Peacock, who will discuss his work. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.
Press pick of the day
MFA Social Documentary Film student Daysi Burbano.

Documentary magazine profiles the formation and evolution of the MFA Social Documentary Film program.
NYC picks of the day
Lisa Oppenheim and Sara VanDerBeek
The two artists discuss their work on view in "Photo-Poetics: An Anthology." Free for students with RSVP. $15 general admission. 6:30pm, Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, 1071 Fifth Avenue.
Tom Hart and Keith Mayerson
The two faculty members discuss Hart's newly published graphic memoir Rosalie Lightning (St. Martin's Press). 7:00pm, Barnes & Noble, 2289 Broadway.
Take Note
Art History in Southern France Information Session
SVA Destinations presents an information session about this summer's two-week trip (May 27 - June 9) through the art, history and culture of Provence, including visits to many of the sites where Paul Cézanne and Vincent van Gogh lived and worked. Today at 4:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 405C.
SVA Resident Assistant Information Sessions
SVA Residence Life is seeking RAs for the summer and 2016 - 2017 academic year. Interested candidates must attend an information session to apply. Sessions will be held on Monday, February 1, noon, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street; Monday, February 1, 10:00pm, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine; Tuesday, February 2, 3:30pm, SVA Student Center; Wednesday, February 3, 6:00pm, SVA Student Center; Thursday, February 4, 10:00am, SVA Student Center; and Friday, February, 5, 12:30pm, SVA Student Center. Contact raselection@sva.edu for more information.
Remember
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents director Atom Egoyan's Remember (2015), starring Academy Award winners Christopher Plummer and Martin Landau. Producer Robert Lantos will answer questions following the screening. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). RSVP required. Tuesday, February 2, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Taxi Driver
A 40th anniversary screening of Martin Scorsese's Taxi Driver (1976), starring Robert De Niro, Jodie Foster, Cybill Shepherd and Harvey Keitel. Free and open to the public. Searing is first-come, first-served. Monday, February 8, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
