SVA Today
July 23 – 29, 2018

New Inductees to SVA Subway Series Hall of Fame

The latest SVA Subway Series video features MFA Illustration as Visual Essay Chair Marshall Arisman and writer Dee Ito, both of whom are frequent contributors to SVA's subway poster series.

SVA at San Diego Comic-Con

Last weekend, several SVA animators, illustrators and authors, including Molly Ostertag (BFA 2014 Cartooning), Nate Powell (BFA 2000 Cartooning) and Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation), celebrated all things comics at Comic-Con International: San Diego.

Before and After SVA: Miwa Sakulrat

Miwa Sakulrat (BFA 2017 Film) talks about her career, how she has used her network and what advice she has for incoming film students.
THIS WEEK

Tuesday | Edgar Arceneaux

MFA Art Practice presents a talk by artist Edgar Arceneaux. Tuesday, July 24, 12:30 – 2:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Wednesday | MFA Art Practice and Summer Residency Program Open Studios

Featuring new work from residents in the Sculpture, Installation, New Media and Techno-Ceramics summer residency and MFA Art Practice students. Wednesday, July 25, 6:00 – 9:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, lower level and 1st, 2nd, 4th and 5th floors.

Wednesday | 40 Years in the Making: The Magic Music Movie

A preview screening of the documentary 40 Years in the Making: The Magic Music Movie, followed by a Q&A with the film's director Lee Aronsohn. Free; three guest limit per required RSVP. Wednesday, July 25, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Thursday | "Constellations" Reception

SVA presents a juried exhibition of works by SVA students who utilized the multiplicities within identity to create personal yet socially engaged work. Thursday, July 26, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, August 11. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Thursday | MFA Visual Narrative Information Session

Come and hear about the low-residency MFA Visual Narrative program from faculty and students at this informal information session. RSVP here. Thursday, July 26, 6:00 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 631F.

Thursday | Puzzle

A preview screening of the film Puzzle, followed by a Q&A with the film's star Kelly MacDonald, director Marc Turtletaub and producers Peter Saraf and Wren Arthur. Free; three guest limit per required RSVP. Thursday, July 26, 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Thursday | Help Yourself: Participatory Walk

Join visual and performance-based artist Todd Shalom for a participatory walk on the High Line that encourages participants to poetically respond to their pasts and the current local climate via prompts from poetry, performance and sound art. RSVP here. Thursday, July 26, 7:00 – 8:00pm. The High Line, Gansevoort/Washington Street entrance.

Friday | MFA Visual Narrative Open Studios and Print Slam

MFA Visual Narrative presents its annual extravaganza of storytelling by its first- and second-year students, including a Print Slam sale of work created on the Risograph. RSVP here. Friday, July 27, 6:00 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Saturday | The Equalizer 2

SVA Theatre presents a screening of The Equalizer 2, starring Denzel Washington. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Saturday, July 28, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Celebrating fine dining in NYC at an affordable price. Two-course lunches and three-course dinners, $26 – $42. Through Friday, August 17. See participating restaurants here.

Thomas Bayrle: Playtime

An exhibition highlighting Thomas Bayrle's experiments across media and their prescient commentary on the relationship between consumerism, technology, propaganda and desire. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, September 2. The New Museum, 235 Bowery.

Rockaway! 2018: Narcissus Garden by Yayoi Kusama

MoMA PS1 presents Yayoi Kusama's site-specific installation of Narcissus Garden as the third iteration of the free public art festival Rockaway! Through Monday, September 3. Gateway National Recreation Area, Fort Tilden, Queens.
