SVA Today
July 25 - 31
FEATURES
BFA Photography and Video student Lauren Berthelot on Trolltunga cliff in Odda, Norway.

SVA Around the Globe

SVA students and recent alumni talk about their experiences studying abroad in Switzerland and Norway, as well as the SVA in Rome exchange program.

Behind the Scenes of Vice Principals

Cinematographer Michael Simmonds (BFA 2000 Film and Video) discusses working on the new HBO series, starring Danny McBride and Walter Goggins.

The Beat Goes On

SVA recently announced "The Beat Goes On," an exhibition celebrating the intersection between art and music, with four distinct listening rooms by Elia Alba, Kevin Beasley, Paul D. Miller (DJ Spooky) and Tameka Norris (Meka Jean). Curated by visual artist Derrick Adams, "The Beat Goes On" opens at the SVA Chelsea Gallery on Saturday, August 20.

Before and After SVA: Dana Davenport

The BFA Photography alumnus talks about creating socially charged work, balancing her work life and art life, what attracted her to New York City, and more.

John Irving on the Cover Design for In One Person

The author and Jackie Seow (BFA 1984 Media Arts), executive art director at Simon & Schuster, discuss the cover design for his 2012 novel.

NYC PICKS OF THE WEEK

Ruth's Dream

An exhibition of large-scale photographs of flowers by Ruth Litoff. A documentary based on Litoff was produced and edited by MFA Social Documentary Film faculty members Beth Levison and Toby Shimin, respectively. Through August 7, Bellevue Hospital, South Lobby, 462 First Avenue.

Facade Work Continues at 205, 209 East 23rd Street

The project continues on schedule, weather permitting. Facade work is 95% complete on the east and north (back) sides of the building. Within the next two weeks pipe scaffolding on the east and north sides will begin to be taken down. Masonry facade work continues on the west side. Substantial completion of the project is still on schedule for mid-to-late August. Periodic updates will be provided in SVA Today in the coming weeks. For questions, please contact Richard Riccio, executive director of Facilities, 212.592.2156.

Distance and The Dove

BFA Film, faculty member Salvatore Oppedisano and The Lover Company present screenings of Distance (1975), starring James Woods, and The Dove (1968), a short film that parodies the work of Ingmar Bergman. Both films are directed by Anthony Lover, who will answer questions following the screenings. RSVP required. Thursday, August 11, 6:45pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 21st Street.
