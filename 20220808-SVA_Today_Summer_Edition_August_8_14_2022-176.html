News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
August 8 – 14, 2022
Zaccheo Zhang

Events in August
At the end of this month, SVA will welcome everyone back to campus, but until then, juried exhibitions and the MFA Fine Arts thesis show fill out the August calendar.

July’s Hot Five
The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios last month include a restaurant branding project, a design system to share opinions and a cute animated cat.
Events

Tuesday | “Dwelling in Motion”
A juried exhibition of works that analyze the constantly growing relationship between habitat and inhabitant. Tuesday, August 9 – Monday, September 19. Reception: Thursday, August 11, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Wednesday | Information Session for the Fall 2022 Internship-for-Credit Program
Learn how you can qualify for the SVA Internship-for-Credit Program. This overview will cover the online application system, Symplicity, as well as student enrollment criteria, the responsibilities of both students and employers, how to find and apply for an internship, special requirements for international students and deadlines and procedures for the fall semester. If you are an international student, attendance at this session is a requirement before submitting your application. This presentation is co-hosted by SVA Career Development and the International Student Office. Wednesday, August 10, 3:00 – 4:30pm, via Zoom; register here.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour on Thursday, August 11, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Associate Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Ongoing | “Outro: World-Building Within and Outside the Studio”
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of thesis projects by its class of 2022, curated by Herb Tam, curator and director of exhibitions at the Museum of Chinese in America. Through Monday, August 15. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Ongoing | “Other Visions”
An exhibition of multidisciplinary work exploring hidden structures and unique ways of seeing through language, identity and light. Through Monday, August 22. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

NYC PICKS

Summer for the City Festival
The first annual Summer for the City festival at Lincoln Center presents a series of workshops and performances that reflect New York City’s culture. Free. Through Sunday, August 14. Various locations. See the full calendar of events here.

Animation Block Party 2022
This weekend Animation Block Party returns, featuring work by students Thomas Gambardella (BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects), Gemma Hill (BFA Animation), Anthony Senatore (BFA Animation) and Emily Zullo (BFA Animation), as well as alumni Robert Bohn (BFA 2008 Animation) and Jared D. Weiss (BFA 2012 Animation). Friday, August 12, and Saturday, August 13. Screening tickets: $16. Peter Jay Sharp Building, BAM Rose Cinemas, 30 Lafayette Avenue, Brooklyn.

“Break of Day, Edge of Night”
A group exhibition featuring work by artists such as Adam Cable (MFA 2017 Photography, Video and Related Media), Karl Hartman (MFA 1989 Fine Arts), Baoying Huang (BFA 2019 Illustration) and Stewart Siskind (BFA 1977 Media Arts). Through Saturday, August 13. The Painting Center, 547 West 27th Street, Suite 500.

“Body Politic”
A group exhibition celebrating bodies that do not conform to the current dominant culture’s expectations, including work by Dylan Mortimer (MFA 2006 Fine Arts). Through Saturday, August 27. 440 Gallery, 440 Sixth Avenue, Brooklyn.

“Matrixes Small Works”
A group exhibition exploring the concept of the matrix, including work by Christine Romanell (BFA 1992 Graphic Design). Through Saturday, August 27. Kate Oh Gallery, 31 East 72nd Street.
#SVAwesome
A new immersive installation by photographer and mixed media artist Renée Cox (MFA 1992 Photography and Related Media), currently on view as part of “Shrine” at Mattress Factory in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. Via Arte Fuse.
