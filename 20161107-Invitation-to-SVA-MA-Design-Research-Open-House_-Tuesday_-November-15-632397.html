SVA MA Design Research, Writing & Criticism
Open House/Information Session

Tuesday, November 15, 2016
6:00–9:00 PM
136 West 21st Street, 2nd Floor, New York


Every fall we throw open the doors of the MA Design Research, Writing & Criticism department at the School of Visual Arts and invite prospective students and employers as well as those interested in design and media to visit our New York studio. We hope you'll join us for our 2016 Open House! 

At this event, you'll have the opportunity to: hear presentations by program faculty, alumni, and students; explore the department; and, over drinks and refreshments, ask questions and discuss details of all the engaging courses, lectures, and placement opportunities we offer through the program.

6:00 p.m. Doors open, department tours.  
6:30 p.m. Presentations by program chair, faculty, alumni, and current MA students.
7:30 p.m. Q&A, drinks and refreshments.

Please RSVP if you plan to attend, and you are welcome to share this with friends and colleagues who also may be interested.

Our pioneering one-year program offers a journalistic approach to analyzing and interpreting design through writing and other media. MA candidates work closely with a faculty of expert practitioners and guest critics who are luminaries in their field. The program is particularly well suited to mid-career design or media professionals who wish to deepen research practice, sharpen their writing skills, and enrich their understanding of design in all its forms. It is also geared toward recent graduates with bachelor's degrees who are interested in strengthening their critical thinking and writing skills or developing an area of research expertise. Please find more information below and on our website, designresearch.sva.edu. 



We are now accepting applications for Fall 2017 and are pleased to be able to offer scholarships to successful applicants.

The application deadline for priority consideration is January 17, 2017, with applications accepted on a rolling basis thereafter, as spaces are available. Click here to begin the application process.

If you would like to schedule an individual appointment to visit the department or discuss SVA MA Design Research, please contact the department directly: designresearch@sva.edu

###

The SVA MA in Design Research, Writing & Criticism is now accepting applications for Fall 2017. This rigorous one-year program equips students with tools for researching, analyzing, and interpreting design and its cultural and environmental impacts. Through workshops, seminars, lectures, and site-visits, students examine the issues and policies that shape the designed environment; learn research methods, reporting techniques, and theoretical models; and experiment with a range of media vehicles for communicating their research, including writing, podcasting, video, exhibitions, and events. Each student will identify an individual research territory to explore during the year, culminating in a thesis portfolio of written and applied media projects.

Alumni of the program have gone on to work at companies and institutions such as Metropolis magazine, OMA, Vitra Design Museum, Facebook, Cooper Hewitt, Rizzoli, RISD, Institute of Play, Musem of Modern Art, The Glass House, Rutgers University, Project Projects, Ziba, SY Partners, and Storefront for Art and Architecture. 

The MA in Design Research features an unparalleled core faculty, comprised of celebrated writers, editors, curators, critics, and designers such as: MoMA Senior Curator of Design and Architecture Paola Antonelli; The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin; and urban design critic Karrie Jacobs. With more than 30 guest lecturers and critics visiting the department per semester—including The New York Times senior critic Michael Kimmelman, media critic Virginia Heffernan, author and "Studio 360" host Kurt Andersen, New Journalism progenitor Gay Talese, biographer Deborah Solomon, urban planning designers Interboro Partners, MIT Media Lab founder Nicholas Negroponte, and British design critic Rick Poynor—the program takes particular care to connect students to inspirational mentors and help them to forge relationships with potential employers, mentors and colleagues.
