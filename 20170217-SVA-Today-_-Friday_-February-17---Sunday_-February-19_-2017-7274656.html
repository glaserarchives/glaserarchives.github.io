And the Winners Are . . .

The SVA Alumni Society has announced the winners of this year's Alumni Scholarship Awards, and 78 students will receive over $83,000 to fund their thesis or portfolio projects.
EVENTS

3:15pm | Yoga and Meditation for Wellness and Creativity

MPS Art Therapy and SVA's Student Health and Counseling Services present this two-part workshop, which will introduce students to basic yoga and meditation skills to promote well-being and foster creativity. Yoga mats will be available. Participants will receive free passes to Laughing Lotus yoga studio. Open to students only. RSVP to Navah Steiner at nsteiner@sva.edu. Today and Friday, March 24, 3:15 – 4:45pm. 132 West 21st Street, 5th-floor project space.

3:30pm | ISO Discussion on Executive Order and Immigration Concerns

Are you confused and worried about the recent "travel ban"?SVA's International Student Office staff will provide an overview of the recent political actions about immigration and answer questions in this open discussion forum. All international students are welcome. Light refreshments will be served. 3:30 – 4:30pm. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, concourse-level media room.

5:30pm | HOPE Art Competition Reception

Student Health and Counseling Services present the opening reception for this year's HOPE Competition exhibition, "Expressing Your Unique Identity." This year's theme aimed to honor individuality and unique identities and to create a community of acceptance at SVA. The first-, second- and third-place winners, chosen by BFA Visual & Critical Studies Chair Tom Huhn, will be announced. 5:30 – 6:30pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:30pm | Community Lecture: Biography of Louise Nevelson

MPS Art Therapy presents a talk by Laurie Wilson, PhD, about the sculptor Louise Nevelson, based on Wilson's book, Louise Nevelson: Light and Shadow (Thames & Hudson, 2016). 6:30 – 8:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Saturday | Elegy Screening

SVA Theatre presents the premiere of a short film by Chad "CJ" Gardella (BFA 2005 Film and Video) and seven other SVA alumni, who will all be present. Free and open to the SVA community and their guests, with SVA ID. Saturday, February 18, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

Call for Submissions: Celebrating Middle Eastern Cultures

SVA's International Student Office calls for artwork related to Middle Eastern cultures or by artists from those cultural backgrounds. Work must be smaller than 5 x 5 feet and be framed or ready to be displayed on the wall. Original works only; no digital copies. Fill out the submission form and waiver and bring it with your artwork to the ISO, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor, during walk-in hours, Monday – Thursday, 11:00am – 4:00pm. Artwork will be displayed in ISO's reception area for two months. Deadline: Wednesday, February 22. Contact Yoko Anderson at yanderson@sva.edu with questions.

Routine Systems Maintenance this Weekend

SVA Administrative and Network Services performs routine systems maintenance over the third weekend of each month throughout the year to help ensure that critical software and hardware platforms serving the community continue to operate reliably. Systems that routinely undergo maintenance include MySVA, MyServices, Web Advisor, Web Budget, Colleague, SVA Wireless Networks, SVA Wired Networks and the Cisco Phone System. Maintenance is generally performed late Saturday evening into early Sunday morning; service on these systems may be interrupted during these hours. Any questions can be directed to the SVA Helpdesk at 212.592.2400 or helpdesk@sva.edu.
