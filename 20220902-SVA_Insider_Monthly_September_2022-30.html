🎙️ Get to Know: Scott Lauer, Registered Architect (RA), LEED, Director of Capital Projects

Tell us a little about your history with SVA and your current role.

Having grown up in the NYC area, SVA has been on my radar since high school when several friends were accepted into graphics, illustration and photography programs. SVA has also been generous in allowing Open House New York, the non-profit I founded, to use the SVA Theatre for our programs. Now I'm two months into my role as director of Capital Projects and am learning more each day about SVA's history, programs and capital needs and goals.


As recently appointed director of Capital Projects, is there anything new this year that students and staff can look forward to?

The most visible projects completed this summer include new facilities for BFA Animation, Humanities & Sciences and the Office of the Provost. Equally important but less conspicuous are the campus-wide improvements to building facades, roofs, mechanical equipment and lighting—all of which are currently underway and will make our facilities safer, more comfortable, easier to maintain and more environmentally sustainable.


What makes SVA’s current facilities special? And how would you like to see the SVA campus grow or evolve in the future?

I appreciate that each floor in every SVA building has a unique environment that reflects the program it houses. As for the future, in order to combat climate change, sustainability will drive many campus improvements in the coming years. Many of SVA’s buildings are more than 100 years old. Like any centenarian, they need special attention. I look forward to working with staff, faculty and students to address these and other needs in order to make our campus as inspiring, sustainable and functional as possible.

In Remembrance of Dennis Gillyard

Dennis Gillyard, an SVA staff member for 34 years, died last month at the age of 76. Gillyard first joined the College as a mailroom clerk, working in that role from 1981 to 1987. He returned in 1994, working in Resource Management for nine years and then Mail Processing/Office Services, where he worked for 19 years before retiring in April of this year.
