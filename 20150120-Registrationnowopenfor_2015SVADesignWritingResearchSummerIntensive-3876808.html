Announcing 2015 Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive

June 1–12, 2015
School of Visual Arts, New York City


If you care about design and language, and are interested in learning how to write meaningfully about products, graphics, urban infrastructure, and buildings, then you should join us this summer in the School of Visual Arts MA Design Research department for a a two-week intensive devoted to research into, and writing about, design. 

Participants will be introduced to a range of techniques for constructing compelling narratives about images, objects, and spaces. You will experiment with different research methods, writing formats, and complete several projects across media, including a collaboratively produced publication. 

Each participant in this richly programmed two-week Intensive will have a workstation in the light-filled Design Research department in New York’s Chelsea district, 24-hour access to department resources, and will be instructed by renowned writers and editors such as: The New York Times columnist Steven Heller; Fast Company contributor Karrie Jacobs; critic and The Weeklings co-founder Jennifer Kabat; BBC interviewer and Design Observer contributor Adam Harrison Levy; The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin; design historian and critic Alice Twemlow, technology and culture columnist for Yahoo News and Significant Objects author Rob Walker, and architecture critic Mimi Zeiger.
 
A robust daily schedule of seminars, lectures, workshops, and one-on-one consultations will be supplemented with visits to the city’s design collections, archives, and libraries, as well as behind-the-scenes access to new exhibitions, buildings, and urban planning developments. Participants will also have the opportunity to visit, interview, and profile some of New York’s most prominent design studios in the fields of architecture, interaction design, graphic design, fashion, and product design. 

To find out more about projects, participants, and instructors see the Intensive website.

You'll be in great company if you join us this summer! Intensive participants travel to SVA from all over the world, and arrive with experience in diverse careers ranging from design, architecture, and journalism, to curation, education, and event organization.

Past participants of the Intensive have included: curatorial assistant in the Department of Architecture and Design at The Museum of Modern Art; writer at Ziba, a design and innovation consultancy in Portland, Oregon; Design History teacher at the ESPM Undergraduate Design Program in São Paulo; design and innovation consultant at Claro Partners, Barcelona; editor-in-residence at Abitare.it; partner at architecture practice Venkataramanan Associates in Bangalore; director of Archivo Diseño y Arquitectura, a nonprofit Design and Architecture space in Mexico City; librarian at Virginia Commonwealth University in Doha, Qatar; researcher at Facebook in San Francisco; and contributing editor to the Norwegian fashion journal Personae.

See all of their bios here.

How to Apply

SVA Design Research and Writing Summer Intensive requires the following materials:
  • Completed application form
  • Writing sample: Up to 2,000 words of published or unpublished writing (such as essays, blog posts, or articles) about design, architecture, or related subjects.
  • Statement of purpose (250–500 words)
  • CV
Applications are due April 1, 2015. Tuition is $2,250.
