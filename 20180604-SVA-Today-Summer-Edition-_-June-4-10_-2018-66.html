SVA Today
June 4 – 10, 2018

May Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios last month include elegant illustrations of Marie Antoinette, animated work for filmmaker James Cameron's new sci-fi show and expressive cartooning based on Marvel Comics' number one superhero team.

Young Ones Student Award Winners

Each third- and fourth-year student in BFA Advertising faculty member Frank Anselmo's Unconventional Advertising class had a winning entry in the One Club's Young Ones Brief competition, making it the largest class ever to do so.
THIS WEEK

Tuesday and Wednesday | All-Staff Aft Show Art Drop-Off

Staff members who submitted their artwork online for inclusion in the All-Staff Art Show must drop off their artwork on Tuesday, June 5 or Wednesday, June 6, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street. The exhibition will be on view Wednesday, June 13 – Saturday, June 23. Reception: Thursday, June 14, 5:00pm.

Wednesday | "#Connected" Reception

MPS Art Therapy presents a collaborative art exhibition that explores youth responses to technology, media and relationships and features work from participants at Counseling in Schools. Wednesday, June 6, 5:00 – 8:00pm.Through Thursday, June 14. Viewing times available by appointment; contact 212.592.2610 or arttherapy@sva.edu. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Wednesday | Dog Therapy for Staff and Faculty

Student Health and Counseling Services presents dog therapy with New York Therapy Animals for staff and faculty this summer. Wednesday, June 6, 3:00 – 4:00pm, SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

Thursday | "Life on Pluto" Reception

The MFA Computer Arts class of 2018 presents an exhibition of thesis work that explores the themes and media of digital art. Reception: Thursday, June 7, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 16. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Friday | "Digital Short Story: Small Bytes Big Fiction" Reception

MFA Visual Narrative presents an exhibition featuring work created by students in the program's Digital Short Story class. Friday, June 8 – Monday, June 25. Reception: Friday, June 8, 6:00– 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Saturday | SVA Makers Market

A one-day showcase of exclusively alumni-made items such as apparel and accessories, furniture, jewelry, housewares, stationery, self-published books, magazines and comics. Presented with Artists & Fleas. Saturday, June 9, 11:00am – 6:00pm. Artists & Fleas, 568 Broadway.

Showing Kids They Belong

LA Times on how Rebecca Sugar's (BFA 2009 Animation) commitment to LGBTQ visibility drives her Cartoon Network show, Steven Universe.
NYC PICKS

"David Bowie Is" Evening Hours

As university members of the Brooklyn Museum, SVA students, staff and faculty are invited to view the exhibition "David Bowie Is" free of charge for one evening. Free with SVA ID; one guest limit per ID holder. Tuesday, June 6, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.

Justine Kurland: Girl Pictures, 1997 – 2002

A 20th anniversary exhibition of Justine Kurland's (BFA 1996 Photography) photo series "Girl Pictures." Through Friday, June 29. Mitchelle-Ines & Nash Gallery, 534 West 26th Street.

Tanya Aguiñiga: Craft & Care

An exhibition that demonstrates the link that artist and designer Tanya Aguiñiga forges between design thinking and community work. Tickets free – $16. Through October 2018. Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.

Submit Photos to the SVA All-Staff 365-Day Calendar

The Visual Arts Press is making an all-staff, image-based calendar as a giveaway for the All-Staff Meeting in August. Submit images from your Instagram feed, or any other photos of your choice. Submit here by Thursday, June 7.

#Connected Panel Discussion

Art therapists and art therapy interns from Counseling In Schools will participate in an interactive panel discussion addressing the collaborative art exhibition "#Connected," clinical creative arts therapy in schools and this therapeutic exhibition process. RSVP to arttherapy@sva.edu. Monday, June 11, 5:30pm. 132 West 23rd Street, 3rd floor.

Summer Workshop: Un-Urban Experiments in Træna, Norway

MFA Interaction Design presents a summer workshop that brings together students from New York and Scandinavia to work on a design challenge at the intersection of the urban and the "un-urban" in Træna, Norway, a village in the Arctic Circle, from June 14 – July 2 (optionally until July 8). Click here for more information. Application deadline: Thursday, June 7.
