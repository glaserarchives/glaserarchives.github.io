Brian Floca, via The New York Times.

Keeping the City Going Among NYT's Best of 2021
The New York Times and New York Public Library have announced their selections for this year's best illustrated children's books, one of which is Keeping the City Going (Caitlyn Dlouhy/Atheneum), by Brian Floca (MFA 2001 Illustration as Visual Essay).

press pick
Via Artware Editions.

Artist Plate Project 2021
Artware Editions is once again working with the Coalition for the Homeless for its fund-raising Artist Plate Project, a set of limited-edition dinner plates available for pre-order sale starting today. SVA alumni involved in this year's project include Katherine Bernhardt (MFA 2000 Fine Arts), Keith Haring (1979 Fine Arts), KAWS (BFA 1996 Illustration), Kenny Scharf (BFA 1981 Fine arts), Amy Sillman (BFA 1979 Fine Arts) and Sarah Sze (MFA 1997 Fine Arts).

Pop-Up Live Magazine
Contributors to Pop-Up Magazine’s fall 2021 issue include Emmy-winning TV writer Cord Jefferson (Watchmen, Succession, Master of None, The Good Place) and author and artist Chanel Miller (Know My Name). Students are offered $10 off the ticket price for Pop-Up Magazine Live on November 16 at BAM's Howard Gilman Opera House at 7:30pm. Pop-Up Magazine is created for a stage, screen and live audience. Use code STUDENT for discounted rate. Proof of vaccination and masks required. Tonight, 7:30pm. BAM Howard Gilman Opera House, 30 Lafayette Ave, Brooklyn. Click here to buy tickets.

Dior at the Brooklyn Museum
The Brooklyn Museum's latest major fashion survey, "Christian Dior: Designer of Dreams," covers the long and illustrious history of the legendary House of Dior. Through Sunday, February 20, 2022. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn. Purchase tickets here.

#SVAIRL Call for Submissions
This past summer, SVA launched a new Instagram series, "#SVAIRL (#SVAinRealLife)." Each post focuses on one student, amplifying their unique voice and highlighting their impressive artwork.
