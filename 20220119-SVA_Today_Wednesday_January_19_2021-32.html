SVA Today
Wednesday, January 19, 2022

Michael Cuesta's New Series
Broadway World has posted the trailer to Promised Land, a new ABC series executive-produced and directed by Michael Cuesta (BFA 1985 Photography). The show debuts on Monday, January 24.
Events

How Much Does It Hurt?
Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies, “How Much Does It Hurt?” is a new thesis exhibition from the graduating class of 2022, curated by artist Grayson Cox and faculty member Paloma Crousillat (MA 2014 Critical Theory and the Arts; MFA 2005 Fine Arts). Through Monday, February 7. SVA Flatiron Gallery, Flatiron Windows and the Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

4:30pm | Faculty Book Club
For the next book club meeting, we will discuss a short article and a podcast addressing the presence of white supremacy in artistic and therapeutic contexts, available on the Faculty Book Club Canvas page. Co-facilitated by MA/MAT Art Education chair Cathy Rosamond and MPS Art Therapy faculty member Liz Dellicarpini. As a reminder, all faculty are invited to join on Canvas for more readings and discussion, all of which can be done at your own pace. Today, 4:30 – 5:30pm ET, on Zoom.

Ongoing | Narratives, Stories, Algorithms: Rethinking Independence in Digital Times
Presented by MA Curatorial Practice, "Narratives, Stories, Algorithms: Rethinking Independence in Digital Times," curated by Xiao Zong (MA 2021 Curatorial Practice), questions the ways in which we receive information, form opinions and draw conclusions in our digital era of fragmented information, fabricated truths and malleable narratives. Through Thursday, January 27. Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 5th floor.

Ongoing | SVA ♥ Milton
SVA presents “SVA ♥ Milton,” an exhibition celebrating the legendary life and work of the late designer and longtime faculty member Milton Glaser. Due to COVID-19 precautions, the exhibition is currently open only to SVA students, faculty and staff. Through Saturday, February 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Pavilions at Lisson Gallery
Lisson Gallery presents “Pavilions,” a group exhibition that explores the artistic use of pavilions in various forms. Through Saturday, February 12. Lisson Gallery, 504 West 24th Street.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Friday | Groove Into 2022 With Healthy Habits
Humanities and Sciences TALKS presents “Groove Into 2022!” Set yourself up for success by building good habits and breaking bad ones. Certified personal trainer and self-defense coach Susan Kim offers practical tips and strategies on making small, yet transformational changes in your life. Friday, January 21, 11:00am ET, on Zoom.

Friday | Virtual Support Space for International Students: New Year’s Resolutions
Do you have a New Year's resolution that you want to share? Join Holly from Student Health and Counseling Services and Will from the International Student Office for a Spring 2022 Meet and Greet Virtual Support Space for international students. There will be resources offered as well as opportunities to connect with others, share your resolutions and an open forum for international students to ask any questions. Friday, January 21, 2:00pm ET, on Zoom.

Next Week | Yoga for Artists: Finding Balance and Self-Acceptance
Join Student Health and Counseling Services and yoga instructor Melissa Boo for a free virtual yoga class to begin the new year and spring semester on the right foot. Focus on developing new healthy habits, finding balance and practicing self-acceptance. Beginner-friendly! Open to all SVA students, staff and faculty. Tuesday, January 25, 11:00am ET. RSVP here.
