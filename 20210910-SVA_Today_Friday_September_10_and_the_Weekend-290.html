Friday, September 10, and the Weekend

From the SVA Archives: An Image Collection From 9/11
In honor of the 20th anniversary of 9/11, the SVA Archives reflects on "here is new york," a collective exhibition of images from September 11, 2001, organized soon after the attacks by a group of artists, including MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Chair Charles Traub.
SVA IN THE PRESS
Photo: Susan Meiselas

After 9/11, Do We Prefer Images to Reality?
Aperture considers how in three recent books, former MFA Art Writing Chair David Levi Strauss "considers the profound effects that photography, terror and divisive politics have had on the 21st-century imagination."
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tuesday | Whitney Museum's Walter Annenberg Lecture: Dawoud Bey
For the past 40 years, Dawoud Bey (1977 Photography) has worked to expand upon what photography can be and the subjects and themes it can address. In this free lecture, Bey speaks with Adam D. Weinberg, the Whitney Museum's Alice Pratt Brown Director, about his work, the history of American photography relevant to his own practice and his ongoing engagement with Black history and memory. Tuesday, September 14, 6:30pm. Register here to join on Zoom.

Wednesday | Free Screening: Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings
SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings, starring Simu Liu and Awkwafina. Free with SVA ID (1 non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Click here for SVA Theatre's current COVID guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Wednesday, September 15, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Commemorating 9/11 in NYC
Time Out New York shares a list of ways to commemorate the 20th anniversary of the attacks on September 11 this weekend in the city.

Nathan Fox Talk at LightBox Expo
In a virtual talk at LightBox Expo, MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) will explore the fundamentals and process of visual narrative. The expo connects fans with the artists and creators behind films, animation, games, TV shows and illustrations, as well as an artwork marketplace. Get a $2 ticket now to access past and upcoming content and to see Fox's online session today, September 10, 2:00 – 3:00pm ET.

CineCina Film Festival Discount
The CineCina Film Festival kicks off today, featuring five films from China. Through Sunday, September 12, at SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. SVA students, faculty and staff can use the code SVA20OFF for 20% off tickets; purchase here.
