Hyesu Lee's Signature Styles [Video]

The next SVA Sketchbooks video features the drawings and abstract doodles of SVA faculty member Hyesu Lee (MFA 2011 Illustration as Visual Essay). Her work is on view at "Street Smart: The Intersection of Art and Design in the City."
EVENTS

Tonight and Sunday | The Last Jedi Screenings

SVA Theatre presents the following two screenings of The Last Jedi. RSVPs are not accepted or required; please do not line up any earlier than two hours prior to the screening times. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

-Tomorrow, December 15, 7:00pm. Free with SVA ID. Due to limited seating, non-SVA guests are not permitted. SVA ID holders will be admitted at 6:45pm.

-Sunday, December 17, 3:00pm. Free with SVA ID; two non-SVA guests permitted per SVA ID holder. A simulcast Q&A with the movie's visual effects artists will follow.

Saturday | Ferdinand Screening
SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of the new Blue Sky Studios animated film Ferdinand, directed by Carlos Saldanha (MFA 1993 Computer Art). Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Tomorrow, December 16, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | Critique Night

Don’t feel confident about your work and portfolio? At critique night, fourth-year students and professionals from BFA Animation, BFA Cartooning, BFA Illustration, BFA Design and BFA Fine Arts will offer advice. Cookies and drinks will be served. Tomorrow, December 16, 3:00 – 6:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Saturday | Bodily

MA Curatorial Practice fellows John Elammar, María Sáenz and Andrea Valencia present a performance event with New York-based artists that will make visitors rethink the social and political dynamics of the body. Tomorrow, December 16, 7:00 – 9:30pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
PRESS PICK

Elinor Carucci on Capturing a "Bad Kiss"

The New Yorker interviews MFA Photography, Video and Related Media faculty member Elinor Carucci about her photo of a "bad kiss" that accompanied "Cat Person," the much talked-about short story from last week's issue of the magazine.
