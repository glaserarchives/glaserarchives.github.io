Defining "2017 America"

BFA Photography and Video's upcoming exhibition "2017 America" features work by high school students that reflects life across the country in 2017.
EVENTS

9:30am | Emotional First Aid: Recognizing and Responding to Students in Distress

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services clinicians Christine Gilchrist and Navah Steiner present a workshop on identifying signs that a student is struggling and what you can do to help. Through interactive methods, you will practice ways to respond more effectively to a student in crisis and leave this workshop feeling more competent and confident communicating with students in distress. Breakfast will be served. RSVP to Steiner at nsteiner@sva.edu. 9:30 – 11:30am. 340 East 24th Street, board room.

11:00am | Sign Up for a New York Public Library Card

An NYPL librarian will be on hand to sign up SVA students, faculty and staff and answer questions. 11:00am – 2:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

6:00pm | Brandstand: States of America

The MPS Branding Alumni Network presents its second annual Brandstand, which will examine the radical shifts in the meaning of "America" as a brand. Speakers: Charles Blow, Brian Collins, Jim Johnson, Paola Mendoza, Debbie Millman and Derek Thompson. RSVP here. Wednesday, November 15, 6:00 – 8:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Exchange: Beyond the Boundaries of the Written Word

SVA Library presents a night of readings by Celia Bland, Christine Shan Shan Hou, Emily Hunt and Simone Kearney, four poet-artists who mingle their written works with visual art. Refreshments will be served. 7:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
PRESS PICK

The Crazy World of MythBusters

The New York Post talks with Jon Lung (MFA 2016 Products of Design), co-host of the new MythBusters. The series premieres tonight, 9:00pm, on the Science Channel.
NYC PICKS

Anne Neely and David Cohen

A conversation between artist Anne Neely and editor and publisher of Artcritical David Cohen, in light of Neely's exhibition "Hidden in Plain Sight," curated by Sarah Sze (MFA 1997 Fine Arts) and on view through December 16. Free; RSVP here. 6:00pm. CUE Art Foundation, 137 West 25th Street, ground floor.

How to Report News that Matters

Center for Communication presents some of the best journalists working today—from The Guardian, Rolling Stone and The Bitter Southerner—who tell it like it is from the front lines of politics, national security, natural disasters, racial injustice and humanitarian crises. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff with ID; get tickets here. 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Stephen Shore Opening

Tonight at 7:00pm, The Museum of Modern Art celebrates the opening of the photographer Stephen Shore’s first survey in New York and SVA has received limited invitations to the event for faculty and staff. Each invitation admits two. RSVP is required; do so here, with promo code SVASHORE and company School of Visual Arts. You will receive a confirmation email. Say your name at check-in for entry for you and one guest. If you have any questions, contact MoMA at corporate_membership@moma.org or 212.708.9840. The Museum of Modern Art, 18 West 54th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Behind the Scenes of Disney/Pixar's Coco

BFA Animation, BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects, MFA Computer Arts and SVA Career Development present Adam Molina, co-director of Disney/Pixar's Coco, for a behind-the-scenes look at the film. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Free; RSVP here. Friday, November 17, 11:30am. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

MFA Social Documentary Film Online Information Session

If you are interested in combining social activism and creativity and passionate about storytelling, MFA Social Documentary Film encourages you to join its online information session, where you will connect with the program's chair, faculty, staff and alumni. RSVP here. Monday, November 20, 7:00pm.

I, Tonya Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of I, Tonya, starring Margot Robbie and Allison Janney. Director Craig Gillespie (BFA 1989 Media Arts) will be present for a post-screening Q&A. Admission is free with SVA ID; limited seating is available and non-SVA guests are not permitted. RSVPs not accepted or required. Wednesday, November 29, 5:45pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
