Welcome, new and returning students for the fall semester! Check out a slideshow from move-in day and orientation week.

A New Welcome Center

Just in time for the new school year, a purpose-built space has opened on the ground floor and lower level of 342 East 24th Street, next to the 24th Street Residence, housing SVA's Admissions, Financial Aid and Student Accounts offices.
EVENTS

Sean M. Starowitz

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by artist Sean M. Starowitz, whose work is executed in a variety of social, political and community engaged contexts. Today, September 5, 6:00 – 7:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
PRESS PICK

Are These Laws for Real?

Vogue interviews Olivia Locher (BFA 2013 Photography) about her debut photography book I Fought the Law, featuring pictorial enactments of ridiculous laws in each of the 50 states.
NYC PICK

Mad. Sq. Eats

This food market brings the diverse flavors of the city’s best restaurants and food entrepreneurs to Flatiron. Through Friday, September 29. Open daily 11:00am – 9:00pm. Worth Square, Fifth Avenue between 25th and 26th Streets.
TAKE NOTE

Sign Up for a Free New York Public Library Card

A librarian from the NYPL will be on hand to sign up SVA students, faculty and staff and answer questions. Tomorrow, September 6, and Wednesday, September 7, noon – 2:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

International Students Workshop: Adjusting to Life in NYC

Student Health and Counseling Services presents a workshop for international students interested in learning about adjustment and ways to be successful in the transition to living and learning in New York City. Light refreshments from Paris Baguette will be served. Please RSVP to Stella Kim at skim204@sva.edu. Tomorrow, September 6, 3:00 – 4:00pm. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, Conference Room 2.

"Occupy Rail" with Phong Bui

MFA Art Writing welcomes Brooklyn Rail co-founder and creative director Phong Bui, who will speak on “Occupy Rail,” a two-part endeavor which seeks to encourage and support motivated individuals in various cities in the U.S. to create their own Rail publication. Thursday, September 7, 6:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

Artists, Aritisans and Museums in Mexico

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents anthropologist and arts advocate Gobi Stromberg and SVA Art History faculty member Carla Stellweg, who will discuss Mexico’s institutional approach to its vast tradition of popular arts and culture. Monday, September 11, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Purposeful Meandering: Ralph Eugene Meatyard, Charles Traub and the Lexington Camera Club

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents department chair Charles Traub and filmmaker Don Simandi, who will discuss the history and legacy of Ralph Eugene Meatyard, a leader and mentor for the Lexington Camera Club in Kentucky in the 1960s, a photography group of which Traub was a part, in anticipation of Simandi's upcoming documentary on the topic. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Tuesday, September 12, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
