SVA Today
Thursday, September 22
FEATURES

Art School Rocks

Sultan Al-Saud (BFA 2016 Photography and Video) talks about his work at WSVA, the College's radio station. Click through to listen to a special art-related playlist compiled by the SVA Communication Office.
EVENTS

10:00am: SVA Yearbook Photoshoot

Portrait sessions for the class of 2017. 10:00am - 2:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 309.

5:30pm: MFA Social Documentary Film Thesis Showcase

MFA Social Documentary Film presents thesis films from the class of 2016. A full schedule of screenings can be found here. Through Sunday, September 25, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

6:30pm: The Backlash Election: Campaign '16 in Pictures

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents Michael Shaw, publisher of Reading the Pictures, discussing the images that have defined the 2016 presidential election campaign. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

Love Is Love

The New York Times on a new comic book, to be published in December, featuring work by BFA Cartooning faculty member Phil Jimenez. All proceeds from the book will support victims of the Pulse nightclub shooting in Orlando.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY

Remembered Light: Cy Twombly in Lexington

An exhibition of photographs by Sally Mann that reflects on her relationship with the painter Cy Twombly. Reception tonight, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, October 29, Gagosian Gallery, 976 Madison Avenue.

Gonzo Sketch Night

A gonzo-themed sketch night celebrating the partnership of illustrator Ralph Steadman and writer Hunter S. Thompson, with models in costumes based on their collaborations. $12 for undergraduate students with ID, $20 general admission. 6:30pm, Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.

Filigrees of Form: A Stitch in Time

A panel discussion on the changes in nature wrought by technology, with BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker and faculty member Joseph DeGiorgis. $5 for students, $10 general admission. 7:00pm, Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.
TAKE NOTE

Free Coffee at Moe's Caf

Both locations of Moe's Caf are offering free coffee this Friday to all SVA students, faculty and staff who bring their own mug. Friday, September 23, 8:00 - 10:00am, 209 East 23rd Street, 2nd floor, and 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

Call for Submissions: VASA Halloween Poster Contest

VASA is accepting submissions for its Halloween Party Poster Contest. The winning poster will be used to advertise this year's Halloween party, and the winner will receive two free tickets. The deadline is Thursday, September 29, 5:00pm. The winning poster will be announced Monday, October 3. Contact vasa@sva.edu with the title Halloween Contest for poster specifications and details.

Permanent Press: A Talk by Aki Sasamoto

MFA Art Writing presents Aki Sasamoto discussing her current exhibition "Delicate Cycle," which is on view at the Sculpture Center in Queens. Thursday, September 29, 6:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

Distance and The Dove

BFA Film faculty member Salvatore Oppedisano and The Lover Company present screenings of Distance (1975), starring James Woods, and The Dove (1968), a short film that parodies the work of Ingmar Bergman. Both films were directed by Anthony Lover, who will answer questions following the screenings. A reception will precede the screenings at 6:00pm. Friday, September 30, 6:45pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
