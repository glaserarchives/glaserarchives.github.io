Events

7:00pm | Visiting Artist Lecture: Chie Fueki
Japanese American painter Chie Fueki is the recipient of the 2021 Joan Mitchell Fellowship and has had solo exhibitions in the Shoshana Wayne Gallery in Los Angeles, the Mary Boone Gallery in New York City and many more. Presented by BFA Fine Arts. Tonight, 7:00 – 8:00pm ET, on Zoom. Register here.

Last Chance | How Much Does It Hurt?
Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies, "How Much Does It Hurt?" is a new thesis exhibition from the graduating class of 2022, curated by artist Grayson Cox and faculty member Paloma Crousillat (MA 2014 Critical Theory and the Arts; MFA 2005 Fine Arts). Due to COVID-19 precautions, the exhibition is currently open only to SVA students, faculty and staff. Closing today, February 7. SVA Flatiron Gallery, Windows and Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Ongoing | The Way We See Us
An exhibition of multidisciplinary works by SVA students in honor of Black History Month, co-curated by Angelica Dalzon (BFA Illustration) and Kyra Husbands (BFA Visual & Critical Studies). Through Monday, February 21. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Facing Abstraction
Elisa Contemporary Art Gallery presents “Facing Abstraction,” a group exhibition featuring a combination of figurative artwork and abstract paintings from a range of artists, including Kris Pastuszka (BFA 2008 Fine Arts). Through Wednesday, April 20. Elisa Contemporary Art Gallery, 5622 Mosholu Avenue, Bronx.

Submit to Visual Opinion
Visual Opinion is accepting submissions for its upcoming issue, "Utopia and Dystopia." Email your submission to vo@sva.edu with your name, title of the piece, your year and major by Thursday, February 17, 5:00pm ET. Please note that contributors must be matriculated students and located in the U.S.

WSVA Is Hiring!
Today is the first day to apply for the operations manager and production manager positions at WSVA Radio for the 2022 – 2023 school year. To apply, email Arthur Gathercole, agathercole@sva.edu, with your SVA student ID number, updated résumé, and one-page letter of interest with the subject "WSVA Manager Application." Applicants must have a minimum 3.0 GPA and be full-time registered students to be eligible. Email agathercole@sva.edu with any questions or to learn more about the positions. Interviews will be held Wednesday, February 23 – Friday, February 25.

SVA Community Fridge Lunar New Year Food Share
The SVA Community Fridge will have packaged food and fruits to celebrate the Lunar New Year and will be calling for Lunar New Year-related food donations (no frozen food, please) through Friday, February 11. You can also donate any amount to help fund the fridge. Click here to contribute and write "community fridge" in the comment box to ensure that your donation is directed to the project. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. Student Center hours are Monday – Friday, 10:00am – 10:00pm; Saturday and Sunday, 12:00pm – 6:00pm.
