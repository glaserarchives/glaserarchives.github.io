SVA BFA Photography and Video Newsletter
Summer Pre-College is in session!

Follow Faculty Member Elizabeth Bick's Pre-College Digital Photography Class on Instagram and see what the high school students are up to this summer at @svasummershutters.

Don't forget to share your photos with us by using #SVASummerLens!
Joseph Maida at Yancey Richardson Gallery

Department Chair Joseph Maida is included in the group exhibition Interventions at Yancey Richardson, on view through August 24, 2018.
Penelope Umbrico at Postmasters

Faculty member Penelope Umbrico is included in Postmasters' Screenscapes, on view through August 11, 2018.
Lensculture Features Patricia Voulgaris

Faculty member and alum Patricia Voulgaris (2013) is interviewed on lensculture about her practice involving the body and memory in space.
Jin and Dana for THINX

Alums Dana DeCoursey (2013) and Jin Lee (2013) photographed a nationwide campaign for THINX, a brand dedicated to breaking stereotypes and body taboos, while promoting inclusivity.
Kasey Rodriguez Photographs for The Bronx Times 
 
Alum Kasey Rodriguez (2016) photographed the front page story of the recent Bronx Times, covering a local art exhibition focusing on Bronx natives.
Ilona Szwarc Photographs Comedian Bo Burnham

Alum Ilona Szwarc (2013) photographed comedian Bo Burnham for The New Yorker.
Free Studio Space for 100 Hours

EyeEm and Space for Arts have teamed up to offer photographers in NYC 100 hours of free studio space at one of their locations. The deadline to apply is July 23, 2018.
African American Portraits: Photographs from the 1940s and 1950s on View at The Met

The Met's Department of Photography has been collecting hundreds of studio portraits from around the country, including flea market finds and donations, and have selected 150 from this collection for the exhibition.
The New York Times Features David Wojnarowicz at The Whitney

David Wojnarowicz: History Keeps Me Awake at Night is on view at the Whitney Museum of American Art through September 30, 2018.
Marine Life in Black & White

Mexican social anthropologist and photographer Anuar Patjane Floriuk has been photographing the ocean in black & white since 2012 when he saw his favorite dive sites beginning to deteriorate. He hopes to generate more empathy towards this at-risk environment with his photos.
Katherine Newbegin at Lesley Heller Gallery

Faculty member Katherine Newbegin is included in the group exhibition Picnic at Lesley Heller Gallery, on view through August 17, 2018.
Faculty and Alumni in Ladies Of Change: A Group Show to Benefit WIN NYC

A group show to benefit WIN NYC, which provides homeless women and children with housing and other services, will be taking place on July 21, 2018. The exhibition includes faculty member Penelope Umbrico, and alumni Erin Carr (2017), Crystelle Colucci (2017), Ina Jang (2010), Hallie Neely (2017), and Hanna Washburn (2017).
PDN's The Curator Selects Kaitlyn Danielson for Abstract/Mixed Media Award

Alum Kaitlyn Danielson (2018) has been selected for The Curator's Abstract/Mixed Media award for her series Of Breath and Dust.
BESE Features Antonio Pulgarin

Alum Antonio Pulgarin (2013) is featured on Zoe Saldana's new media platform, BESE, speaking about his identity and the role of masculinity in Latinx culture mediated through photography.
Lorna Simpson at ICP Museum 

Alum Lorna Simpson (1983) is part of Multiply, Identify, Her, on view at ICP Museum through September 2, 2018.
Access Dozens of Cultural Institutions with The New York Library Card for Free

New York Library Card Holders can now access dozens of museums and institutions across the five boroughs like the Brooklyn Botanic Garden and Wave Hill for free with the city's new Culture Pass.
Lucie Scholarship Open Call

The Lucie Foundation is proud to offer three cash grants to support the work of emerging photographers one $2,500 scholarship with an open theme and two $1000 scholarships for photographers working in the fields of Fine Art or Documentary/Photojournalism.

The deadline to apply is July 30, 2018.
Public Art Project Transforms Historic New York Fireboat

Artist Tauba Auerbachs Flow Separation is a collaboration with Public Art Fund and 14-18 NOW to recreate a WWI dazzle ship, offering free weekend voyages through the fall.
Instagram Debuts IGTV

Instagram has unveiled Instagram TV, IGTV for short, which can host videos up to an hour long, and allows the user to browse through different episodes.
Professional Lighting at Hardware Store Prices

ShareGrid's video shows how to get professional looking results using inexpensive equipment, including clamp lights, poster board, and shower curtains.
