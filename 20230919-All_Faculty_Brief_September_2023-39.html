All Faculty Brief

September 2023
Greetings:

Welcome back from what I hope was a restful summer break.  If you are new to SVA, we are delighted to have you join our esteemed faculty. 

From all accounts, the fall 2023 semester is off to a good start.  The College welcomed 1,292 new students (325 of whom are graduate students). The total enrollment at the end of last week stood at 4,061.  We continue to battle our way back from the enrollment declines wrought by the pandemic.  For some perspective, the fall 2019 opening headcount was close to 4,500–an all time record.  It may be some time still before we achieve pre-pandemic enrollment levels. Rebuilding our enrollment is an institutional priority–and in so doing we seek to diversify the student body and continue to work even harder to ensure our students have the best possible academic and personal experience.

This edition of the All Faculty Brief is packed with a lot of important information from the Office of Learning Technologies (OLT), including new content on best practices for teaching and learning online; the Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI), which announces new training and support opportunities in the area “Microaggressions, Bias and Subtle Acts of Exclusion;” the SVA Libraries; a faculty Q&A from the Office of Disability Resources; important updates from the Registrar; and the SVA Theatre’s annual “After School Special” and the “Best of Premiers,” both of which feature the best live action, documentary, and animated work produced by recent graduates.

We are glad to have the SVA community back together in force for a new academic year.  As always, many thanks for the excellent work each of you undertakes in the service of our students and the pursuit of academic excellence.


Wishing you all the very best,

Christopher J. Cyphers, Ph.D.

Two New Tools in Canvas to Support Student Access and Inclusion — NameCoach and Ally

NameCoach is a tool that allows you to record your correct name pronunciation so that it can be shared with all of your students in Canvas. You can also add it to your email signature! Perhaps more importantly, faculty can encourage students to use NameCoach in Canvas and ensure that you know the correct pronunciation of all their names! 

Click here to get started using NameCoach in Canvas or in your email. Students have received information about NameCoach via the Student Knowledge Base in Canvas, but they are more likely to follow your lead!


Ally is a new tool in Canvas that assesses course content for accessibility and provides you with step-by-step tips to improve the accessibility of your content. You will see Ally icons in your Canvas course letting you know if any of your content needs attention. This information is not visible to students. Ally also allows students to interact with course content in a variety of alternative formats, e.g., ePub, Audio, and BeeLine Reader.

Watch this brief video to see Ally in action and hear some of your fellow SVA faculty members' thoughts about this new tool.

Let the Gradebook in Canvas Work for You!
Many instructors use the Canvas gradebook to record grades for student work, but did you know that you can also set up the gradebook to calculate overall grades? By taking advantage of Assignment Groups and points, you can let Canvas handle the math, regardless of whether you use letter grades, percentages, or almost anything else. This can save you some work at the end of the term and can help students see where they stand at any point in the semester.

Click here to sign up for “Mastering the Canvas Gradebook.”

Microaggressions, Bias and Subtle Acts

of Exclusion

Fall 2023 Updates and Information from the Office of the Registrar

ID Cards
All SVA community members must have an active ID card to enter any campus building. If you need a new card, complete the SVA ID Card Request Form, which is available as a tile on your MyID dashboard. The Registrar's Office team will notify you by email and will arrange to have your card available for pick-up. 

Attendance Policy Reminder
Please take accurate attendance in Canvas after every class session. Students who are marked absent for one-third of a course's total sessions will be administratively withdrawn from the course with a grade of "W". Individual faculty members may adopt an attendance policy that is more restrictive than the institution's policy, but students will not be officially withdrawn until the absence threshold in the policy is met. All faculty members should note attendance expectations and how absences may affect a student's final grade in their syllabi. The full attendance policy can be viewed in the most recent
SVA Handbook.

Chosen Names and Pronouns on Rosters
Students are able to officially declare the preferred/chosen names and pronouns by which they wish to be referred, and you will see this information reflected on your class rosters in MyServices and on your attendance rosters in Canvas. If a student informs you of their chosen name or pronouns and the information does not appear on your rosters, please take note of their wishes in your personal records and encourage them to officially declare their personal identity options in their user profile in MyServices. A student's chosen name and pronouns should be respected at all times.

After School Special 2023: SVA's Alumni Film & Animation Festival

Featuring some of School of Visual Arts' most accomplished film, television, animation, and visual effects alumni, SVA's After School Special will run from Thursday 9/21-Sunday 9/24.  The annual festival will feature five free, public screenings and Q&A’s with industry insiders that offer behind-the-scenes looks at the world of television and cinema. Films include The Stroll, Call Jane, Nimona, and The Super Mario Bros. Movie, as well as SVA Premieres: Best of 2023, which includes thesis work by 2023 graduates from ten academic departments.  A full schedule and list of participants can be found here.

Visual Effects Society screenings
SVA Theatre partners with Visual Effects Society to regularly present first-run movies exclusively to SVA students, faculty, and staff throughout the year.  Recent films have included Gran Turismo, The Little Mermaid, Guardians of the Galaxy Part 3, and John Wick: Chapter 4.  These screenings will only be announced via the SVA Today newsletter, so please keep an eye out, and encourage your students to do the same.  Reservations are not accepted nor required, and SVA ID holders may bring one non-SVA guest to the screenings.

A Snapshot in Hip Hop: A Conversation with Angel Ibanez and Friends

Presented by the Office of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion
Date: Monday, Oct. 2
Location: SVA Beatrice Theater
Time: Doors open at 5:30pm, the panel begins at 6:30pm

As we continue to celebrate Hip Hop's 50th Anniversary, the DEI Office will be hosting a conversation with SVA's Angel Ibanez (Sketch Artist and Photographer).  Angel was hired to take photos of a famous 5-day video shoot, Funky Beat, of rap group Whodini.  This video shoot featured a number of famous Hip Hop legends, R&B Artists, and Directors.  One of these photos was developed into an SVA Subway poster, which is currently being displayed throughout the city.  

Please join us for a panel discussion that discusses what happened on that day, discuss the narrative behind select photos, and discuss the significance of having so many famous people coming together for this historical week in Hip Hop.  

Moderated by: Regan Sommer McCoy, Chief Curator of the Mixtape Museum

Guest: Angel Ibanez, Print Media Specialist

Invited Guest Panelist: The Rapper Jalil, a member of Hip Hop Group, Whodini

Invited Guest Panelist: Paradise Gray, Chief Curator of Universal Hip
Hop Museum
