SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, October 2, 2015
Lunchtime in the 1970s
The New Yorker features MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Chair Charles Traub's street portraits from 1977 - 1980, which are collected in his new book, Charles H. Traub: Lunchtime (Damiani).
10:00am: Class of 2016 Yearbook Photos
Photo sessions for the 2016 SVA yearbook take place today from 10:00am - 2:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 612.
6:30pm: Integrating Art Therapy and Yoga Therapy
MPS Art Therapy presents Karen Gibbons (MPS 2005 Art Therapy) discusses her new book, Integrating Art Therapy and Yoga Therapy: Yoga, Art and the Use of Intention, and leads a demonstration integrating mudra meditation, asana and art based on a focused intention. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Press pick of the day
Beyond the Gender Binary
In GOOD, Transparent co-producer Zachary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography) talks with trans actress and model Hari Nef.
NYC Weekend Picks
Archibald Motley: Jazz Age Modernist
A new exhibition featuring work by the master of the Harlem Renaissance. Free admission with SVA ID. Through January 2016, Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.
Designers and Books Fair
Browse books about fashion and all forms of design. Select authors will be on hand to sign books. Click through for full schedule. Begins tonight, 6:00 - 9:00pm. Through Sunday, John E. Reeves Great Hall, Fashion Institute of Technology, Seventh Avenue at 27th Street.
College Night at The Frick
Explore the Gilded Age splendor of The Frick while taking in live music, gallery talks and sketching in the Garden Court. Free with online registration and college ID. Tonight, 6:30pm, The Frick Collection, 1 East 70th Street.
The New Yorker Festival
A weekend's worth of events featuring writers, actors, musicians and more. Click through for full program. Begins tonight. Through Sunday, October 4. Ticket prices vary. Various locations.
Lower East Side Pickle Day
Celebrate all things pickled at this annual street festival with games, local food vendors and live music. Sunday, October 4, 12:00 - 5:00pm, Orchard Street between Houston Street and Delancey Street
Take Note
Hurricane Joaquin
Though it is too early to predict Hurricane Joaquin's impact on the New York City area, SVA is closely monitoring the storm's development and will update the College community as needed. If you have not yet signed up for SVAlert, please do so now at my.sva.edu. All are encouraged to stay informed on the latest weather forecasts and storm-related news posted by the NYC Office of Emergency Management.
PMDA Salutes Photographers Who Give Back
SVA, PDN magazine and the Photoimaging Manufacturers and Distributors Association (PMDA) honor three professional photographers and one student photographer who have used their talents to give back. Featuring presentations by honorees Colby Brown, Tamara Lackey and Ted Kawalerski, and the student recipient of the Anthony M. Ruotolo Photojournalism Award. Singer-songwriter Ben Folds will serve as master of ceremonies. Monday, October 5, 6:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Image of the day
From 'Homeschooled," a series of photos by Rachel Papo (MFA 2005 Photography, Video and Related Media) on children educated outside the conventional classroom. Image via "Daily Mail."
