🎙️ Get To Know Amy Brundage, director of Human Resources

Welcome to SVA! What drew you to bring your HR expertise to higher education?

Having worked in companies of all sizes and as an independent consultant for the last six years, I knew I wanted to work for an organization that I believed in. SVA felt like the right fit because it isn't just about higher education; it is about developing artistic talent. Artistic ability skipped me, but I grew up with an older brother who is a ridiculously talented artist, so I love art. I love talking to artists and seeing the world through a different lens. This position allows me to support employees charged with educating and developing artists of all kinds. I believe in that, and it excites me to be a part of it.


What has been one of the most meaningful experiences in your career in HR so far?

I've had so many, but the most meaningful experience—which was also a life lesson—was working for the VP of HR at Hachette Book Group. Her care and compassion for all employees is unprecedented. She absolutely demanded that everyone be treated with dignity, no matter the situation. We had to downsize, and the care and coordination she took to make sure everyone who was let go knew that we were there to help even after they left was a life lesson I will not forget. No matter how stressed or busy you are, there is always time to treat people with dignity.


How have you seen the field of HR change over the past three years since the beginning of the pandemic?

This is a hard question because I was consulting and not truly part of an organization. But I have seen a new need to find creative ways of keeping employees engaged with one another and with their jobs, and keeping the organizational culture going strong.


Anything else you would like to add?

I am thrilled to be here and look forward to connecting with as many people as possible. The more I learn about SVA and what makes it special, the better I can understand and do my job.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

The 22 Immutable Laws of Branding

Presented by Blinkist, this course is a practical, hands-on guide to the dos and don'ts of branding as expressed by 22 simple laws: the Law of Expansion, the Law of Contraction, the Law of Quantity, and more.


Managing Your Anxiety While Presenting

Whether presenting, pitching, delivering a toast, or contributing to a meeting, most people experience some degree of anxiety about speaking in public. In this course, author Matt Abrahams explores what causes people to feel nervous and offers anxiety management techniques.

Host Rachel Cooke helps you understand how you can harness your creativity to be more productive. Rachel details actionable techniques to burst through productivity barriers, enabling you to produce meaningful outcomes.
