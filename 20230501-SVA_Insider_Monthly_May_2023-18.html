🎙️ Get To Know: Maeri Ferguson, assistant director, Media Relations, Communication

Congratulations on your new position as assistant director of media relations! Could you tell us about your role at SVA and your history with the College?

I came to SVA in May 2019, and within a year the pandemic began, so, strangely, most of my time with the College has been remote. Still, that hasn’t stopped me from getting to work with so many incredible faculty, staff, alumni and students, virtually and in-person. Though I do plenty of typical public relations duties like issuing press releases, fielding media queries and advising on crucial external communications strategies, I see my primary role at SVA as a storyteller. I get to combine all my experience from the sort of unusual path that led me here—working in television, then crisis communications—and ultimately serve as the filter for the plethora of content from our community. My goal is to find the best ways to leverage the voices of our vast network of artists, and to bring awareness to the amazing quality of work coming out of SVA.


What have been some highlights of your media relations work so far at SVA?

Landing a half-page spread in the Sunday print edition of The New York Times celebrating Milton Glaser’s milestone 90th birthday; bringing NY1’s beloved Roger Clark to many student exhibitions, including one focused on anti-Asian hate and the College’s first-ever Black Student Union-organized show; and getting to dive into longform feature writing for the Visual Arts Journal, among countless others. But really, any chance I get to give our students what might be their first opportunity to speak to a media outlet about their work feels like a win.


Speaking of student work, as the end of the year approaches and SVA is flush with thesis presentations and exhibitions, what does the PR strategy for promoting those look like?

Each spring brings a treasure trove of story ideas from our undergraduate and graduate departments. I particularly like to highlight thesis work that feels timely, socially or politically, or like it could pick up steam after its life at SVA. I’ve pitched wide-ranging thesis work exploring themes including abortion access, book bans, the Black Lives Matter movement and LGBTQ stories. I always love seeing what’s inspiring SVA students and alumni, and figuring out how I can give them a platform in a meaningful way. And of course, commencement and the forthcoming “Milton Glaser POP” show are also on my radar right now, both of which I’m really looking forward to promoting and attending!

﻿﻿ Nominate A Staff Member For The Distinguished Staff Awards

Each year at the All-Staff Meeting, SVA recognizes outstanding staff contributions to the College with the presentation of two Distinguished Staff Awards. Each award includes a cash prize of $5,000. It’s up to you to nominate one of your administrative-staff colleagues to receive this award. Award information, a list of eligible employees and the nomination form are available at staffawards.sva.edu (once on the My Apps Dashboard, select the Distinguished Staff Awards tile). Nominations are due at 5:00pm ET on Friday, May 5. Don’t forget to save the date for the 2023 All-Staff Meeting on Thursday, August 24, at the SVA Theatre.
