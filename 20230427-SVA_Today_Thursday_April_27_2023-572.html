Spring NYC Museum Highlights

EVENTS

12:00pm | The Algorithmic State: Artificial Aesthetics With Lev Manovich

MA Curatorial Practice presents artist and writer Lev Manovich to discuss AI media transformation, drawing on the theoretical analysis being developed in his new book, Artificial Aesthetics: Critical Guide to AI, Media, and Design, as well as his experience as an artist working with computers since 1984. Today, April 27, 12:00 – 1:00pm, online.

1:00pm | ISO Virtual Drop-in Hours

Every Thursday, 1:00 – 2:30pm, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).


Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu with any questions.

5:00pm | “Another Last Dance”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents its open studios, featuring the work of current and graduating students in the 4th-floor studios and hallways, along with a fundraising book fair. Books will be available for sale on the ground floor and 4th floor of the building. Tonight, April 27, 5:00 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, ground and 4th floors.

5:00pm | MFA Fine Arts Open Studios

Meet artists from around the world who are pursuing an MFA in fine arts at the department’s spring 2023 open studios. Tonight, April 27, 5:00 – 9:00pm. MFA Fine Arts, 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.

6:00pm | VASA Finals Fair: Speed Friending With WSVA!

Make new friends before the semester ends at VASA’s speed friending event! Snacks, drinks and fun vibes will be provided by WSVA Radio. RSVP here; students are invited to stop by even if they have not RSVP’d. Tonight, April 27, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

6:00pm | “Intimate Documents” Reception

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents a show by graduating students focused on personal reflection and storytelling, curated by faculty member Suzanne Joelson. Through Wednesday, May 3. Reception: Tonight, April 27, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Last Chance | “Not too hot, Not too cold”

MA Curatorial Practice presents a curatorial project organized and facilitated by student Sophia Park exploring the role of friendship in art collectives and collaborative art practices. Closes today, April 27. 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

NYC PICK

Off the Page: A Conversation With Bushra Rehman

A conversation and reading with Bushra Rehman—author of Corona and co-editor of Colonize This! Young Women of Color on Today’s Feminism—on her new novel, Roses, in the Mouth of a Lion, a modern classic about what it means to be Muslim and queer in a Pakistani American community. Today, April 27, 1:40 – 2:55pm. Queens College, Rosenthal Building, Lecture Hall 230, 65-30 Kissena Boulevard, Queens.
