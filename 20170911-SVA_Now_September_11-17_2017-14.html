After School Special

A look at five of the movies that will be shown at this year's After School Special, SVA's alumni film and animation festival, running from today until Sunday, September 17, at the SVA Theatre. See the full lineup here.

A Profession of Courage

A Q&A with BFA Illustration faculty member Steve Brodner, who is currently experiencing an unprecedented demand for his moment-to-moment political commentary on the Trump administration.

My First Year: Hanaan Louis

With the fall semester under way, sva.edu is featuring reflections by returning students on their first year at SVA. First up: BFA Film student Hanaan Louis, who talks about the best feedback she received in class, what surprised her about going to college in the U.S. and more.
IN THE PRESS

Capturing How the 1% Parties

Business Insider on BFA Photography and Video faculty member Jessica Craig Martin's photos of the elite prove she is a "master of the dark side of the glamour world."

Women Drawing Change

Brooklyn spotlights Women Who Draw, an online directory of professional female illustrators that was co-founded by BFA Illustration faculty member Julia Rothman.

Keith Haring Endures

Artsy on how Keith Haring's (1979 Fine Arts) South Philadelphia mural We the Youth, originally intended as a placeholder until the area was redeveloped, has remained for 30 years.
EVENTS
Image by Jeffrey Schiff.

"Paisley"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of objects, ephemera, photographs and textiles that have incorporated the paisley motif, as well as works by more than 40 contemporary artists interpreting it. Through Saturday, October 7. Reception: Thursday, September 14, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

SVA at Photoville

BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography present their own exhibitions of work at Photoville, the annual photography show housed in shipping containers located in Brooklyn Bridge Park. Wednesday, September 13 – Sunday, September 24. Brooklyn Bridge Plaza, Water Street at New Dock Street, Brooklyn.
