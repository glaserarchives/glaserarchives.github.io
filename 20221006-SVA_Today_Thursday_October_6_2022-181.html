NYC PICK

Performative Screening of The Formaldehyde Trip

A performative screening of Naomi Rincón Gallardo’s The Formaldehyde Trip, a mix of live performance with video projections to expand upon “mythical-critical surrealist” narratives, using pre-colonial myths to find anti-colonial poetic weapons against injustices of the present. Free for students with ID. Tonight, October 6, 7:00pm. The New Museum, 235 Bowery.

Next Week | Wellness Wednesdays: Panel Discussion on the Overturning of Roe v. Wade

SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, in collaboration with Student Health and Counseling Services, presents a discussion on the recent Supreme Court decision to overturn Roe v. Wade, and how to understand your reproductive rights and access SVA resources. This discussion will be moderated by Jarvis M. Watson, director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion. Panelists include Arielle Kempler, associate director, Student Health and Counseling Services; Abby Wohl, nurse, Student Health and Counseling Services; and Gabriela Andrade and Morgan Cousins of Planned Parenthood of Greater New York Action Fund. Wednesday, October 12, 3:30pm, online.
