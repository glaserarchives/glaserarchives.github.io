BFA Interior Design: Built Environments at DIFFA by Design
The work of four BFA Interior Design: Built Environments students was recently showcased as part of the prestigious DIFFA By Design, a three-day annual fundraising event to combat AIDS. With the mentorship of 3D Design Chair Kevin O'Callaghan (BFA 1980 Media Arts), Yudi Chen, Junyan Lu, Daniella Lun and Ugur Selin Ozderici designed an installation that reflects the homelessness crisis, one of nearly two dozen pieces from across the design industry that speak to various civil-rights issues. Via Interior Design.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Aya Rodriguez-Izumi Encourages Creation and Collaboration at the Children’s Museum of Manhattan
Aya Rodriguez-Izumi (MFA 2017 Fine Arts) has created an art installation for the Children’s Museum of Manhattan based on the Japanese tradition of Ema, in which community members share prayers, wishes and expressions of gratitude. Via The Villager.
NYC PICK

Pompeii in Color
“Pompeii in Color: The Life of Roman Painting” presents 35 frescoes from the National Archaeological Museum of Naples that are seldom exhibited outside of Italy. These well-preserved frescoes illustrate a vibrant world of Pompeii beyond the ashes of the tragic city. Free. Through Sunday, May 29. Institute for the Study of the Ancient World, 15 East 84th Street.

Call for Entries: Love Letters to Survivors Art Event
April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the College’s Title IX Office invite students to send in empowering expressions of art for sexual assault survivors with messages to uplift and support. Submissions for #SurvivorLoveLetter will be open through Friday, April 8. On Thursday, April 14, 2:00 – 4:00pm, accepted submissions will be displayed in the SVA Student Center alongside an event where survivors and allies are invited to show support and contribute to the art display. #SVAWESOME
Dr. Jeremy Cohan, SVA faculty member and director of the College’s Honors Program, recently moderated a Jacobin magazine-hosted debate between writers Vivek Chibber and Slavoj Žižek. Taking off from Chibber's new book, The Class Matrix, the participants discussed whether it is capitalist ideology that prevents workers from rising up or if class structure within capitalism is what makes non-collective forms of resistance—or worse, resignation—more likely than collective action.
