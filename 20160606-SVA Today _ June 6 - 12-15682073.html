SVA Today
June 6 - 12, 2016
Features
The Keyboard Waffle Iron, by Chris Dimino (BFA 2002 Graphic Design)
7 Things You'll See at SVA Makers Market this Weekend
Get a sneak peek at some of the goods available at this weekend's Makers Market, a showcase of SVA alumni-made products such as a keyboard-shaped waffle iron, handmade animal pillows, an interactive cloud lamp, and more. For more information and a participating vendor list, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
A Relationship in Transition
Watch Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography) discuss her recently published book, Relationship, a collaboration with artist Rhys Ernst that documents their six-year partnership. Drucker and Ernst will be at Strand Books on Thursday, June 9, to discuss the book.
Cover Stories: Steven Millhauser's Voices in the Night
Janet Hansen (BFA 2007 Graphic Design) talks about her cover design for the Pulitzer Prize-winning author's latest collection of short stories, which was named by The New York Times as a "Best Cover of 2015."
THIS WEEK
Tuesday (6/7): Walter Bernstein and The Front
The SVA Theatre and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences present a 40th anniversary screening of The Front (1976), starring Woody Allen. Screenwriter Walter Bernstein will speak with Michael Barker, co-president and co-founder of Sony Pictures Classics, about the years he spent on Hollywood's blacklist, which inspired the film. $5 admission. Tuesday, June 7, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Tuesday (6/7): Bon Duke
MPS Digital Photography presents photographer and director Bon Duke (BFA 2005 Photography, MPS 2012 Fashion Photography) discussing his work. Tuesday, June 7, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Thursday (6/9): Storytellers Lecture Series: Broken Frontier Anthology
MFA Visual Narrative presents a panel discussion moderated by Broken Frontier Anthology co-editor and contributor Tyler Chin-Tanner on how to curate an anthology, fund a project through Kickstarter, manage contributors, and more. Panelists include letterer Taylor Esposito, MFA Visual Narrative Chair Nathan Fox, writer Greg Pak, artist Tom Raney and writer Fred Van Lente. Thursday, June 9, 6:30pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Saturday (6/11): "Digital Short Story: Small Bytes, Big Fiction" Closes
An exhibition of work by MFA Visual Narrative students, curated by faculty members Jim Rugg and Mark Sable. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Saturday (6/11): "Facsimiles" Closes
SVA Galleries presents a juried exhibition of SVA student work that highlights the process of transformation and challenges the concept of authenticity. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Saturday (6/11): Makers Market
A fair showcasing SVA alumni-made products such as apparel, jewelry and accessories; furniture, housewares and decorative objects; and stationery, self-published books, magazines and comics. For more information, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
Saturday (6/11): Alice Through the Looking Glass
The Visual Effects Society presents a 3D screening of Alice Through the Looking Glass, starring Mia Wasikowska, Johnny Depp, Sacha Baron Cohen and Anne Hathaway. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Saturday, June 11, 1:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Saturday (6/11): Ferris Bueller's Day Off
The SVA Theatre presents a 30th anniversary screening of John Hughes' 1986 film, starring Matthew Broderick. Saturday, June 11, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Sunday (6/12): John Singleton and Boyz N the Hood
The SVA Theatre and the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences present a 25th anniversary screening of John Singleton's 1991 film, starring Cuba Gooding Jr., Laurence Fishburne and Ice Cube. A conversation between Singleton and author Walter Mosley will follow. $5 admission. Sunday, June 12, 1:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

NYC PICKS Of The Week
Malkovich and the Masters
An exhibition of photographs by Sandro Miller featuring actor John Malkovich recreating famous images. Through Friday, July 1, Yancey Richardson Gallery, 525 West 22nd Street.
Mystery Train
A free screening of Jim Jarmusch's 1989 film. Monday, June 6, 7:30pm, Caf Grumpy, 193 Meserole Avenue, Brooklyn.
Call Her Applebroog
SVA faculty member and alumnus Beth B premieres her latest film, a portrait of her mother and artist Ida Applebroog. Opens on Friday, June 10, and runs through Thursday, June 16. Screenings coincide with a retrospective of B's earlier films. Metrograph, 7 Ludlow Street.
