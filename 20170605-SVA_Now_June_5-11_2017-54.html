News and events from the School of Visual Arts
June 5 - 11, 2017
From AnnaLiisa Ariosa-Benston’s (MFA 2016 Fine Arts) "Famousonmars" wearable art collection.

8 Things You'll See at SVA Makers Market

A sneak peek at some of the awesome DIY products you'll see this weekend at SVA Makers Market, including some totally "bitchin'" scented candles, bacon-themed accessories, wearable feminist art and more. Saturday, June 10, 11:00am – 6:00pm. Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.

Art for AIDS Awareness

A Q&A with award-winning artist Eric Rhein (BFA Painting and Sculpture 1984, MFA 2000 Fine Arts) about his activist artwork and his involvement with the arts organization Visual AIDS.

Recording Rituals

A Q&A with Alumni Scholarship Award winner Aya Rodriguez-Izumi (MFA 2017 Fine Arts), whose project explores how daily routines can become personal rituals that provide a sense of safety and comfort in modern life.
IN THE PRESS

Graphic Novel to Feature Film?

The Hollywood Reporter on how Fox Animation preemptively picked up the feature film rights for Molly Ostertag's (BFA 2014 Cartooning) graphic novel The Witch Boy, which will be published in October.

"Burying the Community"

The New York Times on Hadassa Goldvicht's (MFA 2007 Fine Arts) "The House of Life," a multiscreen video installation that explores life and death as it documents the work of a cemetery guardian in Venice.

Technology/Transformation: Wonder Woman

Jezebel on how MFA Art Practice and MFA Fine Arts faculty member Dara Birnbaum's 1978-79 feminist critique of Wonder Woman is still relevant today.

"The Swinging Pendulum"

An exhibition of work by SVA students and recent graduates, who used photography, paintings, videos and installations to represent altered perceptions of time. Friday, June 2 – Thursday, June 15. Reception: Today, 6:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"Ferocious Ink"

MFA Visual Narrative and the Goethe-Institut New York present authors and illustrators Line Hoven and Nora Krug (MFA 2004 Illustration as Visual Essay), who will present their explorations of German identity in their work and more. RSVP here. Tuesday, June 6, 7:00pm. SocDoc Auditorium, 136 West 21st Street, ground floor.
