THIS WEEK

Thursday | All Staff Meeting: Don't Miss It!

Chat with colleagues over coffee and breakfast, enjoy a variety of presentations about the College's current and upcoming initiatives and find out who will win this year's Distinguished Staff Awards. A benefits fair will take place before and after the meeting. Thursday, August 23. Breakfast 8:00 – 8:45am; program begins promptly at 9:00am. Your SVA ID is required for check-in. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Continuing Education Information Sessions

Learn about Continuing Education's course offerings. Information sessions start promptly at 6:30pm.

Wednesday, August 22
Film, Video and Animation: 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.

Thursday, August 23
Design: 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

Friday | The Happytime Murders

SVA Theatre presents a screening of The Happytime Murders, starring Melissa McCarthy, followed by a simulcast Q&A from Los Angeles with director Brian Henson. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Friday, August 24, 10:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | "Daytripping Procedures"

This juried exhibition features artwork by SVA students that uses recognizable forms to stimulate both playfully humorous and inquisitive modes of observation. Saturday, August 25 – Saturday, September 8. Reception: Thursday, September 6, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Saturday | "Graphic Design in China"

SVA and Shenzhen Graphic Design Association present this juried biennial that aims to showcase the highest level of Chinese design to an international audience. Saturday, August 25 – Thursday, September 20. Reception: Thursday, September 6, 6:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Saturday | Alpha

SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Alpha, starring Kodi Smit-McPhee. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Saturday, August 25, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
