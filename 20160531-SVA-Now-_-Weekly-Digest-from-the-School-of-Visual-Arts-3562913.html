SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts May 31 - June 5, 2016
FEATURES
Still from L'Appetito Venduto (Sale of An Appetite) by Enrico Granato (BFA 2016 Film) and Luigi Rossi (BFA 2016 Film)
Watch: The Next Wave of Filmmakers Meet Hollywood
Tomorrow, June 1, SVA will launch SVA Premieres, its new film and animation showcase, at the Linwood Dunn Theater in Hollywood. Read more about (and watch trailers for) a few of the films that will screen at the event.
Studio Visits: Meeting Artists Where They Work
An article from the spring 2016 Visual Arts Journal about the allures and benefits of studio visits.
IN THE PRESS
Milton Glaser's desk, photo via QZ.
The Argument for Never Retiring
QZ profiles acting SVA chairman and faculty member Milton Glaser who still works as designer at age 86.
Designing for the Future
PSFK highlights "Parallel Designs," an exhibition of hypothetical products by graduating MFA Products of Design students that was on view at NYCxDesign 2016.
Wonder Woman Mash-Up
Dangerous Minds on SVA faculty member Dara Birnbaum's video piece Technology/Transformation: Wonder Woman.
EVENTS
Illustration by Michelle Nahmad
Digital Short Story: Small Bytes, Big Fiction
MFA Visual Narrative presents its third annual exhibition of digital short stories and their accompanying physical artwork. Reception Friday, June 3, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Pixel Perfect
An exhibition of thesis projects by graduating MFA Computer Art students. Reception Thursday, June 2, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, June 11, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Save the Date: Makers Market
SVA presents a fair showcasing alumni-made products such as apparel, jewelry and accessories; furniture, housewares and decorative objects; and stationery, self-published books, magazines and comics. For more information and a participating vendors list, visit sva.edu/makers-market. Saturday, June 11, 11:00am - 6:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 2nd floor.
