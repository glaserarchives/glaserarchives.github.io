SVA Today
Wednesday, September 7
FEATURE

New MFA Fine Arts Faculty Members

The department welcomes 16 new faculty this fall, including Genevieve Hyacinthe, Kalup Linzy, Dread Scott, Caroline Woolard, and more.
EVENTS

6:00pm: Paul D. Miller (DJ Spooky) Performance

As part of "The Beat Goes On," Paul D. Miller (DJ Spooky) will perform in his listening room, which is inspired by the golden Voyager records that were launched into space by NASA in 1977 as a possible means of communication with extraterrestrial life. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Writing LGBTQ Stories for Kids

PBS Newshour interviews Steven Universe creator Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation), who discusses the show's portrayal of LGBTQ themes.
NYC PICK OF THE DAY

Ralph Steadman: A Retrospective

An exhibition of work by the illustrator best known for his collaborations with the author Hunter S. Thompson. Reception Friday, September 9, 6:30pm. Through Saturday, October 22, Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

The Language of Fashion

An exhibition of garments designed by students in BFA Design faculty member and alumnus Olga Mezhibovskaya's Basic Typography course. A public exhibition will take place Friday, September 9, noon - 6:00pm, at 601 Madison Avenue. The installation is on view Thursday, September 8, through Sunday, September 18, Madison Avenue between 57th and 79th Streets.

Visible Futures Lab Demonstrations

The Visible Futures Lab presents a demonstration of its equipment and facilities for graduate students and faculty members. Sessions begin Friday, September 9, and run through Friday, September 16. RSVP required. 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Discounted Movie Ticket Sales Return

Discounted Regal Cinemas ticket sales will resume Monday, September 12, in the Student Engagement and Leadership Office, 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. Tickets can be purchased Monday, Thursday and Friday, 2:00 - 5:00pm. Contact activities@sva.edu for more information.

SVA at Photoville

BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography are presenting exhibitions at this year's Photoville, the annual photography exhibition housed in shipping containers located along the waterfront of Brooklyn Bridge Park. Wednesday, September 21, through Sunday, September 25, Pier 5 Uplands, Brooklyn Bridge Park.
