Keeping SVA Safe
In advance of in-person learning next semester at SVA, we’re introducing some new rules for keeping our community safe, plus other protocol updates. Check them out in this new video.

Juneteenth: An Authentic African American Narrative
In this episode of SVA's podcast "Diversity in 3D: Defining and Deconstructing through Dialogue," Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Dr. Jarvis Watson speaks with historians Dr. Georgette Grier-Key and Dr. Zebulon Miletsky about the significance of Juneteenth and the importance in being it bring included, recognized and respected, not just as a federal holiday, but as an African American narrative. Watch here.

Ryan Pfluger Captures Zola Creators
Ryan Pfluger (MFA 2007 Photography, Video and Related Media) recently photographed filmmaker Janzica Bravo and playwright and screenwriter Jeremy O. Harris, creators of the upcoming film Zola, for The Hollywood Reporter.

Remembering Milton Glaser
Milton Glaser, the legendary graphic designer, longtime SVA faculty member and acting chairman of the SVA Board, died last year on Friday, June 26, his 91st birthday. Visit the links below to learn more about his prolific 65-year career and to celebrate his legacy.

EVENTS THIS WEEK

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Jean Shin
Artist Jean Shin is nationally recognized for her monumental installations that transform everyday objects into elegant expressions of identity and community engagement. Join her for a lecture presented by MFA Art Practice. Tuesday, June 22, 9:30 – 11:00am ET. Register here.

Tuesday | i3 Photo Lecture: Jerry Takigawa
Photographer, designer and writer Jerry Takigawa is constantly practicing integrative thinking—bringing two things together that seem to be opposites and making them better together than they were separately. Don't miss MPS Digital Photography's last i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture of the summer! Tuesday, June 22, 7:00 – 8:30pm ET. Join on Zoom.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, June 24, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

On View | Ninth Annual All-Staff Art Show
This exhibition features works in various media by administrative staff members at SVA. Organized by the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group and SVA Galleries. On view through Friday, July 2, at galleries.sva.edu.

New York Primary Elections
The New York City Primary Elections will be held tomorrow, June 22. This year, New York City is introducing the new ranked-choice voting systems; click here for more information. For a complete list of candidates on this year’s ballot, visit whosontheballot.org.

For information on additional voting resources visit sva.edu/studentaffairs. Contact SVA Student Affairs if you have any questions; call 212.592.2214 from Monday – Thursday, 9:00am – 5:00pm ET, and Friday, 9:00am – 3:00pm ET, or email studentaffairs@sva.edu.

SUNY SPECTRUM, hosted by the State University of New York, is the nation's largest education conference devoted to preventing and responding to sexual and interpersonal violence against members of the LGBTQI+ community. Monday, June 28 – Friday, July 2. Get tickets for $25 here.
