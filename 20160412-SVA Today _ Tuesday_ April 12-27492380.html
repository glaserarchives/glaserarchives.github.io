SVA Today
Tuesday, April 12, 2016
Introducing the Next Wave of Filmmakers
On June 1, the College launches SVA Premieres, a two-day film and animation showcase at the Linwood Dunn Theatre in Los Angeles that will present the works of recently graduated filmmakers to an audience of industry professionals.
1:00pm: Venture 16: MFA Design Thesis Forum
Design entrepreneurs premiere their unique products in an interactive public forum. This event will stream live at design.sva.edu/venture16. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:00pm: Vincent Cianni
MPS Digital Photography presents the documentary photographer discussing his work, which explores community, memory and social justice, and his recent "Gays in the Military" series. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Capturing the Disappearing World
Fast Company Exist highlights Catalog.Earth, a venture by MFA Interaction Design students David Al-Ibrahim and Saba Singh that documents the world's vanishing places.
NYC PICK OF THE DAY
Review Panel
A discussion of current exhibitions in New York, featuring faculty member Kara Rooney (MFA 2008 Art Criticism and Writing). Admission is free. 7:00pm, Brooklyn Public Library, 10 Grand Army Plaza, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE
Around the World: Multicultural Event
The Student Affairs Office presents a celebration of cultural diversity at SVA, with performances and karaoke, as well as cuisine from the Caribbean, China, Italy, the Philippines, and elsewhere. Open to SVA students. Saturday, April 16, 2:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Continuing Education Information Session: Visual Narrative
An information session for summer 2016 classes. Moderated by Nathan Fox, visual artist and chair, MFA Visual Narrative. Saturday, April 16, 3:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.
John Dowdell on Carol
BFA Animation and BFA Film present colorist John Dowdell discussing his work on and screening clips from the Oscar-nominated film Carol. Open to SVA students and faculty. Tuesday, April 19, noon, 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
RisoLab Summer Residency Open Call
The RisoLab Artist-in-Residence Program provides visual storytellers with work space and full access to the facility. All practicing artists are welcome to apply, including those working in graphic novels, zines, picture books, graphic design and photography. The deadline is Friday, April 22. For questions, contact risolab@sva.edu.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
A postcard by Naz Sahin Ozcan (MFA 2007 Design), from Curious Feast, her recently released collection featuring 100 food-inspired postcards by 10 artists.
