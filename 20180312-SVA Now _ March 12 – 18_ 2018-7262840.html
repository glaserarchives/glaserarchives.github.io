News and events from the School of Visual Arts

SVA at SXSW

A rundown of the many SVA community members participating in the 2018 South by Southwest Conference and Festival, happening through Sunday, March 18, in Austin, Texas.

Escape NYC This Summer

SVA Destinations offers programs around the world that span a range of artistic disciplines and can help stimulate your creativity this summer.

SVA at the Oscars

Many SVA alumni had a hand in the production of films like The Shape of Water, Coco, and I, Tonya, all of which won Academy Awards last Sunday.
IN THE PRESS

Changing Perceptions Through Comics

Black Panther illustrator Shawn Martinbrough (BFA 1993 Illustration) recently gave a TEDx Talk about how he became a comic book artist, how he diversifies his stories and how everyone has the "power of representation."

Women for Women on Instagram

SVA students Tara Anand and Ellie Lee’s inspired Instagram project helps women stop criticizing members of their own gender.

Keith Haring’s Enduring Influence on Fashion

Keith Haring’s graffiti-like squiggles left an indelible impact on the worlds of art and fashion and continue to inspire today.
EVENTS

Juan Giraldo

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by Juan Giraldo, a rising talent who is helping to redefine documentary photography. Tuesday, March 13, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"Mentors" Reception

BFA Photography and Video presents an exhibition of work by the department’s students inspired by their working relationships with leading members of the New York arts community. Thursday, March 15, 6:00pm. On view through Tuesday, March 27. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Kaleidoscope Word and Multimedia Variety Show

SVA Humanities and Sciences presents a mini art fest of spoken word, visual art, music and dance performed by SVA students, alumni and faculty members. Friday, March 16, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Titmouse Five-Second Animation Night

SVA Theatre presents this annual event for which Titmouse animators create whatever goodies have been floating around in their minds all year. Titmouse, founded by SVA alumni Chris and Shannon Prynoski, employs many SVA graduates, and several animators/artists will be in attendance. Tickets $5 – $10; purchase here. Friday, March 16, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
