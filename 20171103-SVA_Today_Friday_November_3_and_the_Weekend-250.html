SVA at the VIEW Conference

A recap of the VIEW Conference in Turin, Italy, the premiere international event for computer graphics, interactive techniques, digital cinema, and more, where SVA hosted a panel featuring recruiters and educators from Europe and the U.S.
EVENTS

6:00pm | Artist in Residence: Xuan Chen

SVA's Visible Futures Lab presents abstract visual artist Xuan Chen, who will discuss her fall residency proposal and her past work. Tomorrow, November 3, 6:00 – 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

7:00pm | MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Fall Salon

A salon showcasing the in-progress works of MFA Photography, Video and Related Media students, ranging from performance-based pieces to mixed-media installations to print-based works. At this salon, there will also be a print and book sale, from which all proceeds will be donated to Puerto Rico relief funds. 7:00 – 10:00pm. 214 East 21st Street.

Saturday | Undergraduate Open House

SVA Admissions presents an open house for prospective undergraduate students of BFA Cartooning, BFA Computer Arts, Computer Animation and Visual Effects, and BFA Illustration. Tomorrow, November 4, 9:00am – 3:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | MFA Computer Arts Information Session

MFA Computer Arts presents its fall information session, where attendees will have the opportunity to meet with the chair, faculty, department staff, current students and alumni, and learn about the program. Tomorrow, November 4, 1:00 – 3:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 1022C.

New York lists "The Masters Series: Christoph Niemann" as one of the 25 things that should be on your to-do list this week. Open through tomorrow, November 4, at the SVA Chelsea Gallery.
NYC PICKS

Study Sessions: Sky Cubacub

Sky Cubacub, a non-binary Filipinx human and editor of the Radical Visibility zine, will select a work of art on view in the Whitney’s permanent collection galleries and use it to think through an urgent question in our current political landscape. Free with SVA ID; register here. Tonight, 7:00pm. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.

Frick First Fridays

Free access to the museum's permanent collection, special exhibitions, talks and performances on the first Friday of every month. Tonight, 6:00 – 9:00pm. The Frick Collection, 1 East 70th Street.

"Mapping America's Road from Revolution to Independence"

An exhibition that showcases hand-drawn and engraved maps from the 18th and early 19th centuries that illuminate the geographic, political and economic changes that occurred before, during and just after the Revolutionary War. Museum admission up to $21; purchase tickets here. Through March 2018. New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West.
TAKE NOTE

The Imaging Alliance Salutes Photographers Who Give Back

The Imaging Alliance salutes photographers who have used their talents to heighten awareness of important social and cultural issues through the power of imaging. At this event, Sophie Brill (BFA 2017 Photography and Video) will be honored with the Anthony M. Ruotolo Photojournalism Award. Honorees will give presentations, and a Q&A and reception will follow. Internationally-acclaimed photographer Robert Caplin will serve as emcee. Tickets $10 for SVA students, faculty and staff with code SVASTUDENT; purchase here. Monday, November 6, 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
