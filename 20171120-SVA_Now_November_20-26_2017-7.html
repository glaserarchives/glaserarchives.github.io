SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
November 20 – 26, 2017
Image by Robert Herman (MPS 2009 Digital Photography)

The City as a Muse

"Street Smart: The Intersection of Art and Design in the City" features work by SVA alumni that is for or about the urban environment. On view through Wednesday, December 20, at the SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

A MythBusting Alumnus

Jon Lung (MFA 2016 Products of Design), co-host of the Science Channel's reboot of MythBusters, talks about his TV experience so far. Watch the show on Wednesdays at 9:00pm.

Defining "2017 America"

BFA Photography and Video's current exhibition "2017 America" features work by high school students that reflects life across the country in 2017. On view through Saturday, December 2, at the SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

SVA Guide to Williamsburg

Places to see, things to do and foods to eat in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, as recommended by members of the SVA community.
IN THE PRESS

Whiteout at the Park

Time Out New York on Erwin Redl's (MFA 1995 Computer Art) "dreamlike art installation," now lighting up Manhattan's Madison Square Park until March 2018.

Composing Coco

Variety on how composers Germaine Franco and Michael Giacchino (BFA 1990 Film and Video) worked together to infuse the score of Disney/Pixar's Coco with traditional Mexican music.

Winter Wedding Gowns

New York on this season's most "angelic" bridal gowns, photographed by Hailun Ma (BFA 2016 Photography and Video).
EVENTS

MFA Social Documentary Film Online Information Session

If you are interested in combining social activism and creativity and are passionate about storytelling, MFA Social Documentary Film encourages you to join its online information session, where you will connect with the chair, faculty, staff and alumni from the program. RSVP here. Tonight, 7:00 – 8:30pm.

Elaine Byrne and Mario Marchese

MFA Fine Arts presents artist Elaine Byrne and magician Mario Marchese. Byrne will speak about her recent video installation featuring found footage of the magic trick of women being sawed in half and interviews with barrier-breaking magician Dorothy Dietrich. Marchese will then give a magic performance. A conversation will follow. Tuesday, November 21, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Pass / Age / Tress / Pass

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the Flatiron Project Space present an exhibition of recent work by faculty member Suzanne Joelson, curated by faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). Through Friday, December 1. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Good Art, Bad City: A Pop Up Shop by Artistic Noise

Members of the nonprofit Artistic Noise present a night of participatory art-making, during which visitors can help them transform the MPS Art Therapy Project Space into their vision of a pop-up shop. Presented by MPS Art Therapy. Through Thursday, November 30. MPS Art Therapy Project Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
