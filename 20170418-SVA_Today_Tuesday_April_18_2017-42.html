SVA Today
Tuesday, April 18, 2017

Cure Your Smartphone Addiction

Joe Hollier's (BFA 2012 Graphic Design) Light Phone uses your existing smartphone number to make and receive calls, saves up to seven numbers on speed dial—and does nothing else. (From the Spring 2017 Visual Arts Journal.)
EVENTS

9:00am | BFA Interior Design Thesis Exhibition and Reception

BFA Interior Design presents a student exhibition representing a wide range of projects and interests, each of which integrates conceptual thinking with programmatic layout, design and interior detailing to create an inspiring new place. On view from 9:00am – 8:00pm. Reception: 6:00 – 8:00pm. Affirmation Arts, 523 West 37th Street.

3:00pm | Racially Responsive Leadership in Higher Education

SVA Student Affairs will be live-streaming the Penn Summit on Fostering Racially Responsive Campus Climates. This session will focus on proactively demonstrating care for communities of color on college campuses, as well as on leading efforts to ensure racial equity. RSVP here. 3:00 – 5:00pm. Another live-stream, about reducing racism in college classrooms, will occur on Thursday, April 20, 3:00pm; RSVP here. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, media room.

6:30pm | Alice Twemlow: "Making Waste"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents its founder Alice Twemlow, who will give a talk based on her forthcoming book Sifting the Trash: A History of Design Criticism. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
PRESS PICK

"Baseball’s Technicolor Explosion"

Todd Radom (BFA 1986 Media Arts), a graphic designer specializing in sports, writes about his love for the "ugly" baseball uniforms of the 1970s for The New York Times.
NYC PICK

Painters and Painting Practices in Ancient Egypt

Dimitri Laboury, research director at the National Foundation for Scientific Research of Belgium, will discuss how by studying the techniques and practices of the artists of ancient Egypt, we can get to know them in their social and individual identities. RSVP here. 6:00pm. New York University, Institute of Fine Arts, 1 East 78th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Fulbright Information Session

Career Development presents Fulbright U.S. Student Program representative Lora Seery, who will give an overview of the program and its requirements and will provide tips on creating a strong application. Former Fulbright Scholar and current SVA faculty member Steve DeFrank will present on his Fulbright experience. RSVP here. Thursday, April 20, 11:00am. SocDoc Auditorium, 132 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Database Trial: ACLS Humanities eBook Collection

SVA Library has begun a trial of the ACLS Humanities ebook collection, a collection of 5,000 books in the humanities and related social sciences. It is accessible from our A-Z Databases page until the end of the 2017 calendar year.
