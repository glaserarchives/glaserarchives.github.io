NYC PICK

Been Seen
Highlighting the work of Harlem-based photographer Austin Hansen (1910 – 1996), “Been Seen” focuses on Black photographers illuminating the fullness of Black life, constructing a catalog of survival that includes joy, pageantry and leisure alongside struggles for justice, dignity and equity. Hansen’s work has been placed in conversation with seven contemporary photographers, including Dario Calmese (2012 MPS Fashion Photography). Opening reception takes place tonight at 5:00pm followed by a photography program at 6:30pm. Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture, 515 Malcolm X Boulevard.

Submit to the Visual Opinion “Graduation” Issue
Visual Opinion is accepting submissions for its annual “Graduation” issue, highlighting the work of graduating SVA students. Both undergraduate and graduate students are welcome to submit work by Thursday, April 7, 5:00pm ET. Contributors must be matriculated students, must be located in the U.S. and must be graduating in the 2021 – 2022 academic year. Please send submissions to vo@sva.edu with the subject line format “firstname_lastname_title.” Visit the Visual Opinion Instagram, @VisualOpinion, for more information.

Call for Entries: Love Letters to Survivors Art Event
April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the College’s Title IX Office invite students to send in empowering expressions of art for sexual assault survivors with messages to uplift and support. Submissions for #SurvivorLoveLetter will be open Friday, April 1 – Friday, April 8. On Thursday, April 14, 2:00 – 4:00pm, accepted submissions will be displayed in the SVA Student Center alongside an event where survivors and allies are invited to show support and contribute to the art display.
