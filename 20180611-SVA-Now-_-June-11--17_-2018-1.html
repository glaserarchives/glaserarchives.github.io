News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
June 11 – 17, 2018
News and event from the School of Visual Arts

SVA Returns to Hollywood

A recap of this year's SVA Premieres student film showcase in Hollywood, which included screenings, studio visits and networking with SVA alumni in the industry.

SVA Alumnus Wins Obama Fellowship

Out of more than 20,000 applicants, 20 dedicated civic leaders form the current group of the inaugural Obama Foundation Fellowship, including artist, activist and interactive storyteller Melissa Malzkuhn (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative).

Violence, Lost Innocence and Beyond

Visual Arts Journal on Robert Lazzarini's (BFA 1990 Fine Arts) disorienting exhibition "Inflorescence," which was recently on view in New York.
IN THE PRESS

Celebrating Pride Month in NYC Subways

Adweek on the Pride Train ads that are popping up in New York City subway stations, created by Ezequiel Consoli (BFA 2018 Design), Kyle Harrison (BFA 2018 Design) and SVA faculty member Thomas Shim (BFA 2011 Graphic Design).

New Jurassic Music by Giacchino

Entertainment Weekly releases a teaser of Michael Giacchino's (BFA 1990 Film and Video) score for Jurassic World: Fallen Kingdom.

Drawing for the Next Generation

Communication Arts interviews children's book illustrators and writers Ying-Hwa Hu and Cornelius Van Wright (BFA 1982 Media Arts).

Image: Lucas Thorpe

All-Staff Art Show Reception

The sixth annual exhibition of work by administrative staff members at SVA. Thursday, June 14, 5:00 – 8:00pm. Wednesday, June 13 – Saturday, June 23. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"#Connected" Panel Discussion

Art therapists and art therapy interns from Counseling in Schools will participate in an interactive panel discussion addressing the collaborative art exhibition "#Connected," clinical creative arts therapy in schools and this therapeutic exhibition process. RSVP to arttherapy@sva.edu. Monday, June 11, 5:30 – 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

Drawing in the Park: Madison Square

SVA Continuing Education presents an afternoon of drawing in the park, where you can hone your observational skills and work in any medium you choose. Registration required. Wednesday, June 13, noon – 2:00pm. Madison Square Park, 23rd Street at Broadway.

SVA Bio Art Summer Residency Final Exhibition

This one-night-only exhibition is the culmination of an intensive, five-week interdisciplinary residency housed in SVA's Bio Art Laboratory. Come see the work of this dynamic and experimental group of artists working at the intersection of art and biology. Wednesday, June 13, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Bio Art Lab, 335 West 16th Street, 3rd floor.
