The Artist Residency Project Virtual Open Studios
SVA Summer Residency Programs presents a virtual open studios event featuring work from current participants in the Artist Residency Project, SVA’s fully online residency program. Artists will be joining from around the world and sharing their studios and work. Viewers are welcome to ask questions and participate in an open dialogue with the artists. Today, 12:00pm ET, online. NYC PICK

First Look 2022
The Museum of Moving Image’s acclaimed festival of new international cinema, First Look, is back for its 11th edition. The festival will be accompanied by Working on It, a program offering a lab-like environment for works-in-progress screenings, workshops and discussions. Wednesday, March 16 – Sunday, March 20. Screening tickets begin at $11 for students; purchase here.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

2022 VASA Election Voting Closes Today
Today is the last day to make your voice heard in this year’s VASA election. Vote online at vasavote.sva.edu until 4:30pm ET. All current students are eligible to vote.

Friday | Fab Friday: Expectation vs. Reality
This week’s guests for BFA Design and BFA Advertising’s Fab Friday will be interaction and product designers Steven Jung and Jaquelyn Wang. Jung and Wang have amassed a tremendous amount of industry know-how in the past eight years, working at such companies as Google, AKQA and R/GA. They will share their self-proclaimed “cringe-worthy” trials and errors to help soon-to-be graduates avoid the mistakes that they made. Friday, March 18, 6:30pm ET, on Zoom.

Next Week | Yoga for Artists: Back, Shoulder and Headache Relief
Take a yoga break with SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and instructor Melissa Boo. This free, virtual class will focus on emotional and physical tension relief. Learn poses that may help reduce back, shoulder and head pain while reducing other stress symptoms. Beginner-friendly and open to the entire SVA community. Tuesday, March 22, 11:00am ET. RSVP here.

Next Week | Titmouse 5 Second Night
SVA Theatre presents this annual event, for which Titmouse animators create and screen whatever goodies have been floating around their minds all year. Titmouse, founded by SVA alumni Chris and Shannon Prynoski, employs many SVA graduates, and several animators/artists will be in attendance. Friday, March 25, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. Tickets $5 – $15; purchase here.
