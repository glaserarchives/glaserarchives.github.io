SVA Now
December 18 – 24, 2017
News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Making Ferdinand

Carlos Saldanha (MFA 1993 Computer Art), director of the new animated film Ferdinand, talks about adapting the beloved children's book and offers advice for aspiring animators.

Life as an Artist in NYC

Michael De Feo (BFA 1995 Graphic Design), whose floral patterns have adorned many New York City bus stop ads, discusses what it's like to be an artist in the city.

SVA "Subway Series" Hall of Fame: Ivan Chermayeff [Video]

MFA Social Documentary Film Chair Maro Chermayeff and MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller talk about design legend Ivan Chermayeff's work and legacy.

SVA Holiday Gift Guide: Books

To follow the SVA Holiday Gift Guide: Product Edition, here is a roundup of books written or illustrated (or both!) by SVA alumni and faculty that will satisfy a variety of people on your "nice" list.

SVA Sketchbooks: Hyesu Lee

The next SVA Sketchbooks video features the drawings and abstract doodles of SVA faculty member Hyesu Lee (MFA 2011 Illustration as Visual Essay).
IN THE PRESS

Elinor Carucci on Capturing a "Bad Kiss"

The New Yorker interviews MFA Photography, Video and Related Media faculty member Elinor Carucci about her photo that accompanied "Cat Person," the much talked-about short story from last week's issue of the magazine.

"The Reckoning"

The New York Times Magazine presents essays and art by women, including Ina Jang (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography, BFA 2010 Photography), Olivia Locher (BFA 2013 Photography) and BFA Design faculty member Paula Scher, on women and power in the workplace.

Glaser's Messages of Unity

Curbed New York on SVA Acting Chairman of the Board Milton Glaser's new posters for the College, which can be found in subway stations across the city.

"Taking on Tonya"

The Boston Globe talks with Craig Gillespie (BFA 1989 Media Arts), director of the film I, Tonya, about making the film and how he got to where he is today.
EVENTS

Street Smart: The Intersection of Art and Design in the City

SVA's Alumni Society presents an exhibition featuring art, design and photography by SVA alumni that is for or about the urban environment. On view through Wednesday, December 20. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

M'Bongi: A Place of Non-Warship

BFA Interior Design presents an exhibition of design concepts for a nonprofit peace center by its third-year students. On view through Saturday, January 6. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

The Book Show

Presented by MFA Illustration as Visual Essay, this exhibition challenges students to conceive, write and illustrate a book based on a personal experience or something that is meaningful to them. On view through Saturday, January 13. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
Happy Holidays!

SVA will be closed from Thursday, December 21, through Monday, January 1, for its winter recess.
