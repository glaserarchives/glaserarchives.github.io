SVA Now
News and events from around the School of Visual Arts
May 8 - 14, 2017

2017 Commencement Preview

In advance of tomorrow's exercises, a look at the singular life and career of keynote speaker Gloria Steinem, as well as highlights from past SVA commencements.

All-Star Dusty Awards Presenters

Among the more than a dozen notable presenters are Academy Award-nominated producer and director David Silverman, producer and New York Women in Film & Television president Alexis Alexanian, editor Jim Helton, Academy Award-winning production sound mixer Chris Newman, writer and director Gillian Robespierre (BFA 2005 Film and Video) and Emmy award-winning documentary filmmaker Sam Pollard. Watch the live stream here starting at 6:30pm tonight.

KAWS Exclusive [Video]

Artist and designer KAWS (BFA 1996 Illustration) talks about his beginnings as a street artist, the influence of New York City and fellow alumnus Keith Haring on his work and how he "lucked out" by going to SVA.

The Possibilities of Universal Design

From the spring 2017 Visual Arts Journal, seven top creatives and SVA faculty discuss expanding the idea of universal design, just in time for NYCxDESIGN.

Cultural Differences and Similarities

A Q&A with BFA Photography and Video student Rujie Wang, who won a 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award for her project "Made in China," which explores Western stereotypes of Asians through a series of photos of products purchased at 99-cent stores in America.
IN THE PRESS

"Defying Categorization"

Artnet News lists Joshua Citarella (BFA 2010 Photography) as one of 10 contemporary artists taking photography in new directions.

From SVA to Spotify

Technically on the steps that Datrianna Meeks (MFA 2016 Interaction Design) took to become a senior product designer at Spotify.

Sprayground's "Spark and Stimulation"

Forbes interviews Sprayground chief creative officer and CEO David Ben-David (BFA 2005 Graphic Design) about the inspiration for company's new Smart Pack backpack.
EVENTS
"Family Portrait" by Phoebe Snyder

BFA Photography and Video Senior Show Reception

BFA Photography and Video presents a show of work by its graduating students. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Friday, May 12. 214 East 21st Street, 2nd, 5th and 6th floors.

"Cognitive Dissidence"

An exhibition of work by artists in the MFA Fine Arts class of 2017, curated by Dan Cameron. On view Thursday, May 11, through Saturday, May 27. Reception: Thursday, May 11, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Ray Smith Studio, 261 Bond Street, ground floor, Brooklyn.
