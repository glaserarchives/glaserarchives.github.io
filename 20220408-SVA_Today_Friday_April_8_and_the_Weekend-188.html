The Artist as Healer
Guadalupe Maravilla (BFA 2003 Photography) deploys the power of vibrational sound as a form of healing in his sculptures, now on view at the Brooklyn Museum and Museum of Modern Art. Via The New York Times.
Events

1:00pm | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 1:00pm ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

6:00pm | Community Lecture Series: The Mural Project—Building Restorative Relationships
MPS Art Therapy presents a virtual lecture exploring ways to build restorative relationships, interpersonally, intrapersonally and with institutions, through an ongoing community-based art therapy mural project with young people who are system-involved in New York City. Tonight, 6:00pm ET, online.

Saturday | Spring Salon
The annual exhibition of multidisciplinary work by BFA Advertising and BFA Design students and recent alumni, featuring over 150 projects spanning graphic design, motion graphics, interaction, three-dimensional design and advertising concentrations. Tomorrow, April 9 – Saturday, April 30. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Jennifer Sirey in Bomb
Jennifer Sirey (1990 Fine Arts) explores the interplay of nature and nurture through her sculptural installations featuring bacteria in glass tanks. Via Bomb.
NYC PICKS

Marvels of Media: ReelAbilities Screenings
The Marvels of Media Awards is the first media awards ceremony, festival and exhibition to celebrate media-makers on the autism spectrum. Saturday, April 9, 1:00pm. Free admission with purchase of a museum ticket; $11 for students. Museum of the Moving Image, 36-01 35 Avenue, Astoria.

16mm Cartoon Carnival
Faculty member Tommy José Stathes hosts the 100th installment of Cartoon Carnival, his film screening series showcasing historic animation from the 1920s through 1950s in real 16mm film format. Tickets are $15. Sunday, April 10, 3:00 – 6:00pm. Rubulad, 338 Flushing Avenue, Brooklyn.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Deadline Extension: Love Letters to Survivors Art Event
April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the College’s Title IX Office invite students to send in empowering expressions of art for sexual assault survivors with messages to uplift and support. Submissions for #SurvivorLoveLetter will be open through Monday, April 11, 9:00am ET. On Thursday, April 14, 2:00 – 4:00pm, accepted submissions will be displayed in the SVA Student Center alongside an event where survivors and allies are invited to show support and contribute to the art display. To see entry guidelines and submit art, click here. To learn more about the #SurvivorLoveLetter movement, click here.

Next Week | Children of the First Uprising: Strategies for Change and Land Reclamation in Ecuador
SVA Humanities and Sciences faculty member Quito Ziegler and their class, Advanced Studies in Collective Action, in collaboration with the Indigenous Student Union, present indigenous land defender Emilio Paulo Chong. Chong will speak on the history of his people, the Wankavilka, an indigenous community that was never conquered by the Incas and is one of the oldest civilizations in South America, as he shares the ways they have regained ancestral territories from the Ecuadorian government, companies and descendents of colonial landowners. Monday, April 11, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.

This will also be an opportunity to receive an I-20 travel signature from Will. Friday, April 15, 2:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street.
