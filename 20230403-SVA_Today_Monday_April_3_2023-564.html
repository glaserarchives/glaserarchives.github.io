April Events and Exhibitions

Spring is here and SVA’s April calendar is filled with end-of-the-year exhibitions, thesis presentations and open studios from all around the College.

EVENTS

12:00pm | The Algorithmic State: Digital Repair With Kader Attia

MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with artist Kader Attia on notions of repair, both in terms of physical objects and societies, that help us to reconsider damage, dysfunction and trauma in relation to the ways in which AI is being deployed now and in the future. Today, April 3, 12:00 – 1:00pm, online.

7:30pm | John Wick: Chapter 4 Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of John Wick: Chapter 4. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Tonight, April 3, 7:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Ongoing Exhibitions

  • •“Cultivating Oneself,” through Monday, April 10. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
  • •“Shells for Teeth,” through Thursday, April 20. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
  • •“Début 23,” through Saturday, May 6. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

“Destiny Is a Hell of a Mapmaker”

Cultured chronicles the relationship between cult fashion designer Susan Cianciolo and her muse and collaborator, photographer Liz Goldwyn (BFA 1999 Photography).

NYC PICK

“After ‘The Wild’: Contemporary Art From The Barnett and Annalee Newman Foundation Collection”

An exhibition of art made by the recipients of The Barnett and Annalee Newman Foundation Award, including work by MFA Fine Arts alumni Luca Buvoli (1991), Gary Petersen (1987) and Sarah Sze (1997). $8 for students. Through Sunday, October 1. The Jewish Museum, 1109 Fifth Avenue and East 92nd Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Chance to Apply: Visual Opinion Is Hiring

Visual Opinion is seeking a managing editor and interns for the fall 2023 and spring 2024 semesters. Matriculated students located in the U.S. who are creative, proactive and organized are invited to apply. Visit the Visual Opinion’s Instagram at @visualopinion for position requirements and details. To apply, email your résumé, statement of interest and portfolio or portfolio sample to vo@sva.edu. Applications close today, April 3, 11:59pm. Questions? Email vo@sva.edu.

3x3 Digital Magazine Trial

The SVA Library has begun a 30-day digital trial of 3x3 Magazine from Exact Editions. 3x3 is a visually stunning title devoted to the best illustration happening across the globe. Check it out along with the library’s other Exact Editions digital subscriptions, in﻿cluding Aesthetica, AnOther, Another Man, Art Monthly, Cineaste, Cover, Creative Review, Dazed, Foam and Sight & Sound.

Submit to Urbanworld Film Festival

Calling all filmmakers: Urbanworld Film Festival is open for submissions! Don’t miss out on the opportunity to screen your film in one of the most diverse film festivals in New York City. Use the code “UW23STUDENT” for discounted student submission fees when submitting via FilmFreeway here through Thursday, June 1.

The Culture, Design and Fine Arts in New York Summer Program Is Hiring

SVA’s Culture, Design and Fine Arts in New York Summer Program, a three-week workshop for international college students, is seeking program assistants for July 2023. SVA students and recent alumni are invited to apply. Contact the Office of Programs for International Students at opis@sva.edu to request position details. To apply, email your resume and statement of interest to opis@sva.edu by Saturday, April 15.
