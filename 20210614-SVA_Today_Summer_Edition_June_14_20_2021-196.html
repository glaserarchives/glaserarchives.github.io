EVENTS THIS WEEK

Opening Tuesday | All-Staff Art Show 2021
An exhibition of works in various media by administrative staff members at the College. On view Tuesday, June 15 – Friday, July 2, at galleries.sva.edu.

Opening Tuesday | Making & Mentoring: Artist-Teachers Speak
An exhibition of paintings, photography and sculptures by 16 MAT Art Education graduates who have dedicated their lives to mentoring and teaching art to children and students of all ages. Click here to register for the opening reception tomorrow, June 15, 5:30 – 7:00pm ET. On view through Thursday, July 1, online at galleries.sva.edu.

Wednesday | Keynote Lecture: Ayana Evans
Join MFA Art Practice for a lecture by New York City-based artist Ayana Evans. Wednesday, June 16, 6:00 – 7:30pm ET. Registration required to join on Zoom.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, June 17, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Thursday | Artivism: Children on Guns in America
How does gun violence impact us all and what can we do as individuals and educators to bring about change? Join SVA Continuing Education and MA/MAT Art Education for an intensive workshop for in-service educators on the subject of gun violence in America facilitated by photographer Kathy Shorr (BFA 1988 Photography) and art educator Adrienne Mikulka (MFA 2004 Photography, Video and Related Media). Thursday, June 17, 4:30 – 6:00pm ET. Register here to receive a Zoom link.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Thursday | Baseball by Design: Looking at the Rich Visual History of Our National Pastime
Todd Radom (BFA 1986 Media Arts) will give the Worcester Art Museum's June Master Series lecture on his expertise of the graphic design for professional sports. Presented with the exhibition "The Iconic Jersey: Baseball x Fashion." Thursday, June 17, 6:00pm ET. Get tickets here.

Friday | Annecy Festival: Adapting Your Skills to Changes in the Industry with SVA Alumni
Attend the Annecy Festival and tune into this panel on Friday, June 18, 11:00am ET, during which SVA alumni will talk with BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects Chair Jimmy Calhoun (BFA 2003 Animation) about how they have used the skills they learned at school to make advancements in the creative and technological aspects of their work in animation, VFX, gaming and XR/AR/VR. Student tickets €25.

Friday | LIFELINES: Art, Intimacy, and HIV—an Intergenerational Conversation
Join artist Eric Rhein (MFA 2000 Fine Arts; BFA 1985 Fine Arts) in conversation with poet Mark Doty and writer and curator Paul Michael Brown as they celebrate and reflect upon the themes that run through Rhein's first monograph-memoir, Eric Rhein: Lifelines. Friday, June 18, 6:00 – 7:30pm ET. Register here to receive a Zoom link.

On View | PRIDE: A Celebration of Diversity, Identity and History
This exhibition, co-curated by SVA alumnus Efrem Zelony-Mindell and featuring work by fellow alumni Edward Gia, Dan Halm and Nicholas Loffredo, displays strong, personal work, as well as projects that frame the historical struggles of the LGBTQ+ community and its increasing acceptance into the ever changing mainstream.
