SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
September 4 - 10, 2017

An Illustrator's Call to Action

The latest trio of posters in the College’s Subway Series has hit the platforms, bringing BFA Illustration faculty member Edel Rodriguez’s work to riders around New York City.

August Hot Five

The top-five most-discussed project from SVA Portfolios last month feature sleek packaging design, fun drawings of monsters and masks, an illustrated nod to The Great Gatsby, and more.

SVA's New Welcome Center

Just in time for the new school year, a purpose-built space has opened on the ground floor and lower level of 342 East 24th Street, next to the 24th Street Residence, housing SVA's Admissions, Financial Aid and Student Accounts offices.
IN THE PRESS

Interviewing an "Iconic Illustrator"

Vice interviews MFA Illustration as Visual Essay Chair Marshall Arisman about his life and career in light of his retrospective, which is on view at the SVA Chelsea Gallery until September 16.

Are These Laws for Real?

Vogue interviews Olivia Locher (BFA 2013 Photography) about her debut photography book I Fought the Law, featuring pictorial enactments of ridiculous laws in each of the 50 states. The book will be published by Chronicle Books tomorrow, September 5.

Exploring Identity Through Art

Huffington Post profiles Monika Lin (MFA 2017 Art Practice), an artist whose struggle with her identity has enlarged and enriched the art she produces today.
EVENTS

Sean M. Starowitz

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by artist Sean M. Starowitz, whose work is executed in a variety of social, political and community engaged contexts. Tuesday, September 5, 6:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Art and Activism Panel

SVA Continuing Education presents an evening of interdisciplinary response to our uncertain times, featuring illustrator and faculty member Steve Brodner, actress, comedian and commentator Nancy Giles, artist, activist and faculty member Ed Woodham and actress, producer and writer Valerie Smaldone. RSVP to ce@sva.edu. Wednesday, September 6, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

"Occupy Rail" with Phong Bui

MFA Art Writing presents Brooklyn Rail co-founder and creative director Phong Bui, who will speak on "Occupy Rail," a two-part endeavor which seeks to encourage and support motivated individuals in various cities in the U.S. to create their own Rail publication. Thursday, September 7, 6:30pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
