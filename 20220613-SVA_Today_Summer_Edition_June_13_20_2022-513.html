SVA Returns to LA
For the first time in three years, SVA Premieres returned to Hollywood for a weekend of film screenings, talks with industry experts and alumni networking.

Celebrating Juneteenth
On Sunday, June 19, and Monday, June 20, we celebrate Juneteenth, a holiday commemorating emancipation and the immeasurable contributions and traditions of African American arts and culture. To honor the occasion, the SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Office has compiled a list of events, exhibitions, books, music, movies and more.
EVENTS THIS WEEK

Last Chance | “Guided by Echoes”
An exhibition of interactive installations, video projections and animations by 40 MFA Computer Arts students. Closes today. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Tuesday | i3 Photo Lecture: Endia Beal
MPS Digital Photography presents Endia Beal, a North Carolina-based artist, author and curator internationally known for her photographic narratives and video testimonies that examine the personal stories of Black women working within the corporate space. Tuesday, June 14, 7:00pm, via Zoom.

Wednesday | The Storyteller as Illustrator
MFA Visual Narrative presents the next installment of Storyteller’s Series, featuring Feifei Ruan (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative), a Chinese illustrator and visual storyteller. Feifei is the Gold winner of the Promax Awards 2019, Gold Medal winner of the SOI MoCCA Award 2018, Silver winner of the CLIO Awards 2020 and Bronze Cube winner of the ADC Awards 2019. Attendees must show their Cleared4 pass or proof of COVID-19 vaccination and booster and remain masked. Non-SVA community members must register to attend; RSVP here. This event is co-hosted by the SVA Library. Wednesday, June 15, 6:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Wednesday | Bio Art Residency Open Studios
An exhibition of work by artists in the Bio Art Residency at SVA’s Artist Residency Programs. Wednesday, June 15, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Registration required; RSVP here. BFA Fine Arts Building, 335 West 16th Street.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour on Thursday, June 16, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

Thursday | 2022 Keynote: Alfredo Jaar
MFA Art Practice presents artist, architect and filmmaker, Alfredo Jaar. Thursday, June 16, 6:00pm. Registration is required; RSVP here. MFA Art Practice, 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.

Friday | “Layers of Our Visible Past”
An exhibition of artwork by MA/MAT Art Education students, curated by department chair Catherine Rosamond. Reception: Thursday, June 30, 5:00pm. Friday, June 17 – Saturday, July 2. Registration required; RSVP here. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

NYC PICKS

Juneteenth Events
Juneteenth Unityfest 2022
A multi-artist celebration aimed at uniting people of all backgrounds and amplifying the many narratives of the Black experience in America through storytelling and music. Sunday, June 19, 6:30pm. Lena Horne Bandshell, Ninth Street and Prospect Park West, Brooklyn.

13th Annual Juneteenth NYC Festival
A virtual and live three-day celebration of Juneteenth through music, dance, poetry, skits, history, vendors and family activities. Friday, June 17, through Sunday, June 19. Free; register here. Linden Park/Gershwin Park and Prospect Park, Brooklyn.

Juneteenth Event Roundups
In their 2022 Juneteenth blog, Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA shared roundups of local holiday events from The New York Times, New York Family, NYC Parks, NY1 and Eventbrite. Click the links for more information!

Pride in the Park
New York City Opera’s annual LGBTQ+ Pride concert will feature a diverse program of selections from opera and musical theater sung by stars from City Opera’s Pride Series. Free. Friday, June 17, 7:00pm. Bryant Park, near 41st Street and Sixth Avenue.

The Coney Island Mermaid Parade
An annual art parade bringing mythology to life through pageantry, art and community pride. Saturday, June 18, 1:00pm. The parade begins at West 21st Street and Surf Avenue, Brooklyn.

New York International Children’s Film Festival Industry Forum
A three-part virtual forum connecting filmmakers, cultural organizers and industry leaders with the goal of expanding children’s media toward a more inclusive future. Free; register here. Tuesday, June 21, 5:00pm and Tuesday, June 28, 5:00pm, online.

“The Artist’s Experience: From Brotherman to Batman”
A historic exhibition featuring 16 Black sequential artists from all over the comic book spectrum, curated by culture journalist and writer Karama Horne (Marvel’s Protectors of Wakanda) and award-winning artist and BFA 1993 Illustration alumnus Shawn Martinbrough (Thief of Thieves, Red Hood). Student admission: $10. Through October 29. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Make-up Commencement Is Coming!
The make-up commencement ceremony for the class of 2020 and 2021 will be held Monday, June 27, 1:00pm, at Radio City Music Hall, with filmmaker, artist and author John Waters as keynote speaker. The event will be live-streamed on sva.edu, and on Facebook Live.

Next Week | Jurassic World: Dominion Screening
SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Jurassic World: Dominion. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Visit svatheatre.com for SVA Theatre’s current COVID-19 guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Saturday, June 25, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Next Week | Narcan Training
Join Nurse Abby Wohl from Student Health and Counseling Services for a virtual SVA staff and faculty Narcan training session to learn more about how to reduce risk and prevent an opioid overdose. As a Narcan trainee, you will receive your own Narcan kit. Register here for either date, Tuesday, June 28, 2:00pm, or Wednesday, June 29, 2:00pm. If you are unable to attend, additional trainings will be offered throughout the year. Email Wohl at awohl@sva.edu for more information.
#SVAwesome
“RE-ACTORS,” this year’s NYCxDESIGN exhibit by MFA Products of Design students, won the “Best of Schools Award” from ICFF +Wanted Design! The award was presented by the CEO of Alessi, Emanuele Gaeti, who spoke about the students’ work and what they had accomplished. Via MFA Products of Design.
