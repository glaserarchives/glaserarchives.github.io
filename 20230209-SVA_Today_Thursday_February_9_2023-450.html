“Origami in the Garden”

EVENTS

12:00pm | RA Selection Information Session

Interested in being a resident assistant (RA) this summer or next academic year? Attend one of the mandatory virtual information sessions to learn more about the position and receive the application materials. For the information session, attendees must be on video, signed on singularly (one candidate per device) and be present for all content. In order to apply you must attend one information session. Contact raselection@sva.edu with any questions or for more information. Today, February 9, 12:00pm, online via Zoom here.

12:00pm | The Algorithmic State: God Is AI

MA Curatorial Practice presents a virtual discussion with artist Zach Blas and curator Christiane Paul about the artist’s work and its ramifications for contemporary society under the regime of the digital. Today, February 9, 12:00 – 1:00pm, online.

1:00pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours

Every Thursday, 1:00 – 2:30pm, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).


Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu with any questions.

6:00pm | “Shades of Darkness” Reception

A reception for four BFA Fine Arts exhibitions: “Shades of Darkness,” curated by department chair Suzanne Anker; “Hanging by a Thread,” curated by faculty member and assistant to the chair Sam Sherman; “Prompt Response: Fusion of Forms,” curated by faculty members Joseph Tekippe (MFA 2006 Computer Art), Luis Navarro (MFA 2009 Computer Art), Federico Muelas Romero (MFA 2002 Computer Art) and artificial intelligence tools; and “From Here to There,” curated by faculty member Gunars Prande (MFA 1993 Fine Arts; BFA 1982 Media Arts). Tonight, February 9, 6:00 – 8:00pm. On view through Monday, February 27. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

6:30pm | Radical: A Political Graphic Novel

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the SVA Honors Program present cartoonist and writer Sofia Warren and New York State Senator Julia Salazar to discuss Warren’s new graphic novel, Radical: My Year With a Socialist State Senator. Tonight, February 9, 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

#SVAwesome

SVA faculty member Yuko Shimizo (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay), illustrator of The Cat Man of Aleppo—a book about Mohammad Alaa Aljaleel, who in the midst of the Syrian civil war offered safe haven to Aleppo’s abandoned cats—shared an update on the sanctuary in the aftermath of the recent earthquake, and a pledge to help with the region’s recovery and rescue efforts, on Instagram.
