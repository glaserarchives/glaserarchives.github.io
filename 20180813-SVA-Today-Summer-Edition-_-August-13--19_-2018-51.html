SVA Today
August 13 – 19, 2018

Before and After SVA: Antony Rush

Antony Rush (BFA 2017 Advertising) talks about working at a prestigious New York advertising agency and offers advice to incoming students.
THIS WEEK

Friday | BlacKkKlansman

SVA Theatre presents a screening of BlacKkKlansman, starring John David Washington and Adam Driver and directed by Spike Lee. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Friday, August 17, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | Drawing in the Park: The Great Lawn

SVA Continuing Education presents an evening of drawing in the park, where you can hone your observational skills and work in any medium you choose. Registration required. Saturday, August 18, 6:00 – 8:00pm. The Great Lawn, Central Park. Enter at 81st and Central Park West and meet at the waffle stand by the Delacorte Theater.

Saturday | "Tales from the Crit #2"

BFA Illustration presents an exhibition of work by artists from its class of 2018. Saturday, August 18 – Saturday, September 8. Reception date TBD. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
PRESS PICKS

Answers from Gail Anderson

AIGA's Eye on Design interviews creative director of the Visual Arts Press and SVA alumnus Gail Anderson about getting started in your career, being a designer of color, and more.

New York's Flower Guy

Forbes interviews Michael De Feo (BFA 1995 Graphic Design), a.k.a. The Flower Guy, about his artistic style and using urban landscapes his medium.
NYC PICKS

Brooklyn Poetry Slam

A gathering of Brooklyn's best slam poets perform, along with a documentary screening and an open mic session. First come, first served, as space allows. RSVPs are encouraged but not required. Monday, August 13, 7:00 – 9:00 pm. BRIC, 647 Fulton Street, Brooklyn.

Mixed Media Workshop: Scratchboard!

Learn new skills at this scratchboard workshop and take home your finished product. Free. RSVP here. Thursday, August 16, 10:00am – noon. Bryant Park Art Cart, Fifth Avenue Terrace of the New York Public Library.

"CON Art" by Dan Alva

A solo exhibition by street artist Dan Alva about the materialistic underbelly of well-known and popular ad campaigns. Free. Opening reception: Thursday, August 16, 7:00pm. Guy Hepner, 520 West 27th Street, 3rd floor.
TAKE NOTE

Call for Submissions: Submit a "Back to School" Image for Inclusion in SVA Newsletters

SVA Communication encourages students, faculty and staff to submit back-to-school-themed images for consideration to be featured in SVA Today and other communications. All mediums accepted. Please submit your work as a .jpg file to proffice@sva.edu, and include your name, major and graduation year for students, office or department for staff and faculty, and Instagram and Twitter handles (if you have them) in the email. Deadline: Wednesday, September 5.

Vote for Terrence Masson's SXSW Panel by Thursday, August 30

PanelPicker is the official SXSW user-generated session proposal platform, where the public can vote for which panels they would like to see at the next SXSW Conference. MFA Computer Arts Chair Terrence Masson has proposed a panel titled "Tickling Your Brain with VR," for which leading virtual reality pioneers will discuss the future of VR, augmented reality and mixed reality, how to encourage people to explore beyond these boundaries and what is being done now to do so. Click here to vote for Masson's panel by Thursday, August 30.

After School Special 2018

SVA Theatre presents the lineup for its annual free alumni film and animation festival, running September 9 – 16. Featured films and SVA alumni include Carlos Saldanha (Ferdinand), Shawn Martinbrough (Black Panther), Gini Santos (Coco), Derek Drymon (The SpongeBob SquarePants Movie), Dash Shaw (My Entire High School Sinking Into the Sea) and Jenni Morello (One of Us). The festival will also feature VR projects by Remington Scott (of MacInnes Scott). Click here for a full schedule. RSVPs not required; seating is first-come, first-served. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23d Street.
