FEATURE

SiTE:LAB's Paul Amenta On Site-Specific Art

In advance of tomorrow's talk on sustainable art practices with MFA Fine Arts alumni Paul Amenta (2000) and Julie Schenkelberg (2011), an interview with Amenta on SiTE:LAB, his award-winning arts nonprofit.
EVENT

Last Chance: BK BioReactor

An exhibition documenting an investigation of the Gowanus Canal's microbiology, by the latest Visible Futures Lab artists-in-residence, the BK BioReactor collective. Closes tomorrow, October 25. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Mark Tribe on Early Internet Art

The MFA Fine Arts chair talks to The New York Times about "Net Art" and Rhizome, the influential digital art organization he founded in 1996.
NYC PICKS

An Evening with Alexander Kluge

The acclaimed German filmmaker and writer presents and discusses a series of works whose themes relate to current events. $8 students, $10 seniors, $12 adults, members free. 7:00pm, MoMA, Theater 2, 11 West 53rd Street.

Mel Brooks, Young Frankenstein

The comedian and filmmaker discusses his classic 1974 film, in conjunction with the release of his latest book, Young Frankenstein: The Story of the Making of the Film. Limited admissions with $25.99 purchase of pre-signed book; sales begin at 9:00am. 7:00pm, Barnes & Noble, 33 East 17th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Chronicle Books Design Fellowship Info Session

Representatives from the Chronicle Books Design Studio in San Francisco will discuss their paid, one-year design fellowship program for graphic and industrial designers from the graduating classes of 2015, 2016 and 2017. The application deadline is April 3, 2017, and the fellowship will run from July 2017 to June 2018. This event is open to SVA students and alumni only. Registration required; RSVP at the link above. Wednesday, October 26, 4:00 - 5:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
