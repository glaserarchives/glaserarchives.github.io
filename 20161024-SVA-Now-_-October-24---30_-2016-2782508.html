SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
October 24 - 30, 2016

From Pixar to Beyoncé: 30 Years of Pioneering Computer Art

A selection of work by eight SVA MFA Computer Arts alumni from throughout the program's history. The department's 30th anniversary exhibition is now on view at the SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Three Must-See Fashion Films

Shorts from "Fashion Films, 2011 - 2016," an exhibition of work by MPS Fashion Photography graduates, now on view at the SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Q&A: SiTE:LAB's Paul Amenta

The MFA 2000 Fine Arts alumnus and co-founder of SiTE:LAB, an award-winning arts nonprofit, talks about site-specific art and community engagement.

Drawing Inspiration from NYC, Each Other [Video]

Three BFA 2016 Cartooning graduates on how their friendship and time at SVA shaped their work.

SVA Guide: West Village

Go off Manhattan's grid and explore the West Village's historic streets, with SVA's latest neighborhood guide as your compass.
IN THE PRESS

The "Genius" Lego Handbag Designed by SVA Students

A project by two BFA Design students at SVA goes viral.

A.V. Club Interviews Ti West

The BFA 2003 Film and Video alumnus talks about his new movie, In a Valley of Violence.

Guardian Q&A with Dash Shaw

The BFA 2005 Illustration alumnus talks about his directorial debut, My Entire High School Sinking Into the Sea.

War, Through an Artist's Eyes

Steve Mumford (MFA 1994 Fine Arts) talks to Creative Time Reports about his experiences as an artist embedded with the U.S. military in Afghanistan.

Geek on Chris Prynoski and Titmouse Inc.

A profile of the BFA 1994 Animation alumnus and the animation studio he co-founded with his wife, Shannon Prynoski (BFA 1994 Film and Video).
EVENTS

Creating a Sustainable Art Practice: Julie Schenkelberg and Paul Amenta

A talk with installation artist Julie Schenkelberg (MFA 2011 Fine Arts) and SiTE:LAB co-founder Paul Amenta (MFA 2000 Fine Arts). Presented by MFA Fine Arts and SVA Career Development. Tuesday, October 25, 6:00 - 8:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Russell Frederick

A talk by the photographer, best known for his documentation of Bedford-Stuyvesant, a historically black neighborhood in Brooklyn. Presented by MPS Digital Photography. Tuesday, October 25, 7:00 - 8:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

118 Years of Colonialism: A Series of Events

MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition addressing the current spirit of protest in Puerto Rico. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Natalia Viera Salgado. Thursday, October 27 - Friday, November 4; reception Thursday, October 27, 6:00 - 8:00pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

MFA Computer Art 30th Anniversary Exhibition and Celebration

A multimedia exhibition of work by alumni from throughout the program's 30-year history. On view through Wednesday, November 30; reception Friday, October 28, 6:00 - 8:00pm, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
