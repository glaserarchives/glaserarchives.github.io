NYC PICKS

First Friday at the Frick

Free admission to the museum and its events. Tonight, 6:00 - 9:00pm, Frick Collection, 1 East 70th Street.

The Holy Mountain

A screening of Alejandro Jodorowsky’s 1973 surreal fantasy film. Tickets $10; purchase here. Tonight, 9:30 - 11:45pm, Rubin Museum of Art, 150 West 17th Street.

Charles Dickens's A Christmas Carol at the Morgan

See the original manuscript of the author's holiday classic. Through Sunday, January 8. Admission prices vary. Morgan Library & Museum, 225 Madison Avenue.

"Graphic Alert: AIDS Posters from Around the World"

An exhibition of 25 AIDS-awareness posters from 19 countries, dating from the 1980s through the early 2000s. Curated by BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects faculty member Adrienne Klein. Free. Through Monday, January 30. The Graduate Center, City University of New York, 365 Fifth Avenue.

Holiday Angels Program Begins Today

SVA Student Affairs, The Hudson Guild Community Center and Abraham House are providing gifts for those in need through SVA's annual Holiday Angels Program. To view a list of children and their wishes, click here. Confirm your selection of a child to be sponsored by calling 212.592.2140, emailing akrumm@sva.edu or visiting Student Affairs, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. Cash donations are accepted in person only. The Hudson Guild also collects items for seniors, including socks, scarves, gloves, hats, new and lightly used coats, board games and books (multiple languages accepted). All gifts are due to Student Affairs Thursday, December 15, 4:00pm.

Matthew Burnett

MFA Products of Design presents a lecture by the co-founder of Maker's Row, an online American manufacturing marketplace. Wednesday, December 7, 7:00 – 8:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Workshop for Bystander Empowerment

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the Title IX Office have partnered with the Domestic & Other Violence Emergencies (DOVE) program from New York Presbyterian Hospital to host See Something, Say Whaaat?, a workshop to develop skills and understanding of how every person can intervene in instances of violence and assault. All students, staff and faculty are encouraged to attend. Light refreshments will be served. Thursday, December 8, 6:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Four Free Anniversary Screenings

On Saturday, December 17, SVA Theatre is presenting a full day of free anniversary screenings: Hook (1991) at 2:00pm, Rocky (1976) at 6:00pm, Little Shop of Horrors (1986) at 9:30pm and Scream (1996) at 11:59pm. Seating is first-come, first-served. RSVPs are appreciated, but not necessary.
