SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, September 8, 2015
BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker with current and former department students.
Soil Testing Bio Art Project Wins Gold Medal
A team of current and former BFA Fine Arts students, led by department chair Suzanne Anker, won Best Art and Design Project at the iGEM 2015 Giant Jamboree for their innovative soil testing kit that doesn't use or create toxins.
6:00pm: Business Basics for Artists
SVA Career Development presents an information session from the artist's perspective about the basic principles of copyright law and intellectual property, including royalties and licensing fees and contracts. Open to SVA fourth-year students, graduate students and alumni. Registration is required. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Press pick of the day
Underground Images in Cork, Ireland [Video]
In the Irish Examiner, SVA faculty member David Sandlin talks about the College's traveling exhibition of subway posters that is currently on view at the Cork Institute of Technology in Ireland. It is the first time the show has been exhibited in the country.
NYC picks of the day
"The Arbitrary Art of Numbers" Reception
Curator and faculty member Hans Tammen presents an exhibition of work exploring ways to visualize and make meaning of data. MFA Computer Art students Yuxi Cao and Yunling Song will perform at the reception. 6:00pm, Made in NY Media Center, 30 John Street, Brooklyn.
Ryan McGinley: Way Far
Photographer Ryan McGinley signs copies of his new book, Way Far. 6:00 - 7:30pm, Rizzoli Bookstore, 1133 Broadway.
Dennis Lim on David Lynch
Lim, author of a new biography of David Lynch, discusses the enigmatic filmmaker's life and work. Tickets are free and will be available at the box office on a first-come, first-served basis starting one hour prior to the talk. Limit one ticket per person. 7:00pm, Lincoln Center, Elinor Bunin Munroe Film Center, 144 West 65th Street.
Take Note
The MFA Question
Emerging Leaders of New York Arts (ELNYA) presents a panel discussion and information session about the pros and cons of MFA degrees and how to find the right program. Speakers include SVA Provost Jeff Nesin and ELNYA fellow Elizabeth Lee. Thursday, December 10, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
