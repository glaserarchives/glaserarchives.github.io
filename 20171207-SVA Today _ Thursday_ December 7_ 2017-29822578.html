SVA Today
Thursday, December 7, 2017

SVA Guide to Bushwick

Places to see, things to do and foods to eat in Bushwick, Brooklyn, as recommended by members of the SVA community.
EVENTS

10:00am | Fulbright Program Information Session

Learn about the Fulbright U.S. Student Program, a fully funded, year-long grant to travel abroad and immerse yourself in another culture and country while working on an art or art history project of your own design. SVA's Fulbright Program advisor Anna Ogier-Bloomer will provide details on the program, eligibility and requirements, how to apply and tips on creating a strong application. If you are interested in applying for a Fulbright, attendance at one of these sessions is strongly encouraged. Students of all class levels, as well as alumni and faculty, are welcome. Open only to U.S. citizens. RSVP here. 10:00 – 11:30am. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

1:00pm | MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Information Session

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents the opportunity to meet with the department’s chair, faculty, department staff, current students and alumni. Attendees are encouraged to bring a portfolio sample for review. RSVP here. 1:00 – 3:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.

Alison Wright: Human Tribe

MPS Digital Photography presents Alison Wright, a New York-based documentary photographer talking about her latest book. Signing to follow. 7:00 – 9:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
PRESS PICK

Top Honors for Jane Smith

Office Insight on how architect, entrepreneur and BFA Interior Design Chair Jane Smith won the prestigious President’s Award from the American Institute of Architects New York State chapter.
NYC PICKS

MoMA Corporate Member Shopping Day

Save 20% online and in-store at The Museum of Modern Art Design Store today. Present your business card or SVA ID in store or enter promo code 18CMH online at checkout. Faculty and staff only. MoMA Design Store, 11 West 53rd Street; 44 West 53rd Street; 81 Spring Street.

No Reservation Conversation and Book Launch

To mark the launch of the book No Reservation by Native American artist David Bunn Martine, Martine and three other leading Native American artists will discuss the diversity of Native art practices in New York City. Free. 7:00pm. Artists Space, 55 Walker Street.

Rodin at the Brooklyn Museum: The Body in Bronze

For the 100th anniversary of Auguste Rodin’s death in 1917, the museum presents an installation of its collection of Rodin bronze sculptures. Free with SVA ID. On view through April 2018. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE

Sean Hemmerle Reading and Book Signing

BFA Photography and Video, MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and Dear Dave, magazine present photographer and SVA faculty member Sean Hemmerle (MFA 1997 Photography, Video and Related Media), who will talk about his book Them, featuring portraits from Iraq and Afghanistan. A book signing will follow. Tuesday, December 12, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

SVA in San Francisco: Last Chance to Register to Spend Spring Break with Google, Pixar and ILM

Don't miss the chance to visit many of the leading animation, film and VFX houses from March 4 – 10, 2018. This class will introduce you to both the creative talent behind these companies and the recruiters who seek out interns, freelancers and staff. Virtual reality technology will be a focus on many of the studio visits, which include: Google Tilt Brush, Industrial Light & Magic, Tippett Studio, Pixar, Autodesk and Steel Wool Studios. Space limited to 12 students. One undergraduate studio credit. Tuition: $2,600 (does not include roundtrip airfare). Apply here. Deadline: Monday, December 18.
