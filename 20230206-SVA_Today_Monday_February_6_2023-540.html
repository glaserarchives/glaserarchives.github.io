“Making a Career Change”

Matt Williams (MFA 2019 Illustration as Visual Essay) talks about making the shift from footwear designer to illustrator for clients like Nike and Vogue. Via Creative Review.

NYC AND ONLINE PICKS

Lifting Women and Justice

Artist Shahzia Sikander and human rights attorney Becca Heller discuss what justice is today, how the legal field has advanced or failed women in juridical positions and how works of art guide transformation on central questions upholding entrenched systems. Presented by Madison Square Park Conservancy. Free. Tonight, February 6, 6:00 – 7:30pm. Sony Square, 25 Madison Avenue at 25th Street.

Celebrating Black History Month: What It Means to Be an Artist of Color

Artists Aleathia Brown (BFA 1987 Fine Arts), Larry Rushing and Khalid “Kha-Tees” Sabree discuss their work and the experiences that have shaped it. Presented by Artists Talk on Art (ATOA). Free. Tonight, February 6, 7:00 – 8:00pm, online.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Call for Submissions: 2023 HOPE Art Competition

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents the 2023 HOPE Art Competition. The creator of the winning piece will win a prize of $500 and all of the finalist’s work will be showcased in the SVA Flatiron Windows space at 133/141 West 21st Street. This year’s theme is a suicide prevention campaign because together, we can find hope. The artwork will serve as a reminder to SVA students that #YouAreNotAlone and #YourLifeMatters. You can find more information here, along with submission guidelines. The submission deadline is today, February 6.
