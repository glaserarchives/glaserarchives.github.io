News and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
September 8, 2016 - September 16, 2016

Yuko Shimizu's New Subway Posters [Video]

A Q&A with BFA Illustration faculty member Shimizu (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay) on the new posters she designed for SVA, which currently grace New York City subway stations.

Kelley Hensing Makes Her Marc

A Q&A with Kelley Hensing (MFA 2010 Illustration as Visual Essay) about the bedazzled and airbrushed jackets she created for Marc Jacobs.

Brand New Boop

Four MPS Branding students joined forces with King Features to rebrand the iconic character of Betty Boop.

‘Make It Here.’

SVA is the official education partner of the Interaction Design Association's 10th annual conference, Interaction 17, which will be held in New York City in February.
IN THE PRESS

Four Weeks for a Masterpiece

Entertainment Weekly previews the score of Rogue One: A Star Wars Story and interviews composer Michael Giacchino (BFA 1990 Film and Video) about the four and a half weeks he had to write the music. Rogue One is now in theaters.

MPS Digital Photo Alumni at Addis Fest

Aperture's list of photographers to look for at the 2016 Addis Foto Fest includes two MPS 2015 Digital Photography alumni: Tahir Karmali and Ima Mfon. The festival runs through Tuesday, December 20, in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.

From Drawing to Directing

IGN on how Marvel Entertainment's Chief Creative Officer Joe Quesada (BFA 1984 Media Arts) made his directorial debut with the company's new digital series, Slingshot.

Fighting Stereotypes with Art

The Huffington Post on how black women artists, including alumna Lorna Simpson (BFA 1982 Photography) and MFA Fine Arts faculty Mickalene Thomas and Wangechi Mutu, use art to challenge society's stifling stereotypes.
EVENTS

On Sustainability

Featuring work by BFA Interior Design students and curated by faculty member Ralph Ottaiano, this exhibition explores the pressing issue of globalization and its relationship to planetary health. On view through Thursday, January 12, at the SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

The Never Ending Project 2.0

An exhibition curated by BFA Interior Design third-year students and their Design Studio faculty, Jack Travis and Florencia Vetcher, "NEP 2.0" is the result of students of three different institutions—the School of Visual Arts, Penninghen, Paris, and The American University of the Emirates, Dubai—producing a collaborative project by meeting virtually and working around the clock, benefiting from the multiplication of teams in different time zones. On view through Thursday, January 12, at the SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
