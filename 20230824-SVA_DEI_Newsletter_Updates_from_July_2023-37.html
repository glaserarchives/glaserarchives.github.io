50 Years of Hip Hop


Hip Hop began on August 11, 1973, at a birthday party in the recreation room of an apartment building in the West Bronx, New York City. The location was 1520 Sedgwick Avenue, and the man who presided over that historic party was the birthday girl’s brother, Clive Campbell—better known to history as DJ Kool Herc, founding father of hip hop. The DJ has served as the foundation for what we recognize as the sound provider, accompanied by the messenger or MC (Master of Ceremonies).

Since its birth in the 70’s, Hip Hop has been embraced intergenerationally, spread internationally, and has positively influenced the lives of many disenfranchised people.  Hip Hop provides a voice to the voiceless, and a platform for those to express themselves authentically.  If you would like to know more about Hip Hop, please visit the Universal Hip Hop Museum in the Bronx, NY.
DJ Workshop and MC Competition with DJ Scratch


On Wednesday, July 19, in the third-floor amphitheater at SVA’s 209 West 23rd Street building, DJ Scratch, award-winning and internationally recognized DJ of EPMD, hosted a DJ workshop and MC competition.  DJ Scratch led a dynamic workshop on the art of turntablism and the history of hip hop’s influence on popular music, and shared personal narratives associated with his development as an artist. 

Standing behind the iconic setup of two turntables, a mixer, and microphone, DJ Scratch engaged the audience with popular call-and-response exchanges, provided the original sources of popular hip hop hits and masterfully fielded questions from an intercultural group of hip hop fans within the audience.  The second highlight of the evening occurred when random audience members courageously signed up for the MC Freestyle competition. The diverse group of contestants ranged in age, height and nationality.  

Their ability to rhyme within 60 seconds was judged by hip hop representatives Sommer McCoy, chief curator of the Mix Tape Museum; DJ Tymeek, producer and entrepreneur; and DJ, producer, and hip hop legend DJ Wiz from the group Kid N’ Play.  SVA Diversity, Equity and Inclusion looks forward to strengthening its relationship with these and many other hip hop leaders. This is an art form that has a symbiotic relationship with the visual arts, and we want to continue to foster and honor its global impact. In recognition of hip hop’s 50th anniversary, SVA DEI Office will be hosting several events related to the nine elements of hip hop. Please stay tuned.
