SVA Today
Wednesday, March 16, 2016
Present in Between by Idit Nissenbaum.
"Mentors" Preview
SVA Close Up previews the upcoming "Mentors" exhibition, which pairs BFA Photography and Video students with prominent figures in the arts community. This year's mentors include My Chemical Romance frontman Gerard Way (BFA 1999 Cartooning), photographer Adam Fuss and Pace/MacGill founder Peter MacGill. "Mentors" opens on Saturday, March 19, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Opening: "Help Is on the Way!"
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present “Help Is on the Way!,” an exhibition of work by the departments' students that salutes the work of Good Samaritans in a satirical "museum" of vehicles designed in an attempt to help others. Curated by Kevin O’Callaghan, chair of 3D Design. Reception Wednesday, March 23, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, April 9, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

NYC PICK OF THE DAY
New Directors/New Films 2016
The annual film festival introduces the work of emerging or not-yet-established filmmakers from around the world. Student tickets are $12, general admission $16. Films screen at Walter Reade Theater, Lincoln Center, 165 West 65th Street, and The Roy and Niuta Titus Theaters at MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street. Through Sunday, March 27.

TAKE NOTE
Discounted Student Tickets to Fun Home
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office is offering discounted $20 tickets to a performance of Fun Home, winner of the 2015 Tony Award for Best Musical, on Friday, March 18, 8:00pm. Tickets can be purchased daily at the George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine, 9:00am - 5:00pm. SVA ID required for purchase. Limit one ticket per student. Credit/debit only. For questions, contact activities@sva.edu.
Nicolas Lobo
MFA Fine Arts presents artist Nicolas Lobo discussing his work, which has incorporated materials such as grape flavored cough syrup, an aphrodisiac soft drink and Play-Doh. Tuesday, March 22, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Salman Rushdie In:Pictures
School of Visual Arts and The Modern School of Film present Booker Prize-winning author Salman Rushdie discussing and screening clips from the films Pather Panchali (1955), Star Wars (1977) and The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014), all of which have influenced and inspired him. Doors open at 7:30pm. Tickets are $5 for SVA students and $10 for faculty and staff. VIP reserved seating tickets are $60; $20 general admission. Saturday, April 9, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
