Your weekly guide to news and events from the School of Visual Arts
SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts May 23 - 29, 2016
FEATURES
Carrie Mae Weems speaks at SVA's 2016 Commencement. Photo by Joe Sinnott
How Do You Measure a Life? Carrie Mae Weems at SVA Commencement
On May 18 over 1,000 students walked across the stage at Radio City Music Hall and received their diplomas at SVA’s 41st formal commencement ceremony. The commencement speaker was photographer and MacArthur Fellow Carrie Mae Weems. Read the full story with slideshow.
Expanding Photographs
Photographer Mischelle Moy's (BFA 2016 Photography and Video) project "Branches of the Plum Tree" mixes photography with illustration to examine memory and family.
Students Top Advertising Awards
Students in BFA Advertising faculty member Frank Anselmo’s Unconventional Advertising class once again dominated the One Show Young Ones Competition this year.
A Visualization of Transition
Liz Ran Yang (BFA 2016 Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects) on her haunting short film Rinse and Repeat.
In the Press
The "Steven Universe Feeling" and the Future of the Verse
The Los Angeles Times interview's Rebecca Sugar (BFA 2009 Animation) on the launch of the third season of her hit show, Steven Universe.
"Art Is a Demanding Mistress"
Hyperallergic on artist Carrie Mae Weems' SVA commencement speech.
New York City, Caught on Canvas
Faculty member John Parks' work is featured on Artists Network and in The Artist's Magazine.
Events
Martin Puryear, Big Bling, 2016. Collection of the artist, courtesy Matthew Marks Gallery. © Martin Puryear. Photo by Rashmi Gill
Dreaming Public Art
Madison Square Park Conservancy and SVA present Dreaming Public Art, a symposium featuring a keynote conversation with artist Martin Puryear and Columbia University art historian Dr. Kellie Jones. Martin Puryear's Big Bling is on view in Madison Square Park through January 8, 2017. Tuesday, May 24, 7:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 W 23rd Street, New York, NY, free and open to the public, reservations required.
Lydia Panas
MPS Digital Photography presents Lydia Panas (BFA 1984 Photography) discussing her work, which has been featured in The New York Times Magazine, Photo District News and Popular Photography. Tuesday, May 24, 7:00 - 8:30pm, Room 418F, 136 West 21st Street
Popular Culture Is Where the Pedagogy Is: Explorations of Provocation and Praxis
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work by graduating students. Curated by Jasmine Wahi. Open daily noon - 7:00pm, Thursday, May 26 - Sunday, June 5. Reception May 26, 6:00 - 9:00pm, The Hole, 312 Bowery.
