Free Access to the Full Adobe Creative Cloud


Greetings:
I am very excited to share the good news that all current staff and faculty with an active teaching contract for this academic calendar now have access to the full Adobe Creative Cloud apps and services for ­free!  Starting today, you can sign in to Adobe and get full access. 
 
Follow these steps: 
1. Go to Adobe.com, 
2. Sign in with your SVA email address, 
3. Select Company or School Account and 
4. Finish logging in using your myID login credentials. 

Please note the following important points:
  • Please see the attached terms; by activating your account and logging in you are agreeing to the terms.
  • It is advised not to use Adobe cloud as the only means of storing your files; we cannot guarantee files will remain in Adobe Cloud for any length of time or the security of data.  Back up using your preferred method!
  • Note in the terms, our contract bars usage of Adobe Creative Cloud in mainland China, and anywhere else it is prohibited by law or Adobe policy.

Enjoy, and continue making great work with Adobe!
 

Warm regards,

Christopher J. Cyphers
Provost

NOVEMBER / 13 / 2019

       
As a student, staff member or faculty member of the School of Visual Arts (the “School”), you are being entrusted with access to the School’s systems and networks, and also to certain third party software products and services which the School makes available for your use either directly on School computers or via online access to web based products and services (we’ll refer to all of these systems, networks, products and services, collectively, as “Systems” from now on, and each one, as a “System”).
      When using any System, you must at all times comply with the following requirements:
  1. It is your responsibility to review and ensure you comply with all terms of use or terms of service applicable to each System you access or use.

  2. Note that your personal information submitted by you or collected by or through the use of any System when you access or use it is governed by the applicable privacy policy of the System in use (e.g., Adobe products and services are subject to the privacy policy posted on Adobe’s website, at www.adobe.com/go/privacy). You are responsible for reviewing all such privacy policies and notices, and understanding them. The School disclaims any liability for data collection and processing by third parties.

  3. You must use all on-premise software (that is, software that is loaded onto servers or computers at the School) solely while present on School premises and from the computers designated for such use. You must never try to log in to use such on-premise software remotely.
  4. You may log remotely into Systems only when the School has given you authorized access credentials for doing so, and when using Systems that have been designated by the School for such remote access.
  5. When logging in to use any System, you must use only your own authorized credentials issued to you by or on behalf of the School, and never those of another person, even if those other credentials belong to another student, staff member or faculty member.
  6. You must always log out of your session after you are done using any System.
  7. You must protect your log-in credentials for all Systems from unauthorized access, disclosure or use by anyone else. The credentials are issued to you alone, and must never be shared (unless the School gave you shared credentials which were meant to be used by multiple parties, as designated by the School). You are responsible for any and all activity that occurs using your credentials, even if not by you, so please protect those credentials carefully. If you think your credentials have been lost or compromised, please let the School know immediately so that they can issue you new credentials.
  8. You must never include any sensitive personal data (e.g., information about your religious beliefs, sexual history, preferences or identity, medical or health conditions or treatment, information about your criminal convictions or arrests, information about your race, ethnicity, country of origin, etc.) in or post them through any System.
  9. You must never use any System in a manner that violates the privacy or other rights of another person.
      
  10. You must never use any unauthorized, unlicensed, hacked, or jail-broken software, hardware or other items in connection with or in combination with any System.
      
  11. Do not use any Systems for data or file storage unless the School has informed you specifically that (i) such Systems are intended to be used for backup storage, and that (ii) your data and files will be held there indefinitely until you delete them. Most software and online services are not designed to hold your data or files indefinitely, and many third party service and software providers in fact delete data and files on a regular basis, so please be sure you back up your data and retain copies of it for yourself at all times. The School is not responsible for holding onto your data or files, and does not undertake to do so for you.

  12. You must never log into any System from outside the limits of the continental United States of America, unless the School has authorized you in writing to access the particular System you need from such jurisdiction, after written request. This authorization can only come from the School’s administrative bodies, and no individual faculty or staff member has authority to grant it.
  13. You may not make any copies of any software, website, code, files or documentation, in whole or in part, of any kind which are part of or connected to any System, without receiving express prior written instructions from the School that this is permissible to do. If the School authorizes it, you will only make the number of copies onto the media that the School has authorized, and no more.
  14. If you are permitted to make copies of any Systems or portions thereof (e.g., a particular software module), you must destroy and uninstall any such copies immediately when you cease being a student, or faculty or staff member of the School, or otherwise upon the School’s request.
      
  15. If you are granted a license to use a particular System on more than one device, you will end your session on the one device before initiating a new session on the next device. If you are not granted permission by the School to use any System on multiple devices, you shall select one device for use of such System, and not use any others. The School may designate some Systems for use only on PCs, and not for use on tablets or other mobile devices. You will follow and comply with all such instructions.
  16. You must never distribute, copy, publish or display any underlying content or materials available on any System, unless the School grants you specific permission to do so.
  17. You may not use any Systems to store, create, transmit, publish, display or process any content or information that is illegal, inappropriate or offensive. If the School identifies any of your files, information, content or data as illegal, inappropriate or offensive it may instruct you to delete it form all Systems, and you shall immediately comply.

  18. You may not under any circumstances access or attempt to access, control, disable, remove, use or distribute any electronic signature or digital credential features in any System for any purpose whatsoever.

  19. You shall not under any circumstances access or attempt to access any back- end or other systems, networks or devices which have not been made available to you by the School specifically for your use, and you shall not interfere with or attempt to interfere with, or do anything which shall interfere with anyone else’s use of any such back-end or other systems.
Please contact the helpdesk@sva.edu for assistance if needed.
