SVA Today
Tuesday, October 4
FEATURE

Exploring Feminine Identity

BFA Fine Arts presents a talk by artist and MFA Fine Arts faculty member Kate Gilmore (MFA 2002 Fine Arts), whose work explores themes of displacement and struggle through video, sculpture, photography and performance. Tonight at 7:00pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
EVENTS

11:00am: Yearbook Photoshoot

Portrait sessions for members of the class of 2017. 11:00am - 4:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor. Another photoshoot will take place Friday, October 7, 10:00am - 2:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 309.

3:30pm: Yoga and Meditation for Wellness and Creativity

Student Health and Counseling Services presents Nicole Brucato-Cooley leading a workshop that will introduce basic principles of yoga and meditation to promote well-being and foster creativity. RSVP to nsteiner@sva.edu. Open to SVA students. 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:00pm: Dread Scott

MFA Fine Arts presents faculty member and interdisciplinary artist Dread Scott discussing his work. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:30pm: Gringo: The Dangerous Life of John McAfee

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of the documentary on John McAfee, who went from millionaire software mogul to jungle recluse to prospective presidential candidate for the American Libertarian Party. Filmmaker Nanette Burstein will answer questions following the screening. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23d Street.

6:30pm: Seeing Science: Marvin Heiferman and Steel Stillman in Conversation

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents faculty members Steel Stillman and Marvin Heiferman discussing Heiferman's yearlong project Seeing Science: Photography, Science and Visual Culture. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

Damage Control

Readi, a disaster and crisis preparedness smart device designed by MFA Interaction Design students David Al-Ibrahim, Kohzy Koh and Elushika Weerakoon, was named a finalist for the 2016 Fast Company Innovation by Design awards.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY

New Photography

An exhibition of digital and analog photography, featuring work by Quinn Tivey (MFA 2016 Photography, Video and Related Media). Through Friday, December 23, BAM, 30 Lafayette Avenue, Brooklyn.

Photographing Politics: Campaigns, Protests, Demonstrations

A panel discussion on how photography documents the discord of politics. 6:30pm, International Center of Photography, 250 Bowery.
TAKE NOTE

Photography and Homoerotic Desire

BFA Photography and Video and the Museum of the City of New York present a panel discussion on how homoerotic photography found its way into New York City galleries and museums in the 1970s. Free for SVA students, $16 general admission. Friday, October 14, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
