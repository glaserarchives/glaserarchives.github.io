The Creative Minds Behind Krammer & Stoudt

A Q&A with Courtenay Nearburg (MPS 2013 Fashion Photography) and Mike Rubin, the creators of menswear label Krammer & Stoudt.
EVENTS

6:00pm | Street: NYC Photo Walk

Join SVA Continuing Education on a photo walk and refine your visual-language skills. RSVP here. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Location details will be shared with those who RSVP.

6:30pm | Gloria Steinem and Barbara Nessim: In Conversation

SVA Career Development presents Gloria Steinem and artist Barbara Nessim, who will talk about the role their long-lasting friendship has played in sustaining their work and how the context of New York City itself influenced their careers. The event will stream live here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

6:30pm | Group Chat

Three members of an artists' critique group—Elizabeth Glaessner, Jessica Stoller and Robin Williams—will present their work and experiences, discussing how to form an active group as a strategy to successfully move out of the classroom and into the world in a sustainable way. A Q&A will follow. Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

All About Nina

SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of All About Nina, followed by a Q&A with director Eva Vives and star Mary Elizabeth Winstead. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Tomorrow, September 25, 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

"Outside These Walls" Reception

BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of artworks made outside of SVA's facilities during the last year. Organized by the program's director of operations, Tyler Rowland. Wednesday, September 26, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Saturday, October 13. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21 Street.

Global Guest Lecture: On Emergency Preparedness Solutions

MFA Design for Social Innovation presents the president of +Arts and vice director of the Design and Creative Center Kobe Hirokazu Nagata and art director, graphic designer and illustrator Bumpei Yorifuji, who will discuss emergency preparedness and design solutions developed after the 1995 Kobe Earthquake in Japan. They are part of the Earth Manual Project, an international design project that helps people prepare for natural disasters. Wednesday, September 26, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Mario Sorrenti and Dennis Freedman

BFA Photography and Video presents photographer Mario Sorrenti in conversation with creative director Dennis Freedman. Tuesday, October 9, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
