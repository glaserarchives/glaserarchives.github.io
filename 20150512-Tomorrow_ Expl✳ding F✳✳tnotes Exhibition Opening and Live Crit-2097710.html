Exploding Footnotes: Design Research in Action

Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism
School of Visual Arts
136 West 21 Street, Second Floor
Between 6th and 7th Aves
 
Opening Reception and Live Crit: Evening of Wednesday, May 13, 5:00–10:30 p.m.
Exhibition: Wednesday, May 13–Monday, May 18, 2:00–8:00 p.m.

As you can see, exhibition installation is in full swing here in the SVA Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism! "Exploding Footnotes: Design Research in Action” is an exhibition that turns the spotlight on the thesis research process. It seeks to retrieve the gold dust often buried in footnotes at the bottom of the page, to expose and examine the behind-the-scenes travails, legwork, drama, breakthroughs, doubts, and dead-ends that are a part-and-parcel of in-depth research, but normally smoothed over by the linear narrative of scholarly writing. By zeroing in on these tiny superscript numerals and the riches they contain, we celebrate the research process as well as its products.

The exhibition opens tomorrow with an event where the graduating students deliver tightly honed presentations of their thesis work, followed by a live critique led by invited guest critics, and moderated by Nicola Twilley. There's still time to reserve your spot at the exhibition opening and Live Crit event.
 
The exhibition is organized by the SVA MFA Design Criticism Class of 2015, collaborating with Superscript co-founder Molly Heintz, graphic designer Neil Donnelly, and HAO / Holm Architecture Office.
