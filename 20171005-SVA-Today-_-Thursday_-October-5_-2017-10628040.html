SVA Today
Thursday, October 5, 2017

SVA at NY Comic Con

Comic Con returns to the Javits Center from today through Sunday, October 8, and SVA will be there to showcase work from its students and alumni.
EVENTS

6:00pm | So You Want to Be an Activist?

Come meet local community leaders from Dominicanos USA (DUSA) and learn about the importance of being civically engaged and how to make change happen. Voter registration forms will be available. Open to SVA students. Light refreshments and food will be served. 6:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Leigh Ledare and Steel Stillman

BFA Photography and Video and Dear Dave, magazine present artist Leigh Ledare and artist and writer Steel Stillman, who will discuss Ledare’s current solo exhibition at The Art Institute of Chicago and the complex interpersonal dynamics that are at the heart of his work. 7:00 – 8:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Behind the Scenes of New York Indie Theater

PDN on Kent Meister's (MPS 2017 Digital Photography) photo series "We Are Stories: Face of New York Indie Theater," which documents the "camaraderie and hard work that goes on backstage."
NYC PICKS

Giggles N' Cream

A free comedy show, followed by free ice cream. 7:30pm. The Lantern, 167 Bleeker Street.

Black Intimacy

A film series that explores the ways in which black familial, romantic and platonic relationships have been portrayed onscreen. Through Monday, October 16; schedule here. Tickets free with SVA ID, but must be picked up in-person. The Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

Student Appreciation Week at SVA Campus Store

From Monday, October 9, through, Friday, October 13, SVA Campus Store will be giving two free gifts (an ID holder and a candy cup) to all students who visit the store. Receive a free hard case and a 500GB external hard drive with any Apple notebook purchase. There will also be discounts on SVA merchandise throughout the week, and a student raffle on Wednesday, October 11, 3:00pm. SVA Campus Store, 207 East 23rd Street.
