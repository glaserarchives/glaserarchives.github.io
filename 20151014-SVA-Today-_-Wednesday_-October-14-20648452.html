SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, October 14, 2015
Pursuing Challenges as an Artist [Video]
Illustrator Yuko Shimizu (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay) talks about teaching SVA students how to create a body of work that shows who they are.
11:00am: Behind the Scenes of Pixar's "The Good Dinosaur"
BFA Animation, MFA Computer Art, BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects, BFA Film and SVA Career Development present a behind-the-scenes look at Pixar's newest film, The Good Dinosaur, with animator Kevin O'Hara. Open to SVA students, alumni, faculty and staff. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:00pm: The 36th Annual W. Eugene Smith Grant in Humanistic Photography
BFA Photography and Video presents the 36th annual W. Eugene Smith Grant in Humanistic Photography and Howard Chapnick Grant for the Advancement of Photojournalism. The program includes presentations of photo essays by this yearâ€™s grant and fellowship recipients and finalists, a unique tribute to the work of W. Eugene Smith and a special keynote speech. 7:00pm: Michael Bierut in Conversation with Steven Heller
Michael Bierut, recipient of the 2015 Masters Series Award, discusses his singular career in logo, graphic and exhibition design with MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller. Presented in conjunction with the exhibition "The Masters Series: Michael Bierut," on view at the SVA Chelsea Gallery through Saturday, November 7. This event will be live-streamed on YouTube. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
If You Can See It, It Can See You
MFA Social Documentary Film faculty member Kirsten Johnson speaks with The Intercept about her new short film, The Above, which examines surveillance of Afghan communities and recently premiered at the New York Film Festival.
NYC picks of the day
Architecture & Design Film Festival
Celebrating the unique creative spirit that drives architecture and design, the ADFF features films, events and panel discussions. Venue locations and ticket prices vary. Through Saturday, October 17.
Roy Lichtenstein: "Greene Street Mural"
The final viewing days of Lichtenstein's site-specific work, originally made for the Castelli Gallery in 1983. Through Saturday, October 17, Gagosian Gallery, 555 West 24th Street.
Take Note
CORRECTION: T.J. Demos Live From Caracas
MA Curatorial Practice presents a special live-stream of After Landscape: Perspectives and Traces of the Traveler, a conference from Caracas, Venezuela, featuring art historian and cultural critic T.J. Demos' lecture "Decolonized Nature: Art and Politics of Earth Jurisprudence." Demos' talk begins at 12:00pm and will be broadcast in English; the rest of the conference will be in Spanish. Friday, October 16, 12:00 - 3:15pm, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Mirrorless Camera
MPS Digital Photography and Sony present department chair Katrin Eismann, Sony Alpha product manager Kenta Honjo, photojournalist Ben Lowy, photographer and filmmaker Ben Moon and portrait and wedding photographer Miguel Quiles in a panel discussion on how technological developments such as the mirrorless camera have improved the quality of digital photography, reduced the size and weight of cameras and increased photography's accessibility. Moderated by Pulitzer Prize-winning celebrity and portrait photographer Brian Smith.Tuesday, October 20, 7:00pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
