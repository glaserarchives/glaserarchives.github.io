SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Friday, November 6, and the weekend
SVA and the Peanuts Gang
The Peanuts Movie, out today, is a testament to the hard work of many BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects and MFA Computer Art alumni and faculty. SVA Close Up spoke to Alen Lai (MFA 2000 Computer Art) about his experience leading Blue Sky Studios' FX team during the film's production.
Open Houses and Information Sessions
This weekend, SVA hosts an undergraduate open house for BFA Film, BFA Interior Design and BFA Photography and Video, as well as graduate information sessions for MFA Computer Art, MPS Fashion Photography, MFA Illustration as Visual Essay, MFA Products of Design, MFA Social Documentary Film and MFA Visual Narrative.
"Me U and the Robot" Opens
MFA Fine Arts presents a collaborative performance by department students Eli Barak and Ragnheiður Káradóttir, who will transform the white space of the gallery into an alternate world filled with sculptures and found-art objects that explore the potential of chain reactions and unhindered play. Curated by department students Jonathan Shouela and Ali Shrago-Spechler. Through Monday, November 30. Reception on Friday, November 13, 6:00 - 8:00pm. VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
6:30pm: The Doli Healing Project
MPS Art Therapy presents Jennifer Tedesco (MPS 2007 Art Therapy) discussing doll-making art therapy intervention for victims of gender-based violence. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
7:00pm: I'm Making a Boy Band
MA Curatorial Practice, Asia Contemporary Art Week (ACAW), the Asian Contemporary Art Consortium and SCREEN present a performance of original songs by EXP, the reverse-engineered K-Pop band that is part of the conceptual group I'm Making a Boy Band (IMMABB) led by artist Bora Kim. A conversation with Kim, Karin Kuroda, Samantha Y. Shao and EXP will follow the performance. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Saturday: David Campany
DEAR DAVE, magazine presents writer, curator and artist David Campany speaking about his new book, A Handful of Dust (MACK, 2015), with SVA faculty member Adam Bell. 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Sunday: Max Kozloff, Unfolding Photography Criticism
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a symposium on the work of photographer and critic Max Kozloff. Presenters include curator Sandra Phillips, author and scholar Phillip Lopate, photographer Duane Michals and Yale School of Art Dean Robert Storr. Moderated by SVA faculty member Lyle Rexer. 2:00 - 5:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
More Productive? Take Time Off
Time's list of eight TED talks that will make you more productive includes MFA Design faculty member Stefan Sagmeister's talk about the value of time away from work.
NYC weekend picks
Nothing Personal at Big Apple Film Festival
BFA Film faculty member Ralph Toporoff's short film Nothing Personal screens as part of a short film program. Tickets are $20. Saturday, November 7, 10:30am, Village East Cinema, 181 - 189 Second Avenue.
Comic Arts Brooklyn
Brooklyn-based bookstore and publisher Desert Island presents more than 100 cartoon, comic and illustration artists exhibiting and selling their work. Saturday, November 7, 11:00am - 7:00pm, Mt. Carmel Gymnasium, 12 Havemeyer Street, Brooklyn.
Francis Bacon at Gagosian
Gagosian New York presents an exhibition of late work by the painter known for his stark self-representations. Saturday, November 7, through December 12. Gagosian Gallery, 980 Madison Avenue.
Dark Circuits II
Culture Hub presents the Improvised Electronics Round Robin performing the Dark Circuits Light Orchestra, composed by SVA faculty member Hans Tammen. $10 suggested donation. Sunday, November 8, 8:00pm, Culture Hub, 47 Great Jones Street, 3rd floor.
Take Note
Teresita Fernández Event Cancelled
Please note that the Teresita Fernández event scheduled for Wednesday, November 11, at the SVA Theatre has been cancelled.
John C. Welchman on Mike Kelley
MFA Fine Arts presents John C. Welchman discussing artist Mike Kelley's contributions to contemporary art, including his influential body of critical and creative writings, his special brand of parodic humor and the lifelong internal conflicts that may have led to his suicide in 2012. Tuesday, November 10, 6:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
PMDA Salutes Photographers Who Give Back
The School of Visual Arts, PDN magazine and the Photoimaging Manufacturers and Distributors Association (PMDA) honor three professional photographers and one student photographer who have used their talents to give back. The special event features presentations by honorees Tamara Lackey, Colby Brown, Ted Kawalerski and the student recipient of the Anthony M. Ruotolo Photojournalism Award. Tuesday, November 10, 6:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MFA Art Practice Information Session
MFA Art Practice presents an information session and facilities tour for prospective students, with department chair David A. Ross, faculty, students and alumni. Tuesday, November 10, 6:30pm, 335 West 16th Street, 5th floor.
