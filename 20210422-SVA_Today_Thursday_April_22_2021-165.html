Statement on Recent Police Shootings
On Tuesday, a jury convicted former Minneapolis police officer Derek Chauvin of murder in the death of George Floyd. However, nearly a year after Mr. Floyd’s tragic and senseless death at the hands of police, all of the issues surrounding his death remain largely unchanged.

In light of the continued police shootings in America of unarmed people of color, SVA's Provost Dr. Christopher J. Cyphers and Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion Dr. Jarvis Watson have released a statement reiterating that SVA stands against the excessive use of force by police, especially that aimed at BIPOC people; supports the Black Lives Matter movement; and maintains its commitment to working toward a more diverse, equitable and inclusive campus and community. Click here to read the full statement, which includes short- and long-term action steps that the College is taking to address the concerns of our BIPOC communities and allies.

10:30am | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Within the past year, members within the Asian and Asian American communities have been facing an increased number of instances of harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Today, 10:30am – 12:00pm ET, the DEI Office is hosting a space for open conversation with our AAPI peers across campus about how they have been coping. Faculty and staff who are interested and who self-identify as AAPI can fill out this Google Form and register for the Zoom call at the end of the form.

Please note: As we begin to develop more inclusive campus-wide conversations and initiatives, there will be opportunities for allies to be involved and provide support.
