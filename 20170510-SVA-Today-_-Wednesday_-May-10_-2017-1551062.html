Staying Connected Post-Graduation

Congratulations to the class of 2017! In this article from the spring 2017 Visual Arts Journal, Director of Alumni Affairs and Development Jane Nuzzo shares the joys and benefits of staying connected with SVA after graduation.
EVENTS

1:00pm | Encore!: BFA Computer Art Thesis Screening

Members of the BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects class of 2017 present their thesis projects. 1:00 – 4:00pm. Walter Reade Theater, Lincoln Center, 165 West 65th Street.
PRESS PICK

"Rising Through Difficulties"

Dangerous Minds profiles artist and designer James Jean (BFA 2001 Illustration), who "rose through personal difficulties to achieve incredible success."
NYC PICK

Making It Here

A conversation between author, activist and undocumented Mexican immigrant Julissa Arce and entrepreneur AJ Gogia, who will share their stories of struggle and achievement in New York. Tickets $20 – $25; purchase here. 7:00 – 8:30pm. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Ave.

On Antoine de Saint Exupéry

Antoine de Saint Exupéry’s grandnephew Olivier de Giraud d’Agay, filmmaker Mark Osborne, and Pulitzer Prize-winning author Stacy Schiff will discuss the life and work of the aviation pioneer and internationally renowned writer. Free; register here. 7:00 – 8:30pm. Albertine, 972 Fifth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Last Chance: Distinguished Staff Award Nominations

It's time to nominate one of your colleagues for the 2017 Distinguished Staff Award. The nomination form, a list of eligible staff members and further details are available at staffawards.sva.edu. Deadline for submissions is Friday, May 12, 5:00pm.

2017 LDV Vision Summit

A summit where experts from companies like Google, Facebook, Union Square Ventures and First Round Capital discuss how computer vision, machine learning and artificial intelligence are disrupting business and society. Tickets are $25 for students, $50 for professors. Contact abby@ldv.co by Monday, May 15 for the promotional code to register. Wednesday, May 24 – Thursday, May 25. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
