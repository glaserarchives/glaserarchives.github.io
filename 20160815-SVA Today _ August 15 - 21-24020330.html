SVA Today
August 15 - 21
FEATURES

The Anti-Instagram

From the spring issue of the Visual Arts Journal, Clint Woodside (BFA 2001 Graphic Design) talks about Deadbeat Club, the indie publishing company he founded that produces limited-edition print zines.

SVA Short Takes: Documenting Protest [Video]

Watch a clip of Accra Shepp discussing his portraits of Occupy Wall Street protesters. His talk was presented by MFA Fine Arts.

SVA Students Win Donghia Foundation Scholarships

BFA Interior Design students Lucie Meichun Cai and Stephanie Chan were among those selected as winners of the Angelo Donghia Foundation's 2016 Student Scholarship Program in Interior Design, and will receive up to $30,000 in scholarships for their senior year tuition, board and other expenses.
THIS WEEK

Monday (8/15): The Fly

The SVA Theatre presents a 30th anniversary screening of David Cronenberg's 1986 film, starring Geena Davis and Jeff Goldblum. Admission is free. Seating is first-come, first-served; doors open approximately 45 minutes prior to showtime. 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Tuesday (8/16): "In Cylinder" Reception

A juried exhibition of work by SVA students and recent graduates that confronts different forms of identity and the challenges of the present moment. Organized by SVA Galleries. 5:00 - 7:00pm, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street. Exhibition runs through Saturday, September 10.

Thursday (8/18): Kubo and the Two Strings

Focus Features and Laika present an advance screening of Kubo and the Two Strings, featuring voice work by Charlize Theron, Matthew McConaughey and Ralph Fiennes. Travis Knight, president and CEO of Laika, and Art Parkinson, voice of Kubo, will introduce the film. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff; free with SVA ID. 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Friday (8/19): Sausage Party

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of Sausage Party, featuring the voices of Seth Rogan, Salma Hayek, Jonah Hill, James Franco and Kristen Wiig. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff; free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday (8/20): "The Beat Goes On"

An exhibition celebrating the connection between music and art, with four listening room installations created by Elia Alba, Kevin Beasley, Paul D. Miller (DJ Spooky) and Tameka Norris (Meka Jean). Curated by visual artist Derrick Adams. Saturday, August 20, through Saturday, September 17, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

An exhibition of photographs by David Burnett that documents Olympic athletes since 1984. Through Sunday, September 25, Anastasia Photo, 143 Ludlow Street.

Nan Goldin: The Ballad of Sexual Dependency

An exhibition of nearly 700 portraits set to a music soundtrack. Admission is free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, February 12, Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53rd Street.

The Big Chill

A free screening of the 1983 film, starring Tom Berenger, Glenn Close and Kevin Klein. The lawn opens for seating at 5:00pm; screening starts between 8:00 and 9:00pm. Monday, August 15, Bryant Park, 42nd Street at Sixth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Elia Alba Performance

As part of "The Beat Goes On," Elia Alba will perform in her listening room, which is set up as a homage to legendary DJ Larry Levan. The room recalls the church-like and spiritual experience of Levan’s events, with a dimly lit atmosphere, DJ setup, photographic works, sculptural masks, disco ball, checkered dance floor and video projection. Saturday, August 27, 4:00 - 6:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

After School Special 2016: Alumni Film & Animation Festival

SVA presents its annual, multi-day festival featuring some of the College’s most accomplished film, television, animation and visual effects alumni, which will include screenings, premieres of new work, behind-the-scenes looks at cinema and television hits and classics, special talks with industry insiders, and more. Thursday, September 15, through Sunday, September 18, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
