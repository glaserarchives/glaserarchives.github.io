SVA Today
August 15 – 21, 2022
Lynsey Addario photographed by Sam Taylor Johnson.

32nd Annual Masters Series: Lynsey Addario
SVA has announced Pulitzer Prize-winning photographer Lynsey Addario as the recipient of its 32nd Masters Series Award and Exhibition.
EVENTS THIS WEEK

Last Chance | “Outro: World-Building Within and Outside the Studio”
MFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of thesis projects by its class of 2022, curated by Herb Tam, curator and director of exhibitions at the Museum of Chinese in America. Closes today, August 15. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Ongoing | “Other Visions”
An exhibition of multidisciplinary work exploring hidden structures and unique ways of seeing through language, identity and light. Through Monday, August 22. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “Dwelling in Motion”
A juried exhibition of works that analyze the constantly growing relationship between habitat and inhabitant. Through Monday, September 19. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
SVA IN THE PRESS

“3 Days. 68 Kids. Snapshots of Summer on Coney Island.”
SVA faculty member Caroline Tompkins (BFA 2014 Photography) captures portraits of kids at Coney Island for The New York Times.

2022 National Book Festival Poster Design
Chair of BFA Advertising and BFA Design Gail Anderson (BFA 1984 Media Arts) on how she communicated the theme of “Books Bring Us Together” in her poster design for the 2022 National Book Festival, hosted by the Library of Congress.

Women’s International Film Festival Recognizes Lisa Durden
Lisa Durden (MFA 2021 Social Documentary Film) was honored by Women in Media–Newark at the Women’s International Film Festival earlier this month, for her work giving a platform to “underserved and overlooked” voices.

On the Fickle Nature of Creativity
Yam Chew Oh (MFA 2019 Fine Arts) talks about collecting, inspiration and mixing mediums in an interview for BmoreArt.
NYC PICKs

Book Talk with Laura June Kirsch
A conversation with photographer Laura June Kirsch (BFA 2007 Photography) on her monograph, Romantic Lowlife Fantasies. Wednesday, August 17, 7:00pm. Launch Photo Books, 59 Orchard Street.

Harlem Bazaar
A monthly market in Harlem featuring artists, merchants and food vendors. Friday, August 19, 3:00 – 9:00pm. Adam Clayton Powell Jr. State Office Building, 163 West 125th Street.

“More Art of Coney Island”
A group exhibition including work by Lynn Pauley (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay). Through Sunday, August 28. Brooklyn Waterfront Artists Coalition, 481 Van Brunt Street, Brooklyn.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Next Week | 2022 All-Staff Meeting
The 2022 All-Staff Meeting takes place next week on Thursday, August 25. To ensure everyone’s safety, your SVA ID is required and you must have completed the Cleared4 process at least two hours prior to the start of the meeting in order to enter the theater. Your SVA ID is required for check-in. Masks are mandatory when indoors.

For those unable to attend in person, the meeting will be live-streamed. Pre-register here to receive your link to the program.

Create a Video/Animation, Win $500
SVA Theatre seeks a 10 – 20 second pre-roll video (animated or live action) to play before film screenings shown during this September’s After School Special 2022: SVA’s Alumni Film and Animation Festival. Set the video to music that you have created yourself, own the copyright for, have obtained a license for, or is in the public domain, and please note its source when submitting. Download logos for the festival, SVA and SVA Theatre here. View examples of past winning videos here and here.

Upload submissions to Vimeo or YouTube, and send an email with a link to the video, your name, graduation year and program major to Adam Natale, director of SVA Theatre, at anatale@sva.edu, by Friday, August 26, 5:00pm ET. Questions may also be directed to Natale. Final video format should be a digital file (e.g., QuickTime ProRes). The winner will receive $500.
