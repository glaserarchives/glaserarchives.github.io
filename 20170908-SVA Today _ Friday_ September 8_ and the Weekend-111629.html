Study Sessions: Che Gossett

Che Gossett, black trans femme writer and archivist at Barnard Center for Research on Women, will select a work of art on view in the Whitney’s permanent collection galleries and use it to think through an urgent question in our current political landscape. Free with SVA ID; register here. Tonight, 7:00pm. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.

Hurricane Relief

Hurricane Harvey has devastated communities along Texas's coastline, and Hurricane Irma continues to inflict damage in the Caribbean as it approaches Florida. You can help relief efforts by making a donation to organizations rated by reputable organizations like Charity Navigator, including local food banks, animal rescue organizations, and more. And should you need support, please reach out to Student Health and Counseling Services at health@sva.edu or by phone at 212-592-2246.

IMAGE OF THE WEEKEND
Charles Traub (Chair, MFA Photography, Video and Related Media), Ralph Eugene Meatyard, 1969, Lexington, Kentucky. On Tuesday, September 12, Traub and filmmaker Don Simandl will discuss the history and legacy of Meatyard, leader and mentor of the Lexington Camera Club in Kentucky in the 1960s, in anticipation of Simandl's upcoming documentary on the topic. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
