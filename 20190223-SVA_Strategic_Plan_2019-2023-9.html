Dear Members of the SVA Administration,
I am pleased to announce the formal adoption by the SVA Board of Directors the College’s Strategic Plan, 2019-23. A copy of the full plan can be viewed and downloaded here.
The plan was crafted by a dedicated group of SVA faculty and administration, led by Associate Provost Emily Ross, with guidance offered by immediate past Provost Jeffery Nesin and me, your current Provost. Well over 100 members of the SVA community were interviewed as part of developing the plan. And our Executive Vice President and President offered valuable input throughout the process of drafting and, ultimately, presenting to the Board, the final version of the plan.
Creating a new strategic plan offered an occasion to review and update the College’s mission, which now reads: “To educate future generations of global creative citizens to foster cultural and social change that promote our core values through the pursuit and achievement of their professional goals.”
The Core Values referenced in the mission statement, include:
  • Participation in the Global Creative Community
  • Diversity and Inclusion
  • Freedom of Expression
  • Professionalism and Integrity
Our mission and core values, when set in the context of our recognized strengths, future challenges, and institutional vision, inform a set of five strategic priorities that all members of the SVA community will strive to accomplish over the life of the new strategic plan.  These strategic priorities (listed here in no particular order), include:
  1. Instilling a standard of excellence in our students through a dynamic and focused curriculum
  2. Maintaining and sustaining a faculty of working professionals active in their fields
  3. Recruiting and supporting a fully inclusive community of students, faculty, staff and alumni
  4. Encouraging diversity, experimentation and implementation of bold concepts that challenge conventions
  5. Engaging with and contributing to the vibrant cultural and professional landscape of New York City
To move the College forward towards accomplishing the goals embedded in each of these priorities, a set of planning committees that represent a broad cross-section of the College community will spend the next six to nine months developing a set of sub-plans, each aligned with these five strategic priorities.  You can learn more about these sub-plans and their planning committee chairs by reviewing the strategic plan.
