SVA Today
Monday, March 28, 2016
Résumés, Portfolios, Interviews, Oh My!
SVA Career Development presents its annual career fair for third- and fourth-year undergraduate students, graduate students and alumni from the class of 2015. Attendees will have the chance to meet with recruiters from IBM, Ralph Lauren, Refinery29, and more. A full list of companies is available at sva.edu/career. Registration deadline is tomorrow. The fair will take place on Friday, April, 1, 1:00 - 4:00pm, Metropolitan Pavilion, 123 West 18th Street, 4th floor.
Last Chance: "The Student Looking in the Mirror"
An exhibition of self-portraits by first-year drawing students, curated by BFA Fine Arts faculty member Brooke Larsen. Closes today, SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Sci-fi in Bangkok
The Bangkok Post interviewed Stephan Zlotescu (MFA 2007 Computer Art) about his short film True Skin, which was filmed throughout the city and recently optioned by Amazon Studios for a potential sci-fi series.
NYC PICK OF THE DAY
Presenting Shakespeare: Posters From Around the World
MFA Illustration as Visual Essay faculty member Mirko Ilić and MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller will lead a discussion on the art of designing posters for Shakepeare's plays, with faculty member and legendary designer Milton Glaser, poster artist Paul Davis and director Julie Taymor. Admission is free. 7:00pm, Polonsky Shakespeare Center, 262 Ashland Place, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE
"The Tide" Reception
A juried exhibition of student work, inspired by the inner workings of the mind. Reception tomorrow, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, April 9, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
April Fools' Ball
VASA presents the April Fools' Ball, a combination masquerade and burlesque featuring performances by SVA students and professional performers. Today is the final day for $10 tickets; $15 after. Tickets can be purchased here. Open to SVA students (limit one non-SVA guest per student). SVA ID required for entry. Friday, April 1, 7:30 - 11:30pm, The Box, 189 Chrystie Street.
The Art of Creative Collaboration with Celine Rattray and Trudie Styler
The SVA Theatre and New York Women in Film & Television present Celine Rattray and Trudie Styler of Maven Pictures discussing their collaboration on independent feature films, including the upcoming Freak Show, directed by Styler. Free for SVA students and staff; $25 general admission. Monday, April 4, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre 333 West 23rd Street.
Words Call for Submissions
Words, SVA's literary publication, is now accepting submissions. Students, faculty and staff can submit poetry, short stories, personal essays, short film scripts and one-act plays. Submissions should be addressed to SVA faculty member and Words editor Louis Phillips via email: lphillips@sva.edu. Please include the phrase "Submission to Words 84" in the subject line, and include a name, address, email and phone number on all submissions. RTF or Word documents are preferred; no PDFs or Adobe documents. The deadline is Wednesday, June 1.
