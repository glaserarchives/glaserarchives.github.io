🎙️ Get to Know: Keren Moscovitch, Ph.D., Associate Director, Division of Continuing Education

Tell us about your role at SVA and your history with the College.

As the associate director of the Division of Continuing Education, I manage our curriculum and operations, from noncredit courses, to free public events, to special programs and publications. I've been at SVA for over 20 years in a variety of roles, including as faculty in BFA Photography and Video, BFA Visual and Critical Studies, and Art History. I'm also a 2005 alumnus of MFA Photography, Video and Related Media.


How has the pandemic dictated or changed the Continuing Education program's offerings positively?

The pandemic invited us to develop a truly global program, with courses, residencies and events accessible to people worldwide. We've seen growth and expansion in our community, as well as exciting new modalities that we never realized were possible, such as our online Artist Residency programs.


As we approach another summer of virtual offerings from the Continuing Education program, what lessons are you taking with you from last year?

Last year was about establishing ourselves online and building a community. This year, we have more courses, more programs and a global audience. We learned that we could be ambitious and succeed.


Are there any new courses you're particularly excited about this summer?

I'm excited about our new "Global Arts" courses, which center language and culture in creative practice. We also collaborated with MFA Design for Social Innovation to produce "Community Design for Leaders," a master class that will generate profits to fund an Equity in Design scholarship. And we are facilitating a partnership between SVA's MA Curatorial Practice program and the HDK-Valand Academy of Art and Design at the University of Gothenburg to offer a star-studded bi-continental intensive in the philosophy of friendship.


Hopefully, we’re looking forward to being back in the SVA Theatre in November for our 5th annual Art & Activism event, when we will unveil the creative fruits of our collaboration with environmental non-profit Solar One. Also, look out for us at Photoville this fall. It should be a lot of fun!

🍎 Health and Wellness

June is Employee Wellbeing Month


Employee Wellbeing Month is a time for employers and employees to bring healthy choices and habits into the workplace. We all spend a lot of time working. It is important for that time to be fulfilling, supportive and enjoyable.

  • A one-hour workout is just 4% of your day! Set the alarm, go for a run before work, walk on your lunch break, or hit the gym before dinner.
  • Relax and Recharge. Keep electronics out of your bedroom, take five minutes to practice mindfulness, get some fresh air.
  • Fuel Your Brain. When you fuel your day with whole foods, your body and mind reap the benefits.
  • Find Your Balance. Take time off to make your time on the job more effective. When you put your health first and restructure your days to focus on wellness, you will not only have time for things you love, you will be more productive and focused at work, too.

Learn more at employeewellbeingmonth.org.
