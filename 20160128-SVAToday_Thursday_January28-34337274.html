Thursday, January 28, 2016

The Hot Five
The most talked-about projects on SVA Portfolios for January include hyper-detailed portraits, Yuko Shimizu's step-by-step process behind her design for A Wild Swan and a futuristic museum concept.
6:30pm: Articulating Artwashing, Gentrification and Responsibility: What Is the Place of the Curator?
MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on the concept of "artwashing," in which art, the artist and the larger creative economy are complicit in the commodification of community and culture. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Press pick of the day
Modern-Day Musicians, Old-Time Feel
CNN features portraits from "American Folk," a series of tintype photographs by faculty member Lisa Elmaleh (BFA 2007 Photography).
NYC picks of the day
Phil Jimenez and Ramzi Fawaz
Ramzi Fawaz discusses his new book, The New Mutants: Superheroes and the Radical Imagination of American Comics, with BFA Cartooning faculty member Phil Jimenez. Purchase a copy of the book or a $15 gift card to attend. 7:00pm, The Strand, 828 Broadway.
The Man Who Fell to Earth
n+1 presents a screening of the 1976 cult film, starring David Bowie. Tickets are $5 or free for subscribers. RSVP to rsvp@nplusonemag.com. 7:00pm, 68 Jay Street, Suite 405, Brooklyn.
About Women
Writers Lisa Alther and Françoise Gilot discuss their careers in the arts. 7:00pm, La Maison Française, New York University, 16 Washington Mews.
Peter Hujar: "Lost Downtown"
An exhibition of portrait photography featuring members of New York's "downtown" scene during the 1970s - '80s. Through Saturday, February 27, Paul Kasmin Gallery, 297 10th Avenue.
Take Note
Platypus Reading Group
The Platypus Affiliated Society presents a Marxist reading group at SVA. Learn about the historical and intellectual roots of the Left and Marxism and their development to the present day. Group meetings are every Saturday beginning January 30, 2:00 - 5:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 631F.
HOPE Art Competition Call for Submissions
Student Health and Counseling Services is accepting poster submissions for the annual HOPE Competition. The winner will receive $500 and finalists will have their work displayed at the SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space. Visit sva.edu/health for submission forms and guidelines. The deadline is Monday, February 1, 4:00pm.
SVA Club Fair
VASA presents the spring 2016 club fair for students to browse and join student-run organizations. Monday, February 1, 6:30 - 9:00pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Adam Pendleton
BFA Fine Arts presents conceptual artist Adam Pendleton discussing his multidisciplinary practice, which incorporates painting, publishing, photographic collage, video and performance. Tuesday, February 2, 7:00pm, 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
