🎙️ Get to Know: Vennette Jones, Executive Director, Human Resources

Congratulations on your new role! Tell us a little about your history with SVA and what you're working on right now in this position as we start the new school year.

Thank you. I started my career at SVA in 2006 as an employment manager handling all recruitment and employee relations matters. A handful of promotions and many years later, I’m now the executive director of Human Resources. I oversee all things HR, from helping ensure members of the SVA community return to a safe campus amid a pandemic, to recruiting for the many open positions that accumulated over the past year and a half, to resolving employee concerns. I see my new role as an opportunity to change the view of HR from a more transactional department to an office that fosters change and opportunity by building trust in our community members. We all benefit when there is open dialogue and an embrace of change.


You're also a member of the Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) Task Force. What work or training led you to be a part of this group?

As an HR professional with over 20 years of experience, DEI has always been part of my profession. I am also credentialed by the Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM) in DEI, specifically Inclusive Workplace Culture. I have often thought about how SVA can improve in the DEI space, and after participating in the most recent Strategic Plan as an interviewer, I was asked to chair the diversity committee. As our initiatives evolved, we hired a director of DEI and I remain a member of the task force. Ultimately my work and training led me to the group, but my life experiences as a black woman of Muslim faith help provide a fresh and necessary perspective to the College.


What other new tech platforms or initiatives have you launched in recent months or years to help streamline your department's ability to support SVA employees?

I have overseen implementing an applicant tracking system that provides hiring managers with better access to candidates, resulting in decreased delays and reduced load for certain staff. I managed the transition to online faculty contracts, saving the institution money in labor and mailing expenses. My department also implemented E-verify, a government platform that allows us to verify an employee’s eligibility to work in the U.S. Our office supports approximately 600 staff, over 800 faculty and another 400 student workers, streamlining initiatives and using technology to improve employee services is a constant goal.

﻿﻿ Did you Know?

All students, staff and faculty must use Cleared4, a health-screening platform linked to your SVA ID, to access campus buildings each day. Cleared4 (which replaces SVA Safe) enables SVA to monitor any COVID-19 symptoms and helps us quickly identify others who might have been exposed if someone has symptoms.


Each day, at least two hours before coming to campus, you will need to complete a Cleared4 health survey via a link sent by email or text. You can also access your link through the Cleared4 tile in your myID dashboard. Based on your survey answers, you will receive a color-coded status. If you have no symptoms, or possible exposure for at least 10 days, you will receive a Blue or Green Pass on your phone, which means you are safe to come to SVA. To enter an SVA building, you should have your pass available and swipe your ID card at security stations. Click here for more information.
