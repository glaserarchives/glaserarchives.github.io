SVA BFA Photography and Video Newsletter
Happy Holidays from all of us in the BFA Photography & Video Department!

The Winter Break Raffle will be held from 8:50AM to 12PM on Wednesday, December 20, as we will be closing at 1PM that day.
 
Natalie Krick Publishes First Book with Skylark Editions

Alum Natalie Krick's (2008) first book, NATURAL DECEPTIONS, features photographs from her series of the same name, showcasing bright images that touch on sexuality, beauty, and popular glamour cues.
DEAR DAVE, Features Sean Hemmerle

DEAR DAVE,'s newest Features interview with Chair Stephen Frailey and faculty member Sean Hemmerle (MFA Photo Alum) explores his portraits of people from Kabul, Baghdad, Gaza, Juarez, and Beirut and the issues of foreign policy.
Aperture's Winter Edition Explores Transgender Histories in Photography

Alum Zackary Drucker (2005) guest-edits the newest issue of Aperture, which is dedicated to the representation of transgender lives, communities, and histories in photography.
 
Elinor Carucci on Her Photograph for The New Yorker's Short Story, Cat Person

Faculty member Elinor Carucci discusses her kiss photograph, which was paired with the much-talked about short story "Cat Person" by Kristen Roupenian.
DEAR DAVE, Magazine Call for Entries

DEAR DAVE, Magazine is holding a call for entries for their 27th issue. This call for entries is open to all photographers of all backgrounds – professional, non-professional, amateur, and student. Deadline to apply is Tuesday, January 2, 2018.
Hunger TV Interviews Robert Whitman on Prince

Hunger TV interviews Robert Whitman, the photographer who shot Prince's first photoshoot in 1977.
Martin Parr Documents Scotland's Red Postboxes in New Book

AnOther shares selections from Martin Parr's newest book "Remote Scottish Postboxes," a collection of Scotland's stark red postboxes amidst its foggy landscapes.
Masterclass with Annie Leibovitz

In her first-ever online class, award-winning photographer Annie Leibovitz teaches her process for working with light, creating concepts, and finding your point of view as an artist.
1,000fps Slow-Motion 4K Storm Footage on DP Review

Check out this slow-motion and incredibly hi-res footage created by digital photographer Dustin Farrell with the Phantom Flex4K.
PDN Shares Their Selections of the Year's Most Notable Photo Books

See the list that PDN has compiled of their notable photo books for 2017 in parts one, two, and three.
Pantone Names Ultra Violet as The Color of 2018

Pantone sent people to scour the globe for color signals pointing toward the new year and have arrived at this shade of purple. The vice-president of the institute remarks that "It’s truly a reflection of what’s needed in our world today.”
The New York Times Features Ina Jang and Olivia Locher

Alumni Ina Jang (2010) and Olivia Locher (2013) are included in The New York Times' recent collection of essays and artwork about sexual harassment in the workplace.

MacArthur Foundation Awards Dawoud Bey 2017 MacArthur Fellowship

Alum Dawoud Bey (1977) has been awarded the 2017 MacArthur Fellowship for his approach to photography and its juncture with communities and their cultural institutions.
Zak Krevitt & Thomas McCarty Photograph Drag Nightlife for The New York Times

Alumni Zak Krevitt (2014) and Thomas McCarty (2016) photograph denizens of Brooklyn's expansive drag scene for The New York Times.
Brian Finke Photographs Sadik Hadzovic for Men's Health

Alum Brian Finke (1998) photographs bodybuilder and Instagram fitness celebrity Sadik Hadzovic for Men's Health article "Muscle in the Age of Instagram."
A&U Features Eric Tenorio

Alum Eric Tenorio (2017) is featured in A&U, America's AIDS Magazine, for his recent portraits.

João Enxuto and Erica Love Receive Arts Writers Grant

Faculty member João Enxuto and collaborator Erica Love have been awarded the Creative Capital Andy Warhol Foundation Arts Writers Grant.
2018 SVA Galleries Open Call

SVA Galleries is holding an open call for their upcoming juried exhibition, open to all students. Deadline to apply is Friday, January 12, 2018.
TIME Editors Compile List of The Year's Best Portraits

TIME Magazine has put together their year-end list of the the best photo portraits of 2017.
The New York Times Lens Features The Ongoing Story of Domestic Slavery in India

Economic fallout from colonialism helped pave the way for human trafficking and the disappearance of young women in India. The New York Times shares this story alongside photographs by Smita Sharma.
TIME Names The Silence Breakers as 2017's "Person of the Year"

The Silence Breakers have been named as TIME's "Person of the Year," including activist Tarana Burke, creator of the #MeToo phrase used more than a decade ago, artist and activist Rose McGowan, and Isabel Pascual, a farmworker from Mexico, among many others.
Buzzfeed Shares a List of 17 Women Who Made History with Their Photography

This list chronicles the vast history of photography, from Anna Atkins' mid-1800s cyanotypes of British algae to Carol Guzy's documentation of the 2010 Haitian earthquake.
