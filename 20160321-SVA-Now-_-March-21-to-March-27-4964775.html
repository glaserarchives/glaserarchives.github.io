SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts. Week of March 21 - 27, 2016
Features
Students checking out Google Spotlight Stories. Photo by Hsiang Chin Moe.
The Future of Storytelling
Angie Wojak, SVA's director of Career Development, talks about this year's SVA Destinations trip to San Francisco, which included visits with Google Spotlight Stories, Industrial Light and Magic, Pixar and Oculus Rift.
Disney, Cartoon Network, FOX Studios and More: SVA Takes LA
A recap of recent College activities in Los Angeles, including the SVA in LA spring break trip and an Alumni Affairs reception with artist Wyatt Mills (BFA 2013 Fine Arts).
From the Founder of Pace/MacGill to the Photo Director of W: SVA Students Work with Their Mentors
A look at the "Mentors" exhibition, which pairs BFA Photography and Video students with prominent figures in the arts community. This year's mentors include photographer Adam Fuss, Pace/MacGill founder Peter MacGill and My Chemical Romance frontman Gerard Way (BFA 1999 Cartooning). "Mentors" is on view through Friday, April 1, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Rosson Crow on Interior Spaces, Fashion and the Big Sky of LA
An interview with artist Rosson Crow (BFA 2004 Fine Arts) about her inspirations. Crow's work was most recently exhibited at the Armory Show in New York.
EVENTS
Moscow Street by Michelle Moy.
"Mentors" Reception
BFA Photography and Video presents an exhibition of student work inspired by their mentorships with photographers, curators, art directors, publishers, art dealers, critics and writers. Curated by BFA Photography and Video Chair Stephen Frailey. Reception Thursday, March 24, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Friday, April 1, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
BFA Advertising and BFA Design Portfolio
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition of student work, featuring ads, campaigns, designs, motion graphics and 3D designs. Reception Tuesday, March 22, 5:00 - 8:00pm. Tuesday, March 22, through Friday, April 1, Art Directors Club, 106 West 29th Street.
Russell Flinchum: New Perspectives on Women and Cars
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Russell Flinchum, associate professor in the College of Design at North Carolina State University, giving the 2016 Phil Patton Memorial Lecture, in which he will discuss the role of female designers throughout the history and stylistic development of the automobile. Tuesday, March 22, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
"The Never Ending Project 1.0" Reception
BFA Interior Design presents an exhibition about the department's participation in last year's Never Ending Project, a week-long design challenge that brought together three teams from three different continents. Reception Thursday, March 24, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Monday, April 4, VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
IN THE PRESS
Tim Okamura at Redbird Gallery, New York. Photo by Stephanie Tartick.
The Inner Strength of Women
The Huffington Post interviews artist Tim Okamura (MFA 1993 Illustration as Visual Essay) about his female portraits.
From Punk to Pee-wee
In The Village Voice, star of Pee-wee's Playhouse Paul Reubens discusses meeting BFA Cartooning faculty member Gary Panter, who designed the show's famous set.
A Pitch for Kindness
The New York Times on a demonstration led by BFA Design faculty members Timothy Goodman (BFA 2007 Graphic Design) and Jessica Walsh, as an extension of their 12 Kinds of Kindness campaign.
