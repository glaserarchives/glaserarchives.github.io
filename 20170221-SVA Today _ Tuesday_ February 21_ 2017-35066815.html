EVENTS

4:00pm | Photographing Flowers for Transparency

MPS Digital Photography presents digital artist and professional photographer Harold Davis, who will show how he enhances his floral imagery. 4:00 – 5:50pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:00pm | Jasmine Wahi

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by faculty member Jasmine Wahi, who curates international exhibitions dealing with issues of cultural identity, intersectionality and female empowerment. 6:00 – 7:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:00pm | VASA 2017 Elections Information Session

Any students interested in running for a leadership position in VASA, SVA's student government, must attend an information session. Candidates run as a team of four for the positions of president, vice president, treasurer and student senate chair. 6:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. Election packets can be picked up at the information session or at Student Engagement and Leadership, 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. The packets include information about eligibility requirements and are due Thursday, March 16, 3:00pm. Email activities@sva.edu with questions.

6:15pm | Newtown Documentary Screening and Q&A

SVA Theatre and MPS Directing present an advance screening of the documentary Newtown, about the Sandy Hook Elementary mass shooting. The screening will be followed by a Q&A with director Kim Snyder, moderated by Ralph Appelbaum of SVA Filmmakers Dialogue. Open to the SVA community and up to four guests per SVA ID holder. 6:15pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Thomas Roma

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer Thomas Roma as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

An exhibition of photographs of Muslim New Yorkers from the mid-20th to early 21st centuries. Ticket prices vary. Through Sunday, July 30. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
