SVA Today
May 29 – June 3, 2018

Sculpting Sandcastles

Calvin Seibert (BFA 1983 Fine Arts) talks with the Visual Arts Journal about his fantastical sandcastles, which he has been constructing on Rockaway Beach for the past three years.

SVA Premieres Returns to LA

SVA returns to Hollywood on Thursday, May 31, to present its annual film and animation showcase, SVA Premieres, which will feature a curated selection of works by recently graduated filmmakers from SVA's film, animation and visual effects programs.

"Cartographic Dreams"

A look at artist Nadia Haji Omar's (MFA 2014 Fine Arts) bright, complex canvases.

THIS WEEK

Tuesday | David Arky

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by award-winning photographer David Arky, part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tuesday, May 29, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Tuesday | Life on Pluto: MFA Computer Arts Thesis Exhibition

The MFA Computer Arts class of 2018 presents an exhibition of thesis work that explores the themes and media of digital art. Tuesday, May 29 – Saturday, June 16. Reception: Thursday, June 7, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Friday | Mad. Sq. Art Symposium: Removing Public Art

The role of public art has been at the center of the controversy over Confederate and other historic monuments and contemporary art. Madison Square Park Conservancy and SVA present a symposium where artists, cultural leaders and scholars will lead a conversation on historic and contemporary monuments and current solutions. RSVP to symposium@madisonsquarepark.org. Friday, June 1, 9:00am – noon. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday | #Connected

MPS Art Therapy presents a collaborative art exhibition that explores youth responses to technology, media and relationships and features work from participants at Counseling in Schools. Saturday, June 2 – Thursday, June 14. Viewing times available by appointment; contact 212.592.2610 or arttherapy@sva.edu. Reception: Wednesday, June 6, 5:00 – 8:00pm. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Saturday and Sunday | MFA Photography, Video and Related Media at Greenpoint Open Studios

Come see multimedia work by MFA Photography, Video, and Related Media students at the 2018 Greenpoint Open Studios. Saturday, June 2 – Sunday, June 3. Greenpoint Open Studios, 37 Greenpoint Avenue, #E2B, Brooklyn.
PRESS PICK

Capturing "Girlhood Across America"

T: The New York Times Style Magazine on Justine Kurland's (BFA 1996 Photography) photo series "Girl Pictures," which is now on view in a special exhibition at Mitchell-Innes & Nash Gallery in New York, celebrating the project's 20th anniversary.
NYC PICKS

Posthaste Perennial Patterns: Talk and Screening with Jodie Mack

Centering on the use of domestic and recycled materials, this program of short films by Jodie Mack, Paul Gailiunas and Hellen Hill illuminates formal and cursory elements shared between fine-art abstraction and mass-produced graphic design. Followed by a Q&A with Mack. Tickets $5 – $10; purchase here. Thursday, May 31, 6:30pm. Museum of Arts and Design, 2 Columbus Circle.

Book Con

An immersive experience featuring interactive events including writer panels, storytelling podcasts, screenings, writing workshops, and more. Ticket prices vary; purchase here. Saturday, June 2 – Sunday, June 3. Javits Center, 655 West 34th Street.

David Levine: Some of the People, All of the Time

A performance that examines the fragility of our democracy by addressing issues of personal identity, agency, labor and suspicion. Free with SVA ID. Performances occur Thursday – Sunday, through Sunday, July 8. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.

Through a Different Lens: Stanley Kubrick Photographs

This exhibition explores a formative phase in the career of one of the 20th century's most renowned motion picture directors. Tickets Free – $18; purchase here. Through October 2018. Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
