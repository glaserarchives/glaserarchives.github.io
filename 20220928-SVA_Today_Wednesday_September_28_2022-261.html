EVENTS

9:00am | The Curatorial Roundtable: Clémentine Deliss

MA Curatorial Practice presents Clémentine Deliss, associate curator at KW Institute for Contemporary Art in Berlin, to speak on contingency, ultra temporary exhibitions and reinterpreting collections. Today, September 28, 9:00 – 10:00am, online.

6:00pm | “Darwin’s Paradox: A Decade of Bio Art” Reception

BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work by students, alumni and visiting artists, curated by department chair Suzanne Anker and Bio Art Laboratory Manager Tarah Rhoda (MFA 2020 Fine Arts; BFA 2010 Fine Arts), and on view through Monday, October 17. Reception: tonight, September 28, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:00pm | “Dot Dot Dot” Reception

MPS Digital Photography presents an exhibition of thesis work by the MPS Digital Photography class of 2022, on view through Monday, October 17. Reception: tonight, September 28, 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

6:00pm | SVA Graduate Admissions Preview Night

This SVA Admissions event provides prospective graduate students with an overview of graduate programs offered as well as tips about the application process. Attendees will receive an $80 application fee waiver if they apply for summer 2023 or fall 2023 admission. Tonight, September 28, 6:00 – 8:00pm, online.

Last Chance | “A Sticker History Exhibition”

Get Paid While Teaching Art

Teaching Lab is a two-hour training workshop designed for undergraduate students interested and available to teach in an after-school art program at New York Edge, a nonprofit dedicated to quality after-school programming for K-12 students in New York City. Artists who are selected to teach will take part in the curriculum process! Contact Courtney Treglia, MA/MAT Art Education, ctreglia@sva.edu, for more information.

#SVAwesome

The members of the ISO Cultural Partner Program pose next to a mural they painted at an elementary school in Staten Island, October 2019. SVA’s International Student Office welcomes all students to apply for this two-semester long and membership-based program, featuring local culture trips, a monthly discussion series and the opportunity to participate in and lead a community service project. It is a small, supportive group where you can connect with students from different majors and years. Program requirements and the application can be viewed here.
