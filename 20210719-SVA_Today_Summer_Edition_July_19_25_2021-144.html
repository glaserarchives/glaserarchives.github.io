EVENTS THIS WEEK

Monday | Vision Board Party: Meditate and Create for BIPOC Mental Health Month
Join Purple, an educator, activist, community organizer, healer and founder of WOC Sister Collective, for an Instagram takeover! Purple will facilitate a meditation focused on mental wellness, healing and empowerment and teach us how to make a vision board to manifest our dreams. Learn about radical visioning, intention setting and self-care! Presented by SVA Student Health and Counseling Services. Open to all SVA students, staff and faculty. Monday, July 19, 12:00pm ET. Join on Instagram Live.

Tuesday | Practice Lecture Series: Seph Rodney
Seph Rodney, PhD, is the opinions editor for Hyperallergic and writes on visual art and related issues. Join his lecture with MFA Art Practice on Tuesday, July 20, 9:30 – 11:30am ET. Register here to join on Zoom.

Thursday | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development's virtual office hour this Thursday, July 22, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

SVA Alumni NYFA Finalists
The New York Foundation for the Arts (NYFA) has announced the recipients and finalists of the NYSCA/NYFA Artist Fellowship program, including interdisciplinary work fellow Katelyn Kopenhaver (BFA 2016 Photography and Video) and video/film finalist James Bascara (BFA 2011 Illustration).

Can Bad Design Be Good?
MFA Products of Design Chair Allan Chochinov talks with Fast Company about how bad design can lead to a good thing.

Both recordings are streaming on Vimeo: click here for the Monday, July 12 session; click here for the Tuesday, July 13 session.

Next Week | COVID-19 Community Conversations: Mental Health, Equity and Resilience
In observance of BIPOC Mental Health Awareness Month, SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents this training for all SVA staff and faculty. It will cover the mental-health impact of COVID-19, the effect of trauma and grief, health disparities in communities of color as well as coping skills and self-care. It is a three-hour training that will take place on Zoom. Wednesday, July 28, 9:30am – 12:30pm ET. Sign up here.

Next Week | Faculty Book Club
For the next book club meeting on Wednesday, July 28, the group will discuss the documentary, Tyrus (2017, 1:17 minutes) which is available on the Faculty Book Club Canvas page. Join for a faculty-led discussion on Zoom, 4:30 – 5:30pm ET.

As a reminder, all faculty are invited to join on Canvas for more readings and discussion, all of which can be done at your own pace.

Congratulations to MPS Digital Photography 2020 alumni Phuong Vo (producer) and Natcha Wongchanglaw (translator) for their work on Centuries and Still, a mixed-media short film telling the history of anti-Asian racism and violence in the U.S. The film premiered last week as a Vimeo Staff Pick and is available for the public to watch here.
