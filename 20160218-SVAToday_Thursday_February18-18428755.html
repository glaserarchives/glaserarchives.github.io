SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, February 18, 2016
This year's HOPE winner, by MFA Illustration as Visual Essay student David Leutert.
Take Care!
This year's annual HOPE Art competition challenged students to create poster designs on the theme of self-care for the Office of Student Health and Counseling Services. The posters are on view in "Take Care," through Saturday, February 20, at the SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
6:30pm: "Duplicates, Dummies and Dolls" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by Sara Mejia Kriendler (MFA 2013 Fine Arts), which explores modes of production through her use of discarded Styrofoam and plastic packaging as a representation of waste and excess. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Becky Nahom. Through Thursday, March 3. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
7:00pm: NY Photo Salon: Patricia Lopez, Greg Miller and J. Sybylla Smith
MPS Digital Photography presents photographers Patricia Lopez, Greg Miller and J. Sybylla Smith discussing their work. Each photographer will answer questions following their 20-minute presentation. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Press pick of the day
Back of a Horse Costume x 2 by Amy Sillman.
Amy Sillman and Metabolic Painting
Examiner spotlights "Stuff Change," the new exhibition by the BFA Fine Arts alumnus, which is on view at Sikkema Jenkins & Co. through Saturday, March 12.
NYC picks of the day
Athena Film Festival
The fifth annual festival celebrates women and leadership and features screenings, panels and workshops. SVA students, faculty and staff can claim one free ticket to any workshop or master class here. Locations vary. Through Sunday, February 21.
Winter Film Awards Festival
A festival of feature, short and animated films by up-and-coming filmmakers. Tickets are $5 with student ID. Through Friday, February 26, Cinema Village, 22 East 12th Street.
Marc Asnin: "Uncle Charlie"
An exhibition by the BFA Photography alumnus, featuring photographic portraits of his family spanning 30 years. Through Sunday, April 10, Queensborough Community College Art Gallery, 222-05 56th Avenue, Bayside, NY.
Munch and Expressionism
An exhibition on the influence of Edvard Munch on the German expressionists. General admission is $20, students and seniors $10. Through Monday, June 13, Neue Galerie, 1048 Fifth Avenue.
Take Note
Muriel Hasbun
MPS Digital Photography presents artist Muriel Hasbun discussing how her work reflects an intergenerational, transnational and transcultural vision. 7:00pm, Tuesday, February 23, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
