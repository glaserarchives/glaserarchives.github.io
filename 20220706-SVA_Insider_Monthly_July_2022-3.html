🎙️ Get to Know: William Berrios, director, Financial Aid

Tell us a little about your history with SVA and your current role.

I started at SVA on March 1, 1999, as the associate director of Financial Aid until my promotion to the position of director of Financial Aid on July 1, 2005.


The 2022 Distinguished Staff Awards nominees were just announced. Can you tell us about the history behind the awards and the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group (DSAG)?

The Distinguished Staff Awards were created by the Organization and Communication Task Force (later renamed Distinguished Staff Advisory Group), which reports to Executive Vice President Tony Rhodes. The award nomination process starts with an administrative staff member nominating one of their peers. I was brought onboard DSAG around 2003 by Tony Rhodes and [former Executive Director of External Relations] Sam Mondenstein. One thing we did differently this year was organize the return of the All-Staff Art Show to the Gramercy Gallery in June after two years of hosting it online.


How does the Financial Aid office use the summer months to prepare for the upcoming school year?

We don't just prepare during the summer months, we prepare for over 12 months on several projects for the upcoming school year. Our office makes every effort to notify students and their families–over phone, email and text message–on several items. It can be for those who have yet to file their 2022-2023 FAFSA forms, have incomplete documentation (verification forms, tax returns, etc.) or must complete their entrance counseling to comply with federal student loans.


Anything else you would like to add?

I want to thank my staff for making the work enjoyable in the office. They are hard-working and dedicated to guiding students to reach their financial goals. We could not entirely do our job without the support from Tony and President David Rhodes and our fellow departments.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Reinventing You with Debbie Millman

In this course, creative professional, designer, strategist and MPS Branding chair Debbie Millman shares her personal life lessons from a career steeped in design and learning from others. She helps you identify the parts of your life you want to pay attention to and those you can accept and leave be.


Creative Cloud Crash Course

Explore everything the Creative Cloud has to offer and discover the best features in each tool. Focus on the areas that interest you or watch the entire course to get a strong foundation on the complete Adobe toolset.

In this course, instructor Andy Needham walks through four hands-on projects that demonstrate how to effectively create motion graphics for your branded video content using Adobe After Effects.
