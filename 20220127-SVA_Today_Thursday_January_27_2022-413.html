Last Chance | Narratives, Stories, Algorithms: Rethinking Independence in Digital Times
Presented by MA Curatorial Practice and curated by 2021 program graduate Xiao Zong, this exhibition questions the ways in which we receive information, form opinions and draw conclusions in our digital era of fragmented information, fabricated truths and malleable narratives. Closing today, January 27. Pfizer Building, 630 Flushing Avenue, 5th floor.

IN TOWN AND ONLINE

The Shape I’m In
Asya Geisberg Gallery, run by Asya Geisberg (MFA 1999 Fine Arts), presents “The Shape I’m In,” the third solo exhibition by Ricardo Gonzalez featuring self-aware paintings of various characters contorting themselves into shapes within the confinement of their canvas’s borders. Through Saturday, February 12. Asya Geisberg Gallery, 537 West 23rd Street.

Tomorrow | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Members of the Asian and Asian American communities continue to face harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Faculty and staff who self-identify as AAPI are invited to join this virtual forum to connect on these or other topics for peer support. New members are welcome. If you are interested in participating, please fill out this Google Form to register. If you are unable to participate, you can still fill out the Google Form to indicate your interest in future opportunities and be included in future correspondence. Tomorrow, January 28, 11:00am – 12:30pm ET, on Zoom. Zoom information will follow.

Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work from the SVA community that reflect on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.'s legacy and life's work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA's observance of Black History Month in February. Submissions are due Monday, January 31. For more information or to submit your work, click here.

MFA Visual Narrative Application Q&A Sessions
The MFA Visual Narrative program empowers the next generation of storytellers with the skills and expertise to change the world through story. If you are interested in applying, drop in to chat with department chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay), who will be available to answer any questions about the program and the application process. Applications are due Friday, February 11, for second-round reviews, with acceptances continuing on a rolling basis for a June 2022 start as space remains available. Wednesday, February 2, 12:00 – 1:00pm ET, and Thursday, February 3, 8:00 – 9:00pm ET, online. Register for your preferred session at the links.
