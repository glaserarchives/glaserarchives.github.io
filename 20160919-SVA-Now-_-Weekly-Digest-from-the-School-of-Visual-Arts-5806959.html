SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts September 19 - 25
FEATURES

SVA Guide to Flatiron

Following our guide to Chelsea, here are a few suggested places to visit, things to do and foods to eat in the Flatiron District. Stay tuned for more New York City neighborhood guides.

Coming to America

International exchange students share their SVA experiences.

Peter Hristoff on His Met Residency and Teaching at SVA [Video]

Faculty member Peter Histoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts) discusses his recently completed 15-month residency at the Metropolitan Museum of Art and his approach to art and teaching at SVA.

Zackary Drucker on Transparent [Video]

Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography), producer of the Golden Globe- and Emmy-winning series Transparent, talks about working on the show, whose third season premieres on Friday, September 23, and finding her voice in photography.

"Be a Freedom Machine": Five More Tips from Jerry Saltz

Following his talk at SVA last winter, the senior art critic at New York returned this summer to speak with students and offer even more advice for artists.
IN THE PRESS
Photo by Chance Yeh for Getty Images and SVA

Runway Style on the Street

New York's The Cut on "The Language of Fashion," an exhibition of typographic-themed garments designed by BFA Design students. The designs were on view along Madison Avenue during New York Fashion Week.

"Kids Can Have Problems, Too"

The New Yorker profiles graphic novelist Raina Telgemeier (BFA 2002 Illustration), whose latest, Ghosts, was published earlier this month.

A New Tune for Star Wars

The Hollywood Reporter breaks the news that Michael Giacchino (BFA 1990 Film and Video) will compose the score to the forthcoming Rogue One: A Star Wars Story.
EVENTS
Illustration by Helen Li

"The Book Show" Reception

MFA Illustration as Visual Essay presents its annual "Book Show" exhibition, featuring works illustrated and written by members of the class of 2017. Reception Monday, September 19, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Thursday, September 29, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"The Biophilia Hypothesis" Reception

BFA Fine Arts presents an exhibition of work inspired by biologist E. O. Wilson's belief that human beings share an instinctive bond with other living systems. Curated by department chair Suzanne Anker. Reception Wednesday, September 21, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Saturday, October 15, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

SVA at Photoville

BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography are presenting exhibitions at this year's Photoville, the annual photography exhibition housed in shipping containers located along the waterfront of Brooklyn Bridge Park. Wednesday, September 21, through Sunday, September 25, Brooklyn Bridge Plaza, Water Street at New Dock Street, Brooklyn.
