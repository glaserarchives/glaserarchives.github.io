The nonprofit organization Students for Fair Admissions (SFFA) filed several federal lawsuits against Harvard University (2014) alleging racial discrimination in their admissions process and, more recently, against the University of North Carolina’s (2022) use of racial or ethnic classifications as factors in making admissions decisions. On October 3, 2022, the Supreme Court of the United States (SCOTUS) heard these arguments separately. Next month, the court is expected to render a decision on these cases, which will ultimately determine how race and ethnicity is considered in all higher education admissions decisions.

In anticipation of the court’s upcoming decision, an ad hoc committee has been formed at SVA to evaluate our current recruitment, enrollment and retention practices and policies to determine whether they are racially and ethnically based. The committee will:
  • Assess and evaluate our preparedness for inclusive enrollment management processes
  • Revisit and redefine Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion
  • Inventory and evaluate our current enrollment management/retention practices and policies, with the goal of providing solutions and suggestions for greater levels of inclusivity
The SVA community stands behind the principles articulated in our mission statement and core values. We have aligned our strategic planning efforts with the institution’s goal to improve the structural diversity of our faculty and student body. Regardless of the court’s decision, we continue to strengthen our commitment to creating a culture and a climate of equity, inclusion and belonging.  

Over the next few months, we will continue to update you on the court’s decision and our response as an institution.
