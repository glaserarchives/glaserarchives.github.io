SVA BFA Photography and Video Presents Untitled (Gender and Representation) Symposium at SVA Theater 

SVA and BFA Photography and Video present Untitled (Gender and Representation) 2016, a day-long symposium featuring Kate Bernstein, alum Zackary Drucker (2005) (above), Ethan James Green, Ron Gregg, M. Lamar, Zanele Muholi, and others.

Untitled (Gender and Representation) 2016 seeks to address the complex issues of gender that have dominated cultural conversations in the last several years, and the ways in which representation has significantly participated in the discourse. The conference will include panels on queer histories and photography, gender outlaws, fashion and gender, transfeminism, and gender and performance.

Alum Lorna Simpson Gives Inaugural 
Photography Lecture at National Gallery of Art

Alum Lorna Simpson (class of 1983) gave a talk at the National Gallery of Art in Washington, D.C. as part of the inaugural presentation of the National Gallery’s Arnold Newman Lecture Series on Photography. She offered a visual journey of her practice over the past three decades, sharing the concepts behind some of her most recognized projects. 

At the National Gallery, Untitled (Two Necklines) by Simpson is on view in “Intersections: Photographs and Videos from the National Gallery of Art and the Corcoran Gallery of Art.” The exhibition is organized around five themes and “Untitled” is featured in the Identity section.

First Exhibition of Mike Disfarmer's Vintage Portraits Held in Switzerland

The debut of Disfarmer’s original vintage photographs is the culmination of an unprecedented two-year historical reclamation project in which a dedicated team of researchers scoured family albums in every home along every road in Cleburne County, Arkansas.

Disfarmer captured the faces of the American heartland at a defining period in history.  He documented the families of the farmland as they struggled through the Depression and World War II.

Alum Caitlin McCann's Interview with Urban Outfitters about Her Zine Cake

Mentor Taryn Simon at Park Avenue Armory

Each night at sundown, more than thirty professional mourners populate Simon’s sculptural installation, broadcasting their lamentations. The status of the lamenters as professionals—performing away and apart from their usual contexts—underscores the tension between authentic and staged emotion, spontaneity and script. Open during the daytime, visitors are invited to activate the sculpture of inverted wells with their own sounds. 

Taryn Simon's An Occupation of Loss has both day and evening showtimes, and the performance is approximately 35 minutes.

The Park Avenue Armory

Faculty Member Joseph Maida Featured on Time Lightbox

Joseph Maida's project, Things are Queer, was inspired by pop art humor, Japanese kawaii and the millions of photographs of food shared on social media every day. “Pop art engages with the culture directly at the moment it’s being made,” says Maida. 

While Maida’s work engages people to think about their own perceptions, it also functions on the most superficial level: giving an interesting visual experience.

Junior Todd Midler's Book Covered Now Available on Big Cartel

In Los Angeles, California, cars are a major part of everyday life. People obsess over their cars. They spend thousands of dollars to own and maintain their precious vehicle. But, what happens when they aren’t driving to and from work? What happens when a car is no longer functioning? What happens when the owner deems the car too nice to drive? This series, published as a photo book titled Covered, is a study of what happens when a car is no longer in motion.
