EVENTS THIS WEEK

Opening Tuesday | "Consuming Entities"
This juried exhibition features eight SVA student-artists who use commercial products and displays as vehicles for personal narratives. See it Tuesday, August 3 – Monday, August 23, at galleries.sva.edu.

Opening Wednesday | "Correspondence: The Installation"
Born from her acclaimed art series “To Be Free,” artist, activist and SVA faculty member Sophia Dawson's (BFA 2010 Fine Arts) book Correspondence (Maria Editions, 2021) is a collection of letters and ephemera from incarcerated members of the Black Panther Party and Black Liberation Movement. Curated in conjunction with The Bishop Gallery, this exhibition will feature select works from "To Be Free" that inspired the book. Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies. On view Wednesday, August 4 – Friday, September 10, in the SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Thursday | Repositioning Culture: Protection | MPS Branding 2021 Thesis Presentations
As vaccinations begin to offer glimpses of a post-COVID world, the MPS Branding class of 2021 directed its theses towards a general theme of "protection," and has sought to critically reflect on the past year and reimagine ways to protect our collective future. Learn more and join them live on Thursday, August 5, 2:00pm ET, on YouTube Premiere.

Thursday | Fine Arts: Residency in Contemporary Practices Virtual Open Studios
This open studios event will feature individual studio visits with 11 summer residency participants via Zoom. Viewers will be invited to ask questions and participate in an open dialogue with the artists. Thursday, August 5, 6:00 – 8:00pm ET. Register here.

Opening Friday | "And Then..." MFA Visual Narrative Thesis Exhibition
MFA Visual Narrative presents an exhibition of work by its class of 2021. The exhibition will be on view from Friday, August 6 – Monday, September 6, at andthen.sva.edu.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Lauren Redniss' Oak Flat
The New Yorker discusses Lauren Redniss' (MFA 2000 Illustration as Visual Essay) latest illustrated book Oak Flat: A Fight for Sacred Land in the American West, in which "she confronts her trickiest subject yet: the sanctity of Apache land that, for decades, has been under threat."

Disney's First Nonbinary Character
Dana Terrace's (BFA 2013 Animation) animated Disney Channel TV show The Owl House has introduced Disney's first-ever nonbinary character. Via Bustle.

Reconnecting With Keith Haring's Grace House Mural
SVA alumnus Keith Haring's mural, which was installed decades ago at the Grace House youth center in Manhattan, is now on view at the Museum of Contemporary Art Denver. Via The New York Times.

Create a Video/Animation, Win $500
SVA Theatre seeks a 10 – 20 second pre-roll video (animated or live action) to play before film screenings shown during this September's After School Special 2021: SVA's Alumni Film & Animation Festival. Screenings will be free and open to the public and will feature Q&As with prominent alumni.

Set the video to music that you have created yourself, own the copyright for, have obtained a license for, or is in the public domain, and please note its source when submitting. Download logos for the festival, SVA and SVA Theatre here. Examples of past winning videos can be viewed here and here.

Upload submissions to Vimeo or YouTube, and send an email with a link to the video, your name, graduation year and program major to Adam Natale, director of SVA Theatre, at anatale@sva.edu, by Friday, August 27, 5:00pm ET. Questions may also be directed to Natale. Final video format should be a digital file (e.g., QuickTime ProRes). The winner will receive $500 and an invitation to the festival's VIP reception.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Natural Dye Workshops at First Street Green Park
Join this free two-day workshop with visiting artists Ann Shostrom of Sustainable Studio at Penn State University, and Christina Roberts, director of education, Fabric Workshop and Museum, Philadelphia. Participants will use natural dyes from botanicals (and other materials) while learning about the process from planting to harvesting to making and exhibiting creative and usable objects. Saturday, August 7, and Sunday, August 8. Register for free here.

New York Asian Film Festival
The 20th edition of the New York Asian Film Festival (NYAFF) runs from Friday, August 6 – Sunday, August 22, online and in-person at SVA Theatre and Lincoln Center. The festival will showcase NYAFF's largest lineup to date, screening over 60 films. Click here to see the lineup and buy tickets.
