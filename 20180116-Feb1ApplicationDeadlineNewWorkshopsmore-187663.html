Applications for Fall 2018 Due February 1st!

The deadline is fast approaching for our Fall 2018 intake! Don’t miss your chance to be part of SocDoc’s tenth class! Apply by February 1, 2018.

Regardless of your previous experience, if you have a passion for non-fiction storytelling and want to build a career in the documentary film industry, then SVA's Social Documentary MFA could be the place for you!

Find the Application Here and get in touch with any questions, or for a personal tour of the department (online or in person!) at mfasocdoc@sva.edu

 

Camera Intensive Workshop: January 27-28

New for SocDoc, our expert faculty are leading this Documentary Camera and Sound Intensive. Build your skills as a filmmaker and gain hands-on experience with the latest technology used in documentary production. 

Industry-level techniques for cinematography, interview and sound recording will be covered, as well achieving professional results on a limited budget. The workshop is perfect for budding filmmakers, and for journalists, photographers and writers keen to build on their career with new professional skills.
FEW SEATS LEFT! Click here for more information and to register.

 

Documentary Sound Masterclass: February 24-25 

 

Join this fantastic introduction to the world of professional sound recording taught by award-winning independent filmmaker and sound recordist, JT Takagi, is well known throughout the industry for her work on Black Panthers: Vanguard of the Revolution and Through a Lens Darkly as well as the recent, critically acclaimed Strong Island.

Students will learn techniques for film productions, podcasts, and other audio work and gain hands-on experience with professional-standard sound equipment.
Click here to for more information and to register.

 

Prospective Students: 
Attend Spring Process & Style Classes

 

There’s no better way to see if SVA’s MFA Social Documentary Film program is right for you than being part of the class! All prospective students are invited to sit in on our weekly screening series, Process & Style – an immersive evening of film and discussion with some of the hottest names in documentary!

Screenings are only for SocDoc students and prospective students. Check out the full schedule, and find info on how to attend here.

 

SocDoc at Sundance 2018

 

The world-famous Sundance Film Festival begins this week, and we couldn't be more excited for the alumni and faculty whose films are featured! If you're heading to Park City, be sure to check these out!

Alumni films include: CRIME + PUNISHMENT (Dir.: S. Maing; Consulting Editor David Osit, '11) and DARK MONEY (Dir.: K. Reed; Associate Producer/Assistant Editor Sandra Itainen, '17).
Faculty films include: GENERATION WEALTH (Dir.: L. Greenfield; DP Jerry Risius); MATANGI / MAYA / M.I.A (Dir.: S. Loveridge; Producer Lori Cheatle); THE PRICE OF EVERYTHING pictured above (Dir.: N. Kahn, DP Bob Richman); SEEING ALLRED (Dirs.: S. Sartain, R. Grossman; DP J. Risius) and THIS IS HOME (Dir.: A. Shiva; Editor Toby Shimin).
