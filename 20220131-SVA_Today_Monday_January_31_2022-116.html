Events

Ongoing | SVA ♥ Milton
SVA presents "SVA ♥ Milton," an exhibition celebrating the legendary life and work of the late designer and longtime faculty member Milton Glaser. Due to COVID-19 precautions, the exhibition is currently open only to SVA students, faculty and staff. Through Saturday, February 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | How Much Does It Hurt?
Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies, "How Much Does It Hurt?" is a new thesis exhibition from the graduating class of 2022, curated by artist Grayson Cox and faculty member Paloma Crousillat (MA 2014 Critical Theory and the Arts; MFA 2005 Fine Arts). Due to COVID-19 precautions, the exhibition is currently open only to SVA students, faculty and staff. Through Monday, February 7. SVA Flatiron Gallery, Windows and Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Last Chance | Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work from the SVA community that reflect on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.'s legacy and life's work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA's observance of Black History Month in February. Submissions are due today, January 31. For more information or to submit your work, click here.

Tomorrow | Bystander Intervention to Stop Anti-Asian/American Harassment and Xenophobia
Hosted by Student Affairs, Hollaback will host a one-hour interactive bystander intervention training session to discuss the types of disrespect that Asians and Asian Americans currently face, the five strategies for intervention and how to prioritize your own safety while intervening. There will be time at the end of the session for practice, so you’ll leave feeling more confident about intervening the next time you see anti-Asian/American harassment online or in person. Tomorrow, February 1, 12:00 – 1:00pm ET, online. Register here.

Campus Fire Drills
SVA Facilities Management and Environmental Health and Safety will be conducting fire drills as per New York City compliance this week. A representative from Global Security will walk through each location with a member of SVA Security and Facilities Management. The fire drills will begin on the top floors and proceed down until all floors have completed the drill. A short briefing will take place on each floor during the walk-through; depending on building occupancy, some locations will have a briefing in the lobby. We apologize in advance for any inconvenience that this may cause and will do our best to minimize any disruption to your day.

SVA Wireless Network Update
The "SVA WiFi Registration" network allows users to properly register their mobile devices via wifi.sva.edu in order to access SVA's primary "SVA Secure WiFi" network. The "SVA WiFi registration" network will be available in residence halls, both locations of the SVA Library (East & West) and the SVA Student Center.

Because the network is open and unsecured, "SVA WiFi Registration" will only be available for a limited time through Sunday, February 6. Any users who do not properly register their devices within the next week will need to follow the "Guest" wireless registration process to gain temporary Internet access which can be used to perform the secure certificate install via wifi.sva.edu.

*Note: wifi.sva.edu can always be accessed from anywhere off-campus with an Internet connection.
