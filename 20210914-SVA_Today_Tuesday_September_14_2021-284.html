Celebrating Milton Glaser
With the introduction of its Glaser Gazette, SVA has kicked off a year-long celebration of the life and work of Milton Glaser, the legendary designer, longtime SVA faculty member and former acting chairman of the College's Board, who died last year. Learn more about what else is planned here.

10:00am | Coffee Talk with SVA Nurse Abby Wohl
Join Student Health and Counseling Services therapists Brett and Arielle to get to know SVA's newest nurse Abby Wohl and the role she plays at SVA. 10:00am, on Instagram Live @svastudenthealth.

7:00pm | i3 Photo Lecture with Jaime Permuth
This lecture with photographer Jaime Permuth (MPS 2009 Digital Photography; MFA 1994 Photography and Related Media) kicks of MPS Digital Photography's i3: Images, Ideas and Inspiration 10th anniversary lecture series. Tune in tonight at 7:00pm, on Zoom.

On View | A Quiet Scale
BFA Visual & Critical Studies is pleased to present this two-person exhibition featuring the work of Josephine Halvorson and Hanneline Røgeberg and curated by Catherine Haggarty. Through Friday, October 15. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Thursday and Friday | 11th Annual SocDoc Thesis Showcase
The MFA Social Documentary Film class of 2021 will share their work-in-progress thesis films, which inspire and amaze—especially as they completed their films through the global pandemic. Screenings will occur online with select showings in-person on Thursday, September 16, and Friday, September 17, at SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street. View the full schedule here.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Whitney Museum's Walter Annenberg Lecture: Dawoud Bey
For the past 40 years, Dawoud Bey (1977 Photography) has worked to expand upon what photography can be and the subjects and themes it can address. In this free lecture, Bey speaks with Adam D. Weinberg, the Whitney Museum's Alice Pratt Brown Director, about his work, the history of American photography relevant to his own practice and his ongoing engagement with Black history and memory. 6:30pm. Register here to join on Zoom.
