SVA IN THE PRESS

Chemin Hsiao wins Noguchi Museum Open Call
Hyperallergic features Chemin Hsiao (MFA 2013 Illustration as Visual Essay) and his winning project, Dandelions Know, for the Noguchi Museum’s banner competition. The Queens museum’s open call aimed to transform its outdoor banners into symbols of anti-racism. Hsiao's project will be on display outside the museum through spring 2022.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

SVA Community Fridge
Concerned about fellow SVA students who are challenged with food insecurity, Kalani Van Meter, president of the College’s Indigenous Student Union, approached the Diversity, Equity and Inclusion office and Student Affairs to establish an SVA community fridge.

The community fridge is located in the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street, and will offer fruits, vegetables, eggs and bread; boxed salads, sandwiches and wraps donated by Trader Joe’s; and nonperishables such as rice, pasta and granola bars. The fridge policy is, “Take what you need; leave what you can.” Student Center hours are Monday – Friday, 10:00am – 10:00pm, and Saturday and Sunday, 12:00pm – 6:00pm.

With a credit or debit card, you can donate any amount from $1 up to help fund the fridge. Click here to contribute and write “Community fridge” in the comment box to ensure that your donation is directed to the project.
