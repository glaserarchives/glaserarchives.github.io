Events

7:00pm | i3 Photo Lecture: Rania Matar
Presented by MPS Digital Photography, the latest i3 Photo Lecture features Rania Matar, a Lebanese-born photographer with work featured in the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, National Museum of Women in the Arts and more. Tonight, 7:00 – 8:30pm ET, online via Zoom. Click here to join.
IN TOWN AND ONLINE

Alison Bradley Projects: Kankaku
Alison Bradley Projects presents “Kankaku,” an exhibition showcasing the works of six artists that aims to instill a moment of reflection on the convergence of identity, sensation and temporality. Through Saturday, February 26. Alison Bradley Projects, 526 West 26th Street.
ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tomorrow | Virtual Dog Therapy
Student Health and Counseling Services and New York Therapy Animals will host a virtual dog therapy session every last Wednesday of the month throughout the spring semester. Our next virtual dog therapy hour will take place on tomorrow, January 26, 7:00 – 8:00pm ET, online via Zoom. RSVP here.

Last Chance | Call for Submissions: Lunar New Year Poster Competition
The International Student Office is calling for poster submissions with the theme of Lunar New Year or this coming year's zodiac animal, the tiger. All submitted posters will be showcased on the ISO Instagram page, and a winner will receive a $100 gift certificate. To participate, students must complete this form and email a poster as a JPEG to Yoko Anderson at yanderson@sva.edu by tomorrow, January 26. Open to all students.

Friday | AAPI Faculty/Staff Forum
Members of the Asian and Asian American communities continue to face harassment, discrimination and physical violence. Faculty and staff who self-identify as AAPI are invited to join this virtual forum to connect on these or other topics for peer support. New members are welcome. If you are interested in participating, please fill out this Google Form to register. If you are unable to participate, you can still fill out the Google Form to indicate your interest in future opportunities and be included in future correspondence. Friday, January 28, 11:00am – 12:30pm ET, on Zoom. Zoom information will follow.

Call for Submissions: Reflecting on the Legacy of Rev. Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.
Diversity, Equity and Inclusion at SVA is inviting submissions of work from the SVA community that reflect on Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.'s legacy and life's work. This call is open to all students, faculty and staff; examples of work include written pieces, paintings, videos, photos, illustrations, etc. Select work may be included in programming and on social media for SVA's observance of Black History Month in February. For more information or to submit your work, click here.
