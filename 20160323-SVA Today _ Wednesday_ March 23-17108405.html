SVA Today
Wednesday, March 23, 2016
Photo by Jaime Permuth.
Photographing Cuba in a Time of Transition
Following President Obama's historic trip to Cuba, SVA Close Up highlights the recent SVA Destinations trip to Havana, where MPS Digital Photography Chair Katrin Eismann and faculty member Jaime Permuth led a weeklong exploration of the country's culture and heritage. A second trip to Cuba is scheduled for May. Click here for more information.
12:30pm: Home and the Poetics of Space
BFA Interior Design and Student Health and Counseling Services present Esther Sperber, founder of Studio ST Architects, and psychoanalyst and SVA staff member Navah Steiner discussing the concept of home. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
1:30pm: Smart Classroom Training
SVA Audio Visual Services presents an information session on how to use the College's "smart" classroom technology. Topics include classroom equipment, trouble-shooting techniques and technical support information. RSVP to rbarton@sva.edu. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 311.
6:00pm: "Help Is on the Way!" Reception
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition of student work that salutes the work of Good Samaritans in a satirical "museum" of vehicles designed in an attempt to help others. Curated by Kevin O’Callaghan, chair of 3D Design. Through Saturday, April 9, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
6:30pm: Remote Area Medical
BFA Animation and BFA Film present a screening of Remote Area Medical (2012), a documentary about the pioneers of no-cost health care clinics near Bristol, Tennessee. Filmmakers Jeff Reichert and Farihah Zaman will answer questions following the screening. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
7:00pm: BK BioReactor
The Visible Futures Lab presents its latest artists-in-residence, the collective of designers and scientists known as BK BioReactor, discussing their investigations into the microbiology of the Gowanus Canal. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Benjamin Prosky: Designing Culture
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Benjamin Prosky, Architizer co-founder and executive director of the American Institute of Architects New York Chapter (AIANY) and the Center for Architecture, discussing the role of contemporary design publications in addressing the challenges of presenting visually compelling architecture and design. Tuesday, March 29, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
