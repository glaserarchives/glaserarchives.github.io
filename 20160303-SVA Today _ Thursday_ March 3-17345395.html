SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, March 3, 2016
VITREOPHOBIA: Proc. 1-3 by MFA Photography, Video and Related Media student Erin Davis.
SVA at PULSE New York
SVA Galleries is hosting a booth for the second year in a row at the 2016 PULSE New York Contemporary Art Fair, taking place at the Metropolitan Pavilion (125 West 18th Street) from Thursday, March 3, through Sunday, March 8. Check out some of the featured work by nine graduate and undergraduate students, and stop by booth A-314 to see it in person. SVA students can purchase discounted $10 one-day passes with the code "DAYSVA" by clicking here.
Last Chance: "Duplicates, Dummies and Dolls"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by Sara Mejia Kriendler (MFA 2013 Fine Arts), which explores modes of production through her use of discarded Styrofoam and plastic packaging as a representation of waste and excess. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Becky Nahom. Closes today, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
6:00pm: An Affair of Books, Open House and Book Fair
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents a fair featuring over 50 titles by SVA faculty members and program alumni for sale. Book signings and tours of the department's new space will also be available. 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.
6:00pm: "LinkedIn," "Surrogates," "Artist/Printer" and "Books" Reception
BFA Fine Arts presents four exhibitions of student work, curated by department chair Suzanne Anker, assistant to the chair Gary Sherman and faculty members Gunar Prande (MFA 1993 Fine Arts) and David Sandlin. Through Saturday, March 12, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
7:00pm: Adam Peacock: "Machines for Automating Taste, a New Meritocracy"
The Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence Adam Peacock discussing and presenting a preview of his new exhibition, "Machines for Automating Taste: A New Meritocracy." 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

A Lens Into Drawing

Independent NY

Take Note
If You're Sick, Stay Home
Student Health and Counseling Services asks the SVA community to help minimize the impact and spread of the flu and other illnesses. Respiratory illnesses spread when an infected person coughs, sneezes or touches a surface that is then touched by others. Individuals who are sick should stay home to recover to prevent spreading the illness. In the case of the flu, this means staying home for 24 hours after fever has subsided. For more information, please visit sva.edu/flu.
Orientation Leader Information Sessions
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents information sessions for students interested in becoming orientation leaders. The sessions will be held on Monday, March 14, 10:00am and 3:00pm, and Tuesday, March 15, 6:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
SVA Summer Housing Registration
Registration for summer housing at the Ludlow Residence is now open. Students can apply through the Residence Life housing portal by visiting reslife.sva.edu. The deadline is Friday, April 1, for students currently living in the Ludlow Residence who would like to reserve their room, and Friday, April 15, noon, for everyone else. For rates and more information, click here. Contact summerhousing@sva.edu for questions.
