I, Tonya Screening

SVA Theatre presents a of I, Tonya, starring Margot Robbie and Allison Janney and directed by Craig Gillespie (BFA 1989 Media Arts). Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Tonight, 5:45pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
EVENTS

1:00pm | SVA Galleries Call for Submissions: How-to-Apply Workshop

SVA Galleries presents a workshop on how to apply for inclusion in 2018 juried exhibitions. Free pizza will be served. Today, 1:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Call for submissions: SVA Galleries welcomes submissions of work by all current matriculated students (undergraduate and graduate) in any medium. A select student jury will review entries to be included in a group exhibition in 2018. Minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0 is required. Application available here. Deadline: Friday, January 12, 5:00pm. Contact Marysia Komer at mkomer@sva.edu with any questions. Upcoming how-to-apply workshops: Tuesday, December 5, 10:00am, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C, and Thursday, December 14, 10:00am. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 302.

6:00pm | Printmaking Facilities Tour

SVA Continuing Education presents a tour of SVA's printmaking facilities, including the silkscreen, lithography, etching, letterpress and monoprint studios, as well as the printmaking computer lab. RSVP here. 6:00 – 7:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

6:30pm | The Inside Scoop on How to Succeed in TV News

Two agents from United Talent Agency representing some of the biggest names in TV join Brian Stelter, host of CNN’s Reliable Sources, to reveal the secrets of the trade, how to land an agent, what’s needed in your resume reel, and more. Presented by Center for Communication and SVA Theatre. Tickets free for SVA students and faculty, $10 general admission; purchase here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

I Am Jane Doe Screening

Metro NY Chapter of the US National Committee to UN Women presents a screening of the new documentary I Am Jane Doe, in recognition of the Orange the World Campaign, the UN Women’s initiative to end violence against women. Following the screening, there will be a discussion with renowned experts in the fight against human trafficking. A reception will precede the screening. Tickets $5 for SVA students, faculty and staff with the code SVAUN; purchase here. Friday, December 8, 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Paris Impressionists: A Walking Tour

SVA Destinations presents a spring break trip to Paris. Refresh your creative palate with the masters of impressionist painting; explore Paris and its surrounding suburbs by walking in the footsteps of artists such as Degas, Monet, Van Gogh and Renoir; discover where these masters set their easels and visit the museums where their work is exhibited today; and learn about the main characteristics of impressionist painting and how it changed the course of art history. For more information, contact Aziza Gaines, program coordinator, at againes@sva.edu or 212.592.2070, or visit the SVA Destinations website.
