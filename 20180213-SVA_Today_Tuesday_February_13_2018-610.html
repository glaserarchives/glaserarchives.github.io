Celebrate the Lunar New Year in NYC

Here are a few things to see, do and eat in and around New York to commemorate the Lunar New Year, which begins Friday, February 16.
EVENTS

1:00pm | Turner Broadcasting Information Session

Join SVA Career Development to learn more about opportunities with Turner Broadcasting in New York and Atlanta. A company recruiter will be on campus to discuss their paid internships, apprenticeships and full-time trainee programs. Registration required; do so here. 1:00 – 2:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.

4:00pm | Capturing the Crossroads

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents two documentary screenings and a Q&A with filmmakers Rachel Shuman and Ann Collins, followed by a discussion led by program thesis candidates Nachiket Guttikar and Emily Cohen. RSVP here. 4:00 – 9:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

6:00pm | BFA Fine Arts Exhibition Receptions

BFA Fine Arts presents four exhibitions of painting, sculpture, new media and printmaking by its students: "The Politics of Identity," "This, That & the Other," "Day In, Day Out" and "The Printed Image." 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Monday, February 26. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

6:30pm | Noa Raviv, "Essential Mistakes"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by designer and artist Noa Raviv, who works at the intersection of fashion, art and technology. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

6:30pm | Omar López-Chahoud

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by faculty member and curator Omar López-Chahoud. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

7:00pm | Howard Schatz

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer Howard Schatz. He will present work from his most recent book, KINK. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
PRESS PICK

"One of the Most Original and Compelling" Photographers

Vogue profiles photographer, and now painter, Lorna Simpson (BFA 1983 Photography), and discusses her new work.
NYC PICK

Galentine's Day

A night of art making, cookie eating, music listening and wine tasting. Tickets $10; purchase here. 7:00pm. Strand Bookstore, 828 Broadway.
TAKE NOTE

Information Session: SVA Destinations Rome, Italy, and Avignon, France

Learn about two SVA Destinations programs, Underground Rome (May 14 – 28) and Art History in Southern France (June 1 – 14). For more information, contact Justin Elm, jelm@sva.edu or 212.592.2255. Friday, February 16, 1:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 402C.

SVA Humanities and Sciences' Sixth Annual Writing Contest

Submissions are now open for Humanities and Sciences' Sixth Annual Writing Contest. First-, second- and third-place prizes are given in each of the five categories: poetry, short story, memoir or personal essay, critical essay and short script. You can submit either three works in the same genre or three different pieces in differing categories. 4,000-word limit. Deadline: Thursday, March 1. Submit here.
