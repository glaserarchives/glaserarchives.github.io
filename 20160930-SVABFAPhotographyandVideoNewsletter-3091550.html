It's Nice That Features Alum Tim Schutsky's Portfolio

Within photography portfolios a sense of style is key. It is necessary for the photographer to document and display an image appropriately, but additionally speak of the personality behind the lens. This is a characteristic Brooklyn-based photographer Tim Schutsky (alum 2015) defines in his broad yet considered work. The photographer’s ability to be broad within his practice means his work can be appropriated commercially and editorially with work featured in Vice, VSCO and Men’s Health.

Click here to see more of Tim's work. 
Faculty Steel Stillman Interviews Donald Moffatt for Art in America

In the latest issue of Art in American (September, 2016) faculty member Steel Stillman sits down with New York City artist, Donald Moffatt to discuss his work. The two talk about Moffatt's up-bringing in Texas, his first jobs in NYC, and his work from the wheat pastings in the late 1980's to present.

Visit Art in America to read more.
Fader Features Alum Anthony Geathers's Photographs of Streetball Culture

Over the past two years, Anthony Geathers (alum 2016) has chronicled New York City’s hallowed streetball culture in great detail. As part of an ongoing series, Geathers has shot at legend-making courts like Harlem’s Rucker Park, Gersh Park in East New York, and “The Cage” in the West Village, rendering the movement and spontaneity of the game with a cinematic eye.

Click here to see more of Geathers's photographs.
Bushwick Open Studios

Bushwick Open Studios (BOS) is an arts and culture festival hosted October 1-2, 2016 by the volunteer-run organization Arts in Bushwick. Most studios are open 11am – 7pm on Saturday and Sunday of the festival.
BOS has been running since 2006 and is New York’s largest open studios event, encouraging thousands of Bushwick-based artists to open their doors and share their work.

Vist BOS to get a full list of open studios and events.

Submit to the Arnold Newman Prize in Photographic Portraiture for a Chance at the $20,000 Prize  

The Arnold Newman Prize was established by the Arnold and Augusta Newman Foundation in 2009 to recognize and reward excellence in new directions in photographic portraiture. The annual prize winner receives an award of $20,000 and is acknowledged in New York City with an exhibit and celebration. Photographers must submit twelve photographs that represent a cohesive vision and body of work, a resume, and a statement of up to 500 words. 

Click here for submission guidelines.

The 37th Annual Eugene Smith Grant Ceremony at the SVA Theater October 13th

The W. Eugene Smith Memorial Fund Grant, selected from over 300 worldwide submissions, is presented annually to photographers whose work carries forward the tradition practiced by Eugene Smith during his 45-year career in photojournalism. 

This year's keynote speakers include Oscar-winning Director and Founder of Jigsaw Productions, Alex Gibney and Director of Photography for New York Times Magazine, Kathy Ryan. The grant enables recipients to undertake and complete worthy photojournalistic projects. This event is free and open to the public on a first come, first serve basis.

Snapshots of Coney Island 

For nearly 100 years Coney Island has been one of Brooklyn's most iconic destinations. For creative photographers interested in documenting New York City's people, landscape, and attractions, Coney Island has proved a rich subject for nearly as long. Join photographers Elaine Norman, Larry Racioppo, Doug Ross, and Norman Borden in conversation with the Transit Museum's Associate Curator Jodi Shapiro for a discussion on Coney Island's transformation as seen through their cameras' lenses.

Alum Darin Mickey Documents Disappearing Record Stores in Photobook

From 2014 through mid-2015, Mickey (1998) visited and photographed several independent record shops in New York, New Jersey and Pennsylvania. In its 64 pages, "Death Takes a Holiday" takes a look at the people, possessions and gathering spaces of a once-thriving subculture on the wane.

Click here to read an interview with Darin and watch a short documentary. 
Sally Mann Exhibit at Gagosian 

Gagosian presents, Remembered Light, an exhibition of color and black-and-white photographs by Sally Mann, taken between 1999 and 2012. 

Mann is known and regarded for her images of intimate and familiar subjects rendered sublime and disquieting: children, landscape, family, and the nature of mortality.

Visit Gagosian for more information.
