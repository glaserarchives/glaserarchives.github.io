SVA Today
Tuesday, October 24, 2017

The Art of Artists' Journals [Video]

Three SVA alumni, Frank Ockenfels 3 (BFA 1983 Photography), Kara Rooney (MFA 2009 Art Writing) and Carl Titolo (G 1967), discuss the role that sketchbooks play for them. A version of this article appears in the fall 2017 issue of the Visual Arts Journal.
EVENTS

6:30pm | Dan Chen, "File – Save As – Intimacy"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by improvisational engineer, interaction designer and technology investigator Dan Chen. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

6:30pm | Doubt Night

SVA's Christian Fellowship Club invites students, faculty and staff to attend a Q&A session on faith, science, apologetics and doubt with guest speaker Ken Findlay. Click here to submit anonymous questions for the event. 6:30 – 8:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

6:30pm | Seph Rodney

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by Seph Rodney, an adjunct faculty member at SVA and writer, who is working on a book based on his ongoing research regarding the personalization of the museum visit. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

7:00pm | Brooklyn Arts Press at SVA Library

SVA Library hosts an evening with Brooklyn Arts Press featuring readings by authors Jason Koo, Anaïs Duplan, Debora Kuan and Sheila Maldonado, with music by John Leitera from Bushwick-based rock band Tuff Sunshine. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Eli Schmidt

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer Eli Schmidt, whose work is a fusion of fashion, documentary and editorial sensibilities, as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Using Photos to Communicate

NYC PICKS

Caroline Champetier at CinéSalon

A CinéSalon double feature: The Innocents, 4:00pm, and Holy Motors, 7:30pm, followed by a Q&A with cinematographer Caroline Champetier. Two-film package is $10 for students and $23 for general public at the box office, or you can purchase tickets here. French Institute Alliance Francaise, 55 East 59th Street.

The Apparitionists Book Talk

Author and curator of religion at the National Museum of American History Peter Manseau shares the story of infamous spirit photographer William Mumler, the fraud allegations that haunted him and a nation grasping for the promise of the afterlife. Tickets $5; purchase here. 6:30pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.

Open to SVA students, faculty and staff; RSVP here. Wednesday, November 8, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
