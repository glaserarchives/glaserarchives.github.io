Message from the Provost
Dear Colleagues:

A mere two weeks ago, I sent my first communication announcing the start of our transition to fully online instruction—a 180-degree shift in the way we have taught art and design for close to a century.  In these two weeks, our lives, and those of our students and their families, have been completely upended. We all have been thrust into the world of the unknown, and the dire prognostications of the medical and scientific communities about what lie ahead seem unthinkable.  Yet, we continue on as best we can.

Against the backdrop of this world-historical moment, you, our remarkable faculty, have adapted in ways I could not have anticipated.  In fact, in the first couple of days of planning, I dismissed transitioning all our classes online as an impossibility. This is not because I lacked faith in you all, but I could not comprehend the magnitude of what we were about to undertake. 

I am amazed—and humbled—by the efforts you all have made to rise to the challenge of retrofitting your classes so that students may continue their studies remotely.  Your chairs have provided outstanding leadership, and our Office of Learning Technologies has—and continues to—perform above and beyond the call of duty. Every single office and department across the College is supporting the herculean task of maintaining the continuity of our academic, student support, and business operations.

Thank you!

What follows is a digest of the various and ongoing sources of support for you and your students.
Office of Learning Technologies Resources

The Office of Learning Technologies (OLT) has incorporated online office hours into its "new normal."  If you need assistance with online teaching and learning, you can now click on a Zoom link and be connected face-to-face with an OLT staff member on your screen.

OLT online office hours will run Monday through Friday, from 10:00 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. and will remain at THIS LINK for the remainder of this spring 2020 semester. We encourage you to click on the link and then bookmark it for future reference. If you have never joined a Zoom video conference before, then you will be prompted to download the Zoom app, but can also simply join through your web browser.

You can also leave OLT an email or voicemail and they will respond to you as quickly as possible, olt@sva.edu and 212.592.2313. Additionally, faculty are encouraged to check out resources in the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas.

SVA/IT is providing free VPN accounts for students who returned to China to continue studying full-time in the Spring 2020 semester.  To request an account, students may contact the Help Desk: helpdesk@sva.edu.
SVA Registrar Extends the Grading Deadline by Two Weeks 

The official grading deadline for spring 2020 will be extended for two weeks. Final grades will now be due by close of business Monday, May 18. All faculty members must submit grades online by following the instructions in this document. Please reach out to rosters@sva.edu with any questions or concerns.

Student Health and Counseling Services Resources for Students

The team at Student Health and Counseling Services thanks the SVA faculty for the support they have shown to students during this transitional time. Structure and social support are incredibly helpful in unsettling times and continuing your classes via Zoom has provided students with structure and social support.

If you or someone you know experiences fever, cough or shortness of breath, please consult with a health-care provider. In order to limit in-person interaction while you are ill, arranging a virtual appointment is strongly encouraged. Below are links to some virtual urgent-care providers:

City MD (accepts Cigna)
NYU Virtual Urgent Care (accepts Cigna)
New York-Presbyterian (low-cost consultations; does not bill insurance)

Student Health and Counseling Services staff are working remotely to support students through this time of uncertainty. Please encourage your students to take part in the health programming that is posted on their instagram page and listed in SVA Today. Students needing support should email health@sva.edu to set up an individual online meeting with a clinician.
