SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, February 11, 2016
It's Not Too Late
Many spring Continuing Education courses have not begun and are open for registration. SVA Close Up offers a rundown of some of the CE courses still available, including online courses. Above image by Seth Michael Forman, instructor for Memory, Imagination and Mixed Media.
Opening: "The Artist's Journal"
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of small-scale paintings that investigate personal iconography, narrative and color theory. Reception tonight, 6:00pm. Through Sunday, March 13, VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Opening: "Take Care"
SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents an exhibition of student posters on the theme of self-care for this year's HOPE Competition. The winner will be announced at tomorrow's reception, 5:30pm. Through Saturday, February 20, SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Noon: Lunar New Year Celebration
The International Student Office and Student Health and Counseling Services present a celebration of the Year of the Monkey with an array of Chinese and Korean foods, sweets and tea. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
Press pick of the day
New York Institutions Join Forces
Arte Fuse calls "elmuseo@SVA" a "landmark group show," and profiles several of the MFA Fine Arts alumni featured in the exhibition. "elmuseo@SVA" runs through Saturday, February 20, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
NYC picks of the day
Aida Ruilova: "The Pink Palace"
An exhibition of work by the MFA Photography and Related Media alumnus inspired by the aesthetic of 1960s and '70s erotic horror films. Reception tonight, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, March 12, Marlborough Chelsea, 545 West 25th Street.
Andrea Burgay: "Destroy Edit Transform"
An exhibition of work by the BFA Fine Arts alumnus that reconfigures the mundane into the transcendent. Reception tonight, 6:00pm. Through Sunday, March 13, A.I.R. Gallery, 155 Plymouth Street, Brooklyn.
Portraiture in Early New York
A discussion of portrait painting in colonial New York. General admission is $16, $12 for students and seniors. 6:30pm, The Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
