Thursday, April 7, 2016
A still from MFA Computer Art student Tomonari Michigami's virtual reality game Stray Sheep.
Enter the Virtual
Beginning today at noon, MFA Computer Art students open their studios to visitors to show off their recent work. Reception tonight, 5:00 - 7:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor. Studios are also open tomorrow, noon - 7:00pm.
Construction Scheduled at 205 and 209 East 23rd Street
Local Law 11 requires that the facades of New York City buildings of more than six stories be regularly inspected. After a fall 2015 inspection, it was deemed sections of the facade of 205 and 209 East 23rd Street buildings need to be removed and replaced. This project will begin on Monday, April 11, and continue until the end of July. In the first two weeks of the project, scaffolding will put up on all sides of the buildings. Select facade removal will take place at the end of April. During the removal period (approximately two to four weeks) there will be loud construction noise associated with the project between the hours of 7:30am and 3:30pm. Periodic updates will be provided in SVA Today in the coming weeks. For questions, contact Richard Riccio, executive director of facilities, 212.592.2156.
3:15pm: Mindfulness Workshop
Student Health and Counseling Services presents a workshop on practical mindfulness techniques that can help reduce the impact of negative thoughts, anxiety and stress, as well as identify patterns that may block creative energy. Open to SVA students. RSVP to nsteiner@sva.edu. 136 West 21st Street, Room 415F.
6:30pm: Aaron Shurin, The Skin of Meaning
MFA Art Writing presents Aaron Shurin, poet and professor emeritus of the University of San Francisco, discussing his new book, The Skin of Meaning, a collection of essays fueled by gender and queer studies combined with radical traditions in poetry. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
