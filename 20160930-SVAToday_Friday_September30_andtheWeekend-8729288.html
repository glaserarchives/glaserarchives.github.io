EVENTS

Fashion Film, 2011 - 2016

An exhibition of fashion films by MPS Fashion Photography alumni. Reception Wednesday, October 19, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Saturday, November 5, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Sight Seen

MPS Digital Photography presents an exhibition of thesis projects by members of the class of 2016. Curated by SVA faculty member Michael Foley. Reception Wednesday, October 19, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Saturday, November 5, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

6:45pm: Distance and The Dove

BFA Film faculty member Salvatore Oppedisano and The Lover Company present screenings of Distance (1975), starring James Woods, and The Dove (1968), a short film that parodies the work of Ingmar Bergman. Both films were directed by Anthony Lover, who will answer questions following the screenings. A reception will precede the screenings at 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm: BK BioReactor

The Visible Futures Lab presents artists-in-residence BK BioReactor discussing the collective's investigation into the microbiology of Brooklyn's Gowanus Canal, an EPA-designated Superfund site. An exhibition of their work runs through Tuesday, October 25, Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

8:00pm: The Magnificent Seven

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of The Magnificent Seven, starring Denzel Washington, Ethan Hawke and Chris Pratt. Admission is free with SVA ID (two guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Saturday: Untitled (Gender and Representation) 2016

SVA and BFA Photography and Video present a daylong symposium that seeks to address the complex issues of gender that have dominated cultural conversations in the last several years. Panelists include Jennifer Blessing, Kate Bornstein, Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography), Ron Gregg, M. Lamar, Zanele Muholi, and others. Free for SVA students, $40 general admission. 9:30am - 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

SVA Annual Security and Fire Safety Reports

The School of Visual Arts is committed to the safety and security of all members of the College community. In accordance with the federal Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act of 1998, the latest SVA Annual Security Report and Fire Safety Report are now available. These reports contain statistics and institutional policies concerning fire safety, as well as information on campus security issues such as sexual assault and other matters. Copies of both reports can be accessed on the Health and Safety section of the SVA Consumer Information page at sva.edu/about/consumer-information. If you have questions regarding this information, please contact SVA Institutional Research at 212.592.2829.

RSVP to nsteiner@sva.edu. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Tuesday, October 4, 3:30pm, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
