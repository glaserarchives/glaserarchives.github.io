SVA Today
Wednesday, November 1, 2017

A "One-of-a-Kind" Exhibit

BFA Design students are making their mark on Madison Avenue, where their one-of-a-kind fashion ensembles are on display, as part of the Madison Avenue Business Improvement District’s One-of-a-Kind Luxury event.
EVENTS

2:00pm | Andrea Steele

MFA Products of Design presents a talk by Andrea Steele, AIA, a partner at TEN Arquitectos. She is in charge of the firm's U.S. projects, with a particular focus on institutional, cultural and community oriented projects. Open to graduate students only. 2:00 – 4:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

6:00pm | MFA Art Practice Information Session

MFA Art Practice presents an opportunity to tour the facility and meet with the chair, faculty, department staff, current students and alumni. It will also be broadcast on Facebook Live for guests unable to travel to NYC. Please email artpractive@sva.edu for more details. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 519H.

6:30pm | Superpool, "Groundwork in Istanbul"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Superpool, an international multidisciplinary design studio based in Istanbul. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
PRESS PICK

Litovsky in Tel Aviv

AI-AP on how Dina Litovsky (MFA 2010 Photography, Video and Related Media) recently traveled to Tel Aviv to photograph the city and "create her own story and vision" for AFAR magazine.
NYC PICKS

Songy of Paradise: Gary Panter and Dash Shaw

BFA Cartooning faculty member Gary Panter and Dash Shaw (BFA 2005 Illustration) discuss Panter’s newest graphic novel, Songy of Paradise. Free; register here. 7:00pm. Berger Forum, New York Public Library, 475 Fifth Avenue, Room 227.

Women's Suffrage Turns 100! Part 1: Women Can Vote. Now What?

Brooklyn Historical Society's director of public history Julie Golia and historian Deborah Gray White discuss the 100th anniversary of women's suffrage and its reverberations. Tickets $5; purchase here. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE

Student Leadership Series: From College to Professional and Beyond

SVA Student Engagement and Leadership and Residence Life present a panel discussion where SVA alumni will talk about navigating a professional career as an artist. Refreshments will be served. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Wednesday, November 8, 6:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

How to Report the News that Matters

Center for Communication presents some of the best journalists working today—from The Guardian, Rolling Stone, and The Bitter Southerner—who tell it like it is from the front lines of politics, national security, natural disasters, racial injustice and humanitarian crises. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff with ID; get tickets here. Wednesday, November 15, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Behind the Scenes of Disney/Pixar's Coco

BFA Animation, BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects, MFA Computer Arts and SVA Career Development present Adam Molina, co-director of Disney/Pixar's Coco, for a behind-the-scenes look at this new film. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Free; register here. Friday, November 17, 11:30am. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
