SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Wednesday, February 10, 2016
"Sinister Beauty"
Pulitzer Prize-winning author Michael Cunningham reflects on his collaboration with MFA Illustration as Visual Essay faculty member and alumnus Yuko Shimizu (2003), who illustrated his new book A Wild Swan, featured in the exhibition "Women in Illustration." Reception tonight, along with the reception for "Gone," 6:00pm, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
1:00pm: Time Management Tips for Artists
SVA Career Development presents an information session on time management for artists, including tips such as creating technology boundaries when a deadline looms. Click here for a full list of spring workshops and information sessions presented by Career Development. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 301.
7:00pm: Entrepreneurial Design Annual 2016
MFA Interaction Design presents students of the Entrepreneurial Design course presenting their crowdfunded projects, including details of the problems these projects aim to solve, their solutions and the communities they hope to serve. RSVP with code "YAYSVA." 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
Press pick of the day
Black Swan [Video]
InStyle features a promotional campaign video directed by Bon Duke (MPS 2012 Digital Photography) for dance apparel company Capezio, starring Maddie Ziegler.
NYC pick of the day
Paul Graham
The photographer discusses his work with Nicholas Dawidoff. Purchase a copy of his new book, The Whiteness of the Whale, or a $20 gift card to attend. 7:00pm, Strand Books, 828 Broadway, 3rd floor.
Take Note
Movie Ticket Sales Suspended Today
Discounted AMC ticket sales are suspended today. Sales will resume on Friday, February 12, at 2:00pm in the Student Engagement and Leadership Office, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine. Contact activities@sva.edu for more information.
Galentine's Day
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office and VASA present a dinner buffet of breakfast foods, female empowering crafts and a raffle for a prize donated by Babeland. Open to SVA students who donate $3 or more to support the National Network of Abortion Funds. Friday, February 12, 7:00pm, SVA Student Center, 333 West 23rd Street.
The Vanishing Points of Treece: Natasha Chuk and Dina Kantor in Conversation
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and BFA Visual & Critical Studies present a conversation between art writer Natasha Chuk and photographer Dina Kantor. Monday, February 15, 6:30pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
Humanities and Sciences Writing Contest
The Humanities and Sciences department presents its fourth annual writing contest. Undergraduate students are invited to submit original poetry, short stories, personal essays, critical essays and film scripts or plays. Click here to learn more and submit work. The deadline for submissions is Friday, April 1. Winners will be announced Monday, April 18.
