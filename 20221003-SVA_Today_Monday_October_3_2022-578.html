SVA’s Nurse Abby continues to answer your questions regarding COVID-19 and other health-related concerns. Read below for the latest Q&A and use this link to see the question archive. You can also submit any questions you have here.



Is it safe to get the flu shot and the new COVID-19 booster at the same time?


Yes! I just received both (I call it the “flooster”)—this is a no-brainer this year. Here are just a few reasons why:

  • •COVID-19 continues to spread and the new bivalent boosters are equipped with the BA.4 and BA.5 Omicron strains. Either can help protect individuals from serious illness and/or hospitalization.
  • •Flu season has just started in our part of the world, and one of our very best defenses against serious illness or death from the flu is getting an annual flu vaccine, which typically includes the most common and recently circulating variants of flu.
  • •Side effects from each vaccine are similar, manageable and much preferred over actual infection from either virus.

Currently, anyone aged six months or older is eligible for the flu vaccine; anyone aged 12 or older is eligible for the updated Pfizer booster; and anyone aged 18 or older is eligible for the updated Moderna booster.


Students are encouraged to reach out to health@sva.edu for a nursing appointment with me to discuss any questions or concerns you may have about the flu shot, the new boosters, or any other health-related issues.
