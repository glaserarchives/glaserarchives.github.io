SVA Today
Monday, September 17, 2018

"The Art of Sharing"

This exhibition, held at a psychiatric clinic in southern France, marks the ninth year of partnership between SVA and the Valetudo Association and features work by SVA students and alumni that promotes the therapeutic value of art.
EVENTS

6:00pm | Free Risograph Workshop

Join Zen and the Art of Riso, a workshop held by Stewart Wilson from the artist-in-residence program at MFA Visual Narrative's RisoLab. Limited seats, no experience needed. Free but must register here. 6:00 – 9:00pm. RisoLab, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.

6:30pm | Colette

An advance screening of Colette, starring Keira Knightly and Dominic West, followed by a Q&A with producer Christine Vachon. Admission is free for SVA students, faculty and staff and their guests with SVA ID. 6:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

Challenging Notions of Femininity

CNN speaks with MFA Fine Arts faculty member Marilyn Minter about her current exhibition in Hong Kong, which "re-imagines classic female bathers."
NYC PICK

Feast of San Gennaro

Enjoy Italian food, parades, free concerts and family-friendly activities in New York City's Little Italy. Through Sunday, September 23. Along Mulberry Street between Canal and Houston Streets.

Chitra Ganesh: "Her Garden, a Mirror"

Visual artist Chitra Ganesh explores gender and power in an imaginary future in this solo exhibition. Through Saturday, October 20. The Kitchen, 512 West 19th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Constitution Day 2018

Today is Constitution Day, a day of federal observance which commemorates the day in 1787 when the U.S. Constitution was signed at the Constitutional Convention in Philadelphia. To learn more about the U.S. Constitution visit the U.S. Senate website, or pick up a free pocket-sized edition at Student Affairs, 340 East 24th Street.

The Predator and White Boy Rick Screenings

SVA Theatre presents two screenings on Saturday, September 22: The Predator, starring Sterling K. Brown, at 5:00pm, and White Boy Rick, starring Matthew McConaughey, at 8:00pm. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Group Chat

Three members of an artists' critique group—Elizabeth Glaessner, Jessica Stoller and Robin Williams—will present their work and experiences, discussing how to form an active group as a strategy to successfully move out of the classroom and into the world in a sustainable way. A Q&A will follow. Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies. Monday, September 24, 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

ISO Annual Mid-Autumn Festival

Join the International Student Office to celebrate the Mid-Autumn Festival. Chinese, Korean and Filipino food will be served, along with moon cakes and Japanese tea. RSVP required. Monday, September 24, 5:00 – 6:30pm. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, roof terrace.
