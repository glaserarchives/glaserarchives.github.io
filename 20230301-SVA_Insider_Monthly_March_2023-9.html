🎙️ Get To Know Kenyetta Lovings, associate director, sustainability and energy management, Office of the President

Welcome to SVA! Throughout your career in sustainability and energy management, you’ve worked within a variety of different fields. What led you to SVA?

My heart has always been devoted to the arts. As an undergraduate at Sarah Lawrence College, I worked in curatorial departments at arts institutions like the Studio Museum in Harlem and Bronx Museum of the Arts and held a role with the Ministry of Culture of Senegal, supporting the Dak’ART Biennial during a year abroad. I attained my first graduate degree in the social sciences from the University of Chicago, focusing my thesis on socio-political comic strips in the Democratic Republic of Congo. I then worked in international development for a few years, engaging oil companies on corporate social responsibility. From this experience, I became curious about working in the energy and sustainability space, so I returned to school to pursue a second graduate degree in sustainability management at Columbia University. Now that I’m at SVA, I feel my career has come full circle back to the arts, and I am elated to be part of the SVA community.


Why is sustainability important for an organization?

The benefits to SVA are threefold: First, it’s great for the planet when we focus our resources on energy conservation measures that reduce carbon emissions from our buildings. Second, when we reduce the consumption of energy on our campus, we save money on our utility bills, which frees up resources for SVA to redirect into building operations or other school services. Third, sustainability at SVA enhances our built environment. There are green building studies that show with the reduction of carbon emissions through energy conservation, indoor air quality can improve, as well as occupant productivity.


As recently appointed associate director of sustainability and energy management in the Office of the President, are there any projects in particular that you’re excited about?

We’re focusing our immediate efforts on energy conservation measures that can be deployed at low- or no cost to SVA through incentives offered by utility companies—mainly lighting projects that will convert lighting no longer compliant with the energy code with more efficient LEDs. We’re also exploring opportunities to electrify buildings within integrated systems that could provide the on-site generation of renewable energy. We’re also excited about optimizing existing building automation systems that can control the operation of our energy assets from a desktop.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Design Your First Infographic

Instructor Nigel French helps you identify when a graphic representation is better than a written one, and he goes over the fundamental design aspects of infographics such as icons and font choices.


Editing Mastery: How to Edit Writing to Perfection

In this course, instructor Shani Raja acquaints you with a powerful technique—deep editing—that can help you transform nearly any piece of nonfiction writing into magnificent, sparkling prose.


20 Rules for Visual Communication

How can you communicate information more visually? Instructor Amy Balliett has you covered. In this course, she introduces you to 20 rules that go into the “why,” the “who,” and the “how” of effective visual communication.
