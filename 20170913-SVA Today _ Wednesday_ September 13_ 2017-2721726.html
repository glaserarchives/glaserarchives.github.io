See SVA at Photoville

BFA Photography and Video and MPS Digital Photography present exhibitions of student work at this year's Photoville, the annual photography show housed in shipping containers in Brooklyn Bridge Park. Through Sunday, September 24. Brooklyn Bridge Plaza, Water Street at New Dock Street, Brooklyn.
EVENTS

7:00pm | Artist-in-Residence John Hartmann

Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence John Hartmann, who will talk about his past work with Freecell Architecture and his residency proposal: to create a series of autonomous sculptures that depict observations of the city, ranging from object fetish to urban analysis. 7:00 – 8:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

7:00pm | Bill Plympton's Revengeance

Continuing the After School Special, SVA Theatre presents the New York premiere of Bill Plympton's (1969 Cartooning) and Jim Lujan's Revengeance, followed by a Q&A with the directors. Free. 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Artists to Watch this Month

Artspace's list of eight artist to watch in September includes Andrea McGinty (MFA 2014 Fine Arts) and Claire Christerson (BFA 2015 Photography).
NYC PICKS

The Mulleavy Sisters of Rodarte

Fashion brand Rodarte's Mulleavy sisters join The New York Times for a conversation about their brand, collaborations, upcoming movie Woodshock and more. Tickets $10 for SVA community with discount code "SVA"; purchase here by 5:00pm. 7:00pm. Merkin Concert Hall at Kaufman Music Center, 129 West 67th Street.

Rooftop Farms: Greening the Concrete Jungle

A panel discussion held in conjunction with "Beneath the Same Sky," an exhibition of paintings by Elizabeth Downer Riker that reveals commonalities between two vastly different cultures through the depiction of agricultural labor. 7:00 – 8:30pm. Exhibition open through Saturday, September 30. Ceres Gallery 547 West 27th Street, Suite 201.
TAKE NOTE

"Hey! You! Get Off of My Cloud, Part 1" Reception

MA Curatorial Practice presents "Hey! You! Get Off of My Cloud, Part 1," an exhibition that explores the impact of cloud computing on culture, economy and artistic practice, curated by program fellow Michele Thursz. Reception: tomorrow, September 14, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Pitches, Previews and Performance

T.D. White and Loose Moon Productions presents a night of selected film pitches, short film screenings, including Last Licks and Will at Work, and performances. Free. Tomorrow, September 14; doors open at 7:30pm, event begins at 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sign Up for Visible Futures Lab Equipment Demonstrations

SVA's Visible Futures Lab invites graduate students, faculty and staff to attend equipment demonstrations at the lab. Find dates, times and required RSVP here. Through Tuesday, September 19. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.
