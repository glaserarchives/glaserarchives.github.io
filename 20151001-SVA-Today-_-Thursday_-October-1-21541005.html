SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, October 1, 2015
Solving Problems Through Advertising [Video]
Eric Weisberg is an executive creative director at JWT in New York City, and teaches in the BFA Advertising program. In the video above, he discusses advertising as problem-solving, the current creative digital landscape and his unique approach to teaching students at SVA.
5:00pm: RisoLab Demo and Open House
MFA Visual Narrative and the Division of Continuing Education present RisoLab, a Risograph printing facility that combines the vibrant spot colors of silkscreen and the high resolution and mass production of offset lithography with the user-friendly automation of an advanced color copier. Open house ends at 7:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.
6:00pm: "Zeit und Geist" Closing Reception
An exhibition featuring BFA Visual & Critical Studies alumni who were residents of the Leipzig International Art Program. VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
6:30pm: Charles Stein in Conversation with Terry Winters
Poet, critic and MFA Art Writing faculty member Charles Stein talks with artist Terry Winters about Winters’ work and Stein’s critical engagement with it. Presented by MFA Art Writing. 132 West 21st, 6th floor.
8:00pm: Joseph Maida with Eva Respini
BFA Photography and Video and DEAR DAVE, magazine present artist Joseph Maida in conversation with Eva Respini, the Barbara Lee Chief Curator at the Institute of Contemporary Art, Boston. RSVP: info@deardavemagazine.com. Spring Studios, 6 St. Johns Lane.
Last Chance: "A Thin Line Separates Laughter and Pain"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by guest artists featuring video installations, sculpture and textile work that explores the limbo between boundaries, and plays with the concepts of propriety, comfort zones and accepted norms. Closing today, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Take Note
SVA Annual Security and Fire Safety Reports
The School of Visual Arts is committed to the safety and security of all members of the College community. In accordance with the federal Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act of 1998, the SVA Annual Security Report and Fire Safety Report for this year are now available. These reports contain statistics and institutional policies concerning fire safety, as well as information on campus security issues such as sexual assault and other matters. Copies of both reports can be accessed on the Safety and Security section of the SVA Consumer Information page at sva.edu/about-sva/consumer-information. If you have questions regarding this information, please contact SVA Institutional Research at 212.592.2829.
Pixar Company Overview
Join Pixar's University Relations team as they discuss the animation studio's internship program and what they look for when reviewing student reels. Monday, October 5, 10:30am - 2:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
MFA Design Info Session

The 36th Annual W. Eugene Smith Grant in Humanistic Photography
BFA Photography and Video presents the 36th annual W. Eugene Smith Grant in Humanistic Photography and Howard Chapnick Grant for the Advancement of Photojournalism. Wednesday, October 14, 7:00 - 8:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Will Eisner: Celebrating 75 Years of "The Spirit"
The School of Visual Arts Alumni Society, in partnership with the Will and Ann Eisner Family Foundation, presents Pulitzer Prize-winning cartoonist and author Jules Feiffer, along with former publisher and president of DC Comics, Paul Levitz, discussing the legacy of pioneering comic artist and longtime SVA faculty member, Will Eisner. Wednesday, November 11, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
