News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Friday, January 20 – Sunday, January 22, 2017

Top Five at the Summit

A guide to five of the many business, design and health care leaders who will be exchanging ideas and expertise at The Measured Summit, an event hosted by MFA Design for Social Innovation on Tuesday, January 24, at the SVA Theatre.
EVENTS

1:00pm | Rome/Avignon SVA Destinations Information Session

Learn more about two of SVA Destinations' two-week summer programs: Underground Rome, where you can explore the city's most hidden layer – the underground (May 14 – May 28); and Art History in Southern France, where you can draw, paint and photograph on location while exploring the region's art history (June 1 – June 14). Refreshments will be provided. 1:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 401C.

7:00pm | Chris Hackett Exhibition and Talk

The Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence Chris Hackett, who will talk about working with the resources found in the debris of our society, using examples from his time at SVA. Refreshments will be served. 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

Saturday | "The Thin Edge of the Wedge"

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents the diverse thesis projects of its class of 2017, all reflecting the unique expressions of young artists on the cusp of emerging into a larger public. Tomorrow, January 21 – Friday, February 11. Reception, Tuesday, January 24, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
PRESS PICK

What's in Vogue?

Fashion Gone Rogue on Balenciaga’s spring/summer 2017 campaign, shot by Peter Ash Lee (MPS 2012 Fashion Photography) for the February 2017 issue of Vogue Korea.
NYC PICKS

Master Drawings New York

An Upper East Side gallery crawl, featuring seven centuries of work on paper. Free. Tomorrow, January 21 – Saturday, January 28. Begins at Academy Mansion, 2 East 63rd Street and proceeds uptown.

"Dreamlands: Immersive Cinema"

An exhibition on how artists have reimagined the conventions of cinema to create new moving image experiences. Accompanied by a screening series. Through Sunday, February 5. Exhibition free with SVA ID; screening tickets $12. Whitney Museum of American Art, 99 Gansevoort Street.

"Nectar: War Upon the Bees"

The work of BFA Fine Arts Chair Suzanne Anker is featured in this group exhibition, which addresses the growing environmental threats in our world. Free. Through Saturday, February 11. Pratt Manhattan Gallery, 144 West 14th Street, 2nd floor.
TAKE NOTE

Art, Technology, Ecology: A Talk by Artist Frank Gillette

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents video pioneer Frank Gillette, who will talk about the intersections between technology, politics, culture and the natural world. Monday, January 23, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Call for Submissions: Words 85 Deadline Extended

Words 85 is seeking poetry, stories, personal essays, short film scripts and one-act plays. Submit by email to lphillips@sva.edu with subject heading: “Submission to Words 85.” All submissions should include the writer's name, address, email address and phone number. Deadline is January 30.
