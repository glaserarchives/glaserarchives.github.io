NYC AND ONLINE PICKS

Ray Guns, Dames, and the Guilty Gaze: Feminism and the Golden Age of Science Fiction Pulps

Alison Paul (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative) will lead a virtual tour of “Ray Guns, Dames, and the Guilty Gaze: Feminism and the Golden Age of Science Fiction Pulps,” an exhibition she co-curated that turns a feminist lens on the golden age of pulp fiction magazines to question the production and consumption of gender, race and dis/ability through the popular medium of “pulps.” The show also works to shine a light on the female and/or bipoc contributors that have been ignored or erased. This show is also on display at the William Benton Museum of Art at the University of Connecticut. Free. Registration required; register here. Today, September 23, 5:30 – 6:30pm.
