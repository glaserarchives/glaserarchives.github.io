All Faculty Brief | October 2020


Dear Colleagues:

I hope this letter finds you and your families safe and healthy, and that your fall 2020 classes are going well.  Based on feedback received from students, there is a high level of satisfaction with the remote learning experience.  While we are all eager to return to some semblance of a “normal” classroom experience, students and faculty have found online teaching and learning to carry certain benefits, such as higher levels of student engagement.  Students have remarked that many of their professors often spend more time than is allotted for a typical class meeting to provide additional help and support.

I commend all those who have found innovative and creative ways to teach their classes online.  The time and effort you have invested in preparing for the fall 2020 semester is greatly appreciated—and it underscores the fact that no matter the modality, the SVA faculty continues to provide a superior art and design education.

In this installment of the All Faculty Brief, you will find useful information from the Office of Learning Technologies (OLT), the SVA Library, and the Office of the Registrar.  Collectively, these offices and many others are focused on helping you, as faculty, continuously improve your online classes and to support your students’ learning goals. 

An important service the College offers its students is free, confidential, short-term personal counseling, the details of which can be found below.  The Office of Student Affairs encourages faculty to submit a STAR (Students at Risk) Report for students about whom you may have concerns regarding their personal welfare.  Excessive abcences, for instance, could signify a student who is experiencing some form of distress.

Our students are not the only members of the SVA community experiencing the sense of social dislocation and isolation engendered by the Covid-19 virus.  Many of us are struggling in one way or another with the consequences of this virus, now in its eighth month and showing no signs of slowing. Many of us can benefit from some help in managing these struggles and challenges.  SVA’s health insurance plans provide coverage for a range of mental health services, as do most other insurance plans.  There are also free services available to New Yorkers, such as NYC WELL, which provides free mental health support in English, Spanish and Mandarin and has access to translators in about 200 languages. Call 1.888.NYC.WELL (1.888.692.9355) or text WELL to 65173.  Crisis Text Line offers free, around-the-clock support. Text HOME to 741741. 

I sincerely hope that if you need help during these stressful and disorienting times, you will avail yourself of these and other mental health services.  All such services are strictly confidential.

As we enter the fifth week of this fall’s truncated semester, planning for the spring term is well under way.  The Pandemic Recovery Team, of which I am a member, is finalizing plans for the spring, and we hope to share them with you within the next couple of weeks.

In the meantime, please take good care of yourselves and stay safe and healthy.  As I have said since the beginning of the pandemic, we will get through this crisis and will, I am confident, emerge the better for it.  

 
All best wishes,

Chris

Student Affairs: Supporting Students in Distress
These are challenging times for our community, and the Students at Risk (STAR) Committee is here to support SVA students, faculty and staff who encounter concerning or disruptive behavior within the virtual classroom setting or on campus. If anyone at the College is concerned about a student's behavior, health or well-being, they should fill out the form at star.sva.edu. After receiving your report, the STAR Committee (comprised of senior staff members from Student Affairs) will provide you with guidance and support for handling challenging and sensitive student issues. For more information, click here.

STAR reports should be completed when there is a concern about a student's behavior, health or well-being. If the concern about a student is academic in nature, you may complete the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form (see Registrar's section below) on your MyID dashboard. While excessive absences, lack of participation, etc. can be indicative of larger issues, the student's academic advisor will respond to issues expressed on the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation and refer the situation to the STAR Committee if warranted.

The Students at Risk (STAR) Committee is offering a workshop for faculty and staff that affords attendees the opportunity to pose current challenges and seek direct guidance. The workshops will provide tips for self-care and address concerns while sharing some recent data that confirms what we already know: This is a tough time, but when we work together, we are better able to support our students.
Sign up here to join one of their upcoming worksops on Tuesday, October 27, 11:00 am ET, or Thursday, October 29, 3:30pm ET.

Reminders from the Registrar’s Office


Important Note on Daylight Savings Time

Please note that Daylight Savings Time (DST) ends on Sunday, November 1. There are several countries around the world that do not observe DST, so while we set our clocks back one hour theirs will not change. As a result, if you have a class that meets at 8:30 AM New York time, students studying from China are currently 12 hours ahead (8:30 PM). On November 2, their class will begin one hour later (9:30 PM). If you have students who are studying in a country that does not observe DST, please communicate the time difference or make appropriate accommodations for them before November 1.

Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form

Please note that the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form is now available right from your MyID dashboard. The form may be completed at any point in the semester if you feel that a student is underperforming academically. Once completed, the form is sent to the student, the student's advisor, and the Chair of the student's major department.

Please do not consider this form a penalty to your students. Rather, this is a tool to make clear to students that you have serious concerns about their academic performance and to give them actionable suggestions for what they will need to do in order to pass your class. Ideally, this notice provides students the impetus to get back on track before it is too late.

Attendance

Although classes will be conducted virtually this semester, students are still expected to attend all synchronous class sessions. Please continue to take attendance each week in Canvas as you normally would. If you need a refresher, please review this document.

For at least the Fall 2020 semester, we will not administratively withdraw students who are excessively absent due to illness. We will continue to monitor attendance and may withdraw students from courses if they accrue an excessive amount of absences not due to illness. While you may adopt an attendance policy that is more stringent than the institution’s policy, for the fall 2020 semester we suggest a certain amount of clemency.

If you have any students on your roster that have not attended any synchronous sessions, or are excessively absent without reason, please contact the Registrar's Office at rosters@sva.edu.

FERPA and Online Learning 

All faculty members should make sure they are familiar with the basic tenets of FERPA, a federal law designed to protect the privacy of student education records. The 2020-2021 SVA Handbook provides guidance for faculty on pages 49-50, particularly with regard to staying in compliance in a virtual environment. Please review this portion of the Handbook carefully.

It's important to note that any recordings of Zoom sessions, which may include the voice, image, or work of students, become part of the students’ education records and are considered protected under FERPA. Class session recordings should only be shared via Canvas in order to restrict access to those authorized to participate in the course session. Any requests to share class recordings with anyone not officially registered for the course should be directed to the Registrar’s Office.

OLT Online Office Hours and Common Online Challenges


The Office of Learning Technologies will continue offering online office hours throughout the fall semester to help answer faculty and student questions about online learning at SVA. Connect with OLT staff via Zoom, Monday through Friday from 10 a.m. to noon, and 2:00 to 4:00 p.m. Click on this link and bookmark it in your web browser for ongoing access to person-to-person assistance. Below are the two topics faculty have brought to online office hours most frequently since the start of the Fall semester.

Using Canvas Modules Effectively

The fall semester template in Canvas employs a course design using Modules to organize content and activities for each week of the course. If you chose to follow this organization by adding your course content to the appropriate weekly module, then your students will not need to directly access the Assignments, Discussion, or Files sections of your course (and will probably be happy to find everything in one spot).

However, if you would like to make other sections of your course (such as Assignments or Discussions) directly visible in the course navigation, go to Settings and then Navigation. Then, drag the appropriate sections to the top and click the Save button.

Sharing Zoom Recordings with Your Class

The default Zoom Cloud Recordings interface can be a little troublesome (plus those cloud recordings are deleted after a month). The great news is that anything you record to the Zoom Cloud will appear in your My Media in Canvas. The "My Media" section is private to you, but you can easily make specific videos available to students by:

1) Visiting the Media Gallery in your course (visible to all students)

2) Click + Add Media

3) Click the checkbox next to the appropriate video from your personal "My Media" and then click Publish at the top
In order to manage data storage limits, on Friday, October 16, 2020, all Zoom Cloud Recordings were deleted. Moving forward, new Zoom cloud recordings will be deleted every 15 days. For this reason, it is imperative that all faculty share their Zoom recordings using the instructions above. However, it is also better for your students' online learning experience for them to consistently access all SVA course recordings from their course Media Gallery in Canvas.

SVA Teachers on Teaching Online

Over the summer months, many SVA faculty were busy ramping up their technical skills and adapting their tried and true classroom practices to a very different online format. A few of these instructors have shared their tips for teaching online to help others navigate the process. Visit the Teachers on Teaching page in the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas to hear what they have shared. Also, if you have learned something valuable to share with other SVA faculty, please email oltvideo@sva.edu. There is so much room for innovation in online education and the SVA faculty is a powerful group of creative problem-solvers. So, please share your knowledge with others!

Faculty Knowledge Base Training and Live Workshops

Learning something new requires some degree of repetition and practice. If you feel uneasy about your online teaching skills, then please review the Transitioning to Online video in the Faculty Knowledge Base. This is also a terrific way to prepare for attending an upcoming live workshop. The video covers much of the same material as the live workshop. However, the foundation you gain from the video will enable you to make better use of the workshop time, as you will have more clear and concrete problems to resolve within your own course. To register for an upcoming Transitioning to Online workshop, visit OLT’s Events page.

LinkedIn Learning Options

A terrific way to supplement the hands-on assistance that students are accustomed to receiving in-person is to provide them with quality demonstration videos relevant to their coursework. Producing your own videos may still seem daunting, but the OLT staff has broken down some of the basics of enhancing your course with video and media in the Faculty Knowledge Base. Additionally, all SVA students, faculty, and staff have access to LinkedIn Learning via myid.sva.edu. Therefore, you can use appropriate videos from LinkedIn Learning to help enrich the custom content you might already provide for your students. Whether you are sharing your own videos, LinkedIn Learning videos, or links to other online resources, all of the content you add to your online course can be rolled over for use in future semesters.
