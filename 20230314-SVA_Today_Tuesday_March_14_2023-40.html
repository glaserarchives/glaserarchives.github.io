Tuesday, March 14, 2023

SVA Shows 2023

SVA has begun its rollout of year-end and thesis events and exhibitions. Keep tabs on the ever-expanding schedule at sva.edu/svashows.

EVENTS

11:00am | 2023 VASA Election Candidate Q&A

Meet this year’s Visual Arts Student Association candidates during a Q&A session where they will discuss their platforms and answer students’ questions. Today, March 14, 11:00am, via Zoom here. Polls open today at 9:00am. All current students are eligible to vote online until tomorrow, March 15, 4:30pm; vote here.

3:00pm | Ask Humanities and Sciences

It's time to register for the 2023 – 2024 academic year. Learn more about Humanities and Sciences courses and ask questions during the department’s open-hours event. Today, March 14, 3:00 – 4:00pm, 310 East 22nd Street, Room 818G or online via Zoom. You may also email Susan Kim at skim60@sva.edu to schedule an appointment.

3:00pm | Talks: Salman Toor

MFA Fine Arts presents a virtual artist talk with Salman Toor on his figurative paintings that depict intimate moments in the lives of fictional young, brown, queer men. Today, March 14, 3:00 – 5:00pm, online.

6:30pm | SVA Destinations Picturing Arizona Information Session

Art History faculty member Kayla Gibbons is hosting an information session on the Picturing Arizona SVA Destinations program—learn what national monuments, national parks, geological features and utopian architecture will be included in this 12-day epic road trip! See additional details about the program here. Today, March 14, 6:30pm, online via Zoom.

  • •“The Neighborhood of the Self,” through Friday, March 17. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Yuan Fang

“A Visual Vortex That Sucks the Viewer Into the Action”

Yuan Fang (MFA 2022 Fine Arts; BFA 2019 Visual & Critical Studies) is included in Phillips’s list of six contemporary women artists to know.

NYC PICK

“Annette Back: Layered Thoughts”

A solo exhibition of paintings by Annette Back (BFA 1992 Graphic Design) that draw from current events and personal experiences. Through Saturday, April 29. Mulberry Street Library, 10 Jersey Street.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tomorrow | MFA Visual Narrative Application Q&A

MFA Visual Narrative presents a virtual Q&A with Chair Nathan Fox (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) about the program and application process. Attendees will receive an $80 application fee waiver if they apply for summer 2023 admission. Register here. Wednesday, March 15, 7:00 – 8:00pm, online.

Next Week | Making the Cut at Pixar With Bobbie O’Steen

MFA Computer Arts presents a talk with Bobbie O’Steen on her book Making the Cut at Pixar: The Art of Editing Animation. Attend for an in-person look behind the scenes of Pixar and the art of film editing. Wednesday, March 22, 6:00pm. 133 West 21st Street, room 301C.

Book a New Museum Tour for Your Class

For the spring and summer 2023 semesters, SVA’s New Museum University Level membership allows select opportunities for free docent-led and self-guided tours. Faculty are encouraged to take advantage of these tours for their classes. Please contact Miranda Pierce, mpierce1@sva.edu, for details and scheduling.

WSVA Is Hiring

Have you ever wanted to be more involved with SVA’s radio station? WSVA is hiring staff for the 2023 – 2024 school year. The application closes Friday, March 24; apply here.

Student Workers Needed for Accepted Students Day 2023

Admissions is looking for student ambassadors for Accepted Students Day on Saturday, April 15, 8:30am – 4:30pm. Passionate, engaged students are needed to welcome hundreds of new students and their parents and showcase the best of SVA. Paid mandatory training included. Sign up here by Friday, March 24.
