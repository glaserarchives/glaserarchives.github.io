I. Just Published: Vital Signs

2016 MA Design Research Thesis Publication


We're excited to share Vital Signs, the online publication of the MA Design Research Class of 2016. Drawing from graduates’ multimedia thesis portfolios, these written and audio narratives reflect yearlong research quests into a broad array of design topics. The authors use maps, posters, experiences, cards, kits, props, renderings, sets, grids, and devices as starting points to consider the implications of context, agency, money, politics, technology, risk-taking, and rhetoric in analyzing critical issues in design today. 

The very name of this special collection of thesis excerpts and podcasts, Vital Signs, nods to the immediacy and relevance of these investigations as well as the thrilling and “messy vitality,”  in the words of architect and writer Robert Venturi, of the research and writing process.

Find Vital Signs live now on our homepage and tune in to our website for audio podcasts, video talks, and lots of insightful writing on topics ranging from the design of passports and instagramming in museums, to projections of NYC in the year 2027.

II. SVA Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive
Platform Project: Debate!

Watch online

The Platform Project: Debate was a two-week long assignment during the recent 2016 Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive that culminated in a live debate on the streets of New York plus the publication of the pamphlet, "What is the Future of the Skyline?"

Led by architecture writer and critic Mimi Zeiger and designer Neil Donnelly, the project explored how, as a design writer and thinker, to take a critical position around issues in the built environment.  The class considered “platform” both a means of production and a place to take a stand, using the public debate format and publication to document, research, and critique developments along 57th Street in Manhattan and to grapple with issues of private versus public space in the urban environment.

In addition to a close survey of 57th Street, the class visited the studios of Rafael Viñoly Architects and BIG Architects and also heard from architect and activist Quilian Riano of DSGN AGNC. Chelsi Bullard of SVA's MFA Social Documentary program captured the culminating event that took place in midtown at midday on June 15, when the class brought Platform to life. III. SVA Design Research + Van Alen Sessions!

Season 2, Episode 1: The Future of Solar Energy in Los Angeles

We are proud to announce a fellowship program with the Van Alen Institute, where SVA MA Design Research students collaborate as researchers and writers to help produce the Van Alen Sessions, a new short-documentary series in collaboration with CityLab/The Atlantic.

This spring Alex Klimoski and Jenni Young, 2016 graduates of Design Research, teamed up with the Van Alen on "Power Lines," the second season of Van Alen Sessions. Exploring the relationship between energy infrastructure and communities, each episode spotlights the outdated systems that currently put millions of city dwellers at risk and reveals ambitious ideas for tomorrow’s electricity networks.

Directed and produced by artist and journalist Kelly Loudenberg, with research conducted by Jenni and Alex, "Power Lines" broadcasts the voices of people behind oftentimes invisible energy infrastructure and offers an up-close examination of the cities, landscapes, and regions in three corners of the U.S. impacted by major shifts in the electric grid. Check just released Season 2, Episode 1: The Future of Solar Energy in Los Angeles.
 

IV. Alumni News

Congratulations to alums Aileen Kwun (MFA Design Criticism 2011) and Bryn Smith (MFA Design Criticism 2012) on the recent publication of Twenty Over Eighty: Conversations on a Lifetime in Architecture and Design with Princeton Architectural Press. The duo interviewed graphic design greats Seymour Chwast, Milton Glaser, Lora Lamm, and Deborah Sussman; architects Michael Graves, Denise Scott Brown, and Stanley Tigerman; urbanist Jane Thompson; industrial designer Charles Harrison; furniture designer Jens Risom; and critic (and emeritus SVA MA Design Research faculty member!) Ralph Caplan and many others, revealing essential insights and inspiring career reflections of some the last century's most luminous design minds.

On June 13, recent MA Design Research grad Derek Love took command of the formidable stage at the New School's Tishman Auditorium to present "Keeping up Appearances: How the Design of Storefronts Define Place and Value in NYC," an overview of his thesis research, for AIGA New York's 2016 Fresh Grad series. Well done, Derek!

Also in June, Jenni Young (MA Design Research 2016) spoke at the 2016 Travel & Tourism Research Association Conference held in Boulder, Colorado AND took home the prize for best graduate paper, based on her thesis "Non-Travel: Designing the Contemporary Travel Experience." Congratulations, Jenni!
