NYC PICKS

The 2022 New York LGBTQ+ Film Festival

A festival featuring over 130 new LGBTQ+ stories, special events, panels, conversations and more. The 2022 New York LGBTQ+ Film Festival will take place throughout Manhattan (including at the SVA Theatre) and Brooklyn. It will also be available online throughout the U.S. SVA students and staff can access $4 off tickets for all screenings (maximum two tickets per screening) with the code NFSVASTUTIX22. Through Tuesday, October 25. Visit newfest.org/festival for more information and tickets.

First Annual Indigenous Peoples of the Americas Parade

This year’s Indigenous Peoples of the Americas Parade will be the first of its kind in New York City, honoring all Indigenous peoples of North, Central and South America, as well as the Caribbean. Tomorrow, October 15, 12:00 – 3:00pm. South Broadway between 26th and 17th Streets.

Writing the Land: Poetry Reading and Book Signing

A collection of poetry readings that explore the relationships among the communities and lands of Prospect Park by Rachelle Parker, Michaeline Picaro and Opalanietet. Free; RSVP here. Sunday, October 16, 12:00 – 5:00pm. Prospect Park Boathouse and Audubon Center, 101 East Drive, Brooklyn.

Jazz in the Garden: Axel Tosca Trio

For the Brooklyn Museum’s Sculpture Garden performance series, Cuban jazz pianist Axel Tosca leads a trio joined by famed Cuban trova singer Xiomara Laugart. Free. Sunday, October 16, 2:00 – 4:00pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway.

Classical Interludes: Young Composers Festival: Persist

A performance by ETHEL, a genre-defying string quartet, and Allison Loggins-Hull, composer, flutist, producer and Daniel R. Lewis Composer Fellow to the Cleveland Orchestra. Free; RSVP here. Sunday, October 16, 4:00 – 6:00pm. Central Library, Dweck Center, 10 Grand Army Plaza, Brooklyn.

Sunday | MPS Directing’s Counterblow at the Chelsea Film Festival

Each year, MPS Directing faculty and students collaborate to produce a “learning film.” This year’s short film is based on an article by the late writer Pete Hamill (1954 Illustration). Counterblow tells the story of a sober, New York Chinese immigrant who weighs heavily the morally complicated possibility of avenging his teenaged son’s death at the hands of a classmate in a senseless fight over headphones. It will premiere at the Chelsea Film Festival on Sunday, October 16. Tickets for each film block are $17.18. Learn more and purchase tickets here.
