Subject: David Sandlin and Riccardo Vecchio
From: President SVA
Date: 5/2/23, 5:00 PM
To: allstudents@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu, allstaff@sva.edu

To the SVA Community, 


I would like to begin by thanking David Sandlin for an extraordinary year of service as Acting Chair of MFA Illustration as Visual Essay, and for working with the Department, and SVA, during a very difficult time to help ensure that the students in the program received the care and attention required to complete their studies and work. David has informed me that he will step down as Acting Chair before the beginning of the new academic year.  He has decided to continue his teaching at SVA, while he pursues his personal work.

 

I am pleased to announce that Riccardo Vecchio, an internationally recognized artist, lecturer and illustrator, and member of the SVA community for nearly three decades has been appointed Chair of the MFA Illustration as Visual Essay program effective July 24, 2023.

 

A Fulbright scholarship recipient, Riccardo was accepted into the MFA Illustration as Visual Essay program in 1994. Exceptional achievement earned him the Paula Rhodes Memorial Award upon graduation.

 

Mr. Vecchio joined the SVA faculty in 1997, teaching drawing at the undergraduate level and continuing education. In 2003 he became a thesis advisor in the MFA Illustration as Visual Essay program and has taught second-year painting in the program since 2021.

 

Mr. Vecchio’s painted works have been shown in numerous group and solo exhibitions, both here and in Europe. In 2022, as a recipient of an NYC Artist Corp Grant, Vecchio created a public project to bring attention to issues of climate and environmental injustice.  The launch was held at the Brooklyn Public Library, which also commissioned a permanent 42ft. mural featured in the Dweck Gallery of the Central Brooklyn Library at Grand Army Plaza.

 

His numerous awards include the LEAD Award with Sueddeutsche Zeitung, American Illustration, Communication Arts, Society of Illustrators, Art Director’s Club, New York Times Notable Op-Ed Art, and Print Magazine. His multifaceted work is routinely published in the The New Yorker, The New York Times, New York Magazine, La Feltrinelli Editore (Italy), Die Zeit (Germany), Rolling Stone, Harper's, National Geographic, The Smithsonian, Vanity Fair, Wired, and several Broadway and off-Broadway productions.

 

Mr. Vecchio has guest lectured or been a panelist at numerous museums and institutions including, MiMaster Istituto (Milan), NYU Institute of Fine Arts, The Brooklyn Museum, The Brooklyn Public Library, The Metropolitan Museum of Art, and The Society of Illustrators.

Riccardo is eager to ensure the program founded by Marshall Arisman will continue to provide a supportive and mentoring environment that will strengthen the presentation and personal artistic vision of students.

 

Please join me in welcoming Riccardo Vecchio as Chair of the MFA Illustration as Visual Essay program and thanking David Sandlin for his contributions to SVA and the program. 

David Rhodes
President
School of Visual Arts
