SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, November 12, 2015
The Sometimes Painful Search for a Fresh Idea in 60 Seconds [Video]
For anyone who has procrastinated or felt creatively blocked, alumnus Sarah Richardson's short film The Box perfectly captures the struggle of the artist's life. MPS Directing will host an information session today from 6:00 - 8:00pm at 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.
Last Chance: "Hard as a Rock"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that reinterprets Kay Rosen’s video Sisyphus (1991), which addresses the subject of futility through 72 misspellings of the name “Sisyphus.” Curated by curatorial fellow Lal Bahcecioglu. Closes today, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
12:30pm: Teaching Art at the College Level, Searching for a Job
Career Development presents a workshop for SVA graduate students and alumni with a master's degree about teaching art at the college level, including an overview of the academic job landscape, how it differs from other job markets and how to put together a CV. 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.

Keith Mayerson's American Dream
In connection with Keith Mayerson's new show at Marlborough Chelsea, Interview spoke with the SVA faculty member about his "idiosyncratic brand of pop figurative painting."
NYC picks of the day
Unorthodox: On Art History
Art historian Claire Bishop and SVA faculty member Joshua Decter discuss the role and function of art in post-avant-garde times. Tickets are $12 for students and seniors, $15 general admission. 6:30pm, The Jewish Museum, 1109 Fifth Avenue.
Linda Nochlin: The Art Historian as Seen by Artists
SVA faculty member Jovanna Stokic moderates a panel discussion about art historian Linda Nochlin. Admission is free. 7:00pm, New York University, La Maison Française, 16 Washington Mews.
Video Art & Experimental Film Festival
The fifth annual festival introduces audiences to the most conceptually and aesthetically arresting, daring and provocative video art and experimental films from around the world. Tickets are $8.25 for students, $15 general admission. Through Saturday, November 14, Tribeca Film Center, 375 Greenwich Avenue.
DOC NYC
The largest documentary film festival in America, DOC NYC celebrates new directions in storytelling. MFA Social Documentary Film will present a showcase of student short films on Friday, November 13, at IFC Center, 323 Sixth Avenue. Ticket prices and locations vary. Through Thursday, November 19.
Take Note
Last Call for Discounted Jets Tickets
Discounted tickets to the New York Jets-Buffalo Bills football game tonight at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey are available for $20 with SVA ID (limit two per person). Credit and debit card purchases only. Tickets can be purchased at the Student Engagement and Leadership Office from 9:00am - 5:00pm at the George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine.
Yoga and Art Therapy Workshop
MPS Art Therapy presents a yoga, meditation and art therapy group session. Experience the benefits of calming yoga and breathing techniques before engaging in creative exercises. Friday, November 13, 2:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.
Brooke Hodge: The Curator's Eye
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents curator and writer Brooke Hodge discussing the behind-the-scenes explorations, conversations and negotiations that go into developing exhibitions about architecture, design and fashion. Tuesday, November 17, 6:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Anomalisa
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a preview screening of writer-director Charlie Kaufman's Anomalisa, co-directed by Duke Johnson and starring Jennifer Jason Leigh, Tom Noonan and David Thewlis. Admission is free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Reservations are required. Wednesday, November 18, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre.
Critical Information Conference
MFA Art Writing presents an interdisciplinary conference examining the contemporary dialogue between art, media and society. Organized by second-year MFA Art Writing students, Critical Information provides a forum for discussions at the juncture of media, theory, criticism and the visual arts. Friday, November 20, noon - 6:00pm, 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
