🎙️ Get to Know: Christopher Gutierrez, Director, Resource Management and Commencement Committee Member

Tell me a little about your role with SVA and as a member of the Commencement Committee.

I work with the Purchasing Department, the Model Registry and Casting Departments, and Mail and Office Services. As service departments, we engage with staff, faculty and students to get them the resources they need to thrive at SVA. My role within the Commencement Committee, which includes folks from all different departments across the College, normally includes contracting the venue for the in-person ceremony; coordinating with IT, the Registrar and Academic Advisement on the procurement of things like diplomas, regalia, videographers, caterers and more; and coordinating the physical load in and out of Radio City Music Hall. From the larger picture things, down to details like cleaning the dais cap and gowns, the calligraphy on the diplomas, or the tissue paper going into shipments, it’s truly a collaborative effort to make students’ graduation as spectacular as possible.


How have the logistical aspects of commencement, like distributing diplomas, yearbooks and swag, changed amidst the pandemic, with students being spread out worldwide?

We wanted the 2020 class to have the best possible experience and to offer them as close to a traditional ceremony as we could. To support this, we moved from the tradition of handing out student regalia in-person to an online ordering system so they could enjoy their day just like if they were at Radio City Music Hall. After the ceremony concluded, we shipped diplomas and graduation gifts to students all over the globe.


As we approach our second virtual commencement ceremony, can you talk about the biggest challenges you’ve overcome since the 2020 commencement, which was our first virtual ceremony?

Last year, many students left New York City and were either in transitional spaces or had not settled into a permanent address, which created significant challenges with shipping items. Many students relied on their classmates to accept their swag, and it was a great thing to see students coming together to support each other in such a difficult time. We also dealt with shipping delays because the world slowed down and there were delays in customs clearances for international addresses. Shipping items worldwide has always been a part of what we do, but now we’re better prepared for the volume. The uncertainty of where students reside has diminished, too, making shipments a lot less challenging. Still, it’s no small task, but with the help of the Commencement Committee, we can pull it off again.


This is a difficult time for students grappling with the experiences they won’t get to have in the traditional sense. But is there a silver lining in having a virtual commencement?

It is unique and unusual and something these students will never forget. While it’s certainly an adjustment, with a virtual ceremony, last year’s graduates were able to invite as many family and friends as they wanted. For those families who maybe wouldn’t be able to make the trek to NYC, this is an excellent alternative to celebrating this milestone.

🍎 Health and Wellness

May Is Mental Health Awareness Month

Although some COVID-19 restrictions have been lifted, the mental-health effects of the ongoing pandemic remain a significant issue for many people, particularly those with existing conditions. The Mental Health Association offers a wealth of educational and supportive information ranging from general mental-health topics to COVID-specific resources. Additionally, the Anxiety and Depression Association has provided the following tips for coping with stress and anxiety:

  • Accept that you cannot control everything. Put stress in perspective: Is it really as bad as you think?
  • Do your best.
  • Maintain a positive attitude.
  • Learn what triggers your anxiety.
  • Limit alcohol and caffeine.
  • Eat well-balanced meals.
  • Get enough sleep.

Learn more at adaa.org.
