Studio Visit With Inka Essenhigh

Inka Essenhigh (MFA 1994 Fine Arts) talks about her studio practice in an interview with Artnet News on the occasion of her current solo exhibition at Miles McEnery Gallery, on view through Saturday, June 3.

EVENTS

4:00pm | EVOLVE

MFA Interaction Design presents its 2023 thesis festival, EVOLVE, a showcase of work from program graduates inspired by the act of changing ourselves and broadening our perspectives to persevere, grow and push boundaries through design. Today, May 18, 4:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

7:00pm | Daredoodles With Tom Motley

SVA Continuing Education presents another round of daredoodles with faculty member Tom Motley, featuring the primary “dare” exercises used to warm up for his upcoming online course, Comics Inventions. Tonight, May 18, 7:00 – 8:30pm, online.

SVA IN THE PRESS

11 Emerging Asian Women Directors in Film and TV

Chie Hayakawa (BFA 2001 Photography) is included in South China Morning Post’s round-up of significant emerging Asian women directors for her work on Ten Years Japan and her 2022 debut feature film, Plan 75.

NYC PICKS

MOCA Performs: An Evening of Letters, Stories and Narratives

In celebration of Asian American and Pacific Islander Heritage Month, the Museum of Chinese in America presents a night of letter reading and storytelling that illustrates the history of Chinese Americans. Free. Tonight, May 18, 7:00 – 8:30pm. Museum of Chinese in America, 215 Centre Street.

Brooklyn Talks: Legacies of the Great Migration With Akea Brionne, Leslie Hewitt and Robert Pruitt

A conversation with artists Akea Brionne, Leslie Hewitt and Robert Pruitt, who are featured in the current exhibition, “A Movement in Every Direction: Legacies of the Great Migration,” and exhibition curator Kimberli Gant. Tickets: $16. Tonight, May 18, 7:00 – 9:00pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tomorrow | Second Annual Apple Box Short Film Festival

MPS Directing presents its thesis film festival. This year, the festival will be broken into two two-hour screening blocks of eight films, with each screening block followed by a Q&A, a screening of the department’s “learning film,” Goodbye Jonathan’s Soul, and an awards presentation. Friday, May 19, 2:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
