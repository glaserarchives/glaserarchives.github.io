All Faculty Brief | February 2021


Greetings:

I hope this edition of the All Faculty Brief finds you well, and your spring semester off to a good start.  Today marks the start of Black History Month (BHM).  Be sure to check SVA Today to learn about BHM programming for the SVA community.

As we close in on a year of life in the middle of a once-in-a-century global pandemic, there is a glimmer of hope that the end may be near:  a new presidential administration that has made controlling the coronavirus its top priority, and several vaccines with a high efficacy rate that we hope will make its way into most American arms by June.  It is our plan to resume in-person instruction for the fall 2021 semester.  Of course, a lot must happen between now and late summer for us to realize this goal.  Stay tuned.

In the meantime, we must continue to focus on providing our students with the best possible art and design and general education, under these extremely challenging circumstances.  To that end, the Office of Learning Technologies (OLT) continues to make available to faculty a range of support services and resources. The SVA Library continues to enhance its digital resources, including remote access to The Milton Glaser Design Study Center and Archives.  

Our new Director of Diversity, Equity and Inclusion, Dr. Jarvis Watson, has been busy since his arrival meeting with academic department chairs and administrative offices, and he has begun to lay out his vision for the future of this vitally important office.  Dr. Watson is eager to meet and engage with the SVA faculty.  Please read on to learn more about Dr. Watson’s agenda.

Finally, our Registrar provides some important information and updates about student attendance, submitting unsatisfactory academic progress reports, and the basics of FERPA in online teaching.

I am, as always, grateful for your continued support of and commitment to our students.  I look forward to updating you on the College’s plans for conducting classes this summer and fall.  Thank you!

Please stay safe and healthy.


All best wishes,

Chris

Dr. Watson will fulfill these goals through following initiatives:

Support and Online Course Requirements from the Office of Learning Technologies


Fully online courses require faculty to repurpose in-person course curriculum for online delivery and to post all course assignments and resources in Canvas. Given that faculty are teaching online out of necessity during a pandemic, the College has tried to be as flexible and supportive as possible as we all face varying learning curves when it comes to using web-based tools. However, we still must recognize that the quality of online courses provided will impact our students’ overall perception of SVA.

Consistently organizing course content using weekly modules in Canvas will not affect the personal approaches each of our talented instructors brings to the classroom. It will, however, make it easier for students to successfully navigate online learning at SVA. Therefore, the template provided in Canvas should be used as a guide for all online courses. Also, to serve as a guide for all spring faculty, below are the minimum requirements for online courses that will be outlined in future teaching contracts for online courses at SVA. As we are in the third week of this semester, your course modules for the second and third weeks should also be published now.

By the first day of class:

     • The course is published so that students can see it in Canvas;

     • The instructor has posted their course syllabus;

     • The instructor has posted a welcome video or, at minimum, a welcome                       message or Announcement.

     • Course Zoom meetings are created and visible on the Zoom Meetings tab.

     • The module for the first week of class is published for students to view and                  includes:

             -  content for students to review asynchronously before the course’s first live                 (Zoom) synchronous session, or "synch session"

             -  asynchronous course work for students to complete throughout the week,                  e.g. Assignments and/or discussions with clear instructions.

Spring Semester 2021 Policy Reminders and Updates from the Registrar’s Office


Faculty Abscence Form

Due to new sick leave policies, all faculty must inform SVA when they are absent from a course they are teaching, even if a make-up session will be scheduled at a later time. To streamline this process, faculty members should complete the Faculty Absence Form when they are absent. You will need to be logged in to your MyID account to complete the form.  

Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form

Please note that the Academic Unsatisfactory Progress Evaluation Form is now available right from your MyID dashboard. The form may be completed at any point in the semester if you feel that a student is underperforming academically. Once completed, the form is sent to the student, the student's advisor, and the Chair of the student's major department.

Please do not consider this form a penalty to your students. Rather, this is a tool to make clear to students that you have serious concerns about their academic performance and to give them actionable suggestions for what they will need to do in order to pass your class. Ideally, this notice provides students the impetus to get back on track before it is too late.

FERPA and Online Learning

All faculty members should make sure they are familiar with the basic tenets of FERPA, a federal law designed to protect the privacy of student education records. The 2020-2021 SVA Handbook provides guidance for faculty on pages 49-50, particularly with regard to staying in compliance in a virtual environment. Please review this portion of the Handbook carefully.

It's important to note that any recordings of Zoom sessions, which may include the voice, image, or work of students, become part of the students’ education records and are considered protected under FERPA. Class session recordings should only be shared via Canvas in order to restrict access to those authorized to participate in the course session. Any requests to share class recordings with anyone not officially registered for the course should be directed to the Registrar’s Office.

Attendence

Although classes will be conducted virtually this semester, students are still expected to attend all synchronous class sessions. Please continue to take attendance each week in Canvas as you normally would. If you need a refresher, please review this document.

For at least the Spring 2021 semester, we will not administratively withdraw students who are excessively absent due to illness. We will continue to monitor attendance and may withdraw students from courses if they accrue an excessive amount of absences not due to illness. While you may adopt an attendance policy that is more stringent than the institution’s policy, for the spring 2021 semester we suggest a certain amount of clemency.

If you have any students on your roster that have not attended any synchronous sessions, or is excessively absent without reason, please contact the Registrar's Office at rosters@sva.edu.
