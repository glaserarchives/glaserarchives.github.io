SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, February 9, 2016

Design Decisions 2016
With primary season upon us, SVA Close Up took a look at what a few faculty members at the College have been saying about the visual identities of the 2016 campaigns. Not registered to vote? Click here for New York State voter registration information and deadlines.
6:00pm: Jill Magid
MFA Fine Arts presents artist and writer Jill Magid discussing her performance-based practice, which explores the emotional, philosophical and legal tensions between the individual and "protective" institutions. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
7:00pm: Brian Finke
MPS Digital Photography presents documentary and editorial photographer Brian Finke (BFA 1998 Photography) discussing his work, which has appeared in Men's Journal, National Geographic, The New Yorker and The Washington Post. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
7:00pm: Seasons of Loss: Racism and Resistance in the Cultural Field
MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on racism, as a subject and influence, and the general trend of broader social critique in the arts. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Press pick of the day
If You Smell Something, Smell Something Else
Untapped Cities highlights BFA Design alumnus Angela Kim's guerrilla scratch and sniff posters displayed in subway stations.
NYC picks of the day
Bob Bowen
The SVA faculty member gives a presentation on the history of the city's skyline in 3D hyper-stereoscopic depth. Admission is free. RSVP to programs@skyscraper.org. 6:30pm, The Skyscraper Museum, 39 Battery Place.
Heart of Hearts
The Times Square Alliance presents Collective-LOK's Valentine Heart Design-winning public art installation of a ring of 12 golden, mirrored hearts. Through Sunday, March 6, Times Square.
Take Note
VASA Election Applications and Information Sessions
Interested in student government? VASA is seeking candidates for president, vice president, treasurer and student senate chair. Candidates must apply together as a group of four. Applications are located at the Student Engagement and Leadership Office, George Washington Residence, 23 Lexington Avenue, mezzanine. Applications are due Wednesday, March 23. Information sessions will take place on Tuesday, February 23, 3:00pm, and Wednesday, February 24, 10:00am and 6:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. For more information, contact activities@sva.edu.
Let's Talk: Curating Your Life in the Arts | Alumni Panel Discussion
SVA Alumni Affairs presents a panel of six alumni at varying stages of their careers discussing how their paths as illustrators, fine artists, photographers and filmmakers have evolved since graduating from SVA. Panelists include Scott Bakal (BFA 1993 Illustration), Jenny Morgan (MFA 2008 Fine Arts), Kate Neckel (MFA 2002 Computer Art), Vincent Peone (BFA 2007 Film and Video), Annie Watt (BFA 1974 Media Arts) and Heidi Zito (MFA 2012 Photography, Video and Related Media). Tuesday, February 23, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
