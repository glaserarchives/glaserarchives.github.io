TAKE NOTE

Chris Hackett Exhibition and Talk

The Visible Futures Lab presents artist-in-residence Chris Hackett, who will talk about the resources found in the debris of our society, using examples from his time at SVA. Refreshments will be served. Tomorrow, January 20, 7:00pm. Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

The Measured Summit: Measuring the Impact of Social Design on Human Health

MFA Design for Social Innovation presents a participatory, daylong designed conversation about how to evaluate and improve the impact that social design has on human health. Ticket prices vary; purchase here. Tuesday, January 24, 8:00am – 6:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Workshop for Bystander Empowerment

SVA's Student Health and Counseling Services and Title IX Office and the Domestic & Other Violence Emergencies (DOVE) program from New York Presbyterian Hospital present "See Something, Say Whaaat?", a workshop for staff and faculty to develop their skills and understanding of how to intervene in cases of violence and assault. Light refreshments will be served. RSVP to Laurel Christy at lchristy@sva.edu. Friday, January 27, 9:30 – 11:30am. 23rd Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, media room.

Call for Submissions: 2017 HOPE Art Competition and Exhibition

Student Health and Counseling Services invites SVA students to submit a piece for the HOPE Competition and Exhibition that expresses their identity, aspects of identity and/or the intersectionality of various identities. Submission forms are available at sva.edu/health or can be picked up at Student Health and Counseling Services, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. Deadline is Monday, February 6, 4:00pm. Finalists’ work will be on view Thursday, February 16 – Saturday, February 18 at the SVA Flatiron Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street. First, second and third place winners will be announced at a reception on Friday, February 17, 5:30 – 6:30pm, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
