Dear Friend of MACP,
December has arrived! Here’s the latest news of faculty and collaborators with the MA Curatorial Practice program.
Faculty member Mark Beasley has been appointed the Robert and Arlene Kogod Secretarial Scholar, Curator of Media and Performance Art at the Hirshhorn Museum and Sculpture Garden in Washington, D.C. We’re thrilled for Mark and look forward to his presence in the program in the years ahead.
Faculty member Joshua Decter, who teaches our Practicum on curatorial enterprise, is part of a panel discussion on December 2 (tomorrow) at the Steinhardt School of NYU, entitled “The Expansion of Exhibition Formats and its Consequences.”
Gabi Ngcobo, with whom we’re collaborating on a panel discussion in our “Local Address” series on March 28, has been named director of the tenth Berlin Biennale for Contemporary Art, taking place in the summer of 2018. (Only two more years of Trump to go after that!)
By the way, the next “Local Address” session happens here on December 15, entitled “Remapping the Landscape: Culture, Nature, Politics and the Place of Change." Special host is José Roca, director of Flora, a unique art center and school in Bogotá, Colombia, with panelists Ana Sokoloff and Gabriela Rangel.
Our second-year students are now in the midst of collaborating with the Zurich University of the Arts’s post-graduate program in curating, creating a special issue of the program’s prestigious journal, OnCurating.
Dexter Wimberly, the newly appointed executive director of the Aljira Center for Contemporary Art, will be our guest this coming week in the Curatorial Roundtable.
And we are now accepting applications for the September 2016 incoming class! See more about the program here. Apply here.
 
More soon,
Steven Henry Madoff
Chair, MA Curatorial Practice
