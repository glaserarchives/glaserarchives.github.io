Monday, April 10, 2023

“Hear Me Now”

Adebunmi Gbadebo (BFA 2017 Fine Arts) contributed two pieces to “Hear Me Now: The Black Potters of Old Edgefield, South Carolina,” a traveling exhibition of ceramics made by African American potters in the 19th century shown alongside contemporary Black artists. Read more in an article from the spring/summer 2023 Visual Arts Journal.

EVENTS

6:30pm | Art in a Democracy: Selected Plays of Roadside Theater

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the SVA Honors Program present artist and community organizer Ben Fink to discuss his new two-volume series of Appalachian and intercultural grassroots plays, Art in a Democracy: Selected Plays of Roadside Theater. Tonight, April 10, 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Sarah Palmer for The New York Times

Faculty member Sarah Palmer (MFA 2008 Photography, Video and Related Media) captures the women-centric themes of Yellowjackets in her cover artwork for a New York Times article on the hit TV show.

NYC PICK

“A Blooming Resilience”

An exhibition exploring flowers as a symbolic subject for artists to convey social ideals, cultural values, personal emotions and visions, featuring work by Michael De Feo (BFA 1995 Graphic Design). Through Saturday, June 3. Waterfall Gallery, 170 East 80th Street.

Correction: Q&A With Rich Hickey from Moonbug Entertainment Tomorrow

BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects presents a Q&A with Rich Hickey, global chief creative officer for Moonbug Entertainment, on Moonbug's digital first strategy and what that means when identifying who and where audiences are and how to bridge a gap between traditional content and digital first content, as well as what it is like to be a CG artist at Moonbug. Tuesday, April 11, 2:00pm. 133 West 21st Street, 10th floor, room 301C.
