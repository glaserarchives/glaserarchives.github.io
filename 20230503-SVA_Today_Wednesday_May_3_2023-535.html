May Events and Exhibitions

As the end of the 2022 – 2023 academic year approaches, May’s calendar is filled with exhibitions, thesis presentations and screenings presenting students’ year-end and thesis work.

EVENTS

5:30pm | Print Slam 15

A print sale and exhibition of Riso-printed works by students and printers of the RisoLAB. Free and open to the public. RSVP required; register here. Tonight, May 3, 5:30 – 8:30pm. RisoLAB, 136 West 21st Street, 11th floor.

6:30pm | The Body Speaks: From Stage to Page

A talk with acclaimed illustrator, former SVA faculty member and 2012 SVA Masters Series honoree James McMullan on his work, including his new book, Hello World: The Body Speaks in the Drawing of Men. Faculty member and 2019 Masters Series honoree Steve Brodner will introduce McMullan. Copies of Hello World will be on hand for purchase and signing (a discount is available for students). Free; RSVP here. Tonight, May 3, 6:30 – 8:30pm. 209 East 23 Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

Last Chance | “Intimate Documents”

A show by graduating BFA Visual & Critical Studies students curated by faculty member Suzanne Joelson. Closes today, May 3. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

NYC PICK

Steven Heller, Illustrator and Art Director: From Mobster Times to The New York Times

Steven Guarnaccia, illustrator and professor emeritus of the Parsons School of Design, talks with MFA Design Co-Chair Steven Heller about his recent coming-of-age memoir, Growing Up Underground, a chronicle of Heller’s journey as a cartoonist and illustrator at the center of New York’s youth culture in the ’60s and ‘70s. Student admission: $7. Tonight, May 3, 6:30 – 8:30pm. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.
