SVA MA Design Research, Writing & Criticism
Open House/Information Session

Tuesday, November 15, 2016
6:00–9:00 p.m.
136 West 21st Street, 2nd Floor, New York


A reminder that you're warmly invited to join us for the 2016 Open House of the MA Design Research, Writing & Criticism department at the School of Visual Arts! Prospective students are particularly encouraged to attend.

At this event, you'll have the opportunity to: tour our New York studio; hear presentations by program faculty, alumni, and students; explore the department; and, over drinks and refreshments, ask questions and discuss details of all the engaging courses, lectures, and placement opportunities we offer through the program.

6:00 p.m. Doors open, department tours.  
6:30 p.m. Presentations by program chair, faculty, alumni, and current MA students.
7:30 p.m. Q&A, drinks and refreshments.

Please RSVP if you plan to attend, and you're welcome to share this with friends and colleagues who also may be interested.

Our pioneering one-year program offers a journalistic approach to analyzing and interpreting design through writing and other media, with a faculty of expert practitioners and guest critics who are luminaries in their field. The program is particularly well suited to mid-career design or media professionals who wish to deepen research practice, sharpen their writing skills, and enrich their understanding of design in all its forms. The intensive course is also geared toward graduates with bachelor's degrees who are interested in strengthening their critical thinking and writing skills or developing an area of research expertise. Please find more information below and on our website, designresearch.sva.edu.

We are now accepting applications for Fall 2017 and are pleased to be able to offer scholarships to successful applicants.

The application deadline for priority consideration is January 17, 2017, with applications accepted on a rolling basis thereafter, as spaces are available. Click here to begin the application process.

If you would like to schedule an individual appointment to visit the department or discuss SVA MA Design Research, please contact the department directly: designresearch@sva.edu

 

Fall 2016 Lecture Series

Daniel Perlin 
Artist, Designer, and Director of UX at Droga5 
"Listen Closely: Listening as a Strategy for Design"
Tuesday, November 22, 6:30 p.m.


What is listening? How do we listen? Why? What does it mean when AI listens? When a drone listens? Do you listen to the voices in your head? How can we listen better in order to design? Just a few of the questions that Daniel Perlin will discuss in his interactive lecture, "Listening Closely." 

Daniel likes designing experiences, and he believes that the best way to practice good design is by listening closely to users. He got his start in design making things with sound such as music, film, objects, and sometimes spaces. After a few years spent in Rio de Janeiro, he made his way back to New York, where he attended NYU’s Interactive Telecommunications Program (ITP) and the Whitney Independent Study program. Following some time spent between art and interactivity, he found himself back in the open arms of experiential design and UX.

Daniel has had the privilege of designing with a diverse group of people, places, and things. Recent work has been with clients such as Google, IBM, Under Armour, Vito Acconci, Maya Lin, Errol Morris, Todd Solondz, Chase, Verizon the Cooper Hewitt Museum and Domus Magazine. Before joining Droga5, Daniel was a director of UX at Rosetta (a Publicis agency) and Local Projects (an exhibition and experiential design studio), both in New York City.
