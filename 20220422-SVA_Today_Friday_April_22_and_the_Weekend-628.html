“My Art Portfolio” Spotlights the Evolution of SVA Students
As part of the “Life@SVA” video series, “My Art Portfolio” showcases individual students and alumni as they share the progression of their work from the start of their time at SVA to their senior year. Watch the videos here.
Events

1:00pm | Earth Day Walkout
Join the Indigenous Student Union for an Earth Day school walkout. Students are invited to walk out of class and attend a series of intergenerational Indigenous teach-ins featuring climate activists from Abya Yala (Mexico, Central and South America). Learn about the climate crisis, intergenerational organizing, United Nations Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues (UNPFII) and how to stay connected and involved within your communities. The teach-in will be held at 209 East 23rd Street in the 3rd floor amphitheater. Open to SVA students, faculty, and alumni only. Visitors are asked to bring their Eventbrite registration with them to the event. Everyone must remain masked, except where otherwise noted. RSVP here. Space is limited. Today, April 22, 1:00pm. Those who cannot attend in-person, or who are outside the SVA community, are welcome to join the conversation via Zoom. Meeting ID: 819 8917 3695.

4:00pm | Give and Take with SVA

As part of Earth Week, VASA invites the SVA community to join them for Give and Take with SVA, an opportunity to declutter and drop off any unused art materials at the SVA Student Center. Exchange or give away your art supplies to peers from the hours of 4:00 – 7:00pm. In addition to this event, free bamboo cutlery will also be available at the SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Last Chance | Touch
BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an installation by faculty member Connie Beckley exploring the idea that sound is felt not only by the ears but by the whole body. Through Sunday, April 24. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Explore cherry blossom season and all the spring blooms at Brooklyn Botanic Garden with music and dance performances, chances to talk with BBG gardeners, craft activities for kids and families and Garden tours. Tomorrow, April 23. Student tickets: $12. Brooklyn Botanic Garden, 990 Washington Avenue, Brooklyn.

Queens Green Day: Dirtfest 2022
Celebrate Earth Day at Socrates Sculpture Park with a day of hands-on spring activities, including planting and composting workshops and tastings by the Connected Chef. Free. Tomorrow, April 23, 12:00 – 5:00pm, 32-01 Vernon Boulevard, Long Island City.
