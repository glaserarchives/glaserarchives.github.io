June Events and Exhibitions

With the end of the academic year comes a summer full of exhibitions and events, including the continuation of thesis presentations, virtual lectures and a last chance to see “Milton Glaser: POP.”

May’s Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios last month include a concept for a museum about loss and grief, surreal dream illustrations and a branding concept for the now-defunct 1960s Judson Dance Theater.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Pay or Die Acquired by MTV

MTV Documentary Films acquired the rights to Pay or Die—Scott Ruderman’s (MFA 2016 Social Documentary Film) new film with consulting editor and MFA Social Documentary Film faculty member Toby Shimin—and is planning a robust awards campaign for the film before it launches on Paramount+. Via Deadline.

Creating Narratives From Fragments

John MacConnell (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay) talks about fragmentation, exploring human form and queer art in an interview for Natural Pursuits on the occasion of his latest exhibition, “Fragments.”

“If You Don’t See Yourself Represented, Go Out and Represent Yourself”

Murray Hill (MFA 1997 Photography, Video and Related Media) talks about his favorite queer media, his work on HBO’s Somebody Somewhere and paving the way for more queer folks in show business for Queerty.

Celebrating Inclusivity and Fostering Understanding Through Art

Maya Khalil (BFA 2021 Design) is profiled in Hudson Weekly for her multidisciplinary practice, which draws upon the power of cultural fusion and 3D expression to reshape perspectives.

NYC PICKS

Photoville 2023

An annual festival of projects across the spectrum of visual storytelling installed in shipping containers and photo cubes in Brooklyn Bridge Park, including “Tributaries,” an exhibition of work by three members of the SVA Continuing Education community. Read more about SVA’s involvement in Photoville 2023 here. Through Sunday, June 18. Various times and locations; learn more here.

Tribeca Film Festival

Tribeca Productions’ annual film festival returns, showcasing a diverse selection of film, episodic, talks, music, games, art and immersive programming taking place all over the city, including the SVA Theatre. Through Sunday, June 18. Locations, prices and times vary; see the schedule here.

The Stroll Screening

A chronicle of New York City’s Meatpacking district by the transgender women of color who created its history, co-directed by Zachary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography). Free. Monday, June 5, 5:00 – 7:00pm. Gansevoort Plaza, Ninth Avenue and Gansevoort Street

Queer to Tell: A Pride Month Storytelling Series

A monthly storytelling series taking place weekly during Pride Month at different LGBTQIA+ owned businesses. This week’s theme is “Tales of the City”: stories about love, loss, success and peace that all take place in New York City. Tickets $15. Friday, June 9; doors at 7:00pm, show at 8:00pm. Til Death, 603 Hart Street, Brooklyn.

Ube Festival

In celebration of Philippines Independence Day, Philippines Fest partners with Smorgasburg for a ube-themed festival featuring savory food, sweets, drinks, merchandise and more! Free entry. Saturday, June 10, 10:00am – 6:00pm. Smorgasburg, 90 Kent Avenue, Brooklyn.

Join SVA at the NYC Pride March

SVA Academic Affairs and Diversity, Equity and Inclusion are excited to announce that the provost has again secured a place for students, faculty, alumni and staff to walk in the 2023 NYC Pride March! Together, let us protest and demonstrate against the recent spread of anti-LGBTQIA+ and anti-trans legislation currently moving through U.S. state houses.


This year’s NYC Pride March theme is “Strength in Solidarity.” SVA Alumni Affairs is donating custom-printed crayon boxes to hand out as we march to show our support for queer and trans youth. Let’s raise our voices, rise up and thrive!


The 2023 NYC Pride March takes place on Sunday, June 25. Click here or on the headline to sign up to march with us.
