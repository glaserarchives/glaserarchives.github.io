Please join us on Monday, May 8 for SIGHTLINES, an afternoon symposium where graduating students will present final thesis projects and discuss future research directions. Following the presentations, help celebrate the launch of the SIGHTLINES publication, the collected writings of the Class of 2017, at a party with music by DJ DK!

The capsule presentations draw from the multi-media portfolios the MA students have developed over their course of study. After each set of presentations, students will engage in brief conversations led by designer and consultant Ida Benedetto, design writer Anne Quito, and writer-editor Avinash Rajagopal. Award-winning author and Design Research faculty member Akiko Busch will deliver the closing keynote address. 

An event and publication, SIGHTLINES collects and communicates different points of view on design, celebrating the revelations and debate generated when those views intersect, clash, or are simply juxtaposed. Sightlines also refers to breadth of design research topics in the Class of 2017, who critically considered cars, craft, display, fashion, food packaging, games, safety, signage, and OpenAPS internal organs. While students’ investigations propelled them along diverse paths of inquiry, they all have one thing in common: a deep interest in the systems at work and how their particular topic sits in a larger cultural context.

You’re invited to attend for the full afternoon or any one of the one-hour sessions. After 5:00 p.m., join us upstairs for the launch of the SIGHTLINES publication, with drinks and refreshments! RSVP here.

Akiko Busch

Akiko Busch is the on the faculty of the Design Research MA program at the School of Visual Arts and author of Geography of Home, The Uncommon Life of Common Objects, and Nine Ways to Cross a River. The Incidental Steward, her collection of essays about citizen science and stewardship, was published by Yale University Press in 2013 and awarded an Honorable Mention in the Natural History Literature category of 2013 National Outdoor Book Awards. She was a contributing editor at Metropolis magazine for twenty years, and her essays have appeared in numerous national magazines, newspapers, and exhibition catalogues. In addition to SVA, she has taught at the University of Hartford and Bennington College, and her work has been recognized by grants from the Furthermore Foundation, NYFA, and Civitella Ranieri. She is working on a new book about invisibility.

Avinash Rajagopal

Avinash Rajagopal is senior editor at Metropolis magazine and cofounder of the editorial consultancy Superscript. Hacking Design, his master's thesis for the MFA Design Criticism program at the School of Visual Arts was published by the Cooper-Hewitt, National Design Museum in 2013. Rajagopal was the co-editor of the catalog for Adhocracy, an exhibition at the inaugural Istanbul Design Biennial in 2012, and contributed to the catalog of Making Africa, a 2015 exhibition at the Vitra Design Museum. With Superscript, he has organized events at the Museum of Art and Design in New York and at the 2014 Venice Architecture Biennale. Rajagopal has lectured on design history and criticism at the National Institute of Design, India, the School of Visual Arts, New York, and the University of Texas at Austin.

Anne Quito

Anne Quito covers design and architecture for Quartz. She holds a master's degree in Visual Culture from Georgetown University and an MFA in Design Criticism from the School of Visual Arts. Her MFA thesis on the nation branding of the world’s newest country, South Sudan, was featured on NPR. Anne has contributed to numerous publications including Works that Work, AIGA Design Eye, and Core 77. An experienced art director, she is also the founding director of Design Lab, a design practice within an international development organization. Anne was recently awarded the inaugural AIGA Steven Heller Prize for Cultural Commentary.

Ida Benedetto

Ida C. Benedetto is an experience designer and strategist working in real world adventures, game design, and documentary media. She gained notoriety creating trespass adventures through her previous design practice, Sextantworks, with coverage in the New York Times, the New Yorker, Businessweek, NPR and Fast Company. Her recent research into the design of transformative social experiences, called Patterns of Transformation: Designing Sex, Death, and Survival in the 21st Century, has been deemed groundbreaking by leading ethnographers, VR designers, and theater directors. Ida holds an MA in Design Research from the School of Visual Arts, a BFA in Design and Technology from Parsons School of Design, and a BA in History from The New School. She is now a senior designer at SYPartners in New York City.
