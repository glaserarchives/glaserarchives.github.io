News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Tuesday, November 14, 2017

A MythBusting Alumnus

Jon Lung (MFA 2016 Products of Design), co-host of the Science Channel's reboot of MythBusters, talks about his TV experience so far. The show premieres tomorrow, November 15, 9:00pm.
EVENTS

10:00am | SVA Library Fall Book Sale

SVA Library presents its fall book sale, featuring a wide range of books from across collections, including fine art and design. All books will be free – $5; debit/credit only. Open to SVA students, faculty, staff and alumni. Through Thursday, November 16, 10:00am – 6:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

2:30pm | Dog Therapy

SVA Student Health and Counseling Services presents dog therapy with New York Therapy Animals. Open to all SVA students. 2:30 – 3:30pm. MFA Fine Arts flex space, 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.

6:30pm | Mei Lum of the Wing on Wo Project

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by Mei Lum of Wing on Wo & Co, her family’s 92-year-old porcelain-ware business in Chinatown, which was on the brink of sale in early 2016. Since then, Lum began the WOW Project to reclaim ownership over Chinatown’s future. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:30pm | Mimi Zeiger, "Intersect, Instigate, Exchange"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by critic, editor and curator Mimi Zeiger, who will investigate how collaboration, structure, research and critique shape exhibition making. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Clay Patrick McBride

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer and filmmaker Clay Patrick McBride whose portraits of top athletes and musicians have appeared in numerous magazines. This talk is part of the i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

7:00pm | Copyright Fundamentals for Art Information Professionals

SVA Library presents Greg Cram, the associate director of copyright and information policy at the New York Public Library, who will present tools and strategies for addressing the intellectual property, privacy and contract rights associated with works in museum and library collections. 7:00 – 8:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
PRESS PICK

Scher Shares Her Wisdom

Surface talks with renowned graphic designer and BFA Design faculty member Paula Scher about her work for New York City institutions and the importance of mistakes and intuition.
NYC PICKS

IndieWorks November Screening

Catya Plate (1988 Fine Arts) screens her award-winning short film, Meeting MacGuffin: An Animated Ecological Thriller (2017), as part of this monthly film festival dedicated to building the filmmaking community in New York. Q&A with filmmakers to follow. Free. Doors open at 6:30pm; screening starts at 7:30pm. IndieWorks at The Local, 13-02 44th Avenue, Queens.

George Booth: A Cartoonist's Life

The first exhibition dedicated to the work of alumnus George Booth (1951 Cartooning). Free. Open through Saturday, December 30. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.
TAKE NOTE

SVA in Los Angeles Information Session

An information session about the spring break trip to Los Angeles, where students will visit film and animation studios and interact with leading film and animation artists. Thursday, November 16, 2:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
Every November, during National Novel Writing Month, 30 professional designers volunteer to create book cover art inspired by novels by aspiring authors. Called 30 Covers, 30 Days, the project is presented in partnership with MPS Branding Chair Debbie Millman. Image: Joseph Schwartz (BFA 1988 Media Arts) for 30 Covers, 30 Days.
