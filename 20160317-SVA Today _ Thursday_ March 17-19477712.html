SVA Today
Thursday, March 17, 2016
The SVA in LA crew outside Disney Animation Studios.
SVA Takes LA
SVA Close Up recaps recent College activities in Los Angeles, including the SVA in LA spring break trip and an Alumni Affairs reception with Wyatt Mills (BFA 2013 Fine Arts).
Opening: "The Never Ending Project 1.0"
BFA Interior Design presents an exhibition about the department's participation in last year's Never Ending Project, a week-long design challenge that brought together three teams from three different continents. Reception Thursday, March 24, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Monday, April 4, VCS Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
6:30pm: The Back of the Painted Beyond
MFA Art Writing presents art historian Michael Ann Holly discussing the invisible realm beyond the vanishing point of backgrounds in early modern Northern paintings. Aertsen, Breughel, Elsheimer, Patinir and van Eyck, among others, will be addressed. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
6:30pm: Learning Through Performance: An Evening with Ernesto Pujol
MFA Art Practice presents site-specific performance artist, social choreographer and faculty member Ernesto Pujol discussing his work. 335 West 16th Street, ground-floor media room.
6:30pm: New York Fashion Film Festival
MPS Fashion Photography presents this annual screening of fashion films. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
7:30pm: The Will Eisner Influence
The SVA Library and the National Cartoonists Society present a panel discussion on the influence of pioneering comics artist and longtime SVA faculty member Will Eisner. The event is part of Will Eisner Week, a worldwide celebration of comics, graphic novels and the legacy of the comics visionary. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
A Pitch for Kindness
The New York Times on a demonstration led by BFA Design faculty members Timothy Goodman (BFA 2007 Graphic Design) and Jessica Walsh, as an extension of their 12 Kinds of Kindness campaign.
NYC PICKS OF THE DAY
St. Patrick's Day Parade
The annual parade begins at 11:00am and runs along Fifth Avenue from 79th Street to 44th Street.
Gary Stephan and Alina Tenser
An exhibition of new work by MFA Fine Arts faculty member Stephan and Tenser (BFA 2003 Fine Arts). Reception tonight 6:00 - 8:00pm. Through Saturday, April 23, Susan Inglett Gallery, 522 West 24th Street.
TAKE NOTE
Luigi Ballerini
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and the SVA Library present Italian poet, translator and scholar Luigi Ballerini reading from his new epic poem Cephalonia (Rail Editions, 2016). Sunday, March 20, 5:00pm, SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue.
BFA Advertising and BFA Design Portfolio
BFA Advertising and BFA Design present an exhibition of student work, featuring ads, campaigns, designs, motion graphics and 3D designs. Reception Tuesday, March 22, 5:00 - 8:00pm. Tuesday, March 22 - Friday, April 1, Art Directors Club, 106 West 29th Street.
Strong Voices in Action
The Student Engagement and Leadership Office presents a discussion on leadership, advocacy and social justice, featuring music, dance, poetry, and more. If you are interested in performing, contact Oliver Colbert, ocolbert@sva.edu. Friday, March 25, 6:30pm, SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
naked romance and then some
Humanities and Sciences presents a multimedia performance by faculty member and poet Davida Singer, who will be accompanied by musicians and perform poetry from her collection naked romance and then some. Friday, March 25, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
