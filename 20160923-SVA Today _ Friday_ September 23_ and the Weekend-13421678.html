Friday, September 23, and the Weekend
FEATURE

SVA Guide to Gramercy

Continuing with the SVA Guide to NYC neighborhood series, here are a few suggested things to do, see and taste in Gramercy. Catch up on the other guides to Chelsea and Flatiron.
EVENTS

8:00am: Free Coffee at Moe's Café

Both locations of Moe's Café are offering free coffee this morning to all SVA students, faculty and staff who bring their own mug. 8:00 - 10:00am, 209 East 23rd Street, 2nd floor, and 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

10:00am: Critical Engagement: Art and Community

MPS Art Therapy presents its 32nd annual conference, with a keynote speech by multidisciplinary artist Pablo Helguera, workshops and a panel discussion. Participating speakers include storyteller and creative director Melissa Malzkuhn (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative), artist and Elastic City founder and director Todd Shalom and sensory experience artist Miriam Simun. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff. 10:00am - 4:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

2:30pm: Dog Therapy

Student Health and Counseling Services and New York Therapy Animals present a special visit from therapy dogs to help students de-stress in between work and classes. Open to SVA students. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY

SVA Alumnus Wins Genius Grant

Lauren Redniss (MFA 2000 Illustration as Visual Essay) was named a 2016 MacArthur Foundation Fellow, which is commonly referred to as a "genius grant."
NYC WEEKEND PICKS

Legacy Stone

A viewing of the Trump tombstone that made headlines in March, created by Brian Whiteley (MFA 2013 Fine Arts). Tonight, 6:00 - 10:00pm, Christopher Stout Gallery, 119 Ingraham Street, Brooklyn.

Free Museum Day

Museums all over New York, including The Morgan Library & Museum, Jewish Museum and Asia Society and Museum, offer free admission in a national initiative sponsored by Smithsonian. Check list for more participants. Saturday, September 24.

Dumpling Festival

The 13th annual festival includes an eating contest and dumpling making lessons. Tasting tickets are $20. Saturday, September 24, noon - 5:00pm, Sara D. Roosevelt Park, Houston Street between Chrystie Street and Forsyth Street.

Lower East Side Pickle Day

Celebrate all things pickled at this annual street festival with games, local food vendors and live music. Sunday, September 25, noon - 5:00pm, Orchard Street between Houston Street and Delancey Street
TAKE NOTE

Under Pressure: Institutional Practice and China

MA Curatorial Practice presents a panel discussion on the question of artistic national representation in the current global order, in which China is more connected and influential than ever. Panelists include Pi Li, Philip Tinari and Xiaoyu Weng. Thursday, September 29, 7:30pm, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

The Magnificent Seven

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of The Magnificent Seven, starring Denzel Washington, Ethan Hawke and Chris Pratt. Admission is free with SVA ID (two guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Friday, September 30, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Style Frames Design Conference Discount

Style Frames presents The Art of the Pitch design conference. Learn how top talent in design, animation, VFX, interactive and VR pitch their stories. SVA students, faculty and staff can use the code "SVA" for a 20% discount on tickets. Monday, November 7, and Tuesday, November 8, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
