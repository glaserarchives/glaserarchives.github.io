SVA Today
Monday, March 19, 2018

Illustrating Gloria's Voice

A Q&A with Aura Lewis (MFA 2017 Illustration as Visual Essay), the writer and illustrator of Gloria's Voice, a recently released children's book about the life of activist Gloria Steinem.
EVENT

3:30pm | 2018 VASA Candidates Meet and Greet

Meet this year's Visual Arts Student Association candidates as they discuss their platforms and answer students' questions. The event will be live streamed on VASA's Facebook page. 3:30pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

Undergraduate students can vote online at votevasa.sva.edu beginning tomorrow, March 20, 9:00am.

6:30pm | Presence: A Poetry Reading with Ian E. Manuel

BFA Design presents a reading and Q&A with poet Ian E. Manuel as well as presentations of Manuel-inspired work by faculty member Olga Mezhibovskaya's students. 6:30 – 8:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

New Tech for Young Students

Verizon on MFA Interaction Design students Amy Ashida, Christine Lawton and Amsha Kalra, who are creating technology to aid elementary school teachers and to generate young students' interest in STEM topics.
NYC PICKS

Unreality Bomb

A survey of painting and sculpture that concerns itself with the combination of abstract and figurative expression. Through Sunday, April 15. FiveMyles, 558 St. John's Place.

Peter Hujar: Speed of Life

An exhibition of photographs by artist Peter Hujar, ranging from his beginnings in the mid-1950s to his central role in the East Village art scene in the 1980s. Tickets $13 – $20; purchase here. Through Sunday, May 20. The Morgan Library and Museum, 225 Madison Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

John Yau and Wayne Koestenbaum: A Reading

SVA Library presents readings by prominent writers, poets and art critics John Yau and Wayne Koestenbaum. MFA Art Writing and MA Critical Theory and the Arts students Sahar Khraibani, Mike Cavuto and Irene Villaseñor will also read their own work. Tomorrow, March 20, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

Art + Feminism Wikipedia Edit-a-Thon

SVA Library presents an Art + Feminism Wikipedia edit-a-thon, which aims to teach people how to edit articles on Wikipedia to affirm that its content is meaningful. Saturday, March 24, 12:30 – 5:00pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

Pacific Rim Uprising

SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of Pacific Rim Uprising, featuring VFX work created by SVA alumni. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Sunday, March 25, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Seeking Photos of Tim Rollins

MFA Art Practice is gathering photos of the late SVA alumnus and faculty member Tim Rollins for a visual archive of his life and work, and to draw from for his memorial on April 30, at the SVA Theatre. Upload photos as .jpeg files to this Dropbox and include a text file with your photo file name, names of people pictured, location and date, as well as your name and email. Images sized at 1920x1080 pixels (or close) are preferred. Email artpractice@sva.edu with questions.
