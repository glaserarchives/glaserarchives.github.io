SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
May 1 - 7, 2017

Stephen Doyle's Subway Series Debut [Video]

A Q&A with MFA Design faculty member Stephen Doyle about the SVA posters he designed for display in New York City's subway stations, which involve his signature "labyrinthine constructions of text and image."

April Hot Five

The five most-discussed projects from SVA Portfolios this month features mesmerizing motion graphics, colorful character designs, a funny, food-based twist on flower crowns and more.

Dash Shaw on the Beauty of Comics [Video]

Comic book writer/artist and animator Dash Shaw (BFA 2005 Illustration) talks about how he started drawing, the unique beauty of comics and his experiences at SVA.

Where Physical and Digital Memory Meet

A Q&A with MFA Photography, Video and Related Media student Alex Hovet, who won a 2017 Alumni Scholarship Award for her project "External Memory Devices," a multimedia installation that examines the new scientific process of encoding a person’s digital files within DNA.

Art Show in a Dungeon

A Q&A with MA Curatorial Practice student Ikechukwu Casmir Onyeruenyi about his thesis project "Sexual Fragments Absent," which featured the work of three artists who untangle the aesthetic, affective and socio-political trappings of BDSM using performance, video and self-portraiture.
IN THE PRESS

Edel Rodriguez Goes Viral

Arsty on the covers that BFA Illustration faculty member Edel Rodriguez created for the German magazine Der Spiegel.

"Unsung NYC," a VR Project

The New York Times on a new virtual reality project by MFA Interaction Design student David Al-Ibrahim that takes participants into an immersive soundscape of 1600s Manhattan.

An Engaging Young Writer

AIGA awards Anne Quito (MFA 2014 Design Criticism) the first Steven Heller Prize for Cultural Commentary, which "spotlights a young writer who amplifies the tradition Steven’s prolific voice and writing have inspired."
EVENTS

Major Year-End Thesis Exhibitions and Presentations

This week features thesis exhibitions, senior shows, open studios and thesis presentations from a variety of SVA's undergraduate and graduate programs. For a full list of this week's exhibitions and presentations, click here.

Dusty Film & Animation Festival

BFA Film and BFA Animation present the 28th Dusty Film & Animation Festival, a three-day screening of student films. Film screenings take place Friday, May 5, through Sunday, May 7. Animation screenings take place Sunday, May 7. The awards ceremony will take place Monday, May 8, 6:30pm, and will stream live at dusty.sva.edu. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
