September Hot Five

The top-five most-discussed projects from SVA Portfolios this month include award-winning graphic design work, a promo illustration for an imaginary airline and retro-inspired horror comic panels.
EVENTS

6:30pm | Mark Hansen

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by Mark Hansen, whose ability to craft stories through algorithm, computation and visualization led him to Columbia’s School of Journalism, where he teaches data and computational journalism. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Jenna Pirog

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by Jenna Pirog, the virtual reality editor at The New York Times Magazine, as part of the i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

7:00pm | On Assignment

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents three program alumni—photographers Ryan Koopmans and Ryan Pfluger and Jacqueline Bates, award-winning director of photography for The California Sunday Magazine—who will discuss their work and decision-making process. A Q&A will follow. RSVP here. 7:00 – 9:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

Victoria and Abdul Screening

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of Victoria and Abdul, starring Judi Dench and Ali Fazal. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
PRESS PICK

A New Kind of Design Education

Design Boom interviews MFA Products of Design Chair Allan Chochinov about his unique program.
NYC PICK

Michael Chabon with Emma Straub

Michael Chabon will discuss his novel, National Book Critics Circle Award finalist Moonglow, with author Emma Straub. Free. RSVP here. 7:30pm. Books Are Magic, 225 Smith Street, Brooklyn.
TAKE NOTE

Yoga and Meditation for Creativity and Wellness

Student Health and Counseling Services presents this workshop on basic yoga and meditation skills to promote well-being and stress reduction. Participants will receive free passes to Laughing Lotus Yoga Studio. Open to SVA students only. RSVP to Navah Steiner at nsteiner@sva.edu. Thursday, September 28, 12:30 – 2:00pm. Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Kingsman: The Golden Circle

The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of Kingsman: The Golden Circle, starring Taron Egerton, Colin Firth and Julianne Moore. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). Friday, September 29, 8:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

The 2017 AICP Show

In collaboration with SVA, The Association of Independent Commercial Producers (AICP) presents a free screening of the 2017 AICP Show, which offers insight into commercial filmmaking and the power of advertising. Work in the show is included in the archives of the film department at The Museum of Modern Art and is assembled by a curatorial panel of prominent industry leaders. A Q&A with representatives from Final Cut, Union Editorial and COPILOT Music + Sound will follow. Free. Tuesday, October 3, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
