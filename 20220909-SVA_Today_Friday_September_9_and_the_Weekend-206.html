EVENTS

10:00am | Yoga for Artists: Preparing Body and Mind for New Beginnings

Start the semester right with a free, beginner-friendly virtual yoga class. Explore ways to prepare your body and mind for the fall semester with SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and instructor Melissa Boo. All you need is a flat surface and one to three soft squares (pillows, blankets or towels). Open to the entire SVA community. RSVP required; register here. Friday, September 9, 10:00 – 10:45am, online.

6:30pm | Lynsey Addario in Conversation with Kathy Ryan

On the occasion of “The Masters Series: Lynsey Addario,” SVA hosts a conversation between acclaimed photojournalist Lynsey Addario and Kathy Ryan, director of photography at The New York Times Magazine. Tonight, September 9, 6:30 – 8:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Sunday | Medieval Screening

SVA Theatre presents a screening of Medieval, starring Ben Foster. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Visit svatheatre.com for SVA Theatre's current COVID-19 guidelines, including information on masks and vaccination requirements. Sunday, September 11, 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “The Masters Series: Lynsey Addario”

An exhibition of work by Lynsey Addario, acclaimed photojournalist, MacArthur Genius Grant and Pulitzer Prize recipient, as part of the 32nd annual Masters Series Award and Exhibition. Curated by Maya Benton and Perri Hofmann. Through Saturday, October 29. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Ongoing | “The Health Poster Project”

An exhibition of posters by 24 students from BFA Illustration, MFA Illustration as Visual Essay and the 2022 Summer Illustration Residency, curated by BFA Comics and BFA Illustration Chair Viktor Koen (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay). Through Monday, September 19. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “MPS Fashion Photography Class of 2022”

MPS Fashion Photography presents an exhibition of works by the class of 2022. Through Monday, September 19. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “Dwelling in Motion”

A juried exhibition of works that analyze the constantly growing relationship between habitat and inhabitant. Through Monday, September 19. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Ongoing | “A Sticker History Exhibition”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents “A Sticker History Exhibition: A Snapshot from 100 Years of Design Told Through Adhesive Materials,” an exhibition of stickers by various cultural creators from diverse backgrounds and eras curated by DB Burkeman and Stuck-Up. Through Thursday, September 29. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

NYC PICKS

SPRING/BREAK Art Show

The annual curator-driven art fair returns, featuring curation and work by many SVA alumni. Tickets: $36.01. Through September 12, 11:00am – 8:00pm. Atlantic Production Center, 625 Madison Avenue.

“A Maze Zanine, Amaze Zaning, A-Mezzaning, Meza-9”

A group exhibition of work that explores the dynamics between performance and painting, featuring work by Katherine Bernhardt (MFA 2000 Fine Arts), Marilyn Minter (faculty, MFA Fine Arts), John H. Sandroni (BFA 2017 Fine Arts) and Ryan B. Sullivan (MFA 2007 Fine Arts). Curators include Ei Arakawa (BFA 2004 Fine Arts). Opening reception: Tonight, September 9, 6:00 – 9:00pm. Through Thursday, October 15. David Zwirner, 519 West 19th Street.

The Films of Beth B and Scott B

As part of faculty member Beth B’s (BFA 1976 Fine Arts) retrospective at the Museum of Modern Art, the museum will be screening the films of Beth B and Scott B. $8 for students. Through Sunday, September 11; learn more about screening times here. Museum of Modern Art, 11 West 53 Street.

Raphael Montañez Ortiz: Panel Discussion and Performance

A day-long celebration including a panel discussion about the impact of artist, educator and founder of El Museo Raphael Montañez Ortiz, culminating with a special participatory performance piece by Ortiz. Free. September 10, 3:00 – 6:00pm. El Museo del Barrio, 1230 Fifth Avenue.

Jaguar Parade

An open-air exhibition of decorated jaguar statues dedicated to raising funds and awareness about the conservation of jaguars and their habitats. Through Wednesday, October 5. Locations vary across New York City; see the map here.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Due Today | Fall 2022 Club Application

Register your own club with SVA Student Engagement and Leadership and VASA today. Clubs are a great way to be involved on campus and engage with people who share your interests. Check the application for requirements and email clubs@sva.edu with any questions. Application is due today, September 9.

2022 Halloween Poster Contest

Excited about the VASA Halloween Party this year? Submit a poster design for a chance to win a free ticket! The winner’s design will be used as the official marketing for the 2022 VASA Halloween Party. View requirements and submit a design here. Questions? DM VASA on Instagram @vasa.sva or email them at vasa@sva.edu. Submissions are due Friday, September 16.

SVA Cartoon Allies and MFA Visual Narrative at SPX

SVA Cartoon Allies and MFA Visual Narrative are partnering on a table at the Small Press Expo in Bethesda, Maryland, Saturday, September 17 – Sunday, September 18. Small Press Expo is the premier event for indie comics, cartooning and graphic novels. Drop by Table G2 to meet SVA students and browse and purchase student and alumni comics!

Art Therapy In Person or Over Zoom

MPS Art Therapy is offering free art therapy services to the SVA community. Helpful with stress, school and or personal issues. These confidential services are no-fee and facilitated by a second-year graduate student using art. Email Val Sereno at vsereno@sva.edu for more information.
