SVA Today
Thursday, February 8, 2018

Explore the World with SVA Destinations

SVA Destinations offers programs around the world that span a range of artistic disciplines and can help stimulate your creativity this spring.
EVENTS

12:30pm | RA Selection 2018 Information Sessions

Resident assistant selection information sessions are happening this week. Anyone interested in applying to be an RA for the 2018 – 2019 academic year or summer 2018 must attend an information session to receive application materials. Email raselection@sva.edu for more information. Today, 12:30pm, and tomorrow, February 9, 10:00am. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

6:00pm | "1987: Drawings by John Ruggeri" Reception

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents this exhibition of drawings by John Ruggeri (1973 Illustration, BFA 1984 Media Arts, MFA 1986 Illustration as Visual Essay) that document the devastating early years of the AIDS crisis in New York City. 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Wednesday, February 28. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

6:30pm | SVA in Cambridge, England Information Session

Join SVA Destinations for an information session to learn about the course Writing Visual Culture in Cambridge, England, taking place July 22 – August 5. Worth 3 Humanities and Sciences credits. 6:30 – 7:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 605C.

HOPE Competition Exhibition

SVA Health and Counseling Services presents an exhibition of work by BFA Animation, BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects and BFA Design students, that celebrates The Samaritans organization and their message of reaching out to those in need of a helping hand. Through Wednesday, February 28. SVA Flatiron Gallery Windows, 133/141 West 21st Street.
PRESS PICK

"Colorful and Tactful" Style

Print selects designer Colin Webber (BFA 2014 Design) as one of its 15 best new visual artists under 30.
NYC PICK

Cloud Chamber with Dan Ziskie

Actor and photographer Dan Ziskie presents his monograph of New York street photos, Cloud Chamber. Free. 6:00pm. Rizzoli Bookstore, 1133 Broadway.
TAKE NOTE

Lunar New Year Flyer Competition

SVA's International Student Office welcomes submissions from all students for a Lunar New Year flyer. The theme should be Lunar New Year or dog-related. Winner will receive $100 prize. Contact Akihiro Okayama at aokayama@sva.edu for more details. Deadline: tomorrow, February 9.

"Eco-Femmes: Next Nature" Reception

CP Projects Space presents this exhibition, curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Jesse Bandler Firestone, which brings together queer eco-feminist artists, "eco-femmes," to posit a scenario in which nature and humans are entwined in exchanges that are emotional, physical and, at times, perverse. Tomorrow, 7:00 – 9:00pm. Through Wednesday, February 21. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.

Faculty and Staff Participating in SXSW

SVA Communication asks that all faculty and staff members who will be participating in screenings or events at the South by Southwest Conference and Festival this March send their information, including title, date, time and location, to news@sva.edu.
