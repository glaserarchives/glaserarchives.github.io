Subject: SVA Supports our Black Community
From: SVA Info
Date: 7/7/20, 1:00 PM
To: allstaff@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu, allstudents@sva.edu
CC: David Rhodes <drhodes@sva.edu>

The following message was posted on SVA’s Instagram feed on Wednesday, July 1. To read President David Rhodes’ June 2 statement regarding the civic response to the murder of George Floyd, click here.


Racism has no place at SVA or in the world. We are actively listening and working toward a future where our current and former Black students, as well as faculty and staff, feel supported in the classroom and in our community. SVA accepts that we have work to do when engaging the Black community and creating an environment of equity and inclusiveness. This work includes first acknowledging that we don’t have all of the answers but as a community, we are open to having the difficult conversations to help change the landscape for today and tomorrow. Currently, there are initiatives at work, both large and small, that will address the many concerns put forth by the Black community. However, in order to truly affect long-term change, some initiatives will take time, and others will be immediate. We ask for your patience.

 

SVA is committed to change, and we have begun the work to effectively shift the ways we hire and train faculty and staff, respond to concerns brought forth by community members, recruit prospective students and build a culture free of hatred, ignorance and intolerance. These efforts are being put into effect by a Diversity, Equity and Inclusion task force that has been in place for more than a year. We look forward to providing more information in the coming weeks about the work of the DEI task force, and to continuing this important conversation.
