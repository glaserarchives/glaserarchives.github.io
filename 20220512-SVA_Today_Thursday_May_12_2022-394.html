Events

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 10:00am. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).

7:00pm | Daredoodles with Tom Motley
BFA Cartooning instructor Tom Motley presents a series of drawing exercises derived from his classes Cartooning Basics, Figure Drawing for Cartoonists, Thinking in Ink and Short Form Comics. Today, 7:00pm, online via Zoom; register here.

7:00pm | MFA Computer Arts 2022 Thesis Screenings
MFA Computer Arts presents a two-day thesis screening event, featuring short talks and videos of graduating students’ work. Today and Friday, May 13, 7:00 – 9:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street, and streaming live on YouTube.
SVA IN THE PRESS

Manship Medallion Awardees
BFA Design and BFA Advertising Chair Gail Anderson (BFA 1984 Media Arts), SVA Masters Series honoree George Lois, BFA Design faculty member and Masters Series honoree Paula Scher, SVA Board of Directors member Eileen Hedy Schultz, Rich Tu (MFA 2009 Illustration as Visual Essay) and BFA Advertising and BFA Design Chair Emeritus Richard Wilde are among this year’s recipients of the prestigious Manship Medallion, which will be given out at the ADC 101st Annual Awards ceremony this month in New York City.

#SVAwesome
Behind-the-scenes photo from Still Searching: Chicago’s Missing Black Women and Girls, a full-length documentary directed by Latoya Flowers (MFA 2012 Social Documentary Film) following Chicago hip-hop artist and muralist Damon Lamar Reed, who has been creating a portrait series of missing Black women and girls in the Chicagoland area for the past two decades. Flowers and Reed will be presenting a talk about the project on Thursday, May 26, 7:30pm ET, via TEDxQuincy.
