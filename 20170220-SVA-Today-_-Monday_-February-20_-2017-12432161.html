PRESS PICK

A Graphic Novel Comes to Life

Deadline on how Oscar-nominated director Marjane Satrapi will direct a film based on Radioactive: Marie & Pierre Curie: A Tale of Love and Fallout, a graphic novel by Lauren Redniss (MFA 2000 Illustration as Visual Essay).
NYC PICKS

Film Comment Selects

A screenings series of new and unseen titles chosen by Film Comment magazine. Tickets $9 – $14; purchase here. Through Thursday, February 23; schedule here. Film Society Lincoln Center, Elinor Bunin MonroeFilm Center, 144 West 65th Street, Francesca Beale Theater.

"Paris Refashioned, 1957 – 1968"

An exhibition that examines impact of French haute couture and popular culture on this period in fashion history. Free. Through Saturday, April 15. The Museum at the Fashion Institute of Technology, 227 West 27th Street.
TAKE NOTE

Photographing Flowers for Transparency

MPS Digital Photography presents digital artist and professional photographer Harold Davis, who will show how he enhances his floral imagery. Tomorrow, February 21, 4:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Jasmine Wahi

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by faculty member Jasmine Wahi, who curates international exhibitions dealing with issues of cultural identity, intersectionality and female empowerment. Tomorrow, February 21, 6:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Thomas Roma

MPS Digital Photography presents a talk by photographer Thomas Roma as part of its i3: Images, Ideas, Inspiration lecture series. Tomorrow, February 21, 7:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

Students At Risk Webinar

SVA’s Student Affairs recently developed a webinar explaining details about the Students At Risk (STAR) team and guidelines for reporting students of concern. This recording is available for faculty and staff on their MySVA home page. Faculty can also access the video in the Faculty Knowledge Base in Canvas.
