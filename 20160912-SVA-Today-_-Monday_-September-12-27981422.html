FEATURES

Overcoming Creative Fear [Video]

Wyatt Mills (BFA 2013 Fine Arts) talks about overcoming creative fear, how New York inspired him, measuring success, and more.
EVENTS

6:00pm: "n e w f l e s h" Artists' Panel

BFA Photography and Video and DEAR DAVE, magazine present a panel of artists discussing their work in the exhibition. A conversation between photographer Ellen Carey and department chair Stephen Frailey will take place Saturday, September 17, 4:00pm. Rubber Factory, 29 Ludlow Street. "n e w f l e s h" runs through Friday, September 23.

7:00pm: Kevin Amato in Conversation with Alix Browne

BFA Photography and Video, DEAR DAVE, magazine and Phaidon present Kevin Amato (BFA 2003 Photography) discussing his recently published monograph, The Importants, with Alix Browne, features director at W. A book signing and reception will follow. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Get to Know Lauren Hom

Brit + Co. interviews the BFA Advertising alumnus, whose design studio, Hom Sweet Hom, has done work for clients such as Starbucks and Google.

Gabrielle Bell in Conversation with Cyril Pedrosa

A conversation between the American graphic novelist and French graphic novelist, moderated by MFA Visual Narrative faculty member Bill Kartalopoulus. 7:00pm, Albertine Books, 972 Fifth Avenue.
TAKE NOTE

Untitled (Gender and Representation) 2016

SVA and BFA Photography and Video present a day long symposium that seeks to address the complex issues of gender that have dominated cultural conversations in the last several years, and the ways in which representation has significantly participated in the discourse. The conference will include panels on queer histories and photography, gender outlaws, fashion and gender, transfeminism, and gender and performance. Panelists include Kate Bornstein, Zackary Drucker (BFA 2005 Photography), Grace Dunham, Ethan James Green, Ron Gregg, M. Lamar, Zanele Muholi, and others to be announced. Free for SVA students, $40 general admission. Saturday, October 1, 9:30am - 7:00pm.
IMAGE OF THE DAY
A 19th-century engraving with ink and watercolor additions by BFA Illustration faculty member Ruth Martens. From Fountain & Alligators, a recently published book to accompany an exhibition of her work in Cologne, Germany. A selection of work from the book was recently featured in The New Yorker.
