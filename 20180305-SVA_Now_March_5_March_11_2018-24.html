SVA Now | March 5 – 11, 2018
News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Reading Recommendations

The students of BFA Visual & Critical Studies share their current reading lists.

The New Creators 2018

Presenting the lineup for SVA's Talent Shows, the annual series of exhibitions, screenings and other events featuring work by SVA’s graduating students.

February's Hot Five
The top five most-discussed projects from SVA Portfolios this month include a set of delicate branding designs, spectacular drawings and cartoony illustrations for Men's Health.

The Impact of March

A Q&A with Nate Powell (BFA 2000 Cartooning), about March, the trilogy of graphic novels that he illustrated about U.S. Congressman John Lewis' civil rights work, in conjunction with an exhibition of original art from the project at Society of Illustrators, on view through June 30.
IN THE PRESS

Glaser's "Good Work"

Creative Boom interviews SVA's Acting Chairman of the Board Milton Glaser about his most iconic work, his art-making process and changes in the design industry.

The Importance of a "She Shed"

Fox News interviews HGTV host and interior designer Genevieve Gorder (BFA 1998 Graphic Design) about the "she shed," which she describes as a "man cave, but for women."

Brush and Ink on Paper

AI-AP interviews illustrator Zach Meyer (MFA 2017 Illustration as Visual Essay) about his process and inspirations.
EVENTS

Mentors

BFA Photography and Video presents "Mentors," an exhibition of work by the department’s students inspired by their working relationships with leading members of the New York arts community. Saturday, March 10 – Tuesday, March 27. Reception: Thursday, March 15, 6:00pm. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

One of a Kind: The Dresses of Madison Avenue

An exhibition of fashion ensembles repurposing everyday objects, created by Kevin O'Callaghan's 3D Design students. Through Friday, March 16. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

The Craftswomen

MFA Fine Arts presents this exhibition, celebrating the enduring traditions and the exciting future possibilities of craft as an art form that is treated with the respect it deserves. Curated by current student Sarah Malekzadeh. Through Thursday, March 29. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

Consumed

SVA Galleries presents an exhibition of works by students, selected by a jury of their peers, that explores consumerism, biodegradation, consciousness and gradual decline. Saturday, March 10 – Saturday, April 7. Reception: Tuesday, March 20, 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
