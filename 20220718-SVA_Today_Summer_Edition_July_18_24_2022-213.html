What do I need to know about monkeypox?

Since May, there has been a small monkeypox (orthopoxvirus) outbreak in Europe and the U.S. As of July 11, 223 people in New York City have tested positive for the orthopoxvirus. Most of these individuals have not been hospitalized and have recovered on their own. Symptoms like rashes and sores can be painful and last from two to four weeks.

Anyone can get and spread monkeypox. If you have a new or unexpected rash or sores, please contact a healthcare provider. If you start experiencing flu-like symptoms, please isolate yourself from others immediately and contact your healthcare provider.

Learn what steps you can take to reduce the chance of getting and spreading monkeypox and please reach out to health@sva.edu for a nursing appointment with me to discuss any questions or concerns you may have about this ongoing outbreak, which SVA is monitoring closely.
