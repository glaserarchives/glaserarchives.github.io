1:00pm: SVA Yearbook Photoshoot

Portraits sessions for the class of 2017. 1:00 - 5:00pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 309. A second photoshoot will take place Thursday, September 22, 10:00am - 2:00pm, at the same location.

6:30pm: Mobilizing to Defeat Climate Change

The New Republic presents three of the nation’s leading environmental activists—writer and organizer Bill McKibben; Reverend Lennox Yearwood Jr., environmental campaigner and president of Hip Hop Caucus; and Elizabeth Yeampierre, environmental justice attorney and executive director of UPROSE—discussing the social, political and biological impacts of climate change. Admission is free. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

The Backlash Election: Campaign '16 in Pictures

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents Michael Shaw, publisher of Reading the Pictures, discussing the images that have defined the 2016 presidential election campaign. Thursday, September 22, 6:30pm, 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

Apply for the International Student Office's Cultural Partner Program

The International Student Office is accepting applications from undergraduates for the Cultural Partner Program, which fosters intercultural awareness and friendship through an exchange of ideas and experiences. The application deadline is Friday, September 23. Click here to apply, or contact international student advisers Yoko Anderson and Angelique Cordero: cpp@sva.edu.

Critical Engagement: Art and Community

MPS Art Therapy presents its 32nd annual conference, with a keynote speech by multidisciplinary artist Pablo Helguera, workshops and a panel discussion. Participating speakers include storyteller and creative director Melissa Malzkuhn (MFA 2015 Visual Narrative), artist and Elastic City founder and director Todd Shalom and sensory experience artist Miriam Simun. Free for SVA students, faculty and staff. Friday, September 23, 10:00am - 4:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
