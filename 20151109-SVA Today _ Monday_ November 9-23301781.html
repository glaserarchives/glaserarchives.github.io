SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Monday, November 9, 2015
Bob Mankoff and The New Yorker Cartoons
Tonight at 7:00pm at the SVA Theatre, BFA Cartooning and BFA Illustration present a screening of the new HBO documentary, Very Semi-Serious: A Partially Thorough Portrait of New Yorker Cartoonists, with The New Yorker cartoonist and editor Bob Mankoff discussing his career. As host of the event, faculty member Keith Meyerson spoke to SVA Close Up about Mankoff's work and the cartoons published in The New Yorker.
6:00pm: Spotlight
SVA Filmmakers Dialogue presents a screening of Spotlight, starring Mark Ruffalo, Michael Keaton and Rachel McAdams. Screenwriter Josh Singer will answer questions following the screening. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
6:30pm: Rosemary Balsam
Yale University psychoanalyst and psychiatrist Rosemary Balsam discusses the history and impact of distorted views on the female-sexed body and traditionally female roles like motherhood. Presented by BFA Visual & Critical Studies. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
9:00pm: Spectre
The Visual Effects Society presents a screening of the new James Bond film, starring Daniel Craig, Lea Seydoux, Monica Bellucci and Christoph Waltz. Admission is free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

In-House Perspectives
InSource presents three discussions on the unique challenges faced by in-house creative teams and professionals. Speakers include Ilana Greenberg, creative director, Graphic Design USA Magazine; David Lesué, creative director, Workfront; and Martin Schott, director, creative services, Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts. Free for SVA students with code "InSourceSVAFree"; $55 general admission tickets. Wednesday, November 18, 6:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Axure Meetup
MFA Interaction Design student David Mahmarian is starting a local chapter of the Axure Meetup, which is group dedicated to the interactive prototyping tool that allows users to create prototypes without coding, with varying levels of fidelity. The output can be something very basic that works in a browser, or a fully functional, more complex prototype that passes variables and uses conditional logic. No prior knowledge of coding is required to join the group. The first group meeting will take place on Wednesday, November 18, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.
West Side Story
West Side Story (1961) star George Chakiris will be present for two screenings of the Academy Award-winning film, for which he won Best Actor. Chakiris will answer questions following each screening. Tickets are $5 for SVA students, faculty and staff members with the code "SVA2015." (Limit four tickers per order.) $35 general admission tickets. A portion of the evening’s proceeds will benefit the CHARGE Syndrome Foundation. Sunday, November 22, 5:00pm, and Monday, November 23, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
