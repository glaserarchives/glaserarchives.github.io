SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
April 17 - April 23, 2017
Photo by Terrance Carney.

SVA in Cuba

MPS Digital Photography hosted its second Spring Break trip to Cuba last month, bringing a group of students, staff and SVA community members to Havana for a weeklong location shoot, history lesson and professional workshop.

VAP's New Creative Director

Award-winning designer, writer and BFA Design faculty member Gail Anderson has been promoted to creative director of the Visual Arts Press.

From Well-Heeled Folks to Abstract Southern Landscapes

Current New York exhibitions feature photos by BFA Photography and Video faculty member Jessica Craig-Martin and paintings by Brian Rutenberg (MFA 1989 Fine Arts).

SVA Students Beautify NYC

Members of the International Student Office’s Cultural Partner Program volunteered their first weekends of spring to beautify Stuyvesant Cove Park in New York City.
IN THE PRESS
From SHOT: 101 Survivors of Gun Violence in America. Image by Kathy Shorr.

Documenting Gun Violence

The Fader interviews Kathy Shorr (BFA 1988 Photography) about her photography project and book SHOT: 101 Victims of Gun Violence in America.

Starting an Art Career in Your 30s

Creative Boom interviews BFA Illustration faculty member Yuko Shimizu (MFA 2003 Illustration as Visual Essay) on her "fascinating" artistic journey and her "breathtaking" work.

Quirky Wearable Art

New York Post on Christina J. Wang's (MFA 2012 Fine Arts) scarves and accessories based off of everyday objects such as Shu Uemura hair products and ramen noodles, Birkenstock sandals and Momofuku Milk Bar cakes.
EVENTS

Alice Twemlow: "Making Waste"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents its founder Alice Twemlow, who will give a talk based on her forthcoming book Sifting the Trash: A History of Design Criticism. RSVP here. Tuesday, April 18, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

David Julian Leonard and W.M. Hunt

BFA Photography and Video presents a conversation between photographer and filmmaker David Julian Leonard, whose documentary work has been about subjects from the heart of American music (Stax Records, Johnny Cash, Mississippi Blues, etc.), and department faculty member W.M. Hunt. Wednesday, April 19, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, ground floor.
