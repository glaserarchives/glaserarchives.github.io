Subject: The Lasting Legacy of MFA Art Writing
From: SVA Info
Date: 11/16/21, 2:36 PM
To: allstudents@sva.edu, allstaff@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu

To the SVA Community:

 Last May, while so much attention was focused on how to safely reopen the College, our MFA Art Writing program closed after 16 years, led for the last 14 years by critic and poet David Levi Strauss. Such programs are rare and require a great deal of care and attention. David had to be persuaded to take over Art Writing, but once in charge he shaped and nurtured a small and remarkable group of critical thinkers and a space—both literal and metaphorical—for such work. Essential to this effort was a dedicated faculty and passionate students from around the world. Sadly, this last group proved unsustainable in the current circumstances.

To celebrate the accomplishments of the Art Writing program, The Brooklyn Rail, currently “the newspaper of record” for critical thinking, organized several tributes, including this recording of a live panel with faculty and alumni, from August 19, now on the Rail’s YouTube channel; and further, this online tribute with testimonials from faculty and alumni.

Other valuable Art Writing work to be preserved includes the Quijote Talks & Lecture Series Archive on SVA’s YouTube channel and Holding Everything Dear: Selections from Degree Critical and the School of Visual Arts MFA Art Writing Published Archive.

Most of all, I salute David Levi Strauss, his faculty and alumni—and I thank them for what they created.

 

David Rhodes

President
