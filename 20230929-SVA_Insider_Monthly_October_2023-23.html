🎙️ Get To Know: Winnie Kwan, Financial Aid Advisor, Financial Aid Office; 2023 Distinguished Staff Award Winner

Tell us a little about your history with SVA and your current role.

I joined the SVA Financial Aid Office and became an advisor in 2005. I am responsible for assisting new and returning students and their parents who would like to apply for financial aid and learn about their financing options to cover tuition, housing and other related educational expenses. Besides my financial aid advisor's job responsibilities, I am also the federal work-study coordinator who provides information on on-campus and off-campus work-study opportunities, handles online timesheets and coordinates with various SVA offices for the work-study payroll process.


Congratulations on being named a 2023 Distinguished Staff Award winner! What does it mean to you to win this award?

It is a great honor for me to receive this Distinguished Staff Award. It means a lot to me as a recognition for all I have done and brings encouragement for me to continue to grow as a professional. It also motivates me to explore more ways to assist students and keep my passion to provide better service. I was touched, especially when I received calls and emails from some SVA employees and SVA alumni congratulating me and thanking me for the assistance and support that I have provided.


As a financial aid advisor, what challenges are you seeing incoming students facing and how has a role like yours adjusted to meet them?

The biggest challenges that I have seen include how to help incoming students finance the tuition and other related costs. Ever since the pandemic, some students have experienced financial strains due to the unemployment or health issues of their parents. They have been afraid to ask for assistance and do not know what they would be qualified for. As a financial aid advisor, I am willing to listen and understand their circumstances. I encourage them to apply for financial aid, offer the appeal process, and suggest financing options that would fit into their needs and make them feel at ease about the process. I also believe that building up trust between students and their families with the Financial Aid Office is important, so they would know who they could contact when they need assistance for financing their education costs.


What are you hoping to contribute as a member of the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group?

As a new member of DSAG, I wish to share more creative ideas with the [other] members and provide support for the SVA events. I believe that my tenure here gives me insight that would be beneficial to the DSAG group.

Eugene Gologursky

﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿🚇﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿﻿ ‘Underground Images: A History’ Reception Photos

SVA faculty, staff, alumni and design legends gathered earlier this month to celebrate the opening of “Underground Images: A History,” SVA’s exhibition of its more than 200 subway posters, now on view at the SVA Chelsea Gallery. Among the attendees were Executive Vice President Anthony P. Rhodes, many current and former chair and faculty poster artists, including Steven Heller, Louise Fili, Nathan Fox, Edel Rodriguez, Yuko Shimizu, Marvin Mattlelson and Gail Anderson. Check out Alumni Affairs’ Flickr account for more images from the reception.

📆 Save The Date: A Snapshot in Hip Hop: A Conversation with Angel Ibañez and Friends

As part of their ongoing celebration of hip-hop's 50th Anniversary, the DEI Office will be hosting a conversation with SVA's Angel R. Ibañez (1974 Illustration). Ibanez and special hip-hop guests— including rapper Jalil from the group Whodini—will be on hand for an epic discussion of hip-hop on October 2 at 6:30pm at the SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
