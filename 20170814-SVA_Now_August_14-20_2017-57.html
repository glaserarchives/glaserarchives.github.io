SVA Now
News and events from the School of Visual Arts
August 14 - 20, 2017

Directing DuckTales [Video]

Dana Terrace (BFA 2013 Animation) talks about how she was influenced by Garfield and The Simpsons as a kid, how SVA prepared her for the job she has now, directing Disney's reboot DuckTales, which premiered this past weekend, and more.

LACMA Reimagined [Video]

Vera Lutter (MFA 1995 Photography and Related Media), an artist-in-residence at the Los Angeles County Museum of Art, discusses the inspiration and intrigue behind her most recent project: a photo-documentation of museum buildings that are slated for demolition.
IN THE PRESS

Skating to the Big Screen

Variety on how Crystal Moselle (BFA 2002 Film and Video), director of the critically acclaimed documentary The Wolfpack, is slated to direct her first feature film about female teenage skateboarders, starring Jaden Smith and several New York City skaters.

Edible Art

CNN interviews Maayan Zilberman (BFA 2001 Fine Arts), an artist-confectioner who makes sculptures using sugar and sugar-like materials, about her sweet art.

Gravity-Defying Lamp

Colossal on Richard Clarkson's (MFA 2014 Products of Design) newest cloud lamp—the Floating Cloud—which can hover above your desk.
EVENTS
Katrín Inga Jónsdóttir Hjördísardóttir (MFA 2014 Fine Arts), photo by Arto Sivonen.

Feeding the Vanity

MFA Fine Arts presents a performative lecture by 2014 program alumnus Katrín Inga Jónsdóttir Hjördísardóttir, in which she will reproduce and frame an archive of her own performance works from 2015 to the present. Tuesday, August 15, 6:00 – 7:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th-floor flex space.

Drawing in Madison Square Park

SVA Continuing Education presents an evening of drawing en plein air in Madison Square Park. Space is limited and registration is required. Tuesday, August 15, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Madison Square Park, 23rd Street at Broadway.
