Events

7:00pm | Children of the First Uprising: Strategies for Change and Land Reclamation in Ecuador
SVA Humanities and Sciences faculty member Quito Ziegler and their class, Advanced Studies in Collective Action, in collaboration with the Indigenous Student Union, present indigenous land defender Emilio Paulo Chong. Chong will speak on the history of his people, the Wankavilka, an indigenous community that was never conquered by the Incas and is one of the oldest civilizations in South America, as he shares the ways they have regained ancestral territories from the Ecuadorian government, companies and descendents of colonial landowners. Monday, April 11, 7:00pm ET, on Zoom.

7:30pm | Morbius

Deadline Today | Love Letters to Survivors Art Event
April is Sexual Assault Awareness Month. SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the College’s Title IX Office invite students to send in empowering expressions of art for sexual assault survivors with messages to uplift and support. Submissions for #SurvivorLoveLetter will be open through today, 9:00am ET. On Thursday, April 14, 2:00 – 4:00pm, accepted submissions will be displayed in the SVA Student Center alongside an event where survivors and allies are invited to show support and contribute to the art display. To see entry guidelines and submit art, click here. To learn more about the #SurvivorLoveLetter movement, click here.
