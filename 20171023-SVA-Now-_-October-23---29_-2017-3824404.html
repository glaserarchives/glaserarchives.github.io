October 23 - 29, 2017

SVA's Emmy Winner

A Q&A with David Osit (MFA 2011 Social Documentary Film), who took home the Emmy Award for Outstanding Arts and Culture Documentary for his film Thank You for Playing.

SVA Guide to the Lower East Side

Here are a few suggested places to visit, things to do and foods to eat in NYC's Lower East Side and East Village.

Challenges for Creative Professionals

BFA Advertising and BFA Design Chair Richard Wilde discusses the challenges that creative professionals face, as well as the opportunities they may encounter in the real world with Education and Career News.
IN THE PRESS

Collier Schorr Speaks Out

ArtNet News on how artist and fashion photographer Collier Schorr (BFA 1985 Communication Arts) has called on agents, magazines and designers to stand up to harassment.

Niemann's Abstract Memories

Cultured interviews illustrator Christoph Niemann about social media, New York and his work, in light of his retrospective at the SVA Chelsea Gallery, on view through November 4.

"The Turbulence Series"

Blouin Artinfo on BFA Fine Arts faculty member Alice Aycock's "The Turbulence Series," featuring some of her most recent artwork and on view at Marlborough Gallery, 40 West 57th Street, through Saturday, November 18.
EVENTS

The Artist Book with Mario Santoro

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents Italian photographer Mario Santoro, whose lecture will explore how artist books can incorporate a variety of mediums. Wednesday, October 25, 1:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

"Detours" Reception

MPS Digital Photography presents an exhibition of work that represents the journeys the 13 graduates of the class of 2017 undertook to complete a personal and unique thesis project. Reception: Wednesday, October 25, 6:00pm. Through Saturday, November 11. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

"Electric Encounters" Reception

MFA Computer Arts presents an exhibition of work by students, faculty and alumni, which explores the varied connections between humans and technology, and how machines influence our experience and connections. Thursday, October 26, 6:00pm. Through Wednesday, November 22. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
