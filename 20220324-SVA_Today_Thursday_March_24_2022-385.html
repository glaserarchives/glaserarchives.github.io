SVA Today
Thursday, March 24, 2022

cardboard: a meditation on medium
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition curated by program student Caroline Taylor Shehan, featuring artists who elevate cardboard from substrate to subject. Through Wednesday, March 30. Reception tonight, 7:00pm. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Events

10:00am | Career Development Virtual Office Hour
Join SVA Career Development’s virtual office hour today, 10:00am ET. During the one-hour session, Assistant Director of Career Development Tricia Ross will be available on Zoom to field questions about your job search, an upcoming interview and other career-related topics. No waiting room—you can jump in, join the conversation and share with your classmates and fellow alumni. Fill out this form during office hours to receive a Zoom link to join.

12:30pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours
Every Thursday, 12:30 – 2:00pm ET, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).

6:00pm | Insurgent Practices Against Neoliberalism
The SVA Honors Program and BFA Visual & Critical Studies present artist Jane Dickson and writer Sarah Schulman in conversation with artist and faculty member Shellyne Rodriguez (BFA 2011 Visual & Critical Studies) on their experiences as working artists in a changing and unequal New York City, from the 1970s to the present. Tonight, 6:00pm ET, online.

By Visceral Effect
StartaArta, a gallery founded by SVA alumni, presents “By Visceral Effect,” a group show featuring artists including Dane LaChiusa (BFA 1983 Media Arts). Reception tonight, 6:00 – 8:00 pm. StartaArta Gallery, 220 East 23rd Street.

RE|CLAIM RE|BUILD: The EstroGenius Festival
Experience the voices of dance-makers, playwrights, experimental performers, teens, musicians, burlesque performers, noise artists, filmmakers, DJs and anyone else looking to break molds and crack gender codes to the stage, street and forefront of public attention. Free. Tonight, 6:30 – 8:00pm. The Great Hall at Cooper Union, 7 East Seventh Street. Registration required.
