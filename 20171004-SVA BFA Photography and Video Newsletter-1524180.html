Surfer Magazine Interviews Mohammid Walbrook About Hurricane Irma's Toll on Barbuda

Alum Mohammid Walbrook (2016) weathered the hurricane with his friends and family in his island home, Barbuda. Surfer interviewed Mohammid about the destruction, featuring several of his photos taken after the storm.
Elizabeth Bick Publishes New Book Movement Study I: Street Ballet

Faculty member Elizabeth Bick's new book, Movement Study I: Street Ballet, published by Roman Numerals, launched at the New York Art Book Fair. This oversize volume contains 16 photos from Bick's street photography.
USC News Features Star Montana

Alum Star Montana (2013) is featured on the University of Southern California's news blog about her practice and history. Star is a first-year MFA candidate at USC Roski School of Art and Design.
Amy Elkins Exhibition on View at High Museum of Art

Alum Amy Elkins' (2007) exhibition Black is the Day, Black is the Night is currently on view at the High Museum of Art in Atlanta, GA through March 4, 2018. This project explores the psychological effects of long-term solitary confinement. 
 
Lisa Elmaleh's Photographs Featured in Harper's Magazine

Faculty and alum, Lisa Elmaleh's (2007) photographs accompany an article about a family in West Virgina's fight to the rights over their farmland with Mountaineer Gas. 
6sqft Interviews Ash Thayer on Photographing The Lower East Side in The 90s

Alum Ash Thayer (1997) talks with 6sqft about her collection of portraits taken of the punk and squatter scene in the East Village in the 90s. The collection, titled KILL CITY, was recently published by powerHouse Books.
