SVA Today
Friday, April 8, and the Weekend
What Are Your #SVASPOTS
In advance of tomorrow's Accepted Students Day, Zak Tebbal (BFA 2015 Design) spoke with SVA Close Up about SVA Spots, a new Visual Arts Press publication that highlights students' favorite hangouts, with tips from Tebbal on living in the Big Apple.
Opening: "The Irreality of Reality"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition of work by Mark Dorf and Sara Ludy that confronts the role of simulacra in their respective practices. Curated by MA Curatorial Practice fellow Valerie Amend. Reception tonight, 6:30 - 8:30pm. Through Thursday, April 21, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Noon: MFA Computer Art Open Studios
MFA Computer Art presents a showcase of work by graduating students, including video, animation, audio, interactive installations and physical computing. Noon - 7:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, 12th floor.
12:30pm: Finding Commissions through IndieWalls
SVA Career Development presents representatives from art and tech start-up IndieWalls discussing how to obtain commissions for public spaces and from corporate clients. Pizza will be served. Open to SVA students and alumni. Registration required. 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
7:00pm: From Darkroom to Daylight
MPS Digital Photography and BFA Photography and Video present a screening of photographer Harvey Wang’s new documentary, which explores how the shift from film to digital has affected photographers and their work. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Opening: "Poisoned Apples and Smoking Lamps"
An exhibition of work by third-year BFA Cartooning and BFA Illustration students that reinterprets popular fairy tales. Saturday, April 9, through Saturday, April 23. Reception Wednesday, April 13, 6:00 - 8:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
Saturday: Closing Exhibitions
Last chance to see "The Big Twin Drawing Show," at SVA Flatiron Gallery Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street; "Help Is on the Way!," at SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street; and "The Tide," at SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street. All three exhibitions close Saturday, April 9.
Saturday: Salman Rushdie In:Pictures
SVA and The Modern School of Film present the Booker Prize-winning author discussing and screening clips from three films that have influenced and inspired him. Doors open at 7:30pm. Tickets are $5 for SVA students and $10 for faculty and staff. VIP reserved seating tickets are $60; $20 general admission. Saturday, April 9, 8:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Sunday: "Repeating Traces" Reception
MA Curatorial Practice presents a collaboration between artist Rachel Garrard and MA Curatorial Practice fellow Lalita Salander, in which the artist physically maps her movements according to a specific algorithm. Reception and performance Sunday, April 10, noon - 10:00pm. Through Thursday, April 14, Pioneer Works, 159 Pioneer Street, Brooklyn.
Sunday: Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice
The Visual Effects Society presents a 3D screening of the new film. Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Seating is first-come, first-served. Doors open 30 - 45 minutes prior to showtime. Sunday, April 10, 3:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE
Construction at 205 and 209 East 23rd Street
Local Law 11 requires that the facades of New York City buildings of more than six stories be regularly inspected. After a fall 2015 inspection, it was deemed sections of the facade of 205 and 209 East 23rd Street buildings need to be removed and replaced. This project will begin on Monday, April 11, and continue until the end of July. In the first two weeks of the project, scaffolding will put up on all sides of the buildings. Select facade removal will take place at the end of April. During the removal period (approximately two to four weeks) there will be loud construction noise associated with the project between the hours of 7:30am and 3:30pm. Periodic updates will be provided in SVA Today in the coming weeks. For questions, contact Richard Riccio, executive director of facilities, 212.592.2156.
Exhibition Installation Skills for Multimedia Artists
SVA Career Development presents a hands-on workshop on how to install screens, projections, multi-part installations, and more. Open to SVA students and alumni. Registration is required. Tuesday, April 12, 12:30pm, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
