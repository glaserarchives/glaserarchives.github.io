August 28 – September 4, 2023

Introducing the New MFA Illustration as Visual Essay Chair

Earlier this summer, Riccardo Vecchio (MFA 1996 Illustration as Visual Essay) was named chair of the College’s MFA Illustration as Visual Essay program, succeeding founding chair Marshall Arisman, who passed away last year. Read our Q&A with Vecchio above.

All Tomorrow’s Parties: The Velvet Underground Story

A new graphic novel by BFA Illustration faculty member and 2006 alumnus Koren Shadmi takes a biographical deep dive into the story of the New York art-punk band The Velvet Underground. Read all about it in our interview with Shadmi at the link above.

EVENTS

Opens Tuesday | “Underground Images: A History”

SVA Galleries presents an exhibition of the more than 200 posters from its famed “subway series,” which has been a mainstay of New York City transit station walls over the past 75 years. Tuesday, August 29 – Saturday, October 14. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Friday | New Student Orientation: Health and Wellness Fair

Student Health and Counseling invites students to the health and wellness fair! The fair will feature a live T’aiChiChuan demo, free STD testing, goodie bags, vendor swag, blood pressure checks, a campus store giveaway and plenty of health and wellness tips. Friday, September 1, 12:00 – 2:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

“It’s Grabbing You by the Lapels”

Director of SVA Galleries Francis Di Tommaso talks to The Guardian about “Underground Images: A History,” SVA’s exhibition of its more than 200 “subway series” posters, which opens this week.

“Time Creates Spaces to Evolve”

Artist and BFA Fine Arts staff member Zoe Schwartz talks to DSCENE about how she explores a connection between mold making and generational trauma through her work on the occasion of her solo exhibition “Mother Mold,” at Alanna Miller Gallery in Manhattan.

2023 National Design Award Winners

MFA Products of Design faculty member Hlynur Vagn Atlason has been awarded the 2023 National Design Awards in the Product Design category for his New York-based studio, Atlason, which specializes in strategic innovation and industrial design.

Charles Traub on “How AI Will Change Your Photography Major”

BestColleges talks to MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Chair Charles Traub to learn how a code of ethics could help photographers and creatives as they tackle AI’s presence in the lens world.

NYC PICKS

ImageNation’s Soul-Train Tribute Celebrating 50 Years of Hip-Hop

Celebrate 50 years of hip hop with a screening of the Biz Markie documentary All Up in the Biz (2023), a hip-hop fashion show, special guest DJ Hollywood and a performance from The Last Poets. Thursday, August 31, 6:00 – 10:00pm. Marcus Garvey Park Amphitheater, 18 Mt. Morris Park West.

Hester Street Fair Labor Day Weekend

Close out the summer season at Hester Street Fair’s two-day Labor Day Weekend party and fair featuring vendors, food, DJs and drinks. Free; RSVP here. Saturday, September 2, 11:00am – 7:00pm. South Street Seaport Pier 17, 89 South Street.

West Indian Day Parade

The largest Caribbean Carnival in North America returns to Brooklyn, marching down Eastern Parkway in celebration of Caribbean culture, arts, food, history, traditions and people. Monday, September 4, parade begins at 11:00am. The parade starts at Schenectady Avenue and ends at Grand Army Plaza, Brooklyn.

U.S. Open on the Big Screen at Hudson Yards

Watch the U.S. Open live on a 30-foot big screen at the Hudson Yard Public Square and Gardens overlooking the river. Thursday, August 31 – Sunday, September 10; times and dates vary. Screenings are free and seating is open to the public on a first-come, first-serve basis. Hudson Yards between 30th and 33rd Streets, 10th and 11th Avenues.

“Weh Dem? De Sparrow Catcher?”

A solo exhibition of new work by Cyle Warner (BFA 2023 Photography and Video) that brings together a reimagined archive of photographs and textiles to reveal a personal exploration of his family’s life in the Caribbean. Through Monday, September 18. Welancora Gallery, 33 Herkimer Street, Brooklyn.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Opening Next Week | “ILLO COMX Preview”

An exhibition of highlights by 125 BFA Comics and BFA Illustration graduates from the class of 2023, curated by the departments’ chair, Viktor Koen (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay). Wednesday, September 6 – Monday, October 2. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Opening Next Week | “Exploded View”

A joint exhibition of short films, prints and puppets by 25 students and alumni from BFA 3D Animation and Visual Effects and BFA Animation, curated by faculty member James Bascara (BFA 2011 Illustration) and BFA Animation director of operations Angelica Vergel. Reception: Thursday, September 28, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Wednesday, September 6 – Monday, October 2. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
