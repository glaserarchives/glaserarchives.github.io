Lynsey Addario Is SVA’s 2023 Commencement Speaker

Acclaimed photographer, MacArthur Genius Grant awardee and Pulitzer Prize recipient Lynsey Addario will be the keynote speaker at SVA’s 48th annual commencement exercises taking place at Radio City Music Hall on Sunday, May 21.

EVENTS

1:00pm | ISO Virtual Drop-In Hours

Every Thursday, 1:00 – 2:30pm, SVA’s International Student Office (ISO) will be offering virtual drop-in hours, during which students can meet with an ISO advisor on Zoom without an appointment. Fill out this form during drop-in hours to receive a Zoom link. Find more details on how to sign up here (scroll to “International Student Office Staff” headline).


Individual virtual appointments with ISO advisors are available throughout the week; to schedule one, click here. Email iso@sva.edu with any questions.

2:30pm | LEGO-of-Stress

Take a break with SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and build your own LEGO creations to relieve stress and practice mindfulness. Enjoy a cup of cocoa, iced tea and some snacks! Today, March 30, 2:30 – 4:30pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

4:00pm | The Bound Book: A Three-Day Bookbinding Workshop

Learn several bookbinding techniques with artist Althea James at SVA Library West. Bring your own projects or use the provided array of fun, eccentric supplies. Today’s workshop will focus on accordion binding. Join tomorrow’s workshop for a free, experimental day. Today, March 30, 4:00 – 6:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

5:00pm | VASA’s Spring Market

The spring market is here—purchase art from fellow SVA students who diligently prepared posters, apparel, prints, zines, crochet, crafts, photography and more! There will be 40 different vendors each day for a total of 80 students. Tonight, March 30, and tomorrow, March 31, 5:00 – 8:00pm. 216 East 23rd Street.

5:00pm | Directors Dialogue: Thelma Golden in Conversation

MFA Art Practice, MA Curatorial Practice and the American Federation of Arts present Thelma Golden, director and chief curator at The Studio Museum in Harlem, to speak about the museum’s current programming initiatives and reflect on the exhibition “Black Refractions: Highlights From The Studio Museum in Harlem.” Tonight, March 30, 5:00 – 7:00pm. SVA Theater, 333 West 23rd Street.

Finding Parallel Worlds Through Shoes

MFA Design for Social Innovation brings together embodied researcher-practitioners and design creatives to discuss body-informed frameworks and methods within the field of social innovation. Sunday, April 2, 10:00am – 3:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Visual Opinion Is Hiring

Visual Opinion is seeking a managing editor and interns for the fall 2023 and spring 2024 semesters. Matriculated students located in the U.S. who are creative, proactive and organized are invited to apply. Visit the Visual Opinion’s Instagram at @visualopinion for position requirements and details. To apply, email your resume, statement of interest and portfolio or portfolio sample to vo@sva.edu. Applications close Monday, April 3, 11:59pm. Questions? Email vo@sva.edu.
