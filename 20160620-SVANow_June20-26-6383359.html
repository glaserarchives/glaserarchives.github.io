SVA Now
A digest of news and events from the School of Visual Arts June 20 - 26, 2016
FEATURES

Scharf on Haring, Basquiat, NYC and SVA [Video]

Watch Kenny Scharf (BFA 1981 Fine Arts) talk about his time at SVA, NYC in the '80s, and his friendships with Jean-Michel Basquiat and Keith Haring (1979 Fine Arts).

Cover Stories: Lauren Beukes' Broken Monsters

Designer Keith Hayes (BFA 2002 Graphic Design) and author Lauren Beukes discuss the cover design for the U.S. edition of Broken Monsters.

Along the Israel-Palestine Border

Recent MFA Photography, Video and Related Media graduate and 2016 Alumni Scholarship Award winner Netta Laufer on her "25FT" project, which examines wildlife along the Israel-Palestine border as a way to consider human conflict.

Before and After SVA: Jeric Pimentel

The 2015 BFA Computer Art, Computer Animation and Visual Effects alumnus talks about why he chose SVA, his triumphant senior year at the College, how he landed his current job at the Moving Picture Company, and more.
IN THE PRESS
Photo by Zandy Mangold

The Scion Also Rises

The New York Post on children's book author, illustrator and MFA Visual Narrative faculty member Edward Hemingway (MFA 2002 Illustration as Visual Essay) and his relationship with his grandfather, Ernest Hemingway.

"Let's Create Our Own World"

Flavorwire interviews SVA faculty member, alumnus and legendary No Wave filmmaker Beth B.

Make Painting Great Again

New York critic Jerry Saltz on a group exhibition at Canada Gallery featuring work by Katherine Bernhardt (MFA 2000 Fine Arts), whom Saltz calls "one of the hottest painters around."
EVENTS
Photo by Nick Alciati

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media Thesis Exhibition and Reception

Reception on Wednesday, June 22, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Friday, July 1, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Ken Burns In:Pictures

SVA and The Modern School of Film present the documentary filmmaker discussing and screening clips from three films that have influenced his understanding of the American experience. $5 for SVA students, $10 for faculty and staff (two ticket limit and one non-SVA guest permitted), $20 general admission, $60 VIP reserved seating. Tuesday, June 21, 7:00pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Cat Del Buono

MPS Digital Photography presents the 2008 MFA Photography, Video and Related Media alumnus discussing her work, which incorporates performance, interactive video and humor. Tuesday, June 21, 7:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.

"All-Staff Art Show" Reception

SVA Galleries and the Distinguished Staff Advisory Group present the annual exhibition of work by College staff. Thursday, June 23, 5:30 - 8:00pm. Exhibition runs through Friday, July 1, SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
