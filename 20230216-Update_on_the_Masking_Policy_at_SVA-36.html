Subject: Update on the Masking Policy at SVA
From: SVA Info
Date: 2/16/23, 11:00 AM
To: allstudents@sva.edu, allfaculty@sva.edu, allstaff@sva.edu

Good Morning,

 

I’m writing to provide an update on our mask-wearing policy on campus. Once again, I appreciate the patience and cooperation all of you have demonstrated in complying with this policy.

 

As you know, SVA determined that we could go to a “masks encouraged but not required” option as long as transmission rates of the virus are low in New York City and at SVA. We defined this to be a transmission rate in the general population of NYC over a seven-day period that was at or below 100 per 100,000 people, or 1,200 new infections per day. That rate, when correlated to SVA’s own community population of 5,500, is equal to about seven new infections per week, or one new infection per day.

 

As of February 15, the seven-day NYC transmission rate is 98 per 100,000 people, or 1166 per day. Here at SVA, the seven-day campus transmission rate for the past week was 10 per 5500 people. During the prior week, that number was 5, making SVA’s daily average over the past two weeks about one infection per day.


With this encouraging downward trend, we feel it is safe to loosen our mask policy to be “masks encouraged but not required,” effective Monday, February 20. We will proceed with caution, however, and should numbers spike, we may need to reintroduce the mask requirement policy. We are also going to continue to require everyone to provide their daily health attestation through Cleared4, as this enables us to continue to track infections on campus effectively. 


Those who have recently been infected with COVID-19 or had a recent exposure should follow public health guidance on mask wearing. Also, some SVA community members may still choose to wear masks. While masking is no longer mandated, we should respect anyone's choice to continue wearing masks.


I wish you a safe and healthy spring semester.


 

David Rhodes

President, SVA
