🎙️ Get to Know Patricia Romeu, director, Career Development

Congratulations on your new position as director of Career Development! Can you summarize your SVA journey up to this point?

This is my third position in Career Development at SVA. Over 11 years, I've been an assistant director, associate director and now, director. I've had the chance to grow into the role and discover the many facets of working in career development for creative fields. Along the way, I've developed a more sophisticated understanding of what young creatives need to succeed once they leave school and how we can better prepare them for the many unknowns that lie ahead.


Looking back on your legacy with Career Development, what were some of your most memorable, rewarding experiences?

For me, the most rewarding parts of this role have been working directly with students and alumni, whether that's meeting with them one-on-one in a career counseling session, conducting a workshop, moderating a panel or helping them with Fulbright applications. There's a tremendous number of energetic, talented young people at SVA, and I enjoy playing a small part in contributing to their success outside of the classroom. I'm struck by how appreciative our students and alumni are to receive undivided attention in a one-on-one counseling session, and I find it gratifying to witness their growth over the course of a semester and often years.


It's also satisfying to bring in guest speakers who discuss their careers and give students and alumni a chance to hear about the unique journeys of creatives, the opportunities that exist and the possibilities of forging one's own path.


What are you most looking forward to in your new role?

To be honest, I am most looking forward to having a fully staffed department! Once we are again a four-person staff, we will recalibrate as a team and together explore new ideas to provide services and programs to our students and alumni.


I heartily enjoy the students, alumni and staff of the SVA community. There are reasons why so many of us have stayed at SVA for a long time.

🤯 Free Courses and Tutorials

LinkedIn Learning’s online classes and tutorials provide opportunities to brush up on your old skills and explore entirely new ones.


Taking Charge of Your Career

Navigating your career now means being able to adapt and stay relevant. This course is designed for anyone who wants to take charge and plan the next move—whether it's choosing a career, negotiating a promotion or changing fields.


Animation for Social Media

A social media animation video is designed to live on social channels. This means short, sharp bursts of content that grab people's attention as they scroll through their feeds. Anyone can post an image or a few lines of text, but few can post a high-quality animated video.


Creating Inclusive Content

Graphic design has an important role in inclusivity. Your designs can literally make the world a better place by promoting a graphic "language" that is inclusive, diverse, and equitable. In this course, instructor Sabrena Deal describes what inclusive content is and why it's important in today's culture.
