THIS WEEK

Monday – Wednesday | OutCinema 2018

SVA Theatre, NYC Pride and NewFest are proud to present OutCinema 2018, three days of special screenings during Pride Week, each followed by Q&As and receptions. Special discounts available to SVA ID holders with the code SVAOC18. Purchase tickets at the links below. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

-Monday, June 18, 7:30pm: Ideal Home with Paul Rudd and Steve Coogan.

-Tuesday, June 19, 7:30pm: Antonio Lopez 1970: Sex, Fashion, & Disco

-Wednesday, June 20, 8:00pm: From Selma to Stonewall: Are We There Yet?

Tuesday | Mental Health First Aid

Student Health and Counseling Services is proud to host Mental Health First Aid training for SVA staff and faculty. This eight-hour training teaches the skills to provide initial support when someone experiences a mental health crisis. Click here for more info about the class. Register here. Tuesday, June 19, 9:00am – 5:00pm. 380 Second Avenue.

Tuesday | Hilton Als

MFA Art Practice presents a talk by New Yorker writer Hilton Als. Click here to see the program's whole summer lecture series lineup. Tuesday, June 19, 12:30 – 2:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, Room 501H.

Tuesday | "The Kindness Experience: Happy Hour 4 Kids" Reception

Presented by MPS Art Therapy, this exhibition features work by the children and adolescents who attend Happy Hour 4 Kids, a full-time therapeutic center. Tuesday, June 19, 1:30 – 3:00pm. Through Saturday, June 30. Viewing times available by appointment; contact 212.592.2610 or arttherapy@sva.edu. MPS Art Therapy Gallery Space, 132 West 21st Street, 5th floor.

Wednesday | Dog Therapy for Faculty and Staff

Wednesday | Dimensional Whateverism

An exhibition of thesis work by the MAT Art Education class of 2018. Wednesday, June 20 – Saturday, July 7. Reception: Friday, July 6, 4:00 – 6:00pm. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Saturday | SVA Short Film Fest

MPS Directing presents its thesis film festival, featuring screenings of thesis films from the class of 2018. A reception will follow. RSVP here. Saturday, June 23, 1:00 – 3:00pm, and 5:00 – 7:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street
PRESS PICK

On Lorna Simpson Collages

The Atlantic on how Lorna Simpson (BFA 1982 Photography) "maps the complex galaxies of black women's hair" in her new collage series, made from magazine clippings, watercolor and images of geological formations.
NYC PICKS

Frederick Law Olmstead: Designing America

A screening of the documentary about the man who designed Central and Prospect Parks, followed by a talk by the film's lead researcher and historian Laurence Cotton. Free; RSVP here. Wednesday, June 20, 6:30pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.

BAMcinemaFest Filmmaker Talks

BAMcinemaFest presents a free day of panels with established filmmakers and film professionals to help emerging filmmakers make the strongest impact at the start of their careers. Panels include: Film Financing for Beginners, Connecting with Your Audience, and keynotes from filmmaker Jim McKay (En el Séptimo Día) and Franklin Leonard, founder of The Black List. Programmed by Rose Vincelli Gustine, director of operations in MFA Social Documentary Film. RSVP here. Saturday, June 23, 11:00am – 4:00pm. BAMcinématek, 30 Lafayette Avenue, Brooklyn.
