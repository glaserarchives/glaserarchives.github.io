Friday, May 26, and Memorial Day Weekend

Sarah Sze on PBS

PBS Newshour featured artist Sarah Sze (MFA 1997 Fine Arts) in a segment for its arts and culture series, “CANVAS,” on the occasion of her new exhibition at the Guggenheim.

EVENTS

Last Chance | “BFA Photography and Video Senior Show”

BFA Photography and Video presents a virtual thesis show, providing a dynamic and interactive experience with the final projects of the graduating class of 2023. Closes today, May 26, online.

Sunday | The Little Mermaid Screening

SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of The Little Mermaid, starring Halle Bailey. Free with SVA ID (one non-SVA guest permitted per SVA ID holder). RSVPs not accepted or required. Seating is first-come, first-served. Masks are encouraged, but not required. Sunday, May 28, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Ongoing Exhibitions

  • •“Milton Glaser: POP,” through Monday, June 5. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.
  • •Animation Celebration Exhibition, through Tuesday, June 6. SVA Flatiron Windows and SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.
  • •“Ephemeral Vortex,” through Thursday, June 15, online.

SVA IN THE PRESS

“One Painting One Prayer”

Xinya Li (BFA 2020 Design) curated a series of pop-up exhibitions featuring artist Shih Pao Lin’s daily painting and meditation challenge documenting his prayers for peace in Ukraine.

NYC PICKS

May Game Jam Black Web Fest

A weekend-long event for underrepresented creators to come together and learn how to develop video games. Creators of all skill levels are welcome. Attendees must bring their own laptops. Free. Today, May 26 – Sunday, May 28. 461 West 126th Street.

Fleet Week 2023

Explore a variety of displays, activities and demos from the U.S. Marine Corps, U.S. Coast Guard and USO. Fleet Week concludes on Monday, May 29, with the Intrepid Museum’s annual Memorial Day ceremony. Intrepid Sea, Air & Space Museum, Pier 86, West 46th Street.

Brooklyn’s 156th Memorial Day Parade

Watch the borough’s annual parade celebrating Memorial Day, a federal holiday honoring those who died while serving in the U.S. Armed Forces. Begins Monday, May 29, 11:00am, at 78th Street and Third Avenue, Brooklyn. Click here for more information and the full route.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

SVA Today Switching to Summer Schedule

Today’s SVA Today is the last daily newsletter of the 2022 – 2023 academic year. Beginning Tuesday, May 30, it will switch to a weekly edition for the duration of the summer break.


Congratulations on another year in the books and happy summer to all!

Next Week | The Made in New York Animation Project Showcase

The Made in New York Animation Project presents a showcase of participants’ animated work, alongside speakers from the Mayor’s Office of Media and Entertainment, New York City Department of Probation and program graduates. Free and open to the public. RSVPs appreciated, but not required. Wednesday, May 31, 11:30am. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Next Week | Flamin’ Hot Screening

Women in Entertainment presents an advance screening of Flamin’ Hot, directed by Eva Longoria. The film follows the story of how a Frito-Lay janitor disrupted the food industry by turning Flamin’ Hot Cheetos from a snack into a phenomenon. Tickets are $5; register here. The first five people to register using the promo code “SVA” will get in for free! Wednesday, May 31, 6:00 – 8:00pm. Regal Union Square, 850 Broadway.

Next Week | Amalgam Thread

SVA Artist Residency Programs presents an exhibition of artwork by four program alumni exploring materials and techniques traditionally associated with crafts. Reception: Thursday, June 1, 5:00 – 7:00pm. Thursday, June 1, through Thursday, June 8. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21 Street.

Next Week | Transforming Public Art

Join Madison Square Park Conservancy for their annual symposium, Transforming Public Art, which considers the ways contemporary artists and art world leaders are challenging conventions in public art practice. Speakers include artist Shahzia Sikander, artist and SVA faculty member Sheila Pepe and curators from the Whitney Museum, the Pérez Art Museum and the Smithsonian American Art Museum. Free and open to the public with RSVP. To RSVP, email tmurraycole@madisonsquarepark.org. Friday, June 2, 9:00am – 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
