News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Friday, January 27 - Sunday, January 29, 2017

"Born to Rock"

NY1 reporter Roger Clark visited 3D Design’s "For the Record: The Art of a Song" yesterday and gave it a rave review. The exhibition is on view through Saturday, January 28.
EVENTS

9:30am | Workshop for Bystander Empowerment

SVA's Student Health and Counseling Services and Title IX Office and the Domestic & Other Violence Emergencies (DOVE) program from New York Presbyterian Hospital present "See Something, Say Whaaat?," a workshop for staff and faculty to develop their skills and understanding of how to intervene in cases of violence and assault. Light refreshments will be served. RSVP to Laurel Christy at lchristy@sva.edu. 9:30 – 11:30am. 23rd Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, media room.

2:00pm | Humane Regeneration

MFA Interaction Design presents Gill Wildman from PLOT, who will host an interactive workshop where you will learn to design materials for an alternative future. RSVP here. 2:00 – 4:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 3rd floor.

2:30pm | Dog Therapy

Student Health and Counseling Services hosts a dog therapy event for SVA students, with New York Therapy Animals. 2:30 – 3:30pm. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

6:30pm | Community Lecture: Art, Migration and Youth of Courage

MPS Art Therapy presents a talk by Eileen McGann, LCAT, ATR-BC. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Saturday | Phyllis Posnick in Conversation with Olivia Bee

MPS Fashion Photography presents executive fashion editor of Vogue Phyllis Posnick and photographer Olivia Bee, who will discuss collaboration in fashion photography during a time of cultural and technological change. They will sign copies of their respective books, Stoppers (Abrams 2016) and Kids in Love (Aperture 2016), which will be available for purchase. RSVP here. Tomorrow, January 28, 2:00 – 4:00pm. Milk Gallery, 450 West 15th Street.

Sunday | Hate in America Screening and Panel Discussion

SVA Theatre and New York State Senator Brad Hoylman present this film screening, followed by a panel discussion between Hoylman, film producer and director Rebecca Teitel, director of the Center on Extremism at the Anti-Defamation League Oren Segal and intelligence project director of the Southern Poverty Law Center Heidi Beirich. RSVP to hoylman@nysenate.gov. Sunday, January 29, 5:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

Lunar New Year Gathering

SVA's International Student Office and Student Health and Counseling Services present a celebration of the Lunar New Year, with Chinese, Korean and Filipino food and Japanese tea, and a calligraphy and origami workshop. The winner of the Lunar New Year Flyer Competition will be announced. Monday, January 30, noon – 2:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.
