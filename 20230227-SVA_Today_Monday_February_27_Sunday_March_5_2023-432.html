NYC PICKS

Invisible to Whom? A Dialogue in Verse

In celebration of the seventieth anniversary of Ralph Ellison’s Invisible Man, poets Cameron Awkward-Rich, Kadeem Gayle and Lorelei Williams present readings of their poems inspired by the novel and Elizabeth Catlett’s sculpture Invisible Man: A Memorial to Ralph Ellison (2003). Free. Wednesday, March 1, 6:30pm. The Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture, 515 Malcolm X Boulevard.

Watersprite Film Festival 2023

A virtual weekend of free master classes, workshops, panels and keynote speeches from voices in the film and TV industry, with topics ranging from demystifying the role of a producer to comedy on screen. Free. Friday, March 3, 12:00am, through Sunday, March 5, 9:00pm, online.

Women’s History Month Trilogy: “Golden Girls”

Flushing Town Hall Theater presents the first performance in its 2023 Women’s History Month Trilogy series celebrating the music of Dionne Warwick, Shirley Bassey, Barbra Streisand, Carole King and Petula Clark. Student tickets: $12. Friday, March 3, 7:00pm.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Tomorrow | Bystander Intervention Training: How To Safely Intervene and Respond in the Face of Anti-Black-Police-Sponsored Violence

Nonprofit advocacy organization Right to Be hosts this free interactive training session that will give you the tools to intervene safely and effectively in the face of anti-Black police-sponsored violence using their “5 Ds” of bystander intervention. The session will cover the types of violence and racism Black people are facing right now, what anti-Black police-sponsored violence looks like, why it repeatedly exists and what concrete strategies can be used to distract, delegate, document, delay or directly intervene in a way that is safe, effective and centered on the needs of the person being targeted. Tuesday, February 28, 6:00 – 7:30pm, online.

Career Development Virtual Office Hours This Month

Next Week | The Algorithmic State: Cybersecurity and Reality Glitches

MA Curatorial Practice presents a talk with artists Maithu Bu, Payton Croskey and cultural anthropologist Rebecca Stein on AI security and ways to address socially harmful uses of AI. Thursday, March 9, 12:00 – 1:00pm, online.
