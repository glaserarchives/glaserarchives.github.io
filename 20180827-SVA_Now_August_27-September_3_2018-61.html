News and events from the School of Visual Arts

Before and After SVA: Lily Maslanka

Lily Maslanka (BFA 2017 Visual & Critical Studies) talks about her plans "to dedicate her life to understanding early modern craft."

City Living: SVA Residence Halls

Get to know SVA's four different residence halls, which give students the opportunity to live in Manhattan among other artists within a safe, supportive environment.
IN THE PRESS

Roz Chast Retrospective
Gothamist talks with illustrator Roz Chast about how her upcoming SVA Masters Series Exhibition, which the College will present this fall.

Progetto in Puglia

ArtNews on how Jamie Sneider (MFA 2013 Fine Arts) is raising money to start a residency program and exhibition space called Progetto in a farmhouse in Puglia, Italy.

Crystal Moselle's Skate Kitchen

CR on Crystal Moselle's first scripted film Skate Kitchen, about an all-girl skateboarding crew.
EVENTS

Tales from the Crit #2

An exhibition of work by figurative artists from the BFA Illustration class of 2018, who explore mood, atmosphere, humor, identity, sexuality, beauty, fantasy and perversity. Through Saturday, September 8. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Continuing Education Information Sessions

Attend an information session for SVA's Continuing Education programs like Advertising, Computer Art, Fine Arts and Photography, where you will learn about course offerings from faculty members and be able to ask questions. Click here for dates, times and locations.

Me

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the SVA Flatiron Project Space present an exhibition featuring work by program alumnus William Patterson and two current students, Joey Gonnella and Nani Inocentes, and curated by faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). Through Friday, September 7. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.
