SVA's Nurse, Abby Wohl, continues to answer your questions regarding COVID-19 and other health-related concerns. Read below for the latest Q&A and use this link to see the question archive. You can also submit any questions you have here.

I know COVID-19 is "airborne" and can spread easily, but why does it matter what type of mask I wear as long as my nose and mouth are covered?
Since the beginning of the pandemic, there has been debate over which mask to wear, in which circumstances, and for how long. This respiratory virus is airborne, so the best mask to wear in certain situations is an approved N95 mask (for more on their efficacy, see the chart posted in the archive). However, N95 masks are sometimes hard to find and can be difficult to put on properly if you were never "fit-tested." If you only have access to a KN-94 or KN-95 mask, which are examples of well-fitting surgical masks, you still have decent protection. Some people double- or triple-mask, depending on the situation, but no matter what you have access to or choose to wear, always remember these two things:
  • Any well-fitting mask is better than no mask at all.
  • The better the fit, the better the protection. (If it doesn’t fit properly or it’s too stifling, you must consider a different mask.)
