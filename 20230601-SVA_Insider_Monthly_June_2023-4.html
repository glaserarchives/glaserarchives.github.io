🎙️ Get To Know: Ryan El Naggar, director, Environmental Health & Safety

Congratulations on your new position as Director of Environmental Health & Safety! Could you tell us about how you got your start in the field and about your current role?

Thank you! Truthfully, I got into this field by luck. When I went to college, I had aspirations to go to medical school, but when that did not happen, I graduated without knowing where I was headed. I was looking for something in the science field and eventually got a job at a hazardous waste shipping company. I drove a truck to different sites every day, ranging from hospitals, colleges, pharmaceutical companies, etc. When I got to these sites, I shipped out various hazardous chemicals, separating them based on their constituents, putting them on shipping documents, and ultimately taking them back to our headquarters, where they would eventually go out to an end facility and get burned, treated, etc. It was an intense job, but it introduced me to this field, and I knew that it was something that I wanted to stay with. I am forever grateful that this is the path that I ended up on. I then spent the next four years working at Columbia University in the Environmental Health and Safety and Facilities departments. This enhanced my background in EH&S as a whole, going from mostly hazmat knowledge to a more diverse background.


My current role as director of Environmental Health & Safety will be to ensure compliance here at SVA, making sure all regulations and codes are followed, performing inspections, leading the hazardous waste and fire safety programs and ultimately promoting a safe and healthy environment.


Tell us more about what Environmental Health & Safety does.

The most important part of Environmental Health & Safety is the safety and well-being of an organization's occupants. The goal is always to be proactive rather than reactive. Certain measures, such as a monthly quick check inspection of a fire extinguisher, may seem small, however, it is important to always be prepared in case of an emergency. So one of the things I take pride in is checking all the bases and ensuring that we do everything we can to remain safe in case of an unlikely event.
