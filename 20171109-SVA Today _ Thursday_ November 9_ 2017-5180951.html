SVA Guide to West Village

Places to visit, things to do and foods to eat in the West Village. Stay tuned for more New York City neighborhood guides.
EVENTS

10:30am | DOC NYC: What's Your POV?

As part of DOC NYC PRO, MFA Social Documentary Film co-presents a panel, moderated by department director of operations Rose Vincelli Gustine, about how to bring a great story to life. Panelists are Nanfu Wang, Jennifer Brea and Drew Xanthopoulos. Must have a DOC NYC PRO pass to attend; purchase here. Thursday, November 9, 10:30 – 11:40am. Cinepolis Chelsea, 260 West 23rd Street.

2:00pm | SVA in San Fransisco Information Session

SVA Career Development and SVA Destinations present two information sessions about the spring break course in San Francisco, which will introduce students to working at Pixar, Google, Industrial Light & Magic, and more. Today, 2:00 – 3:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 1015C. Monday, November 13, 2:00 – 3:00pm, 133/141 West 21st Street Room 301C.

6:00pm | MPS Directing Information Session

MPS Directing presents an opportunity to tour its facilities, meet with the chair, faculty, department staff, current students and alumni, and learn about the program. 6:00 – 8:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, 8th floor.

6:00pm | "Pass / Age / Tress / Pass" Reception

BFA Visual & Critical Studies and the Flatiron Project Space present an exhibition of recent work by faculty member Suzanne Joelson, curated by faculty member Peter Hristoff (BFA 1981 Fine Arts). 6:00 – 8:00pm. Through Friday, December 1. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street, ground floor.

6:30pm | Alex Fattal, Suenos, with Michael Taussig

MFA Art Writing presents a screening of Suenos, an experimental film by anthropologist and filmmaker Alex Fattal, followed by a conversation between Fattal and author Michael Taussig. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.

7:00pm | Paul Pfeiffer

BFA Fine Arts presents a talk by artist Paul Pfeiffer, who uses moving images, sound, sculptural objects and architectural installations to explore how images shape perceptions of ourselves and the world. 7:00 – 9:00pm. 209 East 23rd Street, 3rd-floor amphitheater.

8:00pm | Leo Bersani and Daniel Heller-Roazen

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media present scholars Leo Bersani and Daniel Heller-Roazen, who will talk about Paola Mieli's book, Figures of Space: Subject, Body, Place. Their presentations will be followed by a discussion with the author. RSVP online. 8:00 – 10:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

TAKE NOTE

Mei Lum of the Wing on Wo Project

MFA Fine Arts presents a talk by Mei Lum of Wing on Wo & Co, her family’s 92-year-old porcelain-ware business in Chinatown, which was on the brink of sale in early 2016. Since then, Lum began the WOW Project to reclaim ownership over Chinatown’s future. Tuesday, November 14, 6:30pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

Mimi Zeiger, "Intersect, Instigate, Exchange"

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents a talk by critic, editor and curator Mimi Zeiger, who will investigate how collaboration, structure, research and critique shape exhibition making. Tuesday, November 14, 6:30pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

Words Call for Submissions

Words is a collection of writings by SVA students, faculty and staff, and guest writers, and is published twice a year by Humanities and Sciences. The most recent edition, Words 86, is available at most SVA sites.

To be considered for publication, submit poetry, short stories, short film, theater scripts and/or personal essays as a document attachment to Louis Phillips, SVA faculty member and editor of Words, at lphillips@sva.edu, with the subject "Submission to Words." Include your name, email address and phone number.

"David Bowie Is..." Advance Discount Tickets

"David Bowie Is...," opening March 2, 2018, at the Brooklyn Museum, explores the creative process of an artist whose reinventions revolutionized the way we see music, inspiring people to shape their own identities while challenging social traditions. SVA students, faculty and staff can each purchase one discounted ticket; click here and enter code BOWIESVA. Please note that an SVA ID will be required at the museum for ticket redemption. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway, Brooklyn.
