SVA Today
Friday, March 18, and the Weekend
Students checking out Google Spotlight Stories. Photo by Hsiang Chin Moe.
The Future of Stories
Angie Wojak, SVA's director of Career Development, talks about this year's SVA Destinations trip to San Francisco, which included visits with Google Spotlight Stories, Industrial Light and Magic (ILM), Pixar and Oculus Rift.
Opening: "What Are You Trying to Say?"
MA Curatorial Practice presents an exhibition that focuses on image and language production and comments on mass consumer culture and channels of information. Curated by Vera Petukhova, MA Curatorial Practice fellow. Reception tonight, 6:30 - 8:30pm. Through Friday, April 1, CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
Opening: "The Tide"
A juried exhibition of student work, inspired by the inner workings of the mind. Reception Tuesday, March 29, 6:00 - 8:00pm. Saturday, March 19, through Saturday, April 9, SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
Sunday: Luigi Ballerini
MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and the SVA Library present Italian poet, translator and scholar Luigi Ballerini reading from his new epic poem, Cephalonia (Rail Editions, 2016). Sunday, March 20, 5:00pm, SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.
PRESS PICK OF THE DAY
Designing Infrastructure
In Architecture, MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism faculty member Karrie Jacobs writes about how Manhattan's Spring Street Salt Shed was designed and built.
NYC Weekend Picks
The Art and Whimsy of Mo Willems
An exhibition of work by the acclaimed children's book author and illustrator. Admission is $12 for students, $20 general public. Through September 25, New-York Historical Society, 170 Central Park West.
Sincerity (The Post-Americans)
A group exhibition featuring BFA Visual & Critical Studies student Benjamin Lee Sperry and Quinn Tivey (MFA 2015 Photography, Video and Related Media). Saturday, March 19, and Sunday, March 20, DEP ART, 272 Seigel Street, Brooklyn.
Doughfest
Sample the best doughnuts in New York, with coffee stations and a live DJ. Tickets are $37 for 90-minute sessions. 10:30am - 4:00pm, Marquee New York, 289 10th Avenue.
Coffee and Tea Festival
A celebration of all things coffee and tea, with seminars, pairings, tastings and more. Tickets are $25. Saturday, March 19, noon - 5:00pm, and Sunday, March 20, noon - 4:00pm, Brooklyn Expo Center, 72 Noble Street.
Macaron Day
To commemorate the first day of spring, bakeries are providing one free macaron per customer, while supplies last. Sunday, March 20, 10:00am - 5:00pm. Locations throughout the city.
TAKE NOTE
Home and the Poetics of Space
BFA Interior Design and Student Health and Counseling Services present Esther Sperber, founder of Studio ST Architects, and psychoanalyst and SVA staff member Navah Steiner discussing the concept of home. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Wednesday, March 23, 12:30pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
Remote Area Medical
BFA Animation and BFA Film present a screening of Remote Area Medical (2012), a documentary about the pioneers of no-cost health care clinics near Bristol, Tennessee. Filmmakers Jeff Reichert and Farihah Zaman will answer questions following the screening. Open to SVA students, faculty and staff. Wednesday, March 23, 6:30pm, 209 East 23rd Street, Room 502.
Undergraduate RisoLab Course
Beginning in the fall, MFA Visual Narrative's RisoLab will an offer interdisciplinary studio course open to all undergraduate students on the Risograph Duplicator printing technology, which combines the vibrant spot colors of silkscreen, high-resolution mass production of offset lithography and user-friendly automation of an advanced color copier. Search and register for VND-2134, Risograph Printing in the Age of Digital and Mechanical Reproduction. For questions, contact risolab@sva.edu.
