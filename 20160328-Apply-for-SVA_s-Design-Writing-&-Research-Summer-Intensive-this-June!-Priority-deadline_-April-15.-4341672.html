Join the 2016 Design Writing and Research Summer Intensive!

June 6–17, 2016
Department of Design Research, Writing & Criticism
School of Visual Arts, New York City

Spaces are filling up fast, so apply today! Priority deadline: April 15.

If you care about design and language, and are interested in learning how to write meaningfully about products, graphics, urban infrastructure, and buildings, then you should join us this summer in the School of Visual Arts MA Design Research department for a a two-week intensive devoted to research and writing about design. 

Participants will be introduced to a range of techniques for constructing compelling narratives about images, objects, and spaces. You will experiment with different research methods and writing formats, and complete several projects across a variety of media, including a collaboratively produced publication. 

Each participant in this richly programmed two-week Intensive will have a workstation in the light-filled Design Research studio in New York’s Chelsea district, 24-hour access to department resources, and classes with renowned writers and editors such as: The New York Times columnist Steven Heller; Architect contributor Karrie Jacobs; critic and The Weeklings co-founder Jennifer Kabat; BBC interviewer and Design Observer contributor Adam Harrison Levy; The New York Times culture reporter Robin Pogrebin; technology columnist and Significant Objects author Rob Walker; and architecture critic Mimi Zeiger.
 
A robust daily schedule of seminars, lectures, workshops, and one-on-one consultations will be supplemented with visits to the city’s design collections, archives, and libraries, as well as behind-the-scenes access to new exhibitions, buildings, and urban planning developments. Participants will also have the opportunity to visit, interview, and profile some of New York’s most prominent design studios in the fields of architecture, interaction design, graphic design, fashion, and product design. 

To find out more about projects, participants, and instructors see the Intensive website.
  • Participants
You'll be in great company if you join us this summer! Intensive participants travel to SVA from all over the world, and arrive with experience in diverse careers ranging from design, architecture, and journalism, to curation, education, and event organization.

Past participants of the Intensive have included: director of the Art Foundation Department at VCU Qatar; Design History teacher at the ESPM Undergraduate Design Program in São Paulo; in-house designer at the Milwaukee Art Museum; partner at architecture practice Venkataramanan Associates in Bangalore; director of Archivo Diseño y Arquitectura, a nonprofit Design and Architecture space in Mexico City; founder of design-led startup Make Works in Glasgow; owner of Spark Design Space in Reykjavík; photographer for Cookfox Architects in New York City; and contributing editor of the Norwegian fashion journal Personae.

See all of their bios here.

How to Apply

SVA Design Research and Writing Summer Intensive requires the following materials:
  • Completed application form
  • Writing sample: Up to 2,000 words of published or unpublished writing (such as essays, blog posts, or articles) about design, architecture, or related subjects.
  • Statement of purpose (250–500 words)
  • CV
For priority consideration, applications are due April 15, 2016. Tuition is $2,250.
