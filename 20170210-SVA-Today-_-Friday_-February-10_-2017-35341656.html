News and events from around SVA
SVA Today
Friday, February 10 - Sunday, February 12, 2017

Two Trains Runnin’ with Director Sam Pollard

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and MFA Social Documentary Film present a screening of Two Trains Runnin' and a conversation between the film’s director Sam Pollard and faculty member Grahame Weinbren about the film. RSVP here. 7:00 – 10:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
EVENTS

10:00am | Writing in the Land of Enchantment: Taos, New Mexico, Information Session

Learn about the SVA Destinations program Writing in the Land of Enchantment: Taos, New Mexico. SVA Faculty members Isabelle Deconinck and Davida Singer will discuss this unique, U.S.-based program and answer questions. Manager of SVA Global Melinda Richardson will give a complete overview of all global study opportunities. 10:00 – 11:00am. 209 East 23rd Street, Room 202.

6:00pm | VASA 2017 Elections Information Session

Any students interested in running for a leadership position in VASA, SVA's student government, must attend an information session in order to run. Candidates run as a team of four for the positions of president, vice president, treasurer and student senate chair. Tonight, 6:00pm and Tuesday, February 21, 6:00pm. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street. Election packets can be picked up at information sessions or at Student Engagement and Leadership, 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. The packets include information about eligibility requirements and are due Thursday, March 16, 3:00pm. Email activities@sva.edu with questions.

6:30pm | "K(NO)W MORE" Reception

CP Projects Space presents an immersive exhibition that disorients and reorients the way we look at everything around us, with the participating artists acting as philosophical tricksters. 6:30 – 9:30pm. Through Friday, February 17. CP Projects Space, 132 West 21st Street, 10th floor.
PRESS PICK

DeFrank Funnies

Romanov Grave asks SVA faculty member Steve DeFrank (MFA 1990 Fine Arts) for his opinion on the use of humor in art.
NYC PICKS

Mad, Dangerous Love

An exhibition of cartoon shorts, an arts and crafts activity and lectures about the complicated side of love. Free. Tonight, 5:00 – 9:00pm. Brooklyn Historical Society, 128 Pierrepont Street, Brooklyn.

"Voyeur Village" Opening

MFA Visual Narrative’s RisoLAB artist-in-residence Harvey Redding exhibits "Voyeur Village," an 800-piece three-dimensional cityscape created in the lab. It features multiple structures with open windows displaying scenes of erotic allure and intrigue. 6:00 – 9:00pm. On view Saturday, February 11 – Sunday, February 12, noon – 6:00pm. Leslie-Lohman Museum of Gay and Lesbian Art, Prince Street Project Space, 127-B Prince Street.

"A Pen of All Work"

An exhibition of drawings by Raymond Pettibon that chronicle the history, mythology and culture of America. Free with SVA ID. Through Sunday, April 9. New Museum of Contemporary Art, 225 Bowery.
TAKE NOTE

Mark Neville in Conversation with Adam Bell

MFA Photography, Video and Related Media presents renowned photographer Mark Neville, who will be in conversation with department faculty member and advisor Adam Bell to discuss Neville's first commercially available book, Fancy Pictures (Steidl 2016). RSVP here. Thursday, February 16, 7:00pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.
