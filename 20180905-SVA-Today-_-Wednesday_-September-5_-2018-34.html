SVA Today

Join an SVA Club

Organized through the Visual Arts Student Association (VASA), SVA helps build community through clubs. This year, there's no shortage of clubs and associations to join.
EVENTS

6:00pm | CE Visible Futures Lab Information Session

Take a tour with the faculty of SVA's state-of-the-art digital fabrication lab and learn about its continuing education course offerings. 6:00 – 8:00pm. SVA Visible Futures Lab, 132 West 21st Street, 7th floor.

6:30pm | Art & Activism

SVA Continuing Education presents its second annual Art & Activism event, where five celebrated artists across various disciplines will talk about their work as it relates to social justice. Panelists include comedians Scott Blakeman and Negin Farsad, writer Jake Halpern, photographer and alumnus Kathy Shorr and illustrator Michael Sloan. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:30pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

TAKE NOTE

John Edmonds and Marjon Carlos in Conversation

SVA Library presents artist and photographer John Edmonds and writer and editor Marjon Carlos, who will discuss Edmonds' most recent series, "Tribe" (2018), which is featured in Esopus 25. Their conversation will focus on race, representation, art history and pop culture. Friday, September 7, 7:00pm. SVA Library West, 133/141 West 21st Street, lower level.

Group Reservations at the Whitney

Faculty members who bring a group of over 10 students to the Whitney Museum for group visits must make a reservation through the online form prior to their visit. This will allow groups to skip the line when they arrive and facilitate a smoother entry process. When filling out the form, choose ticket category "Corporate Partner – Affiliation Required – $0." Contact grouptours@whitney.org with any questions.
