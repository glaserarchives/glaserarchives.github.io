SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Thursday, November 19, 2015
The Earth Eaters by Hope and Panayiotis Terzis.
Metaphor, Memory and Mythos
Three current exhibitions by MFA Fine Arts alumni explore transformation from different perspectives: still-life abstraction, self-portraiture and the epochs of civilization.
SVA Emergency Training
On Monday, November 23, 10:30am, SVA's Emergency Management Committee will conduct a training session on how to respond, stay safe and keep informed during specific incidents that may occur on campus, including medical emergencies and acts of violence. Space is limited, so reservations are recommended by tomorrow, November 20. Click the headline to RSVP. For questions, contact proffice@sva.edu.
10:00am: Faculty and Staff Appreciation Day at the SVA Campus Store
Today is faculty and staff appreciation day at the SVA Campus Store. Faculty and staff receive free gift cards and 500GB hard drives with every new (in-stock) computer purchase, as well as 20% discounts on SVA merchandise. All purchases made today by faculty and staff include entry in a raffle for prizes. The SVA Campus Store is located at 207 East 23rd Street and is open Monday - Friday, 10:00am - 6:00pm.
12:30pm: Teaching Art at the College Level, Creating a Teaching Packet
SVA Career Development presents a workshop about teaching art at the college level for SVA graduate students and alumni with a master's degree. The workshop will provide an overview of the various components of the academic teaching packet and how to prepare them. Following the workshop, 15 minute one-on-one sessions will be available for feedback on materials such as CVs, cover letters or any other documents from your teaching packet. 133/141 West 21st Street, 9th floor.
6:30pm: NY School, Inside the Rhizome, with Anne Waldman
Poet, performer, educator and activist Anne Waldman discusses her experiences as a member of the New York School, a loosely affiliated group of artists and writers who lived and worked in New York City in the mid-20th century. Presented by MFA Art Writing. 132 West 21st Street, 6th floor.
Press pick of the day
Student Film Wins Jury Prize at DOC NYC
MFA Social Documentary Film student Yuqi Kang's Last Days of Domino earned special mention for "experimental vision" at the DOC NYC festival's student showcase. An encore screening of the film, along with the showcase's other winners, will take place tonight at 7:30pm, IFC Center, 323 Sixth Avenue. Tickets are $17.
NYC picks of the day
Union Square Holiday Market
The annual open-air holiday market opens today and features local crafts, handmade goods, artwork and food. Through Thursday, December 24. Open weekdays 11:00am - 8:00pm, Saturdays, 10:00am - 8:00pm, and Sundays, 11:00am - 7:00pm. Union Square Park, 14th Street and Broadway.
Robert Mapplethorpe Panel Discussion
Panelists will discuss the 25th anniversary of the obscenity trial caused by the photographer's controversial exhibition, "The Perfect Moment." Tickets are $10. 7:00pm, New Museum, 285 Bowery.
Take Note
Zumba Class at New York Sports Club
VASA presents a free Zumba class for students, faculty and staff tonight at 8:45pm, New York Sports Club, 113 East 23rd Street. NYSC currently offers discounted memberships to students ($29.95/month with no annual fee) and faculty and staff ($39.95/month with a $49.95 annual fee). Macbeth
The Shakespeare Society presents a preview screening of the new film adaption of Macbeth, starring Michael Fassbender and Marion Cotillard. Preceding the screening will be a brief introduction by Yale University Shakespeare scholar Brian Walsh. Free with SVA ID (one guest limit per student, faculty and staff member). Sunday, November 22, 6:30pm, SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
Tickets to MoMA's Picasso Exhibition
Timed tickets are now required to view MoMA's exhibition of Picasso sculptures during regular museum hours. Timed tickets are not required for SVA faculty and staff to view the exhibition. However, they are required for SVA students and are available on a first-come, first-served basis. This offer does not apply to Continuing Education students. Admission to MoMA is free with SVA ID. For complete guidelines, visit the SVA Museum Benefits page. Through Wednesday, February 7, MoMA, 11 West 53rd Street.
