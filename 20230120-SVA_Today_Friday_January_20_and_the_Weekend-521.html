NYC PICKS

Black and Female

Zimbabwean novelist, playwright and filmmaker Tsitsi Dangarembga is joined by Pulitzer Prize-winning cultural critic Margo Jefferson to discuss Dangarembga’s new essay collection that weaves the personal and political in an exploration of race and gender. Free; RSVP here. Tonight, January 20, 7:00 – 8:30pm. Frederick P. Rose Auditorium, 41 Cooper Square.

Lunar New Year at the Met

The Metropolitan Museum of Art celebrates the holiday with a series of performances, activities and artist-led workshops. Free. Saturday, January 21, 11:00am – 5:00pm. The Metorpolitan Museum of Art, 1000 Fifth Avenue.

Saturday Afternoon Cartoons: Winter Wonderlands

SVA faculty member Tommy José Stathes hosts a winter-themed screening of animated cartoons from the 1920s through the 1950s. Tickets: $17. Saturday, January 21, 12:30pm. Metrograph, 7 Ludlow Street.

For Freedoms News Special Report: News From the Inside

Artist and activist Bryonn Bain and transformative justice practitioner Claudia Peña discuss the injustice of mass criminalization as seen through the eyes of people who are currently and formerly incarcerated. Free admission with SVA ID, talk is pay-what-you-wish; RSVP here. Saturday, January 21, 2:00 – 3:30pm. Brooklyn Museum, 200 Eastern Parkway.

Chinese New Year Firecracker Ceremony and Cultural Festival

Students are invited to apply to be a VASA student senator. Senators represent their major in interdepartmental collaborations and events. Get involved on campus and organize events for your peers; apply here. The priority deadline is Monday, January 27. Contact senators@sva.edu with any questions.

Announcing the Fall 2022 Term Honors Recipients

A total of 751 students from SVA’s 11 undergraduate majors have been awarded term honors for Fall 2022, and the list of recipients is now available to view by clicking on the “Resources” tile in your MyID dashboard. To be included, students must successfully complete 12 credits of coursework, without any grades of incomplete, and earn a term GPA within the top 20% of all students in their major department. Students whose term GPA is within the top 5% of all students in their major department receive high term honors. Congratulations to the honorees!
