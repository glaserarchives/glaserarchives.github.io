NYC PICK

Friday Night Funnies
SVA faculty member Tommy José Stathes hosts a specially curated screening of early and rare animated cartoon adaptations of famous early comic strip characters. Tickets: $20. Tonight, 7:00pm. Society of Illustrators, 128 East 63rd Street.

Japan Day Parade
Every year, Japan Day @ Central Park celebrates Japanese culture, art, tradition and food. Saturday, May 14. Opening ceremony starts at 12:30pm; parade begins at 1:00pm. The parade will proceed along Central Park West from West 81st Street to West 68th Street.

Asian American and Pacific Islander (AAPI) Cultural and Heritage Parade
Celebrate Asian American and Pacific Islander culture and heritage at New York City’s first AAPI parade. Sunday, May 15, 10:45am. The parade will take place on Sixth Avenue, proceeding north from West 44th Street to West 55th Street.

NYCxDesign Festival Exhibitions
BFA Design and BFA Interior Design: Built Environments present the exhibitions “45 Degrees” and “Hemlines from Skylines,” on view as part of the NYCxDesign Festival. Through Sunday, May 15. 601 LEX, 601 Lexington Avenue.
ANNOUNCEMENT

Unearthing Public Art
Unearthing Public Art Madison Square Park Conservancy’s annual symposium, which convenes eminent artists, scholars and curators from the art world, will investigate the central role of public art in exploring buried and manufactured histories in civic space. Featured guests include artists Cristina Iglesias, Alice Aycock (faculty, BFA Fine Arts), Maren Hassinger, Alan Michelson and Kennedy Yanko. Free and open to the public. Friday, June 3, 9:30am – 12:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.
