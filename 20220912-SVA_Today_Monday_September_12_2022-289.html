August’s Hot Five

The top five most-discussed projects on SVA Portfolios last month include traditional but spooky paintings fit for Halloween, a still-life photo project and terrific illustrations from our student and alumni community members.

EVENTS

Ongoing | “The Masters Series: Lynsey Addario”

An exhibition of work by Lynsey Addario, acclaimed photojournalist, 2009 MacArthur Fellow and Pulitzer Prize recipient, as part of the 32nd annual Masters Series Award and Exhibition. Curated by Maya Benton and Perri Hofmann. Through Saturday, October 29. SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.

Ongoing | “The Health Poster Project”

An exhibition of posters by 24 students from BFA Illustration, MFA Illustration as Visual Essay and the 2022 Summer Illustration Residency, curated by BFA Comics and BFA Illustration Chair Viktor Koen (MFA 1992 Illustration as Visual Essay). Through Monday, September 19. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “MPS Fashion Photography Class of 2022”

MPS Fashion Photography presents an exhibition of works by the class of 2022. Through Monday, September 19. SVA Gramercy Gallery, 209 East 23rd Street.

Ongoing | “Dwelling in Motion”

A juried exhibition of works that analyze the constantly growing relationship between habitat and inhabitant. Through Monday, September 19. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

Ongoing | “A Sticker History Exhibition”

BFA Visual & Critical Studies presents an exhibition of stickers by various cultural creators from diverse backgrounds and eras, curated by DB Burkeman and Stuck-Up. Through Thursday, September 29. SVA Flatiron Project Space, 133/141 West 21st Street.

SVA IN THE PRESS

Kenny Rivero in T

Kenny Rivero (BFA 2010 Fine Arts) is interviewed by T: The New York Times Style Magazine on the occasion of his latest show at Charles Moffett Gallery in Manhattan, “Steward: The Ballad of a Super Super.”

NYC PICK

“Play to Escape”

A group exhibition exploring play as a form of escapism, curated by Shannon Finnell, formerly of SVA Career Development, featuring work by Jingyao Huang (BFA 2019 Photography and Video). Through Saturday, October 29. Here Arts Center, 145 Sixth Avenue.

ANNOUNCEMENTS

Join the International Student Office, SVA Student Health and Counseling Services and the Chinese Student Organization for a Mid-Autumn Festival celebration gathering with dumplings, noodles and mooncake. Open to everyone in the SVA community. RSVP required; register here. Thursday, September 15, 6:00 – 7:30pm. 24th Street Residence, 340 East 24th Street, terrace.
