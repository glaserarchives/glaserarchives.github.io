SVA Today | Your guide to everything
Tuesday, December 8, 2015
Women Speaking to Power
On Friday, December 11, at the SVA Theatre, MA Curatorial Practice presents artists Tania Bruguera and Shirin Neshat in a conversation moderated by department chair Steven Henry Madoff. SVA Close Up spoke with Madoff about the artists and their work.
1:00pm: SVA in San Francisco Information Session
Join SVA Career Development Director Angie Wojak for an information session about the weeklong SVA in San Francisco trip from March 6 - 12, 2016. The session will go over the trip's itinerary, which includes studio visits at top technology and entertainment companies, such as Autodesk, Industrial Light and Magic, Oculus Rift’s Story Studio and Steel Wool Games. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 301C.
6:00pm: "We Are Not Decorators" Reception
An exhibition of work by BFA Interior Design students that sets out to distinguish the terms "interior decorator" and "interior designer," clarifying distinctions between decorative arts, architecture and interior design that are often misunderstood. Through Saturday, January 9, 2016. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.
6:00pm: The Secret World of Art Dealers with Chris Verene
SVA Career Development and MFA Fine Arts present artist Chris Verene discussing his 20 years of experience in the art world, including advice on artist-gallery relationships. Open to SVA graduate students. Standing room only. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.
6:30pm: Michael Garofalo, The Cut and Paste Narrative
MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents producer Michael Garofalo discussing his work with NPR's StoryCorps. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.
Press pick of the day
Star Wars Fashion
In Seventeen, 2014 Visible Futures Lab Artist in Residence Maddy Maxey talks about her work on the high-tech dress actress Lupita Nyong'o wore to the recent Star Wars: Force 4 Fashion event.
NYC picks of the day
Madison Sq. Holiday
The annual tree-lighting ceremony features live singing, seasonal activities and neighborhood restaurants serving holiday treats. 3:30 - 5:30pm, Madison Square Park, Broadway at 23rd Street.
The City Is My Muse
Artists Jane Dickson, Christopher "Daze" Ellis and Lee Quinones discuss how New York City's environment, culture and daily life have inspired their work. Co-sponsored by VASA. Tickets are $12 for students, $16 for general public. 6:30pm, The Museum of the City of New York, 1220 Fifth Avenue.
Take Note
Business Basics for Artists
SVA Career Development presents an information session about the basic principles of copyright law and intellectual property, including royalties and licensing fees and contracts. Open to SVA seniors, graduate students and alumni. Registration is required. Wednesday, December 12, 6:00pm, 136 West 21st Street, Room 418F.
BFA Fine Arts Open Studios
BFA Fine Arts presents students showing recent work in their studios. 10:00am - 5:00pm, Saturday, December 12, and Sunday, December 13. Reception Monday, December 14, 6:00 - 9:00pm. 335 West 16th Street, 2nd - 5th floors.
"Intimacy In Discourse" Panel Discussion
SVA Galleries and Rail Curatorial Projects present curator Phong Bui speaking with a selection of artists whose work is on view in "Unreasonable Sized Paintings" at the SVA Chelsea Gallery and a concurrent exhibition, "Reasonable Sized Paintings," at Mana Contemporary. Panelists include Mark Greenwold, Matvey Levenstein, Catherine Murphy, Thomas Nozkowski, Ann Pibal, Julia Rommel, James Siena, Robert Storr, Roger White and Lisa Yuskavage. Saturday, December 12, 3:00 - 5:00pm, SVA Chelsea Gallery, 601 West 26th Street, 15th floor.
