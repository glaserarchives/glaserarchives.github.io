SVA Today
Tuesday, December 12, 2017

21 Titles by SVA Artists

To follow the SVA Holiday Gift Guide: Product Edition, here is a roundup of books written or illustrated (or both!) by SVA alumni and faculty that will satisfy a variety of people on your "nice" list.
EVENTS

1:00pm | Finals Fair

VASA presents two days of fun, relaxation and snacks to help students get through finals. Today, enjoy massages from 1:00 – 4:00pm, and make Play Doh stress balls with Student Health and Counseling, 2:00 – 3:30pm. Tomorrow, December 13, will feature Zumba, aromatherapy and board games. SVA Student Center, 217 East 23rd Street.

2:00pm | Pete Souza: Obama: An Intimate Portrait

The B&H Event Space and MPS Digital Photography present former chief official White House photographer Pete Souza, who will discuss his book Obama: An Intimate Portrait. Tickets required. 2:00 – 4:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

6:00pm | "M'Bongi: A Place of Non-Warship" Reception

BFA Interior Design presents an exhibition of design concepts for a nonprofit peace center by its third-year students. 6:00 – 8:00pm. On view through Saturday, January 6. SVA Flatiron Gallery, 133/141 West 21st Street.

6:30pm | Amy Whitaker

MFA Fine Arts presents Amy Whitaker, an assistant professor at NYU Steinhardt in Visual Arts Administration who approaches economics and finance as creative design mediums and teaches business to artists and arts administrators. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, Room 101C.

6:30pm | Rob Giampietro

MA Design Research, Writing and Criticism presents Rob Giampietro, design lead for Google’s Research and Machine Intelligence team in New York. RSVP here. 6:30 – 8:00pm. 136 West 21st Street, 2nd floor.

7:00pm | Sean Hemmerle Reading and Book Signing

BFA Photography and Video, MFA Photography, Video and Related Media and Dear Dave, magazine present photographer and SVA faculty member Sean Hemmerle (MFA 1997 Photography, Video and Related Media), who will talk about his book Them, featuring portraits from Iraq and Afghanistan. A book signing will follow. 7:00 – 8:30pm. 214 East 21st Street, Room 120.

TAKE NOTE

SVA Library: Extended Hours and Free Coffee

SVA Library will have extended hours through Tuesday, December 19. On select nights, free coffee will also be served. Click here for details. SVA Library, 380 Second Avenue, 2nd floor.

MFA Fine Arts Open Studio

MFA Fine Arts presents its fall open studio, a one-night-only event where visitors can see what the students in the program are making, doing and thinking about. Thursday, December 14, 5:00pm. 133/141 West 21st Street, 8th and 9th floors.

Holiday Angels Program

SVA Student Affairs and Abraham House are providing gifts for children in need through the annual Holiday Angels Program. Click here to view the list of children and their wishes. Confirm your selection of a child to be sponsored by calling 212.592.2214, writing Adam Krumm at akrumm@sva.edu or visiting SVA Student Affairs, 340 East 24th Street, ground floor. Cash donations accepted in-person only. All gifts are due to Student Affairs by Friday, December 15, 3:00pm.

Ferdinand Screening

SVA Theatre presents a 3D screening of the new Blue Sky Studios animated film Ferdinand, directed by Carlos Saldanha (MFA 1993 Computer Art). Free with SVA ID (three guest limit per SVA student, faculty and staff member). RSVPs not accepted or required. Saturday December 16, 2:00pm. SVA Theatre, 333 West 23rd Street.

Apply Today for Paris Impressionists: A Walking Tour

SVA Destinations presents a spring break trip to Paris from March 3 – 11, 2018. Rediscover the works of the impressionist masters. Explore Paris by walking in the footsteps of artists such as Degas, Monet, Van Gogh and Renoir. Visit the museums where their work is exhibited today. Learn how impressionists changed the course of art history. Limited spaces available. Click here to apply today. For more information, contact Aziza Gaines, program coordinator, at againes@sva.edu or 212.592.2070, or visit the SVA Destinations website.
