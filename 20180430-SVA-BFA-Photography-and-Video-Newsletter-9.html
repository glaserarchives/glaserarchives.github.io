Todd Midler Photographs Story for The New Yorker

Current student Todd Midler shot a story for The New Yorker's "Annals of Gastronomy" on the rising popularity of the "all-day café."
Devin Yalkin Photographs The New York Times Magazine Cover Story

Alum Devin Yalkin (2010) photographed the Arlee Warriors from Montana’s Flathead Indian Reservation, which accompanies an article following these Native high schoolers facing systemic disenfranchisement and a suicide epidemic.
Lyle Rexer Reviews Ed van der Elsken at Howard Greenberg Gallery

Faculty member Lyle Rexer reviews "Love" & Other Stories for Photograph, on view through May 5, 2018 at Howard Greenberg Gallery.
Efrem Zelony-Mindell Curates Alums in Upcoming Re: Exhibition

Alum Efrem Zelony-Mindell (2011) is curating the latest iteration of Re:, staff member Max C. Lee's curatorial project, opening on May 26, 2018. The exhibition includes current student Cameron Kelly McLeod, faculty member and alum Alex McTigue (2009), and alums Vincent Bezuidenhout (2014), Desiree Kong (2017), Marco Scozzaro (2011), Jake Sigl (2015), Ilona Szwarc (2013), and Particia Voulgaris (2013).
Shanna Merola in Conversation with Stephen Frailey

In the newest DEAR DAVE, INTERVIEWS, photographer Shanna Merola speaks with department chair Stephen Frailey about her work documenting civil and human rights abuses for the National Lawyers Guild, and the body of work born from it.
New African Photography III at Red Hook Labs

Red Hook Labs and Nataal are delighted to announce their third co-curated group exhibition: New African Photography III, on view at Red Hook Labs from May 1 to May 13, 2018.
Adobe's Massive Update to Camera Profiles in ACR and Lightroom 

Among other updates, Adobe has created six new profiles to work within Adobe Camera Raw (ACR) and Lightroom.
LaToya Ruby Frazier Photographs Black Mothers for The New York Times

Photographer LaToya Ruby Frazier documented Simone Landrum, an expectant mother during her prenatal preparations, as well as her doula, for a story that follows her pregnancy and speaks about the crisis facing black mothers and their babies.
Aperture Shares The Woman Behind the First Photo Gallery 

Out now as an e-book from Aperture, Helen Gee: Limelight, a Greenwich Village Photography Gallery and Coffeehouse in the Fifties recalls the late Helen Gee and the art world of NYC in its early days.
Photographer Tim Franco Creates Portraits of DPRK Defectors

In an interview series from The New York Times Lens Blog, photographer Tim Franco took portraits of North Korean defectors using an old wooden 4x5 camera.
Peter Hujar's Speed of Life Resonates

Artnet reviews Peter Hujar's posthumous exhibition at The Morgan Library & Museum and speaks to the power it holds in today's art world and beyond. The exhibition is on view through May 20, 2018.

SVA Destinations: Art Education and Conservation in Madagascar

This SVA course offers a unique opportunity for art educators to collaborate with Malagasy educators to create an art and science curriculum that can instill in present and future generations the importance of preserving and caring for this delicate ecosystem.
Maya Lin to Speak at 2018 SVA Commencement Exercises

Artist, designer, and environmentalist Maya Lin will be the keynote speaker at the 42nd annual commencement exercises, to be held at 1 PM on Monday, May 7, 2018, at Radio City Music Hall, located at 1260 Avenue of the Americas in Manhattan.
i-D Features Sam Stoich

Current student Sam Stoich was interviewed on i-D for his ongoing photo series In A Red Room, which explores the topics of stigmatization, HIV, and the politics of fear.
SVA Features Interviews SVA Alumni Scholarship Winner Ken Castaneda

Current student and SVA Alumni Scholarship winner Ken Castaneda spoke with SVA Features about his ongoing body of work that centers around Mexican lucha libre wrestling, performance, and his own Latinx identity.
The New Yorker Features Ina Jang

Alum Ina Jang (2010) is featured on The New Yorker's Photo Booth. Jang's current exhibition "Utopia" is on view at Foley Gallery through May 13, 2018.
SVA NYC Features Ken Ockenfels

Alum Ken Ockenfels (1983) speaks with SVA about his time in the program and his practice photographing some of the biggest names in film, music, and television.
 
Balam Features Caroline Tompkins & Samantha Friend

Alums Caroline Tompkins (2014) and Samantha Friend (2016) are featured in the latest issue of Balam.
Joseph Maida Contributes to New Book By Charlotte Cotton

Faculty member Joseph Maida is one of the contributors to Charlotte Cotton's new book Public, Private, Secret: On Photography and the Configuration of Self.
Barbara Pollack Reviews William Wegman at MoMA

Faculty member Barbara Pollack reviews Before/On/After: William Wegman and California Conceptualism for Photograph, on view through July 15, 2018, at MoMA.

Historic Harold Edgerton Exhibition at the Whitney

Harold Edgerton developed flash technology in the 1930s, allowing the camera to perceive things moving faster than our sight allows. This collection covers 30 years of his work, depicting single and multiple-exposure images of household products, performances, sporting events, and staged scenarios.
